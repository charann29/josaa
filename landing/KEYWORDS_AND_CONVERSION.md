## 1. The conversion thesis (read this first)

Three facts from your research decide everything about how to run ads:

1. **This is a painkiller, not a vitamin.** The pain is acute, high-stakes, and *irreversible* — one locked choice list decides a 4-year college, and there's no undo. Severity is real (people pay ₹3,000–₹25,000 for human counselling). Sell the *cure to the fear of locking wrong*, not "a tool."
2. **The window is tiny and event-driven.** Demand compresses into the ~3-week JoSAA choice-filling window (June), with micro-spikes on **mock allotment days** and each **round result**, then a CSAB/state tail through September. You are not running ads year-round — you are running *blitzes* timed to events.
3. **Trust is the whole game.** The category is poisoned by lead-gen spam and "guaranteed seat" scams. Your single biggest conversion lever is being visibly *not that*: "we charge you, not colleges; we never sell your number; we never promise a guaranteed seat." Every ad and the page lead with this.

**Implication for spend:** Google **Search** is your highest-intent, highest-ROI channel (people are actively typing the problem). YouTube + Meta are for *retargeting* and cheap reach during spikes. Put ~70% of budget on Search during live events.

## 6. Conversion levers on the page (already built in — keep/tune)

1. **One CTA, repeated.** Every button does the same thing: *Book my slot → calendar → Razorpay.* No competing actions. (WhatsApp is the only secondary, for not-yet-ready traffic — it's a softer commitment, not a competing offer.)
2. **Price as a painkiller, not a cost.** "One wrong locked choice = a year + lakhs. Getting it right = ₹1,999." Anchored against ₹5,000–₹25,000. Keep the strike-through.
3. **Trust before features.** Micro-proof under the hero (official data · we charge you not colleges · no spam · refund) is the first thing after the headline. This is the conversion unlock for a spam-burned audience.
4. **The honesty/anti-scam band** turns the category's poison into your credibility. Counter-intuitive but it's your strongest differentiator — keep it prominent.
5. **Comparison table** does the "why not free / why not ₹25k" objection-handling silently.
6. **Urgency is real, not fake.** Tie the top bar to the *actual* verified close/lock/mock date. Real deadlines convert; invented ones get you reported.
7. **Optional free lead-magnet funnel (recommended):** run a *separate* campaign on Cluster B "free predictor" terms → a free rank→shortlist tool that captures WhatsApp → retarget with the ₹1,999 offer. This lets you compete for the huge free-intent volume without polluting your paid campaigns, and feeds retargeting cheaply. (Your research shows free-expectation is strong; don't fight it head-on — wedge it.)

## 7. Campaign structure & timing (the blitz calendar)

Structure Google Ads as **one campaign per cluster** (clean budget control + negatives), each with 1–2 tight ad groups.

| Phase | When (each cycle) | Turn ON | Budget tilt |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-fill | result out → choice filling opens | A, B, F + brand | warm up, gather search terms |
| **Peak** | choice filling open → lock date | **A, C, F** hard | 70% of total here |
| Mock spikes | each mock-allotment day | **D** (day-parted) | burst |
| Round results | each round result day | **C, D** | burst |
| Extension | JoSAA ends → CSAB/state | **E** + retarget | keep the season alive |
| Off-season | rest of year | brand only + content/SEO | near-zero paid |

- **Day-part** D and C to the hours around official releases (your research shows these are intraday spikes).
- **Geo:** start where your delivery capacity is strongest; your held domains hint at a Hyderabad/India focus — consider a Telugu-belt + Hindi-belt split with language-matched ad copy.
- **Device:** mobile-first (aspirants are on phones). The page + WhatsApp secondary are built for that.

## 8. What to measure

| Metric | Why | Watch for |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per slot booked | true CAC | keep < ₹1,999 ARPU (stress-test kill line: CAC > ₹3–5k = stop) |
| Booking → payment rate (Razorpay) | calendar→pay leak | if low, the calendar→Razorpay handoff is the bottleneck, not the ad |
| LP click→book rate | page conversion | benchmark 8–15% for hot search traffic on a painkiller offer |
| Search-terms junk % | negative-list health | prune every 48h during peak |
| WhatsApp → paid conversion | warm-lead value | tells you if the soft CTA earns its place |
| Refund rate | trust/fit honesty | a low rate validates the "refund if not fit" promise is safe |

**Kill criteria (from your own stress test):** if paid conversion of clicks is < 1–2% *and* CAC > ARPU after a real peak-window pilot, the issue is offer/trust, not traffic — fix the page and the proof, don't just raise bids.
