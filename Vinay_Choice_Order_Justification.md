# Vinay's JoSAA 2026 Choice Order — Methodology & Justification

*Prepared to explain exactly what was chosen, in what order, and why — so every decision can be checked.*

---

## 1. Who we are optimizing for

These facts drive every decision below:

- **Ranks:** OBC-NCL-PwD **388** (his strongest), Common-PwD (CRL-PwD) **940**, OBC-NCL 1,64,298, CRL 4,48,918.
- **PwD type:** **Locomotor (leg / walking) disability.** This is both a legal eligibility factor *and* a practical fit factor.
- **Gender:** Male → eligible only for **Gender-Neutral** seats (Female-only seats excluded entirely).
- **Home state:** Telangana → **Home-State quota at NIT Warangal**; Other-State everywhere else; All-India at IIITs/GFTIs.
- **Eligibility:** JEE-Main (B.E./B.Tech) seats only — not eligible for B.Arch or JEE-Advanced/IIT seats.
- **Stated family goal:** a genuinely good campus and culture, strong placements (target ≈ 12 LPA), and a branch/role that suits his condition and gives an upward trajectory.

The two ranks that actually get him seats are **388 (OBC-PwD)** and **940 (Common-PwD)** — his non-PwD ranks are too weak for NITs/IIITs. So reachability is judged against those two, using the correct quota (Home-State for Warangal, Other-State for other NITs, All-India for IIITs/GFTIs).

---

## 2. The one goal, and the rules that follow

**Goal:** the best *career outcome and quality of life* for a locomotor-disabled student aiming at ~12 LPA — not the most prestigious-sounding line on paper.

From that goal, five rules fall out, in priority order:

1. **Branch family first — computer/circuit branches lead.** CSE, AI, Data Science, Maths & Computing, IT, ECE/VLSI, EEE.
2. **Within a branch tier, college brand decides** (NIT > IIIT > GFTI, with exceptional institutions like University of Hyderabad pulled up).
3. **Core/field branches (Mechanical, Production, Chemical, Civil, Metallurgy, Bio) are kept only at *strong* colleges, as backup** — never at weak colleges.
4. **Anything the portal restricts for his walking disability is excluded** (the portal's own medical rule), and weak-environment locations (Bihar) sit at the very bottom.
5. **Reaches are still listed at the top** (above his safe options) because in JoSAA that costs nothing — see §5.

## 3. Rule-by-rule justification, with data

### Rule 1 — Why computer/circuit branches come first (the biggest call)

Two independent reasons point the same way for *this* student:

**(a) Placements.** Across NITs, software-facing branches dominate core branches on both "how many get placed" and "how much":

- CSE ≈ **81% placed, ~₹20 LPA average**; Mechanical ≈ **73% placed, ~₹12 LPA average** (intake-basis data across NITs).
- At NIT Patna, CSE's highest was ₹34 L vs Mechanical ₹18.9 L / Civil ₹19 L; core averages sat around ₹7.5 L.
- The career consensus is blunt: *"branch defines your starting point, skills define your final outcome"* — and a software starting point is far easier to reach from a computer/circuit branch.

**(b) His condition.** A locomotor (leg) disability makes **desk-based, remote-friendly software/IT roles the single most accessible and sustainable career path** — no field sites, plant floors, or mobility-heavy work. Software is repeatedly cited as the most accessible high-paying field for people with walking difficulty. So the branch that pays best *also* fits him best. These rarely align so cleanly; here they do, so branch leads.

This is why a great branch at a decent college outranks a weak branch at a great college in our order.

### Rule 2 — Why brand decides within a branch tier

Once branch is fixed, the college still controls peer group, recruiter footfall, alumni network, and campus life. So inside the computer/circuit tier we sort by brand: top NITs first, then strong IIITs, with standout institutions (e.g., University of Hyderabad) lifted above weaker NITs/IIITs. This honors "NIT > IIIT > GFTI" while not blindly burying an exceptional campus.

### Rule 3 — Why core branches are kept only at strong colleges

A core branch (say Mechanical) at a *top* NIT still carries brand, decent core recruiters, and the option to pivot to software — a legitimate fallback. The same core branch at a *weak* college offers neither good core placements nor a software pipeline — a genuine dead end. So core branches appear only at strong colleges, and only **after** every computer/circuit option (they are the backup, not the plan). Core at weak colleges was dropped entirely (187 such entries removed).

### Rule 4 — Why restricted seats and Bihar are excluded

- **Restricted seats:** The portal itself shows, for certain institutes, *"Not given to Orthopedically Handicapped Candidates with disability in walking…"* — i.e., it bars his exact condition. We detect that message and **decline it automatically (51 entries)**. This isn't our guess; it's the institute's own medical rule. Listing a seat he is barred from is pointless and risky.
- **Bihar (NIT Patna, IIIT Bhagalpur, BIT Patna, NIELIT Patna):** placed at the very bottom on the family's location/safety preference — present only as an absolute last resort, never steering him there.

## 4. The hard tradeoff you raised: top-NIT core vs mid-IIIT CSE

You used a perfect example. Here is the actual data:

| Option | Avg package (2024) | Career nature |
|---|---|---|
| NIT Trichy **Production** | ~₹13.7 L | Core/manufacturing default; software possible but uphill |
| IIIT Vadodara **CSE** | ~₹12–15 L | Software-default; remote/desk roles |
| NIT Trichy **CSE** (reference) | ~₹27 L | Software-default, top brand |

The packages for top-NIT Production and mid-IIIT CSE are **roughly similar** — so money alone doesn't settle it. What settles it for *Vinay* is **role and fit**:

- IIIT-CSE puts him on a **software track by default** — the role with the best long-term ceiling *and* the one most compatible with a walking disability (desk/remote).
- NIT-Production puts him on a **manufacturing/operations track by default**, which often involves shop-floor/plant exposure and requires extra, uphill effort to convert into software.

So even when the starting salaries look similar, **CSE wins for him on trajectory and on physical suitability.** That is precisely why our order places IIIT/decent-college CSE *above* top-NIT Production — your instinct was right, and the data backs it.

(The one nuance: a few strong IIITs, including Vadodara, turned out to be **barred for his walking disability** by their own rule, so they were excluded — see §7. The principle still holds for the IIITs/colleges he *can* take.)

## 5. Why this order is both ambitious and safe

JoSAA allocates **the single highest choice in your list that you qualify for** — nothing lower can "override" something higher. Two consequences:

1. **Listing reaches at the top costs nothing.** Warangal CSE, Trichy CSE, etc. are reaches at his rank, but listing them first only means: *if a miracle happens, take it; otherwise fall through to the next.* There is zero downside.
2. **The order must reflect true preference, top to bottom.** Because he gets the best he qualifies for, the safe options must sit in the right relative order *below* the reaches. They do: computer/circuit everywhere, then core at strong colleges, then last resorts.

So the list reads as: **dream → realistic → safe → last resort**, and the allocator automatically stops at the best one he clears.

## 6. What to realistically expect for Vinay

From the 2023–25 cutoffs (his quota, his PwD pools):

- **Top-NIT/IIIT CSE/ECE are reaches** (those PwD seats close at ranks far better than 388/940) — listed for upside only.
- **His genuinely reachable good outcomes** are: computer/circuit branches at **less-saturated colleges** where his 388/940 clears, and **core branches at NIT Warangal (home quota)** — Mechanical/Chemical/Metallurgy/Bio show Borderline-to-Safe for him.
- **Most likely realistic landing:** either a **computer/circuit seat at a mid-tier but decent college**, or a **core branch at home NIT Warangal** — both solid, and Warangal keeps him close to home in a strong-culture campus.

The order is built so that *if* a computer/circuit seat is within reach anywhere decent, he gets that first; only if none clears does he fall to a Warangal core branch.

## 7. What we deliberately excluded, and why

- **51 seats auto-declined** — institutes whose own rule bars walking-disabled candidates. This unfortunately removes some otherwise-strong IIITs **institute-wide** (Sri City, Vadodara, Dharwad, Kottayam, Guwahati, Raichur, Kalyani, Bhagalpur, Diu) and a few others. Correct call given his condition; full list in `vinay_SKIPPED_restricted.csv`.
- **187 core/field seats at weak colleges** — dropped (poor outcomes, no software pipeline).
- **B.Arch and Mining-type seats** — not offered to him / unsuitable.
- **Bihar institutes** — bottom of list only.

Net: **458 choices filled and saved**, in the order above.

## 8. Honest limitations

- Placement figures are **directional averages** from public 2024-25 sources; individual outcomes vary, and "average" hides spread.
- Cutoff-based reachability uses 2023-25 history; 2026 can shift.
- The walking-restriction exclusions follow the portal's wording; if Vinay's exact disability certificate doesn't match a specific institute's stated restriction, that's a judgment he and you can make seat-by-seat on the portal.
