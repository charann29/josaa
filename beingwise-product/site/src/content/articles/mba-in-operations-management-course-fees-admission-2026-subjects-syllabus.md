# MBA in Operations Management: Course Fees, Admission 2026, Subjects, Syllabus, Salary in India

An MBA in Operations Management is a two-year postgraduate degree that focuses on optimizing business processes, from production and manufacturing to service delivery. It's an excellent choice for those looking to develop strong analytical and leadership skills to manage complex operational challenges.

## Key takeaways

*   This two-year postgraduate program is available in full-time and part-time formats, with Executive MBA options for experienced professionals.
*   The curriculum emphasizes planning, organizing, and supervising production and service operations.
*   Graduates are highly sought after in growing sectors like e-commerce, manufacturing, and services, with diverse career paths in both private and government sectors.
*   Expected salaries for graduates typically range from INR 4 LPA to INR 30 LPA, depending on experience and the organization.

## Understanding Operations Management

Operations management forms the backbone of any organization, ensuring that goods and services are produced and delivered efficiently and effectively. An MBA in this specialization equips students with the expertise to streamline processes, improve productivity, and make strategic decisions that impact an organization's bottom line. The skills gained, such as analytical thinking, problem-solving, decision-making, and process optimization, are highly valued in today's dynamic business environment.

## Eligibility Criteria and Admission 2026

To be eligible for an MBA in Operations Management, candidates generally need a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university. Most institutions require a minimum aggregate score, often around 50%, though this can vary. For admission in 2026, prospective students will typically need to clear one of the national-level MBA entrance examinations. These exams are usually followed by a Group Discussion (GD) and a Personal Interview (PI) round, which are crucial components of the selection process at most reputable B-schools.

### Popular Entrance Exams

Some of the widely accepted entrance exams for MBA programs in India include:

*   **CAT (Common Admission Test):** Conducted by the IIMs, it's one of the most competitive exams.
*   **MAT (Management Aptitude Test):** A national-level exam accepted by a large number of B-schools.
*   **XAT (Xavier Aptitude Test):** Primarily for XLRI Jamshedpur and other associated institutions.
*   **TISS NET (Tata Institute of Social Sciences National Entrance Test):** For programs at TISS.

Candidates should regularly check the official websites of these examination bodies for specific dates, application procedures, and syllabus details.

## MBA in Operations Management Syllabus

The curriculum for an MBA in Operations Management is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of managing complex operational systems. While the specific subjects may vary slightly between institutions, the core focus remains on strategic planning, execution, and control of operations.

Common subjects often include:

*   Operations Strategy
*   Supply Chain Management
*   Logistics and Inventory Management
*   Quality Management and Lean Systems
*   Project Management
*   Service Operations Management
*   Business Analytics and Decision Making
*   Forecasting and Demand Planning
*   Risk Management in Operations
*   Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

Through case studies, simulations, and practical projects, students learn to apply theoretical concepts to real-world business challenges.

## Career Scope, Job Profiles, and Salary

The demand for skilled operations professionals is consistently high across various industries, including manufacturing, IT, e-commerce, healthcare, and consulting. The increasing complexity of global supply chains and the rise of digital commerce further amplify this need. Graduates can explore diverse career paths, ranging from direct operational roles to strategic management positions.

### Common Job Profiles

*   **Operations Manager:** Oversees daily operations, ensuring efficiency and productivity.
*   **Supply Chain Manager:** Manages the flow of goods and services, from raw materials to final delivery.
*   **Logistics Manager:** Focuses on transportation, warehousing, and distribution.
*   **Process Improvement Consultant:** Analyzes and optimizes business processes for better performance.
*   **Project Manager:** Leads and manages projects from initiation to completion.
*   **Plant Manager:** Manages the overall operations of a manufacturing facility.

### Salary Expectations

Salaries for MBA in Operations Management graduates in India can vary significantly based on factors like the reputation of the institution, individual skills, work experience (for executive programs), and the recruiting company. Entry-level salaries might begin around INR 4 LPA, while experienced professionals or graduates from top-tier institutions can command packages up to INR 30 LPA or more. Many graduates also find opportunities in Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs), Indian Railways, and government procurement departments in logistics and supply chain roles.

An MBA in Operations Management offers a robust foundation for a rewarding career, equipping you with the skills to drive efficiency and innovation in any organization.
