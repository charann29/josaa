# CSIR NET Exam 2026: June Notification (Soon), Application Process, Exam Date, Eligibility, Syllabus & Pattern

The CSIR NET exam is a crucial national-level test for science and technology graduates aspiring to careers in research or academia. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), it opens doors to Junior Research Fellowships (JRF) and Assistant Professorships/Lectureships across Indian universities and colleges.

## Key takeaways

*   The official notification for the June 2026 session is expected from NTA in March 2026.
*   Applications will be submitted online on the official CSIR NET portal once the notification is released.
*   The exam determines eligibility for Junior Research Fellowships and Lectureships/Assistant Professorships in Science & Technology fields.
*   It's a computer-based test (CBT) with multiple-choice questions (MCQs) across five science disciplines.

## Application Process and Notification

The NTA is anticipated to release the detailed notification for the June 2026 session of CSIR NET in March 2026. This notification, available on the official CSIR NET portal (csirnet.nta.nic.in), will contain all essential information, including the application timeline, eligibility criteria, exam pattern, and syllabus. The application window will open concurrently with the notification's release, and candidates must complete their online submissions before the specified deadline. It is highly recommended to review the eligibility requirements carefully before applying.

## Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the CSIR NET exam, candidates must meet specific academic qualifications. While detailed criteria will be in the official notification, generally, a Master's degree (M.Sc./equivalent degree, Integrated BS-MS, BS-4 years, BE/B.Tech, B.Pharma, MBBS) with a minimum percentage of marks (typically 55% for General/EWS/OBC and 50% for SC/ST/PwD candidates) is required. There are also age limits for the Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) component, though no upper age limit applies for Lectureship/Assistant Professor.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The CSIR NET is a Computer-Based Test (CBT) conducted online. It features multiple-choice questions (MCQs) and is split into two shifts. The exam duration is 180 minutes (3 hours). It covers five main science subjects:

*   Chemical Sciences
*   Earth, Atmospheric, Ocean and Planetary Sciences
*   Life Sciences
*   Mathematical Sciences
*   Physical Sciences

Each subject paper is generally divided into three parts: Part A (General Aptitude), Part B (Subject-specific MCQs), and Part C (Higher-order reasoning and analytical questions from the subject). The marking scheme typically includes +2 marks for each correct answer and a deduction of 0.25 marks for each incorrect answer. The detailed syllabus for each subject will be available in the official notification.
