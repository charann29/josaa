# M.Com: Full Form, Duration, Admission 2026, Fees, Subjects, Salary & Career Scope

For students aiming to deepen their understanding of commerce and business, the Master of Commerce (M.Com) is a postgraduate degree designed to provide specialized knowledge across various financial and management domains. This program is a stepping stone for many aspiring professionals in the corporate world.

## Key takeaways

*   M.Com stands for Master of Commerce, a two-year postgraduate degree.
*   It offers advanced learning in finance, accounting, economics, and business management.
*   Admission typically requires a Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com) or equivalent degree.
*   Graduates can pursue diverse roles like Financial Analyst, Auditor, or even become academics.

## What is M.Com?

The Master of Commerce is a two-year postgraduate program that delves into the intricacies of commerce, accounting, finance, economics, and business management. The curriculum is usually structured across four semesters, providing a comprehensive overview and specialized insights into these fields. It's an excellent choice for those looking to build a strong foundation for professional qualifications like CA, CS, or CMA, or to advance directly into various corporate roles.

## Eligibility and Admission Process for M.Com 2026

To be eligible for an M.Com program, candidates generally need to hold a bachelor's degree such as a B.Com or BBA, or an equivalent qualification from a recognized university. The minimum percentage required can vary by institution.

Admission to M.Com programs can be based on either merit (your undergraduate academic performance) or entrance examinations. Several prominent entrance tests are accepted by various universities across India. For instance, for the 2026 academic year, the CUET-PG examination, a widely accepted national-level test for postgraduate admissions, is expected to be conducted between March 6 and March 27, 2026, as per NTA announcements. Other common entrance exams include CPGET, AP PGCET, and MAH CET, depending on the state and university.

## M.Com Colleges and Fees

India boasts a significant number of institutions offering M.Com programs, with over 4,000 colleges providing this degree. These include highly regarded institutions like Delhi University, Christ University, Narsee Monjee College of Commerce & Economics, Jain University, Mithibai College, St. Joseph's College of Commerce, and Madras Christian College, among many others.

The fee structure for an M.Com course can vary substantially based on the institution, its location, and whether it's a government-funded or private college. On average, the fees can range from as low as INR 1,000 to as high as INR 12.4 Lakh for the entire two-year program.

## Career Scope and Salary after M.Com

An M.Com degree opens doors to a wide array of career opportunities across various sectors. Graduates are well-equipped for roles in finance, accounting, banking, taxation, and corporate management. Some popular career paths include:

*   **Financial Analyst:** Analyzing financial data to guide investment decisions.
*   **Tax Consultant:** Advising individuals and organizations on tax planning and compliance.
*   **Auditor:** Examining financial records to ensure accuracy and compliance with regulations.
*   **Professor/Lecturer:** Teaching commerce and related subjects at colleges and universities.
*   **Banking Professional:** Working in various roles within the banking sector, from retail to corporate banking.
*   **Corporate Sector Roles:** Positions in management, finance departments, or business development within private companies.

The specific salary can depend on factors like the industry, company size, individual skills, and geographical location. With an M.Com, you gain specialized knowledge that is highly valued in today's competitive job market.

An M.Com degree is a robust qualification for anyone looking to build a strong career in the dynamic world of commerce and finance.
