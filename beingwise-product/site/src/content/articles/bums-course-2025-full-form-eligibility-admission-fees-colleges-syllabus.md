# BUMS Course 2025: Full Form, Eligibility, Admission, Fees, Colleges, Syllabus, Scope

The Bachelor of Unani Medicine and Surgery (BUMS) is an undergraduate medical degree program that trains students in the ancient Unani system of medicine. This holistic approach combines traditional healing practices with modern scientific understanding to prepare future healthcare professionals.

## Key Takeaways

*   BUMS is a 5.5-year undergraduate course, including a mandatory one-year internship.
*   Eligibility typically requires a 10+2 qualification with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
*   Admission to most BUMS programs is primarily through the NEET UG entrance examination.
*   The curriculum covers Unani principles, modern medical subjects, and practical clinical training.

## Understanding the BUMS Degree

BUMS, which stands for Bachelor of Unani Medicine and Surgery, is a comprehensive program for those aspiring to practice Unani medicine. This system focuses on natural remedies, herbal formulations, and lifestyle adjustments to prevent and treat illnesses. The course structure is designed to give students a deep understanding of traditional Unani concepts while also integrating relevant aspects of modern medical science.

### Course Duration and Structure

The BUMS program spans five and a half years. This includes four and a half years of academic study and examinations, followed by a compulsory one-year rotating internship. The internship provides essential practical experience in various clinical settings, allowing students to apply their knowledge under supervision.

## Eligibility Criteria for BUMS Admission

To be considered for a BUMS program, candidates must meet specific academic requirements. Generally, these include:

*   **Educational Qualification:** Completion of 10+2 (or equivalent) from a recognized board.
*   **Subjects:** Mandatory subjects in 10+2 must be Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A minimum aggregate score, typically around 50% for general category candidates, is usually required in the science subjects. Reserved categories may have some relaxation in this percentage.
*   **Age Limit:** Candidates usually need to be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year.

## BUMS Admission Process

Admission to BUMS colleges across India largely depends on performance in a national-level entrance examination. The primary examination for BUMS admissions is:

*   **NEET UG (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test - Undergraduate):** Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), NEET UG scores are widely accepted for admission to BUMS programs in both government and private institutions. Candidates must qualify for NEET UG to be eligible for counseling.

After the NEET UG results are declared, counseling processes are conducted by various authorities. For all-India quota seats, the counseling is managed by the Ayush Admissions Central Counseling Committee (AACCC). State-level counseling bodies handle admissions for state quota seats based on NEET UG scores.

## BUMS Course Fees and Top Institutions

The cost of pursuing a BUMS degree can vary significantly depending on the type of institution (government or private) and its location. Generally, fees can range from approximately INR 70,000 to INR 12.5 lakhs for the entire program.

Some prominent institutions offering BUMS courses include:

*   Aligarh Muslim University (AMU), Aligarh
*   Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan Rajasthan Ayurved University (DSRRAU), Jodhpur
*   Ayush and Health Sciences University, Chhattisgarh
*   Baba Farid University of Health Sciences, Faridkot

Prospective students are advised to check the official websites of individual colleges for precise fee structures and admission details.

## BUMS Syllabus Overview

The BUMS curriculum is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of Unani medicine, blending theoretical knowledge with practical skills. The subjects covered across the years typically include:

### Pre-Clinical Subjects

*   Kulliyat Umoor Tabiya (Basic Principles of Unani Medicine)
*   Tashreeh-ul-Badan (Anatomy)
*   Munafe-ul-Aza (Physiology)
*   Tarikh-e-Tibb (History of Medicine)
*   Mantiq, Falsafa wa Haiyat (Logic, Philosophy and Astronomy)

### Para-Clinical Subjects

*   Ilmul Advia (Pharmacology and Materia Medica)
*   Mahiyat-ul-Amraz (Pathology)
*   Ilmul Saidla (Pharmacy)
*   Tibbe Qanooni wa Samaji Tibb (Forensic Medicine and Toxicology & Community Medicine)

### Clinical Subjects

*   Moalijat (Medicine)
*   Jarhat (Surgery)
*   Ilaj Bit Tadbeer (Regimenal Therapy)
*   Amraze Niswan wa Qabalat (Gynaecology and Obstetrics)
*   Amraze Atfal (Paediatrics)
*   Amraze Ain, Uzn, Anaf wa Halaq (Ophthalmology, ENT)

## Career Scope After BUMS

A BUMS degree opens up various career paths in the healthcare sector, focusing on the Unani system. Graduates can pursue roles in clinical practice, research, education, and public health.

### Common Career Opportunities

*   **Unani Physician/Consultant:** Establishing private practice or working in government/private Unani hospitals and clinics.
*   **Researcher:** Engaging in studies on Unani formulations, clinical trials, and developing new treatment protocols.
*   **Lecturer/Professor:** Teaching in Unani medical colleges.
*   **Medical Officer:** Working in government health departments or dispensaries that integrate Unani medicine.
*   **Pharmacist (Unani):** Specializing in the preparation and dispensing of Unani medicines.
*   **Public Health Specialist:** Contributing to health programs focused on traditional medicine.

Graduates can find opportunities in various settings, including Unani hospitals, research institutes, wellness centers, and educational institutions. The average starting salary for a BUMS graduate can range from approximately INR 3 LPA to INR 12 LPA, depending on experience, specialization, and the type of employer.

The BUMS course offers a unique and fulfilling career for those passionate about traditional healing systems and dedicated to patient care through natural and holistic methods.
