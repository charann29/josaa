# Advanced Diploma in Brain and Behaviour by Alison via NPTEL: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Exploring the intricacies of the human brain and its impact on behaviour is a fascinating journey. This Advanced Diploma, offered through Alison, provides a comprehensive introduction to cognitive neuroscience, making it accessible to a wide audience.

## Key takeaways

*   This diploma is offered completely free of charge, with an optional certificate available from Alison.
*   The course is delivered through a blend of video lectures and textual materials, making it flexible for learners.
*   It covers fundamental aspects of brain function, from basic neuronal communication to complex topics like emotion and language.
*   The program is self-paced, allowing students to learn at their own convenience.

## Course Overview

The Advanced Diploma in Brain and Behaviour delves into the core principles of cognitive neuroscience. It aims to equip learners with a foundational understanding of how the brain influences our thoughts, feelings, and actions. The curriculum is structured to progressively build knowledge, starting with the basic building blocks of the nervous system and moving towards more advanced concepts like perception, attention, and social cognition.

## What You Will Learn

The diploma is divided into several modules, each focusing on a specific area of brain and behaviour studies. Here's a glimpse into the topics covered:

*   **Cognitive Neuroscience Fundamentals:** You'll start by understanding the history, philosophy, and basic cellular structure of the nervous system, including how neurons communicate.
*   **The Human Brain:** This section explores the anatomy of the brain, particularly the cerebral cortex, and introduces various methods used in cognitive neuroscience research, including studying brain damage and structural analysis techniques.
*   **Sensation and Perception:** Learn about how our brains process sensory information and create our perception of the world, covering aspects like somatosensory and visual perception.
*   **Attention Systems:** This module examines the mechanisms and neural networks involved in attention, exploring different models and their anatomical bases.
*   **Memory Systems:** Delve into the fascinating world of memory, understanding its mechanisms, the role of the medial temporal lobe, and how long-term memories are formed and consolidated.
*   **Neuroscience of Emotions:** Explore the biological underpinnings of emotions, how they are categorized, and their influence on cognitive processes like attention and perception.
*   **Neuroscience of Language:** Understand how the brain processes language, from comprehension to word processing.
*   **Neuroscience of Social Cognition:** This module covers how the brain handles social interactions, including topics like the theory of mind, mental state attribution, and autism.

Regular assessments are integrated throughout the course to help you check your understanding and reinforce learning.
