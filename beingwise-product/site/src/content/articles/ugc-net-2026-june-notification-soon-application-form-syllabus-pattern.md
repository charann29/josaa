# UGC NET 2026: June Notification (Soon), Application Form, Syllabus, Pattern & Eligibility Criteria

The University Grants Commission National Eligibility Test (UGC NET) is a crucial national-level examination for individuals aspiring to teach at Indian universities and colleges or pursue research. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), it assesses candidates' eligibility for Assistant Professor roles or both Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and Assistant Professor positions.

## Key Takeaways

*   The NTA is expected to release the UGC NET June 2026 notification and application form in April 2026.
*   The exam will be conducted in a Computer-Based Test (CBT) format across 85 different subjects.
*   Qualifying candidates can become Assistant Professors or pursue Junior Research Fellowships with a stipend.
*   The official information bulletin will contain all essential details regarding dates, syllabus, and eligibility criteria.

## Understanding UGC NET

UGC NET serves as a gateway for careers in academia and research within India. Successfully clearing this exam can open doors to two distinct career paths:

*   **Junior Research Fellowship (JRF):** Candidates who qualify for JRF are eligible to apply for both Assistant Professor positions and research scholar roles. As research scholars, they receive a stipend – currently ₹37,000 per month for the initial two years, increasing to ₹42,000 per month from the third year onwards – to support their doctoral research. This fellowship allows them to pursue a PhD while receiving financial assistance.
*   **Assistant Professor:** Those who qualify solely for Assistant Professor eligibility can apply for teaching positions in various universities and colleges across India. While they can also pursue a PhD, they will not receive a government stipend for their doctoral studies.

Until June 2018, the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) managed the NET exam on behalf of the UGC. However, the NTA now holds the responsibility for conducting this prestigious examination.

## UGC NET 2026 Notification and Application Process

The NTA is anticipated to release the official notification for the UGC NET June 2026 session in April 2026. This notification, typically in PDF format, will be available on the official UGC NET website. It functions as a comprehensive information bulletin, detailing every aspect of the examination, including important dates, the application procedure, syllabus, exam pattern, and eligibility requirements. The online application form link will be activated concurrently with the notification's release, allowing candidates a specific timeframe to submit their applications.

## Eligibility Criteria for UGC NET 2026

To be eligible for the UGC NET 2026, candidates must meet specific academic qualifications and age limits. While the exact details will be outlined in the official notification, generally, candidates need a Master's degree or equivalent with a minimum percentage of marks (usually 55% for General category, 50% for reserved categories) from a UGC-recognized university. There are also age limits, particularly for the JRF component, which typically have an upper age limit, while there is no upper age limit for Assistant Professor eligibility. It's crucial to refer to the official NTA notification for the precise and most current eligibility conditions.

## UGC NET 2026 Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The UGC NET exam consists of two papers, both conducted in a single session without a break. The exam is a Computer Based Test (CBT) with multiple-choice questions (MCQs).

*   **Paper 1:** This paper is common for all candidates and assesses teaching/research aptitude, reasoning ability, comprehension, divergent thinking, and general awareness. It comprises 50 questions, each carrying 2 marks, totaling 100 marks.
*   **Paper 2:** This paper is subject-specific, chosen by the candidate from a list of 85 subjects. It tests in-depth knowledge of the chosen subject. It consists of 100 questions, each carrying 2 marks, totaling 200 marks.

There is no negative marking for incorrect answers. The syllabus for Paper 2 varies significantly across the 85 subjects and is prescribed by the University Grants Commission itself. Candidates are strongly advised to download the detailed syllabus for their chosen subject from the official NTA website.
