# Midjourney AI Image Generator Pauses its Free Trials

Midjourney, a popular AI image generation tool, has temporarily halted its free trial service. This decision comes amidst a surge in demand and a notable increase in the creation of misleading content, often referred to as "deepfakes," using the platform.

## Key takeaways

*   Midjourney has paused free trials due to high demand and the misuse of the service for creating deepfakes.
*   Deepfakes are AI-generated images, videos, or audio that depict real people doing or saying things they never did, potentially harming reputations.
*   The company has observed an increase in the creation of fake images of public figures and has updated its content moderation policies.
*   While the free trial is paused, users can still access Midjourney through its paid subscription plans.

## Why the Pause? Unprecedented Demand and Misuse

The primary reasons cited for disabling free trials are an overwhelming demand for the service and instances of users exploiting the free trial offer. Midjourney founder David Holz shared on the company's official Discord server that the pause is a temporary measure until system improvements can be rolled out. This suggests a need to better manage user traffic and content generation.

## The Rise of Deepfakes and Their Impact

A significant factor contributing to the decision is the proliferation of high-profile deepfakes created using Midjourney's AI. Deepfakes leverage artificial intelligence to alter images, videos, and audio, making it appear as though real individuals have said or done things they haven't. While sometimes used for entertainment, deepfakes can also spread misinformation, damage reputations, and influence public opinion.

Recent examples that have circulated widely include AI-generated images of Pope Francis in unusual attire or former President Donald Trump in various arrest scenarios. These images, though entirely fabricated, gained significant traction on social media platforms, highlighting the potential for misuse.

## Content Moderation and Banned Terms

In response to these incidents, Midjourney has been actively expanding its list of banned words and phrases to curb the creation of problematic content. For instance, the term "arrested" was recently added to the banned list after fake images depicting a public figure's arrest went viral. Despite these efforts, it's been noted that users can often find ways to circumvent such restrictions by using alternative phrasing. For example, while directly prompting for "Donald Trump being arrested" might be blocked, a similar outcome could be achieved with prompts like "Donald Trump in handcuffs surrounded by police."

## Subscription Plans and the Future

Currently, Midjourney offers various subscription plans ranging from approximately $10 to $60 per month, depending on the user's processing needs and desired features. Annual subscription options are also available. The company's founder has indicated that many of the more problematic political images were created using Midjourney v5, which was already exclusive to paying users, with free trials being limited to the older v4 version. While the closure of the free tier aims to protect the company's resources and encourage paid subscriptions, the broader challenge of regulating AI-generated misleading content remains a complex and ongoing debate within the generative AI community.

Ultimately, the pause in Midjourney's free trials reflects a growing awareness of the ethical considerations and potential for misuse inherent in powerful AI image generation tools. It underscores the ongoing need for platforms to balance accessibility with responsible content creation and moderation.
