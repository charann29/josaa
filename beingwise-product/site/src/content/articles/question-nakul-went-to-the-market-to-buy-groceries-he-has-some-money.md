# Question: Nakul went to the market to buy groceries. He has some money with him, 20% of which he used to buy wheat flour and 25% of the remaining money is spent on cooking oils. If he is left with Rs. 900, then find out the total amount of money that Nakul had.

This problem is a classic example of percentage calculations often encountered in various competitive exams and everyday financial planning. Understanding how to work with percentages, especially when they apply to remaining amounts, is a valuable skill for students and adults alike.

## Key takeaways

*   Always clarify if a percentage is taken from the initial amount or a remaining balance.
*   Break down multi-step percentage problems into smaller, manageable steps.
*   Working backward from a known remaining amount can be an effective strategy.
*   Such questions test basic arithmetic and logical reasoning, crucial for many entrance exams.

## Understanding the Problem Statement

Nakul starts with an unknown amount of money. He makes two purchases: first, wheat flour, and then cooking oils. The key detail here is that the second purchase (cooking oils) is a percentage of the *remaining* money, not the original total. After these two expenses, he has Rs. 900 left. Our goal is to determine his initial sum.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Let's denote the total amount of money Nakul had initially as 'X'.

### Step 1: Expense on Wheat Flour

Nakul spends 20% of his total money on wheat flour. This means the amount spent is 0.20 * X.

The money remaining after buying wheat flour would be X - 0.20X = 0.80X.

### Step 2: Expense on Cooking Oils

Next, Nakul spends 25% of the *remaining* money on cooking oils. The remaining money, as calculated above, is 0.80X.

So, the amount spent on cooking oils is 25% of 0.80X, which is 0.25 * 0.80X = 0.20X.

### Step 3: Money Left

After both expenses, the money left with Nakul is Rs. 900.

We can find the money left in terms of X in two ways:

*   **Method A (Subtracting expenses):** Original money - (Wheat flour expense + Cooking oils expense)
    X - (0.20X + 0.20X) = X - 0.40X = 0.60X

*   **Method B (Calculating remaining percentages):**
    After spending 20% on wheat flour, 80% of the money remains (0.80X).
    From this remaining 80%, he spends 25% on oils. This means 75% of *this remaining amount* is left.
    So, the money left is 75% of (80% of X) = 0.75 * (0.80X) = 0.60X.

Both methods lead to the same conclusion: 60% of the original money is left.

### Step 4: Finding the Total Amount

We know that the money left is 0.60X, and this amount is equal to Rs. 900.

So, we set up the equation:

0.60X = 900

To find X, we divide 900 by 0.60:

X = 900 / 0.60
X = 900 / (60/100)
X = (900 * 100) / 60
X = 90000 / 60
X = 1500

Therefore, the total amount of money Nakul had initially was Rs. 1,500.

## Importance of Percentage Problems

Percentage-based problems are fundamental in quantitative aptitude sections of many entrance examinations, including those for management, banking, and various government jobs. They evaluate a candidate's ability to apply basic mathematical concepts to practical scenarios, which is a crucial skill in higher education and professional life. Mastering these concepts can significantly boost scores in exams that rely on logical reasoning and numerical ability.

Let's quickly check the given options:

*   **Option 1: Rs. 1,200** - If Nakul started with Rs. 1,200, 20% for flour is Rs. 240. Remaining: Rs. 960. 25% of Rs. 960 for oil is Rs. 240. Left: Rs. 960 - Rs. 240 = Rs. 720. (Incorrect)
*   **Option 2: Rs. 1,500** - If Nakul started with Rs. 1,500, 20% for flour is Rs. 300. Remaining: Rs. 1,200. 25% of Rs. 1,200 for oil is Rs. 300. Left: Rs. 1,200 - Rs. 300 = Rs. 900. (Correct)
*   **Option 3: Rs. 1,350** - If Nakul started with Rs. 1,350, 20% for flour is Rs. 270. Remaining: Rs. 1,080. 25% of Rs. 1,080 for oil is Rs. 270. Left: Rs. 1,080 - Rs. 270 = Rs. 810. (Incorrect)
*   **Option 4: Rs. 1,800** - If Nakul started with Rs. 1,800, 20% for flour is Rs. 360. Remaining: Rs. 1,440. 25% of Rs. 1,440 for oil is Rs. 360. Left: Rs. 1,440 - Rs. 360 = Rs. 1,080. (Incorrect)

This verification confirms our calculated answer of Rs. 1,500.
