# World Class Maths: Asian Teaching Methods by Macmillan Publishers India Private Limited via Futurelearn: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This program explores the highly effective Asian teaching methods for mathematics, a pedagogy that has gained global recognition for its success in making primary math engaging and accessible. It's an excellent opportunity for anyone in the education sector to understand and potentially implement these innovative approaches.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn about successful mathematics teaching principles from Shanghai and Singapore.
*   Understand the importance of professional development for math educators in the Asian context.
*   Explore techniques like Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract (CPA) and the Model Method.
*   Evaluate your country's math education performance against Asian systems.

## Course Overview

Around the world, different educational approaches are used, but the Asian method for teaching mathematics, especially at the primary level, stands out for its effectiveness and ability to engage students. This certification program is designed for anyone involved in education – from teachers and administrators to policymakers and even students interested in mathematical pedagogy.

The course is developed in collaboration with the University of Southampton and Macmillan Education, both prominent names in the educational field. It aims to introduce learners to the core features and principles that underpin the success of Asian math education, encouraging critical thinking about current teaching practices.

## What You Will Learn

The program offers deep insights into various aspects of Asian math teaching. You will gain a comprehensive understanding of:

*   The foundational principles of Asian mathematics teaching for primary education.
*   Specific methods from Singapore, such as the Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract (CPA) approach and the Model Method.
*   Established principles from Shanghai, including the 'two basics' and 'teaching with variation'.
*   The critical role of ongoing professional development for primary math teachers in Asian education systems.
*   How to evaluate and compare national math education performance with successful Asian models.
*   The reasons behind the international success of Asian educational systems in primary mathematics.

## Course Structure and Delivery

This entirely online program is delivered through video-based content, allowing for self-paced study. It is designed to be completed over four weeks, requiring approximately three hours of study per week. The course content is provided by the University of Southampton, with the overall program developed by Macmillan Education. Expert faculty from Southampton and Shanghai contribute to the learning experience.

## Eligibility and Certification

While the basic course material is free to access, obtaining an official certificate requires purchasing an upgraded or unlimited learning plan. To qualify for the certificate, participants must complete at least 90% of the course steps and achieve an average score of at least 70% in all available course tests. The certificate is issued by Futurelearn.

## Who Is This Course For?

This program is ideally suited for a wide range of professionals within the education sector, including:

*   Primary school teachers looking to enhance their mathematics teaching methods.
*   School administrators responsible for ensuring high-quality education.
*   Education policymakers interested in improving national mathematics curricula.
*   Individuals passionate about mathematics education and pedagogical innovation.

By exploring these world-class teaching methods, participants can gain valuable skills to transform primary mathematics education.
