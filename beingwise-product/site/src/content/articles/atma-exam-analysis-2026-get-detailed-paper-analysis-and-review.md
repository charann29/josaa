# ATMA Exam Analysis 2026: Get Detailed Paper Analysis & Review

Understanding the ATMA exam analysis helps prospective students prepare effectively by knowing the paper's structure, difficulty, and key topics. This analysis for the February 2026 session offers insights into what to expect and how to strategize for future attempts.

## Key Takeaways

*   The ATMA February 2026 examination was conducted on February 22, 2026, as a three-hour, center-based online test from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM.
*   The overall difficulty level of the paper was assessed as moderate, maintaining consistency with prior years.
*   The Quantitative Skills sections were generally considered the most challenging and time-consuming parts of the exam.
*   Each correct answer was awarded one mark, with a deduction of 0.25 marks for every incorrect response.

## ATMA 2026 Exam Pattern and Marking Scheme

The ATMA question paper for 2026 is meticulously structured into six distinct sections, ensuring comprehensive evaluation of a candidate's abilities. These include two sections each for Analytical Reasoning Skills, Quantitative Skills, and Verbal Skills. It's crucial for candidates to be aware of the sectional timings, as adherence to these limits is mandatory during the exam.

The following table details the distribution of questions and the time allocated for each section:

| Sections                           | Number of Questions | Time Allotted |
| :--------------------------------- | :------------------ | :------------ |
| Analytical Reasoning Skills Part 1 | 30                  | 30 minutes    |
| Analytical Reasoning Skills Part 2 | 30                  | 30 minutes    |
| Quantitative Skills Part 1         | 30                  | 30 minutes    |
| Quantitative Skills Part 2         | 30                  | 30 minutes    |
| Verbal Skills Part 1               | 30                  | 30 minutes    |
| Verbal Skills Part 2               | 30                  | 30 minutes    |
| **Total**                          | **180**             | **180 minutes** |

## ATMA 2026 February Session Analysis

The February 2026 ATMA exam was largely perceived as having a moderate difficulty level, aligning with the pattern observed in previous years. Among all sections, the Quantitative Skills parts were frequently cited by test-takers as the most difficult, often described as both tricky and lengthy. The Verbal Skills sections also posed a challenge due to their length. Conversely, the Analytical Reasoning sections, particularly those focusing on critical reasoning, were considered manageable.

## Insights from Previous ATMA Exams

Looking back at previous ATMA examinations offers valuable context. For instance, the February 2022 Analytical Reasoning section was heavily skewed towards critical reasoning, with approximately 15 to 18 questions specifically from this area. This consistent focus on critical reasoning highlights its importance across different exam iterations.

## Preparing for Future ATMA Sessions

Based on the analysis, future ATMA aspirants should prioritize strengthening their Quantitative Skills, as this section consistently presents the highest difficulty. Regular practice with time-bound questions, especially for lengthy Verbal Skills passages, will also be beneficial. Familiarity with critical reasoning concepts will be crucial for the Analytical Reasoning sections. Consistent preparation and strategic time management during the exam are key to achieving a good score.

This detailed analysis should provide a clear roadmap for your ATMA preparation, helping you approach the exam with confidence.
