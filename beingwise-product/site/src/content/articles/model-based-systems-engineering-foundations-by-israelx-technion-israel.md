# Model Based Systems Engineering: Foundations by IsraelX Technion - Israel Institute of Technology via Edx: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This course from the Technion – Israel Institute of Technology, offered through IsraelX, provides a strong introduction to Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE). It's designed to help learners understand how to apply this systematic approach to design and manage complex systems effectively.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn the fundamentals of Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) and Object-Process Methodology (OPM).
*   Understand how to model various system types, from natural to socio-technical, using diagrams.
*   Gain practical experience through a business case study focused on modeling an artificial system.
*   The course content is available for free, with an optional paid certificate from IsraelX.

## Course Structure and Effort

The entire program is delivered online through video and text-based materials, allowing for self-paced learning. The medium of instruction is English. To get the most out of the course, learners are advised to dedicate approximately 5-6 hours per week to the materials and exercises.

## Detailed Syllabus

The curriculum is structured into several modules, guiding you from foundational concepts to practical application:

### Module 0: Prologue

*   An introductory overview to set the stage for the course.

### Module 1: Introduction to MBSE and OPM

*   Explore OPM Things: Understanding the components of a system.
*   Delve into OPM Transformations: How systems change and interact.
*   Examine System Aspects & OPM Modalities: Different views and behaviors of systems.
*   Includes quizzes and checks to reinforce understanding.

### Module 2: System Diagram

*   Focus on creating the top-level system diagram of an OPM model.
*   Features a quiz to test your grasp of diagramming principles.

### Module 3: System Diagram 1

*   Learn to model diverse systems:
    *   Natural systems.
    *   Social systems.
    *   Socio-technical systems.
*   Concludes with a quiz and a check-your-understanding exercise.

### Module 4: Test Your Understanding - PI/MCQ

*   A series of short tests to consolidate learning from previous modules.

### Module 5: Modeling an Artificial System: A Business Case Study

*   **Case Study Introduction:** Setting up a real-world business scenario.
*   **Top Level of the System – System Diagram (SD):**
    *   Analyzing problem occurrence.
    *   Defining system purpose.
    *   Identifying the system's main function.
    *   Understanding main process enablers.
    *   Mapping environmental things.
*   **First Detail Level of the System – System Diagram 1 (SD1):**
    *   Refining the main process.
    *   Connecting subprocesses with operands, agents, and instruments.
*   **First Detail Level of the System – System Diagram 2 (SD2):**
    *   Refining parts of the main object.
    *   Understanding types of main object refinement.

## Fees and Certification

The core course content is available for free, allowing anyone to access the learning materials. For those who wish to receive formal recognition of their completion, a certificate is available. The certificate is provided by IsraelX, and the fee for obtaining this certificate is ₹15,868.

To enroll in this course, you typically visit the platform offering it (in this case, EdX) and search for the specific course title. You can choose to audit the course for free or opt for the verified track to pursue the certificate by paying the stated fee. Follow the enrollment instructions on the official course page.
