# SBI PO Exam 2026: Notification (SOON), Application Form, Eligibility, Vacancies & FAQs

The State Bank of India (SBI) Probationary Officer (PO) examination is a highly sought-after opportunity for graduates aspiring to build a career in banking. This national-level exam selects candidates for the Probationary Officer role across various SBI branches, offering a stable career path with significant growth potential.

## Key takeaways

*   The official SBI PO 2026 notification is expected to be released soon, likely in August 2026, on the official SBI careers portal.
*   The application process will commence alongside the notification, requiring candidates to meet specific graduation and age criteria.
*   The selection process involves three stages: a preliminary exam, a main exam, and an interview.
*   A strong performance in both the main exam and the interview determines the final merit list for selection.

## What is the SBI PO Exam?

The SBI PO exam is an annual recruitment drive conducted by the State Bank of India to induct Probationary Officers. These officers undergo a training period and are then posted in different branches, handling various banking operations. Given SBI's reputation, attractive salary, and job security, this exam draws approximately 10 lakh applicants each year, making it one of the most competitive banking exams in India.

### Why consider an SBI PO career?

*   **Prestigious Role:** Being a Probationary Officer at SBI is a highly respected position in the banking sector.
*   **Attractive Remuneration:** The role offers a competitive salary package.
*   **Career Advancement:** SBI provides extensive opportunities for professional growth and development within the organization.

## Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the SBI PO exam, candidates typically need to fulfill the following requirements:

*   **Educational Qualification:** A bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university or any equivalent qualification recognized by the Central Government. Final year/semester students are also eligible to apply provisionally, provided they produce proof of passing the graduation examination on or before a specified date.
*   **Age Limit:** Candidates must be between 21 and 30 years of age as of a specific cut-off date. Age relaxations are applicable for reserved categories as per government norms.

## Application Process

Once the notification is released, the application form will be available on the official SBI careers website. The process generally involves:

1.  **Registration:** New users must register with basic details to create a login.
2.  **Filling the Application Form:** Provide personal, educational, and contact details accurately.
3.  **Uploading Documents:** Scan and upload photographs, signatures, and other required documents in the specified format and size.
4.  **Fee Payment:** Pay the application fee online. General/EWS/OBC candidates typically pay INR 750, while SC/ST/PWD candidates are exempt from fees.
5.  **Submission:** Review all entered details carefully before final submission.

It is crucial for all applicants to meticulously read the official SBI PO notification PDF before applying to ensure they meet all eligibility conditions and understand the application procedure fully.

## Selection Process

The SBI PO selection process is a rigorous multi-stage procedure designed to identify the most suitable candidates:

1.  **Preliminary Examination:** This is an objective-type online test designed to screen candidates. It typically includes sections on English Language, Quantitative Aptitude, and Reasoning Ability. Marks from this stage are not counted towards the final merit list.
2.  **Main Examination:** Candidates who clear the preliminary exam proceed to the main exam. This is also an online test, comprising objective tests (Reasoning & Computer Aptitude, Data Analysis & Interpretation, General Economy/Banking/Awareness, English Language) and a descriptive test (English Language: Letter Writing & Essay).
3.  **Interview/Group Exercises:** Shortlisted candidates from the main exam are called for an interview and/or group exercises. The final merit list is prepared based on the combined scores of the Main Examination (objective and descriptive parts) and the Interview/Group Exercises.

Prepare thoroughly and stay updated with the official announcements for a successful application.
