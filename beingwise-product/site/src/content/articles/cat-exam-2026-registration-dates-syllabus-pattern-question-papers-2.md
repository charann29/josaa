# CAT Exam 2026: Registration, Dates, Syllabus, Pattern, Question Papers

The Common Admission Test (CAT) is a crucial national-level entrance exam for admission to MBA and postgraduate management programs across India. This guide provides essential information for aspiring students planning to appear for CAT 2026.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Exam Date:** CAT 2026 is scheduled for November 29, 2026, conducted in three shifts.
*   **Registration:** The application process is expected to begin in the first week of August 2026 and conclude by the third week of September 2026.
*   **Conducting Body:** IIM Indore is anticipated to be the conducting institute for CAT 2026.
*   **Eligibility:** Candidates need a bachelor's degree with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved categories).

## Understanding the CAT Exam

CAT is an annual computer-based test conducted by one of the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) on a rotational basis. It assesses candidates' skills in Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), and Quantitative Ability (QA). A strong CAT score is essential for admission to the prestigious IIMs and over 1,300 other management institutes across India, including top B-schools like FMS, MDI, and SPJIMR.

## Eligibility Criteria for CAT 2026

To be eligible for CAT 2026, candidates must meet the following requirements:

*   **Educational Qualification:** Hold a bachelor's degree with at least 50% aggregate marks or an equivalent CGPA. For candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disabilities (PwD) categories, the minimum requirement is 45% aggregate marks.
*   **Final Year Students:** Candidates in their final year of a bachelor's degree program are also eligible to apply, provided they complete their degree with the required percentage within the stipulated time. Such candidates must submit a certificate from their college/university stating they are registered for the final year and will complete all requirements for the degree.
*   **No Age Limit:** There is no age restriction to appear for the CAT exam.

## CAT 2026 Registration Process and Fees

The application form for CAT 2026 will be available on the official iimcat.ac.in website. The registration process typically involves several steps:

1.  **New User Registration:** Create an account on the official CAT portal.
2.  **Fill Application Form:** Complete personal, academic, and contact details.
3.  **Upload Documents:** Upload scanned copies of photographs and signatures as per specifications.
4.  **Select IIM Programs:** Choose your preferred IIM programs and interview cities.
5.  **Pay Application Fee:** Submit the application fee online. Based on past trends, a slight increase in the fee amount is expected for CAT 2026.

**Anticipated Application Fees:**

*   **General/EWS/NC-OBC Categories:** INR 2,600
*   **SC/ST/PwD Categories:** INR 1,300

## CAT 2026 Exam Pattern and Syllabus

CAT is a Computer Based Test (CBT) consisting of both Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and Non-MCQs (Type-In-The-Answer or TITA questions). The exam duration is 120 minutes (2 hours), with 40 minutes allotted to each section. There is a sectional time limit, meaning you cannot switch between sections once the time for a particular section is over.

### Exam Pattern Highlights:

| Section                                     | Number of Questions (Approx.) | Time Allotted | Marking Scheme (Approx.) |
| :------------------------------------------ | :---------------------------- | :------------ | :----------------------- |
| Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC) | 24                            | 40 minutes    | +3 for correct, -1 for incorrect (MCQ) |
| Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR) | 20                            | 40 minutes    | +3 for correct, -1 for incorrect (MCQ) |
| Quantitative Ability (QA)                   | 22                            | 40 minutes    | +3 for correct, -1 for incorrect (MCQ) |

### Syllabus Overview:

*   **Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC):** This section includes Reading Comprehension passages, Para Jumbles, Para Summary, and Odd One Out. Focus on improving reading speed, comprehension, and vocabulary.
*   **Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR):** This section tests your ability to analyze data presented in various formats (tables, charts, graphs) and solve logical puzzles. Key topics include arrangements, blood relations, sets, Venn diagrams, and data sufficiency.
*   **Quantitative Ability (QA):** This section covers topics from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and modern math. A strong foundation in high school mathematics is crucial.

Preparing for CAT requires dedicated effort and a strategic approach. It is advisable to practice with previous year's question papers and take mock tests to familiarize yourself with the exam environment and question types.
