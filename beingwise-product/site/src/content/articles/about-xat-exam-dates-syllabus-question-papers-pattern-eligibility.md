The Xavier Aptitude Test, or XAT, is a national-level entrance examination for admission to MBA and PGDM programs at numerous business schools across India. Conducted annually by XLRI Jamshedpur, XAT is a gateway to over 250 institutions, including XLRI itself and 10 other XAMI member colleges.

## Key takeaways

*   XAT is held once a year, typically on the first Sunday of January.
*   The exam consists of 95 multiple-choice questions covering Decision Making, Verbal & Logical Ability, Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation, and General Knowledge.
*   XLRI's PGDM-BM program usually sees cutoffs around the 95th percentile for male candidates and 92nd percentile for female candidates.
*   Preparation for XAT often requires 6-9 months of dedicated study, including extensive practice with mock tests.

## XAT Exam Dates and Schedule

The XAT examination is consistently scheduled for the first Sunday of January each year. For XAT 2027, the exam date is January 3, 2027. ## XAT Exam Pattern

The XAT is an online, computer-based test with a duration of three hours. It comprises 95 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) across four main sections. There are no subjective questions or essay writing components in the XAT.

## XAT Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the XAT examination, candidates must hold a bachelor's degree of at least three years' duration in any discipline from a recognized university. Those in their final year of a bachelor's degree program are also eligible to apply, provided they complete their degree requirements by a specific date, usually by June of the admission year. There is no age limit for appearing in the XAT exam.

## XAT Syllabus

The XAT syllabus is comprehensive and designed to assess various aptitudes crucial for management studies. It is divided into four main sections:

*   **Verbal & Logical Ability:** This section evaluates reading comprehension, vocabulary, grammar, critical reasoning, and logical deduction skills.
*   **Decision Making:** Unique to XAT, this section presents scenarios requiring candidates to make ethical and logical choices, often involving business or managerial dilemmas.
*   **Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation:** This part tests mathematical skills, including arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and modern math, along with the ability to interpret data presented in tables, charts, and graphs.
*   **General Knowledge:** This section covers current affairs, business and economy news, static GK (history, geography, science), and awards/personalities. Scores from this section are typically not used for the initial shortlisting but may be considered during the final selection process.

Candidates can find the detailed XAT syllabus PDF on the official XAT website, xatonline.in, once the notification is released.

## XAT Cutoffs for XLRI

XLRI Jamshedpur, being one of the most prestigious institutions accepting XAT scores, has competitive cutoffs. Historically, the cutoffs for XLRI's Post Graduate Diploma in Management - Business Management (PGDM-BM) program have been around the 95th percentile for male candidates and the 92nd percentile for female candidates. For other programs and XAMI member institutes, the cutoffs may vary. These cutoffs are influenced by factors such as the difficulty level of the exam, the number of applicants, and the seats available.

For more information and updates, always refer to the official XAT portal.
