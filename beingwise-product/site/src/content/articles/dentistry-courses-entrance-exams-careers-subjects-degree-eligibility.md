# Dentistry - Courses, Entrance Exams, Careers, Subjects, Degree, Eligibility

Dentistry is a vital branch of medical science dedicated to the health of the teeth, gums, jaw, and entire oral cavity. It encompasses the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of various oral conditions, playing a crucial role in maintaining overall well-being.

## Key takeaways

*   Dentistry offers a respected career path focused on oral health, combining theoretical knowledge with extensive clinical practice.
*   Undergraduate courses like BDS typically require NEET qualification, while postgraduate programs like MDS often need NEET MDS or INI CET.
*   The field provides diverse specializations, from orthodontics to oral surgery, leading to varied career opportunities in both public and private sectors.
*   Average course fees can range significantly, from around Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 28 lakh, depending on the institution and program.

## What is Dentistry?

Professionals in dentistry, known as dentists, work closely with patients to promote good oral hygiene, identify early signs of dental diseases, and perform a wide array of procedures. These can include fillings, root canal treatments, tooth extractions, dental implants, and corrective surgeries. Beyond treatment, dentists also focus on preventive care and patient education, helping individuals understand how to maintain a healthy mouth and how oral health connects to their general health.

## Dentistry Degrees and Eligibility

Dentistry programs are available at various academic levels, from diplomas to doctoral degrees. The most common undergraduate degree is the Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS), while the Master of Dental Surgery (MDS) is the primary postgraduate qualification.

### Undergraduate (BDS)

*   **Duration:** Typically 4-5 years, including an internship.
*   **Eligibility:** Candidates must have successfully completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination from a recognized board, usually with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as core subjects. Admission is primarily through the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test).

### Postgraduate (MDS)

*   **Duration:** Generally 2-3 years.
*   **Eligibility:** Applicants must hold a BDS degree from a recognized institution and have completed their mandatory internship. Admission is often based on national-level entrance exams such as NEET MDS or INI CET (Institute of National Importance Combined Entrance Test).

## Specializations in Dentistry

The field of dentistry offers numerous specializations, allowing practitioners to develop deep expertise in specific areas of oral healthcare. These focused areas provide advanced knowledge and training for diagnosing and treating complex dental conditions.

Some prominent specializations include:

*   **Orthodontics:** Focusing on correcting misaligned teeth and jaws.
*   **Prosthodontics:** Dealing with the restoration and replacement of missing teeth and oral structures.
*   **Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery:** Involving surgical treatment of diseases, injuries, and defects in the head, neck, face, jaws, and the hard and soft tissues of the oral and maxillofacial region.
*   **Periodontics:** Specializing in the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of gum diseases.
*   **Conservative Dentistry and Endodontics:** Concentrating on the restoration of teeth affected by decay, trauma, or developmental defects, including root canal therapy.
*   **Pedodontics (Paediatric Dentistry):** Providing comprehensive oral healthcare for children.
*   **Public Health Dentistry:** Focusing on community oral health programs and disease prevention on a population level.
*   **Oral Medicine and Radiology:** Involving the diagnosis and non-surgical management of oral diseases and the use of imaging techniques.

## Career Opportunities and Top Recruiters

A degree in dentistry opens doors to a variety of fulfilling career paths. Graduates can work as general dentists or pursue further specialization to become experts in specific areas. The average salary for dentists in India can vary widely, typically ranging from Rs. 4 lakh to Rs. 15 lakh per annum, influenced by factors such as qualifications, experience, and the type of practice.

Common job roles include:

*   Dental Surgeon
*   Orthodontist
*   Prosthodontist
*   Pediatric Dentist
*   Public Health Dentist
*   Dental Researcher
*   Professor in Dental Colleges

Top recruiters for dental professionals include major hospital chains, private dental clinics, government health services, and educational institutions. Some prominent names are AIIMS, Fortis Dental, Clove Dental, Apollo Hospitals, the Indian Army Dental Corps, and various government and private dental colleges.
