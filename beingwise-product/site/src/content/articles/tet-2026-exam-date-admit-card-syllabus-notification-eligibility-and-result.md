# TET 2026: Exam Date, Admit Card, Syllabus, Notification, Eligibility & Result

The Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) is a crucial examination for individuals aspiring to become teachers in India. It serves as a benchmark to determine a candidate's suitability for teaching positions in both government and some private schools across the country.

## Key takeaways

*   The TET exam is mandatory for teaching in government schools and several private institutions, as per the Right to Education (RTE) Act and National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) guidelines.
*   TETs are conducted at both the central level (CTET by CBSE) and state levels (e.g., UPTET, Bihar STET, REET, MAHA TET) to assess eligibility for Class 1 to Class 8.
*   The Supreme Court has emphasized the compulsory nature of TET qualification for both new and in-service teachers.
*   Each TET exam (central or state) has its own specific notification, application process, syllabus, exam pattern, and eligibility criteria.

## Understanding the Teacher Eligibility Test (TET)

The Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) is an essential qualifying examination designed to assess candidates' readiness to teach. Passing a TET exam is a prerequisite for securing teaching roles, particularly in government-funded schools, and is increasingly required by private educational institutions. The National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) sets the guidelines for these examinations, which are then implemented by the appropriate government bodies.

## Central vs. State TETs

There are two main categories of TET examinations:

*   **Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET):** Conducted by the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE), the CTET is a national-level exam. A CTET qualification is widely accepted across various states and Union Territories for teaching positions.
*   **State-level TETs:** Individual states and Union Territories organize their own TET exams. Examples include Bihar STET, KARTET, TSTET, UPTET, REET, TNTET, MAHA TET, OTET, HP TET, and KTET. These exams primarily qualify candidates for teaching within the respective state's educational system.

Each of these exams has a distinct notification release, application window, eligibility requirements, exam structure, and syllabus. Aspiring teachers should identify which TET is most relevant to their desired teaching location and carefully review the specific details for that examination.

## Eligibility Criteria for TET 2026

While specific criteria can vary slightly between different TET exams, general eligibility typically includes:

*   **Educational Qualification:** Candidates usually need to possess a bachelor's degree along with a professional teaching qualification such as a Bachelor of Education (B.Ed) or a Diploma in Elementary Education (D.El.Ed). Specific minimum percentage requirements in graduation or professional courses may apply.
*   **Age Limit:** There is generally no upper age limit for appearing in most TET exams, but candidates should check the specific notification for any such provisions.
*   **Nationality:** Candidates must be citizens of India.

It is crucial to refer to the official notification for the specific TET exam you plan to take for precise and up-to-date eligibility details, as these can differ.

## TET Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The TET exams typically consist of two papers:

*   **Paper I:** For candidates aspiring to teach Class 1 to Class 5.
*   **Paper II:** For candidates aspiring to teach Class 6 to Class 8.

Candidates wishing to teach both age groups must appear for both papers. The common subjects generally include Child Development and Pedagogy, Language I (compulsory), Language II (compulsory), Mathematics, and Environmental Studies (for Paper I), or Mathematics and Science / Social Studies (for Paper II). Each paper usually comprises Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) with no negative marking.

For detailed subject-wise syllabus and the exact exam pattern, including the number of questions and marks per section, candidates should always consult the official information bulletin released by the conducting body (e.g., CBSE for CTET, or respective state examination boards).

## Admit Card and Results

**Admit Card:** The admit card is a mandatory document for appearing in the TET exam. It contains vital information such as the candidate's name, roll number, exam date, time, and venue. Candidates can typically download their admit cards from the official portal of the respective TET conducting body a few weeks before the exam date by entering their application credentials.

**Results:** TET results are usually declared a few weeks after the examination. Candidates can check their scores and qualifying status on the official website by logging in with their registration details. The TET certificate validity is often for a lifetime, but candidates should confirm this for the specific TET they appear for, as policies can change.

Staying informed through official channels is key to navigating the TET examination process successfully.
