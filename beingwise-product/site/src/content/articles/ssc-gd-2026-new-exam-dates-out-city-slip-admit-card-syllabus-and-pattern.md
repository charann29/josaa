# SSC GD 2026: New Exam Dates OUT, City Slip, Admit Card, Syllabus & Pattern

The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) has recently announced revised dates for the General Duty (GD) Constable examination in 2026. This is a crucial update for all candidates preparing for this significant recruitment drive.

## Key Takeaways

*   The SSC GD 2026 exam will now run from April 27 to May 30, 2026, across four distinct phases.
*   Candidates had a limited window (April 11-15, 2026) to submit their preferences for examination weeks.
*   Approximately 25,487 vacancies were announced for the 2026 recruitment cycle.
*   The selection process involves a Computer-Based Test (CBT), Physical Efficiency Test (PET), Physical Standard Test (PST), medical examination, and document verification.

## Revised SSC GD 2026 Exam Schedule

The Staff Selection Commission has officially published the new schedule for the GD Constable exam. Originally planned for February 23, 2026, the examination will now take place over an extended period in four phases. This change allows candidates more time to prepare and adapt their study plans.

The updated examination dates are as follows:

*   **Week 1:** April 27 to May 2, 2026
*   **Week 2:** May 4 to May 9, 2026
*   **Week 3:** May 18 to May 23, 2026
*   **Week 4:** May 25 to May 30, 2026

Candidates were given a specific period, from April 11 (3 PM) to April 15 (5 PM) in 2026, to log into their accounts on the official portal and indicate their preferred examination weeks. This feature aims to streamline the examination process for a large number of applicants.

## City Intimation Slip and Admit Card Details

Before the main examination, candidates will receive their city intimation slips, which inform them about their designated exam city. This slip is typically released approximately one week before the examination date, allowing candidates to make necessary travel arrangements.

The official admit card for the SSC GD 2026 exam will be made available around three days before a candidate's specific examination date. It is essential to remember that the admit card is a mandatory document for entry into the examination hall. It will contain critical details such as the candidate's name, roll number, photograph, examination date, time, and venue.

To download your admit card, follow these steps:

1.  Visit the official SSC website.
2.  Locate and click on the SSC GD admit card link.
3.  Log in using your registration ID and date of birth.
4.  Download your SSC GD Constable admit card.
5.  Print a hard copy, as digital copies are not accepted at the exam centre.

Candidates must carry a printout of their admit card along with a valid photo identity proof to the examination centre.

## Application Process and Vacancies

The application window for SSC GD 2026 concluded on December 31, 2025. The official notification, released on December 1, 2025, announced a total of 25,487 vacancies for various General Duty Constable positions. Prospective candidates had a defined period to complete their online application forms, ensuring all eligibility criteria were met.

## Understanding the Selection Process, Syllabus & Pattern

### Selection Process

The SSC GD selection process is comprehensive and multi-staged, designed to identify suitable candidates for the General Duty Constable roles. It includes:

1.  **Computer-Based Test (CBT):** This is the initial stage, an online examination testing candidates' knowledge across various subjects.
2.  **Physical Efficiency Test (PET) & Physical Standard Test (PST):** Candidates who clear the CBT proceed to these physical tests, which assess their physical fitness and measurements.
3.  **Medical Examination:** A thorough medical check-up to ensure candidates meet the required health standards.
4.  **Document Verification:** Verification of all submitted documents and certificates.

### Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The Computer-Based Test (CBT) for SSC GD typically covers four main sections:

*   **General Intelligence and Reasoning:** Assessing logical and analytical abilities.
*   **General Knowledge and General Awareness:** Covering current affairs, history, geography, economics, and general science.
*   **Elementary Mathematics:** Basic arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration.
*   **English/Hindi:** Testing language comprehension and basic grammar skills.

The exam is usually objective-type with multiple-choice questions. A detailed syllabus and the exact exam pattern, including the number of questions per section and marking scheme, are always provided in the official notification released by the Staff Selection Commission. Candidates are strongly advised to refer to the official notification for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding the syllabus and exam structure.
