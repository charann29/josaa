# NIRF MBA Ranking 2022: Top Management Institutes in India

Understanding the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) rankings is crucial for any student considering a Master of Business Administration (MBA) program in India. These rankings, released by the Ministry of Education, offer a reliable guide to the nation's premier management institutions.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIM Ahmedabad maintained its top position in the NIRF Management rankings for 2022.
*   Ten Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) secured spots within the top 20 MBA colleges nationwide.
*   Six Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) also featured prominently in the top 20 for their MBA/PGDM offerings.
*   Significant shifts occurred within the top 10, with IIT Delhi and IIT Madras showing notable improvements in their ranks compared to previous years.

## Understanding the NIRF Framework

The NIRF framework, established in September 2015, assesses higher education institutions across India based on a comprehensive set of parameters. These rankings are announced annually by the Ministry of Education and cover various disciplines, including Management, Engineering, Universities, and overall performance. The objective is to promote healthy competition among institutions and help students make informed decisions.

## How Institutes Are Evaluated: NIRF Parameters

Institutions participating in the NIRF rankings are evaluated on five core parameters, each carrying equal weight. These parameters delve into various aspects of an institution's quality and performance, ensuring a holistic assessment. Below is a breakdown of these key areas:

| Parameters                       | Constituents                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Teaching, Learning & Resources (TLR)** | Student strength (including doctoral candidates), faculty-student ratio (emphasizing permanent faculty), combined metric for faculty with PhD or equivalent qualifications and experience, and the total budget and its effective utilization.                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Research & Professional Practice (RPC)** | Combined metrics for publications, quality of publications, intellectual property rights (IPR) including patents filed, published, granted, and licensed, and the footprint of projects, professional practice, and executive development programs.                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Graduation Outcomes (GO)**     | Combined percentage for placements, higher studies, and entrepreneurship; metric for university examinations; median salary of graduating students; metric for graduating students admitted into top universities; and the number of PhD students who have graduated.                                                                                                                                  |
| **Outreach & Inclusivity (OI)**  | This parameter considers the percentage of students from other states/countries, the representation of women, facilities for physically challenged students, and the economic and social diversity of the student body.                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **Perception (PR)**              | Based on a peer perception survey, this parameter gauges the academic reputation and public perception of the institution among various stakeholders, including employers, academic peers, and the public.                                                                                                                                                                                           |
