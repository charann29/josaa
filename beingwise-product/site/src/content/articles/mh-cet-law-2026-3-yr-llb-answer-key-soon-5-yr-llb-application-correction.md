## MH CET Law 2026 Answer Key for 3-Year LLB

The provisional answer key for the MH CET Law 3-year LLB course is expected to be released shortly after the exam, which concluded on April 2, 2026. Candidates should look for its release on the official CET Cell Maharashtra website, `cetcell.mahacet.org`, likely within the second week of April 2026. This provisional key will be followed by a final answer key after considering any objections raised by candidates.

To download the answer key and submit objections, follow these steps:

*   Visit the official CET Cell Maharashtra website.
*   Log in to your candidate portal.
*   Locate and click on the "Objection Tracking" section.
*   Select "MAH LLB 3 Year Answer Key."
*   If you have objections, fill in the required details on the objection form.
*   Review and submit your details.

## MH CET Law 2026 Application and Correction Window

The application correction window for the 5-year LLB program is currently active and will close on April 9, 2026. Candidates who have applied for the 5-year LLB course should visit the official website, `cetcell.mahacet.org`, to make any necessary corrections to their application forms by logging in with their credentials.

### How to Apply for MH CET Law

For those who haven't yet applied or need a reminder of the general application process:

*   Go to the official MAH CET Cell portal.
*   If you are a new applicant, click on the "New Registration" tab to create an account.
*   If you have already registered, use the "Already Registered" tab to log in.
*   Once logged in, proceed to fill out the application form with all required personal and academic details.
*   Upload your recent passport-sized photograph and your signature as per the specifications.
*   Pay the application fee through the designated payment gateway.
*   Before final submission, carefully review your entire application form for any errors.
*   Save and submit the completed application form.

## Understanding the MH CET Law Syllabus and Previous Year Questions (PYQ)

While specific details about the 2026 syllabus are released by CET Cell Maharashtra, generally, the MH CET Law exam covers sections like Legal Aptitude and Legal Reasoning, General Knowledge with Current Affairs, Logical and Analytical Reasoning, and English. For effective preparation, students are strongly advised to refer to the official syllabus and practice with previous year's question papers (PYQ). Solving PYQs helps in understanding the exam pattern, types of questions asked, and managing time efficiently during the actual examination. These resources are typically available on the official CET Cell portal or through reputable educational guidance platforms.

Stay informed by regularly checking the official CET Cell Maharashtra website for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding MH CET Law 2026.
