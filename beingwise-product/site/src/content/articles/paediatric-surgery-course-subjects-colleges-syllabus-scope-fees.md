# Paediatric Surgery Course, Subjects, Colleges, Syllabus, Scope, Fees, Eligibility, Admission

Paediatric surgery is a highly specialized medical field focused on surgical procedures for children, from newborns to adolescents. It requires unique skills and a deep understanding of how diseases and injuries affect growing bodies, often involving collaboration with other specialists and families.

## Key takeaways

*   The primary qualification for this super-specialty is an M.Ch. (Magister Chirurgiae) in Paediatric Surgery.
*   Admission to M.Ch. programs typically requires an MS in General Surgery and success in national-level entrance examinations like NEET-SS or INI-SS.
*   Graduates can pursue diverse roles such as Paediatric Surgeons, Neonatal Surgeons, or Clinical Researchers, with an average starting salary ranging from ₹15 to ₹35 LPA.
*   The curriculum covers crucial areas like neonatal surgery, minimally invasive techniques, and the management of congenital disorders.

## Understanding Paediatric Surgery

Paediatric surgeons are specially trained to address a wide array of health conditions affecting young patients. This includes correcting birth defects, treating injuries, and managing various surgical conditions specific to different stages of childhood development. The delicate nature of younger patients demands exceptional precision and skill. Effective treatment often involves close cooperation with neonatologists and family physicians, along with clear communication with parents regarding pre- and post-operative care.

### M.Ch. in Paediatric Surgery: Course Overview

The M.Ch. in Paediatric Surgery is a post-doctoral super-specialty program designed to equip surgeons with advanced expertise in this demanding field.

| Particulars | Details |
| :-------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Course Name** | M.Ch. (Magister Chirurgiae) in Paediatric Surgery |
| **Level** | Super Speciality (Post-doctoral) |
| **Duration** | 3 Years |
| **Eligibility Criteria** | MS in General Surgery or an equivalent postgraduate surgical degree |
| **Entrance Exams** | NEET-SS, INI-SS |

## Eligibility and Admission Process

To be eligible for an M.Ch. in Paediatric Surgery, candidates must hold an MS in General Surgery or an equivalent qualification. Admission is primarily through highly competitive national-level entrance examinations. The National Board of Examinations in Medical Sciences (NBEMS) conducts NEET-SS, while institutions like AIIMS conduct INI-SS for admission to their super-specialty programs. Successful candidates then participate in a centralized counselling process based on their exam ranks.

## Career Scope and Salary Expectations

Upon completing an M.Ch. in Paediatric Surgery, graduates have a wide range of career opportunities. They can work in children's hospitals, multi-specialty hospitals, teaching hospitals, and private healthcare chains. Common roles include Paediatric Surgeon, Neonatal Surgeon, Uro-Paediatric Surgeon, or even pursuing academic careers as Professors or Clinical Researchers. The average annual salary for a paediatric surgeon in India typically ranges from ₹15 to ₹35 Lakhs, depending on experience, location, and the type of institution.

This challenging yet rewarding field offers the unique opportunity to make a profound difference in the lives of young patients and their families.
