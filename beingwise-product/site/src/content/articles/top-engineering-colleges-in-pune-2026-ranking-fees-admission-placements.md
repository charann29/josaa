## Admission Process and Eligibility

Admission to engineering colleges in Pune primarily relies on performance in national and state-level entrance examinations. Candidates must meet specific eligibility criteria to be considered for admission.

### Eligibility Criteria

*   Applicants must have completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects.
*   A minimum aggregate percentage, usually around 45-50%, is typically required in the qualifying examination, though this can vary by college and category.

### Accepted Entrance Exams

*   **JEE Main:** Conducted by NTA, this is a national-level entrance exam for admission to engineering programs across India, including many in Pune.
*   **MHT CET:** The Maharashtra Common Entrance Test is a state-level examination for admission to professional courses in Maharashtra, including engineering.

Successful candidates will then participate in counselling processes, such as JoSAA for JEE Main qualifiers or the state-level counselling for MHT CET, to secure their seats.
