# CMAT 2026: Result (OUT), Final Answer Key, Dates, Cutoff, Top Colleges

The Common Management Admission Test (CMAT) is a crucial national-level entrance exam for aspiring MBA students across India. Conducted annually, it serves as a gateway to numerous AICTE-approved management programs.

## Key takeaways

*   The CMAT 2026 exam was successfully held on January 25, 2026.
*   The National Testing Agency (NTA) declared the CMAT 2026 results and released the final answer key on February 17, 2026.
*   Out of 53,453 applicants, 41,872 candidates appeared for the CMAT 2026 examination.
*   Admission windows for various MBA colleges accepting CMAT scores are currently open.

## CMAT Exam Overview

CMAT is a three-hour computer-based test designed to assess candidates' aptitude for management studies. It is accepted by all institutions recognized by AICTE for technical education and also plays a role in admissions to Maharashtra State institutes via MAH CET CAP. The exam features multiple-choice questions (MCQs) across five key sections:

*   Quantitative Techniques & Data Interpretation
*   Logical Reasoning
*   Language Comprehension
*   General Awareness
*   Innovation and Entrepreneurship

To secure admission into top-tier MBA programs, candidates need to achieve a competitive percentile in CMAT.

## CMAT 2026 Result and Final Answer Key

The NTA released the CMAT 2026 result along with the final answer key on February 17, 2026. The result is available as a scorecard online, which candidates can download from the official CMAT website. The final answer key indicated a revision in only one question (question number 97) after considering candidate objections.

### How to Download Your CMAT 2026 Scorecard:

1.  Visit the official CMAT 2026 website.
2.  Locate and click on the 'CMAT-2026 scorecard' tab.
3.  Enter your application number and either your password or date of birth.
4.  Click 'Submit' to view and download your CMAT 2026 result.

## CMAT Cutoffs and Top Colleges

CMAT scores are accepted by approximately 1,300 MBA colleges across India. The cutoffs for these institutions vary significantly based on factors like college ranking, program popularity, and the number of applicants. Generally, top-ranked colleges demand higher CMAT percentiles. Candidates scoring above the 90th percentile typically have better chances of admission to premier institutions.

Many colleges have already commenced their admission processes, including group discussions (GD) and personal interviews (PI) rounds, for candidates who meet their CMAT cutoff criteria. It is advisable to check the individual websites of your target colleges for their specific admission timelines and cutoff requirements.
