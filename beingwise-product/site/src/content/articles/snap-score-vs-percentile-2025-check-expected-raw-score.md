# SNAP Score vs Percentile 2025 - Check Expected Raw Score

Understanding the difference between your raw SNAP score and your SNAP percentile is crucial for evaluating your performance and admission chances for MBA programs at Symbiosis International (Deemed University) (SIU) institutes. While your score reflects the marks you earned, your percentile indicates how well you performed compared to other test-takers.

## Key takeaways

*   **Score vs. Percentile:** Your raw score is your total marks out of 60, which is then scaled to 50 for the merit list. Your percentile shows your rank relative to all candidates.
*   **Admission Basis:** SIU MBA admissions primarily rely on SNAP percentile cut-offs for shortlisting candidates for the Group Exercise (GE) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds.
*   **Result Interpretation:** Knowing the calculation helps you understand your standing and potential eligibility for different SIU colleges.
*   **Dynamic Cut-offs:** Expected cut-offs for top colleges are often in the high percentile range, reflecting the competitive nature of these programs.

## Understanding SNAP Score

Your SNAP score is the total number of correct answers multiplied by the marks awarded for each, minus any penalties for incorrect answers. The exam typically consists of 60 multiple-choice questions, with each correct answer fetching 1 mark and a penalty of 0.25 marks for each incorrect answer. The total raw score you achieve is then scaled down to a score out of 50 for the final merit list preparation. This scaling ensures fairness, especially if the exam is conducted in multiple sessions with varying difficulty levels.

## What is SNAP Percentile?

The SNAP percentile signifies the percentage of candidates who scored less than or equal to you in the examination. For instance, if you achieve a 90 percentile, it means that 90% of the candidates who appeared for SNAP scored less than or equal to your raw score. This relative ranking is a critical factor for admission, as it standardises performance across all test-takers. Symbiosis International (Deemed University) colleges use these percentile cut-offs to shortlist candidates for the subsequent selection rounds.

## How to Calculate SNAP Percentile 2025

The official SNAP percentile calculation process involves a formula that considers the total number of candidates who appeared for the exam. The general formula for calculating percentile is:

`Percentile = ((Total Number of Candidates - Rank of the Candidate) / Total Number of Candidates) * 100`

Here, the 'Rank of the Candidate' refers to your rank based on your raw score among all test-takers. Since SNAP is often conducted in multiple sessions, a normalisation process is applied to ensure fairness before final percentiles are calculated.

## SNAP Marking Scheme 2025

The SNAP exam follows a consistent marking scheme. For every correct answer, candidates are awarded 1 mark. For every incorrect answer, 0.25 marks are deducted. There is no penalty for unattempted questions. Therefore, carefully managing your attempts is crucial to maximise your raw score, as negative marking can significantly impact your overall performance. The total marks are out of 60.

Understanding these calculations will empower you to better interpret your SNAP result and strategise your application process for Symbiosis MBA programs.
