# NIFT Entrance Exam 2026: Stage 2 PG Interview (April 06 to April 11), Situation Test Date, Stage 1 Result (OUT), & Expected Cutoff

The National Institute of Fashion Technology (NIFT) entrance examination is a crucial step for aspiring designers and fashion professionals. This page provides the latest updates on the NIFT 2026 examination, including recent results, upcoming interview schedules, and other key information.

## Key takeaways

*   The NIFT Stage 1 results for all programs were declared on March 20, 2026, shortlisting candidates for the next stage.
*   Postgraduate (PG) interview call letters have been issued, with interviews scheduled from April 06 to April 11, 2026.
*   Stage 2 assessments (Situation Test for B.Des and Personal Interview for PG programs) are slated to occur between April 06 and April 11, 2026.
*   Candidates must download their PG interview call letters using their application ID and password.

## NIFT Stage 1 Results Announced

The results for Stage 1 of the NIFT Entrance Exam 2026 were officially released on March 20, 2026. Candidates who appeared for the exam can access their scores on the NTA-NIFT website by entering their application ID and password. This initial result determines eligibility for the subsequent rounds: the Situation Test for undergraduate (B.Des) candidates and the Personal Interview for postgraduate (M.Des, MFM, MFTech) candidates.

## Postgraduate Interview Call Letters and Schedule

For those applying to postgraduate programs, the NIFT Interview Call Letters have been released. Applicants can download their respective call letters from the official NIFT portal using their application ID and password. The postgraduate interviews are scheduled to take place from April 06 to April 11, 2026. It is mandatory for candidates to bring their call letter and a valid photo ID proof to the interview. While not compulsory, carrying a design portfolio is also advisable for M.Des candidates.

## Understanding the NIFT Selection Process

The NIFT Entrance Exam is a national-level assessment conducted by the National Institute of Fashion Technology for admission to its various design and fashion technology programs. The examination typically involves multiple stages:

### Stage 1: Written Examinations

This stage consists of two main tests:

*   **General Ability Test (GAT):** Mandatory for all programs, this computer-based test assesses quantitative ability, communication ability, English comprehension, analytical ability, and general knowledge and current affairs.
*   **Creative Ability Test (CAT):** This paper-based test is specifically for B.Des and M.Des applicants, evaluating their intuitive ability, observation skills, design ability, and power of imagination.

### Stage 2: Situation Test/Personal Interview

Shortlisted candidates from Stage 1 proceed to Stage 2, which varies by program:

*   **Situation Test:** Required for B.Des candidates, this practical test assesses a candidate's material handling skills and innovative ability in a given situation. The focus is on creativity, conceptualization, and construction skills, rather than drawing ability.
*   **Personal Interview:** Mandatory for all postgraduate programs (M.Des, MFM, MFTech), this round evaluates a candidate's communication skills, general aptitude, career orientation, and suitability for the chosen program. Carrying a design portfolio can be beneficial for M.Des interviews.

Admission to the B.F.Tech program is based solely on the GAT scores, without a Situation Test or Personal Interview component.
