# CBSE Board Exams 2026 (Class 10th & 12th): Result, Date Sheet, Question Paper & Syllabus

The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) conducts the highly anticipated Class 10 and Class 12 board examinations annually, playing a crucial role in students' academic journeys. This page provides essential information for the 2026 examinations, covering results, exam schedules, and syllabus updates.

## Key takeaways

*   CBSE Class 10 results for the first board exam are expected in April/May 2026, with a second exam result likely in June/July 2026.
*   CBSE Class 12 results are anticipated in May 2026, following the conclusion of exams in April.
*   The board has released the syllabus for the 2026-27 academic year, which includes significant changes.
*   Students can access their provisional results online using their roll numbers and should collect original certificates from their schools.

## CBSE Results 2026

The CBSE Class 10 results for the first phase of exams are expected to be declared between April and May 2026. This early release is due to the introduction of a second board examination, which will have its results announced around June or July 2026. For Class 12 students, the results are generally declared in May 2026, after the examinations conclude in mid-April. Students can check their provisional scores by following a simple online process:

*   Visit the official CBSE website.
*   Look for the 'CBSE result link' and click on it.
*   Enter your roll number and any other required credentials.
*   Your result will be displayed on the screen.
*   Download and save a copy for your records. Remember to collect your original mark sheet and certificates from your school once they are available.

### CBSE Class 12 Passing Marks

To successfully clear the CBSE Class 12 examinations, students must meet specific passing criteria in both theory and practical/internal assessment components. The breakdown for key subjects is as follows:

| Subject | Marks in Theory | Marks in Practical and Internal Assessment | Passing Marks for Theory Exam | Passing Marks for Internal Assessment/Practical |
| :-------------------------------- | :-------------- | :----------------------------------------- | :---------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------- |
| Physics | 70 | 30 | 23 | 9 |
| Chemistry | 70 | 30 | 23 | 9 |
| Mathematics | 80 | 20 | 26 | 6 |
| Biology | 70 | 30 | 23 | 9 |
| English Core | 80 | 20 | 23 | 6 |

### CBSE Class 10 Passing Marks

For Class 10, students need to secure an overall minimum of 33% in each subject to pass, combining both theory and internal assessment scores. The structure for maximum marks is:

| Subject | Maximum marks of Theory | Maximum marks of Practical/Internal assessment |
| :-------------------- | :---------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- |
| English | 80 | 20 |
| Mathematics Basics | 80 | 20 |
| Science | 80 | 20 |
| Computer Applications | 30 | 70 |
| Social Science | 80 | 20 |

## CBSE Syllabus 2026-27

The CBSE has officially released the syllabus for the academic year 2026-27 for both Class 10 and Class 12. Students and educators should note that significant changes have been implemented. It is highly recommended that all students refer to the latest syllabus available on the official CBSE website to understand the revised curriculum and prepare accordingly.
