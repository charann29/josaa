Commercial law, also known as trade law or mercantile law, is the area of legal practice that focuses on the rules and regulations governing business transactions and commercial activities. It's a vital field for anyone interested in the legal aspects of how businesses operate.

## Key takeaways

*   Commercial law deals with a wide range of business activities, including sales, contracts, intellectual property, banking, and consumer rights.
*   Courses are available at undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG), and PhD levels, with varying durations and eligibility requirements.
*   Admission to most reputable commercial law programs is typically through national or university-specific entrance exams.
*   Career opportunities are diverse, spanning corporate law, investment banking, legal advisory, and intellectual property.

## What is Commercial Law?

Commercial law forms the backbone of the business world, providing the legal framework within which companies conduct their operations. This field ensures fair practices, resolves disputes, and regulates everything from the formation of a contract between two parties to the protection of a company's unique brand or invention. It touches upon sales, financial agreements, property rights in a business context, and the rights of consumers.

## Commercial Law Eligibility and Admission 2026

Eligibility criteria for commercial law programs vary by course level:

*   **Certificate Programs:** Generally require a Class 12 pass or a graduate degree.
*   **Undergraduate (UG) Programs (e.g., BA LLB, BBA LLB):** Typically require a Class 12 pass, often with a minimum percentage (e.g., 45-55%).
*   **Postgraduate (PG) Programs (e.g., LLM):** Require a three-year or five-year LLB degree.
*   **PhD Programs:** Require an LLM degree.

Admission to most commercial law programs, especially at leading institutions, is entrance-based. Candidates often need to appear for national-level law entrance exams. For 2026 admissions, students should monitor official university websites and exam conducting bodies for specific dates and application procedures.

## Top Entrance Exams for Commercial Law 2026

Aspiring commercial law students will typically need to clear one or more entrance examinations. Some of the prominent exams include:

*   **CLAT (Common Law Admission Test):** Essential for admission to many National Law Universities (NLUs) and other affiliated colleges for UG and PG law programs.
*   **AILET (All India Law Entrance Test):** Specifically for admission to National Law University Delhi (NLU Delhi).
*   **SLS AIAT (Symbiosis Law School All India Admission Test):** For admission to Symbiosis Law Schools.
*   **ULS-AT (UPES Law Studies Aptitude Test):** For admission to law programs at UPES.
*   **CUCET (Central Universities Common Entrance Test):** Some central universities may use this for their law programs.

Students are advised to check the specific requirements of their target colleges on their official portals.

## Commercial Law Career Options

A degree in commercial law opens doors to a wide array of career paths in both the legal and corporate sectors. Graduates can find roles such as:

*   **Legal Manager/Advisor:** Providing in-house legal counsel to businesses.
*   **Contract Attorney:** Specializing in drafting, negotiating, and reviewing contracts.
*   **Corporate Lawyer:** Working for law firms or companies, advising on mergers, acquisitions, and corporate governance.
*   **Intellectual Property Lawyer:** Protecting trademarks, copyrights, and patents for businesses.
*   **Investment Banker:** Applying legal knowledge to financial transactions and compliance.
*   **Legal Researcher/Assistant:** Supporting senior lawyers in research and case preparation.

To practice law professionally in India, including commercial law, graduates must pass the All India Bar Examination (AIBE).

Commercial law offers a dynamic and rewarding career for those passionate about the intersection of business and legal principles.
