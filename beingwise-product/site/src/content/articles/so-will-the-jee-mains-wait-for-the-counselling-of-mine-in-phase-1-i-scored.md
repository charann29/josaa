# So, Will JEE Mains Wait for My Counselling?

It's completely normal to wonder how your JEE Main results and the subsequent counselling process fit together, especially when you're planning to appear for both phases. Let's break down how this usually works and what you can expect.

## Key Takeaways

*   JEE Main Phase 2 results are typically released before counselling for admissions begins.
*   Your best percentile from either Phase 1 or Phase 2 will be considered for ranking.
*   JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) conducts the centralized counselling for both JEE Main and JEE Advanced qualified candidates.
*   Even if you don't qualify for JEE Advanced, JoSAA counselling offers pathways to various engineering colleges based on your JEE Main scores.

## Understanding the JEE Main Phases and Counselling Timeline

Many students appear for both phases of JEE Main to improve their scores, and the system is designed to accommodate this. The National Testing Agency (NTA) is responsible for conducting JEE Main. After both phases are complete, NTA releases the final results, taking your best percentile score from either attempt. This final percentile is crucial for determining your rank and eligibility for various colleges.

Counselling, on the other hand, is generally a separate process that begins *after* the final JEE Main results are declared. This ensures that all candidates, regardless of whether they appeared in Phase 1, Phase 2, or both, have their complete and best scores considered.

## The Role of JoSAA Counselling

JoSAA, or the Joint Seat Allocation Authority, is the body that manages the joint counselling and seat allocation for admissions to IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other Government Funded Technical Institutions (GFTIs). This is a centralized process, meaning you don't have to apply to each college individually through separate counselling rounds.

When JoSAA counselling begins, you will register on their official portal and fill in your choices of colleges and courses based on your JEE Main rank. The allocation of seats is then done in multiple rounds, considering your rank, preferences, and the availability of seats.

## What if I Don't Qualify for JEE Advanced?

It's important not to get discouraged if you don't meet the cutoff for JEE Advanced. While JEE Advanced is the gateway to the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), a strong JEE Main score can still open doors to excellent engineering colleges. NITs (National Institutes of Technology), IIITs (Indian Institutes of Information Technology), and GFTIs are highly regarded institutions that admit students primarily based on their JEE Main performance.

JoSAA counselling includes these institutions, providing a wide array of options. Many of these colleges consistently feature well in the NIRF rankings, offering quality education and good career prospects.

## Maximizing Your Chances

Since you've scored 76 percentile in Phase 1 and are planning to appear for Phase 2, you're taking the right approach to improve your score. Focus on preparing well for Phase 2 to achieve your best possible percentile. A higher percentile will significantly enhance your chances during JoSAA counselling. Keep an eye on the official JoSAA portal for updates on counselling schedules and procedures once the final JEE Main results are out.

By understanding these processes, you can navigate your admission journey with greater confidence.
