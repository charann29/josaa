*   IIM shortlists for the Personal Interview (PI) round for the 2026-28 batch have been released by individual institutes.

## The IIM Selection Process

The IIM admission process involves several stages, beginning with the CAT exam. After the CAT results are declared, IIMs release their individual shortlists based on their specific criteria. These criteria typically include category-wise CAT qualifying cut-offs, past academic performance, and other personality parameters. The final admission score is calculated using a formula that weighs these different components.

### Common Admission Processes (CAP & JAP)

Some IIMs streamline their admission process through common platforms:

*   **IIM CAP 2026:** IIM Bodhgaya, IIM Jammu, and IIM Sirmaur use the Common Admission Process (CAP) for shortlisting candidates for the PI-WAT rounds.
*   **IIM JAP 2026:** IIM Kashipur, IIM Raipur, IIM Ranchi, and IIM Trichirappalli jointly shortlist candidates through the Joint Admission Process (JAP).

### WAT-PI Round

Candidates who are shortlisted receive an interview call letter with details about their Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) schedule and venue. This round is conducted in person, and candidates must be physically present. The WAT assesses writing skills, while the PI evaluates communication, general awareness, and leadership potential.

## Admission Criteria Beyond CAT Score

While the CAT score is a crucial initial filter, it is not the sole determinant for IIM admission. The selection criteria are holistic and encompass:

*   **Academic Record:** Performance in 10th, 12th, and undergraduate degrees.
*   **Work Experience:** Relevant work experience, if any, is often given weightage.
*   **Diversity:** Some institutes consider academic and gender diversity.
*   **WAT-PI Performance:** The scores obtained in the Written Ability Test and Personal Interview rounds are critical.

Each IIM has its own unique weightage system for these components, which is detailed in their admission policy document available on their official websites.

## MBA Admission Through CAT in Non-IIMs

Beyond the IIMs, a large number of other top-tier business schools across India also accept CAT scores for their MBA/PGP programs. These institutes conduct their own selection processes, which typically include CAT cut-offs, followed by group discussions (GD) or written ability tests (WAT), and personal interviews (PI). Aspiring students should check the individual admission criteria and application deadlines for these institutions.

Navigating the IIM selection process requires careful attention to deadlines and specific institute requirements. Best of luck to all applicants!
