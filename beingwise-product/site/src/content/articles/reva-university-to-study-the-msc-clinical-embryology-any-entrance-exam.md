# M.Sc. Clinical Embryology at Reva University: Understanding the Admission Process

Considering an M.Sc. in Clinical Embryology at Reva University in Bangalore? It's natural to wonder about the admission requirements, especially whether an entrance exam is part of the process. Let's break down what you need to know.

## Key Takeaways

*   Admission primarily considers your academic performance in your undergraduate degree.
*   Reva University's own entrance exam, REVA CET, is generally required for M.Sc. Clinical Embryology.
*   A personal interview might also be part of the selection process after the entrance test.
*   No national-level entrance exams like NEET or ICMR are typically needed for this specific program.

## Eligibility Criteria for M.Sc. Clinical Embryology

To be considered for the M.Sc. Clinical Embryology program at Reva University, you must meet specific academic qualifications. Candidates generally need to hold a Bachelor's degree in a relevant field. This includes disciplines such as Life Sciences, Biotechnology, Zoology, Microbiology, Medical Sciences, Dental Sciences, or Pharmacy.

Furthermore, there's a minimum percentage requirement for your undergraduate marks. Typically, applicants need to have secured at least 60% in their qualifying Bachelor's degree. For candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes (SC) or Scheduled Tribes (ST), this requirement is usually relaxed to 40%.

## The Role of REVA CET

While some programs at Reva University might offer direct admission based solely on undergraduate merit, the M.Sc. Clinical Embryology program generally requires candidates to appear for the university's own entrance examination: the REVA Common Entrance Test (REVA CET).

Your performance in REVA CET plays a significant role in the admission decision. The university uses this test to assess candidates' aptitude and subject knowledge relevant to the program. It's advisable to prepare for this test to enhance your chances of admission.

## The Interview Stage

Following the REVA CET, shortlisted candidates may be invited for a personal interview. This interview is a crucial step in the admission process, allowing the university to further evaluate your suitability for the program. It's an opportunity for you to discuss your academic background, your interest in clinical embryology, and your career aspirations.

While the primary focus is on your REVA CET score and academic merit, a strong performance in the interview can certainly solidify your application.

## Application Process and Staying Informed

The most reliable way to apply is through the official Reva University admission portal. This portal will provide the most up-to-date information regarding application deadlines, exam schedules, and any specific requirements for the M.Sc. Clinical Embryology program.

It's always a good practice to regularly check the university's official website or contact their admissions office directly for the latest updates, as admission policies can occasionally be adjusted based on the number of applications received in a given year.

In summary, while your undergraduate performance is key, be prepared to sit for the REVA CET and potentially an interview for admission to the M.Sc. Clinical Embryology program at Reva University.
