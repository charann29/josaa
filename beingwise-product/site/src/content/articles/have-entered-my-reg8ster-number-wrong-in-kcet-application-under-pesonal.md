# I have entered my register number wrong in kcet application under pesonal details dashboard.i cant change it using correction window.it shoes that register number is invalid.what should i do

It can be quite concerning to realize you've made a mistake on your KCET application, especially something as crucial as your registration number, and then find you can't correct it online. Don't worry, there are steps you can take to rectify this.

## Key Takeaways

*   Directly contact the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) as soon as possible.
*   Online correction windows often have limitations on what can be edited.
*   Prepare all relevant application details before reaching out to KEA.
*   Keep a record of all your communication with KEA.

## Understanding the Correction Window Limitations

The KCET application process includes a dedicated correction window, which is a valuable opportunity for applicants to review and amend their submitted information. While this window is designed to help you fix errors, it's important to understand that not all fields are editable. Critical identifiers, such as your registration number, might be locked to prevent fraudulent changes or significant data mismatches. If the system is showing your entered number as 'invalid', it's likely a hard-coded check, and the online portal won't allow you to proceed with that specific correction.

## Your Immediate Next Step: Contact KEA Directly

Since the online correction portal isn't allowing you to modify your registration number, your sole recourse is to reach out to the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) directly. They are the only body with the authority and means to make such a fundamental change to your application data. Do not delay this step; address it as soon as you discover the issue.

### How to Contact KEA

1.  **Phone:** Look for the official contact numbers provided on the KEA's official website. Have your application number, original registration number (if you remember it), and the incorrectly entered number ready. Be prepared to explain the situation clearly and concisely.
2.  **Email:** Send a detailed email to the official KEA support email address. Include your full name, KCET application number, the correct registration number, the incorrect registration number you entered, and a clear explanation of the problem. Attach any relevant documents if they might help (e.g., a screenshot of the error message, if possible, or your admit card from a previous exam if the registration number is from that). Ensure the subject line is clear, such as "Urgent: KCET Application Registration Number Correction."
3.  **In-Person (if feasible):** If you are geographically close and time permits, visiting the KEA office in person might be an option for a quicker resolution, though this isn't always practical for everyone. Always check their official website for office hours and any specific instructions for visitors.

## Preparing for Communication

Before you contact KEA, gather all necessary information and documents. This will make the process smoother and more efficient. Have the following at hand:

*   Your KCET Application Number
*   Your full name as entered in the application
*   The *correct* registration number you intended to enter
*   The *incorrect* registration number you actually entered
*   Any other personal details that might be required for verification (e.g., date of birth, father's name)
*   A clear, concise explanation of what happened.

## Documenting Your Efforts

It's crucial to keep a meticulous record of all your attempts to contact KEA. This includes:

*   **Date and Time:** Note down when you made calls or sent emails.
*   **Recipient:** If you spoke to someone, note their name or designation if provided.
*   **Summary of Conversation:** Briefly jot down what was discussed and any instructions given.
*   **Email Records:** Keep copies of all emails sent and received.

This documentation can be invaluable if there are any further complications or if you need to follow up on your request.

Dealing with application errors can be stressful, but by taking prompt and appropriate action to contact the Karnataka Examinations Authority, you significantly increase your chances of resolving the issue successfully.
