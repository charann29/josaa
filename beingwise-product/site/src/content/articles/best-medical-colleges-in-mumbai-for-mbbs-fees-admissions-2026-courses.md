# Best Medical Colleges in Mumbai for MBBS: Fees, Admissions 2026, Courses

Mumbai is a prime destination for aspiring medical students, offering a selection of reputable institutions for the Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) degree. This guide provides essential information for those considering an MBBS program in Mumbai for the 2026 academic year.

## Key takeaways

*   Mumbai is home to approximately eight medical colleges offering MBBS programs, comprising both government and private institutions.
*   Admission to these colleges is primarily through the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET UG), which is a highly competitive examination.
*   The majority of medical colleges in Mumbai are affiliated with the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences (MUHS), Nashik, and are recognized by the National Medical Commission (NMC).
*   The NEET UG 2026 examination is scheduled for May 3, 2026, as per official announcements.

## Overview of MBBS in Mumbai

An MBBS degree is a comprehensive undergraduate medical program, typically spanning five and a half years, which includes a mandatory one-year rotational internship. The curriculum is broadly divided into pre-clinical, para-clinical, and clinical phases. Securing a seat in Mumbai's top medical colleges requires meeting specific eligibility criteria and achieving a competitive score in the NEET UG exam.

## Eligibility Criteria for MBBS Admissions 2026

To be considered for an MBBS program in Mumbai, candidates must fulfill the following essential eligibility requirements:

*   **Age Limit:** Applicants must be at least 17 years old by December 31 of the admission year. There is no upper age limit for NEET UG as per the latest NTA guidelines.
*   **Educational Qualification:** Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination or its equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects from a recognized board.
*   **Minimum Marks:** General category candidates typically need an aggregate of 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology. For SC/ST/OBC candidates, the requirement is generally 40%, and for Persons with Disabilities (PwD) in the General category, it is 45%.
*   **Entrance Exam:** A valid and competitive score in the NEET UG examination is mandatory for admission to all MBBS colleges in Mumbai.

## Accepted Entrance Exam

For MBBS admissions in Mumbai, the **National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET UG)** is the sole accepted entrance examination. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), NEET UG is a national-level examination that serves as the gateway to undergraduate medical and dental courses across India. Aspiring students must register for and appear in NEET UG to be eligible for both All India Quota (AIQ) and State Quota seats in Mumbai's medical colleges.

## Fee Structure Comparison: Private vs. Government MBBS Colleges

The financial commitment for an MBBS degree varies significantly between government and private institutions. Government colleges typically have substantially lower tuition fees, making them highly sought after. For instance, annual fees in government colleges can range from approximately INR 1 lakh to INR 2.5 lakh. In contrast, private medical colleges generally have higher fees, which can range from INR 7 lakh to over INR 15 lakh per annum, depending on the institution and management quota. Prospective students and their families should consider these differences carefully when planning their applications.
