# Perfusion Technology Courses, Fees, Subjects, Colleges, Syllabus, Scope

Perfusion Technology is a specialised area within healthcare that focuses on supporting heart and lung function during complex surgical procedures. It involves operating advanced life-support equipment to maintain a patient's vital signs when their own heart and lungs are temporarily unable to function.

## Key Takeaways

*   Perfusion Technology is a crucial paramedical field supporting cardiac and pulmonary surgeries.
*   Courses are offered at undergraduate (BSc), postgraduate (MSc, PGD) levels, typically lasting 3-4 years for UG and 2 years for PG.
*   Eligibility for UG courses usually requires a 10+2 science background, while PG courses need a relevant bachelor's degree.
*   Graduates can find roles as Perfusion Technologists, Cardiac Perfusionists, or even pursue academic positions.

## What is Perfusion Technology?

This field trains students in the intricate physiology and pathology of the cardiovascular and respiratory systems. A core part of the curriculum involves mastering the operation of heart-lung machines and other critical life-support devices. Perfusion technologists work directly with surgical teams, monitoring patients and adjusting equipment to ensure proper blood circulation and oxygenation throughout an operation.

## Eligibility and Admission Process

To pursue a Bachelor of Science (BSc) in Perfusion Technology, candidates typically need to have completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, often with a minimum aggregate score of 50%. For postgraduate programs like an MSc or PGD, a bachelor's degree in a related medical or paramedical discipline is usually required.

Admission to these courses can be either merit-based, directly from 10+2 scores, or through entrance examinations. Some institutions may consider scores from national-level exams such as NEET PG for postgraduate admissions, or their own entrance tests like MET.

## Course Structure and Syllabus

Perfusion Technology programs combine theoretical knowledge with extensive practical training. The syllabus covers fundamental medical sciences alongside specialised topics:

*   **Anatomy and Physiology:** Detailed study of the human body, with a focus on cardiovascular and respiratory systems.
*   **Pathology and Pharmacology:** Understanding diseases affecting the heart and lungs, and the drugs used in their treatment.
*   **Cardiovascular Perfusion:** Principles and practice of operating heart-lung machines, blood management, and patient monitoring during surgery.
*   **Medical Ethics and Law:** Professional conduct and legal aspects relevant to healthcare.
*   **Clinical Rotations:** Hands-on experience in operating theatres, intensive care units, and cardiac catheterisation labs, under expert supervision.

## Career Scope and Future Prospects

Graduates in Perfusion Technology have promising career opportunities in hospitals, cardiac care centres, and research institutions. They are vital members of surgical teams, especially in cardiothoracic and vascular surgery departments. Common job roles include:

*   **Perfusion Technologist:** Operating heart-lung machines during open-heart surgeries.
*   **Cardiac Perfusionist:** Specialising in adult cardiac procedures.
*   **Paediatric Perfusionist:** Focusing on perfusion for infant and child surgeries.
*   **Medical Coder:** Applying knowledge of medical procedures for billing and record-keeping.
*   **Lecturer/Educator:** Teaching in academic institutions.

Leading hospitals like AIIMS, Fortis Healthcare, Apollo Hospitals, and Max Healthcare are regular recruiters of skilled perfusion technologists. The average starting salary for a Perfusion Technologist in India can be around ₹4.3 Lakhs per annum, with significant growth potential as experience increases.

Perfusion Technology offers a challenging yet rewarding career path for those passionate about critical care and advanced medical technology.
