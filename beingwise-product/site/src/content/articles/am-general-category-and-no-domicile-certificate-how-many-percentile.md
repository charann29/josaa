# I am general category and no domicile certificate. How many percentile and marks required for vjit admission

Securing a spot at Veermata Jijabai Technological Institute (VJTI), Mumbai, as a general category student without a Maharashtra domicile certificate means you'll be applying under the All India Quota, where your JEE Main score is paramount.

## Key takeaways

*   Admission for non-domicile, general category students at VJTI relies primarily on your JEE Main scores.
*   Aim for a very high JEE Main percentile, typically above 99.5 for competitive branches like Computer Engineering, Information Technology, or ECE.
*   A JEE Main score of 190+ is a good starting point, but 250-270+ marks are often needed for top branches.
*   Always check the official VJTI and Directorate of Technical Education (DTE) Maharashtra websites for the latest cutoff information.

## Eligibility criteria for VJTI

To be considered for admission, candidates must have successfully completed their 12th standard with a minimum aggregate of 45% in core subjects such as Physics and Mathematics. Crucially, you must also possess a valid score from the JEE Main examination, as this will be the primary basis for your application in the All India Quota.

## Understanding JEE Main percentile and marks for VJTI

Admission to VJTI for non-domicile general category students is highly competitive, with cutoffs varying each year. Your JEE Main percentile will be the critical factor in determining your chances. For highly sought-after branches, the required percentile is exceptionally high.

Here are some approximate figures based on recent trends:

*   **Computer Engineering:** In 2023, the closing percentile for Computer Engineering was around 99.9.
*   **Information Technology:** For Information Technology, the closing percentile in 2023 was approximately 99.85.
*   **Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE):** For non-domicile candidates aiming for ECE, a JEE Main percentile of around 99.5 or higher is generally recommended.

To achieve such high percentiles, you would typically need to score very well in the JEE Main exam. While aiming for above 190 marks is a good general target, for the most competitive branches, a score in the range of 250-270 marks out of 300 might be necessary based on previous years' trends. These mark ranges are estimates and can fluctuate with the difficulty of the exam and the performance of other candidates.

## MHT CET and VJTI admissions

While VJTI primarily considers MHT CET scores for Maharashtra domicile candidates, it's important to note how this impacts the overall admission landscape. For candidates from outside Maharashtra, the competition is generally higher due to limited seats under the All India Quota. For instance, in 2023, the closing ranks for ECE at VJTI showed a clear difference:

| Category (MHT CET) | Closing Rank (2023) |
| :----------------- | :------------------ |
| General (Home State) | 1,471               |
| General (Other State) | 1,136               |

These figures, while based on MHT CET ranks, illustrate the competitive nature for non-domicile students, who generally face higher cutoff requirements compared to local candidates. For JEE Main applicants, a similar heightened level of competition applies.

## Your next steps

1.  **JEE Main Preparation:** Focus intensely on Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics to maximize your JEE Main score. A high score is your best asset.
2.  **Exam and Results:** Ensure you are registered for JEE Main and appear for the examination. After the results are declared, carefully review your scores.
3.  **Counseling Application:** Keep an eye on the official VJTI website and the Directorate of Technical Education (DTE) Maharashtra portal for announcements regarding the counseling process. Apply as instructed, ensuring all eligibility criteria are met.
4.  **Document Preparation:** Gather all necessary documents for verification during the counseling process, as this is a crucial step.

Cutoff percentiles and procedures can change annually based on factors like the total number of applicants, exam difficulty, and seat availability. Therefore, continuous monitoring of official sources is highly recommended for the most current information.
