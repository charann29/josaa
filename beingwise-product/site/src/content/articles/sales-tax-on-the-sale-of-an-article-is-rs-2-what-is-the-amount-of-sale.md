# 4% Sales Tax on an Article is Rs 2: What is the Amount of Sale?

Understanding how sales tax works is crucial for both buyers and sellers. When you know the tax amount and the tax percentage, you can easily figure out the original price of an item before tax was added.

## Key Takeaways

*   Sales tax is a percentage of the selling price of an item.
*   To find the original sale amount, you divide the tax amount by the tax rate (expressed as a decimal).
*   In this specific case, a Rs 2 tax at 4% means the item was originally sold for Rs 50.
*   The final price paid by the customer would be the sale amount plus the tax.

## Understanding Sales Tax Basics

Sales tax is a levy imposed by the government on the sale of goods and services. It's usually calculated as a percentage of the item's selling price. This tax is collected by the seller at the point of sale and then remitted to the appropriate tax authorities.

For example, if an item costs Rs 100 and there's a 5% sales tax, the tax amount would be Rs 5, making the total price Rs 105.

## Calculating the Original Sale Amount

Let's break down how to find the original sale amount when you know the tax paid and the tax rate. We're given that a 4% sales tax on an article amounts to Rs 2.

We can think of this as a simple algebraic equation:

*   Let 'x' represent the unknown original amount of the sale.
*   The sales tax is 4% of 'x'.
*   We know this 4% of 'x' is equal to Rs 2.

So, the equation becomes:

4% of x = Rs 2

To solve for 'x', we first convert the percentage to a decimal or a fraction:

(4 / 100) * x = 2

Now, we isolate 'x' by multiplying both sides by 100 and dividing by 4:

x = (2 * 100) / 4
x = 200 / 4
x = 50

Therefore, the original amount of the sale was Rs 50.

## Verifying Your Calculation

It's always a good idea to double-check your work. If the original sale amount was Rs 50, let's calculate 4% of that amount:

4% of Rs 50 = (4 / 100) * 50
= (4 * 50) / 100
= 200 / 100
= Rs 2

This matches the tax amount given in the problem, confirming our calculation is correct.

## Total Price Paid

While the question asks for the amount of the sale (the price before tax), it's useful to know the total amount a customer would pay. This is simply the original sale amount plus the sales tax.

Total Price = Original Sale Amount + Sales Tax
Total Price = Rs 50 + Rs 2
Total Price = Rs 52

So, if an article has a 4% sales tax that amounts to Rs 2, the original sale price was Rs 50, and the customer would pay a total of Rs 52.

Understanding these calculations helps you make informed decisions, whether you're budgeting for a purchase or managing sales for a business.
