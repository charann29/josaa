# How to Calculate Marks Using JEE Main 2026 Session 2 Answer Key?

Understanding your potential score in JEE Main Session 2 is a crucial step for many students, even before the official results are out. The National Testing Agency (NTA) provides the tools you need to do this: the provisional answer key and your individual response sheet.

## Key takeaways

*   The NTA releases a provisional answer key and individual response sheets for JEE Main Session 2.
*   You'll need both your response sheet and the official answer key, along with the NTA's marking scheme, to calculate your probable score.
*   A specific formula is used: `(4 * Number of Correct Answers) - (1 * Number of Wrong Answers)`.
*   This calculation gives you an estimated score, which can be very helpful for planning your next steps.

## Essential Information for Mark Calculation

To accurately estimate your JEE Main Session 2 score, you'll need three key pieces of information readily available:

*   **The Provisional JEE Main Answer Key:** This document, released by NTA, contains the correct answers to all questions asked in the exam.
*   **Your Individual Response Sheet:** This sheet shows the answers you marked during the examination.
*   **The Official JEE Main Marking Scheme:** This outlines how marks are awarded for correct answers and deducted for incorrect ones.

## Steps to Calculate Your JEE Main 2026 Score

Follow these steps to determine your probable score using the NTA's provisional answer key and your response sheet:

1.  **Download the Answer Key and Your Response Sheet:** Access both documents from the official NTA JEE Main portal. Ensure you have the correct answer key for your specific exam shift and date.
2.  **Compare Your Responses:** Go through each question on your response sheet and compare your marked answer with the correct answer provided in the provisional answer key.
3.  **Note Correct and Incorrect Answers:** Keep a tally of how many questions you answered correctly and how many you answered incorrectly. Questions left unanswered are not penalised.
4.  **Apply the Marking Scheme:** Use the NTA's official marking scheme to calculate your score. For multiple-choice questions (MCQs) with a single correct option and numerical value questions, the standard scheme is:
    *   +4 marks for every correct answer.
    *   -1 mark for every incorrect answer.
    *   0 marks for unanswered questions.

## The JEE Main Marking Scheme in Detail

The NTA's marking scheme is straightforward and applies uniformly across all papers of JEE Main. Here’s a quick overview:

| Action                     | Marks Awarded |
| :------------------------- | :------------ |
| Correct Answer             | +4            |
| Incorrect Answer           | -1            |
| Unattempted Question       | 0             |

This scheme is crucial for the calculation. For example, if you answered 50 questions correctly, 10 questions incorrectly, and left 15 questions unanswered, your score would be `(50 * 4) - (10 * 1) = 200 - 10 = 190`.

## How to Download the JEE Main 2026 Session 2 Answer Key

The provisional answer key for JEE Main Session 2 will be made available online on the official NTA JEE Main website. Here's a general guide on how to access it:

1.  Visit the official JEE Main website.
2.  Look for the link related to the 'JEE Main 2026 Session 2 Provisional Answer Key' or 'Answer Key Challenge'.
3.  Click on the link and you may be required to log in using your application number and date of birth or password.
4.  Once logged in, you should be able to view and download the answer key, along with your individual response sheet.
