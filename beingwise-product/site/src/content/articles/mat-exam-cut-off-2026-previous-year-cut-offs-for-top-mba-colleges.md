# MAT Exam Cut Off 2026, Previous Year Cut Offs for Top MBA Colleges

The Management Aptitude Test (MAT) is a widely accepted entrance examination for MBA and PGDM programs across India. Understanding the cut-offs is crucial for aspiring students to gauge their chances of admission into their desired B-schools. This page provides insights into MAT cut-off trends and what to expect for the 2026 admissions cycle.

## Key Takeaways

*   Top MBA colleges generally require MAT percentiles between 80 and 95.
*   Many private B-schools set their MAT cut-offs in the 70-80 percentile range.
*   A composite score of around 650 in MAT is often considered equivalent to a 90 percentile.
*   Colleges determine cut-offs individually, considering both overall and sometimes sectional scores.

## Understanding MAT Cut-Offs

MAT cut-offs are the minimum percentile scores candidates need to achieve to be considered for admission by a particular institute. These cut-offs are not static; they vary each year and from one college to another. While some institutions look at the overall MAT percentile, others might also consider sectional scores or a composite score for shortlisting candidates.

It's important to remember that the exam mode (CBT or PBT) does not affect the cut-off percentile. Only the score itself is taken into account during the shortlisting process.

## Factors Influencing MAT Cut-Offs

Several elements contribute to how MAT cut-offs are determined by participating colleges. These factors help institutions manage the application volume and select the most suitable candidates. Key determinants include:

*   **Number of Test Takers:** The overall performance and percentile range of all candidates who appeared for the MAT exam.
*   **Applications Received:** The total number of applications an institute receives for its MBA/PGDM programs.
*   **Applicant Score Range:** The typical scores achieved by the pool of applicants for that specific year.
*   **Exam Difficulty:** The general difficulty level of the MAT question paper for a given session.
*   **Available Seats:** The total number of seats offered in the MBA or PGDM program at the institute.
*   **Past Trends:** Historical MAT cut-off data and admission patterns from previous years.
