# How to Become a Human Rights Lawyer - Salary, Qualification, Skills, Role and Responsibilities

A human rights lawyer champions the fundamental rights and dignity of individuals, holding organizations, states, and even companies accountable for violations. This challenging yet rewarding path involves defending those whose basic entitlements, irrespective of background, have been threatened.

## Key takeaways

*   Human rights lawyers advocate for marginalized and vulnerable communities, ensuring justice for dignity violations.
*   The primary educational requirement is a Bachelor of Legislative Law (LLB), often followed by a Master of Legislative Law (LLM).
*   Essential skills include strong research, communication, negotiation, and an unwavering commitment to justice.
*   Salaries for human rights lawyers in India can vary, with an average around ₹66,666.5 per month, depending on experience and the type of organization.

## What Does a Human Rights Lawyer Do?

Human rights lawyers are legal professionals dedicated to protecting and promoting the inherent dignity and rights of all people. They represent individuals and groups who have experienced injustice, focusing on cases that involve civil, political, economic, and social rights. Their work often involves protecting minorities, indigenous populations, the LGBTQI community, and other vulnerable groups.

Their day-to-day responsibilities are diverse and impactful:

*   **Advocacy in Court:** Representing clients in legal proceedings, presenting arguments, and challenging human rights abuses.
*   **Client Interviews:** Meeting with clients to understand their experiences, gather crucial information, and build a strong case.
*   **Legal Research:** Thoroughly investigating laws, precedents, and international human rights conventions to support their arguments.
*   **Document Preparation:** Drafting legal documents, petitions, appeals, and reports essential for court proceedings and advocacy efforts.
*   **Negotiation:** Working to achieve settlements and resolutions outside of court when appropriate, always with the client's best interests at heart.

## How to Become a Human Rights Lawyer in India

To embark on a career as a human rights lawyer, a structured educational path is essential:

### 1. Undergraduate Legal Education

The first step is to obtain a Bachelor of Legislative Law (LLB) degree. This can be pursued in two main ways:

*   **Integrated 5-year LLB:** After completing 10+2, you can enroll in an integrated program like BA LLB, BBA LLB, or B.Sc LLB. Admission to these programs is typically through national-level entrance exams such as CLAT (Common Law Admission Test) or other university-specific tests.
*   **3-year LLB:** If you already hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline, you can opt for a 3-year LLB program. Admission again depends on entrance exams or merit, as determined by individual universities.

### 2. Master's Degree (Optional but Recommended)

While not strictly mandatory, pursuing a Master of Legislative Law (LLM) with a specialization in Human Rights Law, International Law, or Constitutional Law can significantly enhance your expertise and career prospects. An LLM provides deeper theoretical understanding and practical skills in this specialized field.

### 3. Bar Council Enrollment

After completing your LLB, you must enroll with a State Bar Council to practice law in India. This involves passing the All India Bar Examination (AIBE) conducted by the Bar Council of India.

## Essential Skills and Qualities

Succeeding as a human rights lawyer requires a unique blend of intellectual and interpersonal skills:

*   **Strong Research Skills:** The ability to delve into complex legal texts, international treaties, and case law.
*   **Excellent Communication:** Articulating arguments clearly and persuasively, both orally and in writing.
*   **Empathy and Compassion:** A genuine understanding and concern for the suffering of others.
*   **Critical Thinking:** Analyzing situations from multiple perspectives and identifying legal solutions.
*   **Resilience:** The capacity to handle emotionally challenging cases and setbacks.
*   **Negotiation Skills:** Effectively advocating for clients' interests in various settings.
*   **Integrity and Ethics:** Upholding the highest professional standards in all legal dealings.

## Career Prospects and Salary

Human rights lawyers in India can find opportunities in a variety of settings:

*   **Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs):** Working with national and international human rights organizations.
*   **Legal Firms:** Specializing in public interest litigation or pro bono human rights cases.
*   **Government Bodies:** Roles in human rights commissions or legal departments.
*   **International Organizations:** Opportunities with bodies like the United Nations or other international advocacy groups.
*   **Academia:** Teaching and research roles in law schools.

The average salary for a human rights lawyer in India is approximately ₹66,666.5 per month. However, this figure can vary widely based on factors such as experience, the employer (e.g., NGO vs. private firm), location, and the scale of cases handled. Entry-level positions might start lower, while experienced lawyers in prominent roles can earn significantly more.

Becoming a human rights lawyer is a commitment to justice, offering a fulfilling career for those passionate about making a tangible difference in society.
