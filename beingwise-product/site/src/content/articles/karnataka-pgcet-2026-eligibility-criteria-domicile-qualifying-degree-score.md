# Karnataka PGCET 2026 Eligibility Criteria: Domicile, Qualifying Degree, Score

To pursue postgraduate courses like MBA, MCA, M.Tech, or M.Arch in Karnataka through the Karnataka PGCET, understanding the eligibility requirements is crucial. The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) sets these criteria, which are detailed in the official information brochure.

## Key Takeaways

*   The KEA defines all eligibility conditions for Karnataka PGCET, covering academic qualifications, minimum scores, and domicile.
*   A bachelor's degree of at least three years from a recognized institution is generally required for most courses.
*   General category candidates typically need a minimum of 50% aggregate marks, while reserved categories receive a 5% relaxation, requiring 45%.
*   Domicile status in Karnataka is essential for claiming government seats and associated reservations.

## Understanding the Basics of Eligibility

The Karnataka PGCET eligibility conditions are comprehensive and cover several key aspects. Before you apply, it's vital to ensure you meet all these requirements. The official KEA website, cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea, is where you can find the detailed information brochure containing these criteria. The main parameters include:

*   Your qualifying degree and its duration.
*   The minimum aggregate score you achieved in your qualifying examination.
*   Any specific work experience requirements for certain programs (though less common for full-time MBA).
*   Your domicile status, which is particularly relevant for reservation benefits.

## Qualifying Degree Requirements

For most full-time and part-time MBA programs accessible via Karnataka PGCET, the primary academic requirement is a bachelor's degree. This degree must meet a few specific conditions:

*   **Duration:** Your bachelor's degree course must have been of at least three years' duration or its equivalent.
*   **Recognition:** The degree must have been awarded by an institution recognized by the appropriate authorities.
*   **Stream:** Generally, candidates from any academic stream (Arts, Science, Commerce, Engineering, etc.) are eligible to apply for an MBA, provided they meet other criteria.

## Minimum Score in the Qualifying Exam

Beyond just holding a degree, candidates must also achieve a certain minimum aggregate score in their bachelor's program. This score is calculated based on all subjects, including language subjects, across all years of the degree course.

*   **General Category:** Candidates belonging to the unreserved categories must have secured a minimum of 50% aggregate marks.
*   **Reserved Categories:** A concession of 5% is provided for candidates from reserved categories, including Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and Category 1 of Karnataka candidates. These candidates need a minimum aggregate score of 45%.

## Domicile Requirements and Reservation

Domicile status plays a significant role, especially for candidates seeking admission to government seats. The KEA reserves these seats for candidates who hold Karnataka state domicile. This means that to avail of reservation benefits, you generally need to be a resident of Karnataka as per the official rules. However, it's important to note that certain specific clauses can sometimes override the standard domicile requirement, and these would be detailed in the official information brochure.

Meeting these eligibility criteria is the first step towards your postgraduate education journey in Karnataka. Always refer to the latest information brochure released by the KEA for the most accurate and updated details.
