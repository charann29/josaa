The Xavier Aptitude Test, or XAT, is a national-level entrance examination for admission to MBA and PGDM programs across numerous prestigious business schools in India. Conducted by XLRI Jamshedpur, XAT is known for its unique structure and challenging questions, making it a significant test for aspiring management students.

## Key takeaways

*   XAT is held annually on the first Sunday of January, with the application process typically starting in July.
*   It assesses candidates across sections like Decision Making, Verbal & Logical Ability, Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation, and General Knowledge.
*   Over 250 B-schools, including XLRI and other XAMI member institutions, accept XAT scores for their management programs.
*   The exam features 95 multiple-choice questions and has a duration of three hours.

## XAT Exam Dates and Schedule

The XAT examination is consistently scheduled for the first Sunday of January each year. For XAT 2027, the exam date is January 3rd, 2027. Prospective candidates can expect the official notification and application forms to be released around July 2026, as per historical trends. The official XAT website, xatonline.in, will provide all detailed information, including the syllabus PDF and exam overview, once the notification is published.

## XAT Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The XAT exam is an online, computer-based test with a duration of three hours. It consists of 95 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) designed to evaluate various skills essential for management studies. There are no subjective or essay-type questions in XAT. The exam is divided into four main sections:

*   **Verbal & Logical Ability:** This section tests comprehension, grammar, vocabulary, and logical reasoning.
*   **Decision Making:** A unique section to XAT, it assesses candidates' ability to make sound judgments in complex scenarios.
*   **Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation:** This part covers mathematics topics from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and modern math, along with interpreting data presented in various formats.
*   **General Knowledge:** Questions in this section cover current affairs, business, economics, and static general knowledge. Scores from this section are typically not included in the overall percentile calculation for admission to most programs but are considered by some institutes.

## XAT Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the XAT exam, candidates must hold a Bachelor's degree of at least three years' duration in any discipline from a recognized university. Students in their final year of a Bachelor's degree program are also eligible to apply, provided they complete their graduation by a specific date, which will be mentioned in the official XAT notification. There are no specific age limits for appearing in the XAT exam.

## XAT Cutoffs and Participating Colleges

Over 250 business schools across India and abroad accept XAT scores for admission to their MBA/PGDM programs. XLRI Jamshedpur, the conducting body, is one of the most sought-after institutions. For XLRI's PGDM-BM program, the cutoff percentiles are typically high, often around 95 percentile for male candidates and 92 percentile for female candidates. Other XAMI member colleges and numerous other participating institutes also set their own cutoffs, which vary based on the program and the specific institute's requirements. Candidates should refer to the individual institute's admission criteria for detailed cutoff information.

Preparing thoroughly for XAT involves understanding its unique sections, practicing with past papers, and taking mock tests to familiarize yourself with the exam environment.
