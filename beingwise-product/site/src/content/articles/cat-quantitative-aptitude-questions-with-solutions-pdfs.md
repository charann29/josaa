# CAT Quantitative Aptitude Questions with Solutions PDFs

To truly excel in the Quantitative Aptitude (QA) section of the CAT exam, dedicated practice with solved questions is essential. This page offers valuable resources, including downloadable PDFs, to help you master CAT Quant problems.

## Key takeaways

*   **Practice is Paramount:** Simply knowing formulas isn't enough; applying them accurately and quickly requires extensive practice.
*   **Comprehensive Syllabus Coverage:** CAT QA covers advanced topics from Class 9 and 10 mathematics, including Algebra, Geometry, Trigonometry, Mensuration, and Modern Math.
*   **Strategic Preparation:** Divide your preparation into two phases: first, thoroughly understanding concepts, and second, rigorously practicing questions and mock tests.
*   **High Difficulty Level:** CAT QA questions are often challenging, time-consuming, and calculation-intensive, demanding both conceptual clarity and efficient problem-solving skills.

## Why Practice CAT Quant Questions?

While understanding the theoretical concepts and formulas is the foundation, it's through consistent practice that you develop the speed and accuracy needed for the CAT exam. The Quantitative Aptitude section of CAT is known for its high difficulty, often featuring complex and calculation-heavy problems. Practicing a wide variety of questions helps you become familiar with different question types, identify common pitfalls, and refine your approach to problem-solving. It's not about solving every single question in the exam, but about maximizing your score by efficiently tackling the ones you can.

## Structuring Your CAT Quant Preparation

Effective preparation for CAT Quantitative Aptitude can be broken down into two critical stages:

1.  **Conceptual Clarity:** Begin by thoroughly understanding each topic within the CAT QA syllabus. This involves going back to basics, grasping underlying principles, and knowing the relevant formulas and theorems. Since CAT Quant is highly calculation-intensive, a strong conceptual base is non-negotiable.
2.  **Extensive Practice:** Once your concepts are clear, move on to solving a large number of practice questions. Start with basic problems and gradually move to more complex ones. Focus on improving your efficiency in applying formulas and achieving 100% accuracy. Aim to solve over 5000 CAT Quant questions throughout your preparation journey.

## Key Topics and Their Importance

The CAT Quantitative Aptitude syllabus broadly covers topics from high school mathematics, but at an advanced level. While all topics are important, some, like Algebra and Geometry, often feature questions of higher difficulty and require more dedicated practice. Percentage, Time and Distance, Number Systems, and other arithmetic topics are also crucial. A balanced approach to covering all areas, ensuring you practice various question types from each, will be beneficial.

## Benefits for Other MBA Entrance Exams

Preparing thoroughly for CAT Quantitative Aptitude offers a significant advantage for other MBA entrance exams as well. The depth and breadth of CAT Quant preparation often cover the syllabi for exams like XAT, SNAP, CMAT, NMAT, and MAT, making your efforts highly efficient across multiple tests.

### CAT Percentage Questions

**DIRECTIONS for questions 1 to 5: Answer the question independently.**

1.  A dishonest shopkeeper sells sugar at cost price but makes 25% profit by using faulty weights. He decides to give a discount to attract more customers. What percent of discount should he offer to make 12% profit?
    1] 15.2%
    2] 12.5%
    3] 10.4%
    4] 13%

2.  N is a natural number having the sum of its digits as 3. If 10^13 < N < 10^14, then how many values can ‘N’ assume?
    1] 93
    2] 91
    3] 78
    4] 105

3.  Two barrels A and C contain alcohol and water in the ratio 1:a and 1:c, respectively. Equal quantity of mixtures from barrels A and C are mixed to get a mixture with alcohol and water in the ratio 1:b, where b is the arithmetic mean of a and c. What can be the value of a, b, c, if a, b and c are integers?
    1] 14
    2] 10
    3] 15
    4] 8

4.  Given 2 positive integers, ‘x’ and ‘y’, such that their arithmetic mean is 12 and their geometric mean is 10. What is the value of x^2 + y^2?
    1] 288
    2] 324
    3] 144
    4] 192

5.  A sum of money, P, is invested at a certain simple interest rate. It grows to 2P in 5 years. In how many years will it grow to 4P at the same rate?
    1] 10 years
    2] 15 years
    3] 20 years
    4] 25 years
