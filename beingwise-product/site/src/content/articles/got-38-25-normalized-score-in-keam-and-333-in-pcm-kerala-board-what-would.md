# I got 38.25 normalized score in KEAM and 333 in PCM (Kerala Board). What would be my rank?

Understanding your potential rank in KEAM with a normalized score of 38.25 and 333/480 in your Kerala Board PCM subjects is a common concern for many students. While an exact rank can only be determined by the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE) Kerala, we can look at past trends to give you a general idea.

## Key takeaways

*   Your KEAM normalized score and PCM marks are combined to calculate your final rank.
*   Based on previous years' data, a rank above 50,000 is a possibility with these scores.
*   Top government engineering colleges might be challenging to secure with this rank.
*   Opportunities in private or self-financing engineering colleges are more likely.

## How KEAM Ranks are Calculated

The KEAM ranking system is a crucial aspect of the admission process for engineering courses in Kerala. It combines two main components: your performance in the KEAM entrance examination and your marks in the qualifying higher secondary examination (Class 12). Specifically, 50% weightage is given to your normalized KEAM score, and the remaining 50% is allocated to your marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) from your Class 12 board exams. These two scores are then combined to generate a final merit list and your individual rank.

## Understanding Your Scores

Your normalized KEAM score of 38.25 is one half of the equation. The other half comes from your Kerala Board PCM marks, which are 333 out of a possible 480. These marks are then converted to a percentage or a standardized score to be combined with your KEAM score. It's important to remember that normalization accounts for variations in question paper difficulty across different KEAM sessions, ensuring fairness in the scoring process.

## Predicted Rank Range

Based on an analysis of previous years' KEAM results and admission trends, a combined score derived from a normalized KEAM score of 38.25 and 333 in PCM from the Kerala Board typically places candidates in a higher rank bracket. Historically, scores in this range have corresponded to ranks that are likely above 50,000. It's crucial to understand that this is an estimation, as the actual rank list is influenced by the performance of all candidates in the current year.

## Impact on College Admissions

A rank above 50,000 generally means that securing a seat in highly sought-after government engineering colleges, such as the Government Engineering College, Thrissur, or College of Engineering, Trivandrum, might be difficult. These institutions usually have very competitive cut-offs. However, this does not mean that all doors are closed. Many reputable private engineering colleges and self-financing institutions across Kerala offer a wide range of engineering programs and often have cut-offs that are more accessible for ranks in this range. Exploring these options during the counseling process will be key.

## What to do next

The official KEAM rank list is expected to be released on July 10, 2025. Once the CEE Kerala publishes this list, you will be able to check your precise rank. This exact rank will be your definitive guide for participating in the KEAM counselling process and making informed decisions about college and course preferences. Keep an eye on the official CEE Kerala announcements for the latest updates.
