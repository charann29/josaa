# Your Chances at MDI Gurgaon and IIM Shillong with a 98.2 CAT Percentile

Scoring a 98.2 percentile overall in the CAT is a fantastic achievement, opening doors to many top-tier management programs. Let's explore your prospects for securing an interview call from MDI Gurgaon and IIM Shillong with your specific sectional scores.

## Key Takeaways

*   An overall CAT percentile of 98.2, especially with strong sectional scores, gives you a good chance for interview calls from IIM Shillong and MDI Gurgaon.
*   Admission decisions are holistic, considering not just CAT scores but also academic background, work experience, and diversity factors.
*   Your performance in the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds is crucial for final selection.
*   Each institute has its own unique selection criteria and weightages for different components.

## Understanding the Selection Process

Both IIM Shillong and MDI Gurgaon employ a comprehensive selection process that extends beyond just your CAT percentile. While a high CAT score is essential for shortlisting, it's merely the first hurdle. Institutes consider a candidate's complete profile to ensure a diverse and well-rounded batch.

### IIM Shillong's Approach

IIM Shillong typically shortlists candidates for the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds based on their CAT percentile, along with academic performance in Class 10, Class 12, and graduation. They also factor in work experience, if any, and aim for academic and gender diversity. Your sectional percentiles, especially your strong Quant and DILR scores, will be viewed favorably. For final selection, IIM Shillong calculates a composite score, giving weightage to your CAT performance, WAT/PI performance, and profile elements.

### MDI Gurgaon's Approach

MDI Gurgaon also follows a multi-stage selection process. Candidates are first shortlisted for the Group Discussion (GD), Written Ability Test (WAT), and Personal Interview (PI) rounds. Similar to IIM Shillong, MDI considers CAT scores, academic records (Class 10, Class 12, graduation), relevant work experience, and diversity. Your strong overall and sectional CAT scores position you well for this initial shortlisting. The final merit list is prepared based on a composite score derived from your CAT percentile, GD/WAT/PI performance, and other profile details.

## Your Sectional Scores Matter

Your CAT sectional percentiles are: VA 76.6, DILR 98.2, and Quant 99.2. While your Verbal Ability score is lower than your other sections, your exceptionally strong DILR and Quant scores significantly boost your overall profile. Many institutes have sectional cut-offs, and while your VA score might be on the lower side for some very specific programs, your overall high percentile and other strong sections should compensate for it in most cases for MDI and IIM Shillong.

## Beyond the CAT: The WATPI Round

Receiving an interview call is a significant step, but the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds are where you truly differentiate yourself. These rounds assess your communication skills, critical thinking, problem-solving abilities, and overall personality. Prepare thoroughly by practicing current affairs, general knowledge, and common interview questions. Be ready to articulate your career goals, strengths, weaknesses, and reasons for pursuing an MBA.

With your strong CAT percentile and well-rounded profile, you have a solid chance of receiving interview calls from both MDI Gurgaon and IIM Shillong. Focus on preparing diligently for the subsequent selection rounds to convert these opportunities into admissions.
