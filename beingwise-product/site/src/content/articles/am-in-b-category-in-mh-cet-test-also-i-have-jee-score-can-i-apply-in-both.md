# I am in B category in MH CET test. Also I have JEE score. Can I apply in both category ? If yes which one will be cosidered for admission process and seat allocation ?

Navigating engineering admissions in Maharashtra can feel complex, especially when you have scores from both the MH CET and JEE Main exams. Understanding how these scores are considered for admission is crucial for making the best application choices.

## Key Takeaways

*   Most engineering colleges in Maharashtra accept both MH CET and JEE Main scores for admission, but often through different quota systems.
*   You typically apply under one specific admission channel (e.g., State Quota via MH CET or All India Quota via JEE Main) for a particular seat.
*   The stronger of your two scores (MH CET or JEE Main) should guide your application strategy, after reviewing previous years' cutoffs.
*   Always consult the specific college's admission brochure or the official State Common Entrance Test Cell (CET Cell) notifications for precise rules.

## Understanding MH CET and JEE Main Admissions

Engineering colleges in Maharashtra generally have distinct admission pathways. The primary route for domicile candidates is through the MH CET exam, which grants access to the State Quota seats. For students, including those from outside Maharashtra, the JEE Main score is often used for the All India Quota (AIQ) seats.

While you possess both scores, the application process usually requires you to choose which score to use for a particular application or quota. It's not typically a scenario where you "apply in both categories" for the same seat and then the system picks the better one. Instead, you might apply for different types of seats using different scores.

## How Your "B Category" Relates to Admissions

Your mention of being in "B category" for the MH CET test likely refers to a specific type of seat allocation or reservation within the MH CET framework. This could pertain to various categories such as Backward Class, Economically Weaker Section (EWS), or other specific state-defined reservations. These categories are primarily relevant for the State Quota seats filled through MH CET. When applying via JEE Main for All India Quota seats, these state-specific categories might not directly apply, as AIQ has its own reservation policies (like EWS, OBC-NCL, SC, ST as per central government norms).

## Deciding Which Score to Use

To make an informed decision, it's highly recommended to compare the previous year's cutoffs for both MH CET and JEE Main for the colleges and courses you are interested in. Many institutions publish these cutoffs on their official websites or they can be found in the official MH CET counselling information brochures.

*   **If your MH CET score is strong:** Focus on applying for State Quota seats where your B category might provide an advantage.
*   **If your JEE Main score is strong:** Consider applying for All India Quota seats. Remember that AIQ competition is nationwide.
*   **If both scores are competitive:** You might apply through both channels for different colleges or even different programs within the same college, depending on their specific rules and seat availability. However, for a single seat, you will typically be considered based on one score through one specific application.

## Official Sources and Guidance

Always refer to the official admission brochure of the specific college you are targeting. These documents provide detailed information on eligibility criteria, the weightage given to different entrance exams, and the application process for various quotas. Additionally, the official website of the State Common Entrance Test Cell (CET Cell) for Maharashtra will publish detailed information and rules regarding MH CET counselling and seat allocation.

It's a good strategy to contact the admissions office of your preferred colleges directly. They can provide precise guidance on how to best utilize both your MH CET and JEE Main scores given your specific profile.

Making the right choice can significantly impact your admission chances, so take the time to research thoroughly and understand the nuances of each admission pathway.
