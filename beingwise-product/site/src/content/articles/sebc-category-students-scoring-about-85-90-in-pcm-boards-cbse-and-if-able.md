# For SEBC Category Students: DAIICT ICT Branch Chances with 85-90% PCM and 100+ GUJCET

Many students dream of joining the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) program at DA-IICT (Dhirubhai Ambani Institute of Information and Communication Technology), Gandhinagar. If you're an SEBC category student with 85-90% in your PCM board exams and aiming for over 100 in GUJCET, let's explore your prospects.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your admission to DA-IICT's ICT program is a blend of your GUJCET performance, board marks, and reservation category.
*   Scoring 100+ in GUJCET combined with 85-90% in PCM boards, particularly for SEBC candidates, presents a reasonable chance.
*   Cutoffs are dynamic and shift annually based on various factors, so past trends are guides, not guarantees.
*   Thorough preparation for GUJCET is crucial, as is understanding the counselling process.

## Understanding the DA-IICT Admission Process

DA-IICT primarily admits students to its undergraduate programs through two main channels: the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main and the Gujarat Common Entrance Test (GUJCET). For students aiming for admission via GUJCET, your performance in this state-level examination, combined with your Class 12 PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) board scores, forms the basis of your merit rank.

## The Role of GUJCET Score and Board Marks

Your GUJCET score is a significant component of your overall admission index for DA-IICT through the GUJCET route. While a score of 100+ in GUJCET is commendable and provides a strong foundation, it's weighted alongside your Class 12 PCM percentage. Typically, a specific formula combines these two scores to generate a merit rank. An 85-90% in PCM boards is a strong academic record that complements a good GUJCET score, enhancing your overall competitive standing.

## Impact of SEBC Category Reservation

As an SEBC (Socially and Educationally Backward Classes) category student, you benefit from reservation policies implemented by the Gujarat government. These reservations mean that a certain percentage of seats are earmarked for candidates from your category, potentially lowering the effective cutoff rank for admission compared to the general category. This is a significant advantage, and it's essential to ensure all your category documents are in order for verification during counselling.

## Navigating Cutoffs and Counselling

Admission cutoffs for DA-IICT's ICT branch, like any prestigious institution, fluctuate each year. These variations depend on factors such as the total number of applicants, the difficulty level of the GUJCET exam, the overall performance of candidates, and the number of available seats. While historical data can offer insights into past trends, it's crucial to remember that these are not definitive for the current year. During the counselling rounds, seats are allotted based on merit rank, category, and choices filled. It's advisable to participate actively in all counselling rounds, as cutoffs can sometimes adjust in later stages.

By focusing on a strong GUJCET performance and maintaining excellent board marks, you are building a solid application for DA-IICT's sought-after ICT program, especially with the advantage of your SEBC category.
