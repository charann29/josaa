Welcome to your guide for the Punjab School Education Board (PSEB) Class 10th and 12th examinations for 2026. This page provides essential details on the examination schedule, revised patterns, syllabus, and how to access important documents.

## Key Takeaways

*   The PSEB Class 10th exams for 2026 were conducted from March 6 to April 1, and Class 12th exams concluded on April 4.
*   The official date sheet for both Class 10th and 12th was released on December 30, 2025.
*   Students need to achieve a minimum of 33% marks in each subject (theory, internal assessment, and practicals) to pass the board exams.
*   The exam pattern has been revised, including changes to objective question weighting and question sourcing.

## PSEB 10th & 12th Important Dates 2026

The Punjab Board released the date sheet for Class 10 and 12 on December 30, 2025, through its official channels. The examinations were conducted across February and April 2026.

Here's a quick overview of the key dates:

| Event                          | Dates                     |
| :----------------------------- | :------------------------ |
| PSEB 10th Exam Dates           | March 6 – April 1, 2026   |
| PSEB 12th Exam Dates           | February 17 – April 4, 2026 |
| PSEB 10th Result Date (Tentative) | May 2026                  |
| PSEB 12th Result Date (Tentative) | May 2026                  |

## PSEB Admit Card 2026

For the 2026 examinations, the PSEB Class 10th and 12th admit cards were made available to schools in February 2026. Students were required to collect their admit cards directly from their respective schools. It's crucial for all students to carry their admit card to the examination hall on every exam day, as entry will not be permitted without it.

## PSEB Syllabus 2026

The Punjab Board published the comprehensive syllabus for both Class 10th and 12th on its official website. Familiarizing yourself with the syllabus is a fundamental step for effective exam preparation. It outlines all the chapters and topics that will be covered in the examination for each subject, ensuring students know exactly what to study to perform well.

## PSEB Exam Pattern 2026 (Revised)

The Punjab Board introduced significant revisions to the Class 10th and 12th exam patterns for 2026. These changes aim to encourage a deeper understanding of subjects and include:

*   **Reduction in Objective Questions:** The proportion of objective-type questions (Multiple Choice Questions) has been reduced from 40% to 25% of the total marks.
*   **Questions from Within Chapters:** Previously, questions were primarily drawn from end-of-chapter exercises. Now, at least 25% of the questions will originate from the content *within* the textbook chapters, with the remaining 75% still coming from exercises. This change requires students to engage with the entire chapter in detail, not just the summary or exercise sections.
*   **Increased Difficulty Level:** The distribution of question difficulty has been adjusted. While specific percentages for easy, average, and difficult questions were previously set, the new pattern indicates an overall increase in the challenge level, demanding more rigorous preparation from students.

These revisions highlight the board's emphasis on conceptual understanding and analytical skills. Students are advised to thoroughly review the revised pattern available on the official PSEB portal to align their study strategies accordingly.
