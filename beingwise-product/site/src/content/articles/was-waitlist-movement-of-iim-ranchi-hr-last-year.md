## Understanding Waitlist Movement

When you receive a waitlist offer from an IIM, it means you've met the admission criteria but haven't been selected in the initial round. Your position on the waitlist determines your chances. As candidates decline their initial offers to join other programs or institutions, those on the waitlist move up. This process continues through several rounds until all seats are filled.

## Factors Influencing Waitlist Movement

Several elements can influence how much a waitlist moves:

*   **Candidate Preferences:** Students often apply to multiple IIMs and other top B-schools. Their final decision to accept an offer at one institution directly impacts seat availability at others.
*   **Batch Size:** The total number of seats available for the HR program each year affects how many candidates can eventually be admitted.
*   **Economic Conditions:** Broader economic trends can sometimes influence students' decisions about pursuing higher education immediately.
