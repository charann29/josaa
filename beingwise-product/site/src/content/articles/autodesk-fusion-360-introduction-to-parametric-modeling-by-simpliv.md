# Autodesk Fusion 360 Introduction to Parametric Modeling by Simpliv Learning: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For engineering students, designers, and anyone interested in 3D modeling, understanding parametric design principles is crucial. This particular course focuses on Autodesk Fusion 360, a popular tool for product development, offering a comprehensive introduction to its features and functionalities.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn to navigate the Autodesk Fusion 360 interface and master 2D sketching with constraints and dimensions.
*   Gain proficiency in creating, editing, and manipulating 3D solid features and T-spline geometry.
*   Understand parametric design by working with equations, parameters, and feature history.
*   Explore multi-component design, including assembly, motion definition, and design documentation through drawings.

## Course Overview and Learning Format

This program is designed to introduce learners to the core concepts of parametric modeling using Autodesk Fusion 360. It aims to equip participants with the skills needed to create and manage complex 3D designs. The course is delivered in English, utilizing a self-study and virtual classroom approach. Learning materials are provided through a combination of video and text-based content, allowing for flexible learning at your own pace.

## Detailed Syllabus

The curriculum covers a wide array of topics, ensuring a thorough understanding of Fusion 360's capabilities. Here’s a breakdown of what you will learn:

*   **Interface Mastery:** Getting comfortable with the Autodesk Fusion 360 environment.
*   **2D Sketching Fundamentals:** Creating, constraining, and dimensioning accurate 2D sketches.
*   **3D Solid Modeling:** Generating and modifying solid 3D features.
*   **Construction Features:** Utilizing construction geometry to aid in design.
*   **Parametric Control:** Implementing equations and working with parameters for adaptable designs.
*   **Design History Management:** Manipulating the feature history of a design for revisions.
*   **Geometry Duplication:** Efficiently replicating design elements.
*   **Assembly Design:** Placing, constraining, and connecting components within a single design file.
*   **Motion Studies:** Defining movement in multi-component designs.
*   **Advanced Multi-Component Design:** Creating features and components within complex assemblies.
*   **T-spline Modeling:** Designing organic shapes using T-spline geometry.
*   **Technical Documentation:** Creating drawings to document your designs.
*   **Basic Simulation:** Defining structural constraints and loads for static analysis.
