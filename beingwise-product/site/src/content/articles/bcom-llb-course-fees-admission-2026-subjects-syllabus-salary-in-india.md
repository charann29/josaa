BCom LLB is an integrated undergraduate program that combines core commerce subjects with legal studies. This five-year course is ideal for students aiming for careers that bridge the gap between business and law, offering a strong foundation in both disciplines.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Integrated Learning:** BCom LLB combines a Bachelor of Commerce and a Bachelor of Legislative Law, offering a comprehensive understanding of both business principles and legal frameworks.
*   **Duration:** This is a five-year full-time undergraduate program.
*   **Admission Pathways:** Entry is typically based on Class 12 scores, but many prominent institutions also require performance in national or state-level entrance exams like CLAT, CUET, or MHCET Law.
*   **Career Versatility:** Graduates can pursue diverse roles in corporate law, legal advisory, taxation, and legal management across various sectors.

## BCom LLB Eligibility and Admission 2026

To be eligible for a BCom LLB program, candidates generally need to have completed their Class 12 (or equivalent) examination from a recognized board. While some colleges may prefer or require a commerce background, many accept students from any stream.

Minimum percentage requirements in Class 12 vary by institution, often around 50% for the general category. Reserved category candidates typically have slightly relaxed criteria. Some universities might also stipulate an upper age limit for applicants.

Admission processes differ across colleges:

*   **Merit-Based:** Many institutions offer admission based solely on Class 12 academic performance.
*   **Entrance Exam-Based:** Top law schools and universities often require candidates to clear specific entrance examinations. These can include:
    *   **CLAT (Common Law Admission Test):** For admissions to National Law Universities (NLUs) and several other private law colleges.
    *   **CUET (Common University Entrance Test):** Used by various central and state universities for undergraduate admissions.
    *   **MHCET Law:** For colleges in Maharashtra.
    *   **APLAWCET:** For colleges in Andhra Pradesh.

It is advisable to check the specific admission criteria and accepted entrance exams for each college you are interested in, as these can vary significantly.

## BCom LLB Course Fees

The tuition fees for a BCom LLB program in India can vary widely depending on the type of institution (government or private), its reputation, and facilities. Generally, course fees can range from approximately INR 35,000 to over INR 30 lakh for the entire five-year duration. National Law Universities and top private institutions usually have higher fee structures compared to state universities or other private colleges.

## BCom LLB Subjects and Syllabus

The BCom LLB curriculum is designed to provide a strong foundation in both commerce and law. Over the five years, students cover a wide array of subjects. While the exact syllabus may vary by university, common subjects include:

### Commerce Subjects

*   Financial Accounting
*   Business Economics
*   Business Management
*   Corporate Accounting
*   Taxation Law and Practice
*   Auditing

### Law Subjects

*   Constitutional Law
*   Family Law
*   Criminal Law
*   Contract Law
*   Corporate Law
*   Property Law
*   Environmental Law
*   Jurisprudence
*   Legal Ethics
*   International Law
*   Civil Procedure Code
*   Criminal Procedure Code

### Interdisciplinary Subjects

*   Legal English and Communication Skills
*   Business Ethics
*   Moot Court and Internship
*   Research Methodology

Practical training, including moot courts, internships, and legal aid clinics, is an integral part of the curriculum, offering students hands-on experience in legal practice.

## Career Scope and Salary in India

A BCom LLB degree opens up a diverse range of career opportunities in both the legal and corporate sectors. Graduates are well-equipped for roles that require a blend of business acumen and legal expertise. Some common career paths include:

*   **Advocate/Lawyer:** Practicing in various courts or specializing in areas like corporate law, taxation law, or intellectual property law.
*   **Legal Advisor:** Providing legal counsel to corporations, individuals, or government bodies.
*   **Solicitor:** Handling legal documentation, transactions, and advising clients outside of court.
*   **Legal Manager/Officer:** Working in the legal department of companies, ensuring compliance with laws and managing legal risks.
*   **Company Secretary:** Assisting in corporate governance and regulatory compliance.
*   **Tax Consultant:** Advising individuals and businesses on tax matters.

Leading recruiters for BCom LLB graduates often include top law firms such as JSA Advocates & Solicitors, Khaitan & Co., Luthra & Luthra Law Offices India, and Saraf & Partners Law Offices, as well as corporate legal departments and financial institutions.

The average starting salary for BCom LLB graduates in India can range from INR 3.5 LPA to INR 8 LPA, depending on the institution, individual skills, and the recruiting organization. With experience and specialization, salaries can significantly increase.

This integrated degree provides a solid foundation for a dynamic career at the intersection of commerce and law.
