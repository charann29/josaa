# What is the Syllabus for MHT CET 2021?

The MHT CET (Maharashtra Health and Technical Common Entrance Test) is a crucial entrance examination for students aspiring to pursue professional courses in engineering, pharmacy, and agriculture in Maharashtra. Understanding the syllabus is the first step towards effective preparation.

## Key Takeaways

*   The MHT CET syllabus primarily aligns with the Maharashtra State Board's curriculum for Classes 11 and 12.
*   It covers Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM) and Biology (PCB) for different streams.
*   While there were discussions about syllabus changes due to the pandemic, the exam generally follows the standard curriculum.
*   Always refer to the official MHT CET website for the most current and detailed syllabus information.

## Understanding the MHT CET Syllabus Structure

The MHT CET is conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Government of Maharashtra. The syllabus for the examination is typically based on the Maharashtra State Board's curriculum for the 11th and 12th standards. This means that students should focus on the topics covered in their junior college education.

## Subject-Wise Syllabus Overview

Here’s a general breakdown of the subjects and key topics usually included in the MHT CET:

### Physics

Candidates should prepare topics such as Circular Motion, Gravitation, Rotational Motion, Oscillations, Elasticity, Surface Tension, Wave Motion, Stationary Waves, Kinetic Theory of Gases, Wave Theory of Light, Interference and Diffraction, Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetic Effects of Electric Current, Magnetism, Electromagnetic Induction, Electrons and Photons, Atoms, Molecules and Nuclei, Semiconductors, and Communication Systems.

### Chemistry

The Chemistry section typically includes Solid State, Solutions and Colligative Properties, Chemical Thermodynamics and Energetics, Electrochemistry, Chemical Kinetics, General Principles and Processes of Isolation of Elements, p-Block Elements, d and f Block Elements, Coordination Compounds, Halogen Derivatives of Alkanes and Arenes, Alcohols, Phenols and Ethers, Aldehydes, Ketones and Carboxylic Acids, Organic Compounds Containing Nitrogen, Biomolecules, Polymers, and Chemistry in Everyday Life.

### Mathematics

For Mathematics, students should focus on Logic, Matrices, Trigonometric Functions, Pair of Straight Lines, Circle, Conics, Vectors, Three-Dimensional Geometry, Line, Plane, Linear Programming Problems, Continuity, Differentiation, Application of Derivatives, Integration, Applications of Definite Integral, Differential Equations, Statistics, Probability Distribution, and Bernoulli Trials and Binomial Distribution.

### Biology

The Biology syllabus is divided into Botany and Zoology sections:

*   **Botany:** Key topics include Genetic Basis of Inheritance, Gene: its Nature, Expression and Regulation, Biotechnology: Process and Application, Enhancement in Food Production, Microbes in Human Welfare, Photosynthesis, Respiration, Reproduction in Plants, and Organisms and Environment - I.
*   **Zoology:** Important areas cover Origin and Evolution of Life, Chromosomal Basis of Inheritance, Genetic Engineering and Genomics, Human Health and Diseases, Animal Husbandry, Circulation, Excretion and Osmoregulation, Control and Co-ordination, Human Reproduction, and Organisms and Environment.

## Importance of Official Sources

While this overview provides a good starting point, it is crucial for all aspirants to consult the official MHT CET website (mahacet.org) for the most accurate and up-to-date syllabus information. Any official changes or updates, particularly concerning the weightage of topics or specific exclusions, will always be published there. Sticking to NCERT books and the 12th standard syllabus for relevant subjects is generally recommended for robust preparation.
