# Is Stamp Mandatory on AP EAMCET Application?

Navigating the application process for competitive exams like the AP EAMCET (now officially known as AP EAPCET) can bring up many questions, especially regarding documentation. A common concern among students and parents is whether a physical stamp, particularly from a school principal, is required on the application form.

## Key Takeaways

*   The AP EAPCET application process is entirely online, meaning physical stamps or signatures on the form are generally not needed.
*   You will submit all required documents, including your photograph and signature, electronically as scanned uploads.
*   Always refer to the official AP EAPCET information bulletin for the most accurate and up-to-date instructions on application requirements.
*   No physical submission of the application form is necessary; the entire process is completed online.

## The Online Application Process

The Andhra Pradesh Engineering, Agriculture and Pharmacy Common Entrance Test (AP EAPCET) application is designed to be fully digital. This means you will complete all steps, from filling out your personal details to uploading documents and submitting the form, through the official AP EAPCET website. This online-only approach streamlines the process and eliminates the need for any physical paperwork to be mailed or hand-delivered.

## No Requirement for Principal's Signature or School Stamp

One of the biggest reliefs for many applicants is that the AP EAPCET application typically does not require a physical signature from your school principal or an official school stamp. Unlike some other entrance exams that might still follow traditional attestation methods, the AP EAPCET system is built for digital verification. This saves you the hassle of coordinating with your school administration specifically for the application form.

## Essential Documents for Online Upload

While a physical stamp isn't required, you will need to prepare several documents for digital upload. These typically include:

*   A recent passport-sized photograph (scanned copy).
*   Your scanned signature.
*   Identity proof, such as your Aadhaar card.
*   Your Class 10 and Class 12 hall ticket numbers.
*   Category certificates (e.g., SC/ST/BC) if you are applying under a reservation quota.
*   Income certificate, if applicable.

Ensure that all scanned documents meet the specifications (file size, format, dimensions) mentioned in the official AP EAPCET information bulletin to avoid any issues during upload.

## Verifying Official Guidelines

It is crucial to emphasize that while generally no stamp is required, applicants must *always* consult the official AP EAPCET information bulletin for the specific year they are applying. This official document, released by the conducting authority, is the definitive source of all rules and requirements. Any changes or special instructions regarding attestation, photographs, or signatures would be clearly outlined there. For instance, sometimes a photograph might need to be attested by a Gazetted Officer or your college Principal *before* scanning, but this would be explicitly stated in the official user guide.

The AP EAPCET application process is primarily online and user-friendly, designed to simplify your journey towards higher education. By carefully following the official instructions, you can ensure a smooth application experience.
