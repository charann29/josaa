## What You Will Learn: A Detailed Syllabus

The curriculum is structured to build knowledge progressively, starting from the basics and moving to advanced concepts and applications. Here’s a breakdown of the key topics covered:

### Introduction to Nuclear Science

This section sets the stage with an overview of radiochemistry and nuclear chemistry, tracing the history of nuclear science. You'll learn about nuclei and nuclides, how to properly denote them, and understand the quantities and units used for mass and energy, including nuclidic mass and unified atomic mass. Key concepts like binding energy and mass excess are also explored.

### The Nuclear Force and Stability

Delve into the fundamental nuclear force, particle classification, and the concept of color charge. You'll study nuclear radius, mass density, spin, and the electric and magnetic properties of nuclei. The course also covers the one-nucleon shell model, the systematics of stable elements, and the Weizsäcker formula with the liquid drop model.

### Nuclear Reactions and Radioactive Decays

Understand various types of nuclear reactions, including those induced by neutrons and positive ions, and reaction cross-section systematics. This module thoroughly characterizes main decay modes and radiations, distinguishing between radioactive decay, chemical, and nuclear reactions. You'll also learn about half-life, mean life, decay schemes, and radionuclides found on Earth.

### In-Depth Radioactive Decay

Explore radioactive decay in the context of the chart of nuclides, investigating the spontaneity of alpha and beta decay. Topics include gamma decay, spontaneous fission, exotic decay modes, and the kinetics of radioactive decay and activation. The aftereffects of decay and nuclear reactions, inner Bremsstrahlung, X-rays, and the Auger effect are also discussed.

### Interaction of Radiation and Nucleosynthesis

This section covers how nuclear radiations interact with matter, introducing dosimetric concepts. Detailed interactions of alpha, beta, and gamma radiation are examined, along with neutron interactions. The course also delves into nucleosynthesis and related processes.

### Detection and Measurement Techniques

Gain practical insights into various detection methods. You'll learn about track measurements, gas counters, semiconductor detectors, and scintillation detectors. The module also covers the electronics used for pulse counting and gamma-spectrometry.

### Nuclear Energy and Reactors

Discover energy production through nuclear fission, neutron moderation, and nuclear fuel. Topics include fuel elements, nuclear fuel cycles, reprocessing used fuel, and different types of nuclear reactors. You'll also learn about thermal power reactors, reactor kinetics, radioactive waste management, reactor safety, controlled thermonuclear reactors, and nuclear explosives.

### Radionuclides in Nature

The course concludes by exploring cosmogenic and primordial radionuclides, as well as elements found in natural radioactive decay series.

This comprehensive course offers a solid foundation for anyone looking to build expertise in nuclear and radiochemistry.
