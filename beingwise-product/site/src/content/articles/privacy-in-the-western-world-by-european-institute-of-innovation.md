# Privacy in the Western World by European Institute of Innovation and Technology via Coursera: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding privacy in today's interconnected world is more crucial than ever. This course, offered by the European Institute of Innovation and Technology (EIT) Digital through Coursera, provides an insightful introduction to the concept of privacy, focusing primarily on its Western interpretations.

## Key takeaways

*   This is a beginner-level, self-paced online course that takes approximately seven hours to complete.
*   It offers a foundational understanding of privacy as a human right, exploring its cultural, legal, and historical dimensions.
*   You can access the course content for free through an audit mode or a seven-day full-access trial.
*   Successful completion leads to a digital certificate, available upon payment of a fee.

## Course Overview

Privacy is a fundamental human right with diverse cultural, legal, and historical facets. This EIT Digital course specifically delves into the concept of privacy within Western societies. It aims to equip learners with the knowledge to form well-researched opinions on personal space and its regulation.

This particular course is the first in a broader "Privacy and Standardisation Specialisation." While it lays a strong groundwork, subsequent courses in the specialisation would explore the nuanced differences between American and European approaches to privacy in greater detail. As a beginner-friendly program, it's designed to give you a comprehensive overview to build upon.

The course content is delivered through a mix of video and text, entirely in English. It's a short-term commitment, estimated at about two hours of effort per week, allowing for flexible deadlines. For enhanced accessibility, transcripts are provided in multiple languages.

## What You Will Learn

Upon completing the Privacy in the Western World certification, you will gain a clear understanding of several key areas:

*   The foundational concept of privacy as a human right.
*   Insights into the historical evolution of privacy.
*   How privacy and personal space are regulated.
*   The context of privacy within international cooperation efforts.
*   An awareness of varying court decisions related to privacy cases.
*   Initial insights into the differences in privacy concepts between American and European societies.

## Fee Structure and Certification

While the course content can be accessed in an audit mode for free, obtaining an official certificate of completion involves a fee. The payment structure depends on the duration for which you wish to subscribe to the Coursera platform:

| Course Type | Amount (approx.) |
| :---------- | :--------------- |
| Audit mode  | Free             |
| 1 Month     | ₹1,683           |
| 3 Months    | ₹3,384           |
| 6 Months    | ₹5,051           |

*Please note that these amounts are approximate and may vary. Financial aid options are also available for eligible students, which can be explored directly on the Coursera platform.*

Upon successful completion of all course modules, including any graded assignments and the final exam, Coursera will issue your electronic certificate.

## How to Apply

Enrolling in the Privacy in the Western World course is a straightforward process. You do not need to fill out a separate application form, but you will need an account on the Coursera platform.

1.  **Visit the Course Page:** Navigate to the official course page on Coursera.
2.  **Choose Enrollment Option:** Click on the "Enroll for free" option. You'll typically find three ways to proceed:
    *   **Audit Mode:** This allows you to access most course materials, including videos and readings, for free, but without graded assignments or a certificate.
    *   **7-Day Free Trial:** This option grants you full access to all course content, including graded assignments, for a week. If you decide to continue and earn a certificate, you will need to subscribe after the trial period.
    *   **Certificate Mode:** To directly enroll for the certificate, you will subscribe and pay the relevant course fee.
3.  **Sign In or Create Account:** If you don't already have one, create a Coursera account using your name, email, and a password. Alternatively, you can sign in using an existing Facebook, Google, or Microsoft account.

Once signed in, you can begin your learning journey immediately.

This course is an excellent starting point for anyone interested in understanding the nuances of privacy in the modern world, especially from a Western perspective.
