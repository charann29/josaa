# Cardio Thoracic Nursing Course, Subjects, Syllabus, Colleges, Admission, Career

Cardio Thoracic Nursing is a highly specialised field within nursing, dedicated to caring for patients with conditions affecting the heart and lungs. These nurses play a vital role in supporting individuals before and after complex cardiac and thoracic surgeries, such as heart bypass, valve replacements, or lung transplants, ensuring their recovery and preventing complications.

## Key takeaways

*   Cardio Thoracic Nursing focuses on pre- and post-operative care for heart and lung surgery patients.
*   It involves advanced skills in critical care, monitoring vital signs, medication management, and surgical assistance.
*   Postgraduate degrees like MSc or Post-Basic Diplomas are common pathways to specialise in this field.
*   Graduates can find rewarding roles in cardiac care units, ICUs, and operating theatres, with competitive salary prospects.

## What is Cardio Thoracic Nursing?

Cardio Thoracic Nurses are essential members of healthcare teams, working in critical environments like cardiac care units, intensive care units (ICUs), operating theatres, and emergency departments. Their responsibilities include continuous monitoring of vital signs, precise medication administration, assisting during surgical procedures, and providing comprehensive post-operative care. Their expertise and swift actions are crucial for patient safety, especially during high-risk surgeries and critical situations.

This specialisation can be pursued through various postgraduate nursing programmes, including Master of Science (MSc) degrees or Post-Basic Diploma courses. These programmes provide in-depth training in cardiac and thoracic patient care, with a strong emphasis on surgical and critical care settings. Upon completion, nurses are well-prepared for roles in leading hospitals and intensive care units across the country.

## Specialisations within Cardio Thoracic Nursing

Cardio Thoracic Nursing encompasses several focused areas, all centred on the intensive care of patients undergoing heart and lung procedures. Nurses in this field receive advanced training in cardiovascular care, surgical support, and ICU management. The domain also overlaps with emergency and critical care services. Some related specialisations and fields include:

*   Cardiac Surgical Nursing
*   Cardiovascular Intensive Care Nursing
*   Thoracic Surgical Nursing
*   Post-Anaesthesia Cardiac Nursing
*   Paediatric Cardiac Nursing (focusing on children's heart conditions)
*   Heart Failure and Transplant Nursing
*   Critical Care Nursing
*   Coronary Care Nursing
*   Vascular Nursing
*   BSc Cardiac Care Technology (a related allied health professional course)

## Career Opportunities and Salary

A career in Cardio Thoracic Nursing offers significant opportunities for professional growth and impact. Graduates are highly sought after in both public and private healthcare sectors. Typical employers include leading multi-speciality hospitals, cardiac centres, and government healthcare facilities. The average salary for a Cardio Thoracic Nurse can range from approximately Rs. 2 to 6 LPA, depending on experience, the institution, and geographical location. With advanced experience and further specialisation, earning potential can significantly increase, reaching up to Rs. 12 LPA or more for highly experienced professionals in critical care units.

This field offers a challenging yet incredibly rewarding path for nurses dedicated to critical patient care and advanced medical interventions.
