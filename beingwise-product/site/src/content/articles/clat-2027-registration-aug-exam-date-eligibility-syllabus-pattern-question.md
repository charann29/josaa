# CLAT 2027: Registration (Aug), Exam Date, Eligibility, Syllabus, Pattern, Question Paper, Preparation, Results

The Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) is a crucial national-level entrance examination for aspiring law students in India, facilitating admissions to undergraduate and postgraduate law programs at National Law Universities (NLUs) and numerous other affiliated institutions.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Registration Begins in August 2026:** The application window for CLAT 2027 is expected to open on August 1, 2026, following the release of the official notification.
*   **Exam on December 6, 2026:** The CLAT 2027 examination is tentatively scheduled for December 6, 2026, and will be conducted offline.
*   **Conducted by NLUs:** The Consortium of National Law Universities is responsible for organizing CLAT, which is accepted by 25 NLUs and many private law colleges.
*   **Comprehensive Syllabus:** The exam assesses candidates on English Language, Current Affairs including General Knowledge, Legal Reasoning, Logical Reasoning, and Quantitative Techniques.

## CLAT 2027 Overview

CLAT stands as a gateway to esteemed legal education in India. It is a pen-and-paper based test designed to evaluate candidates for various undergraduate (BA LLB, BCom LLB, BSc LLB, BBA LLB, etc.) and postgraduate (LLM) law programs. The exam duration is two hours. As per recent data, over one lakh candidates appeared for CLAT, highlighting its competitive nature.

## Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for CLAT 2027, candidates must meet specific academic qualifications:

### For Undergraduate (UG) Programs:

*   **Educational Qualification:** Candidates must have passed their 10+2 examination or an equivalent qualification from a recognized board.
*   **Minimum Marks:** For General/OBC/PWD/NRI/PIO/OCI categories, a minimum of 45% aggregate marks is required. SC/ST candidates need a minimum of 40% aggregate marks.
*   **Appearing Candidates:** Students appearing for their 10+2 examinations in the academic year preceding CLAT are also eligible to apply, provided they submit proof of passing the qualifying examination at the time of admission.
*   **Age Limit:** There is no upper age limit for appearing in CLAT.

### For Postgraduate (PG) Programs (LLM):

*   **Educational Qualification:** Candidates must hold an LL.B. degree or an equivalent from a recognized university.
*   **Minimum Marks:** For General/OBC/PWD/NRI/PIO/OCI categories, a minimum of 50% aggregate marks in the LL.B. degree is necessary. SC/ST candidates require a minimum of 45% aggregate marks.
*   **Appearing Candidates:** Those appearing for their final year LL.B. examination are also eligible, subject to producing passing certificates at the time of admission.

## CLAT 2027 Exam Pattern and Syllabus

Understanding the exam structure and content is vital for effective preparation.

### Exam Pattern

*   **Mode:** Offline (Pen-and-paper based)
*   **Duration:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
*   **Total Questions:** 120 multiple-choice questions for UG, 120 multiple-choice questions for PG.
*   **Marking Scheme:** Each correct answer fetches 1 mark. For every incorrect answer, 0.25 marks are deducted.
*   **Language:** English only.

### Syllabus (UG Program)

The UG CLAT exam comprises five sections:

1.  **English Language:** Comprehension passages, vocabulary, grammar, and inference-based questions.
2.  **Current Affairs including General Knowledge:** Contemporary events of national and international significance, arts and culture, international affairs, and historical events of continuing relevance.
3.  **Legal Reasoning:** Passages with legal principles, requiring candidates to apply these principles to given factual scenarios.
4.  **Logical Reasoning:** Critical reasoning, analytical reasoning, and logical deductions based on provided passages.
5.  **Quantitative Techniques:** Short sets of facts, graphs, or other textual/pictorial representations, followed by questions requiring elementary mathematical operations (up to Class 10 level).

### Syllabus (PG Program)

The PG CLAT exam focuses on core legal subjects:

*   Constitutional Law
*   Jurisprudence
*   Administrative Law
*   Law of Contract
*   Torts
*   Family Law
*   Criminal Law
*   Property Law
*   Company Law
*   Public International Law
*   Tax Law
*   Environmental Law
*   Labour and Industrial Law

## CLAT Preparation Tips

Successful CLAT preparation requires a strategic approach and consistent effort. Here are some key tips:

*   **Thorough Syllabus Review:** Begin by meticulously going through the official CLAT syllabus to understand all topics and sub-topics.
*   **Quality Study Material:** Select reliable and comprehensive study materials and the best books for each section. Avoid accumulating too many resources.
*   **Time Management and Accuracy:** Practice solving questions within strict time limits to improve both speed and accuracy. Time management is crucial in a competitive exam like CLAT.
*   **Mock Tests:** Regularly attempt CLAT mock tests. This helps in familiarizing yourself with the exam pattern, identifying weak areas, and developing test-taking strategies.
*   **Current Affairs Focus:** For the Current Affairs section, make it a habit to read newspapers daily and follow reliable news sources. Maintain notes on important national and international events.
*   **Legal & Logical Reasoning Practice:** These sections often involve passage-based questions. Regular practice with diverse passages will enhance your comprehension and analytical skills.

For more detailed information, candidates should always refer to the official website of the Consortium of National Law Universities.
