# What is the criteria to get admission in IIT BHU?

Securing a spot at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) (BHU) Varanasi is a dream for many aspiring engineers and architects. Admission primarily hinges on a combination of academic performance in your qualifying examinations and strong scores in highly competitive entrance tests.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Class 12 Marks:** A minimum of 75% aggregate marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) in your Class 12 (or equivalent) examination is generally required for General, OBC, and EWS categories. SC/ST/PwD candidates typically need to pass the exam.
*   **JEE Advanced:** Qualification through JEE Main and a competitive score in JEE Advanced are essential for undergraduate engineering programs.
*   **B.Arch Specifics:** For Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch) courses, an additional Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT) is mandatory after JEE Advanced.
*   **JoSAA Counselling:** All undergraduate admissions are finalized through the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) counselling process, which also manages reservation policies.
*   **M.Tech Admissions:** For postgraduate M.Tech programs, a strong GATE score in your relevant discipline is the primary gateway.

## Class 12 Academic Requirements

To be eligible for undergraduate programs at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, candidates must meet specific academic criteria in their Class 12 examinations. For students in the General, OBC, and EWS categories, an aggregate of at least 75% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) is typically required. Alternatively, candidates must be within the top 20 percentile of successful candidates in their respective qualifying examination board. For SC, ST, and PwD categories, the requirement is generally to simply pass the Class 12 examination.

## The JEE Pathway for B.Tech

The primary route to undergraduate engineering (B.Tech) programs at IIT (BHU) Varanasi involves a two-stage national entrance examination process:

### JEE Main

This is the initial qualifying examination conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). Candidates must perform well in JEE Main to become eligible to appear for JEE Advanced. Only the top candidates from JEE Main qualify for the next stage.

### JEE Advanced

Administered by one of the IITs on a rotational basis, JEE Advanced is the ultimate gateway to all IITs, including IIT (BHU) Varanasi. A high rank in JEE Advanced is crucial for securing a seat, as admission is entirely based on these scores and the subsequent counselling process.

## Specifics for B.Arch Admissions

For those aspiring to pursue a Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch) degree at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, the process includes an additional step after JEE Advanced:

### Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT)

Candidates who qualify in JEE Advanced and wish to pursue B.Arch must also appear for and clear the Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT). This test assesses a candidate's aptitude for architecture and is specifically required for B.Arch admissions at IITs. Information regarding AAT is typically released alongside the JEE Advanced schedule.

## JoSAA Counselling and Reservations

Once candidates have cleared the necessary entrance examinations, the final step for undergraduate admissions is through the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) counselling process. JoSAA is responsible for managing admissions to all IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other GFTIs based on JEE Main and JEE Advanced ranks.

During JoSAA counselling, seats are allocated based on merit, candidate preferences, and the official reservation policies of the Government of India. IITs reserve a certain percentage of seats for various categories, including:

*   General-Economically Weaker Section (GEN-EWS)
*   Other Backward Classes - Non-Creamy Layer (OBC-NCL)
*   Scheduled Caste (SC)
*   Scheduled Tribe (ST)
*   Persons with Disabilities (PwD)

Candidates belonging to these categories must provide valid certificates as per the formats specified by the official JoSAA portal.

## Admission to M.Tech Programs

For postgraduate Master of Technology (M.Tech) programs at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, the primary admission criterion is a strong performance in the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE). Candidates must appear for GATE in their relevant engineering discipline and secure a competitive score. Admission is typically based on GATE scores, followed by an interview process conducted by the respective departments at IIT (BHU) Varanasi.

Meeting these criteria opens the door to a world-class education at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, a highly respected institution as consistently reflected in national rankings like the NIRF rankings.
