# AUJET 2026 Exam: Dates, Registration, Eligibility, Syllabus, Results

Adamas University Joint Entrance Test (AUJET) is an annual examination for admission to various undergraduate, postgraduate, and PhD programs offered by Adamas University. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the AUJET 2026, including important dates, the application process, eligibility criteria, exam pattern, and results.

## Key Takeaways

*   AUJET 2026 registration is currently open, with the final date to apply being April 16, 2026.
*   The entrance test is a 90-minute online exam with 50 multiple-choice questions, totaling 100 marks.
*   The test includes sections on English, Aptitude, Reasoning, and General Knowledge, with negative marking for incorrect answers.
*   Admissions to Adamas University programs are based on AUJET scores, followed by a personal interview for qualified candidates.

## Understanding AUJET

Adamas University, a private institution located in Kolkata, West Bengal, conducts the AUJET examination each year. The primary goal of AUJET is to identify and shortlist suitable candidates for its diverse range of undergraduate and postgraduate courses. It serves as a crucial step for aspiring students seeking to join the university's academic community.

Keeping track of key dates is essential for a smooth application process. Here's a tentative schedule for AUJET 2026:

| Event                      | Dates (Tentative)          |

| Personal Interview Round   | May 2026                   |

Candidates should regularly check the official Adamas University website for any updates or changes to this schedule.

## Application and Eligibility for AUJET 2026

The application process for AUJET 2026 is conducted online. Interested candidates must complete the application form and pay a nominal fee of INR 100. Successful submission of the application is a prerequisite for appearing in the entrance test. Specific eligibility criteria vary depending on the program chosen (UG, PG, or PhD), generally requiring candidates to have completed their 10th or 10+2 level education for undergraduate courses. Detailed eligibility requirements for each program can be found on the official Adamas University admissions portal.

## AUJET 2026 Exam Pattern

The AUJET is a computer-based test conducted in English. Here’s a breakdown of the exam structure:

*   **Total Questions:** 50
*   **Total Marks:** 100
*   **Duration:** 90 minutes (unless specified otherwise for particular programs like B.Pharm (Lateral), which is 60 minutes)
*   **Marking Scheme:** 2 marks for each correct answer.
*   **Negative Marking:** 1 mark is deducted for every incorrect answer.
*   **Sections:** The exam is divided into four sections:
    *   English (30 marks)
    *   Aptitude (30 marks)
    *   Reasoning (20 marks)
    *   General Knowledge (20 marks)

Candidates will receive their admit cards, which will specify their exam date and time. It is important to note that no re-examination will be conducted under any circumstances.

## Syllabus and Results

The AUJET syllabus is generally based on the curriculum relevant to the program level, such as Class 10 or 10+2 for undergraduate admissions. Candidates are advised to review foundational concepts in English, General Knowledge, Aptitude, and Reasoning. After the examination, results will be announced, typically in May 2026. Shortlisted candidates will then be invited for a personal interview, which is a crucial part of the final admission process.

We encourage all prospective students to prepare thoroughly and stay informed by regularly checking the official Adamas University website for the latest details regarding AUJET 2026.
