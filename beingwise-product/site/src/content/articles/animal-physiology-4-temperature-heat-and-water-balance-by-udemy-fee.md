# Animal Physiology 4: Temperature, Heat and Water Balance

Understanding how animals manage their internal temperature and water levels is crucial in the field of biology. This online course delves into the fascinating physiological adaptations that allow various species to thrive in diverse environments, from scorching deserts to freezing tundras.

*   The course is delivered entirely in English, making it accessible for a wide audience.
*   It follows a self-paced learning model, allowing students to study at their own convenience.
*   The content is presented through a mix of video lectures and textual materials.
*   Upon successful completion, participants receive a certificate.

## Course Structure and Content

This program is structured into several modules, each focusing on a specific aspect of animal physiology related to temperature, heat, and water balance. The curriculum begins with an introduction to the core concepts before moving into detailed lectures.

Topics covered include:

*   **Temperature Adaptation:** This section explores how animals adapt to varying temperatures, discussing concepts like Fourier's Law, the Scholander model, and Bergmann's Rule.
*   **Temperature and Reaction Kinetics:** You'll learn about the impact of temperature on biological reactions, including thermal limits and the concept of a thermal niche.
*   **Adaptation to Extreme Temperatures:** This module covers strategies animals employ to survive in very hot or very cold conditions, such as cutaneous evaporation, respiratory water loss, and regional heterothermy.
*   **Adaptation to Cold:** Dive deeper into specific cold adaptations like homeoviscous adaptation, freezing avoidance, torpor, hibernation, and thermogenesis.
*   **Water Balance:** This fundamental section introduces water balance, osmosis, and the function of the nephron, including filtration, reabsorption, and secretion.
*   **Urine Production:** Understand the process of urine production, its hormonal control, the renin-angiotensin system, and how animals manage excess salt.
*   **Kidney Adaptations:** Explore the diverse adaptations of kidneys in higher vertebrates, focusing on urine concentration, kidney evolution, and water balance in relation to nitrogen metabolism.

The course concludes with a comprehensive review and wrap-up, consolidating the knowledge gained throughout the program.
