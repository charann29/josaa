Plastic surgery is a highly specialized field of medicine focused on rebuilding or enhancing parts of the body. It involves the transfer of tissue from one area to another to restore function, correct deformities, or improve appearance. This demanding yet rewarding path requires extensive medical training beyond a basic MBBS degree.

## Key Takeaways

*   Plastic surgery specialisation requires an MBBS degree, followed by an MS in General Surgery or a DNB in Plastic Surgery.
*   Admission to these advanced programs is highly competitive, typically requiring national-level entrance exams like NEET PG or institution-specific tests.
*   The field addresses both reconstructive needs (e.g., burns, congenital defects) and aesthetic desires (e.g., facial enhancements).
*   Career opportunities are robust, with growing demand in both public and private healthcare sectors in India and internationally.

## What is Plastic Surgery?

At its core, plastic surgery is about transformation. Surgeons in this field work to reconstruct body parts damaged by injury, disease, or birth defects. This often involves transplanting the patient's own tissue to ensure compatibility and prevent rejection by the immune system. Beyond reconstructive work, plastic surgery also encompasses aesthetic procedures aimed at enhancing features like the face, lips, or jawline, driven by an increasing demand for cosmetic improvements in India.

## Eligibility for Plastic Surgery Courses

To pursue a super-specialization in plastic surgery, aspiring doctors must first complete their foundational medical education. The primary pathway involves a Master of Surgery (MS) or a Diplomate of National Board (DNB) qualification.

### For Master of Surgery (MS) in Plastic Surgery

*   Candidates must hold an MBBS degree from an institution recognized by the Medical Council of India (MCI).
*   A minimum aggregate score of 50% in the MBBS degree is typically required.
*   Completion of the compulsory rotatory residential internship by a specified date, as mandated by the MCI, is essential.
*   Applicants need to possess a permanent or provisional medical registration certificate issued by the MCI or a State Medical Council.

### For DNB (Diplomate of National Board) Plastic Surgery

*   Applicants must have completed both an MBBS degree and an MS degree in General Surgery from a recognized Indian medical institution.
*   Admission is typically through an entrance examination conducted by the National Board of Examinations (NBE).
*   Specific eligibility criteria may also be set by individual medical institutions offering DNB super-specialty courses.

## Entrance Examinations for Plastic Surgery

Admission to advanced plastic surgery programs is highly competitive and primarily determined by performance in rigorous entrance examinations. These tests assess a candidate's comprehensive medical knowledge and aptitude for surgical specialties.

*   **National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Post Graduate (NEET PG):** This is a crucial national-level entrance exam conducted annually by the National Testing Agency (NTA). It serves as the gateway for admission to various postgraduate medical courses, including MS programs that can lead to super-specialization in plastic surgery, across approximately 500 medical colleges in India. The process includes an online examination followed by online and offline counselling.
*   **AIIMS PG Entrance Exam:** The All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) conducts its own national-level entrance examination for admission to its prestigious postgraduate medical programs. Successful candidates proceed to counselling for placements across various AIIMS institutions.
*   **PGIMER Entrance Exam:** The Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research (PGIMER), Chandigarh, a highly-rated institution, conducts an online entrance test, usually in June, for its medical and research programs. A merit list is subsequently released for admissions.

## Career Scope in Plastic Surgery

The field of plastic surgery offers a dynamic and expanding career path. What was once considered a niche or inaccessible specialty is now widely sought after. Indian plastic surgeons are globally recognized for their skills, eliminating the need for patients to travel abroad for complex procedures. The demand is strong for both reconstructive work, addressing conditions like severe burns, birth defects, or post-cancer reconstruction, and for cosmetic enhancements.

Graduates can find opportunities in large private hospitals, government medical colleges, specialized clinics, or even establish their own practices. The continuous advancements in surgical techniques and technology ensure that plastic surgeons remain at the forefront of medical innovation, offering diverse and impactful career prospects both within India and internationally.

Embarking on a career in plastic surgery demands dedication and continuous learning, but it offers the profound satisfaction of transforming lives.
