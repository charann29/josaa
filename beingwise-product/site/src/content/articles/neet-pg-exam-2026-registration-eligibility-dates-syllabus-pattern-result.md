# NEET PG Exam 2026: Registration, Eligibility, Dates, Syllabus, Pattern, Result, Cutoff

For aspiring doctors in India, the NEET PG exam is the essential gateway to pursuing postgraduate medical studies. This national-level entrance test opens doors to MD, MS, and PG Diploma courses across the country.

## Key takeaways

*   NEET PG 2026 will be conducted by the National Board of Examinations in Medical Science (NBEMS) on August 30, 2026.
*   The exam is the sole entrance test for most MD/MS/PG Diploma admissions in India; no other state or institutional exams are valid for these courses.
*   The official information bulletin, released by NBEMS, will contain all crucial details regarding eligibility, application dates, exam pattern, and more.
*   Scores from NEET PG are also utilized for admissions into postgraduate DNB courses.

## Understanding NEET PG

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Post-Graduation (NEET PG) is a computer-based examination held annually. It serves as the primary admission test for a vast number of postgraduate medical seats, including approximately 26,168 MD seats, 13,649 MS seats, and 922 PG Diploma seats offered by various medical colleges throughout India. The NBEMS is responsible for conducting this vital examination.

It's important to note that while NEET PG is widely accepted, some prominent institutions like AIIMS (including AIIMS New Delhi), PGIMER Chandigarh, JIPMER Puducherry, NIMHANS Bengaluru, SGPGI Lucknow, and Sree Chitra Tirunal Institute for Medical Sciences and Technology Trivandrum conduct their own separate entrance exams, such as INI CET, for postgraduate admissions.

## Eligibility Criteria and Application Process

To be eligible for NEET PG 2026, candidates must meet specific criteria outlined by NBEMS. The full details will be available in the information bulletin on the official NBEMS website. Generally, candidates must possess an MBBS degree and have completed their compulsory rotating internship by a specified cut-off date. There is no upper age limit or restriction on the number of attempts for the NEET PG exam.

Once the application window opens, eligible candidates will need to register online, fill out the application form with accurate details, upload necessary documents, and pay the application fee. It is crucial to review the information bulletin thoroughly before applying to ensure all conditions are met.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The NEET PG exam is conducted in a computer-based format. The syllabus for NEET PG covers subjects taught during the MBBS curriculum. This includes Pre-Clinical, Para-Clinical, and Clinical subjects. Aspirants are advised to refer to the detailed syllabus provided in the official information bulletin for a comprehensive understanding of the topics to be covered. The exam pattern typically involves multiple-choice questions, and details on the number of questions, marking scheme, and duration will be specified by NBEMS.

## Results, Cutoff, and Counselling

The NEET PG 2026 results are expected to be announced within one month of the examination date. Following the result declaration, the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) will conduct the NEET PG counselling process. This involves online registration, choice filling, and seat allotment based on NEET PG ranks, eligibility, and choices filled by the candidates.

The NEET PG cutoff refers to the minimum qualifying percentile required to be eligible for counselling. This cutoff can vary each year based on factors such as the number of candidates, exam difficulty, and available seats. Candidates are advised to keep an eye on official announcements from MCC regarding counselling schedules and cutoff details.

NEET PG is a challenging yet rewarding examination that paves the way for a specialized career in medicine. Staying informed through official sources and preparing diligently are key to success.
