# Communication Management Courses, Subjects, Colleges, Syllabus, Scope, Fees, Eligibility

Communication management is a dynamic field focused on strategically planning, executing, and monitoring all aspects of communication within an organization, from content creation to audience feedback. It blends creative thinking with methodical approaches to manage communication processes and structures effectively.

## Key Takeaways

*   Communication Management is offered at both undergraduate (BBA) and postgraduate (MBA) levels.
*   Eligibility typically requires 10+2 completion for BBA and a bachelor's degree for MBA, with minimum percentage requirements.
*   Admissions often involve entrance exams, followed by group exercises, written tests, and personal interviews for MBA programs.
*   The field equips individuals with skills in communication processes, techniques, analytics, and the socio-psychological aspects of business communication.

## Understanding Communication Management Degrees

Communication Management programs are available at two main academic levels:

### BBA in Communication Management

This is an undergraduate program, usually lasting three years, divided into six semesters. It's designed for students who have just completed their 10+2 education and wish to build a foundational understanding of communication principles and management.

### MBA in Communication Management

As a postgraduate program, the MBA typically spans two years, comprising four semesters. It's suitable for graduates looking to specialize in managing communication strategies at a higher level. Some institutions may also offer this course through distance learning modes.

## Eligibility Criteria

Meeting the eligibility requirements is the first step towards pursuing a degree in Communication Management.

### For BBA in Communication Management

*   Candidates must have successfully completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination from a recognized board, preferably in the commerce stream.
*   A minimum aggregate score of 50% in 10+2 is generally required (with a relaxation to 45% for SC/ST candidates).

### For MBA in Communication Management

*   Applicants need to hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university.
*   A minimum aggregate score of 50% in their undergraduate degree is typically required (with a relaxation to 45% for SC/ST candidates).

## Admission Process and Entrance Exams

Admission to Communication Management programs, especially at the MBA level, is often competitive and multi-faceted.

### MBA Admission Process

Most colleges offering MBA in Communication Management base admissions on performance in relevant entrance examinations, followed by further assessment rounds. A typical admission process includes:

1.  **Group Exercise:** This assesses candidates' innovation, problem-solving abilities, teamwork, and time management skills.
2.  **Written Test:** This round evaluates creative and analytical thinking.
3.  **Personal Interaction:** This stage focuses on assessing interpersonal skills, communication proficiency, and academic background.

### Popular Entrance Exams

For undergraduate programs like BBA, and in some cases for MBA, various entrance tests are conducted across the country. Some common entrance exams include:

*   SET (Symbiosis Entrance Test)
*   DSAT (Dayanand Sagar Admission Test)
*   BUMAT (Bharati Vidyapeeth Undergraduate Management Aptitude Test)
*   ITM NEST (ITM National Entrance and Scholarship Test)
*   DU JAT (Delhi University Joint Admission Test)
*   SUAT (Sharda University Admission Test)
*   NPAT (National Test for Programs)
*   CUCET (Central Universities Common Entrance Test)
*   UGAT-AIMA (Under Graduate Aptitude Test-AIMA)
*   AUMAT (Alliance Undergraduate Management Aptitude Test)
*   GOT (Gitam Online Test)
*   GGSIPU CET BBA (Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University Common Entrance Test)

## Course Duration

The standard duration for these programs is:

*   **BBA in Communication Management:** Minimum 36 months (3 years).
*   **MBA in Communication Management:** Minimum 24 months (2 years).

Colleges may allow extensions, typically up to an additional 12 months, for course completion.

Embarking on a Communication Management journey can open doors to diverse and exciting career paths in various industries.
