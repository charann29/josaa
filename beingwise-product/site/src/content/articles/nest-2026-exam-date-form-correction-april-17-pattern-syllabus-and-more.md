# NEST 2026: Exam Date, Form Correction (April 17), Pattern, Syllabus & More

For students aspiring to pursue an Integrated M.Sc. in basic sciences, the National Entrance Screening Test (NEST) is a crucial national-level examination. This guide provides all essential details for NEST 2026, from important dates to the exam structure, helping you prepare effectively.

## Key takeaways

*   The NEST 2026 registration window has now concluded.
*   A form correction window will be available on April 17, 2026, for making necessary edits to your application.
*   The NEST 2026 examination is scheduled for June 6, 2026.
*   Mock tests will be released from April 14, 2026, offering valuable practice before the actual exam.

## Understanding the NEST Exam

NEST is conducted jointly by NISER Bhubaneswar and UM-DAE CEBS Mumbai to admit students into their five-year Integrated M.Sc. programs. Here are some key highlights:

*   **Exam Name:** National Entrance Screening Test (NEST)
*   **Conducting Bodies:** NISER Bhubaneswar and UM-DAE CEBS Mumbai
*   **Exam Level:** Undergraduate, National
*   **Frequency:** Once a year
*   **Mode:** Computer-Based Test (Online)
*   **Courses Offered:** Integrated M.Sc.
*   **Total Marks:** 240
*   **Duration:** 3 hours
*   **Number of Questions:** 60
*   **Marking Scheme:** +3 for correct, -1 for incorrect, 0 for unanswered
*   **Language:** English and Hindi
*   **Participating Colleges:** 2 (NISER and UM-DAE CEBS)
*   **Total Seats:** 261
*   **Exam Cities:** Approximately 140 across India

### Application Fees

*   **General/OBC Male Candidates:** INR 1,400
*   **Female Candidates (All Categories):** INR 700
*   **SC/ST/PwD Male Candidates:** INR 700

## NEST 2026 Eligibility Criteria

Before applying, candidates must ensure they meet the specific eligibility requirements:

*   **Academic Qualification:** Applicants must have passed their Class 12 Board examination (or equivalent) in either 2024 or 2025. Those appearing for their Class 12 examination in 2026 are also eligible, provided they fulfill all other criteria.
*   **Subject Requirement:** Candidates must have studied at least three of the following subjects in Class 11 and Class 12: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Mathematics.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A minimum of 60% aggregate marks in the Class 12 examination is required. For SC/ST/PwD candidates, the minimum aggregate is 55%.

## NEST 2026 Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The NEST exam is structured to assess a candidate's aptitude in basic sciences. It consists of five sections: General Ability, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Mathematics. The General Ability section is compulsory, while candidates must attempt any three of the remaining four science sections. Each section carries 60 marks, making the total score 240. The questions are multiple-choice.

The syllabus for NEST primarily covers topics from Class 11 and Class 12 NCERT textbooks in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Mathematics. A thorough understanding of these foundational concepts is crucial for success.

For detailed syllabi and mock test access, candidates should refer to the official NEST website.

## Post-Exam Process: Admit Card, Answer Key, and Results

*   **Admit Card:** The NEST 2026 admit cards will be issued on May 15, 2026. Candidates must download and print their admit cards from the official NEST portal.
*   **Answer Key:** A provisional answer key will be released in June 2026, allowing candidates to estimate their scores. An objection window will also be provided for challenging any discrepancies.
*   **Result:** The NEST 2026 results are expected in June 2026. These results will determine eligibility for counselling and admission to NISER Bhubaneswar and UM-DAE CEBS Mumbai.

Begin your preparation early, focus on core concepts, and utilize mock tests to enhance your chances of success in NEST 2026.
