The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) conducts the Combined Higher Secondary (10+2) Level (CHSL) Examination to recruit candidates for various Group C posts in government departments and ministries. This page provides a comprehensive overview of the SSC CHSL 2025 examination, including the latest updates on results, important dates, and exam structure.

## Key Takeaways

*   The SSC CHSL 2025 Tier 1 results have been officially declared on the SSC portal.
*   The Tier 1 examination was held from November 12 to November 30.
*   A total of 3,131 vacancies for Group C posts are expected to be filled through the SSC CHSL 2025 examination.
*   The Tier 2 examination is tentatively scheduled for March or April 2026.

## SSC CHSL 2025 Tier 1 Result

The Staff Selection Commission has released the SSC CHSL 2025 Tier 1 result on its official website, ssc.gov.in. The result is available in PDF format and lists the roll numbers, names, and parents' names of candidates who have qualified for Tier 2. To check your result:

1.  Visit the official SSC website (ssc.gov.in).
2.  Navigate to the 'Result' tab located at the top of the homepage.
3.  Select the 'CHSL' exam tab.
4.  Click on the relevant result link for the CHSL 2025 Tier 1 examination.
5.  Download the PDF and search for your roll number.

Qualified candidates will proceed to the Tier 2 examination.

### SSC CHSL Scorecard

Following the final result and cut-off marks, the SSC CHSL scorecard will be released for all candidates. This scorecard will detail individual marks, including raw scores, secured marks, and category-wise cut-offs. To access your scorecard once it's available:

1.  Go to ssc.gov.in.
2.  Locate the 'Login' section on the right side of the page.
3.  Enter your Registration Number, Password, and the displayed captcha code.
4.  After logging in, your exam dashboard will appear.
5.  Click on the scorecard link under the SSC CHSL exam section.
6.  Download and save your scorecard for future reference.

## SSC CHSL Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The SSC CHSL examination typically consists of two tiers: Tier 1 (Computer Based Examination) and Tier 2 (Skill Test/Typing Test). Understanding the pattern and syllabus is essential for effective preparation.

### Tier 1 Exam Pattern

The Tier 1 examination is an objective-type, multiple-choice computer-based test covering four main sections:

*   **General Intelligence:** Questions designed to test analytical and problem-solving abilities.
*   **General Awareness:** Covers current events, history, geography, economics, and general science.
*   **Quantitative Aptitude:** Basic arithmetic skills, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, and data interpretation.
*   **English Language:** Grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and writing ability.

Each section carries a specific weightage, and there is negative marking for incorrect answers. The duration of the exam is usually 60 minutes.

### Tier 1 Syllabus

The detailed syllabus for Tier 1 includes topics such as:

*   **General Intelligence:** Semantic Analogy, Symbolic Operations, Space Orientation, Venn Diagrams, Drawing inferences, Punched hole/pattern-folding & unfolding, Figural Pattern-folding and completion, Critical thinking, Emotional Intelligence, Word Building, etc.
*   **General Awareness:** India and its neighboring countries, History, Culture, Geography, Economic Scene, General Policy & Scientific Research.
*   **Quantitative Aptitude:** Number Systems, Fundamental arithmetical operations, Algebra, Geometry, Mensuration, Trigonometry, Statistical Charts.
*   **English Language:** Spot the Error, Fill in the Blanks, Synonyms/Homonyms, Antonyms, Spellings/Detecting misspelt words, Idioms & Phrases, One word substitution, Improvement of Sentences, Active/Passive Voice of Verbs, Conversion into Direct/Indirect narration, Shuffling of Sentence parts, Cloze Passage, Comprehension Passage.

Candidates who clear Tier 1 will be eligible for Tier 2, which assesses practical skills relevant to the job roles.

## SSC CHSL Cut-Off and Qualifying Marks

The Staff Selection Commission sets minimum qualifying marks for the CHSL examination. Candidates must achieve these marks to be considered for further stages of the selection process. The minimum qualifying marks for the SSC CHSL exam are:

| Category            | Qualifying Marks | Minimum Marks |
| :------------------ | :--------------- | :------------ |
| Unreserved (UR)     | 30%              | 60            |
| OBC/EWS             | 25%              | 50            |
| All other categories | 20%              | 40            |

These marks represent the minimum aggregate required, and the actual cut-off scores can vary based on factors like the number of vacancies, the difficulty of the exam, and the performance of candidates.
