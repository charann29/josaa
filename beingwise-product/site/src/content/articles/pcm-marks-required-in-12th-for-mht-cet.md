# PCM Marks Required in 12th for MHT CET

Understanding the academic qualifications needed for the MHT CET entrance exam is a crucial first step for aspiring engineering and pharmacy students in Maharashtra. This guide breaks down the essential mark requirements for your 12th-grade Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) subjects.

## Key Takeaways

*   You must have passed your 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics and Mathematics as mandatory subjects.
*   Alongside Physics and Mathematics, you need one additional science-related subject from a specified list.
*   General category candidates need at least 45% aggregate marks in these three subjects.
*   Reserved category candidates from Maharashtra State require a minimum of 40% aggregate marks.

## Core Eligibility for MHT CET

To be eligible for the MHT CET, all candidates must first have successfully completed their 10+2 examination or an equivalent qualification from a recognized board. This foundational requirement ensures that students have a basic understanding of the subjects tested in the MHT CET.

## Essential Subject Combinations

For those aiming for engineering and technology courses through the MHT CET, specific subject combinations in your 12th grade are non-negotiable. You must have studied:

*   **Physics** (compulsory)
*   **Mathematics** (compulsory)
*   **Any one of the following subjects:** Chemistry, Biotechnology, Biology, Technical Vocational Subject, Computer Science, Information Technology, Informatics Practices, Agriculture, Engineering Graphics, Business Studies, Electronics, or Entrepreneurship.

This broad list allows students from various science and vocational backgrounds to pursue higher education in engineering and related fields.

## Minimum Marks for 12th Grade PCM

The aggregate percentage you secure in your crucial 12th-grade subjects (Physics, Mathematics, and your chosen third subject) plays a significant role in MHT CET eligibility. Here's a breakdown:

*   **General Category Candidates:** If you belong to the General category, you must have obtained a minimum of 45% aggregate marks in Physics, Mathematics, and your third chosen subject combined. This applies to candidates from Maharashtra State and outside Maharashtra.
*   **Reserved Category Candidates from Maharashtra State:** For candidates belonging to Backward Class categories, Economically Weaker Section (EWS), and Persons with Disability (PwD) categories within Maharashtra State, the minimum aggregate mark requirement is relaxed to 40% in Physics, Mathematics, and your third chosen subject combined.

It's important to note that these percentages are calculated based on the total marks obtained in these three specific subjects, not your overall 12th-grade percentage.

## Diploma Holders' Eligibility

Candidates who have completed a Diploma in Engineering and Technology are also eligible for MHT CET. Their mark requirements mirror those for 10+2 students:

*   **General Category Diploma Holders:** A minimum of 45% aggregate marks in their Diploma program.
*   **Reserved Category Diploma Holders from Maharashtra State:** A minimum of 40% aggregate marks in their Diploma program.

## Other Key Eligibility Points

Beyond academic qualifications, there are a few other essential criteria:

*   **Nationality:** All candidates must be Indian nationals.
*   **Age Limit:** Currently, there is no upper age limit specified for appearing in the MHT CET examination.

Always refer to the latest official MHT CET information bulletin released by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra State, for the most up-to-date and comprehensive eligibility criteria.

Meeting these academic and nationality requirements is your gateway to participating in the MHT CET and pursuing your desired engineering or pharmacy program.
