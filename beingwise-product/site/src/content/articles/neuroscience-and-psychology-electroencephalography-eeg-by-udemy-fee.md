# Neuroscience and Psychology: Electroencephalography (EEG) by Udemy: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Electroencephalography (EEG) is a fascinating technique that allows us to peek into the electrical activity of the brain. This online certification course, developed by Psy Stuff, an associate professor of psychology, offers a comprehensive introduction to EEG, exploring its uses in both clinical practice and academic research within neuroscience and psychology.

## Key takeaways

*   Learn the fundamental principles and applications of Electroencephalography (EEG).
*   Understand how EEG is used in both healthcare and scientific research.
*   Explore the methods for collecting and analyzing EEG data and brain signals.
*   Gain a certificate of completion from Udemy upon finishing the course.

## Course Overview and Structure

This self-paced online course is designed to provide a thorough understanding of EEG. It includes approximately 1.5 hours of video content, accompanied by quizzes and other learning resources. The course covers essential topics such as basic neuroanatomy and neurophysiology, the characteristics of EEG signals, and how EEG compares to other brain imaging techniques.

### What You Will Learn

Upon completing this certification, participants will have a solid grasp of the core concepts in neuroscience and psychology related to brain activity. You will delve into the methodologies of electroencephalography, learning about the electrical processes within the brain and practical strategies for collecting and interpreting EEG data and signals. This includes understanding brain rhythms, identifying clean data, and recognizing artifacts in recordings.

### Who Is This Course For?

This course is ideal for anyone interested in the human brain, psychology, and neuroscience. It's particularly beneficial for students, researchers, or professionals looking to understand the basics of EEG technology and its applications without needing prior in-depth knowledge of the subject.

## Course Content Details

The syllabus is structured to guide learners from fundamental concepts to practical applications and data analysis. Here’s a look at the modules:

*   **Introduction**: An overview of the course.
*   **The Human Brain**: Basics of neuroanatomy and brain cells.
*   **Recording Brain Electrical Activity**: Explains EEG signals, rhythms, and how EEG differs from other techniques.
*   **What is EEG Used For?**: Covers applications in healthcare, academic research, and other areas.
*   **Collecting EEG Data**: Discusses the history of EEG, types of electrodes, placement (including the International 10-20 system), signal processing, and handling data artifacts.
*   **Analyzing EEG Data**: Introduces methods for frequency-based analysis and Event-Related Potentials (ERPs).
*   **Conclusion**: A summary of the course content.
*   **Test Your Knowledge**: A multiple-choice quiz to assess understanding.
