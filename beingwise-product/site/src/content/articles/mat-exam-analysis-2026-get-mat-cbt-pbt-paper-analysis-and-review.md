# MAT Exam Analysis 2026: Get MAT CBT/PBT Paper Analysis and Review

Understanding the MAT exam structure and typical question patterns is key to preparing effectively. This analysis provides an overview of the MAT 2026 exam, covering both the computer-based (CBT) and paper-based (PBT) test modes, which are generally consistent in their design and difficulty.

## Key Takeaways

*   The MAT 2026 exam, across all sessions, is generally considered to be of easy to moderate difficulty.
*   The test consists of 150 questions distributed equally among five sections, with a total time limit of two hours.
*   Only four of the five sections contribute to the percentile calculation; the Economic and Business Environment section is for general awareness and does not impact your percentile score.
*   There is negative marking, with 0.25 marks deducted for each incorrect answer.

## MAT 2026 Exam Structure at a Glance

The MAT exam pattern for February 2026 remained consistent with previous years. Here's a quick look at the paper's design:

| Specifications                          | Details                                     |

## Detailed Sectional Analysis

### Language Comprehension

This section typically includes two Reading Comprehension passages, accounting for about ten questions. Other question types observed by test-takers included idioms and phrases, antonyms, synonyms, fill-in-the-blanks, parajumbles, sentence correction, one-word substitution, and summary questions. This section was generally considered easy.

### Intelligence & Critical Reasoning

The Intelligence & Critical Reasoning section in the February 2026 MAT session presented a balanced mix of question types. These included blood relations, alphanumeric series, letter series, coding-decoding (letter-based), Venn diagrams, comparison-based questions, and distribution puzzles. Test-takers found this section manageable, often completing it within 30-40 minutes.

### Mathematical Skills

Questions in the Mathematical Skills section primarily focused on Arithmetic. Other areas covered included Modern Math, Geometry, and Algebra. The questions were of moderate difficulty, largely direct, and could be solved within 30-40 minutes.

### Data Analysis and Sufficiency

This section featured accessible table and chart-based questions. Data Sufficiency problems often required the application of arithmetic and mensuration formulas. Overall, the Data Analysis and Sufficiency section was rated as easy to moderate.

### Economic and Business Environment

The questions in this section were typically straightforward, drawing heavily from static General Knowledge (GK). Some questions also touched upon significant events from the past few years. This section was generally easy and could be completed quickly, often in under 15 minutes.

Understanding these patterns will help you strategize your preparation and approach the MAT exam with confidence.
