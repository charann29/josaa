# MBA from IIMs without CAT Exam: Check Courses List & Selection Criteria

Many aspiring managers dream of studying at an Indian Institute of Management (IIM) but are concerned about the highly competitive Common Admission Test (CAT). While CAT is essential for the flagship full-time MBA programs, several alternative pathways allow you to gain an IIM qualification without taking it.

## Key takeaways

*   Full-time, regular MBA programs at IIMs always require a valid CAT score.
*   Working professionals can pursue Executive MBA programs, often requiring GMAT/GRE instead of CAT.
*   After Class 12, students can join Integrated Management Programs (BBA+MBA) without CAT.
*   Other specialized programs like PhDs, FPMs, and certification courses also offer non-CAT routes.

## IIM MBA Courses Without CAT

If you're looking to pursue management education at an IIM but wish to bypass the CAT exam, there are specific programs designed for different stages of your academic or professional journey. These typically include Executive MBA (EMBA), Integrated Management Programs (IPM), and other specialized courses. It's important to note that even without CAT, most of these programs have their own rigorous admission processes, which might involve other entrance tests or interviews.

### Executive MBA for Working Professionals

Executive MBA programs are specifically tailored for experienced working professionals aiming to enhance their skills, gain new perspectives, and advance their careers without pausing their professional lives entirely. These programs often require a minimum of two to five years of relevant work experience at an executive or managerial level. Admission to EMBA programs at IIMs typically involves scores from exams like GMAT or GRE, or sometimes even a dedicated IIM admission test, rather than CAT. These courses can be delivered in full-time, part-time, or blended online formats to accommodate working schedules.

### Integrated Management Programs After Class 12

For students who have just completed Class 12 and are looking for a direct route to an MBA from an IIM, the Integrated Management Program (IPM) is an excellent option. This is a five-year program that combines undergraduate (BBA) and postgraduate (MBA) studies, culminating in an MBA degree. Admission to IPM programs does not require a CAT score. Instead, candidates typically need to clear an institute-specific entrance examination, such as the IPMAT, followed by a personal interview. This pathway is ideal for students who are certain about pursuing a management career early on and wish to secure an IIM degree without the later stress of CAT preparation.
