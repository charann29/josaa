# Basic Anatomy for Pilates Instructors by Udemy: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding the human body is fundamental for anyone aspiring to be a successful Pilates instructor. This course, offered on Udemy, provides a comprehensive introduction to anatomy specifically tailored for Pilates, helping instructors guide their clients safely and effectively.

## Key takeaways

*   The course covers essential anatomical concepts from the skeleton to muscular contractions, focusing on relevance to Pilates.
*   It is a self-paced, video and text-based program, allowing learners to study at their convenience.
*   Upon successful completion, students receive a certificate directly from Udemy.
*   The instruction is entirely in English, making it accessible to a wide audience.

## Course Overview and Learning Format

This self-study course is designed for individuals who prefer to learn at their own pace. The content is delivered through a combination of video lectures and supporting text, ensuring a multi-modal learning experience. All instructional material is provided in English, making it suitable for a broad range of learners.

## What You Will Learn: A Detailed Syllabus

The curriculum is structured to build anatomical knowledge progressively, starting with foundational concepts and moving into more specific details relevant to Pilates practice. Here's a breakdown of the key topics:

### Introduction and Foundational Concepts

The course begins with an introduction from instructor Holly Furgason, emphasizing the importance of anatomy for Pilates. It covers the anatomical position and essential terminology, including planes of motion, with practical in-studio recaps demonstrating their application in Pilates exercises.

### The Skeleton: Structure and Components

This section delves into the human skeleton, differentiating between the axial and appendicular skeletons. It meticulously covers the cranium, various sections of the spine (including quizzes on spinal sections), spinal curvature, and individual vertebrae. Further topics include the sternum, ribcage, sacrum, coccygeal vertebrae, and specific bones of the appendicular skeleton like the clavicle, scapula, humerus, radius, ulna, pelvis, femur, patella, tibia, and fibula. A quiz helps reinforce understanding.

### Movements of the Spine and Joints

Understanding movement is crucial for Pilates. This module explores various types of joint movements such as flexion/extension, abduction/adduction, lateral and medial rotation, circumduction, horizontal abduction/adduction, supination/pronation, and dorsiflexion/plantar flexion. It also details the six movements of the scapula, providing in-studio recaps for shoulder, elbow, ankle, knee, and hip movements, followed by a second quiz.

### Joint Types and Muscular Contractions

This section categorizes different types of joints in the body and reviews them for clarity. It then moves into the mechanics of muscular contraction, explaining concentric, eccentric, isometric, and isotonic contractions – concepts vital for understanding how muscles work during Pilates.

### The Core, Abdominals, and Trunk Muscles

A dedicated module explores the concept of "the core." It then details key abdominal muscles, including the internal oblique, external oblique, and rectus abdominus. Trunk muscles such as the erector spinea, quadratus lumborum, and internal and external intercostals are also covered.

### Lower and Upper Body Musculature

Comprehensive sections are devoted to the muscles of the lower and upper body. The lower body module covers quadriceps, iliopsoas, glutes, hamstrings, gastroc, soleus, tibialis anterior, peroneals, deep six lateral rotators, sartorius, adductors, pectineus, gracilis, tensor fasciae latae (TFL), and the iliotibial band (ITB). The upper body section includes serratus anterior, trapezius, rhomboid major and minor, deltoids, latissimus dorsi, pectoralis minor and major, biceps brachii, brachialis, brachioradialis, triceps brachii, rotator cuff, teres major, coracobrachialis, sternocleidomastoid, and levator scapulae.

### Bony Landmarks and Application

The course concludes by identifying important bony landmarks from different views (side, front, back), which is essential for hands-on instruction. A final quiz helps learners consolidate all the knowledge gained throughout the program.

This course offers a practical and accessible pathway for Pilates instructors to deepen their understanding of human anatomy, enhancing their teaching skills and client safety.
