# 10 Popular MBA in Marketing Colleges in India: Check Fee, Courses, Accepted Exams

Marketing is consistently one of the most sought-after specialisations for an MBA in India, offering exciting career paths and strong earning potential. A robust marketing education equips future professionals with essential skills in branding, advertising, and public relations, crucial for success in today's dynamic business landscape.

## Key takeaways

*   Many top B-schools in India offer specialised PGDM or MBA programs in Marketing.
*   Admission to these programs typically requires strong scores in national-level entrance exams like CAT, XAT, CMAT, or GMAT.
*   Course fees for a two-year MBA in Marketing can range significantly, often between INR 8 Lakhs to INR 25 Lakhs.
*   Beyond the core marketing curriculum, many institutes also offer other specialisations in areas like Finance, Operations, and Information Management.

## Understanding MBA/PGDM in Marketing

An MBA or PGDM (Post Graduate Diploma in Management) with a specialisation in Marketing is designed to develop leaders who can effectively strategise and execute marketing initiatives. These programs delve into consumer behaviour, market research, digital marketing, sales management, and brand strategy, preparing graduates for roles across various industries. While an MBA is a university-affiliated degree, a PGDM is an autonomous diploma, though both are widely recognised for their academic rigour and industry relevance.

## Top Institutions for Marketing Management

Several prestigious institutions across India are renowned for their MBA/PGDM in Marketing programs. These colleges are known for their comprehensive curriculum, experienced faculty, and strong industry connections, providing an excellent foundation for a career in marketing. Here's a look at some popular choices:

*   **SP Jain Institute of Management and Research (SPJIMR), Mumbai:** Offers a two-year full-time PGDM, equivalent to an MBA in Marketing. Accepted exams include CAT and GMAT. The approximate fee is INR 16,00,000.
*   **Institute of Management Technology (IMT), Ghaziabad:** Known for its PGDM (Marketing) program. This institute accepts scores from exams like CAT, XAT, and GMAT.
*   **Xavier Institute of Social Service (XISS), Ranchi:** Provides a PGDM with a specialisation in Marketing.
*   **N. L. Dalmia Institute of Management Studies and Research, Mumbai:** Offers a PGDBM (Marketing) program, accepting scores from CAT, XAT, CMAT, GMAT, ATMA, MAT, and MH-CET.
*   **NIILM Centre for Management Studies, Delhi/NCR:** Features a PGDM (Marketing) course.
*   **School of Management, KIIT University, Bhubaneswar:** Offers an MBA (Marketing Management) program.
*   **MIT School of Business (MIT-SOB), Pune:** Provides a PGDM (Marketing).
*   **Jamnalal Bajaj Institute of Management Studies (JBIMS), Mumbai:** Offers a Master of Management Studies (MMS) program, which includes Marketing as a key specialisation. Admission is primarily through MH-CET.
*   **K J Somaiya Institute of Management Studies and Research, Mumbai:** Also offers a Master of Management Studies (MMS) program with a strong Marketing focus.
*   **Balaji Institute of Modern Management (BIMM), Pune:** Known for its PGDM programs, including Marketing.

## Admission Process and Entrance Exams

Admission to these leading MBA/PGDM in Marketing programs is highly competitive. Candidates are typically required to appear for national-level management entrance examinations. The most commonly accepted exams include:

*   **CAT (Common Admission Test):** Conducted by the IIMs, it's widely accepted by most top B-schools across India.
*   **XAT (Xavier Aptitude Test):** Primarily for XLRI and other XAT-affiliated institutes.
*   **CMAT (Common Management Admission Test):** Conducted by NTA, it's accepted by a large number of AICTE-approved institutions.
*   **GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test):** A globally recognised exam, preferred by some institutes, especially for candidates with work experience or international aspirations.
*   **MAT (Management Aptitude Test):** Accepted by many B-schools across India.
*   **ATMA (AIMS Test for Management Admissions):** Another widely accepted management entrance test.
*   **MH-CET (Maharashtra Common Entrance Test):** Crucial for admission to management programs in Maharashtra, including JBIMS and K J Somaiya.

Following the entrance exam, shortlisted candidates usually undergo a rigorous selection process, which may include Group Discussions (GD) and Personal Interviews (PI).

## Fees and Other Programs

The fee structure for a two-year MBA/PGDM in Marketing varies significantly across institutions, generally ranging from approximately INR 8,00,000 to INR 25,00,000. It's advisable to check the official websites of individual colleges for the most up-to-date fee information. Many of these institutions also offer other postgraduate management programs and specialisations, such as Finance, Operations, Information Management, Human Resources, and Business Analytics, catering to a diverse range of career interests.

Choosing the right MBA in Marketing program can be a pivotal step in your career journey, opening doors to leadership roles in advertising, brand management, sales, market research, and digital marketing.
