# CAT 2024 Topper Interview: Swaraj's Inspiring Journey to a 99.95 Percentile Without Coaching

Swaraj Pal Kesari achieved an outstanding 99.95 percentile in the CAT 2024 examination, a remarkable feat accomplished without any external coaching. His journey from an engineering role at Oracle to becoming a CAT topper offers valuable lessons for aspiring MBA candidates.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Self-Preparation is Possible:** Swaraj's success proves that dedicated self-study can lead to top percentiles in CAT, even for General Engineering Male (GEM) candidates facing high cut-offs.
*   **Strategic Sectional Focus:** He meticulously identified his weaker areas (VARC) and stronger areas (Quant) and tailored his preparation accordingly, focusing on comprehension and extensive practice.
*   **Learning from Previous Attempts:** A prior attempt, though without preparation, provided crucial insights into the exam's demands, particularly in quantitative aptitude.
*   **Mindset and Practice are Key:** Maintaining composure during the exam, especially in DILR, and consistently practicing diverse problem sets were central to his strategy.

## About Swaraj and His Motivation

Originally from Bihar but raised in Hyderabad, Swaraj completed his Engineering in Electronics and Communication from NIT Silchar before joining Oracle. Despite a technical role, his passion for business and entrepreneurship remained strong. He recalls the thrill of a business venture during his second year of college, which fueled his desire for an MBA. Beyond academics, Swaraj enjoys writing, fitness, and learning about the stock market.

## His CAT 2024 Scores

Swaraj's impressive performance across all sections contributed to his high overall percentile:

| Section | Score | Percentile |
| :------ | :---- | :--------- |
| Overall | 130.65 | 99.95      |
| DILR    | 58.49 | 99.98      |
| VARC    | 28.16 | 93.79      |
| QA      | 44.01 | 99.88      |

This was not Swaraj's first attempt. He had taken the CAT exam the previous year without preparation, scoring around 93 percentile. This initial experience, particularly with the quantitative section in Slot 3, highlighted the importance of thorough preparation for his subsequent attempt.

## Preparation Strategy: A Section-Wise Breakdown

Swaraj began his dedicated preparation in mid-February. As a GEM candidate, he knew the cut-offs for top IIMs would be high, necessitating comprehensive coverage of all topics and ample practice time to build familiarity with various question types.

### VARC (Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension)

Recognizing VARC as his weaker section, Swaraj dedicated significant effort here. For the first three months, starting in February, he focused on daily reading of articles, prioritizing deep comprehension even if it took longer. He consistently solved Reading Comprehension (RC) passages and Verbal Ability (VA) questions. He learned to identify the paper setter's intent rather than imposing his own interpretations. For VA, he accepted that 100% accuracy might not be achievable and instead focused on analyzing and correcting mistakes after each mock test. During the actual exam, his strategy was to stick strictly to the passage content and attempt three RCs along with all VA questions.

### DILR (Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning)

Swaraj watched extensive video lectures by Ravi Prakash sir to understand the various types of sets asked in CAT DILR. His primary goal was to practice a wide array of diverse sets to be prepared for any challenge. He also found that mock tests from various institutes helped him prepare for the most difficult scenarios. His exam-day strategy for DILR involved staying calm, recognizing moments of panic, and focusing on cracking at least two sets confidently.

### QA (Quantitative Ability)

As an engineer, Swaraj considered QA his strong suit. His main challenge was avoiding silly mistakes due to overconfidence or excitement, which had cost him marks in the past. To refine his foundational understanding and prevent such errors, he also reviewed Ravi Prakash sir's QA playlist.

Swaraj's journey underscores that with a clear strategy, persistent effort, and a willingness to learn from every experience, achieving a top CAT percentile without coaching is entirely within reach.
