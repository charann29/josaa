# Facing an Aadhaar Linkage Issue with Your KCET Application?

It can be incredibly frustrating to encounter a technical glitch when trying to register for important exams like KCET, especially when your Aadhaar seems to be linked to an application you never completed. This guide will help you understand what might be happening and how to resolve it.

## Key Takeaways

*   An Aadhaar linkage error often means a previous, incomplete attempt at registration might be in the system, even without an application number.
*   You cannot resolve this issue on your own; direct communication with the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) is essential.
*   Be prepared to provide all relevant personal details and explain the situation clearly to KEA officials.
*   Early action is crucial to ensure you don't miss application deadlines.

## Understanding the Aadhaar Linkage Problem

You're trying to apply for KCET, but the system says your Aadhaar number is already associated with another application. The confusing part is, you never finished an application, didn't get an application number, and haven't received any confirmation emails. This usually happens when you start the registration process, enter your Aadhaar details, but then a technical error prevents you from completing it. Even though you didn't get an application number, the initial entry of your Aadhaar might have been recorded in the system, preventing subsequent attempts.

## Why an Application Number Wasn't Generated

When a technical problem occurs during an online application, the process often stops before all steps are completed. Generating an application number is typically one of the final steps, confirming that your submission is officially recorded. If the system crashed or you faced connectivity issues, it's very likely that this crucial step was never reached, leaving you without a reference number and unable to log in.

## What to Do: Contact the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA)

Since this is a system-level issue involving your unique Aadhaar identification, you cannot resolve it by simply trying again or through the online portal. The only way to fix this is to directly contact the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA), the official body responsible for KCET.

### How to Reach KEA

It is vital to get in touch with KEA promptly. They are the only ones who can verify the Aadhaar linkage issue on their backend and potentially reset or enable your Aadhaar for a fresh registration. Here are the contact details:

*   **Phone:** 080 - 23460460
    *   *Operating Hours:* 10:30 AM to 5:30 PM on working days.
*   **Address:** Sampige Road, 18th Cross, Malleshwaram, Bangalore-560012

### Preparing for Your Call or Visit

When you contact KEA, be ready to explain your situation clearly and calmly. Provide them with your full name, your Aadhaar number, and the exact date and time (if you remember) when you first tried to register and encountered the error. Emphasize that no application number was generated and you have no record of a successful submission. Request them to verify the Aadhaar linkage and enable it for you to complete the KCET registration process successfully.

Addressing this issue quickly will help ensure you can complete your KCET application without further delays.
