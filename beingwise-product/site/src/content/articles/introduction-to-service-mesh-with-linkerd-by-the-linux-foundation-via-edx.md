# Introduction to Service Mesh with Linkerd by The Linux Foundation via Edx: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For anyone navigating the modern cloud-native landscape, understanding service meshes is crucial. The Linux Foundation, a leading organization in open-source technology, offers an introductory course on edX focused on Linkerd, an ultralight, open-source service mesh for Kubernetes.

## Key takeaways

*   This is a beginner-friendly, self-paced online course, delivered over ten weeks, requiring 3-5 hours of study per week.
*   It introduces Linkerd, a popular CNCF (Cloud Native Computing Foundation) service mesh, for enhancing reliability, observability, and security in Kubernetes applications.
*   While enrollment is free, a verified certificate and unlimited course access are available through a paid 'verified track'.
*   The curriculum emphasizes practical, hands-on learning, guided by an expert instructor, Mr. Charles Pretzer.

## Course Overview and Learning Experience

This program is designed to equip participants with practical knowledge of service meshes, particularly Linkerd, within a Kubernetes environment. You'll learn how to implement this critical cloud-native technology to add layers of reliability, observability, and security to your applications. The course is entirely online, delivered through video and text-based modules in English, allowing you to learn at your own pace.

Over the ten-week duration, you'll receive instructions on downloading necessary software and performing exercises alongside the lessons. This hands-on approach ensures you gain valuable, real-world experience.

## What You Will Learn

Upon completing this program, you will develop a solid understanding of service meshes and their practical application with Linkerd. Specifically, you will be able to:

*   Define what a service mesh is and articulate its advantages over other technologies.
*   Install and configure a service mesh using Linkerd on Kubernetes.
*   Understand key considerations for deploying a service mesh in production environments.
*   Implement mutual TLS (mTLS) for enhanced security using a service mesh.
*   Apply advanced deployment strategies like blue-green deployments and integrate golden metrics into any Kubernetes application.

## Eligibility Criteria

To make the most of this introductory course, prospective participants should have a foundational understanding of Kubernetes. This includes familiarity with running a Kubernetes cluster and comfort working within a Linux terminal. Additionally, you should be capable of building container images and deploying them in Kubernetes using `kubectl`.

## Course Tracks and Certification

The course offers two distinct tracks for learners:

*   **Audit Track:** This track allows free enrollment and provides limited-period access to the course materials. However, it does not include a certificate of completion.
*   **Verified Track:** For those seeking unlimited access to course content and a shareable, verified certificate upon successful completion, the verified track is available for a fee. This option is ideal for individuals who wish to formally validate their newly acquired skills.

## How to Enroll

Enrolling in the Introduction to Service Mesh with Linkerd course is a straightforward process:

1.  Visit the official course page on the edX platform.
2.  Look for the "Sign In/Register" option, typically located in the top right corner, or click on the "Enroll now" button.
3.  You will be directed to a registration page. Here, you can either sign in with an existing account (using Apple, Google, Facebook, or Microsoft credentials) or create a new edX account by providing the required details.
4.  Once registered, your enrollment in the course will be complete.

There is no separate application form for this training; enrollment is direct through the edX platform.

This program offers a valuable opportunity to delve into a critical aspect of cloud-native development, equipping you with skills highly sought after in today's tech landscape.
