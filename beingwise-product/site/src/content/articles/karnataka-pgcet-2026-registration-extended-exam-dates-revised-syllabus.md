# Karnataka PGCET 2026: Registration (Extended), Exam Dates (Revised), Syllabus, Pattern, Question Papers

The Karnataka Post Graduate Common Entrance Test (PGCET) is your gateway to postgraduate studies in Karnataka. Conducted by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA), this state-level exam opens doors to various Master's programs across the state.

## Key Takeaways

*   The application deadline for Karnataka PGCET 2026 has been extended to April 20, with fee payment allowed until April 22.
*   The examination dates have been set for May 23 (for ME/MTech) and May 24 (for MBA/MCA).
*   This entrance test is crucial for admissions to MBA, MCA, ME, MTech, and MArch programs in Karnataka.
*   The exam is conducted offline, featuring 100 multiple-choice questions to be completed in 150 minutes.

## What is Karnataka PGCET?

The Karnataka PGCET is an essential entrance examination for students aspiring to pursue postgraduate degrees such as Master of Business Administration (MBA), Master of Computer Applications (MCA), Master of Engineering (ME), Master of Technology (MTech), and Master of Architecture (MArch) in various institutions across Karnataka. While the PGCET form is required for MArch admissions to participate in the centralized counseling, the final admission for MArch may also consider other entrance scores like NATA PGETA.

The exam structure consists of 100 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) that candidates must answer within a duration of 150 minutes. It is conducted in an offline mode at designated test centers throughout the state.

## Application Process for Karnataka PGCET 2026

The application process for Karnataka PGCET 2026 is managed online through the official KEA portal. Candidates holding a relevant bachelor's degree are eligible to apply. The KEA has extended the deadline, allowing more time for prospective students to complete their applications. Ensure all details are accurate before final submission, and remember to pay the application fee by the specified date to complete your registration.

### Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for Karnataka PGCET, candidates must possess a relevant bachelor's degree from a recognized university. Specific percentage requirements may vary depending on the course you are applying for. Always refer to the official KEA information bulletin for detailed eligibility conditions for each program.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The Karnataka PGCET exam pattern generally includes sections that assess a candidate's aptitude, reasoning, and subject-specific knowledge. For MBA and MCA courses, sections typically cover General Knowledge, English Language, Test of Reasoning, and Quantitative Analysis. For ME/MTech courses, the syllabus is usually based on relevant engineering disciplines.

*   **Exam Mode:** Offline (OMR-based)
*   **Question Type:** Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
*   **Total Questions:** 100
*   **Total Marks:** 100
*   **Duration:** 150 minutes (2 hours 30 minutes)

It is highly recommended to download the official syllabus from the KEA website to understand the topics thoroughly and prepare effectively. Reviewing previous years' question papers can also provide valuable insights into the exam's structure and common question types.

Prepare well and keep an eye on the official KEA website for any further updates regarding the examination process.
