# Best Medical Colleges in Delhi for MBBS 2026: Fees, Ranking, Admissions, Courses

Delhi is a prime destination for aspiring doctors, offering a wealth of prestigious medical institutions. For those aiming to pursue an MBBS degree in 2026, understanding the admissions process, fees, and rankings is crucial.

## Key Takeaways

*   Delhi boasts over 10 full-time MBBS colleges, including both government and private institutions.
*   Admission to nearly all top medical colleges in Delhi relies on scores from the NEET-UG examination.
*   AIIMS Delhi consistently ranks among the top medical colleges nationally and internationally.
*   Government colleges in Delhi offer over 1200 MBBS seats, with additional opportunities in private institutions.

## Top Medical Colleges in Delhi

Several institutions in Delhi are highly regarded for their MBBS programs. These colleges are known for their rigorous academics, excellent faculty, and state-of-the-art facilities. Leading names include:

*   All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), Delhi
*   Vardhaman Mahavir Medical College (VMMC) & Safdarjung Hospital
*   Maulana Azad Medical College (MAMC)
*   Lady Hardinge Medical College (LHMC)
*   University College of Medical Sciences (UCMS)
*   Jamia Hamdard University (Hamdard Institute of Medical Sciences & Research)

These colleges are consistently featured in national rankings and are highly sought after by students across India.

## NIRF Rankings for Delhi Medical Colleges

The National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF), an initiative by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, provides a comprehensive ranking of higher education institutions. AIIMS Delhi typically holds the top position in the medical category, reflecting its excellence in teaching, research, and patient care. Other Delhi colleges like VMMC, MAMC, LHMC, and UCMS also feature prominently in the top medical college rankings, underscoring their quality education.

## Eligibility Criteria for MBBS Admissions 2026

To be considered for an MBBS program in Delhi, candidates must meet specific academic and age requirements. Generally, these include:

*   **Age Limit:** Candidates must be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year (2026).
*   **Academic Qualification:** Applicants must have passed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A minimum aggregate of 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (40% for SC/ST/OBC candidates) is usually required.
*   **NEET-UG:** A valid and competitive score in the NEET-UG entrance examination conducted by NTA is mandatory for admission to most MBBS colleges.

## Admission Process and Key Dates

Admission to MBBS colleges in Delhi primarily happens through the NEET-UG examination. The NEET-UG 2026 exam is anticipated to be held on May 3, 2026. Following the declaration of NEET results, the counselling process begins.

*   **All India Quota (AIQ):** The Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) manages counselling for 15% of MBBS seats in government medical colleges across India, including those in Delhi, and 100% of seats in Deemed Universities.
*   **State Quota:** The Directorate General of Health Services conducts state-level counselling for 85% of MBBS seats in government and private medical colleges in Delhi, reserved for candidates eligible under the State Quota.

Candidates who qualify for NEET are eligible to participate in both counselling processes.

## MBBS Course and Fee Structure

The MBBS program is a comprehensive undergraduate medical course spanning 5.5 years, which includes a one-year compulsory rotating internship. The curriculum covers a wide array of subjects, including anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pharmacology, pathology, microbiology, forensic medicine, community medicine, and various clinical specialities like general medicine, surgery, paediatrics, obstetrics & gynaecology, and more.

Fees for MBBS programs vary significantly between government and private institutions. Government medical colleges generally have much lower fees, making them highly competitive. Private colleges and deemed universities tend to have higher fee structures.
