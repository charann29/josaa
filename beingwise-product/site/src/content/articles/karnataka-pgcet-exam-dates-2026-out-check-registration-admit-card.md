# Karnataka PGCET Exam Dates 2026 (OUT): Check Registration, Admit Card & Results

For students aspiring to pursue postgraduate studies in Karnataka, the Karnataka Post Graduate Common Entrance Test (PGCET) is a crucial examination. This test opens doors to various Master's programs, including MBA, MCA, ME, MTech, and MArch, offered across colleges in the state.

## Key Takeaways

*   The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) has officially released the PGCET 2026 exam dates for MBA, MCA, ME, and MTech courses.
*   Registration for PGCET 2026 has been extended, with the new deadline for applications set for April 20 and fee payment until April 22.
*   The ME/MTech examination is scheduled for May 23, while the MBA/MCA exams will take place on May 24.
*   All applications and related processes are managed through the official KEA portal.

## Karnataka PGCET 2026 Exam Schedule

Below is a detailed overview of the key dates for Karnataka PGCET 2026, helping you plan your application and preparation effectively.

| Event                               | Dates                       |

| PGCET 2026 Registration             | March 23 - April 20, 2026   |
| Application Fee Payment Deadline    | March 23 - April 22, 2026   |
| Admit Card Download (MBA/MCA)       | May 2026 (Tentative)        |
| Exam Date (ME/MTech)                | May 23, 2026                |
| Exam Date (MBA/MCA)                 | May 24, 2026                |
| Provisional Answer Key Release      | June 2026 (Tentative)       |
| Result Declaration                  | August 2026 (Tentative)     |

## Registration and Application Process

The application window for Karnataka PGCET 2026 is currently open. Aspiring candidates must complete their registration online via the official KEA website, cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea/pgcet2026. It's important to note the extended deadline for registration, which is now April 20, with the final date for application fee submission being April 22. The KEA manages all aspects of the application, including any potential extensions or reopening of the registration period.

### Application Form Correction Window

After the initial registration, KEA typically provides a short window, usually three to four days, for applicants to correct any errors in their submitted forms. Candidates should monitor the official KEA announcements for specific dates regarding this correction facility.

## Admit Card Details

The admit card, also known as the hall ticket, is a mandatory document for appearing in the PGCET examination. KEA will release the admit cards online for all eligible candidates who have successfully completed their application and fee payment. While a precise date for the admit card download for MBA/MCA courses is yet to be confirmed, it is tentatively expected in May 2026. Candidates should regularly check the official KEA portal for updates.

## Examination Format

The Karnataka PGCET for ME and MTech courses is scheduled for May 23. The following day, May 24, will host the examinations for MBA and MCA programs. The MCA exam will be conducted in the first session of the day, followed by the MBA exam in the second session. Both examinations are held in an offline mode.

## Answer Key and Results

Following the examinations, KEA will release a provisional answer key, tentatively in June 2026. This allows candidates to review their responses and raise any objections if necessary. The final results for Karnataka PGCET 2026 are expected to be declared around August 2026. All official announcements regarding answer keys and results will be made on the KEA's official website.

Staying informed about these dates and guidelines is essential for a smooth application and examination experience.
