*   Always refer to the official TS EAMCET counselling notifications and the admission brochures of universities like Professor Jayashankar Telangana State Agricultural University (PJTSAU) for the most accurate and up-to-date cutoff information.

## BSc Agriculture vs. Agricultural Engineering

It's important to distinguish between BSc Agriculture and Agricultural Engineering. While both are related to the agricultural sector, they are distinct disciplines:

*   **BSc Agriculture:** This is a four-year undergraduate program focused on agricultural sciences, including agronomy, horticulture, soil science, plant pathology, and agricultural economics. It's often the most sought-after program for students aiming for careers directly in farming, research, or extension services.
*   **BTech Agricultural Engineering:** This is also a four-year engineering program that applies engineering principles to the design, development, and improvement of agricultural machinery, structures, and processes. It covers areas like farm machinery, soil and water conservation engineering, and post-harvest technology.

Historically, the cutoff ranks for BSc Agriculture tend to be lower (meaning, more competitive, requiring higher ranks) than for Agricultural Engineering, especially in institutions affiliated with PJTSAU.
