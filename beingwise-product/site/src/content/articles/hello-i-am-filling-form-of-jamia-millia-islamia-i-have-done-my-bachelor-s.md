Navigating university application forms can sometimes feel a bit tricky, especially when it comes to entering your academic details correctly. If you're applying to Jamia Millia Islamia (JMI) after completing your Bachelor's in Psychology from Mumbai University, it's natural to wonder how to present your graduation scores.

## Key Takeaways

*   Always provide your **aggregate score** from all six semesters for your undergraduate degree when applying to JMI.
*   Do not just enter your final (6th) semester scores.
*   If your Mumbai University marksheet shows a grand total or overall CGPA for your entire degree, use that figure.
*   If your result is in CGPA, convert it to a percentage using Mumbai University's official conversion formula before entering it into the JMI application portal.

## Understanding Application Requirements for JMI

When universities like Jamia Millia Islamia ask for your graduation details, they are generally looking for your overall academic performance throughout your entire degree program. This helps them assess whether you meet the minimum eligibility criteria for the postgraduate course you are applying for. Providing only a single semester's score would not give a complete picture of your academic standing.

## How to Calculate Your Aggregate Score

For Mumbai University graduates, your marksheets typically include a consolidated grand total or a final Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) that reflects your performance across all six semesters. This is the figure you should use. Look for the section on your official marksheet that summarizes your overall result for the entire Bachelor's degree.

## Converting CGPA to Percentage

If your Mumbai University marksheet presents your final result as a CGPA, it's crucial to convert this into a percentage if the JMI application portal specifically asks for a percentage. Each university has its own official formula for CGPA to percentage conversion. It is vital to use the formula provided by Mumbai University to ensure accuracy. You can usually find this information on your university's official website, academic regulations, or sometimes even printed on your marksheet itself. Do not use a generic conversion formula, as this might lead to discrepancies.

## Why Aggregate Scores Matter

Universities use aggregate scores to establish a fair and consistent standard for evaluating all applicants. This ensures that everyone is assessed on their complete academic journey, rather than just a snapshot of their performance at one point. It's a standard practice across most higher education institutions for both undergraduate and postgraduate admissions.

Ensuring your application details are accurate and complete is a key step towards a successful submission to Jamia Millia Islamia. Good luck with your application!
