# Financial Accounting by Association of Chartered Certified Accountants via Edx: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding financial accounting is crucial for anyone looking to make sense of business finances. This online course, developed by the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA) and delivered through Edx, offers a comprehensive introduction to the core principles and techniques of financial accounting.

## Key takeaways

*   Learn essential financial accounting concepts, from double-entry bookkeeping to creating financial statements.
*   The course is self-paced and delivered entirely online, requiring 5-8 hours of study per week.
*   While the Edx platform provides course content, official certification comes directly from ACCA after passing their computer-based exam.
*   No prior work experience is necessary, making it suitable for both beginners and those looking to formalize existing skills.

## Course Overview

This program is designed to equip you with fundamental business and financial accounting skills. You'll learn how to interpret financial statements, understand the need for accounting in various professional fields, and develop the ability to construct different types of financial reports. The curriculum starts with foundational concepts like double-entry bookkeeping and progresses to more complex aspects of financial statement preparation.

The course is self-paced, allowing you to learn at your own speed. For those on the verified track, access to course materials is unlimited. This flexibility makes it an excellent option for busy individuals seeking to enhance their financial literacy.

## What You Will Learn

Upon successful completion of this program, you will gain a deep understanding of several critical financial accounting areas. The learning outcomes include:

*   Grasping the primary concepts of financial accounting.
*   Developing techniques to determine a business's financial health through its statements.
*   Mastering the approach to creating various business and financial account statements.
*   Understanding the subjective attributes of finance-related information.
*   Gaining knowledge about the factors and purpose behind financial reporting.
*   Learning the methods for constructing a trial balance.
*   Understanding the requirements and utility of accounting systems, including the double-entry system.
*   Developing the methodology for documenting events and transactions within financial statements.
*   Learning how to interpret and correct errors found in a trial balance.
*   Acquiring skills in documenting, sorting, monitoring, and describing transactions essential for financial accounting.

## Who Is This Course For?

This course is ideal for a wide range of individuals, including aspiring accountants, financial planners, financial advisors, and those pursuing certifications like Chartered Accountant or Certified Management Accountant. It's also highly recommended for anyone new to finance or those already in the field who wish to gain a formal qualification or strengthen their foundational knowledge without prior professional experience.

## Certification and Fees

While you will access the course content through Edx, the platform itself does not provide a certificate for this specific program. To earn official recognition, you must register directly with ACCA as a student. After completing the computer-based examination set by ACCA, you will be eligible to obtain the ACCA Diploma in Business and Accounting. Details regarding ACCA registration and examination fees should be checked on the official ACCA website.

## Duration and Learning Commitment

The course is structured to be completed over approximately 12 weeks, with an estimated weekly time commitment of 5-8 hours. It is delivered through a combination of video lectures and text-based materials, supporting a self-study learning model. This allows for flexibility in scheduling your learning around other commitments while ensuring consistent progress.

This program provides an excellent pathway to build a strong foundation in financial accounting, opening doors to various professional opportunities in the finance sector.
