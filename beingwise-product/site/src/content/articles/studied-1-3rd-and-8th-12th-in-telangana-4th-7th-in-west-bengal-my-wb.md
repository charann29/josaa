# Facing Study Certificate Hurdles for TS EAMCET?

It can be a bit stressful when you're trying to apply for important exams like TS EAMCET and run into issues with your documentation. Especially when a school from a different state isn't able to provide a bonafide certificate, and you need study certificates for specific years.

## Key Takeaways

*   An affidavit from a notary can often serve as a valid substitute for missing study certificates.
*   Gather all available study certificates, even if some years are missing.
*   Your local candidate status in Telangana is likely valid based on the 7-year study rule.
*   Always communicate with the TS EAMCET convenor's office for official guidance.

## Understanding the Study Certificate Requirement

TS EAMCET typically asks for study certificates from Class 6th to 12th to confirm your educational background and, importantly, to determine your local or non-local status. If you studied in Telangana for Classes 1st-3rd and then again for 8th-12th, while your 4th-7th grades were in West Bengal, the missing certificates for 6th and 7th become a point of concern. The crucial part here is that the West Bengal school is unable to issue these. Don't worry, there are established ways to address such situations.

## The Power of an Affidavit

When an official document like a study certificate cannot be obtained from the issuing institution, an affidavit often comes to the rescue. Here's how you can approach this:

1.  **Gather What You Have:** Collect all the study certificates you *do* have, especially for Classes 6th-12th from Telangana. This shows your genuine effort to comply.
2.  **Visit a Notary Public:** Explain your situation clearly to a notary. You will need to state that you studied in West Bengal for Classes 6th and 7th, and that despite your best efforts, the school there is unable to provide the bonafide or study certificate. The notary will then prepare an affidavit, which is a sworn statement, confirming these facts.
3.  **Why it Works:** An affidavit is a legally recognized document that verifies facts under oath. Many examination bodies, including those for state-level entrance tests, accept notarized affidavits in place of missing certificates when the original cannot be procured.

## Your Local Candidate Status

Given your study history of 1st-3rd and 8th-12th in Telangana, your 'local candidate' status for Telangana is likely accurate. The '7-year study rule' for local status generally considers the period of study from Class 4th to 10th, or equivalent. Since you have significant years of study within Telangana that meet these criteria, your declaration of being a local candidate on the application form should be valid. The official TS EAMCET information bulletin provides detailed criteria for local status, and it's always a good idea to cross-reference your specific situation with those guidelines.

## Reaching Out to the Authorities

While an affidavit is a strong step, it's always wise to directly communicate with the TS EAMCET convenor's office. You can:

*   **Explain Your Situation:** Clearly articulate the issue regarding the missing 6th and 7th class study certificates and your efforts to obtain them.
*   **Provide Proof:** Have your notarized affidavit ready, along with any other documents that support your claim (like study certificates from other years, or even correspondence with the West Bengal school if available).
*   **Seek Clarification:** Ask if the affidavit will be acceptable for the missing certificates and if there are any other specific procedures you need to follow. The official TS EAMCET website or helpline numbers would be the best point of contact for this.

By taking these steps, you can confidently address the documentation requirements and ensure your TS EAMCET application proceeds smoothly. Remember, persistence and clear communication are key.
