# IBPS Clerk 2026: Notification (Soon), Application Process, Eligibility, Syllabus, Pattern, Vacancies & FAQs

For those aspiring to build a career in India's public sector banks, the IBPS Clerk examination offers a significant pathway. This annual national-level exam recruits candidates for clerical roles, often referred to as Customer Service Associates (CSA), across various nationalized banks.

## Key takeaways

*   The Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) will soon release the official notification for the IBPS Clerk 2026 examination.
*   The preliminary exam is tentatively scheduled for October 10 and 11, 2026, followed by the main exam on December 27, 2026.
*   The selection process involves two stages: a preliminary online examination and a main online examination.
*   Successful candidates are recruited into one of the 11 participating public sector banks across India.

## Application Process and Eligibility

The application process for the IBPS Clerk exam is entirely online. Candidates will need to visit the official IBPS website to register, fill out the application form, upload required documents, and pay the examination fee. Specific dates and detailed instructions will be provided in the upcoming official notification.

While precise eligibility criteria will be outlined in the official notification, candidates typically need to hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university. There are also age limits, usually between 20 and 28 years, with relaxations for reserved categories as per government norms. It's crucial for all applicants to carefully review the detailed eligibility requirements once the official notification is published.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The IBPS Clerk examination is conducted in two phases: Preliminary and Mains. Both exams are held online.

### Preliminary Exam Pattern

The preliminary exam is a qualifying stage. It consists of three sections and candidates must clear the sectional and overall cut-offs to be eligible for the main exam.

| Section             | No. of Questions | Max Marks | Duration (Minutes) |
| :------------------ | :--------------- | :-------- | :----------------- |
| English Language    | 30               | 30        | 20                 |
| Numerical Ability   | 35               | 35        | 20                 |
| Reasoning Ability   | 35               | 35        | 20                 |
| **Total**           | **100**          | **100**   | **60**             |

### Main Exam Pattern

The main exam scores are considered for the final merit list. It has four sections, with a combined duration of 160 minutes.

| Section                  | No. of Questions | Max Marks | Duration (Minutes) |
| :----------------------- | :--------------- | :-------- | :----------------- |
| General/ Financial Aware | 50               | 50        | 35                 |
| General English          | 40               | 40        | 35                 |
| Reasoning Ability & Comp | 50               | 60        | 45                 |
| Quantitative Aptitude    | 50               | 50        | 45                 |
| **Total**                | **190**          | **200**   | **160**            |

**Marking Scheme:** For both preliminary and main exams, there is a negative marking of 0.25 marks for each incorrect answer.

**Syllabus:** The syllabus generally covers topics such as English Language (grammar, vocabulary, comprehension), Reasoning Ability (puzzles, seating arrangements, syllogism), Numerical Ability/Quantitative Aptitude (simplification, data interpretation, arithmetic problems), and General/Financial Awareness (current affairs, banking awareness, static GK).
