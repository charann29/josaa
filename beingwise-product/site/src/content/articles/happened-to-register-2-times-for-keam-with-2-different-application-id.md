# I Registered Twice for KEAM – What Now?

It can be worrying to realize you've submitted multiple applications for a crucial exam like KEAM. Let's clarify what this might mean for your applications and what steps you can take.

## Key Takeaways

*   KEAM guidelines generally permit only one application per candidate.
*   Multiple applications can lead to issues during the verification process, potentially resulting in cancellation.
*   Since you've already emailed the KEAM authorities, retain a record of that communication.
*   Stay vigilant by frequently checking the official KEAM portal for any updates or notifications.

## Understanding KEAM Application Rules

The Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE), Kerala, which conducts KEAM, typically specifies that each candidate should submit only one application form. This is a standard practice for many competitive entrance exams across India, including those managed by NTA. The purpose is to maintain fairness and streamline the application and admission process. Submitting more than one application, even for different courses, can be flagged as a duplicate entry.

## Why Multiple Applications Can Be Problematic

When the CEE processes applications, their system usually identifies candidates based on unique identifiers such as name, date of birth, and other personal details. If two applications share these core details but have different application IDs, it raises a red flag. This can lead to your applications being held for scrutiny. In some cases, authorities might cancel both applications to ensure compliance with their single-application policy. The risk of cancellation is higher if the discrepancy is not resolved promptly.

## What to Do Next

### 1. Keep Records of Your Communication

You mentioned sending an email to the authorities regarding your duplicate registration. This is an excellent first step. Make sure you have a copy of that email, including the date it was sent and the recipient's email address. If you received any automated acknowledgment or a reply, save that as well. This documentation proves you proactively informed the CEE about the situation.

### 2. Regularly Check the Official KEAM Portal

The official KEAM portal is your primary source for all information, updates, and announcements. The CEE might issue a notice, send an individual email, or update your application status if they take action on your case. Log in to your application dashboard frequently to see if there are any messages or changes related to your applications. Look out for sections like 'Notifications,' 'Announcements,' or 'Application Status.'

### 3. Consider Direct Contact (If No Response)

If you don't receive a response to your email within a reasonable timeframe (e.g., a week) and see no updates on the portal, you might consider reaching out through other official channels. Check the KEAM information brochure or the official portal for contact numbers or helpdesks. A phone call, while sometimes challenging, can sometimes provide quicker clarification than email, especially if your query is urgent. Clearly explain your situation, reference your application IDs, and mention your previous email communication.

## Preventing Future Issues

While your current situation needs resolution, it's a good reminder to always double-check application guidelines carefully before applying for any entrance exam. When filling out forms, take your time, review all entries, and ensure you understand the rules regarding single vs. multiple applications.

Addressing this issue promptly is crucial to safeguard your chances of appearing for KEAM and pursuing your desired courses.
