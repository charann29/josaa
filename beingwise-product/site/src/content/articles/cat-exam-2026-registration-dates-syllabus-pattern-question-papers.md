# CAT Exam 2026: Registration, Dates, Syllabus, Pattern, Question Papers

The Common Admission Test (CAT) is a crucial national-level entrance exam for aspiring MBA and PGDM students in India. Conducted annually by one of the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs), CAT scores are accepted by all IIMs and numerous other top business schools across the country.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Exam Date:** The CAT 2026 exam is scheduled for November 29, 2026, held in three shifts on a single day.
*   **Registration:** The application process is expected to open in the first week of August 2026 and close by the third week of September 2026 on the official iimcat.ac.in portal.
*   **Eligibility:** Candidates need a bachelor's degree with at least 50% aggregate marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD categories).
*   **Structure:** The exam tests Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), and Quantitative Ability (QA) through a Computer-Based Test (CBT) format.

## Understanding the CAT Exam

The CAT exam is designed to assess a candidate's aptitude for management studies. It is known for its high difficulty level and requires thorough preparation across its three main sections. Beyond the prestigious IIMs, many other leading institutions like FMS Delhi, SPJIMR Mumbai, MDI Gurgaon, and over 1,300 other B-schools in India consider CAT scores for their MBA/PGDM programs.

## CAT 2026 Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for CAT 2026, candidates must meet the following requirements:

*   Hold a Bachelor's degree with at least 50% aggregate marks or an equivalent CGPA. This requirement is relaxed to 45% for candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disabilities (PwD) categories.
*   Candidates in their final year of a Bachelor's degree or those awaiting results are also eligible to apply, provided they complete their degree within the stipulated timeline by the admitting institution.
*   There is no age limit to appear for the CAT exam.

## CAT 2026 Registration Process and Fees

The registration for CAT 2026 will be conducted online through the official iimcat.ac.in website. Candidates will need to create an account, fill out the application form with personal and academic details, upload required documents (photograph and signature), and pay the application fee.

Historically, the CAT application fee has seen annual adjustments. For 2026, while the exact amount will be confirmed in the official notification, it is anticipated to be around:

*   **General Category:** Approximately INR 2,600
*   **Reserved Categories (SC/ST/PwD):** Approximately INR 1,300

It is crucial to complete the registration process accurately and within the specified deadlines to avoid any issues.

## CAT 2026 Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The CAT exam is a Computer-Based Test (CBT) lasting two hours, divided into three sections with specific time limits. The exam includes both Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs) and Non-MCQ (Type-In-The-Answer) questions.

### Exam Pattern:

| Section                               | Number of Questions (Approx.) | Time Allotted (Minutes) |
| :------------------------------------ | :---------------------------- | :---------------------- |
| Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension| 24                            | 40                      |
| Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning| 20                            | 40                      |
| Quantitative Ability                  | 22                            | 40                      |
| **Total**                             | **66**                        | **120**                 |

*Note: The exact number of questions may vary slightly each year, as per the conducting IIM's discretion.*

### Syllabus:

*   **Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC):** This section evaluates reading comprehension, verbal ability (sentence correction, fill in the blanks), para jumbles, and summary writing.
*   **Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR):** This section assesses a candidate's ability to interpret data presented in various forms (tables, charts, graphs) and solve logical reasoning puzzles.
*   **Quantitative Ability (QA):** This section covers topics from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and modern mathematics, testing mathematical problem-solving skills.

Thorough preparation across all these areas is key to performing well in CAT. Many past question papers are available for practice, which can provide valuable insight into the exam's structure and question types.
