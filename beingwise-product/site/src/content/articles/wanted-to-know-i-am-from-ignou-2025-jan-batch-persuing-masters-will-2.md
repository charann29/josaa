# I wanted to know i am from ignou 2025 jan batch persuing masters. Will i be able to get into mphil programme by the time i finish masters

If you're starting your Master's degree with IGNOU in January 2025, you might be wondering about your future academic options, especially regarding M.Phil programs. Let's clarify the current landscape of higher education in India as it pertains to M.Phil degrees.

## Key Takeaways

*   The M.Phil degree has been discontinued in India under the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.
*   Most universities, including IGNOU, have already phased out their M.Phil programs.
*   After completing your Master's, you can directly apply for Ph.D. (doctoral research) programs.
*   Eligibility for Ph.D. typically requires a minimum of 55% marks in your Master's (or 50% for reserved categories).

## The Discontinuation of M.Phil Programs

The National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 brought significant changes to India's higher education structure. One of the key reforms was the discontinuation of the Master of Philosophy (M.Phil) degree. This means that by the time you complete your Master's degree, likely in late 2026 or early 2027, M.Phil programs will no longer be offered in Indian universities.

This policy change aims to streamline academic progression, allowing students to move directly from their Master's studies to doctoral research, bypassing the intermediate M.Phil stage. Many universities across the country, including prominent institutions like IGNOU, have already implemented this change and stopped admitting students to M.Phil courses.

## Your Path After a Master's Degree

With the M.Phil option no longer available, your direct pathway after completing your Master's degree is to pursue doctoral research, commonly known as a Ph.D. This is a significant shift that simplifies the academic journey for those aspiring to advanced research.

To be eligible for a Ph.D. program, you will generally need to have completed your Master's degree with a minimum aggregate score. The standard requirement across most universities is 55% marks. However, for candidates belonging to reserved categories, this requirement is often relaxed to 50% marks, as per University Grants Commission (UGC) guidelines.

## The Ph.D. Application Process

Once you meet the academic eligibility criteria with your Master's degree, the next steps for applying to a Ph.D. program typically involve an entrance examination and an interview. These components are crucial for assessing your research aptitude, subject knowledge, and suitability for a doctoral program.

Different universities may have their own specific entrance tests. Some national-level examinations, such as the UGC NET or CSIR NET, can also qualify you for Ph.D. admissions and even provide research fellowships. Always check the specific admission guidelines of the university or institution you are interested in for detailed information on their selection process.

In essence, while the M.Phil route is closed, a clear and direct path to doctoral research awaits you after your Master's degree.
