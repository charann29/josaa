# What is the criteria to get admission in IIT BHU?

Securing a spot at the Indian Institute of Technology (Banaras Hindu University) Varanasi, commonly known as IIT BHU, is a dream for many aspiring engineers. Admission to its various undergraduate programs is highly competitive and follows a structured national process.

## Key takeaways

*   Admission to IIT BHU's B.Tech and Dual Degree programs relies entirely on your performance in the JEE Advanced examination.
*   You must first qualify for JEE Main to be eligible to appear for JEE Advanced.
*   Seat allocation is managed through the JoSAA counselling process, considering your JEE Advanced All India Rank (AIR), category, and preferred courses.
*   Candidates need to have passed their 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, achieving at least 75% aggregate marks (65% for SC/ST/PwD categories) or be in the top 20 percentile of their respective boards.

## The JEE Pathway: Main and Advanced

The journey to IIT BHU begins with two crucial national-level engineering entrance examinations: JEE Main and JEE Advanced. JEE Main is the initial screening test conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). Only candidates who perform exceptionally well in JEE Main and secure a rank among the top qualifiers are then eligible to register and appear for JEE Advanced.

JEE Advanced is renowned as one of India's most challenging and prestigious engineering entrance exams. Your performance in JEE Advanced, specifically your All India Rank (AIR), is the sole determinant for admission to IIT BHU and other IITs.

## Academic Eligibility for 12th Standard

Beyond the competitive exams, there are specific academic requirements from your 12th standard. To be eligible for admission to IIT BHU, candidates must have successfully passed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects. Furthermore, candidates are required to have secured at least 75% aggregate marks in their 12th standard board examination. For candidates belonging to Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), or Persons with Disabilities (PwD) categories, the minimum aggregate mark requirement is 65%. Alternatively, candidates must be within the top 20 percentile of their respective qualifying examination boards, as per the official JoSAA portal guidelines.

## JoSAA Counselling and Seat Allotment

Once you have qualified JEE Advanced, the next critical step is participating in the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) counselling process. This centralized process manages seat allocation for all IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other GFTIs. During JoSAA counselling, you will have the opportunity to select your preferred branches and institutes in order of priority. Seats at IIT BHU are then allocated based on several factors:

*   Your All India Rank (AIR) in JEE Advanced.
*   Your category (General, EWS, OBC-NCL, SC, ST, PwD).
*   The availability of seats in your chosen branch and institute during that particular round of counselling.

## Understanding Rank Trends

While specific cut-off ranks can vary slightly each year based on factors like the number of applicants and exam difficulty, historical data from previous years can provide a general idea. For highly sought-after branches like Computer Science Engineering or Electronics Engineering at IIT BHU, candidates typically need to secure an All India Rank (AIR) within the top 1,500 to 2,000 in the general category. For other popular engineering disciplines such as Mechanical Engineering, Chemical Engineering, or Civil Engineering, admission might be possible with ranks extending up to 5,000–8,000, depending on the admission year and category.

IIT BHU offers a diverse range of B.Tech programs and also provides integrated Dual Degree (B.Tech + M.Tech) options, allowing students to pursue both degrees concurrently.

Meeting these criteria and performing well in JEE Advanced are essential steps toward beginning your engineering journey at IIT BHU.
