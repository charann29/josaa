# BLS LLB Course: Course, Full Form, Duration, Admission 2025, Fees, Subjects

For students aspiring to a career in law directly after completing their Class 12, the Bachelor of Legal Science and Bachelor of Legislative Law (BLS LLB) is an integrated five-year undergraduate program. This unique course provides a comprehensive legal education from the outset, combining a foundational understanding of legal principles with practical application.

## Key Takeaways

*   The BLS LLB is a five-year integrated program, meaning you earn two degrees (BLS and LLB) upon completion.
*   Unlike some other integrated law courses, BLS LLB focuses on legal subjects and perspectives right from the initial years.
*   Admission typically requires a Class 12 qualification with a minimum percentage, along with strong performance in relevant entrance exams.
*   The curriculum includes a blend of foundational subjects and core law topics, taught through classroom sessions, case studies, and moot courts.

## What is BLS LLB?

The full form of BLS LLB is Bachelor of Legal Science and Bachelor of Legislative Law. It's an integrated undergraduate program designed for students who wish to pursue a law degree immediately after Class 12. While similar to BA LLB or BBA LLB in its integrated structure, the BLS LLB distinguishes itself by embedding legal perspectives and subjects into the curriculum from the very beginning. This means that even early foundational subjects are often taught with a legal lens, preparing students for a deeper dive into core legal studies in subsequent years.

### Course Duration

The BLS LLB program spans five years, typically divided into ten semesters. This extended duration allows for a thorough grounding in both general academic disciplines and specialized legal subjects, ensuring graduates are well-prepared for the complexities of the legal profession.

## Eligibility and Admission Process for 2025

To be eligible for the BLS LLB program, candidates generally need to have successfully completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination from a recognized board. Most institutions require a minimum aggregate score, often around 55%, though this can vary by college. For specific percentages, always refer to the official admission guidelines of your preferred institutions.

Admission to BLS LLB programs is primarily entrance-exam based. Key national and university-specific law entrance exams that candidates often appear for include:

*   Common Law Admission Test (CLAT)
*   All India Law Entrance Test (AILET)
*   Symbiosis Entrance Test (SET)
*   Law School Admission Test (LSAT - India)
*   DU LLB Entrance Exam (for some institutions, though primarily for the 3-year LLB)

Prospective students for 2025 admissions should regularly check the official websites of these examination bodies and individual colleges for application dates, exam patterns, and detailed eligibility criteria.

## BLS LLB Fees

The tuition fees for a BLS LLB course can vary significantly depending on the institution, its location, and whether it is a government-funded or private college. Generally, the annual fees can range from approximately INR 9,000 to INR 3.5 Lakhs. This range covers the cost of tuition, library access, and other academic resources. It's advisable to check the specific fee structure directly from the official college prospectus or website.

## BLS LLB Subjects and Curriculum

The BLS LLB curriculum is carefully structured to build a strong foundation in legal studies. The initial two years often cover a range of foundational subjects, which are taught with a legal perspective. These may include:

*   Economics
*   History
*   Political Science
*   English Language
*   Legal Language and Writing

In the subsequent three years, the focus shifts to core legal subjects, delving deeper into various branches of law. Typical subjects in these later years include:

*   Law of Contracts
*   Constitutional Law
*   Family Law
*   Criminal Law
*   Labour Laws
*   Jurisprudence
*   Administrative Law
*   Environmental Law
*   Company Law
*   Civil Procedure Code
*   Criminal Procedure Code

Beyond classroom lectures, the program emphasizes practical learning through case studies, mock trials (moot courts), internships, and legal aid clinics, providing students with hands-on experience and critical analytical skills.

Upon successful completion of the BLS LLB program, graduates are well-equipped to pursue various careers in the legal field, including practicing as an advocate, working as a legal advisor, or entering academia.
