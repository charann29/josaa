# GPAT 2026: Result (OUT), Cutoff, Final Answer Key, Counselling & Colleges

The Graduate Pharmacy Aptitude Test (GPAT) 2026 results have been officially released by the National Board of Examinations in Medical Sciences (NBEMS). This page provides essential information for students who appeared for the exam, covering results, cutoff scores, answer keys, and the path to counselling.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Results Declared:** The GPAT 2026 result and merit list were announced on April 7, 2026, by NBEMS on their official website.
*   **Cutoff Information:** Alongside the results, category-wise cutoff scores and ranks for GPAT 2026 have also been published.
*   **Final Answer Key:** The official final answer key for GPAT 2026 is expected to be released on or after April 15, 2026.
*   **Next Steps:** Candidates who have qualified based on the cutoff will proceed to the counselling process for admission to M.Pharm programs.

## GPAT 2026 Result and Merit List

The NBEMS released the GPAT 2026 result and merit list on April 7, 2026. These are available as downloadable PDF files on the official NBEMS website, natboard.edu.in. The merit list details candidates' scores and their corresponding ranks.

### How to Check Your GPAT 2026 Result

To access your result and the merit list, follow these steps:

1.  Visit the official NBEMS website.
2.  Navigate to the section dedicated to the GPAT exam.
3.  Look for the link to the GPAT 2026 Result/Merit List PDF.
4.  Click on the link to view and download the PDF.
5.  Save the document for future reference.

## GPAT 2026 Final Answer Key

The provisional answer key for GPAT 2026 was released on March 13, 2026, along with candidates' response sheets and question papers. Candidates had a window until March 16, 2026, to submit any objections against this provisional key. The NBEMS is expected to release the final answer key on or after April 15, 2026, after reviewing all submitted challenges.

## Counselling and Colleges

Following the declaration of results and cutoffs, qualified candidates will be eligible to participate in the counselling process for admission to various M.Pharm programs. While NBEMS conducts the GPAT exam, the counselling for admissions to centrally funded technical institutions, including those offering pharmacy programs, is often managed by agencies like JoSAA based on GPAT scores. For state-level institutions and private universities, counselling is typically conducted by their respective state admission authorities or the institutions themselves. It is advisable to regularly check the official websites of your target colleges and relevant counselling bodies for specific admission schedules and procedures.
