# Introduction to Agile Development and Scrum by IBM via Coursera: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For anyone looking to understand modern project management, the "Introduction to Agile Development and Scrum" course, offered by IBM through Coursera, provides a solid foundation. This program is designed to introduce the core concepts of Agile and Scrum, essential methodologies in today's fast-paced work environments.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn the fundamental principles of Agile development and the Scrum framework.
*   Understand the roles, events, and artifacts within a Scrum team.
*   Gain practical experience with Agile planning and daily execution through hands-on labs.
*   The course is self-paced, delivered through videos and text, and available entirely in English.

## Understanding Agile and Scrum

Agile is a flexible and iterative approach to project management that focuses on continuous improvement and collaboration. Scrum is a popular framework within Agile, designed to help teams deliver value incrementally and respond to change effectively. This course delves into both, explaining their principles, benefits, and practical applications.

## Course Structure and Content

The program is structured into three main modules, each combining video lessons, readings, practice quizzes, and graded assignments. Discussion prompts encourage interaction and deeper understanding. A unique aspect of this course is the inclusion of hands-on labs, primarily using GitHub and optionally ZenHub, to simulate real-world Agile practices.

### Module 1: Introduction to Agile and Scrum

This module sets the stage by introducing what Agile and Scrum are, their underlying principles, and how they differ from traditional methodologies. You'll learn about the three core roles in Scrum (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team), key artifacts like the Product Backlog, and events such as Sprint Planning and Daily Scrums. It also addresses common misconceptions about Agile.

### Module 2: Agile Planning

Focusing on the planning aspects, this module teaches how to create effective user stories, estimate effort using story points, and build a robust product backlog. You'll explore how to refine the backlog and conduct sprint planning sessions. The hands-on labs in this module guide you through setting up tools and assembling your product backlog.

### Module 3: Daily Execution

This final module covers the day-to-day operations within a Scrum sprint. Topics include the daily stand-up, using burndown charts to track progress, and conducting sprint reviews and retrospectives. It also discusses how to effectively measure team performance and prepare for subsequent sprints. Hands-on labs allow you to practice moving stories, setting up burndown charts, and performing sprint closure activities.

## Fee and Certification Details

The course offers flexible enrollment options, including a free audit track. For those seeking a certificate of completion from Coursera, there are subscription-based payment models. The fees vary depending on the duration of your subscription:

| Certification Option                  | Monthly Fee (INR) |
| :------------------------------------ | :---------------- |
| Introduction to Agile Development and Scrum (Audit) | Free              |
| Introduction to Agile Development and Scrum - 1 month | ₹4,117            |
| Introduction to Agile Development and Scrum - 3 months | ₹2,745            |
| Introduction to Agile Development and Scrum - 6 months | ₹2,059            |

Upon successful completion of the paid version, Coursera issues a certificate.

## How to Apply

Enrollment for this course is straightforward through the Coursera platform. You can visit the official Coursera website, search for the "Introduction to Agile Development and Scrum" course by IBM, and choose your preferred enrollment option (audit or paid subscription). The course is self-paced, allowing you to begin at any time and progress according to your schedule.

This IBM-backed course provides a comprehensive yet accessible entry point into the world of Agile and Scrum, equipping learners with practical knowledge and skills relevant to many industries today.
