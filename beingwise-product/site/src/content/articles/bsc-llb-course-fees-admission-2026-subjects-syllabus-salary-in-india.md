# BSc LLB: Course Fees, Admission 2026, Subjects, Syllabus, Salary in India

The Bachelor of Science and Bachelor of Legislative Law (BSc LLB) is a comprehensive five-year integrated degree program that blends scientific studies with legal education. This unique course is ideal for students keen on exploring both scientific disciplines and the intricacies of law.

## Key takeaways

*   BSc LLB is a five-year integrated undergraduate program combining a Bachelor of Science and a Bachelor of Legislative Law degree.
*   Admission typically involves both merit-based selection and performance in national or state-level entrance examinations.
*   The course covers a diverse curriculum, including subjects from science streams like Physics, Chemistry, or Biology, alongside core legal principles.
*   Graduates can pursue careers in various legal fields, including roles in environmental law, intellectual property, or as legal advisors for science-based industries.

## Eligibility and Admission Process for 2026

To be eligible for the BSc LLB program, candidates generally need to have completed their Class 12 education, preferably in the Science stream, from a recognized board. Most institutions require a minimum aggregate score, often ranging from 45% to 50% in the qualifying examination. For specific percentage requirements, always check the individual college or university's official prospectus.

Admission to BSc LLB programs for the 2026 academic year will primarily be through entrance examinations. Some of the prominent entrance tests include:

*   **CLAT (Common Law Admission Test):** A national-level exam for admissions to many National Law Universities (NLUs).
*   **CUET (Common University Entrance Test):** Used by various central universities and participating institutions for undergraduate admissions.
*   **MH CET Law:** A state-level examination for admissions to law colleges in Maharashtra.
*   **AP LAWCET:** Andhra Pradesh Law Common Entrance Test for admissions to law colleges in Andhra Pradesh.

Candidates should regularly check the official websites of these examination bodies and their preferred colleges for application deadlines, exam dates, and detailed admission procedures for 2026.

## Course Structure and Syllabus

The BSc LLB curriculum is designed to provide a strong foundation in both scientific disciplines and legal studies. The initial years often focus more on science subjects, gradually transitioning into a greater emphasis on legal subjects in the later years. Common science subjects might include Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, or Biotechnology, depending on the specialization offered by the institution.

Legal subjects covered typically include:

*   Constitutional Law
*   Criminal Law
*   Family Law
*   Law of Torts
*   Contract Law
*   Environmental Law
*   Intellectual Property Rights
*   Legal Research and Writing
*   Moot Court and Internship

The integrated nature of the course allows students to develop a unique interdisciplinary perspective, which is highly valuable in niche legal areas such as environmental law, patent law, or medico-legal practice.

## Course Fees

The tuition fees for a BSc LLB program in India can vary significantly based on the type of institution (government or private), its reputation, and location. Generally, the average course fees can range from approximately INR 34,000 to INR 19 Lakh for the entire five-year duration. National Law Universities (NLUs) and other top-ranked private institutions tend to have higher fee structures compared to some state universities or newer private colleges. Prospective students and parents should consult the official admission brochures of specific colleges for accurate and up-to-date fee details.

## Career Scope and Salary in India

A BSc LLB degree opens up diverse career avenues. Graduates are well-equipped for roles that require both scientific understanding and legal expertise. Some common job profiles include:

*   **Legal Advisor/Consultant:** Providing legal guidance to corporations, particularly those in scientific or technical fields.
*   **Lawyer/Advocate:** Practicing in courts, specializing in areas like environmental law, patent law, or cyber law.
*   **Legal Researcher:** Working for law firms, think tanks, or academic institutions, focusing on research related to science and law.
*   **Assistant Professor:** Pursuing a career in academia after further qualifications, teaching law or science-related legal subjects.
*   **Legal Counsel:** Working within scientific organizations, research institutions, or pharmaceutical companies to manage legal affairs.

Top recruiters for BSc LLB graduates often include prominent law firms like J Sagar Associates, Lakshmi Kumaran & Sridharan, Wadia Ghandy & Co, and Desai & Diwanji, among others. The average starting salary for BSc LLB graduates in India can be around INR 6.2 Lakh per annum, but this can vary significantly based on the employer, location, individual skills, and specialization.

This integrated program offers a robust foundation for those looking to merge their passion for science with a career in law.
