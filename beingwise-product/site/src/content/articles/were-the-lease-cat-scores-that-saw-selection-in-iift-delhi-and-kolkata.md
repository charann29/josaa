# What were the least CAT scores that saw selection in IIFT (Delhi & Kolkata)

Dreaming of an MBA in International Business from a prestigious institution like IIFT? Understanding the minimum CAT scores required for admission to the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade's Delhi and Kolkata campuses is a crucial first step for aspiring candidates.

## Key Takeaways

*   Since 2024, IIFT Delhi has been using CAT scores for its MBA (International Business) admissions, aligning with IIFT Kolkata.
*   While CAT scores are vital, IIFT's selection process is comprehensive, also evaluating Written Ability Tests (WAT), Group Discussions (GD), Personal Interviews (PI), academic records, and work experience.
*   Expected CAT cutoffs for the Delhi campus generally range from 95-98+ percentile for General category candidates, with slightly lower thresholds for reserved categories.
*   IIFT Kolkata typically has slightly more accessible CAT cutoffs, often in the 85-95 percentile range for General category applicants.

## Understanding IIFT's Admission Shift

Historically, IIFT conducted its own entrance examination. However, for the academic year 2024-26 onwards, IIFT Delhi, following the path of IIFT Kolkata, adopted the Common Admission Test (CAT) scores for shortlisting candidates for its flagship MBA (International Business) program. This change simplifies the application process for many aspirants, as they no longer need to prepare for a separate exam specifically for IIFT.

## IIFT Delhi: Expected CAT Percentiles for Shortlisting

For the 2024-26 batch, based on CAT 2023 scores, the expected minimum CAT percentiles for shortlisting at IIFT Delhi were quite competitive. It's important to remember that these are shortlisting cutoffs, and the final selection process is much broader.

### General Category

General category candidates generally needed to score in the range of **96.7-98+ percentile**. It's worth noting that IIFT also considers specific sectional cutoffs within the overall percentile. Candidates with diverse profiles, such as female applicants, non-engineers, or those with relevant work experience, might have found themselves shortlisted at slightly lower overall percentiles due to the institute's emphasis on diversity.

### Reserved Categories

For the Gen-EWS (Economically Weaker Section) and OBC-NCL (Other Backward Classes - Non-Creamy Layer) categories, the expected shortlisting percentiles typically ranged from **90-97 percentile**. For SC (Scheduled Caste), ST (Scheduled Tribe), and PwD (Persons with Disabilities) categories, the range was broader, usually between **75-95 percentile**.

## IIFT Kolkata: Expected CAT Percentiles

IIFT Kolkata generally presents a slightly different picture regarding CAT cutoffs. While still competitive, the thresholds for the Kolkata campus tend to be a bit more flexible than those for Delhi.

### General Category

For the General category, candidates aspiring for IIFT Kolkata could typically expect the shortlisting percentile to be in the **85-95 percentile** range.

### Reserved Categories

For OBC, SC, and ST categories at IIFT Kolkata, the expected percentile range is quite broad, often varying significantly from year to year, but generally falls within **50-95 percentile**.

## The Holistic Selection Process

It is crucial for applicants to understand that a high CAT score, while essential, does not guarantee final admission to IIFT. The institute employs a holistic evaluation process. The CAT score typically carries a weightage of approximately 45-48% in the final selection. Beyond the entrance exam, candidates are assessed on their performance in the Written Ability Test (WAT), Group Discussion (GD), and Personal Interview (PI). Consistent academic performance throughout their undergraduate and school years, along with any relevant work experience, also plays a significant role. IIFT values diversity, and this is reflected in its selection criteria.

Always refer to the official IIFT website for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding cutoffs and the admission process.
