# CMAT 2026: Result (OUT), Final Answer Key, Dates, Cutoff, Top Colleges

The Common Management Admission Test (CMAT) is a crucial national-level entrance exam for aspiring MBA students across India. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), CMAT scores are widely accepted by AICTE-approved institutions.

## Key takeaways

*   The CMAT 2026 results were officially declared on February 17, 2026, by the NTA.
*   The final answer key for CMAT 2026 was also released on the same day, with only one question (number 97) having a revised answer.
*   A total of 41,872 candidates appeared for the CMAT 2026 exam, out of 53,453 applications received.
*   Top MBA colleges accepting CMAT scores have now opened their admission application windows.

## Understanding the CMAT Exam

CMAT is a computer-based aptitude test designed to evaluate candidates for admission to MBA and PGDM programs. It's a three-hour examination consisting of 100 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) spread across five sections:

*   Quantitative Techniques & Data Interpretation
*   Logical Reasoning
*   Language Comprehension
*   General Awareness
*   Innovation and Entrepreneurship

Each section carries equal weightage, and a strong performance across all areas is vital for a high percentile score, which is necessary for admission to leading management institutes.

## CMAT 2026 Result and Scorecard

The NTA released the CMAT 2026 result along with the final answer key. Candidates can access their results and download their scorecards from the official CMAT website by following these steps:

1.  Visit the official CMAT 2026 portal.
2.  Locate and click on the 'CMAT-2026 scorecard' link.
3.  Enter your application number and either your password or date of birth.
4.  Click 'Submit' to view and download your CMAT 2026 scorecard.

The scorecard will display your sectional and overall scores, along with your percentile, which is crucial for college applications.

## CMAT Cutoffs and Top Colleges

CMAT scores are accepted by approximately 1,300 MBA colleges across India, including many prestigious institutions. Each college sets its own CMAT cutoff percentile, which can vary based on factors like the college's ranking, the number of applicants, and the difficulty of the exam.

Generally, top-tier colleges tend to have higher cutoff percentiles, often above 90-95 percentile. Candidates aiming for these institutes should strive for an excellent score. It's advisable to check the specific cutoff requirements of your target colleges on their official websites or through official admission brochures.

Once results are out, colleges accepting CMAT scores begin their admission processes, which typically include group discussions (GD) and personal interviews (PI) for shortlisted candidates. Keep an eye on the individual college admission portals for application deadlines and further details.
