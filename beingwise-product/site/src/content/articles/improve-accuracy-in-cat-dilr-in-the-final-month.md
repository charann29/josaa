# How to Improve Accuracy in CAT DILR in the Final Month

The Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DILR) section of the CAT exam can be a make-or-break component of your overall score. With the exam drawing near, the final month of preparation is critical for refining your approach to DILR, focusing not just on the quantity of practice but also its quality.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Prioritise Accuracy:** In DILR, a higher accuracy rate on fewer attempts can lead to a better score than attempting many questions with low accuracy, especially due to negative marking.
*   **Strategic Question Selection:** Not every DILR set needs to be solved. Learning to identify and tackle sets you can solve accurately is crucial.
*   **Understand Your Strengths & Weaknesses:** Regularly analysing your performance helps you focus your practice on areas that need improvement while maintaining proficiency in your strong suit.
*   **Time Management is Key:** DILR sets can be time-consuming; effective time allocation during practice and the actual exam is vital for success.

## Analyse Your Strengths and Weaknesses

By now, your preparation should have given you a clear picture of your DILR capabilities. The final month is about leveraging this understanding. While consistent practice is essential for both Data Interpretation (DI) and Logical Reasoning (LR), it's important to direct your efforts wisely. Pinpoint the specific topics within DI and LR that you consistently struggle with. Devote more time to these 'pain points' through targeted practice. However, don't neglect your strong areas; a quick review or a few practice questions can help maintain your proficiency.

## Focus on Accuracy Over Attempts

CAT is an examination where high accuracy, often in the range of 90-95%, can significantly boost your percentile. In the final month, your goal should be to achieve this level of accuracy in your DILR practice sets. When you sit for the exam, aim for the same precision. Avoid making educated guesses, as CAT includes negative marking for incorrect answers, which can quickly erode your score.

## Develop Effective Time Management

DILR sets are often calculation-intensive or require significant thought, making them potential time sinks. Past CAT papers have shown that at least one DI set and one LR set can be particularly demanding in terms of time. If you decide to attempt such a set, be mindful of the clock. A good strategy is to avoid spending more than 2-5 minutes on any single question within a set, assuming you've already decided to skip one or two entire sets. Efficient time allocation ensures you get to all solvable questions.

## Learn to Select DILR Questions Wisely

It's a common misconception that you must attempt every question in the DILR section. The reality is that choosing the right sets to attempt is a critical skill. Aim to select sets where you are confident of solving at least 2-3 questions correctly out of a typical 4-5 question set. This selective approach maximises your score while minimising wasted time on overly complex or unfamiliar sets. Familiarise yourself with common focus areas such as:

*   **Data Interpretation:** Bar graphs, line graphs, data comparison, and tables.
*   **Logical Reasoning:** Syllogisms, arrangements, distributions, networks, puzzles, and games & tournaments.

## Practice Sectional Mocks Regularly

Managing around 20-22 set-based questions within the 40-minute DILR section is a significant challenge. In the last month, integrate DILR sectional mocks into your study routine. These mocks are invaluable for building stamina and improving your time management under exam-like conditions. After each mock, meticulously review your performance, noting your errors and identifying areas where you could have been more efficient. Maintain a log to track what strategies work best for you and what doesn't, allowing you to fine-tune your approach.

Your strategy for the last month should involve taking 2-3 mock tests per week, alongside daily dedicated practice sessions of 2-3 hours focusing on DILR questions.
