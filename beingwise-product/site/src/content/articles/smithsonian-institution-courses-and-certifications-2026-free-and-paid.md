# Smithsonian Institution Courses & Certifications 2026 - Free & Paid Online Smithsonian Institution Courses

The Smithsonian Institution, a global leader in education and research, offers a variety of online courses and certifications designed to enhance professional skills and personal knowledge. These programs provide unique learning opportunities drawing from the institution's vast collections and expert insights.

## Key Takeaways

*   The Smithsonian Institution provides both free-to-audit and paid certificate options for its online courses.
*   Programs cover diverse fields, including artisan business, critical thinking, and interdisciplinary teaching.
*   Courses are generally self-paced, allowing learners flexibility to fit studies into their schedules.
*   Many courses are beginner-friendly and require a modest weekly time commitment.

## Fundamentals of Artisan Business Development

This introductory program is ideal for individuals looking to transition from hobbyist artisans to business-level craftspeople. Developed within the framework of the 'My Armenia Program,' it focuses on essential entrepreneurial skills.

**Course Details:**

*   **Duration:** 16 weeks, with an estimated 1-2 hours of study per week.
*   **Language:** Taught in Armenian by Ashkhen Khudaverdyan, an enterprise specialist at the Smithsonian Institution.
*   **Key Topics:** Covers crucial aspects like costing, marketing strategies, export procedures, and business registration. Note that some legal and procedural content is specifically tailored for an Armenian audience.
*   **Skills Covered:** Marketing, business planning, and entrepreneurial fundamentals.
*   **Access:** This self-paced course can be audited for free.

## Teaching Critical Thinking Through Art with the National Gallery of Art

Designed primarily for educators, this course aims to cultivate a culture of critical thinking within classrooms using art as a powerful tool. It encourages teachers from all subjects and levels to explore innovative pedagogical approaches.

**Course Details:**

*   **Duration:** 16 weeks, requiring approximately 3-4 hours of study per week.
*   **Development:** Created in collaboration with Harvard Graduate School of Education's Project Zero development program.
*   **Core Areas:** Explores three main areas: observing and describing artworks, reasoning effectively with evidence, and developing strong questioning and investigation skills.
*   **Resources:** Features over 20 video tutorials, interactive tools like Zoom, polls, discussion boards, video demonstrations, and expert interviews.
*   **Language:** Instruction is in English, with video transcripts available in four different languages.
*   **Access:** Learners can choose between a paid certificate option, offering unlimited access, or a free audit mode with limited access.
*   **Skills Covered:** Enhanced teaching methodologies, critical thinking facilitation, and art-based learning strategies.

## Interdisciplinary Teaching with Museum Objects

This program introduces educators to diverse teaching strategies that integrate museum objects into real-world learning environments. It provides practical insights from leading educators across various Smithsonian museums.

**Course Details:**

*   **Duration:** 14 weeks.
*   **Content:** Features pre-recorded sessions from educators at prestigious institutions such as the National Museum of American History, the National Museum of African American History and Culture, the Smithsonian American Art Museum, the National Portrait Gallery, and the Smithsonian Center for Learning and Digital Access.
*   **Learning Outcomes:** Participants will learn how to use artifacts and art objects to make student learning more engaging and innovative. The program also familiarizes learners with the Smithsonian Learning Lab, a valuable digital resource.
*   **Target Audience:** Ideal for teachers and educators looking to enrich their teaching practices through object-based learning.

These online offerings from the Smithsonian Institution provide valuable opportunities for continuous learning and professional development, drawing directly from the expertise and collections of a world-renowned cultural and scientific hub.
