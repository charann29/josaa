# Teaching Critical Thinking through Art with the National Gallery of Art by Smithsonian Institution via Edx: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This unique program, developed by the Smithsonian Institution in collaboration with the National Gallery of Art, offers educators a powerful way to integrate critical thinking skills into their classrooms using the visual arts. It's designed to help teachers at all levels and across various subjects cultivate a culture of thoughtful inquiry among their students.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Self-Paced Learning:** The course is delivered entirely online and can be completed at your own pace, fitting into your existing schedule.
*   **Art-Based Pedagogy:** Learn innovative strategies to foster critical thinking, observation, and evidence-based reasoning using artwork as a primary tool.
*   **Flexible Access:** You can choose to audit the course for free with limited access or upgrade for a fee to gain unlimited access and a verified certificate.
*   **Comprehensive Resources:** The program provides over 20 video tutorials, interactive tools like a Zoom function for detailed art viewing, polls, and discussion boards.

## Course Structure and Content

The program spans 16 weeks and requires an estimated commitment of 3-4 hours per week. It is an introductory-level course, making it accessible to educators new to these teaching methods. The curriculum focuses on three core areas: observing and describing artworks, reasoning with evidence, and questioning and investigating ideas. This framework helps participants develop robust strategies for encouraging deeper student engagement and understanding.

The course content was developed through Project Zero at the Harvard Graduate School of Education, ensuring a research-backed approach to its methodology. It aims to equip teachers with practical routines and techniques they can immediately apply in their classrooms, regardless of the subject matter or student age group.

## Learning Resources and Language Options

Participants will benefit from a rich array of learning resources, including video interviews with experts, lesson demonstrations, and interactive quizzes. A notable feature is the specialized Zoom tool, which allows for high-resolution examination of artworks, enhancing the learning experience. While the primary language of instruction is English, video transcripts are available in four additional languages to support a wider audience.

## Fees and Certification

While the course offers a free audit option with limited access to materials, participants wishing to receive a verified certificate from the Smithsonian Institution must upgrade their enrollment. The certification fee is $119. To qualify for the certificate, learners must successfully complete all course units, including quizzes and practice sessions. Upgrading provides unlimited access to all course content, ensuring you can revisit materials as often as needed.

## What You Will Learn

This program is designed to empower educators with practical skills and insights, including:

*   **Enhancing Personal Thinking:** Discover how to use 'artful thinking routines' to strengthen your own critical thinking abilities.
*   **Facilitating Meaningful Discussions:** Learn strategies for leading engaging classroom conversations inspired by art.
*   **Developing Evidence-Based Reasoning:** Cultivate the habit of reasoning with evidence, questioning effectively, and describing observations more artfully.
*   **Creating Engaging Activities:** Gain skills in designing new and exciting classroom activities that push students towards original, deeper thinking across various content areas.

This program offers a valuable opportunity for educators to refresh their teaching practices and inspire a new level of critical engagement in their students.
