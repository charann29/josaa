# I had got SNQ quota in my 1st round for DSCE but in the 2nd round i got different branch from same clg but i didn't get snq quota what to do

It can be confusing and concerning when your admission quota status changes between counselling rounds, especially after securing an SNQ (Supernumerary Quota) seat. Let's explore what might have happened and what steps you can take.

## Key takeaways

*   SNQ quotas are often branch-specific and may not transfer if your branch changes.
*   Always review the official counselling guidelines from DSCE or the relevant counselling authority for detailed rules on quota applicability.
*   Contacting the DSCE admissions office directly is crucial for clarification and potential solutions.
*   Keep all your allotment letters and application proofs handy for any discussions.

## Understanding SNQ Quotas and Branch Changes

Supernumerary Quotas (SNQ) are special provisions designed to support specific categories of students, often with benefits like reduced tuition fees. However, these quotas are frequently tied to a particular branch or programme within an institution. When you secure an SNQ seat in the first round for a specific branch at DSCE, it means that quota was allocated for that exact combination.

If, in the second round, you are allotted a different branch at the same college, it's very common for the SNQ quota not to carry over automatically. This is because the quota available for your new branch might already be filled, or the new branch itself might not have an SNQ provision. The system processes your preferences and seat availability, and if a higher preference with a different branch opens up, it might be offered, but without the quota if it doesn't apply there.

## Reviewing Official Guidelines

The first and most important step is to thoroughly check the official counselling information. Look for documents published by DSCE or the state counselling body that outline the rules for SNQ allocation, seat upgradation, and quota transferability between rounds and branches. These guidelines will explicitly state whether an SNQ quota, once allotted, remains valid if you switch branches in subsequent rounds. Understanding these rules will clarify your position.

## Immediate Action: Contact DSCE Admissions

Given your situation, directly contacting the admissions office at DSCE is highly recommended. Explain your precise situation: that you were allotted an SNQ seat in the first round for a particular branch and now, in the second round, you have a different branch at DSCE but without the SNQ status. Be polite, clear, and concise. They are the best resource to provide an accurate explanation and guide you on any potential recourse.

When you contact them, have all relevant documents ready. This includes:

*   Your first-round allotment letter clearly showing the SNQ quota.
*   Your second-round allotment letter.
*   Your application form and any other supporting proofs related to your SNQ eligibility.

They might be able to clarify if there was an error, if there's a process for reconsideration, or if the quota is indeed non-transferable in your specific case. They might also advise you on options for future rounds, if applicable.

## What if Reconsideration Isn't Possible?

If, after contacting the college, you learn that the SNQ quota cannot be reinstated for your new branch, you will likely face a decision point. You might need to either accept the newly allotted branch without the SNQ benefits or explore other options. This could involve waiting for subsequent counselling rounds (if any) to see if you can secure an SNQ seat in a branch of your preference, or considering other colleges and courses. Always weigh the pros and cons of each choice carefully based on your academic goals and financial considerations.

Navigating admission processes can be complex, but staying informed and proactive is key to resolving such situations effectively.
