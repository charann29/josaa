# 15+ Career Options After MA: What To Do After MA

Many students completing their Master of Arts (MA) degree often wonder about the best career path to take next. An MA equips you with valuable skills in critical thinking, communication, and in-depth subject knowledge, opening doors to diverse professional fields.

## Key takeaways

*   An MA is a two-year postgraduate degree in Arts, Humanities, Social Sciences, and Liberal Arts.
*   It offers numerous specialisations like English, History, Psychology, and Sociology.
*   Graduates develop strong analytical, research, and communication skills, highly valued by employers.
*   Career paths range from academia and research to media, administration, and social work.

## Understanding the MA Degree

The Master of Arts (MA) is a postgraduate program typically lasting two years. It delves deeper into subjects within the Arts, Humanities, Social Sciences, and Liberal Arts. Whether your undergraduate focus was on literature, history, philosophy, political science, or sociology, an MA allows you to specialise further, enhancing your expertise. This degree can be pursued through various modes, including full-time, part-time, distance learning, or correspondence.

To be eligible for most MA programs in India, candidates generally need to have completed an undergraduate degree from a recognised university, often with a minimum aggregate score of 50-55%.

## Essential Skills Developed During an MA

Beyond academic knowledge, an MA program helps students cultivate a range of soft and hard skills that are highly sought after in the professional world. These include:

*   **Excellent communication skills:** Both written and verbal, crucial for presenting complex ideas clearly.
*   **Interpersonal skills:** For effective collaboration and networking.
*   **Organisational skills:** Essential for managing projects and responsibilities.
*   **Strategic planning:** The ability to think ahead and devise effective approaches.
*   **Research and analytical skills:** For gathering information, evaluating data, and drawing informed conclusions.
*   **Presentation skills:** Confidently conveying information to an audience.

Developing these competencies not only aids academic success but also significantly boosts your prospects in the job market after graduation.

## Popular MA Specialisations and Entrance Exams

The MA degree offers a wide array of specialisations, allowing students to align their studies with their specific interests. Some common specialisations available in India include:

*   Psychology
*   History
*   Sociology
*   English Literature
*   Economics
*   Political Science
*   Philosophy
*   Linguistics (e.g., French, German, Spanish, Hindi)
*   Social Work
*   Archaeology
*   Anthropology

Admission to many reputable MA programs often requires candidates to clear an entrance examination. Some of the widely recognised entrance exams for MA admissions across India include:

*   TISSNET
*   JNUEE
*   CUCET
*   IPU CET
*   PU CET
*   CPGET (OUCET)
*   MGU CAT
*   TUEE
*   AUCET

Choosing the right path after your MA involves reflecting on your interests, strengths, and long-term career aspirations. With the diverse skills and knowledge gained, an MA degree can be a powerful stepping stone towards a fulfilling professional journey.
