# Organisational Behaviour Courses, Subjects, Colleges, Syllabus, Scope, Fees, Eligibility

Organisational Behaviour (OB) is a fascinating field that explores how individuals and groups behave within organisations, and how this behaviour impacts the organisation's overall effectiveness. It's a blend of psychology, sociology, economics, and management, aiming to understand human dynamics in the workplace to boost productivity and job satisfaction.

## Key Takeaways

*   Organisational Behaviour is primarily offered at the postgraduate (MBA) and doctoral levels in India.
*   Undergraduate OB courses are generally not available in India; students might pursue them abroad.
*   A background in management, commerce, or psychology is beneficial for those interested in OB.
*   Entrance exams like CAT, CMAT, and MAT are crucial for MBA programs with an OB specialisation.

## Understanding Organisational Behaviour

At its core, Organisational Behaviour examines the intricate relationship between employees and their workplace environment. It helps organisations understand individual potential, foster motivation, and create a supportive structure that enhances both the quality of work and overall productivity. Experts in this field analyse human behaviour to identify strengths, address challenges, and build more effective teams.

## Course Levels and Eligibility

In India, Organisational Behaviour is primarily offered as a specialisation within Master of Business Administration (MBA) programs or as a dedicated doctoral degree (Ph.D.).

### Postgraduate (MBA in Organisational Behaviour)

To pursue an MBA with an OB specialisation, candidates typically need:

*   A bachelor's degree from a recognised university or institution.
*   A minimum aggregate score, often around 50%, though specific colleges may have higher requirements.
*   Successful performance in national-level entrance examinations.

### Doctoral (Ph.D. in Organisational Behaviour)

For doctoral studies in OB, the eligibility criteria usually include:

*   A postgraduate degree (e.g., MBA, M.Phil.) from a recognised university.
*   A minimum aggregate score, often 55% or more, with specific college requirements varying.
*   Clearing relevant entrance examinations and interviews conducted by the institutions.
*   Some institutions may also require a strong academic record without any backlogs in previous degrees.

## Key Entrance Examinations

Admission to postgraduate and doctoral programs in Organisational Behaviour often depends on entrance exam scores. Here's a look at some prominent ones:

### For MBA Programs (with OB specialisation)

| Exam Name | Conducted By | Purpose |
| :-------- | :----------- | :------ |
| CAT (Common Admission Test) | IIMs | For admission to IIMs and other leading MBA colleges. |
| CMAT (Common Management Admission Test) | NTA | For admission to various management programs across India. |
| MAT (Management Aptitude Test) | AIMA | For admission to MBA and allied management programs. |

### For Doctoral Programs

*   **CSIR UGC NET:** This national-level exam determines eligibility for Junior Research Fellowship and Assistant Professor positions in Indian universities and colleges.
*   **DBT JRF:** Focuses on providing research grants to postgraduate students in specific scientific fields, which can include relevant aspects for OB research.
*   **State-level CETs (Common Entrance Tests):** Various state governments conduct these exams for admission to postgraduate and doctoral programs within their respective states.
*   **University-specific Entrance Tests:** Many universities and institutes conduct their own entrance exams, often followed by interviews, for Ph.D. admissions.

## Syllabus Highlights for Organisational Behaviour

While specific syllabi vary by institution and program level, common subjects covered in Organisational Behaviour include:

*   Individual Behaviour in Organisations (e.g., personality, perception, attitudes, motivation)
*   Group Dynamics and Teamwork
*   Leadership Theories and Styles
*   Organisational Culture and Climate
*   Organisational Structure and Design
*   Conflict Management and Negotiation
*   Change Management and Development
*   Stress Management and Employee Well-being
*   Ethics in Organisations

## Career Scope and Future Prospects

The demand for Organisational Behaviour specialists is growing as companies increasingly recognise the importance of their human capital. An expert in OB can help organisations create better work environments, resolve internal conflicts, improve employee engagement, and enhance overall operational efficiency. Graduates can find roles in:

*   Human Resources Management (HRM)
*   Talent Management and Development
*   Organisational Development Consulting
*   Change Management
*   Training and Development
*   Industrial-Organisational Psychology roles (with further specialisation)
*   Academic and Research positions

These professionals play a vital role in fostering a positive and productive work culture, which is crucial for any organisation's long-term success.

Understanding human behaviour in the workplace is a valuable skill, and a background in Organisational Behaviour can open doors to impactful careers in various industries.
