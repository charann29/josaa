# What course should I take to become a dermatologist?

Becoming a dermatologist in India is a rewarding path for those passionate about skin health. This guide will walk you through the primary educational routes available after completing your MBBS degree.

## Key takeaways

*   An MBBS degree is the essential first step to becoming a dermatologist.
*   The Doctor of Medicine (MD) in Dermatology, Venereology, and Leprosy is generally considered the most comprehensive postgraduate qualification.
*   A Post Graduate Diploma (PGD) in Dermatology, Venereology, and Leprosy offers a shorter, focused alternative, also enabling a successful career.
*   Admission to both MD and PGD programs typically requires qualifying the NEET PG examination.

## The Foundation: MBBS

The journey to becoming a dermatologist begins with completing a Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) degree. This undergraduate medical program spans 5.5 years, including a compulsory one-year rotating internship. During your MBBS, you'll gain a broad understanding of medical sciences, patient care, and various specialties, including an introduction to dermatology.

## Postgraduate Options: MD vs. PGD

After your MBBS, you have two main postgraduate options to specialize in dermatology:

### Doctor of Medicine (MD) in Dermatology, Venereology, and Leprosy

The MD program is a three-year postgraduate degree that offers an in-depth and comprehensive study of dermatology, venereology (sexually transmitted infections), and leprosy. It involves extensive clinical training, research, and theoretical knowledge. Graduates with an MD are highly sought after and can pursue roles in hospitals, private practice, academia, and advanced research. While both MD and PGD can lead to successful careers, the MD is often perceived as a more advanced and comprehensive qualification within the medical community.

### Post Graduate Diploma (PGD) in Dermatology, Venereology, and Leprosy

The PGD is a two-year postgraduate diploma program. It provides focused training in the essential aspects of dermatology, venereology, and leprosy. While it's a shorter program than the MD, it equips graduates with the necessary skills and knowledge to practice as dermatologists. Many successful dermatologists have pursued the PGD route and built thriving careers. The choice between an MD and PGD often depends on individual career aspirations, the desired depth of study, and the availability of seats.

## Admission Process: NEET PG

Admission to both MD and PGD programs in dermatology, venereology, and leprosy across India is primarily through the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET PG). This highly competitive examination is conducted by the National Board of Examinations in Medical Sciences (NBEMS). After qualifying NEET PG, candidates participate in counseling conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) for All India Quota seats and by respective state authorities for state quota seats. Performance in NEET PG, along with the reservation policies, determines your eligibility and allocation to a specific college and program.

## Career Prospects

Upon completing your postgraduate qualification, you can work as a dermatologist in various settings. This includes government hospitals, private hospitals, setting up your own clinic, or even pursuing academic careers as faculty in medical colleges. The demand for dermatologists remains strong, given the increasing awareness about skin health and cosmetic dermatology.

Choosing the right path after your MBBS is a significant decision. Both the MD and PGD in Dermatology, Venereology, and Leprosy offer excellent avenues to specialize and build a fulfilling career in this dynamic medical field.
