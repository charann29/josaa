      (expressed as a decimal), and 'Time' is the duration of the investment or
      loan in years.

# Understanding Simple Interest: A Practical Example

Many competitive exams include quantitative aptitude questions that test your understanding of basic financial concepts like simple interest. Let's break down a common problem type to help you prepare.

## Key Takeaways

*   Simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal amount.
*   Problems involving multiple investments often require setting up simultaneous equations.
*   Careful algebraic manipulation is essential to arrive at the correct answer.
*   Understanding how to apply interest rate percentages is a fundamental skill.

## The Investment Scenario

Imagine Rajesh has INR 10,000 to invest. He decides to split this amount between two different schemes, let's call them Scheme A and Scheme B. Scheme A offers a simple interest rate of 8% per year, while Scheme B offers a slightly higher rate of 10% per year. After two years, Rajesh earns a total interest of INR 1,680 from both investments combined. The question is, how much did he invest in Scheme A?

This is a classic problem that combines simple interest calculations with basic algebra.

## Setting Up the Equations

To solve this, we need to represent the unknown amounts with variables. Let's say:

*   `x` is the amount Rajesh invested in Scheme A.
*   `y` is the amount Rajesh invested in Scheme B.

From the problem statement, we know two crucial pieces of information:

1.  **Total Investment:** Rajesh invested a total of INR 10,000. So, `x + y = 10,000`.
2.  **Total Interest:** The total interest earned over 2 years is INR 1,680.

Now, let's calculate the interest earned from each scheme individually. The formula for simple interest is `Principal × Rate × Time`.

*   **Interest from Scheme A:** `x × (8/100) × 2 = 0.16x`
*   **Interest from Scheme B:** `y × (10/100) × 2 = 0.20y`

The sum of these individual interests equals the total interest earned:

`0.16x + 0.20y = 1680`

## Solving for the Unknown

Now we have a system of two linear equations with two variables:

1.  `x + y = 10,000`
2.  `0.16x + 0.20y = 1680`

From the first equation, we can express `y` in terms of `x`: `y = 10,000 - x`.

Substitute this expression for `y` into the second equation:

`0.16x + 0.20(10,000 - x) = 1680`

Now, let's simplify and solve for `x`:

`0.16x + 2000 - 0.20x = 1680`

Combine the `x` terms:

`-0.04x + 2000 = 1680`

Subtract 2000 from both sides:

`-0.04x = 1680 - 2000`

`-0.04x = -320`

Finally, divide by -0.04 to find `x`:

`x = -320 / -0.04`

`x = 8000`

So, the amount invested in Scheme A is INR 8,000.

## Verifying the Answer

It's always a good practice to quickly verify your answer. If `x = 8000`, then `y = 10,000 - 8000 = 2000`.

*   Interest from Scheme A: `8000 × 0.08 × 2 = 1280`
*   Interest from Scheme B: `2000 × 0.10 × 2 = 400`

Total interest: `1280 + 400 = 1680`. This matches the information given in the problem, confirming our calculation.

Mastering these types of problems is crucial for success in various entrance examinations.
