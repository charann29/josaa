# Preventing and Managing Infections in Childcare and Pre-school

For anyone working with young children, understanding how to prevent and manage infections is crucial. This online course, developed by the British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy and the UK Health Security Agency, offers practical guidance on keeping early childhood environments safe and healthy.

## Key takeaways

*   Learn effective strategies to prevent the spread of common infections in childcare settings.
*   Understand the different types of microbes, how they spread, and the importance of hygiene.
*   Gain insights into childhood vaccinations and the critical issue of antibiotic resistance.
*   The program is designed for a self-paced learning experience over two weeks, requiring about two hours per week.

## Course Overview

This two-week online program is delivered through the FutureLearn platform and is led by Professor Angela Baker. It's structured for self-study, combining video and text-based content to make learning engaging and accessible. The course addresses the reality that microbes are everywhere, especially where young children gather in close proximity. While some microbes are harmless, others can cause illness. This course will equip you with the knowledge to distinguish between them and implement effective prevention techniques.

## What You Will Learn

Upon completing this certification, you will be able to:

*   Explain the omnipresence of microbes, their diverse forms, and differentiate between beneficial and harmful types.
*   Demonstrate how infections spread through common actions like hand contact, coughing, and sneezing, and identify correct handwashing practices and timings.
*   Illustrate how microorganisms can contaminate food and kitchen surfaces.
*   Apply infection prevention techniques specifically tailored for childcare and nursery environments.
*   Discuss the significance of childhood vaccinations and locate reliable sources for vaccination information.
*   Recognize the importance of antibiotics and describe the phenomenon of antibiotic resistance, outlining methods to mitigate it.

## Who Is This Course For?

This program is ideal for individuals who work with or care for infants and young children (under four years of age). This includes:

*   Nursery nurses
*   Daycare workers
*   Babysitters
*   Professional carers
*   Nurses
*   Staff Nurses
*   Paediatricians
*   Childcare Program Administrators

## Course Structure and Offerings

The course is designed to be introductory, making complex topics easy to understand. It includes:

*   **Self-paced learning:** Study at your convenience, dedicating approximately two hours per week.
*   **Comprehensive materials:** Access course content, including videos and texts, for the duration of your access.
*   **Learning assessments:** Short tests are integrated to help you check your understanding as you progress.
*   **Community interaction:** Engage with other learners and discuss your insights in the comments section.
*   **Designed by experts:** The syllabus has been developed by the British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy and the UK Health Security Agency.
