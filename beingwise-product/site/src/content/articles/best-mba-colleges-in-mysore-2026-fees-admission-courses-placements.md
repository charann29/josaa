# Best MBA Colleges in Mysore 2026: Fees, Admission, Courses, Placements

Mysore, a city known for its rich heritage, also offers a growing landscape for management education. Pursuing an MBA here can provide a strong foundation for your career, with a range of institutions catering to diverse academic and professional aspirations.

## Key Takeaways

*   Mysore hosts over 30 MBA colleges, including a mix of private, public-private, and government institutions.
*   The Karnataka PGCET is a key entrance exam for admission to many MBA programs in the region.
*   The University of Mysore plays a significant role, offering MBA degrees directly and through its affiliated colleges.
*   Prominent independent private institutions like Shri Dharmasthala Manjunatheshwara Institute for Management Development (SDMIMD) and Amrita School of Business also offer high-quality management education.

## Overview of MBA Education in Mysore

Mysore's MBA landscape is quite vibrant, featuring more than 30 colleges. Among these, over 15 are private institutions, while more than 5 are government-owned, alongside one public-private venture. For many of these colleges, particularly 17 of them, the Karnataka Post Graduate Common Entrance Test (PGCET) is the primary gateway for admission.

The venerable University of Mysore not only offers its own MBA program but also extends its academic umbrella to over 25 private colleges in the city, enabling them to award MBA degrees under its affiliation. Alongside these, independent private institutes such as the Shri Dharmasthala Manjunatheshwara Institute for Management Development (SDMIMD) and the Amrita School of Business stand out for their robust and highly-regarded management programs.

## Eligibility Criteria for MBA Admissions

While specific requirements might vary slightly between institutions, the general eligibility for MBA admission in Mysore colleges typically includes:

*   **Educational Qualification:** Candidates must hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A minimum aggregate score, usually around 50% (45% for reserved categories) in the bachelor's degree, is generally required.
*   **Entrance Exams:** Applicants must appear for and achieve a valid score in accepted management entrance tests. These commonly include Karnataka PGCET, CAT, XAT, CMAT, MAT, or ATMA.

## Accepted Entrance Exams and Key Dates

Aspiring MBA students need to be aware of the various entrance exams accepted by colleges in Mysore. The Karnataka PGCET is particularly significant for state-level admissions. Other national-level exams widely recognized include:

*   **Karnataka PGCET:** Essential for many colleges affiliated with the state university system.
*   **CAT (Common Admission Test):** A national-level exam accepted by top B-schools across India.
*   **XAT (Xavier Aptitude Test):** Conducted by XLRI Jamshedpur, accepted by many premier institutions.
*   **CMAT (Common Management Admission Test):** A national-level exam conducted by NTA.
*   **MAT (Management Aptitude Test):** Conducted by AIMA, accepted by a large number of B-schools.
*   **ATMA (AIMS Test for Management Admissions):** Another widely accepted national-level test.

Candidates should regularly check the official websites of the respective examination bodies and individual colleges for precise application deadlines and exam schedules for the 2026 admission cycle.
