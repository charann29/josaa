# Orthodontics for Dental Professionals

This online course offers an introduction to orthodontics, focusing on the principles and techniques used to correct dental and jaw irregularities. It's designed to help dental professionals understand how to improve oral health and aesthetics by realigning teeth.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn the foundational concepts of orthodontics, including occlusion and malocclusion.
*   Understand the stages of dental development, from pre-dentition to permanent dentition.
*   Explore preventive and interceptive orthodontic strategies, such as the use of space maintainers.
*   Gain insights into managing common undesirable oral habits.

## About the Course

Developed by Dr. Malik Hina, a dental specialist, this program provides over an hour of video-based learning content. The material is presented in an accessible and engaging manner, covering essential orthodontic topics.

### What You Will Learn

Upon completing this certification, you will have a detailed understanding of the core principles of orthodontics and their application in treating various dental issues. The course delves into the fundamentals of dentition, exploring different developmental stages like the deciduous dentition period and the 'ugly duckling' stage. You will also learn about preventive and interceptive orthodontics, including the roles of space maintainers and retainers, and techniques to address habits such as thumb sucking and tongue thrusting.

## Course Structure and Content

The curriculum is divided into key modules, ensuring a comprehensive overview of the subject:

### Growth and Development

*   Introduction to Growth (Parts One and Two)
*   Growth Spurts

### Occlusal Development

*   Pre-dentition Period
*   Deciduous Dentition Period
*   Leeway space of Nance
*   Mixed Dentition and Permanent Dentition Period
*   Ugly Duckling Stage

## Who Can Benefit?

This course is suitable for a wide range of healthcare professionals interested in expanding their knowledge of orthodontics. This includes dentists, pharmacists, general physicians, doctors, nurses, and dental surgeons.
