# JEE Main Response Sheet 2026 Session 2 (April 11): Download Link & How to Check

*   **Official Release:** The NTA will tentatively release the JEE Main Session 2 response sheets and question papers on April 11, 2026.
*   **Access Method:** Candidates can download their response sheets from the official NTA JEE Main website using their application number and password.
*   **Score Calculation:** Use your response sheet in conjunction with the official answer key to calculate your estimated JEE Main score.
*   **Transparency Tool:** The response sheet ensures transparency, allowing candidates to confirm that their marked answers were accurately recorded.

## Understanding the JEE Main Response Sheet

The JEE Main response sheet is a digital record of all the answers you selected during your Computer-Based Test (CBT) examination. It essentially reflects what you marked on the screen for each question on the day of your exam. This personal record is vital for every candidate, offering a clear picture of their performance before the official results are declared.

When you download your response sheet, you will see the options you chose for each question. This document, combined with the official JEE Main answer key (which provides the correct answers), forms the basis for you to accurately estimate your raw score.

## How to Download Your JEE Main Response Sheet 2026 Session 2

Accessing your response sheet is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to download your personal record:

1.  **Visit the Official Portal:** Go to the official JEE Main website. While we cannot provide a direct link here, a quick search for "JEE Main NTA official website" will lead you to the correct portal.
2.  **Locate the Login:** Look for the link related to "Candidate Login" or "JEE Main 2026 Session 2 Response Sheet Download."
3.  **Enter Credentials:** You will need to input your JEE Main 2026 Application Number and your Password. Ensure these details are entered correctly.
4.  **Access Dashboard:** After logging in, navigate to your personal dashboard. Here, you should find the option to download your response sheet.
5.  **Download and Save:** Click on the download link. Your response sheet, often accompanied by the question paper PDF, will be available for download. It's advisable to save a copy for your records.

## Calculating Your Score Using the Response Sheet

Once you have both your response sheet and the official JEE Main answer key, you can begin calculating your estimated score. This process involves comparing your marked answers with the correct ones provided by the NTA.

Here’s how to do it:

1.  **Match Questions:** Go through each question on your response sheet and compare it with the corresponding question and its correct answer in the official answer key.
2.  **Apply Marking Scheme:** Use the JEE Main marking scheme to assign marks for correct answers and deduct for incorrect ones.

    *   **Correct Answer:** For every correct answer, add 4 marks.
    *   **Incorrect Answer:** For every incorrect answer, deduct 1 mark.
    *   **Unattempted Question:** No marks are added or deducted for questions you did not attempt.

3.  **Tally Your Score:** Sum up the marks for all questions to arrive at your estimated total score. This will give you a good indication of your performance.

## Difference Between Response Sheet and Answer Key

It's important to understand that the response sheet and the answer key are distinct documents, each serving a unique purpose:

*   **JEE Main Response Sheet:** This document is personal to you. It displays the specific answers you selected for each question during the examination. Think of it as your personal answer record.
*   **JEE Main Answer Key:** This document is generic and publicly released by the NTA. It contains the official correct answers for all questions asked in the JEE Main examination. It is the benchmark against which your responses are evaluated.

By comparing your response sheet with the NTA's official answer key, you can accurately determine which of your answers were correct and which were incorrect, thus enabling self-evaluation.

This self-assessment is a valuable step for all candidates, offering insight into their performance and aiding in future planning, especially with the upcoming JoSAA counselling process.
