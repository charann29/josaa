# Mechanical Engineering Essentials Programme: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For aspiring mechanical engineers and professionals looking to enhance their skillset, understanding Finite Element Analysis (FEA) is crucial. This programme offers a practical deep dive into FEA using industry-standard software.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Comprehensive FEA Training:** Learn the fundamentals and advanced applications of Finite Element Analysis.
*   **Hands-on SOLIDWORKS Experience:** Gain practical proficiency in SOLIDWORKS Simulation for various analysis types.
*   **Real-world Project Focus:** Apply learned concepts to practical scenarios, including drop tests and fatigue analysis.
*   **Skill Enhancement:** Develop skills vital for design, simulation, and product development roles in mechanical engineering.

## Programme Overview

This programme is designed to equip participants with essential knowledge and practical skills in Finite Element Analysis (FEA). FEA is a powerful numerical method used to predict how a product reacts to real-world forces, heat, vibration, and other physical effects. By understanding FEA, engineers can refine designs to improve product performance, reliability, and safety, all while reducing the need for costly physical prototypes.

The curriculum systematically introduces various aspects of FEA, starting from basic concepts and progressing to complex analysis techniques. The focus is on practical application using SOLIDWORKS Simulation, a widely used software in the industry.

## What You Will Learn

The programme is structured to provide a thorough understanding of FEA principles and their application. Here's a glimpse into the weekly breakdown of topics:

### Foundations of FEA

*   **Introduction to FEA:** Understand what FEA is, its purpose, and how it differs from analytical and experimental methods. Learn about stress-strain plots, general steps in FEA, and mesh elements like H-refinement and P-refinement.
*   **Types of FEA Analysis:** Explore different categories such as structural, static, and dynamic analysis. Differentiate between linear and nonlinear analysis methods.

### Practical Application with SOLIDWORKS Simulation

*   **SOLIDWORKS Environment:** Get acquainted with important SOLIDWORKS modelling features, sketching tools, and the simulation interface. Perform a simple cantilever load analysis to start.
*   **Basic FEA Analysis:** Conduct static linear, static nonlinear, and dynamic linear and nonlinear analyses using SOLIDWORKS Simulation.
*   **Dimensional Analysis:** Learn when and how to use 1D, 2D, and 3D simulations, and perform these in SOLIDWORKS.

### Advanced Analysis Techniques

*   **Parametric/Design Study:** Discover the advantages of parametric studies and how to execute them in SOLIDWORKS to optimise designs.
*   **Buckling Analysis:** Understand the phenomenon of buckling, its difference from bending, and how to perform buckling analysis in SOLIDWORKS.
*   **Fatigue Analysis:** Learn about fatigue, its applications, the role of SN curves, endurance limits, and conduct various fatigue studies including constant amplitude, variable amplitude, harmonic, and random vibration.
*   **Drop Test:** Understand the methodology and practical application of drop tests, including performing one on a PCB board and interpreting results.
*   **Frequency Analysis:** Explore reasons for frequency analysis, natural frequency, resonance, mode shapes, and different solver types. Conduct frequency analysis on support brackets and discuss the outcomes.
