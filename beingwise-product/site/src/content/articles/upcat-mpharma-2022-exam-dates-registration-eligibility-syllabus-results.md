# UPCAT MPharma 2022 Exam: Dates, Registration, Eligibility, Syllabus, Results

For students aspiring to pursue a Master of Pharmacy (M.Pharma) in Uttar Pradesh, the UPCAT exam is a crucial gateway. This state-level entrance examination opens doors to various M.Pharma specializations across numerous colleges affiliated with Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU).

## Key Takeaways

*   The UPCAT exam (formerly UPSEE) is a state-level entrance test for M.Pharma admissions in Uttar Pradesh.
*   It is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for various undergraduate and postgraduate programs.
*   Eligibility criteria typically include a Bachelor of Pharmacy degree from a recognized institution.
*   The application process is entirely online, and candidates must meet specific academic and domicile requirements.

## About UPCAT M.Pharma

The Uttar Pradesh Common Admission Test (UPCAT), previously known as UPSEE, is a significant state-level examination. It facilitates admissions into M.Pharma courses offered by colleges and institutions affiliated with Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU) in Uttar Pradesh. The exam is administered by the National Testing Agency (NTA), ensuring a standardized process for candidates seeking postgraduate pharmacy education.

Over 100 colleges accept UPCAT scores for M.Pharma admissions. The exam covers a range of specializations, allowing students to pursue their interests in various branches of pharmaceutical science. Some of the M.Pharma specializations typically offered through AKTU include:

*   Industrial Pharmacy
*   Pharmaceutics (with focus on Drug Regulatory Affairs)
*   Pharmaceutical Chemistry
*   Pharmacognosy
*   Pharmacology

## UPCAT M.Pharma Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the UPCAT M.Pharma entrance exam, candidates must fulfill specific academic requirements. These criteria are set by AKTU and are crucial for successful application. While minor changes may occur annually, the core requirements generally remain consistent:

*   **Educational Qualification:** Candidates must possess a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharma) degree from an institution approved by the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI).
*   **Minimum Marks:** Applicants are typically required to have secured a minimum aggregate percentage (often around 50-55% for general category candidates, with relaxations for reserved categories) in their B.Pharma degree.
*   **Appearing Candidates:** Students in their final year of the B.Pharma program are generally eligible to apply, provided they complete their degree and meet the minimum marks requirement by the time of admission.

Always consult the official information bulletin released by NTA for the exact eligibility details for the specific admission year.

## UPCAT M.Pharma Application Process

The application for the UPCAT M.Pharma exam is an entirely online process. Candidates need to visit the official NTA portal designated for UPCAT to register and submit their applications. The process generally involves several key steps:

1.  **Registration:** Create an account on the official NTA UPCAT portal by providing basic details like name, email, and mobile number.
2.  **Fill Application Form:** Complete the detailed application form with personal, academic, and contact information.
3.  **Upload Documents:** Upload scanned copies of required documents, including photographs and signatures, adhering to the specified format and size guidelines.
4.  **Pay Application Fee:** Remit the application fee online using available payment methods (credit card, debit card, net banking).
5.  **Review and Submit:** Carefully review all entered information before final submission. A correction window is usually provided for minor edits.

Candidates are strongly advised to keep a printout of their submitted application form and fee receipt for future reference.

## UPCAT M.Pharma Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The UPCAT M.Pharma examination is generally a computer-based test (CBT). It assesses a candidate's fundamental knowledge in various pharmaceutical subjects. The exam pattern typically includes multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering the core B.Pharma syllabus. While the exact structure and number of questions can vary, the focus remains on testing comprehensive understanding.

**Typical Exam Pattern:**

*   **Mode of Exam:** Computer-Based Test (CBT)
*   **Type of Questions:** Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
*   **Duration:** Usually 2-3 hours
*   **Marking Scheme:** Specific marks for correct answers, with negative marking for incorrect answers (candidates should verify this in the official bulletin).

**Syllabus:** The syllabus for the M.Pharma entrance exam broadly covers topics from the B.Pharma curriculum. This includes subjects such as:

*   Pharmaceutics
*   Pharmacology
*   Pharmaceutical Chemistry (Medicinal Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry)
*   Pharmacognosy
*   Pharmaceutical Analysis
*   Biochemistry
*   Microbiology
*   Biotechnology

A thorough revision of B.Pharma coursework is essential for preparation. Candidates should refer to the official NTA UPCAT information bulletin for the detailed syllabus specific to the M.Pharma entrance exam.

Embarking on an M.Pharma journey through UPCAT can be a rewarding step for your career in pharmacy. Stay informed by regularly checking the official NTA website for the latest updates and notifications.
