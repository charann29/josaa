# Material Science and Engineering by Udemy: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding the properties and applications of different materials is crucial in many engineering fields. This Udemy course offers a comprehensive introduction to Material Science and Engineering, designed for anyone looking to build foundational knowledge in this area.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn about the mechanical, dynamic, and corrosion properties of various engineering materials.
*   Explore different heat treatment and hardening processes for metals.
*   Understand non-destructive testing methods used in material analysis.
*   Gain insights into a wide range of materials, including steels, non-ferrous metals, plastics, and ceramics.

## Course Overview

This Material Science and Engineering course is delivered entirely in English through a self-study format. It primarily uses video and text-based lessons, allowing learners to progress at their own pace. The content is structured to provide a solid understanding of fundamental concepts, making it accessible for students, professionals, or anyone with an interest in how materials behave and are used in engineering applications.

## Detailed Syllabus

The course covers a wide array of topics, beginning with the basic mechanical properties of materials and progressing to more advanced concepts like heat treatment and corrosion. Here's a breakdown of the modules:

### Mechanical Properties

This section introduces fundamental concepts, including an overview, the tensile test, and the crucial properties of ductility and brittleness.

### Hardness

Learners will explore what hardness means in materials and delve into various methods for testing hardness.

### Dynamic and Creep Properties

This module covers dynamic properties, the impact test, the phenomenon of metal fatigue, and the characteristics of creep in materials.

### Non-Destructive Test Methods

An essential part of material analysis, this section details various non-destructive testing techniques.

### Engineering Materials

This extensive module introduces different types of engineering materials, such as plain carbon steel, alloy steel, stainless steel, non-ferrous metals, refractory metals, plastics, and ceramics.

### Corrosion

Understanding how materials degrade is vital. This section focuses on the principles and processes of corrosion.

### Heat Treatment

This module delves into the introduction to heat treatment, the iron-carbon equilibrium diagram, heating and cooling processes, and the Time-Temperature-Transformation (TTT) diagram, along with practical examples.

### Hardenability

Learn about hardenability and the Jominy test, which is used to measure it.

### Annealing Process

This section covers various types of heat treatment, including spheroidizing annealing, recrystallization annealing, stress relief annealing, full annealing, and normalizing.

### Hardening Process

Explore the hardening process in detail, including different cooling processes and media used.

### Tempering

The final module focuses on the tempering process, which is crucial for achieving desired material properties after hardening.
