# SNAP Eligibility Criteria 2026: Check Educational Qualification and Reservation Criteria of SNAP 202

To pursue an MBA from the prestigious Symbiosis International (Deemed to be) University (SIU) institutes, understanding the SNAP eligibility criteria is the crucial first step. This guide outlines the essential academic qualifications and reservation policies for the 2026 examination.

## Key Takeaways

*   A bachelor's degree with at least 50% aggregate marks (45% for reserved categories) is generally required.
*   Final year students awaiting results can also apply.
*   International candidates have specific English proficiency and documentation requirements.
*   SIU adheres to AICTE guidelines for its MBA/PGDM programs and follows government-mandated reservation policies.

## General Eligibility for Indian Candidates

Symbiosis International (Deemed to be) University, Pune, sets the eligibility standards for the SNAP examination and subsequent MBA/PGDM admissions. These criteria align with the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) guidelines, as an MBA is a postgraduate technical program.

For Indian candidates, the primary requirements are:

*   **Educational Qualification**: Applicants must hold a bachelor's degree from any University Grants Commission (UGC) recognized university or institution deemed equivalent.
*   **Minimum Marks**: General category candidates need a minimum aggregate of 50% in their bachelor's degree. For candidates belonging to reserved categories, this requirement is relaxed to a minimum aggregate of 45%.
*   **Mode of Study**: The graduation degree can be obtained through any recognized education mode.
*   **Final Year Students**: Students who are currently in their final year of graduation and are awaiting their results, or are yet to appear for their final exams, are also eligible to apply. However, their admission would be provisional and subject to meeting the minimum marks requirement upon declaration of their results.

It's vital to ensure you meet these criteria before submitting your SNAP application to avoid any potential rejection later in the admissions process.

## Eligibility for International Students

SIU welcomes applications from a diverse international student body, including Foreign Nationals, Persons of Indian Origin (PIO), Overseas Citizens of India (OCI), and Non-Resident Indian (NRI) candidates. Specific requirements apply to these categories:

*   **English Proficiency**: Candidates applying under the Foreign Nationals/OCI/PIO categories must demonstrate English proficiency.
*   **Proficiency Tests**: This can be achieved by scoring a minimum band of 6 in the IELTS exam or a minimum band of 4 in the IELA exam.
*   **Documentation**: At the time of SNAP registration, international applicants must provide proof of residence, a copy of their valid Visa and passport. For those on a student visa, a Bonafide Certificate and a Letter of Provisional Admission from the University College/Institution are also necessary.
*   **Equivalency Certificate**: Candidates who have obtained their qualifying degree from a foreign university must secure an equivalency certificate from the Association of Indian Universities (AIU) to be eligible.

## SIU Reservation Policy

Symbiosis International (Deemed to to be) University follows a well-defined reservation policy for admissions to its MBA/PGDM programs, ensuring opportunities for various categories. These policies are in line with government regulations:

*   **Scheduled Caste (SC)**: 15% of the total seats.
*   **Scheduled Tribe (ST)**: 7.5% of the total seats.
*   **Differently Abled Candidates**: 3% of the total seats.
*   **Kashmiri Migrants**: 2 seats per program.
*   **Defence Personnel Wards**: 5% of the total seats for children of serving/retired Defence personnel in specific institutes like Symbiosis Institute of Business Management (SIBM) Pune and Symbiosis Centre for Human Resource Development (SCHMRD).

It is important to note that these reservations apply to the intake capacity of each specific program at the constituent institutes of SIU.

## Important Dates

The SNAP 2026 registration process is expected to commence in the first week of August 2026. The examination itself will be conducted in December 2026, typically across three separate sessions. The eligibility criteria remain consistent across all three examination sessions.

Meeting these eligibility requirements is the fundamental step towards your MBA journey at Symbiosis International (Deemed to be) University.
