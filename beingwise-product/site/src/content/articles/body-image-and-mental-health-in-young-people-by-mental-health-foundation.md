# Body Image and Mental Health in Young People by Mental Health Foundation via Futurelearn: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding how young people perceive their bodies and the impact this has on their mental well-being is incredibly important today. This online course, developed by the Mental Health Foundation and delivered through Futurelearn, offers valuable insights and practical strategies for supporting healthy body image.

## Key Takeaways

*   The course explores the intricate link between body image and mental health across different life stages.
*   It delves into various factors influencing body image in children and young people, including social media and family dynamics.
*   Participants will learn actionable strategies to foster positive body image in young individuals within educational, home, and professional settings.
*   The learning is self-paced, conducted in English, and primarily uses video and text formats.

## Course Structure and Effort

This is a self-study course designed to be flexible, allowing you to learn at your own pace. The content is delivered through a combination of videos and written materials. While you can complete it on your own schedule, it's estimated that you'll need to dedicate approximately three hours per week to engage with the material effectively. The entire course is taught in English.

## Detailed Syllabus

The course is thoughtfully divided into three weekly modules, each building on the last to provide a comprehensive understanding of body image and mental health.

### Week 1: Defining Body Image and Mental Health Throughout Life

This introductory week sets the foundation, helping you understand the core concepts. You'll learn the definitions of body image and mental health, and explore how these two aspects are interconnected. The module also touches upon how body image evolves through adulthood and later life, providing a broader context.

### Week 2: Body Image in Children and Young People

Building on the initial definitions, this week focuses specifically on younger populations. It examines the psychological factors, such as thoughts and feelings, that shape body image. You'll also explore external influences like family, social networks, cultural backgrounds, and the impact of health conditions or disabilities. A significant portion covers the role of media, technology, and even cosmetic procedures in shaping body image perceptions among young people.

### Week 3: Supporting Children and Young People to Maintain Healthy Body Image

The final week is highly practical, offering strategies for intervention and support. It guides professionals on personal actions they can take and how to have informed conversations about sensitive topics like cosmetic procedures. The module also provides advice on supporting children and young people within educational environments and at home. Finally, it addresses the role of policy in promoting healthier body image across society.

## Course Fees and Certification

There are a few options for accessing this course. You can purchase one-off access to this specific course for ₹1,600. Alternatively, Futurelearn offers a subscription model where you can access multiple courses for ₹6,500 for a full year. There is also a limited free access option, which typically allows you to view course content for a set period without full access to graded assignments or a certificate. Upon successful completion of the paid course, a certificate of completion is available, issued directly by Futurelearn.

## Instructor

The course content is delivered by Ms. Ruby Kellard, an experienced instructor and freelancer in the field.

This course is an excellent opportunity for anyone looking to deepen their understanding of body image and mental health, particularly those who work with or care for young people.
