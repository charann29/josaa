# UPCAT Counselling 2022: Dates, Application Form, Eligibility, Seat Allotment

For students aspiring to join various undergraduate and postgraduate programs in Uttar Pradesh, the admission landscape has evolved significantly. This guide will clarify the counselling process for 2022 admissions, which previously fell under the UPCET.

## Key takeaways

*   The UPCET exam, formerly known as UPSEE, has been discontinued.
*   Admissions for 2022 to colleges affiliated with AKTU, MMMUT, and other state universities in UP are now based on national-level exams like CUET, JEE Main, and NATA.
*   Undergraduate courses such as B.Tech, B.Des, B.Pharm, BHMCT, and BFA/BFAD use JEE Main, CUET (UG), or NATA scores.
*   Postgraduate courses like MBA, MCA, M.Tech, MURP, M.Pharm, M.Des, and M.Arch are offered through CUET (PG) scores.

## Understanding the New Admission Process for 2022

Before 2022, the Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test (UPCET) served as the primary entrance exam for admissions to many professional courses across Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU), Lucknow, Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT), Gorakhpur, and Harcourt Butler Technical University (HBTU), Kanpur. However, for the 2022 academic year, the National Testing Agency (NTA) announced that UPCET would be replaced by other established national entrance examinations.

This means that candidates no longer need to appear for a separate UPCET exam. Instead, their scores from exams like the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) for both undergraduate and postgraduate programs, JEE Main for engineering courses, and NATA for architecture courses will be considered for admission to these state universities in Uttar Pradesh.

## Courses and Corresponding Entrance Exams for 2022

To help you understand which exam is relevant for your desired program, here's a breakdown:

*   **Undergraduate Courses:**
    *   **B.Tech (including Lateral Entry):** JEE Main
    *   **B.Des, BFA/BFAD, BHMCT, B.Pharm (including Lateral Entry):** CUET (UG)
*   **Postgraduate Courses:**
    *   **MBA (including Integrated MBA), MCA (including Integrated MCA/Lateral Entry):** CUET (PG) or CUET (UG) for integrated programs.
    *   **M.Tech, M.Pharm, M.Des, M.Arch, MURP:** CUET (PG)

This shift aims to streamline the admission process by leveraging national-level examinations that many students already take.

## Eligibility Criteria for 2022 Admissions

While the specific entrance exam has changed, the general eligibility criteria for admissions to UP state universities largely remain consistent. There is typically no age limit for most programs. Key requirements often include:

*   **Domicile:** Candidates should have passed their qualifying examination from an institution located in Uttar Pradesh. Alternatively, if they studied outside UP, their parents must hold a domicile certificate for Uttar Pradesh. However, certain provisions may allow candidates who don't meet the domicile requirements to apply if they fulfill other academic criteria.
*   **Academic Qualification:** Applicants must have passed the relevant qualifying examination (e.g., 10+2 for UG courses, Bachelor's degree for PG courses) with the minimum aggregate marks specified by the respective university or program. Specific subject requirements also apply (e.g., Physics, Chemistry, Maths for B.Tech).
*   **Appearing Candidates:** Students in their final year of the qualifying examination are generally eligible to apply, provided they submit their passing certificates by the stipulated deadline during the admission process.

Always refer to the official admission brochures released by AKTU, MMMUT, or HBTU for the most precise and up-to-date eligibility details for the 2022 academic year.

## Counselling and Seat Allotment Process 2022

The counselling process for admissions based on CUET, JEE Main, or NATA scores will be conducted by the respective state authorities or universities. This typically involves several steps:

1.  **Registration:** Candidates who have appeared for the relevant entrance exam and meet the eligibility criteria must register for the counselling process on the official portal of the conducting body (e.g., AKTU's admission portal).
2.  **Choice Filling and Locking:** Registered candidates will then fill in their preferred courses and colleges from the available options. It's crucial to research colleges and courses carefully, considering NIRF rankings and personal interests, before locking choices.
3.  **Seat Allotment:** Based on the candidate's rank in the entrance exam, their filled choices, and the reservation policies, seats will be allotted in various rounds. The seat allotment results will be published on the official portal.
4.  **Document Verification and Fee Payment:** Allotted candidates must report to the designated centers or complete online verification of their documents. Following successful verification, they will need to pay the admission fee to confirm their seat.
5.  **Withdrawal/Upgradation:** Candidates may have options to withdraw their admission or participate in subsequent rounds for seat upgradation if they are not satisfied with their initial allotment.

Past seat allotment rounds for UPCET 2021, for example, extended into November, indicating a multi-round process. Candidates should closely follow the official announcements for the exact schedule and procedures for 2022 counselling.

The admission process for UP state universities in 2022 leverages national entrance exams, simplifying applications for many students while maintaining a structured counselling and seat allotment system.
