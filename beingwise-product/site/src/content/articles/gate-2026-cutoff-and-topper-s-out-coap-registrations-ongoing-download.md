# GATE 2026: Cutoff & Topper's OUT, COAP Registrations Ongoing, Download Scorecard at GOAPS

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) 2026 results are now out, with Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Guwahati, this year's organising institute, releasing the official cutoff marks and the list of top performers. For those who have qualified, the application window for M.Tech admissions through COAP is currently open.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Results and Scorecards:** GATE 2026 results were declared on March 19, and qualified candidates can download their scorecards for free from the official GOAPS portal until May 31. This scorecard remains valid for three years.
*   **Counselling for M.Tech:** Registrations for COAP (Common Offer Acceptance Portal) are ongoing, with Round 1 of counselling scheduled to commence on May 11. COAP is crucial for M.Tech admissions to IITs.
*   **Exam Details:** IIT Guwahati conducted the GATE 2026 examination across 30 different papers on February 7, 8, 14, and 15.
*   **Benefits of Qualification:** A valid GATE score opens doors to M.Tech/PhD programs, financial assistance during studies, and recruitment opportunities in leading Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs).

## GATE Scorecard and Its Validity

Following the result declaration, all qualified candidates can now obtain their official GATE 2026 scorecard. This document is essential for various post-GATE applications. It is available for download without any fee from the GOAPS website until May 31. Beyond this date, a fee might be applicable for downloading it. Importantly, the GATE 2026 scorecard will remain valid for three years, meaning it can be used for admissions or job applications until March 31, 2029.

## Understanding GATE Counselling: COAP and CCMT

For M.Tech admissions to premier institutions like the IITs, IISc, NITs, IIITs, and other Centrally Funded Technical Institutes (CFTIs), GATE-qualified candidates must participate in counselling processes. There are two primary platforms for this:

*   **COAP (Common Offer Acceptance Portal):** This portal manages M.Tech admissions specifically for IITs. Registrations for COAP 2026 are currently active, and the first round of counselling is set to begin on May 11. Candidates should regularly check the official COAP portal for detailed schedules and updates.
*   **CCMT (Centralized Counselling for M.Tech/M.Arch/M.Plan):** This portal handles admissions to NITs, IIITs, and some GFTIs. The schedule for CCMT will be announced separately.

Candidates are advised to stay informed about the specific counselling dates and procedures for the institutions they wish to apply to.

## GATE 2026 Cutoff Marks and Topper's List

IIT Guwahati has officially released the minimum qualifying marks (cutoff) for each of the 30 papers, along with the list of top-performing candidates. These details are crucial for applicants to understand their standing and eligibility for various programs. The cutoff marks vary significantly across different engineering disciplines and are determined based on factors like the number of applicants, the difficulty level of the exam, and the available seats.

Candidates can view the detailed cutoff information and the names of the toppers for branches like Computer Science Engineering (CSE), Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE), Mechanical Engineering (ME), and Civil Engineering (CE) on the official GATE 2026 website.

## Benefits of a Valid GATE Score

Qualifying the GATE examination offers a multitude of advantages for aspiring engineers and researchers:

*   **Postgraduate Admissions:** A GATE score is mandatory for admission to M.Tech and Ph.D. programs at leading institutions like IITs, NITs, IISc, and other GFTIs.
*   **Financial Assistance:** Students admitted to M.Tech or Ph.D. programs with a valid GATE score are often eligible for financial aid. This typically includes a monthly stipend of INR 12,400 for M.Tech students. For Ph.D./research scholars, the assistance can be around INR 37,000 for the first two years, increasing to INR 42,000 from the third to the fifth year.
*   **Public Sector Undertaking (PSU) Jobs:** Many top PSUs, such as GAIL, AAI, BHEL, and IOCL, recruit engineers directly based on their GATE scores, offering excellent career opportunities.
*   **Research Opportunities:** A strong GATE score can lead to research positions in national laboratories and research centres.
*   **Study Abroad:** A growing number of international universities, including the National University of Singapore, RWTH Aachen University, and the University of Munich, recognize GATE scores for admissions to their postgraduate engineering programs.

Stay updated with official announcements from IIT Guwahati and the respective counselling portals for the latest information.
