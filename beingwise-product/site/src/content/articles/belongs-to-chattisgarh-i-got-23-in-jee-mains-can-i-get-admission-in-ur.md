## Understanding Your JEE Main Score

When you mention '23' in the context of JEE Main, it's crucial to distinguish between a rank and marks. The JEE Main exam assigns both a raw score (marks) and an All India Rank (AIR), as well as a Category Rank. These are very different metrics.

If '23' refers to your All India Rank, that is an exceptional achievement. A rank this low (meaning very high performance) would open doors to virtually any National Institute of Technology (NIT), Indian Institute of Information Technology (IIIT), or Government Funded Technical Institute (GFTI) across India, including the most sought-after branches like Computer Science Engineering in top-tier NITs.

However, if '23' refers to your raw marks out of the total 300 marks in JEE Main, then the situation is different. A score of 23 marks is typically very low and usually falls well below the minimum qualifying percentile required for admission to NITs, IIITs, or even to appear for JEE Advanced.

## JEE Main Qualifying Percentile

The National Testing Agency (NTA), which conducts JEE Main, releases cutoff percentiles for various categories. These percentiles determine eligibility for JEE Advanced and also play a role in admissions to NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs through the JoSAA counselling process. For general category students, the qualifying percentile for JEE Advanced is usually above 90. For reserved categories, it can be lower, but still significantly higher than what a raw score of 23 marks would typically yield.

For instance, even for reserved categories, the qualifying percentile for JEE Advanced typically hovers around 40-50 percentile or more. A raw score of 23 marks would likely correspond to a very low percentile, making admission through the JEE Main pathway challenging.

## Admission through JoSAA and State Counselling

Admissions to NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs are conducted through the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) counselling based on your JEE Main All India Rank. Your Chhattisgarh domicile might also provide benefits in state-level engineering college admissions, but these usually still require a reasonable JEE Main percentile or a good score in a state-specific entrance exam.

If your JEE Main marks are indeed 23, it's advisable to explore other options. This could include preparing again for JEE Main, considering state-level engineering entrance exams for colleges within Chhattisgarh, or looking into private universities that might have different admission criteria.
