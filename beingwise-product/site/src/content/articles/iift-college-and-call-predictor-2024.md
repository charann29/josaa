# IIFT College and Call Predictor 2024

Understanding your chances of admission to the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT) can be a crucial step in your MBA journey. This guide will help you navigate the expected cut-offs and clarify how the IIFT admission process generally works.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIFT offers its flagship MBA in International Business program across its Delhi, Kolkata, and Kakinada campuses.
*   Admission to IIFT's MBA (IB) program is primarily based on a competitive entrance examination, followed by subsequent selection rounds.
*   Cut-off scores vary each year based on factors like exam difficulty, the number of applicants, and seat availability.
*   While specific 2024 cut-offs are yet to be announced, historical data provides valuable insights into what scores might be competitive.

## Understanding IIFT Admissions

The Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT) is a premier institution for international business education in India. Its MBA in International Business (IB) program is highly sought after. The admission process typically involves an entrance exam, followed by a Written Ability Test (WAT), Group Discussion (GD), and Personal Interview (PI) rounds for shortlisted candidates. The National Testing Agency (NTA) previously conducted the IIFT entrance exam, though changes in the examination body or format are periodically announced by IIFT through its official admission notifications.

## IIFT Campuses and Programs

IIFT currently operates three campuses, each offering the renowned MBA in International Business program. While the core curriculum is similar, location and infrastructure can influence a candidate's preference. Here's a quick overview:

*   **IIFT Delhi:** Often considered the flagship campus, it has a long-standing reputation and is located in the national capital.
*   **IIFT Kolkata:** Another well-established campus, offering a strong academic environment and industry exposure.
*   **IIFT Kakinada:** The newest campus, it is developing rapidly and aims to provide quality international business education in a growing region.

## Expected Cut-offs for MBA (IB) 2024

Predicting exact cut-offs for any given year is challenging, as they are dynamic. However, looking at past trends can offer a realistic expectation. The cut-off score refers to the minimum score required in the entrance exam to be shortlisted for the subsequent admission rounds (WAT, GD, PI). It's important to remember that these are general indicators; the actual cut-offs for 2024 will be officially released by IIFT after the entrance exam results.

Based on historical data (e.g., from 2019, which saw a similar exam structure), here are approximate scores that led to calls for the MBA (IB) program:

| :---------- | :--------------------------------------------- |
| Delhi       | 36                                             |
| Kolkata     | 34                                             |
| Kakinada    | 32                                             |

These scores are out of the total marks for the entrance exam. Candidates aiming for IIFT should target scores well above these historical cut-offs to increase their chances of receiving a call for the subsequent selection rounds.

## Program Fees

The fee structure for the MBA in International Business program across IIFT campuses is generally similar, with minor variations. For instance, the approximate total program fees for the MBA (IB) program are:

*   **IIFT Delhi:** Around ₹21.77 Lakhs
*   **IIFT Kolkata:** Around ₹20.97 Lakhs
*   **IIFT Kakinada:** Around ₹21.32 Lakhs

These figures are subject to change and should be verified with the official IIFT admission brochure for the specific academic year.

Staying informed about official announcements from IIFT is key to a successful application. Good luck with your preparation!
