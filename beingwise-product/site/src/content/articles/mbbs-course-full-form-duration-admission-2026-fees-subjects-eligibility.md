# MBBS: Course, Full Form, Duration, Admission 2026, Fees, Subjects, Eligibility, Salary & Career Scope

MBBS, or Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery, is the foundational undergraduate degree for aspiring doctors. It's a comprehensive program designed to equip students with the medical knowledge and practical skills required to practice medicine and surgery.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Full Form and Duration:** MBBS stands for Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery, a 5.5-year undergraduate program including a mandatory internship.
*   **Admission Process:** Admission to all MBBS programs in India is solely based on the NEET UG entrance examination, making it one of the most competitive tests.
*   **Eligibility:** Candidates must have completed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (or Biotechnology) and be at least 17 years old.
*   **Career Scope:** Beyond becoming a general physician, an MBBS degree opens doors to specializations (MD/MS), research, medical writing, and more.

## Understanding the MBBS Course

The MBBS program is a rigorous and rewarding journey into the medical field. It combines theoretical knowledge with extensive practical training, preparing students for real-world medical challenges. The curriculum covers a vast array of subjects, from foundational sciences to clinical disciplines.

### MBBS Course Details

| Particulars | Details |
| :------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Full Form** | Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery |
| **Course Level** | Undergraduate (UG) |
| **Course Type** | Full-Time |
| **Course Duration** | 5 years + 6 months compulsory internship |
| **Admission Mode** | Entrance-Based |
| **Entrance Exam** | NEET UG |
| **Average Fees** | Government Colleges: INR 10,000 - INR 50,000 per year<br>Private Colleges: INR 3 Lakh - INR 25 Lakh per year |

## Eligibility Criteria and Admission Process for 2026

To be eligible for an MBBS program, candidates must meet specific academic and age requirements. The primary gateway to all medical colleges in India is the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET UG).

### Eligibility Requirements

*   **Academic Qualification:** Applicants must have passed Class 12 (or equivalent) with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology as core subjects.
*   **Minimum Age:** Candidates must be at least 17 years old by December 31 of the admission year.
*   **NEET UG Qualification:** A valid score in NEET UG is mandatory.

### Admission Process 2026

Admission to MBBS courses for the academic year 2026 will primarily rely on the NEET UG 2026 examination, which is expected to be held on May 3, 2026, as per NTA announcements. After qualifying NEET UG, candidates participate in counselling rounds conducted by various authorities:

*   **All India Quota (AIQ):** 15% of seats in government colleges are filled through counselling managed by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC).
*   **State Quota:** The remaining 85% of seats in government colleges and all seats in private colleges are filled through state-level counselling bodies.

## Key Subjects in MBBS

The MBBS curriculum is structured to provide a holistic understanding of human anatomy, physiology, diseases, and their treatment. The subjects evolve from foundational sciences in the initial years to clinical specializations later on. Some core subjects include:

*   **Anatomy:** Study of the human body's structure.
*   **Physiology:** Study of how the human body functions.
*   **Biochemistry:** Study of chemical processes within living organisms.
*   **Pharmacology:** Study of drugs and their effects.
*   **Pathology:** Study of diseases, their causes, and effects.
*   **Microbiology:** Study of microorganisms and their role in health and disease.
*   **Forensic Medicine:** Application of medical knowledge to legal questions.
*   **Community Medicine:** Focus on public health and preventive medicine.
*   **General Medicine:** Diagnosis and non-surgical treatment of diseases.
*   **General Surgery:** Surgical treatment of diseases.
*   **Pediatrics:** Medical care of infants, children, and adolescents.
*   **Obstetrics & Gynaecology:** Care of women during pregnancy, childbirth, and the reproductive system.

## Fees and Top Colleges

The cost of an MBBS degree can vary significantly between government and private institutions. Government colleges typically have much lower fees due to subsidies.

### Average Annual Fees

*   **Government Colleges:** Generally range from INR 10,000 to INR 50,000 per year.
*   **Private Colleges:** Can range from INR 3 Lakh to INR 25 Lakh per year.

### Top Colleges for MBBS

Several institutions across India are highly regarded for their MBBS programs. According to various official rankings like NIRF, some of the top colleges include:

*   **Government:** All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) Delhi, Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education and Research (JIPMER) Puducherry, King George's Medical University (KGMU) Lucknow.
*   **Private:** Christian Medical College (CMC) Vellore, Kasturba Medical College (KMC) Manipal, Sri Ramachandra Institute of Higher Education and Research (SRIHER) Chennai, St. John’s National Academy of Health Sciences Bengaluru.

## Career Scope and Salary After MBBS

An MBBS degree is the first step towards a fulfilling career in medicine. Graduates have a diverse range of opportunities, both in clinical practice and other related fields.

### Job Profiles

After completing MBBS, common job roles include:

*   MBBS Doctor / General Physician
*   Resident Medical Officer
*   Junior Doctor
*   Medical Officer
*   Researcher
*   Medical Writer
*   Lecturer

Many MBBS graduates also pursue postgraduate specializations (MD/MS) in fields like Cardiology, Pediatrics, General Surgery, or Radiology to further enhance their career prospects and earning potential.

### Average Salary

The average starting salary for an MBBS graduate in India can range from INR 2.5 LPA to INR 15 LPA, depending on the hospital, location, and experience. With specialization and experience, salaries can increase substantially.

### Top Recruiters

Major hospitals and healthcare groups across India are primary recruiters for MBBS graduates. Some well-known names include Medanta Hospitals, Fortis Healthcare Ltd, Lilavati Hospital and Research Centre, and Wockhardt Ltd.

Embarking on an MBBS journey is a significant commitment, but it leads to a highly respected and impactful profession dedicated to serving humanity.
