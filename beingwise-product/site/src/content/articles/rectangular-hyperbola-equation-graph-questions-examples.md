# Rectangular Hyperbola: Equation, Graph, Questions, Examples

The rectangular hyperbola is a specific type of hyperbola found in coordinate geometry, distinguished by its asymptotes intersecting at a 90-degree angle. This page will explore its definition, various equations, graphical representation, and key characteristics.

## Key Takeaways

*   A rectangular hyperbola has perpendicular asymptotes, forming a right angle.
*   Its transverse and conjugate axes are of equal length.
*   The standard equation for a rectangular hyperbola centered at the origin, with asymptotes along the coordinate axes, is `xy = c²`.
*   The eccentricity of a rectangular hyperbola is always √2.

## Understanding the Rectangular Hyperbola

A rectangular hyperbola is a special case of a hyperbola where its two asymptotes meet at a right angle. This unique property means that the lengths of its transverse axis and conjugate axis are equal. Imagine two curves, often in opposite quadrants (like the first and third), that endlessly approach but never touch two perpendicular lines (the asymptotes).

This topic is a fundamental part of Class 11th mathematics and is crucial for both board examinations and competitive entrance tests like JEE Main, WBJEE, and BITSAT. Over the past decade, the JEE Main exam has featured approximately 30 questions related to hyperbolas, including rectangular hyperbolas.

### Condition for a Rectangular Hyperbola

The defining condition for a rectangular hyperbola is that its transverse axis length, `2p`, is equal to its conjugate axis length, `2q`, which simplifies to `p = q`. This equality leads to its distinctive properties. When centered at the origin, a general equation for such a hyperbola is `x² - y² = p²`.

## Equations of a Rectangular Hyperbola

Depending on its orientation and center, a rectangular hyperbola can be expressed in different forms:

### Standard Equation (Centered at the Origin)

When a rectangular hyperbola is centered at the origin and its asymptotes align with the coordinate axes, its standard equation is:

`xy = c²`

Here, `c` is a constant that influences the size of the hyperbola. The x-axis and y-axis serve as its asymptotes in this configuration.

### General Equation (Symmetry about the Origin)

For a hyperbola that is symmetric about the origin, the general form is:

`x²/a² - y²/b² = 1`

In the specific case of a rectangular hyperbola, `a = b`.

### Parametric Form

The parametric equations for a rectangular hyperbola are:

`x = ct`
`y = c/t`

## Graphing a Rectangular Hyperbola

The graph of a rectangular hyperbola with the equation `xy = c²` will show two branches in opposite quadrants, approaching the x and y axes as asymptotes. If we rotate the coordinate axes by 45 degrees, keeping the origin fixed, the lines `y = x` and `y = -x` become the new axes. Under this rotation, an equation like `x² - y² = a²` transforms into `xy = a²/2`, which is in the standard `xy = c²` form, where `c² = a²/2`.

For a rectangular hyperbola represented by `xy = c²`, key features include:

*   **Vertices:** `A(c, c)` and `A'(-c, -c)`
*   **Transverse Axis:** The line `x = y`
*   **Conjugate Axis:** The line `x = -y`
*   **Foci:** `S(c√2, c√2)` and `S'(-c√2, -c√2)`
*   **Directrices:** The lines `x + y = ±√2c`
*   **Length of Latus Rectum:** `2√2c`

### Eccentricity of a Rectangular Hyperbola

The eccentricity (`e`) of a hyperbola is generally given by the formula `e = √(1 + b²/a²)`. For a rectangular hyperbola, we know that the transverse axis and conjugate axis are equal in length, meaning `a = b`. Substituting `b² = a²` into the eccentricity formula:

`e = √(1 + a²/a²) = √(1 + 1) = √2`

Thus, the eccentricity of any rectangular hyperbola is always √2.

Mastering the properties and equations of the rectangular hyperbola is essential for success in higher mathematics and various competitive examinations.
