# CDS 2026: CDS 1 Exam Date, Pattern, Syllabus, Analysis, Answer Key & Prep Tips

The Combined Defence Services (CDS) examination is a national-level test conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) to recruit officers into the Indian Armed Forces. This page provides essential details for aspiring candidates, covering key dates, exam structure, and preparation advice for CDS 2026.

## Key Takeaways

*   The CDS 1 2026 examination is scheduled for April 12, 2026.
*   A total of 451 vacancies were announced for the CDS 1 2026 cycle.
*   The application window for CDS 1 2026 ran from December 10 to December 30, 2025.
*   The CDS 2 2026 application period will be from May 20 to June 9, 2026, with the exam on September 13, 2026.

## CDS Admit Card 2026

The admit card, often called the hall ticket, is a mandatory document for appearing in the CDS examination. The UPSC releases these online on its official portal. Candidates must download their admit cards using their login credentials (registration number or roll number and date of birth). A physical copy will not be sent by post.

### How to Download Your Admit Card

1.  Visit the official UPSC website.
2.  Navigate to the 'Admit Cards' section and click on the relevant CDS admit card link.
3.  You will be redirected to a new page. Read the instructions carefully.
4.  Enter your application number or roll number and date of birth.
5.  Click 'Submit'.
6.  Your admit card will appear on the screen. Download and print it for the exam day and future reference.

### Addressing Discrepancies in Your Admit Card

It is crucial to thoroughly check all details on your admit card, including your name, father's name, category, date of birth, photograph, and signature. Should you find any errors, contact the UPSC immediately. When reporting an issue, provide the following details:

*   Examination name and year
*   Registration number
*   Roll number
*   Your postal address (also mention your application number)
*   If your photograph is missing or unclear, you are advised to carry a passport-sized photograph to the exam centre along with your admit card and a valid ID.

For any discrepancies, candidates should communicate with the Commission via the e-mail ID: skindo-upsc@gov.in, as instructed by the UPSC.

## CDS Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The CDS exam comprises objective-type papers. For admission to the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Indian Naval Academy (INA), and Air Force Academy (AFA), candidates take tests in English, General Knowledge, and Elementary Mathematics. For the Officers' Training Academy (OTA), the Elementary Mathematics paper is not required.

### Exam Pattern Overview

*   **English:** Objective type, 100 marks, 2-hour duration.
*   **General Knowledge:** Objective type, 100 marks, 2-hour duration.
*   **Elementary Mathematics:** Objective type, 100 marks, 2-hour duration (for IMA, INA, AFA only).

The questions are set in English and Hindi, except for the English paper itself. There is a penalty for incorrect answers.

### Syllabus Highlights

*   **English:** Tests comprehension, vocabulary, grammar, and sentence structure.
*   **General Knowledge:** Covers current events, history of India, geography, Indian polity, economics, and science.
*   **Elementary Mathematics:** Includes arithmetic, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, and mensuration, typically up to the matriculation level.

## Preparation Tips for CDS 2026

Success in the CDS exam requires a well-structured preparation strategy. Here are some effective tips:

*   **Understand the Syllabus and Pattern:** Begin by thoroughly understanding the official syllabus and exam pattern released by UPSC. This will help you focus your studies.
*   **Create a Study Schedule:** Develop a realistic daily or weekly study plan. Allocate sufficient time to each subject based on your strengths and weaknesses.
*   **Focus on Fundamentals:** Ensure you have a strong grasp of basic concepts, especially in mathematics and grammar.
*   **Read Newspapers and Magazines:** To excel in General Knowledge, regularly read national newspapers and current affairs magazines. Pay attention to national and international events, defence news, and government policies.
*   **Practice Previous Year Papers:** Solving past question papers is invaluable. It familiarizes you with the question types, difficulty level, and time management.
*   **Take Mock Tests:** Regularly taking mock tests helps evaluate your preparation, identify areas needing improvement, and build speed and accuracy.
*   **Revision is Key:** Consistent revision of all subjects is essential to retain information and solidify your understanding.

By following these guidelines and maintaining a focused approach, you can significantly enhance your chances of clearing the CDS 2026 examination.
