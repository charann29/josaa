# Antimicrobial Stewardship in Veterinary Practice by British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy via Futurelearn: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This program, offered in collaboration with the British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy and the University of Edinburgh, provides a vital understanding of responsible antimicrobial use in animal care. It's designed to equip veterinary professionals and students with the knowledge to combat antimicrobial resistance effectively.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn critical strategies for responsible antimicrobial use in various veterinary settings.
*   Understand the global challenge of antimicrobial resistance and its impact.
*   Gain insights into evidence-based veterinary medicine and clinical governance in relation to antimicrobial stewardship.
*   The core course content is free, with an optional certificate available for a fee.

## Course Overview and Structure

This self-paced, video and text-based course is delivered entirely in English. It's designed to be flexible, requiring approximately three hours of study per week. The program is structured into three weekly modules, each focusing on a different aspect of antimicrobial stewardship.

### Week 1: Understanding Medicines Use

This introductory week sets the stage by defining antimicrobial stewardship and exploring the pressing issue of antimicrobial resistance. Participants will review current prescribing practices and identify common challenges in implementing effective stewardship programs.

### Week 2: Engaging with Medicines Use

The second week delves into practical applications, connecting evidence-based veterinary medicine (EBVM) and clinical governance with antimicrobial stewardship. It examines how these principles apply across different veterinary fields, including companion animal, production animal, and equine practices.

### Week 3: Transforming Medicines Use

The final week focuses on implementing lasting change. It revisits companion animal, production animal, and equine practices, and also introduces considerations for exotics practice and even human medical practice. The module concludes by exploring strategies to motivate and sustain transformations in antimicrobial use.

## Certificate and Fees

The core learning content of this program is completely free to access. For those who wish to receive formal recognition of their completion and learning, an optional certificate is available. This certificate is provided by Futurelearn, and there is a fee of ₹1,100 to obtain it.

## How to Apply

As this is an online course offered through the Futurelearn platform, the application process is straightforward. Interested individuals can typically enroll directly on the official Futurelearn website by creating an account and selecting the course. There are no specific eligibility criteria mentioned, making it accessible to a wide range of learners in the veterinary field.

Embark on this essential learning journey to contribute to a healthier future for both animals and humans by promoting responsible antimicrobial practices.
