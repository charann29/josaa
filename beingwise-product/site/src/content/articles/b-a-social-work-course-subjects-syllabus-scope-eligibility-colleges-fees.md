# B.A. Social Work - Course, Subjects, Syllabus, Scope, Eligibility, Colleges, Fees, Salary

A Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in Social Work is a three-year undergraduate program designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills to support individuals, families, and communities in need. This course focuses on understanding societal challenges and developing strategies to promote well-being and social justice.

## Key takeaways

*   The B.A. Social Work degree typically spans three years, divided into six semesters.
*   Admission often involves both 10+2 academic performance and scores from entrance exams like CUET UG.
*   Graduates can find roles as counsellors, executive officers, or educators, with an average starting salary around INR 3.30 Lakhs per annum.
*   The program fee generally ranges from INR 16,000 to INR 60,000 across different institutions.

## Eligibility Criteria for B.A. Social Work

To be considered for a B.A. in Social Work program, applicants typically need to meet the following requirements:

*   Successful completion of their 10+2 examinations from a recognized board, in any stream.
*   A minimum aggregate score of 50% in their 10+2 (often relaxed to 45% for SC/ST candidates).
*   While not always mandatory, a background in subjects like psychology, sociology, or social sciences can be advantageous.

It's always advisable to check the specific eligibility details on the official website or prospectus of your chosen college, as criteria can vary.

## Admission Process

The admission process for a B.A. Social Work program can differ between institutions. Many colleges consider a combination of factors, while some rely on specific entrance examinations.

1.  **Application Submission:** Candidates usually begin by filling out an application form, often available on the college's official website. It's crucial to ensure all eligibility criteria are met before applying.
2.  **Entrance Exams:** Many institutions, especially government colleges, use scores from national-level entrance tests like CUET UG. Some private colleges may conduct their own entrance examinations.
3.  **Merit-Based Admissions:** A few colleges offer direct admission based solely on a candidate's academic performance in their 10+2 examinations.
4.  **Interview Rounds:** Shortlisted candidates might be invited for a personal interview as part of the selection process.
5.  **Allotment and Enrollment:** Successful candidates receive admission offers, followed by the necessary steps for fee payment and enrollment.

## Course Structure and Syllabus

The B.A. Social Work curriculum is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of social issues, human behavior, and intervention strategies. The three-year program is typically divided into six semesters, covering a range of theoretical and practical subjects. While specific subjects may vary by college, common areas of study include:

*   Introduction to Social Work
*   Sociology for Social Work
*   Psychology for Social Work
*   Human Rights and Social Justice
*   Social Welfare Administration
*   Community Development
*   Research Methods in Social Work
*   Fieldwork and Internships
*   Counselling and Communication Skills
*   Social Policy and Planning

Practical fieldwork is an integral part of the course, allowing students to gain hands-on experience in real-world social work settings.

## Career Scope and Salary after B.A. Social Work

A B.A. in Social Work opens doors to various rewarding career paths focused on making a positive impact on society. Graduates can work in diverse settings, including NGOs, government agencies, hospitals, educational institutions, and corporate social responsibility (CSR) departments.

### Job Profiles and Average Salary

| Job Profile         | Average Annual Salary (INR) |
| :------------------ | :-------------------------- |
| Counsellor          | 3.30 Lakhs                  |
| Executive Officer   | 3.30 Lakhs                  |
| Teacher/Professor   | 3.30 Lakhs                  |

*Note: These are average figures; actual salaries can vary based on experience, employer, and location.*

Top recruiters often include large corporations with CSR initiatives, as well as various non-profit organizations dedicated to social welfare. Companies like Infosys and Amazon have been known to hire graduates for relevant roles.

## Top Colleges and Fees

Several institutions across India offer the B.A. Social Work program. According to available data, there are a total of 9 colleges offering this degree, with 7 being private institutions and 2 government-run. The average fee for the program generally falls within the range of INR 16,290 to INR 60,000 per annum.

Some notable colleges offering this course include Kasthurba College for Women, Dr. Jagannath Mishra Mahavidyalaya, and the National Institute of Social Work and Social Sciences. When selecting a college, consider factors like faculty expertise, campus facilities, and internship opportunities.

Embarking on a B.A. in Social Work can be a fulfilling choice for those passionate about contributing to societal betterment and supporting vulnerable populations.
