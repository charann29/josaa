# How to get 120 in MHT CET: What Should I Follow?

Aiming for a score of 120 in the MHT CET is a commendable goal that can open doors to many reputable engineering and pharmacy colleges in Maharashtra. Achieving this score requires a well-thought-out strategy, consistent effort, and a clear understanding of the examination itself.

## Key Takeaways

*   Thoroughly understand the MHT CET exam pattern, syllabus, and marking scheme before you begin your preparation.
*   Strategically prioritize high-weightage topics across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology to maximize your score.
*   Master time management during the exam, as there is no negative marking, allowing you to attempt all questions.
*   Focus on revision and strengthening existing knowledge in the final days before the exam, avoiding new topics.

## Understand the MHT CET Exam Structure

Before you even open a textbook, it's crucial to familiarize yourself with the MHT CET's complete framework. This includes the detailed syllabus for Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) or Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB), the exam pattern, and the marking scheme. The official MHT CET information brochure, usually released by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, provides all these essential details. Knowing the distribution of questions from Class 11 and Class 12 syllabi, the number of questions per subject, and the duration for each paper (Paper 1: Mathematics; Paper 2: Physics & Chemistry; Paper 3: Biology) will help you plan your study schedule effectively.

## Strategic Topic Prioritization

To hit the 120-mark target, it's not just about studying everything; it's about studying smart. Identify topics that carry higher weightage in the MHT CET from previous years' analyses. For instance, in Mathematics, topics like Calculus, Vectors, and 3D Geometry often have significant importance. In Physics, chapters such as Rotational Motion, Thermodynamics, and Electrostatics are frequently prominent. For Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, Chemical Bonding, and Solutions are usually key. By focusing more intensely on these high-yield areas, you can ensure that you are dedicating your valuable study time where it will have the greatest impact on your score. This doesn't mean ignoring other topics, but rather allocating your efforts proportionally.

## Master Time Management and Attempt All Questions

A unique advantage of the MHT CET is the absence of negative marking. This is a significant factor in your strategy for achieving a high score. Since there's no penalty for incorrect answers, you should aim to attempt every single question in the examination. However, simply attempting isn't enough; you must also manage your time efficiently. Practice solving mock tests within the stipulated time limits to improve your speed and accuracy. Learn to quickly identify questions you can solve immediately, mark those that require more thought for a second pass, and make educated guesses for the remaining ones. This approach ensures you don't miss out on any potential marks and fully leverage the no-negative-marking policy.

## The Importance of Consistent Revision and Calmness

Consistency is the bedrock of MHT CET preparation. Regular revision of all subjects and topics you've covered is far more effective than last-minute cramming. As the exam date approaches, shift your focus entirely to consolidating what you've already learned. Avoid starting new, complex topics in the final weeks. Instead, dedicate this crucial period to revisiting notes, solving previous year's papers, and taking full-length mock tests. This reinforces your existing knowledge, builds confidence, and helps you identify any lingering weak areas for quick review. Approaching the exam with a calm and confident mindset, knowing you've thoroughly revised, will significantly enhance your performance.

By following these guidelines, you can build a robust preparation plan to confidently aim for and achieve a score of 120 or more in the MHT CET.
