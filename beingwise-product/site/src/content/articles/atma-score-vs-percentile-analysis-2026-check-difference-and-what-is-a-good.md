Understanding the difference between your raw ATMA score and your ATMA percentile is crucial for anyone aiming for MBA or MCA admissions. This analysis helps you gauge what kind of performance is needed to secure a spot in your desired institution.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your ATMA score is your actual mark, while your percentile indicates your rank relative to other test-takers.
*   A higher ATMA percentile (generally 80+ for good colleges, 95+ for top ones) is more important than the raw score for admissions.
*   Different colleges set their own ATMA cutoff percentiles, which can vary each year.
*   ATMA scores from the 2026 exam are typically valid for one year, for the 2026-28 academic session.

## ATMA Score vs. Percentile: What's the Difference?

The ATMA score is simply the total number of marks you achieve in the examination. It's a direct measure of your performance based on the questions you answered correctly. The ATMA exam is typically scored out of a certain maximum mark, and your score reflects your individual achievement.

On the other hand, the ATMA percentile is a relative measure. It tells you the percentage of candidates who scored *less than or equal to* you in the exam. For example, if you score in the 90th percentile, it means 90% of the test-takers scored the same as or lower than you. This is why percentiles are often more significant for admissions, as they provide a comparative ranking among all candidates.

## What is Considered a Good ATMA Score and Percentile for 2026?

While a 'good' score can be subjective and depends on the colleges you're targeting, generally, an ATMA percentile of 80 or above is considered very competitive. For admission to some of the most sought-after MBA and MCA programs, candidates often need to achieve a percentile in the range of 95 to 99.

Many participating institutions release their individual cutoff percentiles after the ATMA results are declared. These cutoffs are the minimum percentile required for a candidate to be considered for their admission process. It's important to research the typical cutoffs for your preferred colleges to set a realistic target.

## ATMA 2026 Score and Percentile Analysis

The ATMA 2026 exam was conducted on February 22, 2026. The results for the February session are expected to be declared on February 28, 2026. Once the results are out, candidates will be able to download their ATMA scorecard online. This scorecard will display both your raw score and your overall percentile.

Admission to MBA and MCA programs for the 2026-28 academic session will be based on these ATMA 2026 scores. Each college will then conduct its own selection process, which typically includes Group Discussions (GD) and Personal Interviews (PI), in addition to considering your ATMA percentile.

## Top MBA Colleges Accepting ATMA Scores for 2026 Admissions

Many reputable management institutes across India consider ATMA scores for their MBA and MCA programs. The required percentile can vary significantly between institutions. Here's a general overview of percentile ranges and some colleges that typically accept them:

### Colleges Accepting 70-75+ Percentile

Several private institutions often accept candidates with ATMA percentiles in this range. These colleges might conduct multiple counseling rounds, where cutoffs could adjust. Examples include:

*   Delhi School of Business
*   Fortune Institute of International Business (FIIB), New Delhi
*   JK Business School, Gurgaon
*   Institute of Public Enterprise (IPE)
*   XIME, Bangalore
*   AIMS School of Business
*   GITAM Hyderabad Business School
*   Kristu Jayanti College (KJC)
*   MS Ramaiah Institute of Management
*   SDA Bocconi Asia Center
*   DSIMS, Mumbai
*   IES Management College and Research Centre (IES MCRC), Mumbai
*   MIT School of Telecom Management

### Colleges Accepting 80+ Percentile

An 80+ percentile is generally considered a strong performance, opening doors to a wider range of good colleges. Some institutions that typically accept scores in this range or higher include:

*   IMS, Ghaziabad
*   Jaipuria Institute of Management, Noida
*   School of Inspired Leadership (SOIL)
*   Christ University, Bangalore
*   Department of Management Studies, University of Pune (PUMBA)
*   SIESCOMS, Navi Mumbai
*   Prin. L. N. Welingkar WE School, Mumbai

It's important to note that for colleges like PUMBA, the cutoff can be very high, often ranging from 95 to 99 percentile. Also, some top colleges like JBIMS no longer accept ATMA scores and instead consider exams like CAT, MAH MBA CET, and GMAT.

Always refer to the official admission brochures of your target colleges for their specific ATMA cutoff criteria for the 2026-28 academic year.
