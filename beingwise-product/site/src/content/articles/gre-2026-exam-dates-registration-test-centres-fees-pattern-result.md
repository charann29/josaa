# GRE 2026 Exam: Dates, Registration, Test Centres, Fees, Pattern, Result

The Graduate Record Examination (GRE) is a globally recognized standardized test essential for students aspiring to pursue master's, MBA, or doctoral programs abroad, particularly in countries like the USA and Canada. Administered by Educational Testing Service (ETS), the GRE assesses your readiness for graduate-level studies through its General Test and various Subject Tests.

## Key Takeaways

*   The GRE General Test has a concise duration of 1 hour 58 minutes, making it one of the shorter standardized graduate admissions exams.
*   Indian students will pay ₹22,000 for the General Test and ₹14,500 for a Subject Test in 2026.
*   Scores for the GRE General Test are typically released within 8 to 10 days of taking the exam.
*   The GRE General Test is available both at authorized test centers and as a proctored home-based option, offering flexibility for test-takers.

## What is the GRE Exam?

The Graduate Record Examination (GRE) is a critical assessment for students seeking admission to graduate and business schools worldwide. It evaluates skills in analytical writing, verbal reasoning, and quantitative reasoning, which are fundamental for success in advanced academic programs. The test aims to provide universities with a standardized measure to compare applicants from diverse educational backgrounds.

There are two main types of GRE tests:

*   **GRE General Test:** This is the most common version, required for a wide range of master's, MBA, and PhD programs. It focuses on general academic abilities.
*   **GRE Subject Tests:** These tests measure achievement in specific fields of study, such as Mathematics, Physics, or Psychology, and are often required for specialized graduate programs.

Your GRE scores remain valid for five years, offering ample time to plan your applications.

## GRE 2026 Fees in India

The fee structure for the GRE in India for 2026 is as follows:

*   **GRE General Test:** ₹22,000
*   **GRE Subject Test:** ₹14,500

Indian students have multiple convenient payment options, including credit/debit cards and digital wallets/UPI, to complete their registration.

## GRE Exam Pattern 2026

The GRE General Test has been streamlined to a total duration of 1 hour 58 minutes. It consists of three main sections:

*   **Analytical Writing:** This section includes one essay task, designed to assess your ability to articulate complex ideas clearly and effectively.
*   **Verbal Reasoning:** Comprises 27 questions, testing your ability to analyze and evaluate written material, summarize text, and understand vocabulary in context.
*   **Quantitative Reasoning:** Also with 27 questions, this section measures your problem-solving abilities in arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and data analysis.

The test features a section-level adaptive design, meaning the difficulty of the second verbal and quantitative sections is adjusted based on your performance in the first section of each type.

## GRE Test Centres and Registration

ETS facilitates the GRE General Test across 46 cities in India, providing widespread access for test-takers. You can choose to take the exam at an official test center or opt for the at-home version, which requires a secondary camera for enhanced security and integrity.

To register for the GRE:

1.  Visit the official ETS GRE portal.
2.  Create an account or log in if you already have one.
3.  Select your preferred test (General Test or Subject Test).
4.  Choose a test date and location (test center or at-home).
5.  Complete the registration by paying the applicable fees.

It's advisable to register well in advance to secure your desired test slot.

## GRE Results

After taking the GRE General Test, your official scores are typically released within 8 to 10 days. These scores are then sent to the universities you have designated during registration or later through your ETS account. Universities use these scores as part of their comprehensive evaluation process for admissions.
