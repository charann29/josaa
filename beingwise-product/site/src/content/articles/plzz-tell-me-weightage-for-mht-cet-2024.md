# MHT CET 2024: Understanding the Weightage

For students aiming to crack the MHT CET, understanding how marks are distributed across different topics is crucial for effective preparation. This guide breaks down the official weightage scheme for the 2024 examination.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Class 11 Topics:** Approximately 20% of the MHT CET questions will be based on the Class 11 syllabus.
*   **Class 12 Topics:** A significant 80% of the questions will be drawn from the Class 12 syllabus.
*   **Maharashtra State Board Focus:** The entire MHT CET syllabus aligns with the Maharashtra State Board curriculum for both Class 11 and Class 12.
*   **Equal Subject Weightage:** Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) or Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) groups each carry specific marks, with subjects generally having balanced importance within their respective groups.

## Official Weightage Distribution

The Maharashtra CET Cell, the official body responsible for the MHT CET, has clearly outlined the weightage distribution for the exam. This structure ensures that students focus adequately on both junior college years, with a stronger emphasis on the final year.

### Class 11 Syllabus: The Foundation (20% Weightage)

While Class 11 topics make up a smaller portion of the exam, they are fundamental. Strong conceptual clarity from these topics is essential, as many Class 12 concepts build upon them. Ignoring Class 11 could lead to difficulties in understanding advanced topics, even if they carry more direct marks.

### Class 12 Syllabus: The Core (80% Weightage)

The vast majority of the MHT CET questions will come from the Class 12 syllabus. This means students must dedicate a substantial portion of their study time to mastering these subjects. In-depth knowledge, problem-solving skills, and a thorough understanding of all Class 12 chapters are paramount for securing a good score.

## Subject-wise Distribution

The MHT CET is conducted for two main groups: PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) and PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology). The official exam pattern dictates how marks are distributed across these subjects.

### For PCM Group (Engineering Aspirants)

| Subject   | Number of Questions | Marks per Question | Total Marks |
| :-------- | :------------------ | :----------------- | :---------- |
| Physics   | 50                  | 1                  | 50          |
| Chemistry | 50                  | 1                  | 50          |
| Mathematics| 50                  | 2                  | 100         |
| **Total** | **150**             | -                  | **200**     |

*Note: Physics and Chemistry questions are based on both Class 11 and 12 syllabi, with the 20:80 split. Mathematics questions also follow this split, with each question carrying 2 marks.*

### For PCB Group (Pharmacy and Agriculture Aspirants)

| Subject   | Number of Questions | Marks per Question | Total Marks |
| :-------- | :------------------ | :----------------- | :---------- |
| Physics   | 50                  | 1                  | 50          |
| Chemistry | 50                  | 1                  | 50          |
| Biology (Botany & Zoology) | 100                 | 1                  | 100         |
| **Total** | **200**             | -                  | **200**     |

*Note: Physics, Chemistry, and Biology questions are based on both Class 11 and 12 syllabi, adhering to the 20:80 split. Biology comprises 50 questions from Botany and 50 from Zoology.*

## Strategic Preparation

Understanding this weightage allows you to create a smart study plan. While the 80% weightage for Class 12 topics demands significant attention, do not neglect the fundamental concepts from Class 11. A strong foundation will make tackling the Class 12 syllabus much easier and more effective. Focus on problem-solving, regular revision, and practicing previous year's questions to solidify your understanding of high-weightage topics.
