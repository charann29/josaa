# LLB: Course, Full Form, Duration, Admission 2026, Fees, Subjects, Salary & Career Scope

For many aspiring legal professionals in India, the Bachelor of Legislative Laws (LLB) is the foundational step. This undergraduate degree equips students with essential legal knowledge and the skills needed to practice law.

## Key Takeaways

*   The full form of LLB is Bachelor of Legislative Laws, and it's an undergraduate degree.
*   You can pursue a 3-year LLB after graduation or a 5-year integrated LLB directly after Class 12.
*   Admission typically involves entrance exams like CLAT, MH CET Law, or others, alongside merit.
*   Career paths are diverse, ranging from litigation and corporate law to legal advising and further academic pursuits.

## What is LLB?

The LLB is India's primary professional qualification for those looking to enter the legal field. It's designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of legal principles, research methodologies, and the ethical responsibilities of a lawyer. The curriculum blends theoretical knowledge with practical applications, often including case studies and moot court exercises, to prepare students for real-world legal scenarios.

## LLB Course Duration and Eligibility

There are two main pathways to obtaining an LLB degree:

*   **3-Year LLB:** This program is for graduates who have already completed a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university. As the name suggests, it spans three years.
*   **5-Year Integrated LLB:** For students who have completed their Class 12, this option combines a bachelor's degree (like BA, B.Sc., B.Com, or BBA) with an LLB. This integrated course typically takes five years to complete.

## Admission Process and Key Entrance Exams 2026

Admission to LLB programs, whether 3-year or 5-year, is generally based on a combination of academic merit and performance in national or state-level entrance examinations. Some important entrance exams for 2026 admissions include:

*   **CLAT (Common Law Admission Test):** A major national-level exam for admission to undergraduate (5-year integrated LLB) and postgraduate law programs at numerous National Law Universities (NLUs). The CLAT 2026 results have been announced for the integrated LLB and LLM courses.
*   **MH CET Law:** Tentatively scheduled for May 8, 2026, this exam is crucial for admissions to law colleges in Maharashtra for both 3-year and 5-year integrated LLB courses.
*   **TG LAWCET:** For admissions in Telangana, the registration deadline for TG LAWCET 2026 (without late fee) was extended until April 10, 2026. The exam for both 3-year and 5-year integrated LLB courses is set for May 18, 2026.
*   **AP LAWCET:** The Andhra Pradesh LAWCET 2026 LLB exam is scheduled for May 4, 2026.

Prospective students should always refer to the official websites of the respective exam conducting bodies for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding application deadlines, exam dates, and eligibility criteria.

## LLB Course Fees and Top Colleges

Course fees for LLB programs can vary significantly depending on the type of institution (government vs. private) and its location. Generally, fees for government colleges can range from approximately INR 1,080 to INR 8.8 Lakh, while private colleges may charge between INR 4,090 and INR 18.75 Lakh.

According to various official rankings and reputations, some of the well-regarded LLB colleges in India include:

*   National Law School of India University (NLSIU), Bengaluru
*   Symbiosis Law School (SLS), Pune
*   Gujarat National Law University (GNLU), Gandhinagar
*   SOA University, Bhubaneswar
*   Dr. B.R. Ambedkar College of Law, Visakhapatnam

Students are encouraged to check the latest NIRF rankings and individual college admission brochures for detailed fee structures and other relevant information.

## Career Scope and Salary after LLB

An LLB degree opens doors to a wide array of career opportunities in the legal sector and beyond. Graduates can choose from various specializations and roles:

*   **Litigation:** Practicing as an advocate in courts, representing clients in civil or criminal cases.
*   **Corporate Law:** Working with companies on legal matters such as contracts, mergers, intellectual property, and compliance.
*   **Legal Advisor/Consultant:** Providing legal guidance to individuals, organizations, or government bodies.
*   **Judiciary:** Aspiring to become a judge through judicial service examinations.
*   **Academia:** Pursuing higher education (like LLM or PhD) and entering legal teaching or research.
*   **Public Prosecutor:** Representing the state in criminal proceedings.
*   **Legal Journalism:** Reporting on legal news and developments.

Salaries for LLB graduates vary widely based on the specific role, employer, experience, and location. Entry-level positions might offer a modest starting salary, but with experience and specialization, earnings can grow substantially. Many graduates also opt to pursue further studies like an LLM (Master of Laws), an MBA, or various diploma and certificate programs to enhance their expertise and career prospects.

An LLB is a robust foundation for a fulfilling career in the dynamic world of law, offering continuous learning and significant impact.
