# List of University Entrance Exam: Dates, Results, Cut-Offs, Merit List, Colleges and Universities

Navigating higher education in India often begins with entrance examinations. These tests are crucial gateways for securing admission to a wide array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across the country.

## Key takeaways

*   **Mandatory for Top Institutions:** Most leading central, state, and private universities in India require applicants to clear specific entrance exams for admission.
*   **Diverse Program Offerings:** These exams cover a broad spectrum of fields, from arts and sciences to engineering and agriculture, at both UG and PG levels.
*   **Beyond Just Scores:** While exam results are paramount, admission also considers factors like qualifying exam merit, seat availability, and reservation policies.
*   **Official Authorities:** Bodies like the National Testing Agency (NTA) and various IITs are key organizers of these national-level assessments.

## Common University Entrance Test (CUET) - UG & PG

The Common University Entrance Test (CUET) has become a pivotal examination for aspiring students aiming for undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) degrees in India. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), CUET streamlines admissions to numerous central, state, and even some private universities.

### CUET UG

For undergraduate programs such as BA, B.Sc, B.Com, and BCA, the CUET UG is essential. It opens doors to prestigious central universities like Delhi University, Jawaharlal Nehru University, Banaras Hindu University, Jamia Millia Islamia, and the University of Hyderabad. After results are declared, participating universities conduct their own counseling processes.

### CUET PG

Similarly, the CUET PG facilitates admissions to a vast range of postgraduate programs across approximately 203 universities nationwide. Candidates must meet the specific eligibility criteria for each program and university to qualify for this entrance test.

## IIT JAM (Joint Admission Test for M.Sc.)

IIT JAM is a national-level examination primarily for admission to postgraduate science programs at the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and integrated PhD programs at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Bangalore. This exam is annually conducted by one of the IITs on a rotational basis.

It offers pathways to various programs including M.Sc. (Two Years), Joint M.Sc.-Ph.D., M.Sc.-Ph.D. Dual Degree, and M.Sc.-M.Tech. With over 3000 seats available across more than 90 postgraduate programs at 22 IITs, IIT JAM is a significant opportunity for science graduates.

## Specialized Entrance Exams

Beyond the broad CUET, several specialized entrance exams cater to specific fields and institutions.

### ICAR AIEEA PG

The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) All India Entrance Examination for Admission (AIEEA) for PG courses is also conducted by the NTA. This exam is the gateway for admissions to postgraduate and research programs across 75 agricultural universities in India. Successful candidates are eligible for the admission process based on their scores.

### NEST (National Entrance Screening Test)

NEST is specifically designed for admission to the integrated M.Sc. programs at the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER) Bhubaneswar and the UM-DAE Centre for Excellence in Basic Sciences (CEBS) Mumbai. Applicants must have passed Class 12 from a recognized board with at least 60% aggregate marks (55% for SC, ST, and Divyangjan candidates) to be eligible for NEST.

### IPU CET (Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University Common Entrance Test)

GGSIPU conducts the IPU CET for admission to its diverse range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. This university-level exam assesses candidates for various courses offered by Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University and its affiliated colleges.
