# Intro to Descriptive Statistics by Udacity: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For anyone starting their journey into data analysis, understanding descriptive statistics is a fundamental step. This online program from Udacity offers a comprehensive, beginner-friendly introduction to these core concepts, all at no cost.

## Key takeaways

*   This is a foundational, beginner-level course designed for those new to statistics.
*   The program is entirely online, self-paced, and can be completed in about two months.
*   It is completely free to enroll and access all course materials.
*   While the course is free, there is no official certificate provided upon completion.

## Course Overview and Learning Experience

This introductory course is delivered entirely online through Udacity's platform, offering a flexible, self-paced learning experience. You can access the study materials, which combine video and text, at any time that suits your schedule. The curriculum is structured into seven chapters, and most students can complete the entire program within approximately two months. There are no strict deadlines or attendance requirements, making it convenient for learners to progress at their own pace.

## What You Will Learn

Upon successfully completing the program, you will gain a solid grasp of various statistical concepts and skills, including:

*   Understanding different statistical research methods and knowing when to apply each.
*   Calculating and interpreting key measures of central tendency like mean, median, and mode.
*   Quantifying data spread using measures such as range, standard deviation, and interquartile range to identify outliers.
*   Creating and interpreting visual representations of data, including histograms, bar graphs, and box plots.
*   Learning about basic probability and the properties of different data distributions.
*   Using the Z-score method to standardize distributions and make data predictions.

## Eligibility Criteria

This course is designed for beginners. The primary prerequisite is a basic understanding of mathematics, specifically foundational concepts in Algebra and Arithmetic. No prior experience in statistics or advanced mathematical knowledge is required.

## Course Structure and Syllabus

The program is divided into logical lessons, building your understanding step-by-step:

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Research Methods

*   Explore various statistical study methods.
*   Understand the advantages and disadvantages of each method.

### Lesson 2: Visualizing Data

*   Learn to create and interpret common data visualizations like histograms, bar charts, and frequency plots.
*   Discover how to effectively display your data.

### Lesson 3: Central Tendency

*   Compute and interpret the three key measures of the center of a distribution: mean, median, and mode.

### Lesson 4: Variability

*   Identify outliers in data sets using the interquartile range concept.
*   Quantify the spread of data using the range and standard deviation.

### Lesson 5: Standardizing

*   Compute proportions using standardized distributions.
*   Convert distributions into the standard normal distribution using the Z-score.

### Lesson 6: Normal Distribution

*   Learn to use the Z-table to find proportions of observations.

## How to Apply

Enrolling in the Intro to Descriptive Statistics course is straightforward:

1.  Visit the official Udacity website.
2.  Look for and select the "Start Free Course" option.
3.  You will then be prompted to sign up for a new Udacity account or sign in if you already have one.
4.  Once signed in, you will gain immediate access to the course materials and can begin your learning journey.

This free program is an excellent starting point for students, job seekers, or anyone looking to build a foundational understanding of descriptive statistics and its practical applications.
