# UPCAT Counselling 2022: Dates, Application Form, Eligibility, Seat Allotment

The Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test (UPCET), previously known as UPSEE, served as a key entrance exam for various undergraduate and postgraduate professional courses in Uttar Pradesh. For the 2022 academic year, the structure of admissions for these programs saw a significant change.

## Key takeaways

*   The UPCET exam itself was discontinued for 2022 admissions.
*   Admissions to courses previously covered by UPCET are now primarily based on national-level exams like CUET, JEE Main, and NATA.
*   Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU) and other state universities in Uttar Pradesh now use scores from these national exams.
*   Counselling processes are managed by the respective universities or state authorities, aligning with the new admission criteria.

## Evolution of UPCET Admissions for 2022

For the 2022 academic session, the National Testing Agency (NTA) no longer conducted the UPCET exam. Instead, admissions to courses offered by institutions affiliated with AKTU, Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT), and Harcourt Butler Technical University (HBTU) in Uttar Pradesh transitioned to accepting scores from other national entrance examinations.

### Which exams replaced UPCET for 2022?

*   **Undergraduate Courses:** For programs such as B.Tech (including lateral entry), B.Pharm (including lateral entry), B.Des, BFAD/BFA, and BHMCT, admissions were primarily based on scores from **JEE Main**, **CUET (Common University Entrance Test)**, and **NATA (National Aptitude Test in Architecture)**, depending on the specific course.
*   **Postgraduate Courses:** For programs like MBA (including integrated), MCA (including integrated and lateral entry), M.Tech, M.Pharm, M.Des, M.Arch, and MURP, admissions were largely through **CUET PG** (for postgraduate programs) or **CUET** (for integrated postgraduate programs).

## Eligibility Criteria for 2022 Admissions

While the UPCET exam was scrapped, the general eligibility principles for admission to Uttar Pradesh universities remained relevant, adapted to the new entrance exam requirements. Typically, candidates needed to:

*   Have passed the qualifying examination from an institution located in Uttar Pradesh, or their parents must hold a domicile certificate of Uttar Pradesh.
*   There was generally no age limit specified for most programs.
*   Candidates in their final year of the qualifying examination or awaiting results were usually eligible to apply, provided they met the specific course requirements upon admission.

Specific eligibility details, including minimum marks and subject combinations, were determined by the respective national entrance exams (like JEE Main, CUET) and the individual universities offering the courses.

## Application and Counselling Process

With the discontinuation of UPCET, the application and counselling processes for 2022 also changed. Candidates were required to:

1.  **Apply for the relevant national entrance exam:** This included registering for JEE Main, CUET, NATA, or CUET PG, as per the desired course and university.
2.  **Appear for the entrance exam:** Candidates took the computer-based test as scheduled by NTA or other conducting bodies.
3.  **Participate in university/state-level counselling:** After results were declared for the national exams, individual universities or the state's counselling authority (often managed by AKTU for its affiliated colleges) conducted online counselling based on the merit lists generated from these national exam scores. This usually involved registration, choice filling, seat allotment, and document verification.

Key dates for counselling, application forms, and seat allotment were announced by the respective counselling authorities or universities on their official portals. Candidates were advised to regularly check the official websites of AKTU, MMMUT, HBTU, and other relevant state universities for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding the 2022 admission cycles.

Navigating the admissions landscape for 2022 required candidates to be aware of the shift from UPCET to national-level exams and to follow the specific guidelines of the universities they wished to join.
