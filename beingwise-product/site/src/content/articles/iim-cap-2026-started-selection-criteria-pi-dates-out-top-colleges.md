# IIM CAP 2026 (Started) - Selection Criteria, PI Dates (OUT), Top Colleges & Cutoff

The Common Admission Process (CAP) is a unified selection procedure for MBA admissions to several Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs). This process simplifies applications for candidates targeting these newer IIMs, basing selection primarily on CAT scores and subsequent interview rounds.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIM CAP 2026 streamlines the admission process for three specific IIMs: IIM Bodhgaya, IIM Jammu, and IIM Sirmaur.
*   IIM Bodhgaya is serving as the coordinating institute for CAP 2026, as it did for the 2025 admissions cycle.
*   The process involves shortlisting based on CAT scores, followed by a Personal Interview (PI) round, with Written Ability Test (WAT) no longer a part of the process.
*   Registration for the Expression of Interest (EOI) for CAP 2026 has already begun.

## What is IIM CAP?

IIM CAP, or the Common Admission Process, is a single-window mechanism designed for MBA admissions to a group of newer IIMs. Instead of applying and undergoing separate selection processes for each institute, candidates participate in one centralized process. One of the participating IIMs takes on the responsibility of conducting this entire selection process on a rotational basis each year. For the 2026 admissions cycle, IIM Bodhgaya is the coordinating institute, managing the process for all participating campuses.

It's important to note that the number of IIMs participating in CAP has changed. While previously more institutes were involved, for IIM CAP 2026, only IIM Bodhgaya, IIM Jammu, and IIM Sirmaur are participating. Other IIMs like IIM Trichy, IIM Ranchi, IIM Raipur, and IIM Kashipur are conducting their admissions through a different common process (IIM JAP), and IIM Udaipur is managing its admissions independently.

## IIM CAP: Qualifying CAT Cutoffs

The initial shortlisting for the IIM CAP process is based on a candidate's CAT exam score. The qualifying CAT cutoffs for 2026 admissions have been announced and are consistent with the previous year's requirements. These cutoffs are minimum percentile scores required across different categories to be considered for the subsequent stages of the admission process. It's crucial for applicants to meet these minimum criteria to proceed. Specific percentile requirements for various categories are detailed on the official CAP website.

## IIM CAP Selection Criteria and Process

The IIM CAP admission process involves several stages designed to evaluate a candidate's overall profile. The primary steps are:

1.  **CAT Score-based Shortlisting:** Candidates are initially shortlisted based on their CAT 2025 scores, meeting the specified minimum sectional and overall cutoffs.
2.  **Expression of Interest (EOI) Submission:** Shortlisted candidates are invited to submit an Expression of Interest through the official CAP portal, indicating their desire to participate in the selection process.
3.  **Personal Interview (PI) Round:** Shortlisted candidates will participate in a Personal Interview. Unlike previous years, a Written Ability Test (WAT) is not part of the CAP 2026 process.
4.  **Final Merit List Preparation:** The final selection is based on a comprehensive evaluation of various parameters. These typically include the CAT score, academic record (Class 10, 12, and graduation scores), performance in the Personal Interview, and consideration of work experience, if any. Weightage is assigned to each of these components to arrive at a final composite score.

Each participating IIM will then use this common merit list to make their final admission offers, applying their individual institutional criteria and seat availability.

## Final Merit List: Parameters and Weightage

The final merit list for IIM CAP admissions is determined by assigning specific weightage to different components of a candidate's profile. While the exact weightages can vary slightly each year and are officially communicated by the coordinating IIM, the typical parameters considered for the final selection include:

*   **CAT Score:** A significant portion of the weightage is allocated to the CAT percentile.
*   **Personal Interview (PI) Performance:** The PI round assesses communication skills, leadership potential, general awareness, and suitability for an MBA program.
*   **Academic Record:** Scores from Class 10, Class 12, and undergraduate degree are evaluated.
*   **Work Experience:** Relevant work experience is given weightage, often increasing with the duration and quality of experience.
*   **Academic and Gender Diversity:** IIMs often consider academic background and gender to promote diversity within their cohorts.

The precise weightage for each component is published by the coordinating IIM on the official CAP portal once the process is underway.

For any further details or specific queries, candidates are advised to refer to the official IIM CAP portal, which is managed by the coordinating institute, IIM Bodhgaya, for the 2026 admissions cycle.
