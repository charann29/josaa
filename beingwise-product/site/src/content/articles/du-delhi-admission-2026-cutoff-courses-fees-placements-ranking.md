# DU Delhi: Admission 2026, Cutoff, Courses, Fees, Placements, Ranking

Delhi University (DU) is a top choice for many students across India, offering a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs. Securing a spot at DU for the 2026 academic year involves a clear process, primarily through national entrance exams and a centralized allocation system.

## Key takeaways

*   Admission to most undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) programs at DU is based on scores from the Common University Entrance Test (CUET).
*   The Common Seat Allocation System (CSAS) portal is central to the application and counseling process for DU admissions.
*   Key dates for CUET UG 2026 are in May, while CUET PG 2026 exams are scheduled for March.
*   PhD admissions involve an entrance test followed by a personal interview.

## How to get into DU: The Admission Process

For most UG and PG courses at Delhi University, the path to admission begins with the Common University Entrance Test (CUET). Aspiring students must perform well in CUET UG for undergraduate courses or CUET PG for postgraduate programs. After qualifying, candidates need to register and apply through the official DU Common Seat Allocation System (CSAS) portal. This portal is where you'll submit your preferences for colleges and courses, and where the university will release selection lists.

### Accepted Entrance Exams for DU Admissions 2026

| Course          | Entrance Exam |
| :-------------- | :------------ |
| UG Programmes   | CUET UG       |
| PG Programmes   | CUET PG       |
| PhD Programmes  | DU Entrance Test & Interview |

For PhD programs, candidates are first shortlisted based on an entrance examination, followed by a personal interview. It's important to note that for PG admissions, 50% of the seats are allocated based on merit from the qualifying examination, while the remaining 50% are filled through the CUET PG entrance test ranks.

## Applying to Delhi University: The CSAS Process

Once CUET results are out, the next step is to register on the official Common Seat Allocation System (CSAS) portal for Delhi University. This online portal will be activated by DU, allowing candidates to submit their counselling registration forms. You'll need to create an account using your CUET UG/PG login details, and then accurately fill in your personal and academic information.

### Steps for CSAS Registration

1.  **Visit the official DU admission portal:** Look for the link to the CSAS 2026 registration.
2.  **Sign up/Login:** Use your CUET credentials to access the registration form.
3.  **Enter details:** Provide all required personal and academic information carefully.
4.  **Course and college preferences:** Select your desired courses and colleges in order of preference.
5.  **Upload documents:** Ensure all necessary documents are uploaded as specified.
6.  **Pay the registration fee:** Complete the application by paying the non-refundable registration fee.

Always ensure you meet the specific eligibility criteria for your chosen program before applying.

## Fees, Placements, and Ranking

Delhi University is known for its relatively affordable fees compared to many private institutions, making quality education accessible. While specific fee structures vary by course and college, they generally remain student-friendly. DU colleges consistently rank well in national frameworks like NIRF rankings, reflecting their academic excellence and research contributions.

Placement opportunities are robust, with many colleges having dedicated placement cells that attract top recruiters from various sectors. The university's strong alumni network also plays a significant role in career development for its graduates. Specific placement statistics and average salary packages are usually available on individual college websites.
