# To Determine Resistance Of A Galvanometer By Half-deflection Method And To Find Its Figure Of Merit.

Understanding how to measure the internal resistance of a galvanometer using the half-deflection method is a foundational experiment in physics. This technique allows us to accurately determine a galvanometer's internal resistance and calculate its 'figure of merit', which essentially tells us how sensitive the device is. These principles are crucial for anyone working with precise electrical measurement tools, from designing medical diagnostic equipment to advanced scientific research.

## Key Takeaways

*   The half-deflection method is a standard technique for finding a galvanometer's internal resistance.
*   This method involves carefully adjusting resistances to reduce the galvanometer's deflection to exactly half its initial value.
*   The 'figure of merit' quantifies the sensitivity of the galvanometer, indicating the current required for a unit deflection.
*   Accurate measurements from this experiment are vital for calibrating sensitive electrical instruments in various scientific and engineering applications.

## Aim of the Experiment

Our primary goal in this experiment is twofold: first, to precisely determine the internal resistance of a given galvanometer using the half-deflection method; and second, to calculate its figure of merit.

## Equipment Needed

To conduct this experiment successfully, you will need the following apparatus:

*   A Weston-type galvanometer
*   A battery or a battery eliminator (power source)
*   Two resistance boxes (one with a high range, e.g., 10,000 Ω, and another with a lower range, e.g., 200 Ω)
*   Two one-way keys (switches)
*   A rheostat (variable resistor)
*   Connecting wires
*   A piece of sandpaper (for cleaning connections)
*   A voltmeter (to measure EMF)
*   An ammeter (of appropriate range)
*   A meter scale and a screw gauge (for general measurements, though less central to this specific experiment's core calculations).

## Theoretical Background

The theoretical framework for this experiment relies on two key formulas:

1.  **Galvanometer Resistance (G) by Half-Deflection Method:**
    
    $$G = \frac{R \cdot S}{R - S}$$
    
    Here, R represents the resistance connected in series with the galvanometer, and S is the shunt resistance (connected in parallel).

2.  **Figure of Merit (k):**
    
    $$k = \frac{E}{(R + G) \theta}$$
    
    In this equation, E is the electromotive force (EMF) of the cell or battery, and θ (theta) is the deflection observed in the galvanometer when resistance R is in series.

3.  **Maximum Current Through Galvanometer (i_g):**
    
    $$i_g = n \cdot k$$
    
    Where 'n' is the total number of divisions on the galvanometer scale from the zero mark to one extreme.

## Experimental Procedure

Follow these steps carefully to perform the experiment:

### Part (a): Determining Galvanometer Resistance by Half-Deflection

1.  **Set up the Circuit:** Assemble all components according to the standard circuit diagram for the half-deflection method. Ensure all connections are secure and clean (use sandpaper if necessary).
2.  **Initial High Resistance:** Insert only key K1. From the high-resistance box (R), select a substantial resistance value (e.g., 2000 Ω).
3.  **Maximum Deflection:** Adjust the value of R until the galvanometer shows a steady, even, and maximum deflection that stays within the scale. Record this deflection as θ.
4.  **Half Deflection:** Now, insert the second key (K2) as well. Without changing the value of R, adjust the shunt resistance (S) from the second resistance box until the galvanometer's deflection is exactly half of the initial value (θ/2). Record the value of S.
5.  **Repeat Measurements:** Repeat steps 3-4 at least three more times, using different initial values for R each time, and adjusting S accordingly to achieve half-deflection.

### Part (b): Finding the Figure of Merit

1.  **Measure EMF:** Measure the EMF (E) of your battery or battery eliminator using a voltmeter. Connect the positive terminal of the voltmeter to the positive terminal of the power source and the negative to the negative.
2.  **Connect for Deflection:** Set up the circuit as per the diagram for measuring figure of merit.
3.  **Observe Deflection:** Adjust the series resistance (R) to obtain a convenient deflection (e.g., 30 divisions) on the galvanometer scale. Record both the resistance R and the deflection θ.
4.  **Vary Resistance:** Change the value of R and note the corresponding galvanometer deflection again.
5.  **Multiple Readings:** Repeat steps 3-4 with different voltage settings if using a battery eliminator (e.g., 2V, 4V, 6V, 8V) to get a range of readings.
6.  **Calculate Figure of Merit:** Use the collected data and the formula for 'k' from the theory section to calculate the figure of merit for each set of readings.

## Calculations and Results

After completing the experimental procedure, use the recorded values of R, S, E, and θ to perform the necessary calculations for G and k. Present your calculated values clearly, perhaps in a table format for different trials, and state your final determined resistance of the galvanometer and its figure of merit.

## Important Precautions

To ensure accurate and reliable results, keep the following precautions in mind:

*   Ensure all electrical connections are clean and tight to minimize contact resistance.
*   Use a suitable range for the resistance boxes to avoid damaging the galvanometer.
*   Adjust the rheostat smoothly and carefully.
*   Take multiple readings and calculate the average to reduce experimental error.
*   Make sure the galvanometer is level and free from external magnetic disturbances.

By following these guidelines, you can conduct a successful experiment to understand the fundamental properties of a galvanometer.
