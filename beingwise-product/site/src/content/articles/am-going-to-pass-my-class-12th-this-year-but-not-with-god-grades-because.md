# I am going to pass my class 12th this year but not with good grades because I didn't study well, so am going to repeat my class 12th as a private candidate. I've applied for JEE and BITSAT. Next year when I'll appear for JEE I'll be considered as a dropper but my main concern is that will I be considered as a dropper for BITSAT also? Or I can appear for BITSAT in 2026 and 2027 or I've only 2026 attempt only.

It's a thoughtful decision to repeat Class 12 to build a stronger foundation for your future studies. Many students choose this path to improve their scores and enhance their chances for competitive exams like JEE and BITSAT.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your official Class 12 passing year will be the year you successfully complete it as a private candidate.
*   BITSAT eligibility is limited to the year you pass Class 12 and the very next year.
*   You will have two BITSAT attempts based on your *new* Class 12 passing year.
*   BITSAT does not formally categorize candidates as 'droppers' in its eligibility criteria.

## Understanding BITSAT Eligibility

The Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test (BITSAT) has specific rules regarding when a candidate can appear. According to the official BITSAT eligibility criteria, you are eligible to take the exam only in the year you pass your Class 12 examination and in the subsequent year. This is a crucial point to remember as it differs from the JEE Main attempt policy.

## Your Specific Situation

Let's break down your scenario. If you are currently in Class 12 and plan to repeat it as a private candidate next year, your official Class 12 passing year will be the year you successfully clear it privately. For example, if you would have passed Class 12 in 2025 originally but decide to repeat and pass it in 2026 as a private candidate, then 2026 becomes your official Class 12 passing year.

Based on this, your BITSAT eligibility window would be:

*   **BITSAT in 2026:** Eligible (as it's your Class 12 passing year).
*   **BITSAT in 2027:** Eligible (as it's the year immediately following your Class 12 passing year).
*   **BITSAT in 2028:** Not eligible (as this falls outside the specified two-year window).

## 'Dropper' Status for BITSAT

Unlike JEE Main, where candidates are often referred to as 'droppers' if they take a gap year after Class 12, BITSAT's eligibility criteria do not use this term. For BITSAT, your eligibility is strictly tied to your Class 12 passing year. When you appear for BITSAT in 2026 or 2027 (based on a 2026 Class 12 pass), you will be treated as any other eligible candidate. Your previous attempt at Class 12 (if any) or the gap year will not impact your BITSAT eligibility, as long as your final Class 12 passing year falls within the permissible window.

## JEE Main Attempts

It's worth noting that the National Testing Agency (NTA), which conducts JEE Main, has different rules. As per NTA guidelines, a candidate can appear for JEE Main in the year they pass Class 12 and for the next two consecutive years. This means you generally get three consecutive years of attempts for JEE Main, including your Class 12 passing year. So, while you might be considered a 'dropper' for JEE Main, this term and its implications are distinct from BITSAT's eligibility framework.

Planning your academic journey carefully, especially when repeating a year, can significantly boost your prospects. Make sure to stay updated with official announcements from BITS Pilani and NTA for any changes in eligibility criteria.
