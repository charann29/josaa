# CAT Exam Pattern 2026: Sectional Time Limit, Marking Scheme & Total Marks

Understanding the structure of the Common Admission Test (CAT) is a crucial first step for any aspiring MBA student. This guide will walk you through the key aspects of the CAT 2026 exam pattern, including its sections, time limits, and how scoring works, to help you prepare effectively.

## Key Takeaways

*   The CAT 2026 exam will consist of three distinct sections: Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), and Quantitative Aptitude (QA).
*   The total duration of the exam is 120 minutes, with a strict sectional time limit of 40 minutes for each section.
*   Questions are primarily Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), but some TITA (Type In The Answer) questions will also be present, particularly in DILR and QA.
*   There is negative marking for incorrect MCQ responses, so careful answering is essential.

## Overview of the CAT 2026 Exam Structure

The CAT is a computer-based test designed to assess a candidate's aptitude across various skills vital for management studies. The official exam notification, typically released in the first week of August, will provide the most precise details regarding the CAT 2026 pattern. Historically, the exam is conducted in late November.

The test is divided into three sections, and candidates must complete each section within its allocated time before moving to the next. This structure demands strategic time management and a clear understanding of your strengths and weaknesses.

## Sectional Time Limits

Each of the three sections in the CAT 2026 exam is allotted a specific time, and candidates cannot switch between sections until the time for the current section expires. This fixed sectional time limit is critical to note for your preparation strategy.

| CAT Section                           | Allotted Time |
| :------------------------------------ | :------------ |
| Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension | 40 Minutes    |
| Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning | 40 Minutes    |
| Quantitative Aptitude                 | 40 Minutes    |
| **Total Exam Duration**               | **120 Minutes** |

## CAT 2026 Marking Scheme

The CAT employs a specific marking scheme that includes negative marking for incorrect answers in MCQs. It's vital to be aware of this to avoid losing marks unnecessarily.

*   **For every correct MCQ answer:** You will be awarded 3 marks.
*   **For every incorrect MCQ answer:** 1 mark will be deducted.
*   **For TITA questions:** There is no negative marking for incorrect responses. This means you can attempt TITA questions with a bit more confidence, even if you are not entirely sure, without the fear of losing marks.
*   **Unattempted questions:** No marks are awarded or deducted.

This scheme highlights the importance of accuracy, particularly for MCQs. While attempting a good number of questions is beneficial, ensuring those attempts are correct is equally, if not more, important to achieve a strong overall score.

## How to Mark Answers in the Test

The CAT is a computer-based test, and candidates use a mouse to select options for MCQs and a keyboard to type answers for TITA questions. The interface typically provides three main options for managing your answers:

*   **Save & Next:** This confirms your selected answer and moves you to the next question. Once you use this, your answer is saved, and you cannot change it later.
*   **Clear Response:** If you've selected an option but wish to change your mind before clicking 'Save & Next', you can use this to deselect your choice.
*   **Mark for Review & Next:** This option allows you to flag a question you're unsure about but might want to revisit later within the same section's time limit. While a question marked for review will be considered for evaluation if an answer is selected, it's always best to return and confirm your final choice by clicking 'Save & Next'. If you don't 'Save & Next' for a question you marked for review with an answer selected, it will be treated as answered. If you mark for review without selecting an answer, it will be treated as unattempted.

It's crucial to ensure that you finalize all your answers by clicking 'Save & Next' before the 40-minute sectional timer runs out. Any answer not officially saved will not be considered for scoring.

Thorough preparation, combined with a solid understanding of the CAT 2026 exam pattern, will significantly boost your chances of success.
