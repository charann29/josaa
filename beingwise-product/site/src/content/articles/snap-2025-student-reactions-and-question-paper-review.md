# SNAP 2025 Student Reactions and Question Paper Review

The SNAP 2025 examination, a crucial step for many aspiring MBA students, was conducted today across various centers. Initial feedback from test-takers suggests a generally accessible paper, particularly for Test 1.

## Key takeaways

*   The SNAP 2025 Test 1 was largely considered easy by a majority of students who appeared for the exam.
*   All three sections – Quantitative Ability, Logical Reasoning, and General English – were perceived to be on the easier side.
*   This year's paper, especially Test 1, appeared simpler compared to the previous year's SNAP examination.
*   The overall ease of the paper might lead to higher cut-off scores for admission to Symbiosis International (Deemed University) programs.

## Student Feedback on Test 1

Students exiting the examination centers shared their immediate impressions of the SNAP 2025 Test 1. Many expressed relief and confidence, citing the straightforward nature of the questions.

Yash Aggrawal, an engineering graduate, noted that all sections – Quantitative Ability, Logical Reasoning, and General English – felt easy. He specifically mentioned finding the Quantitative section very manageable, attempting 19 out of 20 questions with confidence, and highlighted that this year's paper was significantly easier than the previous year's.

Riya Jha emphasized the importance of mock test practice. She found the Logical Reasoning and English sections particularly easy. While she didn't find the Quantitative section difficult, she did observe that some topics she had prepared for were not heavily featured in the paper.

Satyarthi described the overall exam as being on the easier side. He pointed out that while vocabulary questions were somewhat tricky, the Logical Reasoning section was straightforward. In Quantitative Ability, he found about 15-16 questions very easy. Specific question types he recalled from Logical Reasoning included two on syllogisms, three on arrangements, one or two on coding-decoding, one on calendars, and two on clocks.

## Section-wise Difficulty Breakdown

The general consensus from test-takers indicates a consistent level of ease across all three sections of SNAP 2025 Test 1.

### Quantitative Ability, Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency

This section was widely regarded as easy, especially by candidates with a strong numerical background. Many reported being able to attempt a high number of questions. Topics like arithmetic and basic algebra were reportedly well-represented and approachable.

### General English

The English section, encompassing Reading Comprehension, Verbal Reasoning, and Verbal Ability, was also perceived as easy. While some found vocabulary questions to be a bit challenging, the overall structure and question types were generally straightforward.

### Analytical & Logical Reasoning

This section was consistently rated as easy. Students found the questions to be direct and solvable with proper practice. Common reasoning topics such as syllogisms, arrangements, coding-decoding, calendars, and clocks were present and considered accessible.

## Expected Cut-offs

Given the widespread feedback that SNAP 2025 Test 1 was easier than previous iterations, it is highly probable that the cut-off scores for admission to various programs at Symbiosis institutes will be higher this year. Candidates should monitor official announcements for the exact cut-off percentiles once results are declared.

As candidates await their results, the initial sentiment from the SNAP 2025 Test 1 indicates a potentially competitive admission cycle due to the paper's perceived ease.
