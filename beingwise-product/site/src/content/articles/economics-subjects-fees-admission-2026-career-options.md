# What is Economics?: Subjects, Fees, Admission 2026, Career Options

Economics is a social science that explores how individuals, governments, businesses, and nations make choices about allocating limited resources to satisfy unlimited wants and needs. It's fundamentally about understanding decision-making in the face of scarcity.

## Key Takeaways

*   Economics is offered at various levels, including undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG), PhD, diploma, and certificate programs.
*   Admission to economics courses can be based on entrance exams or academic merit, with eligibility typically requiring a Class 12 background in economics for UG and a relevant bachelor's degree for PG studies.
*   Graduates in economics can pursue diverse careers in finance, research, government, and business, with potential roles like economists, financial analysts, and market researchers.
*   Course fees vary significantly, ranging from approximately INR 1,000 to INR 19.55 Lakh in government institutions and up to INR 39.6 Lakh in private colleges.

## Understanding Economics as a Discipline

Within economics, you'll encounter numerous specialized fields. These include microeconomics, which focuses on individual and firm decisions; macroeconomics, dealing with national and global economies; econometrics, involving statistical methods; and public economics, examining government's role in the economy. Core concepts often include economic principles, international economics, and behavioral economics.

## Course Levels and Duration

Economics programs are available across different educational stages. Undergraduate degrees typically span three to four years, while postgraduate programs are usually two years long. Doctoral (PhD) studies generally require three to five years. Diploma and certificate courses offer shorter, focused learning opportunities.

## Eligibility and Admission Process

To pursue an undergraduate degree in economics, candidates generally need to have completed Class 12 with economics as a subject from a recognized board. For postgraduate programs, a relevant bachelor's degree is usually required, and for PhD studies, a master's degree in a related field is typically a prerequisite.

Admission to these courses often involves entrance examinations. For UG programs, the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) is a significant exam. For PG admissions, exams like CUET-PG, CPGET, and AP PGCET are common. Those aspiring for research or teaching roles might take exams such as UGC NET or CSIR NET, as highlighted by NTA. Admission can also be merit-based, considering academic performance.

## Fees and Top Institutions

The cost of an economics education varies widely across institutions. According to official college data, fees in government colleges can range from a few thousand rupees to nearly INR 19.55 Lakh for the entire course. In private institutions, these fees can go up to approximately INR 39.6 Lakh. Some of the well-regarded institutions for economics programs include colleges under Delhi University, St. Xavier's Mumbai, Loyola College, K J Somaiya College of Arts and Commerce, and Madras Christian College, often recognized in NIRF rankings.

## Career Opportunities and Salaries

An economics degree opens doors to a variety of career paths. Graduates can work as economists, financial analysts, market researchers, actuaries, or economic writers. Opportunities also exist in government sectors, such as with NITI Aayog, the Directorate of Economics & Statistics, the Ministry of Finance, and the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). Private sector recruiters include major firms like Deloitte, Mastercard, PwC, Accenture, and WNS Holdings. Entry-level salaries for economists can be substantial, with experienced professionals potentially earning significantly more.

An education in economics equips you with critical analytical skills applicable across numerous industries and sectors, preparing you for a dynamic professional journey.
