# Sir I got 76 %ile in JEE Main From OBC-NCL category, can I eligible to get admission in PICT Pune?

It's natural to wonder about your chances of admission to a respected institution like PICT Pune with your JEE Main percentile. Let's break down what your 76%ile in the OBC-NCL category might mean for your prospects.

## Key takeaways

*   A 76%ile in JEE Main, even within the OBC-NCL category, generally translates to a rank that makes admission to PICT Pune's popular branches challenging.
*   Historical cut-offs for Computer Engineering at PICT Pune are typically much lower than the rank associated with a 76%ile.
*   Admission cut-offs fluctuate annually based on factors like applicant pool and branch demand, but significant shifts are uncommon.
*   Aiming for a percentile above 90 is generally advisable for a more confident chance at top engineering colleges in Maharashtra.

## Understanding JEE Main Percentile and Rank

Your JEE Main percentile indicates how many candidates scored below you. However, for admissions, colleges primarily consider your All India Rank (AIR) or your category rank. While a 76%ile is a decent score, especially within a reserved category, it's crucial to understand how this translates to a rank that would be competitive for an institute like PICT Pune.

Based on past trends, a 76%ile, even with OBC-NCL reservation, might place your rank significantly higher than the usual admission cut-offs for sought-after branches at PICT Pune. For instance, if approximately 10 lakh students appear for JEE Main, a 76%ile means about 7.6 lakh students scored below you, placing you roughly in the top 2.4 lakh. Your category rank would be better, but still potentially high for PICT's most competitive courses.

## PICT Pune Admission Trends and Cut-offs

PICT (Pune Institute of Computer Technology) is renowned for its Computer Engineering, Information Technology, and Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering programs. These branches are highly sought after, leading to very competitive cut-offs.

Historically, the closing ranks for Computer Engineering at PICT Pune, even for reserved categories, tend to be quite low – often well below 20,000. While these cut-offs do vary slightly each year based on the total number of applicants, their performance, and the specific demand for each branch, they rarely shift dramatically. A percentile in the higher 80s or 90s generally offers a much stronger chance for admission to these top branches.

## Maharashtra State Quota and JEE Main

Admissions to engineering colleges in Maharashtra, including PICT Pune, are primarily conducted through the MHT CET exam. However, a certain percentage of seats (typically 15% All India Quota) are filled based on JEE Main scores through counselling processes like JoSAA or CAP rounds conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra. For these All India Quota seats, the competition is even higher.

It's important to remember that the official JoSAA portal and the Maharashtra CET Cell website are the authoritative sources for previous year's cut-off ranks. Reviewing these can give you a clearer picture of the competitiveness.

## What are your options?

Given the competitiveness, with a 76%ile in JEE Main (OBC-NCL), your chances for admission to PICT Pune, particularly for popular branches like Computer Engineering, appear to be very slim. This doesn't mean you should give up on your engineering dreams, but it does suggest you broaden your search.

Consider exploring other reputable engineering colleges in Maharashtra or other states where your percentile might be more competitive. Also, if you appeared for MHT CET, your score in that exam might open up different opportunities within Maharashtra's state quota seats. Always keep an eye on the official counselling schedules and brochures for precise eligibility and cut-off information.

We hope this provides clarity on your admission prospects for PICT Pune.
