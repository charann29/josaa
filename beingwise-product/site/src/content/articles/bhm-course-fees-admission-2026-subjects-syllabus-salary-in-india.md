A Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM) degree is an undergraduate program designed to equip students with the essential knowledge and practical skills needed for a thriving career in the hospitality sector. This course delves into various aspects of hotel and restaurant operations, preparing graduates for diverse roles within this dynamic industry.

## Key Takeaways

*   BHM is a three-year undergraduate program focusing on hospitality management.
*   Eligibility generally requires passing Class 12 from any stream.
*   Admission often involves national-level entrance exams like CUET or NCHMCT JEE.
*   Course fees typically range from approximately INR 2,000 to INR 15 Lakh, depending on the institution.

## BHM Admission 2026: Eligibility and Process

To be eligible for a BHM program, candidates typically need to have successfully completed Class 12 in any stream. Specific colleges might have additional criteria, so it's always wise to check the detailed requirements for your desired institutions.

The admission process generally follows these steps:

1.  **Check Eligibility:** Ensure you meet the academic and age criteria set by the college.
2.  **Application:** Complete and submit the online application form for the respective college or entrance exam.
3.  **Entrance Exam:** Appear for required entrance tests like CUET (scheduled from May 11 to May 31, 2026) or NCHMCT JEE (scheduled for April 25, 2026).
4.  **Merit List & Results:** Await the publication of merit lists based on exam performance.
5.  **Counselling:** If selected, attend counselling sessions.
6.  **Document Verification & Fee Payment:** Verify all necessary documents and pay the admission fees to secure your seat.

Keep in mind that while this outlines the general process, individual colleges may have unique admission procedures.

## BHM Course Syllabus and Key Subjects

The BHM syllabus is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of hotel and hospitality operations. While specific subjects can vary by institution, core areas often include:

*   Food Production and Patisserie
*   Food and Beverage Service
*   Front Office Operations
*   Housekeeping Management
*   Hotel Accounting and Finance
*   Hospitality Marketing and Sales
*   Human Resource Management in Hospitality
*   Facility Planning
*   Communication Skills
*   Hotel Law

Students also gain practical experience through internships and hands-on training, which are integral parts of the curriculum.

## Career Scope and Salary After BHM

A BHM degree opens doors to numerous exciting career opportunities within the vast hospitality industry. Graduates can find employment in hotels, resorts, cruise lines, airlines, event management companies, and even start their own ventures. Some popular job roles include:

*   **Kitchen Chef:** Overseeing food preparation and kitchen operations.
*   **Front Desk Officer:** Managing guest check-ins, check-outs, and inquiries.
*   **Accounting Manager:** Handling financial records and budgeting for hospitality establishments.
*   **Executive Housekeeper:** Supervising cleanliness and maintenance of hotel premises.
*   **Catering Officer:** Planning and executing catering services for events.
*   **Restaurant Manager:** Managing day-to-day operations of dining establishments.

Leading hospitality groups like the Oberoi Group of Hotels, Mansingh Group of Hotels, Ambassador Group of Hotels, and Sarovar Park Group of Hotels are frequent recruiters of BHM graduates. The average starting salary for BHM graduates in India is approximately INR 3.1 Lakh per annum, which can increase significantly with experience and specialization.

Embarking on a BHM journey can be a rewarding choice for those passionate about service and management in the vibrant world of hospitality.
