# I got 147 marks in NEET can I get admission in this college?

Understanding your NEET score and its implications for college admissions is crucial. With a score of 147, it's important to set realistic expectations for your medical career aspirations.

## Key Takeaways

*   A NEET score of 147 is generally insufficient for admission to MBBS or BDS programs in India.
*   Admission cut-offs are significantly higher, even for government-aided seats and reserved categories.
*   Exploring alternative healthcare courses or re-appearing for NEET might be more viable options.
*   Your category (General, OBC, SC, ST, EWS) plays a role, but 147 marks typically fall below the required threshold across all.

## Understanding NEET Cut-offs

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is the gateway to undergraduate medical courses in India. The minimum qualifying marks, known as the cut-off, are determined by the National Testing Agency (NTA) each year. These cut-offs vary based on factors like the difficulty of the exam, the number of applicants, and the available seats.

For instance, the minimum qualifying percentile for the General category is usually 50th percentile, while for OBC/SC/ST, it's 40th percentile. However, simply qualifying the exam doesn't guarantee a seat; actual admission cut-offs for colleges are much higher.

## MBBS and BDS Admissions

Securing a seat in an MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) or BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery) program requires a highly competitive NEET score. For the General category, scores often need to be well above 550-600 for government colleges, and even private colleges have high cut-offs. While reserved categories (OBC, SC, ST, EWS) have lower cut-offs than the General category, a score of 147 typically remains far below the necessary range for any of these programs, across both government and private institutions.

## Exploring Alternative Medical Courses

If your goal is to work in the healthcare sector, and an MBBS or BDS seat seems out of reach with your current score, there are other valuable paths to consider. Courses like BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery), BHMS (Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine and Surgery), and BSMS (Bachelor of Siddha Medicine and Surgery) are also popular. However, even for these courses, the admission cut-offs are generally higher than 147 marks, especially for reputable colleges.

Beyond these, you might explore paramedical courses such as Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT), Bachelor of Optometry, Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT), or Nursing. These fields offer excellent career prospects and often have different admission criteria, though some may still consider NEET scores or require separate entrance exams.

## The Role of Category

Your category (General, OBC, SC, ST, EWS, PwD) significantly influences the cut-off ranks for admission. Reserved categories benefit from lower cut-off scores compared to the General category. However, even with the benefit of reservation, a NEET score of 147 is generally not competitive enough for admission to MBBS, BDS, BAMS, or BHMS courses in most, if not all, colleges across India. It's always advisable to check the official cut-off lists released by the counseling authorities for your specific category.

Considering the highly competitive nature of NEET, it's important to evaluate your options carefully and plan your next steps, whether that means exploring alternative courses or preparing to re-appear for the examination with a renewed strategy.
