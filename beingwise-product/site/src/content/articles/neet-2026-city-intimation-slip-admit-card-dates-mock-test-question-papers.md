# NEET 2026: City Intimation Slip, Admit Card, Dates, Mock Test, Question Papers

For aspiring medical and dental students, the National Eligibility Cum Entrance Test (NEET) is the crucial gateway to undergraduate programs. With a large number of students expected to take the exam, understanding key dates and preparation strategies is vital for success.

## Key takeaways

*   NEET 2026 will be conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) on May 3, 2026, in an offline format.
*   The NEET city intimation slip is expected in mid-April 2026, while the admit card will be available a few days before the exam.
*   Strategic preparation, including extensive practice with mock tests and previous year's question papers, is essential due to the high competition.
*   NEET is the sole entrance exam for various medical and allied health science courses like MBBS, BDS, BAMS, and BHMS.

## NEET 2026 Exam Dates and Schedule

The National Testing Agency (NTA) is responsible for conducting the NEET UG examination. For 2026, the exam is scheduled for **May 3, 2026**. This single-day, offline test is a critical milestone for medical aspirants across India.

## NEET 2026 Exam City Slip and Admit Card

Understanding where and when you will take the exam is crucial. The NTA will first release the **NEET city intimation slip 2026** in **mid-April 2026**. This slip informs candidates about their allotted examination city, helping them make necessary travel arrangements in advance. It is important to note that this is *not* the admit card.

The official **NEET 2026 admit card** will be released by the NTA approximately **three to four days before the examination date**. The admit card is a mandatory document for entry into the examination hall and will contain details such as the candidate's name, roll number, photograph, examination center address, and exam timings. Candidates must download and print their admit cards from the official NTA NEET website.

## NEET Previous Years' Question Papers

Practicing with previous years' question papers is an indispensable part of NEET preparation. It helps candidates understand the exam pattern, the types of questions asked, and the difficulty level. Solving these papers under timed conditions also improves speed and accuracy. Many educational platforms and the NTA's official resources provide access to these valuable papers, often with answer keys.

## NEET Mock Tests by NTA

To help candidates familiarize themselves with the exam environment and question format, the NTA often provides official mock tests. These mock tests are designed to simulate the actual NEET exam experience and are highly recommended for practice. Engaging with these tests regularly can significantly boost confidence and identify areas that need more attention. While official sources are always best, various reputable coaching institutes also offer mock tests that align with the NEET syllabus and pattern.

## How to Prepare for NEET 2026?

Given the intense competition, with over 25 lakh students expected to appear, a well-structured preparation strategy is vital. Here are some key steps:

*   **Understand the Syllabus and Exam Pattern:** Thoroughly review the NEET 2026 syllabus, which covers Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (Botany and Zoology) from Class 11 and 12. Familiarize yourself with the exam pattern, including the number of questions, marking scheme (+4 for correct, -1 for incorrect, 0 for unattempted), and total marks (720).
*   **Create a Study Schedule:** Develop a realistic and consistent study timetable. Allocate sufficient time to all subjects, focusing more on weaker areas.
*   **Practice Regularly:** Solve a large number of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) daily. Practice with previous year's papers and official NTA mock tests to improve speed and accuracy.
*   **Revision:** Regular revision of all topics is crucial to retain information. Make short notes and flashcards for quick review.
*   **Stay Updated:** Keep an eye on official announcements from NTA regarding any changes or updates related to NEET 2026.

NEET is a challenging but achievable examination. With dedication, smart preparation, and effective use of available resources like mock tests and past papers, you can significantly enhance your chances of securing a good rank and pursuing your medical dreams.
