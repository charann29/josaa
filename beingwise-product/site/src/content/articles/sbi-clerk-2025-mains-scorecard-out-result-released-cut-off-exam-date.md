# SBI Clerk 2025: Mains Scorecard (OUT), Result (Released), Cut Off, Exam Date, Pattern, Syllabus & FAQs

The State Bank of India (SBI) recently released the Mains scorecard, response sheets, and answer key for the Junior Associate (Customer Support and Sales) examination. Candidates who appeared for this important exam can now access their detailed results and prepare for the next steps in the recruitment process.

## Key takeaways

*   The SBI Clerk Mains result for the 2025-26 cycle (Advertisement No: CRPD/CR/2025-26/06) was officially announced on February 27.
*   Candidates can download their individual scorecards, along with response sheets and answer keys, from the official SBI website.
*   The Mains examination, a crucial step towards securing a Junior Associate position, was conducted on November 21.
*   Successful candidates from the Mains exam will now proceed to the Language Proficiency Test (LPT), which is a qualifying stage.

## SBI Clerk Result 2025

The SBI Clerk Mains scorecard, response sheet, and answer key have been made available for download. The Mains result, which is the final selection stage before the LPT, was declared on February 27. Candidates can access their results by logging in with their registration number or roll number on the official SBI portal. The result is typically released in two formats: a PDF listing the roll numbers of provisionally selected candidates and an individual scorecard displaying section-wise and overall marks.

To download your result and scorecard:

*   Visit the official SBI website.
*   Locate the direct link for the SBI Clerk Mains Result 2025-26.
*   Enter your registration number or roll number in the designated fields.
*   Your result will be displayed on the screen.
*   Download and save a copy for your records.

## SBI Clerk Cut Off 2025

The cut-off marks for the SBI Clerk Mains examination are expected to be released soon. These cut-offs are announced alongside the results, categorized by state and by candidate category. It's important to note that the SBI Clerk exam does not have sectional cut-offs; selection is based solely on the overall Mains cut-off. Separate cut-offs are determined for both the Preliminary and Mains exams.

## SBI Clerk Exam Pattern and Syllabus

Understanding the exam structure and content is vital for preparation. The SBI Clerk examination typically involves two main stages: Preliminary and Mains. The Preliminary exam is qualifying in nature, while the Mains exam determines final selection. Both stages include sections testing General English, Quantitative Aptitude, Reasoning Ability, and General/Financial Awareness. A detailed syllabus and exam pattern outlining the number of questions, marks, and time duration for each section are usually provided in the official notification released by SBI.

## Language Proficiency Test (LPT)

Candidates who successfully clear the Mains examination will be invited for a Language Proficiency Test (LPT). This test is qualifying in nature, meaning its marks are not added to the final merit list, but passing it is mandatory for selection. The LPT assesses a candidate's proficiency in the local language of the state they applied from. This ensures that Junior Associates can effectively communicate with customers in their respective regions.
