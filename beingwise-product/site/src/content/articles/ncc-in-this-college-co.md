# Is there NCC in this college?

For many students, college isn't just about academics; it's also about holistic development and serving the nation. The National Cadet Corps (NCC) offers a fantastic opportunity for young minds to cultivate discipline, leadership, and a spirit of unity. Let's explore the NCC options at this institution.

## Key Takeaways

*   The college actively supports the National Cadet Corps (NCC) program.
*   It hosts a dedicated NCC unit, established in 1982.
*   Students can pursue B and C Certificate courses, enhancing their skill sets.
*   The program is typically open to B.Tech Semester II students.

## Understanding the NCC Program

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is a premier youth organization in India, known for its motto: "Unity and Discipline." It aims to develop character, comradeship, discipline, a secular outlook, the spirit of adventure, and ideals of selfless service among young citizens. Participating in NCC can be a transformative experience, offering valuable life skills beyond the classroom.

## NCC at This Institution

This particular college proudly hosts an active NCC unit. Specifically, it operates as the 1 (A) EME COY NCC Unit. This unit has a long-standing history, having been established on August 16, 1982, demonstrating the institution's sustained commitment to youth development through the NCC.

## What Courses and Opportunities Are Available?

Students enrolled in the NCC unit at this college have the opportunity to undertake official NCC certification courses. The program guides cadets through both the B and C Certificate Courses. These certificates are recognized nationally and can offer advantages in various career paths, including defense services and government jobs.

## Who Can Join and How Is It Structured?

Typically, the NCC program at this institution is offered to B.Tech Semester II students. This allows first-year students to settle into their academic routines before committing to the NCC's rigorous training. The unit is well-structured, comprising a Commanding Officer, an Associate N.C.C. Officer (ANO), and dedicated Permanent Instructional (P.I.) staff. These experienced personnel guide the cadets through their training, drills, and certificate preparations, ensuring a high standard of instruction and mentorship.

Joining the NCC can be a rewarding journey, fostering personal growth and a sense of national pride.
