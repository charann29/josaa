# Optical Fiber Access Networks Using PON/FTTH by Udemy: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For students and professionals keen on the future of high-speed internet, this Udemy course offers a deep dive into Passive Optical Networks (PON) and Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH) technologies. It's a self-paced learning journey delivered through video and text, designed to equip you with essential knowledge in this rapidly evolving field.

## Key takeaways

*   Learn about the core principles and architecture of Passive Optical Networks (PON) and their applications.
*   Understand the evolution of PON standards from both ITU-T and IEEE bodies.
*   Explore the practical applications of PON technology, particularly in the context of 5G networks.
*   Gain insights into Gigabit PON (GPON) protocols, frame structures, and dynamic bandwidth allocation mechanisms.

## Course Structure and Learning Mode

This course is structured for self-study, allowing learners to progress at their own pace. The content is delivered through a combination of video lectures and accompanying text materials, making complex technical concepts easier to grasp. The primary language of instruction is English, ensuring accessibility for a wide audience.

## What You Will Learn: A Detailed Syllabus

The curriculum is comprehensive, starting with foundational concepts and gradually moving to advanced topics in PON technology. Here’s a breakdown of the key areas covered:

### Introduction to PON

This section lays the groundwork, explaining what PON is, its working principles, and the structure of an Optical Distribution Network (ODN). You'll learn about the advantages of PON, compare it with xDSL technologies (FTTC, FTTB, FTTH), and understand the differences between Optical Network Terminals (ONT) and Optical Network Units (ONU), including various types like SFU, MDU, and SBU. The role of optical splitters in PON is also covered.

### Evolution and Applications

Explore the historical development of PON technology, tracing the evolution of standards set by both ITU-T and IEEE. The course also dedicates a section to the crucial application of PON in 5G technology, detailing its use in 5G Cloud RAN for scenarios like very low latency services, general-purpose cloud networks, small cells in dense urban areas, and hybrid front-haul solutions.

### Optical Fiber Communication Basics

A fundamental understanding of optical fiber communication is essential for PON. This module covers total internal reflection, different types of optical fibers (multimode step index, multimode graded index, single mode), and common impairments like modal dispersion, polarization mode dispersion (PMD), and chromatic dispersion. You'll also learn about attenuation and the specific wavelength windows used in optical communication.

### Gigabit PON (GPON) Architecture and Protocols

This core section dives deep into GPON. It explains the principle of wavelength division multiplexing, downstream and upstream data transmission, and key protocols like OMCI (ONT Management Control Interface). You'll learn about GPON Encapsulation Mode (GEM) ports, Transmission Containers (T-CONT) for upstream, and how GPON protocol layers map to the OSI model. Detailed functional block diagrams for OLT and ONU are provided, along with explanations of GTC frame structure, GPON downstream GEM frame structure, upstream bandwidth maps, and how TDM and Ethernet services are mapped to GEM frames.

### GPON ONU Activation and Dynamic Bandwidth Allocation

Understand the step-by-step ONU activation procedure, from the initial state (O1) through standby (O2), serial number (O3), ranging (O4), and operation (O5) states, including POPUP (O6) and emergency stop (O7). The course also covers dynamic bandwidth allocation (DBA) in GPON, explaining its modes of operation, status report-based (SR-DBA), and non-status report-based (NSR-DBA) operations, along with T-CONT DBA profile types.
