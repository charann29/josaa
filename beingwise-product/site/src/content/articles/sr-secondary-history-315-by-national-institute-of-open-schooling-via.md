# Sr.Secondary: History (315) by National Institute of Open Schooling via Swayam: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For students interested in exploring the rich tapestry of Indian and human history at the senior secondary level, the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) offers a comprehensive course, "Sr.Secondary: History (315)", delivered through the Swayam platform. This program delves into various phases of Indian culture and global civilizations, making it an excellent resource for aspiring historians and anyone keen to deepen their understanding of the past.

## Key takeaways

*   This is a free online course, making quality history education accessible to all.
*   The program spans 24 weeks and is self-paced, allowing flexible learning.
*   Upon successful completion, students earn a certificate jointly issued by NIOS and Swayam.
*   The curriculum covers a broad spectrum, from ancient civilizations to contemporary global events.

## Course Overview and Structure

Coordinated by Professor Dr. Azmat Noori of NIOS, the Sr.Secondary: History (315) course is structured into five core modules and one optional module. Students can choose between "Evolution of State" or "Culture in India" for their optional study, allowing for some personalization of their learning journey. The course is designed for self-study and delivered through video lectures and text-based materials, complemented by interactive elements.

## Learning Experience and Resources

The Swayam platform provides an engaging environment for this course. Learners benefit from a variety of resources, including video lectures, extensive reading materials, and peer discussion forums. These components aim to foster a deeper understanding of social, economic, religious, and cultural developments across different historical eras in India and the world.

## What You Will Learn

By the end of the Sr.Secondary: History (315) program, participants will gain significant knowledge across several key historical domains. The syllabus is meticulously designed to cover:

*   Ancient India
*   Medieval India
*   Modern India
*   The National Movement and Contemporary India
*   The Contemporary World
*   Human civilization through the ages
*   Cultural aspects of human civilization

## Course Syllabus at a Glance

The 24-week curriculum is divided into modules, each focusing on distinct periods and themes:

*   **Module 1 (Weeks 1-8):** Understanding Indian History, Geographical Setting, Pre-historic cultures, Harappan Civilization, Vedic Age, Janapadas to Empire, Post-Mauryan Developments, Guptas and their successors, and India between AD 750–1200.
*   **Module 2 (Weeks 9-12):** Establishment and Expansion of the Delhi Sultanate, Mughal Rule, Emergence of Regional Powers, Administrative Systems, Economy, Cultural Developments in Medieval India, and Eighteenth Century India.
*   **Module 3 (Weeks 13-15):** Establishment of British Rule till 1857, Economic and Social Changes, and Popular Resistance to Company Rule.
*   **Module 4 (Weeks 16-17):** Nationalism and the National Movement & Indian Democracy.
*   **Module 5 (Weeks 18-23):** Legacy of the 19th Century, World War I and the Russian Revolution, Inter-War Period and the Second World War, Cold War and its effects, National Liberation Movements, and Social Transformations in the Twentieth Century.

## Fees and Certification

One of the most appealing aspects of the Sr.Secondary: History (315) course is that it is completely free to enroll and access all learning materials. While the course content is free, certification is available upon successful completion of all course steps. The certificate is jointly provided by the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) and Swayam.

## How to Apply

Enrolling in the Sr.Secondary: History (315) course is a straightforward process:

1.  Visit the official Swayam platform for the course.
2.  Locate the "Sign-in" or "Register" option, typically found in the top right corner of the page.
3.  Create a new account or log in with your existing credentials to begin your learning journey.

This program offers a valuable opportunity to delve deep into historical studies, guided by NIOS experts and delivered through the accessible Swayam platform.
