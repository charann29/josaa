# How To Join Indian Navy After 10th: Eligibility, Selection Process, Salary

For many young Indians, serving the nation through the armed forces is a deeply respected aspiration. The Indian Navy offers a compelling career path, even for those who have completed their Class 10 education. Joining the Navy after Class 10 provides an early opportunity to build a disciplined and rewarding profession at sea.

## Key Takeaways

*   The primary entry route for Class 10 pass-outs is the Agniveer Matric Recruit (MR) scheme.
*   This scheme offers roles such as Chef, Steward, and Hygienist, focusing on essential support services.
*   Selection involves a Computer-Based Test (CBT), a Physical Fitness Test (PFT), and a Medical Examination.
*   Agniveer MR applications are typically released twice a year, offering regular opportunities.

## Indian Navy Agniveer MR Entry: Job Roles

The Agnipath Scheme allows individuals who have passed Class 10 to join the Indian Navy as Agniveer (MR). These roles are crucial for the smooth functioning of naval operations. Applications for these positions are generally announced twice annually, often in June/July and November/December. Aspiring candidates should regularly check the official Indian Navy recruitment website for these announcements.

The main roles available under the Agniveer MR category are:

*   **Chef (MR):** As a Chef, you will be responsible for preparing meals, managing rations, and ensuring dietary needs are met. This role also includes essential firearms training and other organizational duties.
*   **Steward (MR):** Stewards perform duties such as serving in the officers' mess, managing supplies, assisting with menu planning, and maintaining financial records. Like other Agniveers, they also receive weapons training.
*   **Hygienist (MR):** The primary duty of a Hygienist is to maintain cleanliness and sanitation onboard. This role also involves firearms training and contributing to the overall operational efficiency of the organization.

These positions are excellent starting points for disciplined individuals seeking a career in the Indian Navy.

## Eligibility Criteria for Agniveer MR

To be considered for the Agniveer MR recruitment, candidates must meet specific eligibility requirements:

*   **Educational Qualification:** Applicants must have successfully passed their Class 10 examination from an educational board recognized by the Ministry of Education, Government of India.
*   **Age Limit:** Candidates for the Chef, Steward, and Hygienist roles must be between 17.5 and 21 years of age at the time of application.

## Selection Process for Agniveer MR

The selection process for Indian Navy jobs after Class 10 is comprehensive and involves three distinct stages:

1.  **Computer-Based Test (CBT) – Indian Navy Entrance Test (INET):** This is the initial screening stage. The CBT consists of objective-type questions covering subjects such as Science, Mathematics, and General Awareness. The difficulty level of the questions is aligned with the Class 10 syllabus.
2.  **Physical Fitness Test (PFT):** Candidates who clear the CBT proceed to the PFT, which assesses their physical endurance and capability. The PFT includes:
    *   A 1.6 km run to be completed within 6 minutes 30 seconds for male candidates and 8 minutes for female candidates.
    *   20 squats for male candidates and 15 squats for female candidates.
    *   12 push-ups for male candidates and 10 bent-knee sit-ups for female candidates.
3.  **Medical Examination:** The final stage involves a thorough medical examination to ensure candidates meet the strict health standards required for service in the Indian Navy.

## Salary and Career Progression for Agniveers

Under the Agnipath Scheme, Agniveers receive an attractive financial package during their four-year service tenure. The monthly salary starts at ₹30,000 in the first year, increasing to ₹33,000 in the second year, ₹36,500 in the third year, and ₹40,000 in the fourth year. A portion of this salary is contributed to a dedicated 'Agniveer Corpus Fund'.

Upon completion of the four-year service, Agniveers receive a 'Seva Nidhi' package, which is a lump sum amount including their contributions and an equal matching contribution from the government, along with accrued interest. Approximately 25% of Agniveers from each batch will be selected for permanent enrolment in the Indian Navy, based on organizational requirements and their performance during the four-year period. Those not retained will receive support for their transition to civilian life, including skill certifications and assistance in finding employment.

Embarking on a career in the Indian Navy after Class 10 through the Agniveer MR entry is a fantastic way to serve the nation, gain valuable skills, and build a respected future.
