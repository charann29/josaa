# CUET-PG MBA 2026 Counselling & Seat Allotment: Registration Dates, Procedure

After successfully appearing for the CUET-PG 2026 exam, the next crucial step towards securing your MBA seat is understanding the counselling and seat allotment process. This guide will walk you through the essential details you need to know for a smooth admission journey.

## Key takeaways

*   The National Testing Agency (NTA) is responsible for the CUET-PG exam and results, but not for counselling.
*   Each participating university or institution conducts its own online counselling and seat allotment process individually.
*   Candidates must register on the official website of each university they wish to apply to, using their CUET-PG scores.
*   The counselling process typically involves multiple rounds, continuing until all available MBA seats are filled.

## Understanding the CUET-PG 2026 Counselling Process

Unlike some other national exams where a central body handles counselling, for CUET-PG, the NTA's role concludes with releasing the results and scorecards. It is then up to the individual universities accepting CUET-PG scores to manage their own admission procedures, including counselling and seat allotment.

This means that if you've applied to multiple universities, you will need to track and participate in the counselling process for each one separately. Universities will typically notify eligible candidates about important dates and formalities via email or through announcements on their official websites.

### Step-by-Step Counselling Procedure

The general steps for CUET-PG MBA counselling at most participating universities are as follows:

1.  **University Registration:** Visit the official website of your desired university and complete their specific registration form for CUET-PG admissions. This usually involves providing your personal details and CUET-PG scorecard information.
2.  **Course Preference Submission:** After registration, you will typically be asked to select your preferred MBA programs offered by that university.
3.  **Merit List Release:** Based on the registrations received and the CUET-PG scores, the university will publish a merit list. Only candidates whose names appear on this list will be eligible for further counselling rounds.
4.  **Seat Allotment Result:** Universities will release seat allotment results based on candidate preferences, merit rank, and seat availability. These results are usually published on their official portals.
5.  **Admission Confirmation:** If you are satisfied with the allotted seat, you must complete the final admission formalities, which typically include document verification and payment of the admission fee within the stipulated timeframe.

## Important Dates for CUET-PG 2026 Counselling

While the NTA conducted the CUET-PG exam until March 27, 2026, and is expected to release the final answer key and results in April/May, the counselling dates are entirely determined by each individual university. There is no single, consolidated schedule. 

It is crucial for candidates to regularly check the official websites of their preferred universities for specific announcements regarding registration deadlines, merit list publication, and seat allotment rounds. The entire counselling process, across all universities, could span two to three months after the results are declared.

## Documents Required for Counselling

While the exact list might vary slightly from one university to another, generally you should prepare the following documents for the counselling and admission process:

*   CUET-PG 2026 Scorecard
*   CUET-PG 2026 Admit Card
*   Class 10th Mark Sheet and Certificate
*   Class 12th Mark Sheet and Certificate
*   Graduation Mark Sheets (all semesters/years) and Degree Certificate/Provisional Certificate
*   Category Certificate (if applicable, for SC/ST/OBC-NCL/EWS) issued by a competent authority
*   Transfer Certificate (TC)
*   Migration Certificate
*   Character Certificate
*   Passport-sized photographs
*   Proof of identity (Aadhar Card, Passport, etc.)

Always check the specific requirements listed on the university's official admission portal.

## Merit List and Cutoff Scores

Each participating university will prepare its own merit list for the MBA program based on the CUET-PG scores of registered candidates. The CUET-PG cutoff score will also be determined independently by each university, depending on factors like the number of applicants, the difficulty of the exam, and the available seats. Candidates with higher CUET-PG scores will naturally have a higher priority in the seat allotment process.

It is important to remember that the admission is provisional until all documents are verified and fees are paid.
