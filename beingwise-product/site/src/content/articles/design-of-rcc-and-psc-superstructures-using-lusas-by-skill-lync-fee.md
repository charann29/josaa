# Design of RCC and PSC Superstructures using LUSAS by Skill Lync: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This article explores an online program focused on designing reinforced concrete (RCC) and prestressed concrete (PSC) superstructures using the LUSAS software. It's designed for civil and structural engineers looking to deepen their understanding and practical skills in this crucial area of construction.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn to design and analyze RCC and PSC superstructures, including various bridge forms, using LUSAS and MS Excel.
*   The program spans two months and includes hands-on experience with industrial projects and a demo session.
*   It offers comprehensive support, including video lectures, project portfolio development, and dedicated technical assistance.
*   Upon completion, students receive a course certificate, with a merit certificate awarded to the top 5% of participants.

## Course Overview

This online course is specifically crafted for individuals interested in the analytical and design processes behind reinforced concrete and prestressed concrete structures. Offered by Skill Lync, it caters to students and engineers eager to learn about precast structures and advanced design methodologies. The program emphasizes practical application through the use of LUSAS, a finite element analysis software, alongside MS Excel for design calculations.

## What You Will Learn

The curriculum is structured to provide a thorough understanding of designing, analyzing, and detailing various concrete bridge superstructures. Participants will gain expertise in both reinforced concrete and prestressed concrete applications. Key topics include:

*   Solid and voided slabs
*   Ribbed decks
*   Prestressed girders
*   Box girder bridge forms
*   Steel-concrete composite design
*   Concepts like long-term effects, integral bridges, and construction stage analyses.

Beyond theoretical knowledge, the course aims to enhance practical skills in civil engineering, design, and analytical techniques, grounded in the principles of physics as they apply to structural behavior.

## Program Structure and Offerings

The two-month online program is delivered through a self-study model, primarily via video and text-based content, all in English. It offers a rich set of resources and support mechanisms to facilitate learning:

*   **Learning Materials:** Video lectures, a demo session, and comprehensive project work.
*   **Software Tools:** Training on LUSAS and integration with MS Excel.
*   **Practical Experience:** Opportunity to work on two industrially relevant projects, helping students build a project portfolio.
*   **Support:** Email, forum, and telephone support, including access to a dedicated support engineer.
*   **Certificates:** A course completion certificate for all successful participants, and a merit certificate for the top 5% performers.

## Eligibility and Admission Details

This program is ideal for civil and structural engineers at various stages of their careers, from fresh graduates to experienced professionals looking to update their skills in precast structures and related domains.

*   **Eligibility Criteria:** Applicants must hold a graduation degree in civil engineering or structural engineering. A foundational understanding of concrete behavior and design principles is also required.
*   **How to Apply:** Prospective students interested in this program should register online through the official Skill Lync portal.
