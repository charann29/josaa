# CAT Score vs Percentile 2025 (Expected): Overall & Section-wise Analysis

Understanding the difference between your raw CAT score and your CAT percentile is crucial for anyone aiming for top MBA programs. While your score reflects the marks you earned, your percentile indicates your performance relative to all other test-takers, making it a key factor for admissions.

## Key takeaways

*   Your CAT score is the total marks you achieve, while your percentile shows how many candidates you outperformed.
*   IIMs and other leading B-schools primarily use your CAT percentile for shortlisting candidates for further admission rounds.
*   The CAT exam includes negative marking for incorrect multiple-choice questions (MCQs), impacting your raw score.
*   CAT scores undergo a normalization process to ensure fairness across different exam slots and question paper difficulties.

## Understanding CAT Score and Percentile

Your CAT score is simply the sum of the marks you get in each section, considering both correct and incorrect answers. For every correct answer, you typically get 3 marks, and for every incorrect MCQ, 1 mark is deducted. Non-MCQs usually do not have negative marking. The total marks from all sections combine to form your overall CAT score.

On the other hand, your CAT percentile is a relative measure. If you achieve the 99th percentile, it means you performed better than 99% of all candidates who took the CAT exam. This relative ranking is what business schools look at when evaluating applications, as it provides a standardized way to compare candidates from a large pool.

## How CAT Scores Are Calculated

To determine your CAT score, the marks from each section – Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DILR), and Quantitative Ability (QA) – are added up. Here’s a typical breakdown:

*   **VARC:** Often has the highest number of questions.
*   **DILR:** Focuses on analytical and problem-solving skills.
*   **QA:** Tests your mathematical aptitude.

Each correct answer generally adds 3 marks, while an incorrect MCQ typically results in a deduction of 1 mark. Unattempted questions and non-MCQs usually do not incur negative marking. The sum of these marks across all sections gives you your raw CAT score.

## The Normalization Process

Since the CAT exam is conducted in multiple sessions, the difficulty level of the question papers can vary slightly between these slots. To ensure fairness, the scores are subjected to a normalization process. This scientific method, applied by the CAT conducting body, adjusts scores to account for differences in question paper difficulty and candidate performance across various sessions. This ensures that a candidate's percentile is a true reflection of their ability, regardless of when they took the exam.

## Why Percentile Matters More for Admissions

Leading institutions, including the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs), rely heavily on your CAT percentile for their initial shortlisting process. This percentile forms a critical part of their eligibility criteria for subsequent stages like the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI). While your raw score is the foundation, it's the percentile that ultimately determines your standing among all applicants.

Understanding the interplay between your CAT score and percentile is vital for setting realistic goals and interpreting your results effectively.
