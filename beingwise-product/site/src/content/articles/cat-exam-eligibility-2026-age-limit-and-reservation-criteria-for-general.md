# CAT Exam Eligibility 2026: Age Limit & Reservation Criteria for General, SC/ST/PwD

The Common Admission Test (CAT) is a crucial entrance exam for students aspiring to pursue MBA or PGDM programs at the prestigious Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) and numerous other top business schools across India. Understanding the eligibility criteria is the first step towards a successful application. This guide outlines the academic requirements, age limits, and reservation policies for CAT 2026.

## Key Takeaways

*   A Bachelor's degree with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks is generally required for the CAT exam.
*   Candidates from SC, ST, and PwD categories benefit from a relaxed minimum score of 45%.
*   Final-year undergraduate students are also eligible to apply.
*   There is no upper age limit to appear for the CAT exam.

## Academic Qualification Requirements

The fundamental requirement to be eligible for CAT 2026 is the completion of a Bachelor's degree from a recognized university. For general category candidates, including EWS and NC-OBC, a minimum aggregate score of 50% in their undergraduate degree is necessary. For candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disabilities (PwD) categories, this minimum aggregate score is reduced to 45%.

Students who are currently in the final year of their Bachelor's degree or an equivalent qualification are also eligible to apply. However, they must complete all degree requirements and produce proof of their degree within a specified timeframe, typically by June of the admission year, as stipulated by the IIMs.

Professional qualifications such as Chartered Accountancy (CA), Company Secretaryship (CS), or Cost and Management Accountancy (ICWAI) are also accepted as equivalent to a Bachelor's degree, provided they meet the minimum percentage criteria.

Here's a quick overview of the academic requirements:

| Qualification Type                                    | Affiliation Required                                 | Minimum Pass Percentage Required                |
| :---------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------- |
| Engineering Degree (B.Tech/B.E.)                      | Recognized by MHRD/AICTE                             | 50% (General/EWS/NC-OBC); 45% (SC, ST, PwD)     |
| Bachelor's Degree in any stream                       | Recognized by UGC                                    | 50% (General/EWS/NC-OBC); 45% (SC, ST, PwD)     |
| Final Year Bachelor's Degree Students                 | University Recognized by MHRD/AICTE or UGC           | 50% (General/EWS/NC-OBC); 45% (SC, ST, PwD)     |
| Professional Degrees (CA/CS/ICWA/FIAI)                | Certification granting organization                  | 50% (General/EWS/NC-OBC); 45% (SC, ST, PwD)     |

While a postgraduate degree is not mandatory for CAT, having one can strengthen your overall profile during the interview and admission shortlisting process at IIMs. The CAT application form for 2026 will provide options to include details of any additional qualifications.

## CAT Age Limit

One of the welcoming aspects of the CAT exam is the absence of an upper age limit. Candidates of any age who meet the academic eligibility criteria are welcome to apply. This allows individuals at various stages of their career to pursue an MBA, promoting diversity in the classroom.

## Reservation Criteria for CAT 2026

The CAT exam and subsequent admissions to IIMs and other participating institutes adhere to the reservation policies mandated by the Government of India. These policies ensure equitable opportunities for various communities. The reservation percentages are applied to the total number of available seats in each program.

*   **Scheduled Castes (SC):** 15% of the seats are reserved.
*   **Scheduled Tribes (ST):** 7.5% of the seats are reserved.
*   **Other Backward Classes (OBC - Non-Creamy Layer):** 27% of the seats are reserved.
*   **Economically Weaker Sections (EWS):** 10% of the seats are reserved.
*   **Persons with Disabilities (PwD):** 5% of the seats are reserved horizontally across all categories.

Candidates applying under any reserved category must provide valid certificates issued by competent authorities as proof. Failure to produce valid documentation at the time of application or admission can lead to the cancellation of their candidature under the reserved category.

## Who is Not Eligible to Apply?

Candidates who do not possess a Bachelor's degree from a recognized university, or an equivalent professional qualification, with the minimum required aggregate marks, are not eligible to apply for CAT 2026. Additionally, those who are in their final year of graduation but fail to complete their degree requirements and submit proof by the stipulated deadline will have their admission offer withdrawn.

## IIM Eligibility vs. CAT Eligibility

It's important to understand that while meeting the CAT eligibility criteria allows you to appear for the exam, individual IIMs and other B-schools may have their own specific additional eligibility requirements for their MBA/PGDM programs. These can include minimum sectional scores in CAT, work experience requirements, or higher undergraduate percentage cut-offs. Always check the specific program's eligibility criteria on the official IIM websites or the respective institute's portal before applying.

By carefully reviewing these guidelines, aspiring MBA candidates can ensure they meet all necessary conditions for CAT 2026 and successfully navigate their application journey.
