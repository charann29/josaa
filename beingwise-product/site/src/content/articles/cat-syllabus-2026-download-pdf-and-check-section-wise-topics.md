## CAT VARC Syllabus 2026

The VARC section is typically the first part of the CAT exam, comprising 24 questions. It is broadly divided into Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension. Notably, direct vocabulary questions are usually not present. This section can be challenging, especially due to the substantial number of Reading Comprehension (RC) questions. The structure of the VARC section tends to remain consistent year after year.

Below is a general breakdown of topics, question distribution, and difficulty levels, based on recent CAT exams. Candidates preparing for CAT 2026 can anticipate a similar pattern.

### CAT Reading Comprehension Passages

Reading Comprehension questions constitute a significant portion of the VARC section, often making up two-thirds of it. Typically, there are four to five RC passages, leading to about 12-14 questions. These passages can originate from a wide array of fields and are often lengthy and complex. Reviewing topics from previous years can offer insight into the breadth of subjects covered.

For example, recent CAT exams have featured passages on diverse subjects such as income inequality, electronic music, mental insanity, complex systems, blind fish, literature, the use of language in AI, human-ecology relationships, artificial intelligence ethics, tribal artists, dams, forest officials, utility and human economics, entertainment (like Netflix), Australian species, craftsmanship, the spice trade, unintended consequences of technology, peer review of research, and automobiles.

## CAT DILR Syllabus 2026

The Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR) section is designed to test your ability to analyze data and apply logical thinking to solve problems. This section typically features caselets or sets of questions rather than individual standalone questions. Success here relies on careful reading, critical analysis, and the ability to deduce conclusions from given information.

## CAT Quantitative Aptitude Syllabus 2026

Quantitative Aptitude (QA) assesses your mathematical skills and analytical prowess. This section covers a range of topics from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and modern math. Strong foundational knowledge and the ability to apply concepts quickly are key to performing well. Practice with a variety of question types is essential to build speed and accuracy.

## CAT 2026 Exam Pattern & Marking Scheme

Understanding the CAT exam pattern and marking scheme is as important as knowing the syllabus. The exam is computer-based, with specific time limits for each section. Typically, each correct answer fetches three marks, while incorrect answers lead to a deduction of one mark for multiple-choice questions. There are also non-MCQ (Type-In-The-Answer) questions, which usually do not have negative marking. Candidates should refer to the official CAT notification for the precise details regarding the 2026 exam pattern and marking scheme when it is released.

Mastering the CAT 2026 syllabus requires dedication and a strategic approach, ensuring you cover all essential topics and practice regularly.
