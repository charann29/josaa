# CAT 2026 Notification: When will IIM CAT Exam Notification OUT?

Aspiring for an MBA from a top Indian Institute of Management (IIM) means preparing for the Common Admission Test (CAT). The CAT 2026 notification is a crucial document that outlines all the essential details for this national-level entrance examination. It's vital for all candidates to review this official document carefully.

## Key takeaways

*   The official CAT 2026 notification is expected to be released by late July 2026.
*   This notification will contain comprehensive information on the conducting IIM, important dates, eligibility, exam pattern, and application fees.
*   Understanding the details in the official notification is key to a smooth application and preparation process.
*   The CAT exam pattern for 2026 is anticipated to remain consistent with previous years.

## Understanding the CAT Notification

The Common Admission Test (CAT) is a highly competitive examination for admission to postgraduate management programs offered by the IIMs and numerous other prestigious business schools across India. The official notification, typically released by the end of July each year, is the definitive source for all information regarding the upcoming exam. This document will specify the IIM responsible for conducting CAT 2026, the exact exam date, registration window, detailed eligibility requirements, syllabus overview, examination structure, test center locations, and policies concerning reservations.

## CAT 2026 Exam Pattern

The structure of the CAT exam is generally stable from year to year. For CAT 2026, candidates can expect a similar pattern, comprising three main sections. The exam is computer-based and designed to assess various aptitudes crucial for management studies. Here’s a summary of the expected pattern:

| CAT Exam Pattern Highlights | Details |
| :-------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sections**                | Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), Quantitative Ability (QA) |
| **Total Duration**          | 120 minutes (40 minutes per section) |
| **Total Questions**         | Approximately 66 |
| **Section-wise Questions**  | VARC: 24, DILR: 22, QA: 22 |
| **Total Marks**             | 198 (3 marks per correct answer) |
| **Language**                | English |
| **Question Types**          | Mixture of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and Non-MCQs (Type-In-The-Answer) |
| **Marking Scheme**          | +3 for correct MCQs, -1 for incorrect MCQs. No negative marking for Non-MCQs. |

## CAT 2026 Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for CAT 2026, candidates must meet specific academic requirements. These criteria are strictly adhered to, and it's essential to ensure you qualify before applying. The official notification will provide the definitive eligibility rules, but generally, they include:

*   A Bachelor's degree with at least 50% marks or an equivalent CGPA from a recognized university. For candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disability (PwD) categories, the minimum percentage required is 45%.
*   Candidates in their final year of a Bachelor's degree program are also eligible to apply, provided they complete their degree with the required percentage by a specified deadline.
*   Those with professional qualifications such as CA, CS, or ICWA, with a minimum of 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD categories), are also eligible.
*   There is no upper age limit to appear for the CAT exam, making it accessible to both fresh graduates and experienced professionals.

The official CAT portal will detail any specific requirements or conditions that applicants must satisfy.

## CAT Registration and Fees

The registration process for CAT 2026 is entirely online. Candidates will need to visit the official CAT website, create an account, fill out the application form with personal and academic details, upload necessary documents, and pay the application fee. The fee structure typically varies for different categories:

*   **General/EWS/NC-OBC Categories:** The application fee is usually around INR 2,400.
*   **SC/ST/PwD Categories:** A reduced fee, generally around INR 1,200, is applicable.

These fees are non-refundable and must be paid online through various payment modes such as credit/debit card or net banking. It is always advisable to complete the registration well before the deadline to avoid any last-minute technical issues.

Staying informed through the official CAT 2026 notification is the first step towards a successful MBA journey.
