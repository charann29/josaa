## What You Will Learn: The Syllabus

The course curriculum is thoughtfully designed to provide a foundational understanding of nuclear chemistry. Here's a breakdown of the key topics covered:

*   **Introduction to Nuclear Chemistry:** Setting the stage for the subject.
*   **Review of Atomic and Nuclear Structure:** Revisiting essential building blocks.
*   **Nuclear Binding Energy:** Understanding the forces that hold nuclei together.
*   **Nuclear Reactions:** Exploring how nuclei transform.
*   **Nuclear Stability:** Factors determining whether a nucleus is stable or unstable.
*   **Radioactive Decay Processes:** Different ways unstable nuclei transform.
*   **Balancing Nuclear Reactions and Nuclear Decay:** Learning to write and balance nuclear equations.
*   **Radionuclide Half-Lives:** Measuring the rate of radioactive decay.
*   **Radioactive Decay Series:** Tracing sequential decay pathways.
*   **Radiometric Dating:** Applications of decay in dating geological and archaeological samples.
*   **Nuclear Fission:** The process of splitting atomic nuclei.
*   **Nuclear Reactors:** How fission is controlled for energy generation.
*   **Nuclear Fusion:** The process of combining atomic nuclei.
*   **Transmutation Reactions:** Converting one element into another.
*   **Uses of Radioisotopes:** Practical applications in various fields.
*   **Detecting and Measuring Radioactivity:** Techniques and instruments used.
*   **Biological Effects of Radiation:** Understanding the impact of radiation on living organisms.
