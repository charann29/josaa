# What is Library & Information Science?: Subjects, Course Fees, Admission 2026, Career Options

Library and Information Science (LIS) is a fascinating field that focuses on how we create, manage, organise, and use information in all its forms, from ancient manuscripts to modern digital data. It equips students with essential skills for handling vast amounts of knowledge, ensuring it's accessible and preserved for future generations.

## Key takeaways

*   LIS courses cover a wide range of topics, including archiving, digital library management, and information services.
*   Programs are available at various levels: undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG), diploma, certificate, and PhD.
*   Annual course fees typically range from INR 10,000 to INR 3 Lakh, depending on the institution and program level.
*   Graduates can pursue diverse roles like librarians, archivists, digital resource managers, and information architects.

## Understanding Library & Information Science

LIS is an interdisciplinary field that combines principles from library studies, information technology, and management. It's about more than just managing books; it involves understanding how information flows, how to retrieve it efficiently, and how to make it useful to people. Students learn to handle historical, cultural, scientific, and written information, whether it's in physical archives or complex digital systems.

### Course Levels and Duration

LIS programs are offered at several academic levels:

*   **Undergraduate (UG):** Typically a 3-year Bachelor of Library and Information Science (BLIS) degree.
*   **Postgraduate (PG):** Usually a 2-year Master of Library and Information Science (MLIS) degree.
*   **Diploma and Certificate:** Shorter courses focusing on specific skills.
*   **PhD:** A 3 to 5-year research program for advanced study and contribution to the field.

Programs can be pursued through regular, part-time, or distance learning modes, offering flexibility for different students.

## Admission Process and Eligibility for 2026

Admission to Library and Information Science courses can be through various routes:

*   **Direct Admission:** Some colleges offer direct entry based on academic merit.
*   **Merit-based:** Selection is often determined by performance in previous academic qualifications.
*   **Entrance-based:** Many universities require candidates to appear for entrance exams. For example, the CUET (Common University Entrance Test) is a significant examination for UG admissions to many central universities, as per NTA guidelines. Prospective students for 2026 should monitor the official NTA website for CUET updates, including exam day instructions and guidelines.

### Eligibility Criteria:

*   **For UG courses (e.g., BLIS):** Candidates must have successfully completed Class 12 from a recognised board.
*   **For PG courses (e.g., MLIS):** A bachelor's degree in any discipline is generally required.
*   **For PhD programs:** A master's degree in Library and Information Science or a related field is necessary.

## Course Fees and Top Institutions

The cost of pursuing a Library and Information Science course varies significantly based on the institution type (government vs. private), its reputation, and the program level. Generally, students can expect annual fees to range from INR 10,000 to INR 3 Lakh.

Some well-known institutions offering LIS programs include B.K. Birla College of Arts, Science and Commerce, Isabella Thoburn Degree College, Lovely Professional University (LPU), Vardhman Mahaveer Open University (VMOU), Goa University, and Galgotias University. When choosing a college, it's advisable to check their specific LIS program details and any available NIRF rankings for humanities and social sciences disciplines.

## Career Opportunities and Salary Expectations

A degree in Library and Information Science opens doors to a variety of career paths in both the public and private sectors. The average starting salary for LIS graduates typically ranges from INR 3 Lakh to INR 8 Lakh per annum, depending on the role, organisation, and location.

### Popular Job Profiles:

*   **Librarian:** Managing library collections, assisting users, and organising resources.
*   **Archivist:** Preserving historical documents and records.
*   **Digital Resource Manager:** Overseeing digital collections and online information systems.
*   **Information Architect:** Designing and structuring information systems for usability.
*   **Cataloguer:** Classifying and cataloguing materials for easy retrieval.
*   **Records Manager:** Managing an organisation's records from creation to disposal.
*   **Junior Information Analyst:** Analysing data and information for decision-making.
*   **Integrated Library System Application Specialist:** Managing and optimising library software systems.
*   **Teacher/Lecturer:** Educating future LIS professionals in academic institutions.

Graduates find employment in government offices, public and private libraries, educational institutions, museums, corporate information centres, and even in IT companies dealing with data management.

Embarking on a journey in Library and Information Science means becoming a custodian and facilitator of knowledge in an increasingly information-driven world.
