# How to Join ISRO after 12th - A Complete Guide

Dreaming of a career in space exploration and technology? The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) offers incredible opportunities for bright, science-minded students. This guide will walk you through the essential steps to prepare for a fulfilling journey with ISRO right after completing your 12th standard.

## Key takeaways

*   A strong foundation in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) in classes 11 and 12 is crucial.
*   Pursuing a Bachelor's degree in engineering or science from a reputable institution is the primary pathway.
*   Excelling in competitive exams like JEE Main and JEE Advanced can open doors to top engineering colleges.
*   The Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST) is a direct route for aspiring ISRO scientists and engineers.

## Understanding ISRO

ISRO, headquartered in Bengaluru, is a world-renowned space agency responsible for groundbreaking missions like Chandrayaan and Mangalyaan. It's a hub for innovation in satellite technology, rocket science, and space exploration. For those passionate about contributing to India's space program, ISRO offers a dynamic and challenging environment for scientists and engineers.

## Step 1: Laying the Foundation with PCM

The very first step on your path to ISRO begins in your higher secondary education. It is essential to choose Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) as your core subjects in classes 11 and 12. A solid grasp of these subjects, particularly mathematical and physical principles, forms the bedrock for a career in space science and engineering. This strong academic base is fundamental for anyone aspiring to join ISRO after 12th science.

## Step 2: Pursuing an Engineering or Science Degree

After successfully completing your 12th standard with PCM, the next vital step is to pursue a relevant undergraduate degree. This typically involves appearing for national-level entrance examinations to secure admission into top engineering or science institutions. 

### Entrance Examinations

*   **JEE Main and JEE Advanced:** These highly competitive exams are gateways to premier engineering colleges across India. Performing well in these examinations can lead to admission in institutions like the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and National Institutes of Technology (NITs), which are excellent launchpads for an ISRO career.
*   **Other University Entrance Exams:** Depending on your chosen field, you might also consider other university-specific entrance tests for science degrees.

### Key Institutions for ISRO Aspirants

While many engineering and science colleges can prepare you for ISRO, some institutions have a direct or very strong connection:

*   **Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST):** Located in Thiruvananthapuram, IIST is a unique institution established by the Department of Space, Government of India. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in space science, technology, and applications. A significant advantage of studying at IIST is the direct recruitment opportunity at ISRO for top-performing graduates, subject to specific criteria and requirements. Admission to IIST is primarily based on JEE Advanced scores.
*   **Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs):** These institutes offer top-tier engineering programs in various disciplines relevant to ISRO, such as Aerospace Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Computer Science Engineering, and more. Graduates from IITs are highly sought after by ISRO.
*   **National Institutes of Technology (NITs):** Similar to IITs, NITs provide excellent engineering education. A strong academic record from an NIT can also pave the way for a career at ISRO.

## Step 3: Excelling in Your Chosen Degree

Once you've gained admission to an engineering or science program, maintaining an excellent academic record is paramount. ISRO looks for candidates with strong foundational knowledge and a deep understanding of their chosen field. Focus on subjects like:

*   Aerospace Engineering
*   Mechanical Engineering
*   Electronics and Communication Engineering
*   Computer Science Engineering
*   Physics
*   Mathematics
*   Material Science

Participating in projects, internships (if available in relevant fields), and research can further enhance your profile. ISRO often recruits scientists and engineers through competitive examinations and interviews conducted after graduation. Graduates from IIST may have a more direct path, as mentioned, but all aspirants must demonstrate exceptional academic merit and technical skills.

Embarking on a career with ISRO is a challenging yet incredibly rewarding journey that begins with diligent preparation right after your 12th standard.
