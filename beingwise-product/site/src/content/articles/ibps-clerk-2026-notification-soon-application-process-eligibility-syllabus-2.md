# IBPS Clerk 2026: Notification (Soon), Application Process, Eligibility, Syllabus, Pattern, Vacancies & FAQs

For those aspiring to build a career in India's public sector banks, the Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) Clerk examination is a significant opportunity. This annual national-level exam recruits candidates for Clerical Cadre positions, also known as Customer Service Associates, in various nationalized banks across the country.

## Key takeaways

*   The IBPS Clerk 2026 preliminary examination is scheduled for October 10 and 11, with the main examination on December 27, 2026.
*   The official notification for CRP CSA-XVI, detailing the full schedule and application specifics, is expected soon on the official IBPS portal.
*   The selection process involves two online examinations: Prelims and Mains, with no interview stage.
*   Successful candidates are placed in one of eleven participating public sector banks.

## Application Process and Eligibility

The application process for the IBPS Clerk exam is conducted online. Once the official notification is released, candidates will be able to register and submit their applications through the official IBPS portal. It's important to carefully review the eligibility criteria outlined in the notification, which typically includes age limits, educational qualifications, and nationality requirements.

For the 2022-23 cycle, the application fee was INR 850 for General and OBC categories, and INR 175 for SC/ST/PWD candidates. These fees are subject to change and will be confirmed in the 2026 notification.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The IBPS Clerk selection process consists of two online examinations:

### Preliminary Exam

This is a qualifying exam that assesses basic aptitude. It lasts 60 minutes and includes three sections:

*   English Language
*   Numerical Ability
*   Reasoning Ability

There are 100 questions in total, carrying 100 marks. A penalty of 0.25 marks is deducted for each incorrect answer.

### Main Exam

Candidates who clear the preliminary exam proceed to the main exam. This examination is 160 minutes long and comprises four sections:

*   General/Financial Awareness
*   General English
*   Reasoning Ability & Computer Aptitude
*   Quantitative Aptitude

The main exam has 190 questions, totaling 200 marks, with a similar negative marking scheme of 0.25 marks for incorrect responses. The exam is offered in English, Hindi, and 13 regional languages.

The detailed syllabus for each section will be available in the official notification. It's advisable for candidates to thoroughly understand the syllabus and pattern to prepare effectively.

## Participating Banks and Vacancies

IBPS conducts this recruitment drive on behalf of 11 nationalized banks. These banks utilize the scores from the Common Recruitment Process (CRP) to fill clerical positions in their various branches across India. The list of participating banks includes:

*   Bank of Baroda
*   Canara Bank
*   Indian Overseas Bank
*   UCO Bank
*   Bank of India
*   Central Bank of India
*   Punjab National Bank
*   Union Bank of India
*   Bank of Maharashtra
*   Indian Bank
*   Punjab & Sind Bank

The number of vacancies will be announced along with the official notification. This figure is crucial for candidates to understand the scale of the recruitment drive for the year.
