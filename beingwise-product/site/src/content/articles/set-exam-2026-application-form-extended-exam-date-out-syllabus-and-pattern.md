The Symbiosis Entrance Test (SET) is a crucial gateway for students aspiring to join a variety of undergraduate programs at Symbiosis International University (SIU) and its affiliated institutions. This guide provides essential information about the SET 2026 examination, including application details, important dates, and the exam structure.

## Key Takeaways

*   The SET 2026 application window is open until April 15, 2026.
*   Two test dates are scheduled: May 2, 2026 (Test 1) and May 10, 2026 (Test 2).
*   Admit cards will be released on April 24, 2026 (Test 1) and April 30, 2026 (Test 2).
*   Results for SET 2026 will be announced on May 20, 2026.

## What is the SET Exam?

The Symbiosis Entrance Test (SET) is an annual entrance examination conducted by Symbiosis International University (Deemed University) for admission to various undergraduate courses. These programs include Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA), Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA), Bachelor of Science (BSc), and Bachelor of Arts (BA), offered by the 12 institutes affiliated with the university. The SET is an online examination designed to assess candidates' aptitude for these diverse fields.

It's important to note that for specific programs, such as those at the Symbiosis School of Liberal Arts (SSLA), candidates do not need to take the SET exam. Admissions to SSLA's BA Liberal Arts programs, including those with honors or research projects and specializations like Media Studies or International Relations, are handled directly through the SSLA portal.

## SET 2026 Application Process

The application process for SET 2026 began in December 2025 and will conclude on April 15, 2026. Prospective students can complete their registration and submit their application form through the official SET portal. It is advisable to apply well before the deadline to avoid any last-minute technical issues.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The SET 2026 examination is a 60-minute online test. The paper is structured to evaluate candidates across several key areas:

*   **General English:** Assesses language proficiency, comprehension, and grammar.
*   **Quantitative Aptitude:** Tests mathematical skills, including arithmetic, algebra, and data interpretation.
*   **General Awareness:** Covers current affairs, static general knowledge, and various other topics.
*   **Analytical & Logical Reasoning:** Evaluates problem-solving abilities and logical thinking.

Familiarity with these sections is crucial for effective preparation. Candidates should refer to the official SET website for the detailed syllabus and any updates to the exam pattern.
