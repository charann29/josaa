## MAH MBA CET 2026 Analysis (April 7)

The MAH MBA CET 2026 was held on April 6, 7, and 8, followed by a second session on May 9, 2026. For the April 7 exam, the overall difficulty level was perceived as easy to moderate. However, the paper was notably lengthy due to the number of questions and the time-consuming nature of certain sections, particularly Logical Reasoning and Quantitative Aptitude.

### Sectional Breakdown:

*   **Logical Reasoning & Abstract Reasoning:** These sections often featured a high number of questions requiring careful reading and analysis, contributing significantly to the exam's length. While individual questions might not have been overly complex, the sheer volume made time management crucial.
*   **Quantitative Aptitude:** This section also contained lengthy calculations and problem-solving, demanding good speed and accuracy from candidates.
*   **Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension:** Typically, this section is a mix of direct grammar/vocabulary questions and longer reading passages. The difficulty here often depends on the complexity of the passages.

Candidates who appeared for the May 9 session had another opportunity to improve their scores, with the higher of the two attempts being considered for the Common Admission Process (CAP).

## MAH MBA CET 2026 Answer Key & Result

The official answer key for MAH MBA CET 2026 is expected to be released separately for each examination session. This key allows candidates to cross-check their responses and estimate their scores. Following the release of the answer keys, the Maharashtra State CET Cell will publish the consolidated result for all sessions. The result will include the percentile scores of candidates, which are crucial for the CAP.

## MAH MBA CET 2026 Important Dates

While specific dates for 2026 will be announced by the Maharashtra State CET Cell, here are the estimated timelines based on previous years, including the actual exam dates for 2026:

*   **Registration Period:** Typically starts in January/February.
*   **Admit Card Release:** Usually a week or two before the exam dates.
*   **MAH MBA CET 2026 Exam Dates:** April 6, 7, 8, and May 9, 2026.
*   **Answer Key Release:** Expected a few weeks after the last exam date.
*   **Result Declaration:** Typically in May/June.
*   **CAP Counselling:** Begins after results are declared, usually in June/July.

Candidates should regularly check the official portal of the Maharashtra State CET Cell for the most accurate and updated schedule.

## MAH MBA CET 2026 Exam Pattern

The MAH MBA CET is a 200-question, 200-mark examination with a duration of 2.5 hours (150 minutes). There is no negative marking, encouraging candidates to attempt all questions. The exam is conducted entirely in English. The four sections are:

*   **Logical Reasoning:** 75 questions
*   **Abstract Reasoning:** 25 questions
*   **Quantitative Aptitude:** 50 questions
*   **Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension:** 50 questions

Each correct answer fetches one mark. Given the absence of negative marking, a strategic approach to attempting all questions is beneficial.

## MAH MBA CET 2026 Cutoffs

Admission to MBA/MMS programs through MAH MBA CET is highly competitive, especially for top institutions in Maharashtra. Cutoffs are typically expressed in percentiles and vary significantly based on the college, category (MH vs. All India), and program. For premier institutes like JBIMS, SIMSREE, and PUMBA, cutoffs can go as high as the 99th percentile and above for the All India category. For other reputable colleges, cutoffs might range from the 90th percentile upwards. It's important for candidates to aim for the highest possible score to secure admission to their preferred college.

For precise cutoff details from previous years, candidates can refer to the official CAP information released by the Maharashtra State CET Cell.

Aspiring students should start their preparation early, focusing on time management and accuracy to excel in this competitive examination.
