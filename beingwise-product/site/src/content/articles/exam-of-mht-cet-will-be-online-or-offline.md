# Exam of MHT CET will be online or offline?

The Maharashtra Common Entrance Test (MHT CET) is a crucial state-level examination for students aspiring to pursue undergraduate courses in engineering, pharmacy, and agriculture in Maharashtra. Understanding the format of this exam is key to effective preparation.

## Key takeaways

*   The MHT CET examination is conducted entirely online, as a computer-based test.
*   Candidates must appear at designated exam centres to take the online test.
*   The State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra State, is the official body responsible for conducting MHT CET.
*   There is no negative marking in the MHT CET, which is an important detail for test-takers.

## Understanding the MHT CET Exam Mode

According to official notifications, the MHT CET is an online examination. This means candidates will take the test on computers at specified examination centres. While the mode is online, it is not a remote proctored exam from home; presence at a physical centre is mandatory. This format ensures standardized testing conditions for all applicants.

## Important Dates and Application Process

For the 2022 admissions cycle, the application process for MHT CET began on February 10. The initial deadline to submit the application form without a late fee was March 31. Students who missed this date had an extended opportunity to apply with a late fee until April 7. Prospective candidates should always refer to the official MHT CET portal for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding application windows and other key dates. The exam dates for 2022 were not released at the time of the application window opening, but are typically announced on the official website.

## Subject Distribution and Marking

The MHT CET question paper is divided into two main sections:

1.  **Section 1: Physics and Chemistry**
    *   This section comprises 100 questions, with an equal distribution of 50 questions from Physics and 50 from Chemistry.
    *   Each correct answer in this section is awarded 1 mark.
    *   The questions for Physics and Chemistry are available in English, Marathi, and Urdu.

2.  **Section 2: Mathematics**
    *   This section contains 50 questions exclusively from Mathematics.
    *   Each correct answer in Mathematics carries 2 marks, indicating its higher weightage in the overall score.
    *   The questions for Mathematics are provided in English only.

An important aspect to remember is the absence of negative marking. This allows candidates to attempt all questions without the fear of losing marks for incorrect answers.

## Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the MHT CET, candidates must have successfully completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as compulsory subjects. Specific percentage requirements may vary for different courses and categories, and candidates should always verify these details on the official MHT CET information brochure or portal.

Staying informed about the official announcements from the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra State, is crucial for all MHT CET aspirants. Good luck with your preparation!
