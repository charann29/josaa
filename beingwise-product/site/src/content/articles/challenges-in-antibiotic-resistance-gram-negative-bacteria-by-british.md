# Challenges in Antibiotic Resistance: Gram Negative Bacteria

Understanding the complexities of antibiotic resistance, particularly concerning Gram-negative bacteria, is vital for anyone involved in healthcare or medical research. This course offers a focused look at this critical global health issue, equipping learners with essential knowledge.

## Key Takeaways

*   The course explores the mechanisms and impact of antimicrobial resistance in Gram-negative bacteria.
*   It covers the epidemiology and the significant burden these infections place on healthcare systems.
*   Learners will delve into effective antimicrobial therapy strategies, moving from theoretical knowledge to practical application through case studies.
*   The program is self-paced and delivered through a mix of video and text, requiring about three hours of study per week.

## Course Overview

This program, developed by the British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy (BSAC), is designed for self-study and delivered entirely in English. It provides a comprehensive understanding of infections caused by Gram-negative bacteria and the growing challenge of antibiotic resistance. The content is structured to guide you through the fundamental aspects of this topic, from an introduction to real-world case discussions.

## Learning Modules

The course is structured into weekly modules, each building on the previous one to provide a complete picture:

### Week 1: Infections Caused by Gram-Negative Bacteria

This introductory week sets the stage, welcoming you to the course and providing an overview of the challenges posed by Gram-negative bacterial infections.

### Week 2: Antimicrobial Resistance in Gram-Negative Bacteria

Here, you will explore the core concepts of resistance. Topics include:

*   The various mechanisms by which Gram-negative bacteria develop resistance to antibiotics.
*   The epidemiology of these bacteria, understanding how they spread and affect populations.
*   The significant burden and consequences that antimicrobial resistance imposes on public health and healthcare systems.

### Week 3: Antimicrobial Therapy for Gram-Negative Bacteria: From Bench to Bedside

This final week bridges theory with practice. You will revisit earlier case studies and engage with a new one, Case #3 (Vincent), to apply your knowledge of antimicrobial therapy strategies in clinical scenarios.
