## Course Overview and Structure

This program focuses on the complex mechanisms of digestion and metabolism in animals. It's designed for self-study, allowing learners to progress at their own speed through a combination of video lectures and supporting text.

The entire course is taught in English, ensuring clarity for a broad range of students. The learning materials are structured to provide a comprehensive understanding of how animals process food, absorb nutrients, and regulate their energy expenditure.

## Detailed Syllabus

The course is divided into several key lectures, each exploring a specific aspect of animal digestion and metabolism:

### Lecture 14: Digestion

This introductory module explores the fundamental processes of digestion, with a focus on how different macronutrients like lipids, proteins, and carbohydrates are broken down. It also covers the initial stages of fatty acid and protein metabolism.

### Lecture 15: Digestion and Absorption

Building on the previous module, this section delves into the mechanisms of nutrient absorption. Topics include the standing gradient hypothesis, the absorption of sugars, amino acids, fats, and fatty acids, and the role of cholesterol.

### Lecture 16: Optimizing Gut Function

This module examines how digestive tracts can be optimized for efficiency. It discusses strategies for enhancing digestion in various types of tubular digestive systems and the impact of food quality and mixed diets on gut health.

### Lecture 17: Control of Digestion & Appetite

Here, the course explores the regulatory mechanisms governing digestion and appetite. It covers the neurological and hormonal controls that influence digestive processes and manage food intake.

### Lecture 18: The Metabolic Rate

This section introduces the concept of metabolic rate and methods for its measurement. It covers techniques like indirect calorimetry and the doubly-labeled water method, along with the significance of the respiratory quotient.

### Lecture 19: Body Size and Metabolic Rate

This module investigates the relationship between an animal's body size and its metabolic rate. It explores various hypotheses, including statistical artifacts, elastic similarity, and fractal scaling, that attempt to explain this correlation.

### Lecture 20: Metabolic Lifestyle

The final core module examines how metabolic processes relate to an animal's lifestyle. Topics include energy budgets, their impact on fitness, aerobic scope, locomotory capacity, and how natural selection shapes metabolic lifestyles.
