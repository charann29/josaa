# Reinforced Cement Concrete Design by Skill Lync: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For civil engineering students and professionals looking to deepen their expertise in structural design, a specialized program in Reinforced Cement Concrete (RCC) design offers a robust learning pathway. This Skill Lync course focuses on the principles and practical application of RCC design, essential for modern construction projects.

## Key Takeaways

*   The program is delivered entirely online through self-paced video and text-based modules.
*   It covers a comprehensive syllabus, from fundamental material properties to advanced design principles for various structural elements.
*   Upon successful completion, participants receive a certificate from Skill Lync.
*   The total fee for this specialized program is ₹40,000.

## Course Overview and Structure

This online program provides an in-depth understanding of Reinforced Cement Concrete (RCC) design, adhering to industry standards and design codes. The curriculum is structured to guide learners through the complexities of structural systems, material properties, and design philosophies, culminating in practical application for various building components.

The course is delivered in a self-study format, allowing participants to learn at their own pace. All instructional materials are provided in English, through a combination of video lectures and supporting text resources.

## Detailed Syllabus

The program is typically structured over ten weeks, covering a wide array of topics crucial for RCC design:

### Week 1: Structural Systems and Material Properties

This introductory week lays the groundwork, discussing the objectives of structural design, various structural systems (like floor and framing systems), and relevant design codes. It delves into the properties of concrete, including mix proportions, compressive strength, stress-strain characteristics, and key phenomena like shrinkage and creep. Properties of reinforcing steel, such as stress-strain curves for different grades (Fe250, Fe415, Fe500), are also covered.

### Week 2: Design Philosophies and Beam Flexure (Analysis)

The focus shifts to different design philosophies: Working Stress Method (WSM), Ultimate Load Method (ULM), and Limit State Design (LSD). Learners explore serviceability and strength limit states, characteristic loads, safety factors, and material strengths. The week also introduces load distribution in building frames, one-way slabs, and the theory of flexure in beams, including concepts like the neutral axis and different failure modes (under-reinforced, over-reinforced, balanced sections).

### Week 3: One-Way and Two-Way Slabs

Building on the previous week, this module covers the structural analysis and design of one-way slabs, including reinforcement detailing. It then introduces two-way slabs, highlighting their differences from one-way slabs, analysis methods, effective span considerations, and design examples with detailing.

### Weeks 4-6: Beams in Flexure, Bond, and Development Length

These weeks extensively cover reinforced concrete beams. Topics include the moment of resistance, the concept of flanged beams, and the analysis of under-reinforced, over-reinforced, and flanged beams. The design of T-beams and doubly reinforced rectangular beams is also explored. Later in this section, bond strength, development length, and anchorage length—critical for ensuring proper load transfer in reinforced concrete—are discussed, along with reinforcement detailing for beams.

### Week 7: Shear Design

This module focuses on shear in beams, covering principal stresses, modes of cracking, shear transfer mechanisms, and the effect of reinforcement. It details nominal shear stress, critical sections for one-way shear, and shear strength with and without shear reinforcement. Design examples and detailing for shear reinforcement are included.

### Week 8: Torsion and Deep Beams

The program addresses torsion in structural members, differentiating between compatibility and equilibrium torsion. It covers torsional reinforcement provisions as per IS codes, detailing requirements, and design examples. An introduction to deep beams and their design is also provided.

### Weeks 9-10: Column Design

The final section of the course is dedicated to columns. It distinguishes between tied and spiral columns, discusses column loading, effective length (for braced and unbraced frames), and effective length as per IS 456. Slenderness limits for short and slender columns are covered, along with reinforcement detailing and the design strength of short columns under pure axial load.
