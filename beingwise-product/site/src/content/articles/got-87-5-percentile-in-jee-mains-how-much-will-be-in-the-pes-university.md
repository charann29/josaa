# I got 87.5 percentile in JEE Mains, how much will it be in PES University with a CSE course?

Achieving an 87.5 percentile in JEE Main is a solid effort, and it's natural to wonder about your chances for a Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) seat at a reputable institution like PES University. Let's explore what this percentile might mean for your admission prospects.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your JEE Main 87.5 percentile likely translates to a Common Rank List (CRL) rank in the range of 1.3 to 1.5 lakh.
*   Admission to CSE at PES University via the JEE Main channel is highly competitive, with past closing ranks significantly lower than your estimated rank.
*   PES University offers multiple admission pathways, including its own PESSAT exam, KCET (for Karnataka students), and a Management Quota.
*   Exploring alternative admission channels and other campuses might improve your chances of securing a CSE seat.

## Understanding Your JEE Main Percentile and Rank

Based on your 87.5 percentile in JEE Main, the National Testing Agency (NTA) methodology suggests your estimated Common Rank List (CRL) rank would likely fall between 1.3 lakh and 1.5 lakh. This figure can vary slightly each year depending on the total number of candidates who appear for the exam. This rank indicates your position relative to all other test-takers nationwide.

## PES University Admission Channels

PES University provides aspiring B.Tech students with several pathways to admission. Understanding these is crucial for planning your strategy:

*   **PESSAT (PES Scholastic Aptitude Test):** This is the university's own entrance examination. Performing well in PESSAT is often a primary route for many students.
*   **KCET (Karnataka Common Entrance Test):** For students who hold Karnataka domicile, KCET offers another significant admission channel.
*   **JEE Main:** This option is available for All India candidates, allowing them to use their JEE Main scores for admission consideration.
*   **Management Quota:** This channel offers direct admission, typically involving higher tuition fees, and is an option for those who might not meet the cutoffs through other competitive exams.

## Exploring Alternative Options and Strategies

Given the competitive landscape, it's wise to consider all available avenues to pursue your CSE dreams at PES University:

*   **Prioritize PESSAT:** If you haven't already, preparing for and appearing in the PESSAT exam could significantly improve your chances. This is often the most direct route for many students aiming for PES University.
*   **Consider KCET (if eligible):** For students with Karnataka domicile, the KCET exam is a strong alternative that could offer a better opportunity for admission.
*   **Explore Other Campuses:** PES University has multiple campuses, such as the Electronic City campus. While the main campus is highly competitive, other campuses might have slightly different cutoff trends, making them a viable option for CSE.
*   **Management Quota:** As a last resort, the management quota is available for direct admission. However, be aware that this pathway typically involves a higher fee structure.

To maximize your chances, stay regularly updated by checking the official PES University website for the latest admission announcements, deadlines, and specific requirements for each channel.
