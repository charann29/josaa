# IIMC Delhi Cut off 2025: ADPR for OBC NCL Category

For students from the OBC Non-Creamy Layer (NCL) category aspiring to join the Indian Institute of Mass Communication (IIMC) Delhi, understanding past admission trends is crucial. This guide focuses on the Advertising and Public Relations (ADPR) program, offering insights into what to expect for the 2025 admissions.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIMC Delhi's ADPR program saw OBC-NCL closing ranks range from 63 to 832 in 2024 across various admission rounds.
*   The final OBC-NCL candidate admitted to ADPR in 2024 had a rank of approximately 832.
*   For a strong chance of admission in 2025, OBC-NCL candidates should aim for a rank well below 800.
*   Admission cut-offs are dynamic and depend on factors like applicant numbers and seat availability each year.

## Understanding IIMC Delhi Admissions

IIMC Delhi is a premier institution for media and communication studies in India. Admission to its various postgraduate diploma programs, including Advertising and Public Relations, is highly competitive. The institute considers various factors, but entrance exam performance leading to a good rank is paramount, especially for reserved categories like OBC-NCL.

## ADPR Cut-off Trends for OBC NCL (2024 Data)

In the 2024 admission cycle, the cut-off ranks for the Advertising and Public Relations (ADPR) program at IIMC Delhi for candidates under the OBC Non-Creamy Layer (NCL) category showed a specific pattern. Across all rounds of admissions, the opening rank for an OBC-NCL candidate was around 63, while the closing rank reached approximately 832. This indicates that an OBC-NCL candidate with a rank as high as 832 was able to secure a seat in the ADPR program in 2024.

## Comparison with Other Programs

While ADPR is a popular choice, it's helpful to see how its cut-offs compare with other programs at IIMC Delhi. For example, other postgraduate diploma courses like Journalism (English), Journalism (Hindi), Digital Media, and Radio & TV also had closing ranks for OBC-NCL candidates predominantly falling within the 200 to 1,000 range in 2024. This suggests a consistent level of competition across many of IIMC Delhi's offerings for this category.

## Predicting 2025 Cut-offs and Preparation Strategy

It's important to remember that cut-off ranks can fluctuate each year. Factors such as the total number of applicants, the difficulty level of the entrance examination, and the number of seats available for the OBC-NCL category can influence these figures. While the 2024 data provides a strong indicator, it's always wise to aim for a better performance. For OBC-NCL candidates targeting the ADPR program in 2025, securing a rank significantly below 800 would place you in a much safer position for admission.

Aspiring students should focus on thorough preparation for the entrance examination, understanding that a strong performance will open doors to this prestigious institution.
