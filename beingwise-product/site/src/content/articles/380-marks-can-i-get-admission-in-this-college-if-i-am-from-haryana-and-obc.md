# With 380 Marks, Can I Get Admission to Army College of Dental Sciences (ACDS)?

It's understandable to be curious about your chances of admission to a specific college, especially after taking a competitive exam like NEET. Let's explore your prospects for the Army College of Dental Sciences (ACDS) with your NEET score of 380.

## Key Takeaways

*   ACDS offers undergraduate (BDS) and postgraduate (MDS) dental programs.
*   Admission to ACDS for BDS is solely based on NEET merit.
*   A NEET score of 380 is likely to place you in a rank range that makes admission to ACDS very challenging.
*   While cut-offs vary annually, historical data suggests a significantly higher rank is typically required.

## Understanding ACDS Admissions

The Army College of Dental Sciences (ACDS) is a well-regarded institution offering dental education. For its Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) program, admission is determined entirely by a candidate's performance in the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET). This means your NEET score and the corresponding rank are the primary factors considered for entry.

## NEET Score vs. Predicted Rank

A NEET score of 380 is a starting point, but what truly matters for college admissions is your All India Rank. Based on typical NEET score distributions, a score of 380 generally corresponds to an overall predicted percentile in the range of 85.97 to 86.47. This percentile often translates to an estimated All India Rank somewhere between 212,050 and 252,200. It's important to remember that these are predictions, and the actual rank can vary slightly based on the performance of all candidates in a given year.

## ACDS Cut-off Trends

To gauge your chances, it's helpful to look at past admission trends for ACDS. For the BDS program, the closing ranks have historically been much lower than the rank predicted for a score of 380. For instance, the closing rank for BDS at ACDS was approximately 41,646 in 2018 and 56,448 in 2019. These figures indicate that successful candidates typically achieve significantly higher ranks in NEET.

## Impact of Category and Army Background

You mentioned being from Haryana, belonging to the OBC category, and your father being in the army. While ACDS is specifically for the dependents of serving and retired Army personnel, the admission process still prioritizes NEET merit. Your army background is crucial for eligibility, but within the eligible pool, your NEET rank is the deciding factor. The OBC category reservation applies to government colleges and all-India quotas, but ACDS has its specific admission criteria primarily focused on the army ward status and NEET performance.

## What This Means for Your Admission Prospects

Considering the predicted rank range for a NEET score of 380 and the historical closing ranks for ACDS, gaining admission to the BDS program at this college would be extremely challenging. While cut-offs do fluctuate annually due to various factors like the number of applicants, exam difficulty, and seat availability, a substantial improvement in rank is typically needed to secure a seat at ACDS.

It's always wise to explore all your options and consider other colleges that might align better with your predicted NEET rank. You can use official counselling portals once your NEET results are out to get a clearer picture of your possibilities.
