# I've Paid for KCET, But My Application Shows I Haven't: What to Do?

It can be quite stressful to pay for your KCET application only to find the system still shows an outstanding payment. Don't worry, this is a common technical glitch, and there are clear steps you can take to resolve it.

## Key Takeaways

*   Always verify if the payment amount has actually left your bank account.
*   System updates can take a few hours; patience is key before escalating.
*   Keep all payment proofs handy for any communication with KEA.
*   Contacting the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) directly is the final step if the issue persists.

## Verify Your Bank Transaction

The very first thing to do is confirm whether the payment was successfully debited from your bank account. Check your bank statement, transaction history in your banking app, or SMS notifications from your bank. Sometimes, a payment might appear to go through but could have failed at the bank's end, or it might be pending. If the amount hasn't been debited, you might need to attempt the payment again.

## Allow Time for System Updates

Payment gateways and application portals don't always update instantaneously. It's quite common for the system to take a few hours, or even up to 24 hours, to process and reflect a successful payment. Before panicking, give it some time. Try checking the portal again after a few hours have passed.

## Re-Login and Clear Cache

A simple technical refresh can often solve these kinds of problems. Log out of your KCET application portal completely. Clear your browser's cache and cookies, then close the browser. Re-open your browser and log back into your KCET account. This often forces the system to refresh your status and might resolve the payment display issue.

## Check for KEA Confirmation

After making a payment, the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) typically sends a confirmation via email or SMS. Check your registered email inbox (including spam/junk folders) and your phone's SMS messages for any communication from KEA regarding your payment status. A successful confirmation message from KEA is a strong indicator that your payment has been received, even if the portal hasn't updated yet.

## Contact the KEA Helpline

If you've followed all the above steps and the issue still persists after a reasonable waiting period (e.g., 24 hours), it's time to contact the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) directly. Have your application number, transaction ID, and proof of payment (like a bank statement screenshot or transaction receipt) ready. They have dedicated helplines and support channels to assist with such technical payment discrepancies. They will be able to verify your payment on their end and help update your application status.

Resolving payment issues promptly ensures you can complete your KCET application without any further delays.
