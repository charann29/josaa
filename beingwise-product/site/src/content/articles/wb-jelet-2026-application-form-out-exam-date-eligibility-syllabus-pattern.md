# WB JELET 2026: Application Form (Out), Exam Date, Eligibility, Syllabus, Pattern

For students aspiring to secure lateral entry into the second year of B.E./B.Tech/B.Pharm programs in West Bengal, the Joint Entrance Lateral Entry Test (JELET) 2026 is a crucial examination. This guide provides a detailed overview of the exam pattern and other essential information to help you prepare effectively.

## Key Takeaways

*   The WB JELET 2026 is an offline, OMR-based examination lasting two hours.
*   It features a single paper with 100 multiple-choice questions, totaling 120 marks.
*   The question paper is exclusively in English.
*   There's a negative marking for incorrect answers in Category 1 questions.

## Understanding the JELET 2026 Exam Pattern

The West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board (WBJEEB) has officially released the JELET 2026 exam pattern. Familiarizing yourself with this structure is vital for strategic preparation. The test is designed to assess candidates' foundational knowledge relevant to engineering and technology disciplines.

### General Exam Structure

The JELET 2026 will follow these general parameters:

*   **Mode of Exam:** Offline (OMR-based)
*   **Duration:** 2 hours
*   **Number of Papers:** One
*   **Question Type:** Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), with each question offering four options, only one of which is correct for Category 1 questions.
*   **Total Marks:** 120
*   **Language:** English only

The distribution of questions and marks varies slightly depending on whether you are a diploma holder in Engineering/Technology or a B.Sc. graduate. The exam covers core subjects, with a particular focus on Mathematics for B.Sc. degree holders.

## JELET 2026 Marking Scheme

The marking scheme for JELET 2026 is divided into two categories, and it's crucial to understand the nuances of each to maximize your score.

### Category 1 Marking

*   **Correct Answer:** Awards 1 mark.
*   **Incorrect Answer:** Results in a deduction of ¼ (0.25) marks.
*   **Multiple Options Selected:** If more than one option is marked, even if one is correct, it will be treated as incorrect, leading to a -¼ mark deduction.
*   **Unattempted Question:** No marks are awarded or deducted.

### Category 2 Marking

Category 2 questions can have one or more correct options. These questions are designed to test a deeper understanding.

*   **All Correct Options Marked:** Awards 2 marks.
*   **Any Incorrect Option Marked (even with correct ones):** Yields 0 marks.
*   **Partially Correct Answer:** If not all correct options are marked, but no incorrect options are marked, marks are awarded proportionally: `2 * (number of correct options marked) / (total number of actually correct options)`.
*   **Unattempted Question:** No marks are awarded or deducted.

Understanding these details from the official WBJEEB information will significantly aid your preparation for WB JELET 2026.
