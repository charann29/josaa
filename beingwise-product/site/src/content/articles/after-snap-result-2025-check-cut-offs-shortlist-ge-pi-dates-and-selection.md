The declaration of SNAP results marks a significant step for MBA aspirants aiming for Symbiosis International University (SIU) institutes. Now, the focus shifts to the subsequent stages of the admission process.

## Key takeaways

*   Your SNAP score is crucial for being shortlisted for the Group Exercise (GE) and Personal Interaction (PI) rounds.
*   Each SIU institute will individually release its shortlist and schedule for the GE-PI-WAT (Writing Ability Test) process.
*   Preparation for GE-PI-WAT is vital, as these rounds carry significant weight in the final selection.
*   If you're not satisfied with your SNAP score, consider other MBA entrance exams or reappearing for SNAP next year.

## Understanding the SNAP Selection Process

The SNAP exam is the initial filter for admission to MBA programs at various SIU constituent institutes. Once results are out, candidates must prepare for the mandatory personality assessment rounds, which include Group Exercise (GE), Personal Interaction (PI), and a Writing Ability Test (WAT). Selection for these rounds is based on your SNAP exam score.

Each participating SIU college will release its merit list for the GE/PI/WAT individually, typically in February, along with specific dates and venues. It's important to monitor the official websites of the institutes you've applied to for these updates.

## SIBM Pune Shortlist and GE-PI Dates

SIU institutes will announce their PI shortlist and corresponding schedules independently. Candidates who selected specific colleges during their SNAP application process are eligible to apply for these PI rounds. While the exact dates may vary slightly between institutes, the general timeline for SIBM Pune's shortlisting and selection process provides a good reference for other SIU institutes:

| SNAP Admission Events                                            | Tentative Dates                 |
| :--------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------ |
| Announcement of Shortlist for Group Exercise and Personal Interaction | First week of February, 2026    |
| Group Exercise, Personal Interaction                             | Third to Last week of February, 2026 |
| Announcement of First Merit List                                 | Second week of March, 2026      |
| Last date for fee payment for candidates in the First Merit List | Last week of March, 2026        |
| Commencement of Classes                                          | First week of July, 2026        |

## Preparing for GE-PI-WAT Rounds

The Group Exercise, Personal Interaction, and Writing Ability Test are critical components of the SIU admission process. These rounds assess your communication skills, leadership potential, analytical abilities, and overall suitability for an MBA program. Start preparing by practicing group discussions, mock interviews, and honing your essay writing skills on current affairs and business topics.

## SNAP Cut-Offs 2025 (Expected)

SNAP cut-offs are not released with the results. Instead, each SNAP-accepting college publishes its admission cut-offs for PI shortlisting about 15-20 days after the results are declared. These cut-offs can vary significantly between institutes based on factors like the number of applicants, the difficulty level of the exam, and the number of available seats.

## Exploring Other Options

If your SNAP score isn't what you hoped for, or if you wish to broaden your options, consider appearing for other prominent MBA entrance exams. Many universities and colleges accept scores from exams like CAT, XAT, NMAT, or CMAT. Alternatively, you might consider taking the SNAP exam again next year, giving yourself more time to prepare and aim for a higher score.

The period after SNAP results is a crucial time for strategic decision-making and focused preparation for the next stages of your MBA journey.
