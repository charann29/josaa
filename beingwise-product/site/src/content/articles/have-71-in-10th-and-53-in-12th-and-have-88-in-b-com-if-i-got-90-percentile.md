# I have 71% in 10th and 53% in 12th and have 88% in B.Com. If I got 90 percentile in CAT, could I get SJMSOM IIT Mumbai or IIT Delhi or any IIT? I am a SC student.

It's great that you're aiming for top institutions like SJMSOM, IIT Bombay, and IIT Delhi for your MBA. Let's break down your profile and explore your chances, keeping your academic record and SC category in mind.

## Key Takeaways

*   A 90 percentile in CAT, while good, generally falls short of the typical cut-offs for SJMSOM IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi, even for SC candidates.
*   Past academic performance (10th and 12th grades) is a significant factor in the admission process for many top B-schools, including IITs.
*   Your strong B.Com score (88%) is a definite plus, showing recent academic excellence.
*   Several other highly reputable B-schools, including newer IIMs and other IIT DoMS, might be within reach with a 90 percentile.

## Understanding IIT B-School Admissions

IITs offering MBA programs, such as Shailesh J. Mehta School of Management (SJMSOM) at IIT Bombay and Department of Management Studies (DoMS) at IIT Delhi, are highly sought after. Their admission process typically considers several factors beyond just the CAT percentile:

1.  **CAT Percentile:** This is usually the first filter, with high cut-offs.
2.  **Academic Profile:** Marks in 10th, 12th, and graduation are crucial. Consistent academic performance is often preferred.
3.  **Work Experience:** While not always mandatory, relevant work experience can strengthen your application.
4.  **Diversity Factors:** Gender and academic diversity are sometimes considered.
5.  **Personal Interview (PI) & Written Ability Test (WAT):** Shortlisted candidates undergo these rounds.

## CAT Percentile for SJMSOM and IIT Delhi

Based on past admission trends, the CAT percentile cut-offs for SJMSOM IIT Bombay and DoMS IIT Delhi are typically very high. For the General category, these often range from 98-99 percentile. While there is a reservation for SC candidates, the cut-offs, though lower, are still competitive. For SJMSOM and DoMS IIT Delhi, SC category cut-offs generally hover around the 92-93 percentile mark or even higher in some years.

With a 90 percentile, securing a call from these specific IITs might be challenging, especially considering your 12th-grade score.

## Impact of Academic Scores

Your academic record plays a vital role. While your 88% in B.Com is excellent and demonstrates recent strong performance, your 10th (71%) and particularly 12th (53%) scores might affect your composite score in the initial shortlisting stage for some top institutions. Many B-schools assign weightage to these past academic performances. However, your SC category does provide some relaxation in these criteria, but the overall competition remains high.

## Exploring Other Excellent Options

Don't be disheartened! A 90 percentile in CAT, combined with your SC category and strong graduation score, opens doors to many other reputable MBA/PGDM programs across India. Here are some institutions you could consider, which have historically accepted candidates in a similar CAT percentile range for the SC category:

*   **Newer IIMs:**
    *   IIM Visakhapatnam
    *   IIM Nagpur
    *   IIM Sambalpur
    *   IIM Sirmaur
    *   IIM Jammu
    *   IIM Amritsar
*   **Other IIT DoMS:**
    *   DoMS, IIT Roorkee
    *   DoMS, IIT ISM Dhanbad
*   **Other Reputable B-Schools:**
    *   IISWBM Kolkata (Indian Institute of Social Welfare and Business Management)
    *   Great Lakes Institute of Management, Chennai (often accepts CAT scores, check their specific cut-offs)

These institutions offer quality management education and excellent career prospects. It's always advisable to check their specific admission policies and past year cut-offs for your category on their official websites.

## Next Steps

Even if the top IITs are a stretch, remember that your strong B.Com score and SC category are significant advantages. Research the specific admission criteria for each B-school you are interested in, paying close attention to academic weightages and category-wise cut-offs. Prepare thoroughly for the Personal Interview and Written Ability Test rounds, as these can significantly boost your chances once you are shortlisted.

Best of luck with your applications!
