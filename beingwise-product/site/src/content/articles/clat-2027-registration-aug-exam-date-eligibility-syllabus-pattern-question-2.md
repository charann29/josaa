# CLAT 2027: Registration (Aug), Exam Date, Eligibility, Syllabus, Pattern, Question Paper, Preparation, Results

The Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) is a crucial national-level entrance examination for aspiring law students in India. It serves as the gateway for admissions to undergraduate (BA LLB, BCom LLB, BSc LLB, BBA LLB, etc.) and postgraduate (LLM) law programs offered by 25 National Law Universities (NLUs) and numerous other private institutions across the country.

## Key takeaways

*   CLAT 2027 registration is expected to begin on August 1, 2026, following the release of the official notification.
*   The examination is tentatively scheduled for December 6, 2026, and will be conducted offline.
*   The Consortium of NLUs is the conducting body for CLAT, managing all aspects from application to counselling.
*   Preparation requires a thorough understanding of the syllabus, consistent practice with mock tests, and effective time management.

## Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for CLAT 2027, candidates must meet specific academic qualifications. For undergraduate programs, applicants must have completed their 10+2 education or an equivalent examination with a minimum percentage of marks (typically 45% for General/OBC/PWD/NRI/PIO/OCI categories and 40% for SC/ST categories). There is no upper age limit for appearing in CLAT.

For postgraduate programs, candidates need an LLB degree or an equivalent examination with a minimum of 50% marks (45% for SC/ST categories).

## Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The CLAT examination tests candidates on various sections to assess their aptitude for legal education. Understanding the syllabus and pattern is fundamental for effective preparation.

### CLAT UG Syllabus

The undergraduate CLAT exam typically covers five main sections:

*   **English Language:** Comprehension passages, vocabulary, grammar, and inference skills.
*   **Current Affairs, including General Knowledge:** National and international current events, historical events, art and culture, economics, and science.
*   **Legal Reasoning:** Principles of law, legal propositions, and application of legal principles to factual scenarios.
*   **Logical Reasoning:** Critical reasoning, analytical reasoning, and logical deductions.
*   **Quantitative Techniques:** Basic mathematics, data interpretation, and numerical ability up to Class 10 level.

### CLAT UG Exam Pattern

*   **Total Questions:** 120 multiple-choice questions.
*   **Marking Scheme:** +1 for each correct answer, -0.25 for each incorrect answer.
*   **Duration:** 2 hours.
*   **Mode:** Offline.

### CLAT PG Syllabus

The postgraduate CLAT exam focuses on various core law subjects, including Constitutional Law, Jurisprudence, Administrative Law, Law of Contract, Torts, Family Law, Criminal Law, Property Law, Company Law, Public International Law, Tax Law, and Environmental Law.

### CLAT PG Exam Pattern

*   **Total Questions:** 120 multiple-choice questions.
*   **Marking Scheme:** +1 for each correct answer, -0.25 for each incorrect answer.
*   **Duration:** 2 hours.
*   **Mode:** Offline.

## Preparation Strategy

Cracking CLAT requires a well-structured preparation plan and consistent effort. Here are some key tips:

*   **Understand the Syllabus:** Go through the detailed syllabus published by the Consortium of NLUs and identify your strong and weak areas.
*   **Choose the Right Study Material:** Select reputable books and resources that cover all sections comprehensively.
*   **Practice Time Management:** The exam is time-bound, so practice solving questions within strict time limits.
*   **Attempt Mock Tests:** Regularly take full-length mock tests to familiarize yourself with the exam environment, identify areas for improvement, and enhance speed and accuracy.
*   **Analyze Previous Year Papers:** Reviewing past CLAT question papers provides valuable insights into the exam pattern, question types, and difficulty level.
*   **Stay Updated with Current Affairs:** Read newspapers and magazines daily to keep abreast of national and international events.

CLAT is a challenging but rewarding examination that opens doors to a prestigious career in law. With diligent preparation and a strategic approach, you can achieve your goal of securing admission to a top National Law University.
