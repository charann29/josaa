# NDA Exam 2026: NDA 1 Exam (April 12), Analysis (Soon), Admit Card (OUT), Answer Key, Question Papers & Prep Tips

The National Defence Academy (NDA) examination is a highly sought-after gateway for aspiring young men and women to join the prestigious Army, Navy, and Air Force wings. Conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC), this exam opens doors to a distinguished career in the Indian armed forces.

## Key Takeaways

*   **NDA 1 2026 Exam Date:** The NDA 1 2026 examination is scheduled for April 12, 2026, across various centres nationwide.
*   **Admit Card Release:** The admit cards for NDA 1 2026 have been officially released by UPSC and are essential for entry to the examination hall.
*   **Upcoming Courses:** Successful candidates from NDA 1 2026 will be eligible for the 157th Course at NDA and the 119th Indian Naval Academy Course (INAC), both commencing on January 1, 2027.
*   **NDA 2 2026 Dates:** The notification for NDA 2 2026 is expected on May 20, with applications closing on June 9, and the exam slated for September 14, 2026.

## NDA 1 2026 Admit Card: Your Gateway to the Exam

The UPSC has made the admit cards for NDA 1 2026 available for download. It is absolutely mandatory for all registered candidates to download and print their e-admit cards, as entry to the examination hall will not be permitted without this document. Alongside the admit card, candidates must carry a valid photo identity proof, the number of which should match the one mentioned on their e-admit card.

### How to Download Your NDA Admit Card 2026

Follow these steps to access your admit card:

1.  Visit the official UPSC website at upsc.gov.in.
2.  Look for the link titled 'E-admit card National Defence Academy Examination' on the homepage.
3.  Clicking this link will open a new page with login options.
4.  Carefully read all instructions before proceeding to download.
5.  Agree to the terms and conditions presented.
6.  Choose your preferred login method: either using your registration number or your roll number.
7.  Click the 'Submit' button.
8.  Your NDA admit card will then be displayed on the screen.
9.  Download the admit card and ensure you take a clear printout.

## Understanding the NDA Exam Schedule 2026

The NDA examination is conducted twice a year by UPSC. Here’s a summary of the key dates for 2026:

*   **NDA 1 2026 Notification Release:** The notification was released earlier, and the application window closed on December 30, 2025.
*   **NDA 1 2026 Exam Date:** April 12, 2026.
*   **NDA 2 2026 Notification Release:** May 20, 2026.
*   **NDA 2 2026 Application Deadline:** June 9, 2026.
*   **NDA 2 2026 Exam Date:** September 14, 2026.

Candidates should mark these dates carefully and plan their preparation accordingly.

## NDA Exam Centres

UPSC provides a comprehensive list of examination centres with the official notification. Candidates select their preferred centre during the application process. It's important to note that centres are allotted on a first-come, first-served basis, and once a centre reaches its capacity, it will no longer be available. The exact address of your allotted centre will be clearly mentioned on your admit card. Remember to carry a photo ID along with your admit card to the centre.

## Preparation Tips for NDA Aspirants

Preparing for the NDA exam requires dedication and a strategic approach. Here are some general tips:

*   **Understand the Syllabus and Exam Pattern:** Familiarize yourself with the detailed syllabus for Mathematics and General Ability Test (GAT), including English, General Knowledge, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Know the marking scheme and time limits.
*   **Solve Previous Year Papers:** Practicing past question papers is invaluable. It helps you understand the exam format, identify important topics, and improve time management.
*   **Focus on Fundamentals:** Build a strong foundation in core subjects. For Mathematics, ensure clarity in concepts from Classes 10-12. For GAT, stay updated with current affairs and strengthen your general science knowledge.
*   **Regular Revision:** Consistent revision is key to retaining information. Create a study schedule and stick to it.
*   **Mock Tests:** Regularly take mock tests to simulate exam conditions, identify your weak areas, and refine your test-taking strategy.
