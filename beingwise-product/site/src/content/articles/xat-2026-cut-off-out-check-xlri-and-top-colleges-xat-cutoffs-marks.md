# XAT 2026 Cut off (OUT): Check XLRI & Top Colleges XAT Cutoffs Marks

Understanding the XAT cut-offs is crucial for any student aiming for admission to XLRI Jamshedpur or other prestigious management institutes. These cut-offs represent the minimum percentile scores required to be considered for the next stages of the admission process.

## Key takeaways

*   XAT cut-offs for XLRI's Business Management (BM) and Human Resource Management (HRM) programs vary based on an applicant's academic background (engineering vs. non-engineering) and gender.
*   For the BM program, engineering and non-engineering candidates typically face similar overall percentile cut-offs, while for the HRM program, non-engineering candidates often have a slightly lower cut-off.
*   Beyond the overall percentile, XLRI also implements sectional cut-offs for each part of the XAT exam: Verbal Ability & Logical Reasoning (VALR), Decision Making (DM), and Quantitative Aptitude & Data Interpretation (QADI).
*   Many other top B-schools across India also accept XAT scores, each with their own specific cut-off criteria.

## Factors Determining XAT Cut-Offs

The XAT cut-off percentiles are not fixed and are influenced by several dynamic factors each year. These factors help ensure a diverse and capable incoming class for MBA programs. Key determinants include:

*   **Number of Applicants:** A higher number of candidates appearing for the XAT exam can sometimes lead to an increase in cut-offs.
*   **Exam Difficulty:** The overall difficulty level of the XAT question paper in a given year plays a significant role. A tougher paper might result in slightly lower cut-offs.
*   **Available Seats:** The total number of seats offered for admission through XAT at a particular institute impacts the cut-offs.
*   **Candidate Performance:** The average and highest scores achieved by candidates in that specific year's XAT exam are crucial.
*   **Previous Trends:** Past years' cut-off trends are often considered to project current year cut-offs.
*   **Diversity Considerations:** To foster a diverse student body, institutes like XLRI often set different cut-offs based on gender and academic background (engineering vs. non-engineering).
