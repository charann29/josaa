# I have studied Hindi as language subject in my 10th standard. But actually I am weak in Hindi. Whereas I am more stronger in Tamil(which I studied upto my 8th standard). So, could I choose Tamil as my compulsory Indian language in UPSC exam?

It's a common concern for UPSC aspirants to navigate the language requirements, especially when their school language background differs from their actual proficiency. Let's clarify how you can choose your compulsory Indian language for the UPSC Civil Services Mains Examination.

## Key Takeaways

*   You are not restricted to the language you studied in Class 10, 11, or 12.
*   You can choose any language listed in the Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution as your compulsory Indian language.
*   It's highly advisable to select a language you are proficient in, allowing you to focus on other crucial General Studies papers.
*   Tamil is an officially recognized Scheduled Language, making it a valid choice for the UPSC Mains.

## Understanding the Compulsory Indian Language Paper

The UPSC Civil Services Mains Examination includes a compulsory Indian Language paper. This paper is qualifying in nature, meaning its marks are not counted towards your final ranking, but you must achieve a minimum score to pass the Mains exam. The primary purpose of this paper is to assess your ability to read, understand, and express yourself in an Indian language.

## Your Choice of Language

The good news is that the UPSC does not mandate that you choose the language you studied in your 10th, 11th, or 12th standard. The official UPSC guidelines permit candidates to select any of the languages included in the Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution. This schedule lists 22 languages, all of which are valid options for this compulsory paper.

Given your situation, where you feel stronger in Tamil despite studying Hindi in Class 10, you absolutely have the flexibility to choose Tamil. Tamil is indeed one of the languages listed in the Eighth Schedule, making it a perfectly acceptable choice for your compulsory Indian language paper in the UPSC Mains.

## Why Proficiency Matters

While you have the freedom to choose, it's strategically wise to opt for a language in which you are genuinely strong. The compulsory Indian language paper, though qualifying, still requires dedicated preparation. If you choose a language you are not fluent in or have to learn from scratch, it will demand a significant amount of your valuable study time. This could potentially divert your focus and energy away from other critical papers, such as the General Studies papers and your optional subject, which directly contribute to your final merit score.

Since you are confident in your Tamil skills, having studied it up to Class 8, and presumably maintaining some level of proficiency, choosing Tamil would be a sensible decision. It allows you to leverage your existing knowledge, potentially requiring less intensive preparation compared to trying to master Hindi or an entirely new language for the exam.

In conclusion, feel confident in choosing Tamil as your compulsory Indian language for the UPSC exam, as it aligns with both official rules and a smart preparation strategy.
