# NEET 2026 Registration: Over 26 Lakh Candidates Applied

NEET UG 2026 registration has now concluded. Reports suggest a significant interest, with approximately 26 lakh candidates having applied for this crucial medical entrance examination. The National Testing Agency (NTA) is expected to release the official figures for registered candidates along with the examination results.

## Key Takeaways

*   **High Participation:** Around 26 lakh aspirants reportedly registered for NEET UG 2026, indicating continued strong interest in medical careers.
*   **Registration Period:** The application window for NEET UG 2026 was open from February 8 to March 11, 2026.
*   **Correction Window:** A brief correction window was provided by the NTA from March 12 to March 14, 2026, allowing candidates to amend their submitted forms.
*   **Official Data Pending:** The final, verified count of registered candidates will be officially announced by the NTA.

## Understanding the Application Process

The NEET UG 2026 application process was entirely online, managed through the official NTA NEET portal. Prospective medical students were required to complete several steps: first, an initial registration, followed by filling out the detailed online application form. Crucially, candidates also had to upload necessary documents and pay the application fees. A notable requirement this year was the live photo capture feature, ensuring the authenticity of applicant images directly during the form submission.

## The Application Correction Window

Following the close of the initial registration period, the NTA provided a dedicated correction window for NEET UG 2026. This facility, available from March 12 to March 14, 2026, was a vital opportunity for candidates to review and correct any errors in their submitted application forms. It allowed for modifications to various fields, ensuring that all details were accurate before the final processing of applications.

## Number of Registered Candidates: A Growing Trend

Each year, the number of students appearing for NEET UG demonstrates the increasing aspiration for medical education in India. While the official count for NEET 2026 is still awaited from the NTA, the reported figure of approximately 26 lakh candidates suggests a continued upward trend in participation. NEET remains the largest medical entrance examination, attracting a vast pool of talent from across the country. The NTA typically releases comprehensive data, including category-wise breakdowns of registrations, along with the exam results. For reference, the NEET 2024 examination saw over 23 lakh registrations, which was the highest at that time.
