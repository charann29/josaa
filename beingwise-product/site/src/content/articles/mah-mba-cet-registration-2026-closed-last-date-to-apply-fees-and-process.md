# MAH MBA CET Registration 2026 (Closed): Last Date to Apply, Fees & Process

The registration window for MAH MBA CET 2026 has now closed. This page provides a comprehensive overview of the application process, key dates, and essential information for those who applied or are planning for future cycles.

## Key Takeaways

*   The application period for MAH MBA CET 2026 concluded on February 25, 2026, following an extension from the initial February 12 deadline.
*   A form correction window was available from February 27 to March 2, 2026, allowing limited edits to application details.
*   Applicants were required to complete Aadhaar and APAAR verification as part of the online registration process.
*   The MAH MBA CET score is accepted by over 300 MBA colleges across Maharashtra.

## Understanding the MAH MBA CET 2026 Application Process

The application for MAH MBA CET 2026 was entirely online. Candidates needed to register on the official State Common Entrance Test Cell portal and complete a multi-step process, including personal details, domicile information, and category selection. A crucial step involved digital authentication through Aadhaar and APAAR IDs to access and submit the application form.

### How to Register (for future reference)

1.  Visit the official CET Cell portal at cetcell.mahacet.org.
2.  Navigate to the 'CET examination portal AY 2026-27' section.
3.  Click on the 'Register' option to begin.
4.  Enter basic information to create a unique login.
5.  Complete the mandatory APAAR and Aadhaar authentication.
6.  Once authenticated, access the application dashboard.
7.  Select the 'MBA/MMS CET 2026' tab.
8.  Initiate 'New Registration' and fill in personal details, contact information, and address.
9.  Set a password and save the details to proceed.
10. Provide the APAAR ID linked with your DigiLocker account for final registration steps.
11. Complete all sections regarding personal information, domicile status, and category.

## MAH MBA CET Application Fee 2026

The application fee for MAH MBA CET 2026 varied based on the candidate's category and domicile status. It's important to note that the fee, once paid, was non-refundable. Therefore, applicants were advised to ensure all details were accurate before payment.

## Critical Points for a Smooth Application

To avoid complications, candidates needed to be mindful of several instructions during the application process:

*   **Single Application:** Only one application per candidate was accepted. Submitting multiple forms could lead to rejection.
*   **Preserve Records:** Keeping a printout of the completed application form, including fee payment details and the e-receipt, was essential for future reference.
*   **Accuracy is Key:** Many fields in the application form were not editable after submission, even during the correction window. Errors could lead to rejection without a fee refund.
*   **Web Browser:** For an optimal experience, it was recommended to use specific web browsers such as Internet Explorer (version 11+), Mozilla Firefox (versions 50-61), or Google Chrome (versions 50-69). Using smartphones for application submission was not advised.
*   **Exam Centre Preference:** Choosing a home city or the closest convenient location as the first preference for the exam centre was a practical tip.
