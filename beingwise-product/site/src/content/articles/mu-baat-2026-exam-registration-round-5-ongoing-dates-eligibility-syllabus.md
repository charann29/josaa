# MU BAAT 2026 Exam: Registration Round 5 (Ongoing), Dates, Eligibility, Syllabus, Results

The Masters' Union Business Aptitude and Admissions Test (MU BAAT) is a crucial entrance exam for students aiming to join the Post Graduate Programme in Technology and Business Management (PGP-TBM) at Masters' Union. Registration for Round 5 of the 2026 exam is currently open, offering a final opportunity for aspiring candidates.

## Key takeaways

*   MU BAAT is an essential part of the application for Masters' Union's PGP-TBM program.
*   Round 5 registration for MU BAAT 2026 is ongoing, with the last date to apply being May 3, 2026.
*   The exam evaluates candidates on Data Interpretation & Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, Verbal Skills, and a Caselet Assessment.
*   Applicants can also submit valid GMAT, GRE, or CAT scores, though MU BAAT is mandatory for all.

## What is MU-BAAT?

The Masters' Union - Business Aptitude and Admissions Test (MU-BAAT) is an admissions examination conducted by Masters' Union School of Business, located in Gurgaon. It serves as a mandatory component for all applicants seeking admission to their flagship Post Graduate Programme in Technology and Business Management (PGP-TBM).

The score obtained in MU-BAAT is used to shortlist candidates for the subsequent Personal Interview rounds. While MU-BAAT is compulsory, the admissions panel also considers valid GMAT, GRE, or CAT scores if applicants choose to submit them, providing additional insight into their academic capabilities.

## MU-BAAT Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for MU-BAAT and the PGP-TBM program, candidates must meet specific requirements. It's important to complete the online application form before attempting the MU-BAAT.

*   **Educational Qualification:** Applicants must possess a Bachelor's or Undergraduate Degree in any discipline from a recognized institution.
*   **Work Experience:** While candidates with 0-5 years of work experience are preferred, fresh graduates are also encouraged to apply for MU-BAAT.

## Application Process and Fees

The application for MU BAAT 2026 involves several steps, starting with online registration. Candidates who do not have valid GMAT, GRE, or CAT scores are required to appear for the MU BAAT exam. The application fees vary by round:

*   **Round 1:** INR 3000
*   **Round 2 & 3:** INR 3500

The registration for Round 5 is currently open, with the final deadline for submissions being May 3, 2026. Interested candidates should visit the official Masters' Union admissions portal to complete their application.

## MU BAAT 2026 Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The MU BAAT is designed to assess a candidate's aptitude across various business and analytical domains. The examination lasts for 60 minutes and covers the following sections:

*   **Data Interpretation and Reasoning:** This section tests the ability to analyze and interpret data, and solve logical reasoning problems.
*   **Quantitative Aptitude:** Focuses on mathematical skills, including arithmetic, algebra, and geometry.
*   **Verbal Skills:** Evaluates comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary.
*   **Caselet Assessment (Subjective):** This part requires candidates to analyze a business caselet and provide a written response, assessing their problem-solving and communication skills.

The syllabus is broadly divided into Business Aptitude, Analytical Thinking, and Problem Solving Skills, ensuring a comprehensive evaluation of a candidate's readiness for the PGP-TBM program.

## Results and Admission Process

After taking the MU BAAT, candidates will be shortlisted for the Personal Interview (PI) rounds based on their exam scores. The dates for these interviews will be communicated to the shortlisted applicants. The final admission decision for the PGP-TBM program at Masters' Union considers both the MU BAAT score (or accepted alternative scores) and performance in the personal interview.
