# LLM: Course, Full Form, Duration, Admission 2026, Fees, Subjects, Eligibility, Salary & Career Scope

For law graduates looking to deepen their expertise, the Master of Laws (LLM) is a postgraduate program designed to provide specialized knowledge in various legal fields. This advanced degree can significantly enhance career prospects and open doors to diverse opportunities in the legal profession.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Full Form:** LLM stands for Master of Laws.
*   **Specialization:** It's a postgraduate degree allowing specialization in areas like Corporate Law, Criminal Law, Intellectual Property Law, and more.
*   **Duration:** Typically a 1 or 2-year program in India.
*   **Eligibility:** Requires a 3-year LLB or a 5-year integrated LLB degree.

## Understanding the LLM Program

The LLM is a postgraduate qualification in law, typically pursued after completing an undergraduate law degree (LLB). It offers students the chance to delve deeply into a particular branch of law, moving beyond the general legal education provided by an LLB. The curriculum often includes advanced coursework, dissertations, case studies, and practical components like moot courts and internships, providing a comprehensive and specialized learning experience.

## Eligibility and Admission Process for 2026

To be eligible for an LLM program, candidates must have successfully completed either a 3-year Bachelor of Legislative Law (LLB) degree or a 5-year integrated LLB degree from a university recognized by the Bar Council of India. 

Admission to LLM programs for the 2026 academic year is primarily through national and state-level entrance examinations. Some of the prominent exams include CUET-PG, CLAT, AILET, TS PGLCET, and AP PGLCET. Certain institutions may also consider merit based on undergraduate law scores. It's crucial for aspiring students to keep an eye on official notifications from NTA (for CUET-PG) and respective exam conducting bodies for application deadlines and exam dates.

For instance, the CLAT 2026 results for both UG and LLM courses are typically released in December, with counselling registrations following shortly after. Similarly, for AP LAWCET 2026, application forms are usually available early in the year, with exams held around May.

## LLM Specializations and Career Opportunities

An LLM degree allows for deep specialization in various legal fields, significantly enhancing career prospects. Some popular specializations include:

*   **Corporate Law:** Focuses on business law, mergers, acquisitions, and corporate governance.
*   **Criminal Law:** Deals with the study of crimes, punishments, and criminal justice systems.
*   **International Law:** Covers treaties, international relations, and global legal frameworks.
*   **Intellectual Property Law:** Specializes in patents, copyrights, trademarks, and intellectual assets.
*   **Environmental Law:** Addresses legal issues related to environmental protection and sustainability.

Graduates with an LLM can pursue a wide array of career paths. Beyond traditional roles like an advocate or solicitor, opportunities exist as legal advisors in multinational companies, legal managers in banks, consultants, or even as assistant professors in law schools. The specialized knowledge gained makes LLM holders highly sought after in specific legal domains.

The LLM is a strategic choice for law graduates aiming to specialize and advance their careers, offering a pathway to expertise and enhanced professional roles in the dynamic legal landscape.
