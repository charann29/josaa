# Event Management Courses, Fees, Eligibility, Subjects, Colleges, Scope

Event management is a dynamic professional field that teaches you how to plan, organize, and execute various events, from corporate gatherings and exhibitions to weddings and festivals. This career path combines creativity with strong organizational skills, preparing students for exciting roles in a growing industry.

## Key takeaways

*   Event management programs are available at diploma, undergraduate (BBA, BA), and postgraduate (MBA, MA, PGD) levels.
*   Eligibility typically requires completing 10+2 for UG courses and a bachelor's degree for PG programs.
*   Course durations are generally three years for undergraduate and two years for postgraduate studies.
*   Fees can range widely, from approximately INR 2,200 to INR 6.70 lakhs, depending on the institution and program level.

## What is Event Management?

Event management is a specialized area within management that focuses on the detailed planning, coordination, and execution of various events. Students learn crucial skills such as budgeting, marketing, logistics, client relations, and hospitality management, all essential for bringing an event to life successfully. It's a field where creative ideas meet practical implementation.

## Eligibility and Admission Process

To pursue an undergraduate course in event management, you generally need to have completed your 10+2 education from a recognized board, irrespective of your stream. For postgraduate programs, a bachelor's degree in any discipline, typically with a minimum of 50% marks, is required.

Admission to event management courses can be based on academic merit from your previous qualifications or through entrance examinations conducted by individual institutions. For postgraduate courses, common entrance exams like CAT, MAT, XAT, GMAT, and CUCET are often considered, as mentioned by official exam bodies.

## Course Structure and Specializations

Event management courses cover a broad range of subjects designed to equip students with comprehensive skills. Core subjects often include event marketing, financial management for events, public relations, risk management, and vendor management. Practical training, internships, and project work are usually integral parts of the curriculum, offering hands-on experience.

The field also offers several specializations, allowing students to focus on specific types of events or aspects of event planning. Some popular specializations include:

*   Wedding Planning
*   Corporate Event Management
*   Sports Event Management
*   Exhibition Management
*   Event Marketing
*   Hospitality and Event Management
*   Media and Entertainment Management
*   Tourism and Event Management

## Career Scope and Opportunities

The demand for skilled event managers is consistently growing, both in India and internationally. Graduates can find exciting opportunities in various sectors, including dedicated event planning companies, hotels and resorts, media houses, and the broader entertainment industry. Roles can range from an Event Planner or Manager to a Logistics Manager or Project Manager, with average salaries for an Event Manager often around INR 5.7 LPA, depending on experience and company.

Leading companies that frequently recruit event management professionals include Platinum Event, Wizcraft, Britt Worldwide, JM Events, 360 Event Management Solutions, and GRIPS proevents.

Embarking on a career in event management can be incredibly rewarding for those who enjoy creativity, organization, and working with people to create memorable experiences.
