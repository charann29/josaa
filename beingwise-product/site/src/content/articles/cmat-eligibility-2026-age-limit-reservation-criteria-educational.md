# CMAT Eligibility 2026: Age Limit, Reservation Criteria, Educational Qualification

For students aspiring to pursue an MBA in India, the Common Management Admission Test (CMAT) is a significant gateway. Understanding the eligibility criteria is the first crucial step towards a successful application.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Graduation is Key:** A bachelor's degree of at least three years from an AICTE-approved institution is the fundamental requirement.
*   **No Minimum Score:** There is no specific minimum percentage required in your graduation to appear for CMAT itself.
*   **No Age Limit:** CMAT does not impose any upper age restriction for candidates.
*   **Indian Residency:** Only Indian citizens are eligible to apply for the CMAT exam.

## Educational Qualification for CMAT 2026

To be eligible for CMAT 2026, candidates must hold a bachelor's degree. This degree should be of a minimum three-year duration or an equivalent qualification from a university or institution recognized by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). The academic structure should typically follow a 10+2+3 pattern.

Students who are currently in the final year of their bachelor's degree program are also eligible to apply. However, they must ensure they pass their final year examination within the same academic year to qualify for admission to colleges accepting CMAT scores. While the National Testing Agency (NTA), the official body conducting CMAT, does not mandate a minimum percentage in graduation to sit for the exam, many participating business schools often set their own cut-off, commonly around 50% for general category candidates and 45% for reserved categories.

## CMAT Age Limit

One of the most accommodating aspects of CMAT eligibility is the absence of an age limit. The NTA, in line with AICTE guidelines, does not specify any upper age restriction for candidates wishing to apply for or appear in the CMAT examination. This allows individuals from various age groups and professional backgrounds to pursue their MBA aspirations.

## CMAT Reservation Criteria

While the CMAT examination itself does not have a reservation policy, the colleges and universities that accept CMAT scores for admission adhere to the reservation policies prescribed by the Government of India. These policies are implemented at the time of admissions and counselling by the individual institutes or state-level counselling bodies.

Candidates belonging to reserved categories (such as Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes - Non-Creamy Layer, Economically Weaker Sections, and Persons with Disabilities) should indicate their category status accurately in the CMAT application form. This information is crucial for availing reservation benefits during the admission process. It's important to note that specific documentation will be required by the colleges to verify the reservation claims during their admission procedures.

### CMAT Eligibility for Persons with Disabilities (PwD)

Candidates falling under the Persons with Disabilities category are also eligible to apply for CMAT. They must provide relevant certificates as per government norms during the application process and subsequent admissions. The NTA's official CMAT information bulletin provides detailed guidelines on availing scribe/reader facilities for PwD candidates during the examination, if applicable.

Understanding these criteria is essential for a smooth application process and to plan your MBA journey effectively.
