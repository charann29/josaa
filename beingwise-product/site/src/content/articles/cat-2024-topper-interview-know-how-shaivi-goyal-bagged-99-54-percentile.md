# CAT 2024 Topper Interview: Know How Shaivi Goyal Bagged 99.54 Percentile in CAT Exam

Shaivi Goyal achieved an outstanding 99.54 percentile in the CAT 2024 examination. In this exclusive conversation, she shares her journey, preparation strategies, and valuable advice for aspiring candidates.

## Key takeaways

*   **Early Start is Key:** Shaivi began her preparation in February 2024, allowing ample time to balance studies with a full-time job.
*   **Personalized Strategy:** She emphasized identifying strengths and weaknesses early to tailor a study plan, focusing more on challenging sections.
*   **Consistent Practice:** Regular mock tests and in-depth analysis of performance were crucial for her success.
*   **Strong Fundamentals:** Mastering basic concepts, especially in Quantitative Aptitude, was a cornerstone of her strategy.

## Shaivi's Background and Motivation

Shaivi is an Economics graduate from SRCC, Delhi University, and currently works as a Data Analyst at American Express. Her academic journey has been consistently strong, including an impressive 99.75% in her ISC 12th Examinations. Her professional experience has fueled her desire to pursue an MBA, aiming to enhance her leadership skills, strategic thinking, and overall business acumen to make a significant impact in her career.

## Her CAT Journey: A First Serious Attempt

While Shaivi took the CAT in 2023 without preparation to understand the exam pattern, scoring 91 percentile, her 2024 attempt was her first serious one. Her dedication led to remarkable results:

*   **Overall Percentile:** 99.54 (Score: 105.19)
*   **DILR Percentile:** 94.56 (Score: 26.60)
*   **VARC Percentile:** 99.55 (Score: 45.42)
*   **QA Percentile:** 99.08 (Score: 33.17)

## Section-Wise Preparation Strategy

Shaivi highlighted the importance of a personalized approach. Initially, she focused on all sections to identify her strong areas and those needing more attention. She discovered VARC to be her strength and leveraged it while dedicating significant effort to Quantitative Aptitude (QA) and Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR). As a non-engineer, she knew QA required extra work to compete effectively. Her strategy involved thoroughly understanding fundamentals and engaging in extensive practice.

## The Role of Mock Tests

Shaivi stressed that mock tests were indispensable. She took approximately 60-70 mock tests and dedicated equal time to analyzing each one. This analysis helped her understand her mistakes, identify patterns, and refine her test-taking strategy, including time management and question selection. She advises aspirants to treat mocks as real exams and learn from every attempt.

Shaivi Goyal's journey is a testament to focused effort, strategic planning, and consistent practice. Her insights offer a valuable roadmap for anyone aiming to excel in the CAT exam.
