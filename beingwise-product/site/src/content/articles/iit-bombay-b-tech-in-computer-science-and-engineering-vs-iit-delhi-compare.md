# IIT Bombay B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering Vs IIT Delhi: Compare Fees, Placement, Reviews, Cutoffs

Choosing between IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi for a B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) is a significant decision for many aspiring students. Both institutions are highly regarded and offer exceptional academic environments.

## Key takeaways

*   Both IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi consistently rank among the top engineering institutes in India, with very similar NIRF rankings.
*   The B.Tech CSE programs at both IITs share identical fee structures and course durations.
*   While both have excellent placement records, IIT Delhi reported a slightly higher median salary for its B.Tech graduates compared to IIT Bombay in recent data.
*   Admission to B.Tech CSE at both institutes requires stellar performance in JEE Main and JEE Advanced, with cutoffs being extremely competitive.

## Institute Overview

IIT Bombay, established in 1958, is situated in Mumbai, Maharashtra, and is recognized as an Institute of National Importance. IIT Delhi, founded in 1961, is located in the capital city and also holds the status of an Institute of National Importance. Both are autonomous institutions offering a wide array of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs.

## B.Tech CSE Course Details

Both IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi offer a full-time, four-year Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) degree in Computer Science and Engineering. The curriculum at both institutions is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of computer science principles, algorithms, software development, and various advanced topics.

## Fees Structure

The total fees for the four-year B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering program are comparable at both IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi. As per the latest available information, the approximate total fees for the entire duration are INR 8.00 Lakh at both institutions. This amount typically covers tuition, hostel, and other academic expenses, though it is always advisable to check the official institute websites for the most current and detailed fee breakdowns.

## Placements

Placements are a crucial factor for many students, and both IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi boast excellent records. Here's a comparison based on recent data:

| Parameter           | IIT Bombay (B.Tech CSE) | IIT Delhi (B.Tech CSE) |
| :------------------ | :---------------------- | :--------------------- |
| Batch Placed        | 90.74%                  | 83.5%                  |
| Median Salary (UG)  | INR 19.08 Lakh          | INR 20.00 Lakh         |

*Note: These figures represent median salaries for all B.Tech programs, as per NIRF data. Specific CSE branch placement statistics might vary slightly but generally align with these trends. Students are encouraged to review the detailed placement reports available on the official career services portals of each IIT.*

## Student Reviews and Rankings

Student feedback often provides valuable insights. Both IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi receive high praise from their students, with an overall rating of 4.5 out of 5 for their B.Tech CSE programs, based on verified reviews. IIT Delhi had 39 student reviews, while IIT Bombay had 37. Key areas like placements, infrastructure, faculty, and campus life are generally rated very positively at both.

In terms of national recognition, both IITs consistently secure top positions in the NIRF rankings. For example, in the engineering category, IIT Delhi has often been ranked 2nd, while IIT Bombay has frequently been ranked 3rd by NIRF in recent years, showcasing their consistent excellence.

## Cutoffs

Admission to the B.Tech CSE program at both IIT Bombay and IIT Delhi is highly competitive and determined by performance in the JEE Advanced examination. The cutoffs (closing ranks) are published annually by the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA). Historically, candidates require ranks within the top few hundreds (All India Rank) to secure a seat in CSE at either institute, with IIT Bombay often having slightly lower closing ranks than IIT Delhi, indicating marginally higher competition for its CSE program.

Choosing between these two premier institutions ultimately depends on individual preferences regarding location, specific research interests, and campus culture, as both offer a world-class education in Computer Science and Engineering.
