# Diploma in Firefighting and Shipboard Safety by Alison: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For those aspiring to a career at sea or in maritime safety, understanding the essentials of firefighting and shipboard safety is crucial. This Diploma by Alison offers a comprehensive introduction to these vital topics, equipping learners with foundational knowledge for emergency preparedness and safe practices on vessels.

## Key Takeaways

*   This diploma is offered completely free of charge, with an optional certificate available for purchase.
*   The entire course content is delivered through video modules, making it accessible and engaging.
*   It covers a broad spectrum of safety topics, from general shipboard hazards to specific firefighting techniques and equipment.
*   The program emphasizes effective communication and the importance of drills for emergency response.

## Course Overview and Structure

The Diploma in Firefighting and Shipboard Safety is designed to provide a thorough understanding of safety protocols and emergency responses specific to maritime environments. The course is delivered entirely in English and utilizes a video-based learning format, allowing students to learn at their own pace.

While the course content itself is free, those who wish to receive an official certificate upon completion can obtain one from Alison, the certifying authority, for a fee. This certificate can be a valuable addition to your professional credentials.

## Detailed Syllabus

The diploma is structured across multiple modules, each focusing on a distinct aspect of shipboard safety and emergency management. Here's a breakdown of the key areas covered:

### General Safety and Safe Working Practices

These initial modules lay the groundwork by introducing learners to fundamental safety concepts. You'll explore hazardous areas on ships, safe working procedures, and emergency protocols for confined spaces. Topics include lifting heavy loads, handling tools, working on deck in adverse weather, and managing electrical equipment. The curriculum also delves into controlling shipboard hazards, identifying electrical and chemical risks, and understanding pre-job safety planning.

### Training, Drills, and Communications

This section highlights the critical role of training and regular drills, such as abandon ship exercises, in ensuring preparedness. Furthermore, it emphasizes effective communication in a maritime context. You will learn about barriers to communication, report writing guidelines, the impact of cultural diversity, and the importance of feedback. Modules also cover on-board interactions, communication techniques, and understanding cognitive functions for better team coordination.

### Hazardous Materials and Emergency Equipment

Understanding hazardous materials is vital for safety. This part of the course covers the identification and labeling of such materials, including those commonly found on tugboats. It also details various emergency equipment, including alarms, signals, signaling equipment, pyrotechnics, and the proper use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

### Life Saving Appliances (LSA) and Fire Prevention

Crucial for survival at sea, the LSA modules introduce different life-saving appliances, launching mechanisms, GMDSS radio systems, and the operation of survival craft and rescue boats. Fire prevention is extensively covered, including ship construction arrangements, common causes of fire on board, prevention principles, and the use of gas measuring instruments for detecting oxygen concentrations. The process of permits, entry, and rescue is also discussed.

### Firefighting Equipment

This comprehensive section focuses on the tools and systems used to combat fires. It covers portable firefighting appliances, fixed fire extinguishing systems, high-expansion systems for machinery spaces, and various types of fire pumps, hoses, and nozzles. Specific extinguishing agents like HALON, AFFF, Novec-1230, and dry powder systems are also explained. A dedicated module addresses firefighting equipment requirements for smaller vessels, including personal protective gear mandated by SOLAS.

## How to Apply

Applying for this diploma is straightforward as it is offered as a free online course. Interested individuals can typically enroll directly through the Alison platform by creating an account. There are no specific entrance exams or eligibility criteria mentioned beyond a general interest in the subject matter.

This diploma provides an excellent foundation for anyone looking to enhance their knowledge of safety and emergency response in the maritime industry, offering valuable insights into crucial shipboard operations.
