# MAT Exam Eligibility 2026: Age Limit, Qualifying Criteria, Number of Attempts

To pursue a Master of Business Administration (MBA) or Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM) program in India, taking an entrance exam like MAT is often a crucial step. Understanding the eligibility requirements for the MAT exam is essential before you apply, ensuring a smooth application process and avoiding any last-minute surprises.

## Key Takeaways

*   A bachelor's degree in any field from a recognized university is the fundamental academic requirement.
*   There is no upper age limit for appearing in the MAT examination.
*   Candidates in their final year of graduation or awaiting their final results are eligible to apply.
*   You can attempt the MAT exam multiple times, with the latest score typically considered for admissions.

## Academic Qualification for MAT

The primary criterion for MAT is holding a bachelor's degree in any discipline. This degree should be from a university or institution recognized by the appropriate authorities. Even if you are currently in the final year of your bachelor's program or are awaiting your final results, you are still eligible to take the MAT exam. However, you will need to complete your degree successfully within the stipulated time frame set by the colleges you apply to.

It's important to note that while the All India Management Association (AIMA), the body that conducts MAT, does not specify a minimum percentage for graduation to appear for the exam, many business schools (B-schools) do. According to general guidelines for MBA programs, a minimum of 50% aggregate marks in graduation (45% for candidates from reserved categories) is often required by participating institutions. Therefore, while you can take MAT with just passing marks, achieving a good percentage in your bachelor's degree is beneficial for actual MBA admissions.

## MAT Age Limit

One of the welcoming aspects of the MAT exam is that there is no official upper age limit to appear for it. This means that individuals of any age, provided they meet the academic qualifications, can take the test. While most applicants are typically 20 years or older due to the graduation requirement, your age will not be a barrier to sitting for the MAT exam.

## Work Experience Requirements

Work experience is not a mandatory eligibility criterion for the MAT exam. You can apply for and take the MAT test even if you are a fresh graduate with no prior work experience. However, some MBA colleges that accept MAT scores may give additional weightage to candidates with relevant work experience during their selection process. This is more of an admission criterion for specific B-schools rather than an eligibility requirement for the MAT exam itself.

## Number of Attempts

There is no restriction on the number of times you can attempt the MAT exam. AIMA conducts the MAT exam four times a year – in February, May, September, and December. You are free to appear for the exam in any or all of these cycles. Typically, for MBA admissions, the most recent MAT score will be considered by the institutions you apply to. This flexibility allows candidates to improve their scores and apply with their best performance.

## MAT Exam Modes

The eligibility criteria remain consistent regardless of the mode of examination. Candidates can choose to take the MAT exam in Computer-Based Test (CBT) mode, Paper-Based Test (PBT) mode, or even both, if offered in a specific cycle. The academic and other requirements are identical for all these formats.

Understanding these eligibility details is your first step towards a successful MAT application and, eventually, your MBA journey.
