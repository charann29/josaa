# Rank 136000 KCET: Your Chances for Biomedical Engineering in Bangalore

Securing a seat in a sought-after engineering discipline like Biomedical Engineering with a KCET rank of 136000 can feel challenging. This guide will help you understand your prospects and explore potential options, especially in and around Bangalore.

## Key Takeaways

*   A KCET rank of 136000 places you at a competitive but not impossible position for specific engineering branches.
*   ACS College of Engineering in Bangalore previously had a cutoff for Biomedical Engineering that was well within this rank range.
*   Thorough research into previous year's cutoffs is crucial for identifying potential colleges.
*   Consider all rounds of counselling, as cutoffs can shift between them.

## Understanding KCET Ranks and Cutoffs

Your KCET rank is a crucial factor in determining your eligibility for various engineering programs across Karnataka. Each college and each specific engineering branch has a 'cutoff rank,' which is the last rank admitted to that program in the previous academic year. These cutoffs are dynamic and can change slightly year-to-year based on factors like the number of applicants, their performance, and the total seats available.

## Exploring Biomedical Engineering with Your Rank

Biomedical Engineering is a fascinating field that combines engineering principles with medical sciences, focusing on designing and developing healthcare solutions. With a KCET rank of 136000, while many top-tier colleges might be out of reach, there are still possibilities, particularly in specific institutions.

### ACS College of Engineering, Bangalore: A Potential Option

Based on historical data, ACS College of Engineering in Bangalore appears to be a viable option for Biomedical Engineering with your rank. In a recent admission cycle, the cutoff rank for this program at ACS College of Engineering was approximately 148667. This suggests that your rank of 136000 falls comfortably within the previously accepted range, giving you a good chance of securing a seat.

*   **Program Availability:** ACS College of Engineering offers a Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Biomedical Engineering.
*   **Seat Matrix:** The college has a capacity of 60 seats specifically for the Biomedical Engineering program, according to available information. This number of seats provides a reasonable opportunity for admission if your rank aligns with the cutoff.

## Navigating the KCET Counselling Process

Participating in the KCET counselling process requires careful planning. Here are some pointers:

*   **Document Verification:** Ensure all your documents are in order and verified accurately as per the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) guidelines.
*   **Option Entry:** When filling your option entry form, list colleges and courses in your order of preference. It's wise to include a mix of aspirational choices, realistic options, and safer bets based on previous year's cutoffs.
*   **Multiple Rounds:** Don't be disheartened if you don't get your preferred choice in the first round. Cutoffs often change in subsequent rounds of counselling, and seats can become available. Stay updated with the official KEA portal for announcements regarding all rounds.

## Researching Other Colleges Around Bangalore

While ACS College of Engineering is a strong candidate, it's always advisable to broaden your search. Look for other private engineering colleges in and around Bangalore that offer Biomedical Engineering. Check their official websites or the KEA information bulletins for their previous year's cutoff ranks. Some colleges might have slightly higher cutoffs, but it's worth exploring all possibilities.

Your rank certainly opens doors to specific opportunities; diligent research and strategic option entry will be key to your success.
