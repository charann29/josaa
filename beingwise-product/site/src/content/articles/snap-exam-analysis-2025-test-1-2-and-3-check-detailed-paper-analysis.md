# SNAP Exam Analysis 2025 (Test 1, 2 & 3): Check Detailed Paper Analysis

Understanding the SNAP exam analysis is crucial for anyone aspiring to join Symbiosis International University's MBA programs. This detailed breakdown covers the difficulty levels, question patterns, and good attempts for SNAP 2025 Tests 1, 2, and 3, which took place on December 6, December 14, and December 20, respectively.

## Key Takeaways

*   The SNAP 2025 examinations across all three tests were generally considered easier than previous years.
*   A score of around 40-43 correct attempts was often sufficient for a good percentile.
*   The English section did not feature Reading Comprehension passages, focusing instead on grammar and vocabulary.
*   While the Quantitative, Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency (QA-DI-DS) section was mostly easy, Test 1 included some lengthy questions within this segment.

## Overall SNAP 2025 Exam Structure and Difficulty

SNAP 2025 maintained its format of a 60-minute examination with 60 multiple-choice questions (MCQs). The overall difficulty level was consistently easy across all three sessions. The paper was divided into three distinct sections: General English, Quantitative, Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency, and Analytical & Logical Reasoning.

The absence of Reading Comprehension passages in the English section was a notable feature, making it less time-consuming for many candidates.

Here’s a snapshot of the SNAP 2025 question paper structure and difficulty:

| SNAP Sections                                        | Number of MCQs | Difficulty Level |
| :--------------------------------------------------- | :------------- | :--------------- |
| General English (VARC)                               | 15             | Easy             |
| Quantitative, Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency | 20             | Easy to Moderate |
| Analytical & Logical Reasoning                       | 25             | Moderate         |
| **Total**                                            | **60**         | **Easy**         |

## Section-Wise Analysis: General English

The General English section for SNAP 2025 was largely straightforward, with most questions being vocabulary or grammar-based. Candidates found this section to be easy, and approximately 11-12 correct attempts were considered a strong performance.

As mentioned, there were no Reading Comprehension passages, a trend observed in recent SNAP exams. The breakdown of question types included:

| Question Types                             | Number of Questions | Level of Difficulty |
| :----------------------------------------- | :------------------ | :------------------ |
| Analogies (Vocabulary-based)               | 2                   | Easy                |
| Fill in the Blanks (1 & 2 blanks - Grammar/Vocab) | 9                   | Easy                |
| Grammar (Parts of speech, Passive voice)   | 2                   | Easy                |
| Synonyms                                   | 1                   | Easy                |
| Vocabulary (Root words)                    | 1                   | Easy                |

## Section-Wise Analysis: Quantitative, Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency

This section presented a mix of easy to moderate questions. While most questions were manageable, Test 1 did have some lengthier problems in the Quantitative section, requiring more time for calculations. A good number of attempts in this section would typically range from 12-14 questions, depending on the specific test and candidate's proficiency.

## Section-Wise Analysis: Analytical & Logical Reasoning

The Analytical & Logical Reasoning (A-LR) section was generally rated as moderate in difficulty across all three SNAP 2025 tests. A strong performance in this section would involve around 19-20 correct attempts. The question types covered a broad range of reasoning skills, including both verbal and non-verbal reasoning.

Key topics included Syllogisms, Course of Action, Critical Reasoning, Implicit Statements, Cause and Effect, Statement Arguments, Series Completion, Linear and Matrix Arrangements, Family Trees, and Calendar-based problems.

## Expected SNAP Cut-offs 2025

Given the easier overall difficulty of SNAP 2025, the expected cut-offs for admission to Symbiosis Institute of Business Management (SIBM) Pune and other top Symbiosis institutes might see slight adjustments. Typically, a score fetching 98 percentile could be achieved with around 41-43 correct attempts, especially considering the slightly tougher quantitative questions in Test 1.

For precise cut-off details, candidates should refer to the official Symbiosis admissions portal once they are released.

This analysis should help future aspirants prepare strategically for the SNAP exam by understanding the expected pattern and difficulty levels.
