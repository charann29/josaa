# How to Challenge JEE Main Session 2 Answer Key 2026: Steps to Raise Objection, Last Date and Fees

After the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main Session 2, the National Testing Agency (NTA) releases a provisional answer key. This allows students to review the official answers and identify any discrepancies. If you believe an answer is incorrect, you have a window to challenge it.

## Key Takeaways

*   The NTA provides a specific window to challenge the provisional JEE Main Session 2 answer key.
*   A fee of INR 200 per question must be paid for each challenge raised.
*   Challenges require supporting proof and are submitted online through the NTA JEE portal.
*   The final answer key, released after reviewing all objections, will determine your official JEE Main scores.

## Understanding the JEE Main Provisional Answer Key

The provisional answer key is an initial release by the NTA containing the correct answers to all questions in the JEE Main Session 2 examination. Alongside this, your individual response sheet is also made available. By comparing your recorded responses with the provisional answer key, you can calculate an estimated score for your exam performance.

## How to Access Your JEE Main Session 2 Answer Key

To view the provisional answer key and your response sheet, follow these steps:

1.  Visit the official JEE Main website.
2.  Look for the link related to the JEE Main Session 2 Answer Key 2026.
3.  Log in using your application number and date of birth or other required credentials.
4.  You will then be able to view and download both the answer key and your response sheet.

## Steps to Challenge the JEE Main Session 2 Answer Key

If, after reviewing, you find an answer that you believe is incorrect, you can raise an objection. Here's a general outline of the process:

1.  **Log In:** Access the NTA JEE portal using your application number and password.
2.  **Locate Challenge Link:** Find the specific link for challenging the answer key for Session 2, 2026.
3.  **Select Questions:** Identify the question(s) you wish to challenge. You will typically see a list of questions and their corresponding provisional answers.
4.  **Provide Justification:** For each question challenged, you must provide a clear explanation and upload supporting documentation or proof from standard textbooks or reliable academic sources.
5.  **Pay Fee:** A non-refundable fee of INR 200 per question challenged must be paid online. Without successful fee payment, your objection will not be considered.
6.  **Submit:** Confirm and submit your challenge(s).

## JEE Main 2026 Answer Key Challenge Fee

The fee for challenging the JEE Main answer key is INR 200 per question. This payment must be made online using a debit card, credit card, or net banking. It's crucial to complete the payment within the specified deadline, as challenges without the required fee will not be processed by the NTA.

## The Final Answer Key and Result Calculation

After the objection window closes, the NTA's team of subject matter experts will review all submitted challenges and the accompanying proof. Based on this review, necessary corrections will be made, leading to the release of the final JEE Main answer key. This final key is definitive and will be used to calculate your official JEE Main Session 2 results. No further challenges will be entertained once the final answer key is published.

Staying informed about these deadlines and procedures is vital for ensuring fairness in your JEE Main evaluation.
