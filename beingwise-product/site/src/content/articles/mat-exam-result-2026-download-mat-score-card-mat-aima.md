# MAT Exam Result 2026: Download MAT Score card @mat.aima.in

After taking the Management Aptitude Test (MAT), candidates will eagerly await their results. The MAT scorecard, which indicates your performance, is essential for MBA/PGDM admissions across many institutions in India.

## Key takeaways

*   The MAT 2026 result for the May session will typically be available about two weeks after the exam dates.
*   You will need to log in to the official AIMA MAT portal to download your scorecard.
*   Over 800 MBA colleges in India accept MAT scores for their management programs.
*   If you're not satisfied with your score, you have the option to reappear for the MAT exam in a later session.

## Accessing Your MAT 2026 Scorecard

The Association of Indian Management Schools (AIMA) releases the MAT result and scorecard exclusively on its official examination website. While AIMA may send email notifications about the scorecard's availability, the primary method to obtain it is by logging into your student dashboard. There isn't a fixed date for the release; candidates are advised to check the portal directly.

To download your MAT 2026 scorecard, you will typically need your login credentials, such as your registration number and date of birth or password.

Based on these dates, candidates who appeared for the May 2026 exams can generally expect their results in the third week of June 2026.

## How Your MAT Score is Calculated

The MAT result and score calculation are based on your performance in four key sections: Language Comprehension, Mathematical Skills, Data Analysis & Sufficiency, and Intelligence & Critical Reasoning. It's important to note that the fifth section, Economic & Business Environment, is not considered for your MAT percentile calculation.

Here's a breakdown of the MAT marking scheme:

*   Each correct answer is awarded 1 mark.
*   There is a negative marking of 0.25 marks for every incorrect answer.
*   Unattempted questions do not incur any penalty.

MAT employs a unique method for reporting scores. While individual section scores are reported on a scale of 0 to 100, the overall Composite Score ranges from 199 to 801.

## Next Steps After Receiving Your MAT Result

Once your MAT 2026 result is declared, the next phase is to focus on MBA admissions. AIMA directly sends your scores to up to five colleges you selected in your MAT application form. These institutions will then evaluate your score against their minimum MAT cut-offs.

Colleges that accept MAT scores typically conduct further selection rounds, which may include a Group Discussion (GD), Written Ability Test (WAT), and Personal Interview (PI). Your performance in these rounds, combined with your MAT score and academic profile, determines your final admission.

If you are not satisfied with your MAT score, you can register and reappear for the exam in a subsequent session, such as the September 2026 session. A MAT score remains valid for one year for admissions to all participating colleges across India.

Remember to prepare thoroughly for the subsequent admission rounds, enhancing your soft skills and communication abilities, which are crucial for GD and PI stages.
