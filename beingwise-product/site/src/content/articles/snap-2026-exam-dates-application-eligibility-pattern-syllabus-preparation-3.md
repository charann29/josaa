# SNAP 2026: Exam Dates, Application, Eligibility, Pattern, Syllabus, Preparation, Cut Offs

The Symbiosis National Aptitude Test (SNAP) is a vital entrance examination for students aspiring to pursue an MBA from Symbiosis International (Deemed) University (SIU) and its affiliated institutions. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of everything you need to know about SNAP 2026.

## Key takeaways

*   SNAP 2026 will be conducted in December across three separate sessions, with a week between each session.
*   You have the option to appear for one, two, or all three sessions, and your best score will be considered for admissions.
*   The application process for SNAP 2026 is expected to begin in August and conclude in November, available on the official SNAPTEST.org website.
*   The exam is a Computer-Based Test (CBT) lasting 60 minutes, featuring sections on General English, Quantitative-Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency, and Analytical & Logical Reasoning.

SNAP 2026 is scheduled to be held in December, offering multiple opportunities for candidates to take the test. The registration window typically spans several months.

## SNAP Eligibility Criteria 2026

To be eligible for SNAP 2026, candidates must meet specific academic requirements set by SIU. Generally, this involves holding a Bachelor's degree with a minimum percentage of marks from a recognized university. Detailed eligibility guidelines will be published on the official SNAPTEST.org website.

## SNAP Application Process 2026

The application process for SNAP 2026 is entirely online. Aspiring candidates will need to visit the official SNAPTEST.org portal to register, fill out the application form, upload necessary documents, and pay the examination fee. It's crucial to complete all steps accurately within the stipulated deadlines.

## SNAP Syllabus and Exam Pattern 2026

The SNAP exam evaluates a candidate's aptitude across three core areas:

*   **General English:** This section assesses verbal ability, reading comprehension, and vocabulary.
*   **Quantitative-Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency:** Expect questions on arithmetic, algebra, geometry, modern math, and data analysis.
*   **Analytical & Logical Reasoning:** This part tests critical thinking, problem-solving, and logical deduction skills.

The exam pattern involves 60 multiple-choice questions, to be completed in 60 minutes. Each correct answer typically carries one mark, and there is usually a negative marking for incorrect responses, so it's wise to review the specific marking scheme for 2026 once released.

## SNAP Preparation and Cut Offs

Effective preparation for SNAP involves a combination of understanding the syllabus, practicing with mock tests, and improving time management. Focus on strengthening your fundamentals in each section. Consistent practice with previous year's papers can also provide valuable insights into the exam's structure and question types.

SNAP cut-offs vary each year depending on factors like the number of applicants, exam difficulty, and the performance of candidates. Each SIU institute releases its own cut-offs for shortlisting candidates for the GE-PI-WAT rounds. Generally, top SIU colleges might have cut-offs ranging from the 90th percentile and above, while other institutes may have slightly lower requirements.

Appearing for SNAP is the first step towards an MBA from a prestigious Symbiosis institution, and thorough preparation is key to success.
