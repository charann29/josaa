# Youth Mental Health: Supporting Young People Using a Trauma Informed Practice by Mental Health Foundation via Futurelearn: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding and supporting young people's mental health is more crucial than ever. This course, developed by the Mental Health Foundation and offered through Futurelearn, provides valuable insights into using a trauma-informed approach to help young individuals navigate their challenges.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn to recognize and address the impact of Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) on youth mental health.
*   Discover practical strategies for supporting young people's physical health, emotional well-being, and peer relationships.
*   Gain awareness of how life transitions and living conditions can affect mental health.
*   Understand the principles of maintaining a positive and supportive environment for young individuals.

## Course Overview

This program is designed for anyone interested in better supporting young people, whether you're a parent, educator, healthcare worker, or simply someone who interacts with youth. It delves into the concept of trauma-informed practice, which recognizes the widespread impact of trauma and emphasizes physical, psychological, and emotional safety for both service providers and survivors.

The course is delivered in English and follows a self-study format, allowing you to learn at your own pace. Content is presented through a mix of video and text-based materials. The estimated learning effort is approximately one hour per week, making it manageable to fit into a busy schedule.

## Course Syllabus

The program is structured into two main weeks, each focusing on key aspects of youth mental health and trauma-informed care:

### Week 1: PEP

This introductory week sets the stage for the journey into trauma-informed practice. It covers:

*   **Starting the PEP TALK journey:** An overview of what the course aims to achieve.
*   **Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs):** Understanding the profound and lasting effects of childhood adversity.
*   **P: Physical Health:** Exploring the link between physical well-being and mental health.
*   **E: Emotional Wellbeing:** Strategies for nurturing emotional resilience in young people.
*   **P: Peer Relationships:** How social connections and peer dynamics influence mental health.
*   **Weekly review:** A chance to consolidate your learning.

### Week 2: TALK

The second week builds upon the foundational knowledge, focusing on broader environmental and social factors:

*   **What's happening in Week 2?:** An introduction to the week's themes.
*   **T: Transitions:** Addressing the mental health implications of significant life changes.
*   **A: Awareness:** Cultivating greater self-awareness and awareness of others' needs.
*   **L: Living conditions:** Recognizing how a young person's environment impacts their mental health.
*   **K: Keep it positive:** Emphasizing the importance of positive reinforcement and a hopeful outlook.
*   **Course wrap-up:** A summary of key learnings and next steps.

## Fees and Certification

The core course content is free to access. However, if you wish to receive an official certificate upon completion, there is a fee involved. The certificate is provided by Futurelearn, confirming your successful engagement with the material. The certificate fee is ₹2,252.

## How to Apply

To enroll in this course, you would typically visit the official Futurelearn platform and search for the course title: "Youth Mental Health: Supporting Young People Using a Trauma Informed Practice." The platform will guide you through the registration process, allowing you to begin your learning journey immediately.

This course offers a valuable opportunity to develop skills that can make a real difference in the lives of young people, fostering healthier and more supportive environments for their growth and well-being.
