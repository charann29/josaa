Navigating engineering admissions can feel complex, especially when considering specific branches and minority quotas. This guide will help you understand your prospects for B.Tech Artificial Intelligence and Data Science (AI & DS) at Thakur College of Engineering and Technology (TCET), Mumbai, based on your MHT CET scores and Hindi Linguistic Minority status.

## Key takeaways

*   Your MHT CET percentile, not just marks, is crucial for admission.
*   Hindi Linguistic Minority (HLM) status can offer a significant advantage through reserved seats.
*   Cut-offs for AI & DS at TCET are generally competitive, even for minority categories.
*   Always refer to the official MHT CET counselling portal and TCET's website for the most accurate and up-to-date information.

## Understanding Your Chances for AI & DS at TCET (2025)

With an MHT CET score of 76 marks, translating to an estimated 85-90 percentile, and belonging to the Hindi Linguistic Minority category, your admission chances for B.Tech AI & DS at TCET are worth exploring. It's important to remember that cut-offs can fluctuate slightly each year based on various factors, including the number of applicants and their scores.

### MHT CET Percentile and Category Cut-offs

Historically, the closing percentile for AI & DS at TCET under the General (Home State) category has been quite high, often in the range of 92-94 percentile. While specific official cut-off data for the Hindi Linguistic Minority category isn't always publicly detailed in the same way as general categories, it's widely understood that minority quotas typically have more lenient cut-offs compared to the general pool.

However, even with a minority quota, popular branches like AI & DS remain competitive. Based on previous years' trends, including MHT CET 2024 data, the closing percentile for minority categories in AI & DS at TCET has been around 93.4 percentile. This suggests that while your 85-90 percentile is strong, it might be challenging to secure AI & DS specifically at TCET, even with the Hindi Linguistic Minority advantage. For other engineering branches with lower demand, an 85-90 percentile might offer better prospects within the minority quota.

## The Admission Process: What to Expect

Securing a seat at TCET involves a structured process that you need to follow carefully:

1.  **Eligibility Criteria:** Ensure you meet the fundamental academic requirements. This generally includes passing your 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, typically with at least 50% marks overall (or 45% for reserved categories).
2.  **MHT CET Counselling:** After your MHT CET results are declared by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, you must participate in the centralised counselling process. This involves registration, document verification, and filling out your preferred college and course choices.
3.  **Choice Filling:** During counselling, you will list your preferred institutes and engineering programs. Make sure to select TCET and the AI & DS program (or other programs you are interested in) as high as possible in your preference list.
4.  **Seat Allotment:** Seats are allotted based on your MHT CET percentile, your category (Hindi Linguistic Minority in your case), and the preferences you submitted during counselling. Keep a close watch on the official MHT CET counselling portal for allotment results and subsequent admission procedures.

## What if you score 80 percentile in MHT CET 2025?

If your MHT CET percentile is around 80, securing a seat in a highly sought-after branch like AI & DS at TCET, even under the Hindi Linguistic Minority quota, becomes more difficult. While the minority quota does provide an advantage, an 80 percentile might be below the competitive range for AI & DS at TCET, even for minority candidates, based on past trends.

However, an 80 percentile could still open doors to other engineering branches at TCET or other reputable colleges, especially within the Hindi Linguistic Minority category. It's always advisable to explore all available options and consider a broader range of colleges and branches during the counselling process.

## Official Resources are Key

For the most precise and current information, always rely on official sources. The official MHT CET counselling portal will provide the latest cut-offs, counselling schedules, and seat matrix details. For specific details about TCET's programs and their internal admission process, refer to the official TCET Mumbai website. Their admissions office can also provide personalised guidance regarding minority quotas and specific branch availability.

Planning your admissions strategy with accurate information will help you make the best choices for your engineering journey.
