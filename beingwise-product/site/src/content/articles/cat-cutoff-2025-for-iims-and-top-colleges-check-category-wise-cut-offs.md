# CAT Cutoff 2025 for IIMs and Top Colleges: Check Category-wise Cut offs

Understanding CAT cutoffs is crucial for aspiring MBA students aiming for India's premier management institutions. These cutoffs represent the minimum percentile scores required for admission, varying significantly across colleges and candidate categories.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Top IIMs:** For General category students, the most sought-after IIMs like Ahmedabad, Bangalore, and Calcutta typically require a CAT percentile of 99.97 or higher.
*   **Other Top Colleges:** Leading non-IIM institutions often have CAT cutoffs in the 94-97 percentile range.
*   **Tier 2 and 3 Colleges:** More accessible MBA programs generally look for percentiles between 70-80 for Tier 2 colleges and 60-70 for Tier 3 institutions.
*   **Category Variation:** Cutoffs are typically lower for Reserved categories (SC, ST, PwD) compared to the General category.

## Factors Determining CAT Cutoffs 2025

Several elements influence the CAT cutoff percentiles for MBA/PGP programs each year. These factors collectively shape the competitive landscape for admissions:

*   **Exam Difficulty:** The overall difficulty level of the CAT examination impacts how candidates perform and, consequently, the resulting percentiles.
*   **Applicant Pool:** The total number of candidates appearing for the exam, as well as those who successfully clear it, plays a significant role.
*   **Historical Trends:** Past years' cutoff data provides a strong indication, as cutoffs rarely fluctuate drastically for top institutions.
*   **Seat Availability:** The number of available seats in a specific program at an institute directly affects the cutoff.
*   **Institute Reputation:** The overall ranking and prestige of an institute, often reflected in NIRF rankings, also influence its cutoff demands.

For instance, historically, top IIMs consistently maintain cutoffs above 99.95 percentile for General category candidates, while colleges like SPJIMR, MDI, or FMS typically range from 95-97 percentile.

### Understanding IIM CAT Qualifying vs. Final Cutoffs

The qualifying CAT cutoff is the initial threshold for eligibility to participate in the subsequent selection processes like WAT-PI. However, meeting this threshold does not guarantee admission. The *final* cutoff, which is often significantly higher, determines who ultimately receives an admission offer after considering all aspects of the selection process.

For example, while the qualifying cutoff for an IIM might be 80 percentile, the final cutoff for admission could be above 99.95 percentile, reflecting the intense competition.

## Estimated Final CAT Cutoffs for Top IIMs (General Category)

Final CAT cutoffs are generally not disclosed publicly by IIMs. However, based on past admission trends and the competition for the 2026 batch, the estimated final CAT percentile cutoffs for General category students at the top 21 IIMs are typically very high. These are the effective cutoffs based on which interview calls and final admissions are made, often exceeding 99 percentile for the premier institutes.

## CAT Cutoffs 2025 for Top Non-IIM MBA Colleges

Beyond the IIMs, many other highly reputable MBA colleges also accept CAT scores. Their cutoffs are competitive but generally lower than the top-tier IIMs. These institutions offer excellent management education and attract a large pool of talented applicants.

Securing a seat in a top MBA program requires not only meeting these cutoffs but also performing well in subsequent selection rounds.
