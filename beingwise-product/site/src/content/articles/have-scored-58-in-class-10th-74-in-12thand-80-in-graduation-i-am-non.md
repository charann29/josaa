It's natural to wonder about your chances at top institutions like IIM Lucknow and IIT Bombay's Shailesh J. Mehta School of Management (SJMSOM) with a strong CAT score, good work experience, and extracurriculars. Let's break down how your profile might be assessed.

## Key takeaways

*   Your Class 10th score of 58% is a significant hurdle for both IIM Lucknow and IIT Bombay SJMSOM, as both highly value consistent academic performance.
*   A 98 percentile in CAT, 24 months of work experience, and your B.Com (Hons) background are strong positives that will boost your profile.
*   Your KVS National player status is an excellent extracurricular that demonstrates dedication and achievement.
*   While challenging, your profile offers some strengths that might improve your prospects, especially in the interview stage.

## Understanding IIM Lucknow's Evaluation

IIM Lucknow is known for its comprehensive evaluation process, which heavily considers past academic performance. While your 98 percentile CAT score is outstanding, and your B.Com (Hons) degree provides valuable academic diversity (which IIMs often appreciate), your Class 10th score of 58% and Class 12th score of 74% are likely to significantly impact your composite score. IIM Lucknow's shortlisting criteria typically assign substantial weight to both 10th and 12th marks. Even with strong work experience and national-level extracurriculars, these academic scores can make securing an interview call quite difficult.

## IIT Bombay SJMSOM's Perspective

IIT Bombay's SJMSOM also considers a holistic profile, but its weighting scheme might be slightly less penalizing for past academic scores compared to some IIMs. Your 98 percentile CAT score will be a major driving force in their initial shortlisting. Your 24 months of work experience and your national-level sports achievement are definite advantages. However, the 58% in Class 10th remains a considerable challenge. While your chances here might be relatively better than at IIM Lucknow, it's still a competitive environment where consistent academic excellence is highly valued. The final selection will undoubtedly depend on your performance in the Personal Interview (PI) round.

## The Role of Academic Diversity and Extracurriculars

Your B.Com (Hons) background is a significant plus, as it contributes to academic diversity within the MBA cohort. Many top B-schools actively seek candidates from non-engineering backgrounds to enrich classroom discussions and perspectives. Furthermore, your status as a two-time KVS National player is an exceptional extracurricular activity. This demonstrates discipline, teamwork, leadership potential, and the ability to excel outside academics – qualities highly sought after by B-schools. These elements will certainly strengthen your application and provide compelling points for discussion during an interview.

## The Interview Stage: Your Opportunity

Should you receive an interview call from either IIM Lucknow or IIT Bombay SJMSOM, this stage becomes critical. This is your opportunity to articulate how your work experience has prepared you for an MBA, discuss the leadership and teamwork skills gained from your national-level sports, and explain any perceived weaknesses in your academic record positively (e.g., demonstrating growth and learning). A stellar performance in the Personal Interview, coupled with a strong Written Ability Test (WAT) if applicable, can significantly improve your chances of final admission.

Navigating the admissions process for top institutions requires a clear understanding of their criteria and showcasing your strengths effectively.
