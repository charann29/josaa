# Career in Defence after Engineering: Army, Navy, and Air Force

For engineering graduates in India, a career in the armed forces offers a path of immense respect and national service. Many aspire to join the Indian Army, Navy, or Air Force, and fortunately, there are several established routes to do so after completing a B.Tech.

## Key Takeaways

*   Engineering graduates have multiple entry schemes to join the Indian Armed Forces as commissioned officers.
*   Options include permanent and short service commissions across the Army, Navy, and Air Force.
*   Selection processes typically involve an SSB Interview, with some routes also requiring written exams like CDS or AFCAT.
*   Age limits and specific engineering disciplines are crucial eligibility criteria for each scheme.

## Entry Schemes for Engineering Graduates

Engineering graduates can apply to various branches of the Indian Armed Forces through specific entry schemes. These pathways are designed to recruit technically qualified individuals into leadership roles.

### Indian Army Entry Schemes

| Type of Entry | Commission | Age Limit | Qualification | Selection Process |
| :---------------------------------- | :--------- | :-------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------ |
| Technical Graduate Course (TGC) (Men) | Permanent | 20 – 27 Yrs | Engineering Degree in a specified discipline. Final year students are eligible. | SSB Interview (no written exam; based on engineering marks) |
| Short Service Commission (SSC Tech) (Men & Women) | Short Service | 20 – 27 Yrs | Engineering Degree in a specified discipline. | SSB Interview (no written exam; based on engineering marks) |
| Combined Defence Services (CDS) Exam | Permanent (IMA) or Short Service (OTA) | 19-24 years (IMA) / 19-25 years (OTA) | Degree from a recognized university. | UPSC written exam followed by SSB interview |

### Indian Air Force Entry Schemes

| Type of Entry | Commission | Age Limit | Qualification | Selection Process |
| :------------------------------------------ | :--------- | :-------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------- |
| Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT - Technical) | PC and SSC | 20 – 26 Yrs | BE / BTech in a notified discipline with at least 60% marks, plus Physics and Maths in 10+2. | AFCAT CBT, Engineering Knowledge Test (EKT), and AFSB Interview |
| CDS (for Air Force Academy) | Permanent | 20– 24 Yrs | Engineering Degree, plus Physics and Maths in 10+2. | UPSC written exam followed by SSB interview |

### Indian Navy Entry Schemes

| Type of Entry | Commission | Age Limit | Qualification | Selection Process |
| :------------------------------------------ | :---------- | :--------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------ |
| Short Service Commission (Executive & Technical) | Short Service | 19½ - 25 Yrs | BE/ BTech in a notified discipline with at least 60% marks. | SSB Interview (no written exam; based on engineering marks) |
| Special Naval Architect Entry Scheme (SNAES) | Permanent | 21- 25 Yrs | BE / BTech in Naval Architecture with at least 60% marks. | SSB Interview |

## Understanding the Selection Process

Joining the armed forces after engineering involves a rigorous selection process, which can vary based on the chosen entry scheme. This generally includes written examinations, comprehensive interviews, and medical evaluations.

### Combined Defence Services Examination (CDS)

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) conducts the Combined Defence Services (CDS) Examination twice a year. This is a common and highly competitive path for graduates aspiring to become commissioned officers in the Indian Army (through IMA), Navy (through INA), and Air Force (through AFA), or for Short Service Commission in the Officers' Training Academy (OTA). The process involves a written examination followed by an SSB Interview.

### Service Selection Board (SSB) Interview

The SSB Interview is a crucial five-day evaluation process for almost all officer entry schemes. It assesses a candidate's officer-like qualities through various tests, including psychological tests, group tasks, and personal interviews. Performance in the SSB is paramount for selection.

### Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT)

For those targeting the Indian Air Force, the AFCAT is a computer-based test for technical and non-technical branches. Technical branch candidates also need to clear the Engineering Knowledge Test (EKT). Successful candidates then proceed to the Air Force Selection Board (AFSB) interview.

Choosing a career in the Indian Armed Forces after engineering is a commendable decision that promises a challenging yet rewarding journey of service and leadership.
