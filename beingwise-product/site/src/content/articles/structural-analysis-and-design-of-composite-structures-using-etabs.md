# Structural Analysis and Design of Composite Structures using ETABS by Skill Lync: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This page offers a detailed look at a specialized program focused on the structural analysis and design of composite structures, utilizing the industry-standard software ETABS. This course is designed for individuals looking to gain expertise in modern building design, particularly for tall structures and advanced composite materials.

## Key takeaways

*   The program is delivered entirely in English through a self-study, video, and text-based format, offering flexibility.
*   It covers a comprehensive 13-week syllabus, progressing from fundamental structural concepts to advanced composite design using ETABS.
*   Participants will learn to apply Indian Standards (IS codes) for wind and seismic load estimation and design.
*   The course culminates in a certificate from Skill Lync upon successful completion.

## Course Structure and Delivery

This program is structured as a self-paced learning experience, making it accessible for students and professionals with varying schedules. The entire curriculum is provided in English, utilizing a blend of video lectures and textual materials to facilitate understanding. This flexible delivery model allows learners to progress through the material at their own pace, revisiting complex topics as needed.

## Program Fees and Certification

The total fee for the "Structural Analysis and Design of Composite Structures using ETABS" program is ₹40,000. This amount covers all aspects of the course content and access to learning materials. Upon successful completion of the program, participants will be awarded a certificate directly by Skill Lync, acknowledging their acquired skills and knowledge in this specialized area of structural engineering.

## Detailed Syllabus

The 13-week syllabus is meticulously designed to provide a deep dive into the subject, starting with foundational principles and advancing to complex design applications. Here's a week-by-week breakdown:

*   **Week 1-2: Structural Systems for Tall Buildings**
    *   Introduction to tall buildings, global examples (as of 2020), and fundamental structural mechanics.
    *   Exploration of various structural systems, their behavior, and challenges, including case studies like Shanghai Tower and Taipei 101.
    *   Understanding p-δ analysis.
*   **Week 3: Loads on Tall Buildings**
    *   Detailed study of gravity, wind (as per IS 875: 2015 Part 3), and earthquake loads (as per IS 1893: 2016).
    *   Practical application of load estimation and application within ETABS, focusing on design philosophy for dynamic loads.
*   **Week 4: Modal Analysis of Tall Buildings**
    *   Underlying physics of modal analysis, D'Alembert's Principle, and the equation of motion.
    *   Influence of modal response and idealized Single Degree of Freedom (SDOF) structures.
*   **Week 5-6: Structural Analysis and Design of Concrete Structures**
    *   Properties of concrete, characteristic strength, stress-strain curves, creep, shrinkage, and corrosion.
    *   Design methodology, limit state design, load combinations, and detailed design of slabs, beams, columns, and walls.
*   **Week 7-8: Structural Analysis and Design of Steel Structures**
    *   Comparative study of steel versus concrete, structural steel sections, and classification.
    *   Allowable stress design vs. limit state design, design of tension and compression members, including buckling criteria.
    *   Design of beams, deflection criteria, and connections (bolted and welded).
*   **Week 9-10: Fundamentals of Composite Structures**
    *   Introduction to composite structures, their practical applications, and advantages.
    *   Design philosophy and construction of composite deck slabs, beams, columns, and walls.
    *   Understanding load transfer, design criteria, and software advances in composite design.
*   **Week 11-12: Framing Plan for Composite Deck Slab**
    *   Exploration of various composite deck slab profiles and design parameters.
    *   Manual calculations, deck sizing, selection, and modeling/analysis in ETABS.
    *   Construction details, shear connectors, shear studs, floor vibration, and cambering techniques.
*   **Week 13: Structural Design of Composite Buildings using ETABS**
    *   Comprehensive study of the practical application of ETABS for the structural design of composite buildings.
