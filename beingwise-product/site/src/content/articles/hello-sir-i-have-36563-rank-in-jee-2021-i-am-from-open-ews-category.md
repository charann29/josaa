## Why College Name Matters

Every engineering college in India, whether it's an IIT, NIT, GFTI, or a state-level institution, has its own set of admission criteria and cut-off ranks. These cut-offs are influenced by factors such as the college's popularity, the number of seats available in each program, and the overall performance of students in the JEE Main exam that year. Without knowing which college you're considering, it's impossible to tell if your rank of 36563 (EWS category) is competitive for admission.

For instance, a rank that might secure a seat in a newer NIT could be far from the cut-off for a top-tier IIT. Similarly, admission to a popular Computer Science Engineering program will likely have a higher cut-off than, say, Civil Engineering, even within the same institution.

## Understanding EWS Category Benefits

As an EWS (Economically Weaker Section) candidate, you are eligible for reservations in admissions to centrally funded technical institutions (CFTIs) like IITs, NITs, and IIITs, as well as many state government colleges. This reservation means that there are a certain percentage of seats specifically allocated for EWS candidates, often resulting in slightly different (and sometimes more favourable) cut-off ranks compared to the Open (General) category. 

It's important to have your EWS certificate ready and valid as per the guidelines set by NTA and the respective counselling authorities. This certificate is essential to avail the benefits of your category during the counselling process.

## How to Check Your Chances

Once you identify the college and program you're interested in, here's how you can research your admission prospects:

1.  **Consult Previous Year's Cut-offs:** The most reliable way to gauge your chances is to look at the opening and closing ranks from previous years. The official JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) portal is the primary source for this data for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. Many state counselling portals also publish similar information for their affiliated colleges.
2.  **Look for EWS Specific Ranks:** When checking cut-offs, always look for the EWS category specific ranks for the particular program you desire. Do not compare your rank with the General category cut-offs directly.
3.  **Consider All Rounds:** Admission counselling often involves multiple rounds. A program that might seem out of reach in the first round could become accessible in later rounds due to seat vacancies.
4.  **NIRF Rankings:** While not directly related to cut-offs, the NIRF (National Institutional Ranking Framework) rankings can give you an idea of a college's overall standing and quality, which often correlates with its admission competitiveness.
