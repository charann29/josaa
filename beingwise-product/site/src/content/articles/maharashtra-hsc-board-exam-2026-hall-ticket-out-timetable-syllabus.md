# Maharashtra HSC Board Exam 2026: Hall Ticket (Out), Timetable, Syllabus, Question Papers, Pattern, Result

The Maharashtra Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) board examination is a crucial milestone for students in the state, opening doors to various higher education opportunities. Conducted annually by the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education (MSBSHSE), these exams cover Science, Commerce, and Arts streams.

## Key takeaways

*   The Maharashtra HSC exams for 2026 were conducted between February 10 and March 11, 2026.
*   Approximately 14.5 lakh students appeared for the examination across nine divisional boards.
*   Students must achieve at least 35% marks in each subject and in aggregate to pass the examination.
*   Results are typically announced in May, with supplementary exams held in June and July for those needing to improve their scores.

## Understanding the Maharashtra HSC Exam

The Maharashtra HSC board exams are administered by the MSBSHSE across its nine divisions: Pune, Mumbai, Aurangabad, Nashik, Kolhapur, Amravati, Latur, Nagpur, and Ratnagiri. The examination comprises both theory and practical components, designed to assess students' comprehensive understanding of their chosen subjects. To successfully clear the exam, candidates must secure a minimum of 35% marks in each individual subject and overall.

## Important Dates for Maharashtra HSC 2026

Staying informed about key dates is vital for all students. The timetable for the Maharashtra HSC 2026 exams was released on October 31, 2025. The main examinations took place from February 10 to March 11, 2026. Following the main exams, the board typically conducts supplementary examinations for students who wish to improve their grades or who did not pass in the initial attempt. For 2026, these supplementary exams are scheduled from June 24 to July 16, with results usually declared in August.

## Maharashtra HSC Hall Ticket

The hall ticket is a mandatory document for appearing in the Maharashtra HSC examinations. It contains essential details such as the student's name, roll number, examination center, and subject schedule. Hall tickets are typically distributed by schools a few weeks before the exams begin. Students must collect their hall tickets from their respective institutions and ensure all details are correct. It is crucial to carry the hall ticket to the examination center on each exam day, as entry will not be permitted without it.

## Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The MSBSHSE publishes the detailed syllabus for all subjects across Science, Commerce, and Arts streams. Students are strongly advised to refer to the official syllabus available on the board's website to understand the topics and units covered for the 2026 examinations. The exam pattern outlines the structure of the question papers, including the types of questions (e.g., multiple-choice, short answer, long answer), marking scheme, and duration for each paper. Familiarity with both the syllabus and exam pattern is crucial for effective preparation.

## Preparing for the Maharashtra HSC Exams

Effective preparation is key to performing well in the HSC exams. Here are some widely recommended strategies:

*   **Thorough Syllabus Review:** Begin by understanding the complete official syllabus for all your subjects.
*   **Understand the Exam Pattern:** Familiarize yourself with the types of questions and marking scheme.
*   **Practice with Past Papers:** Solve previous years' question papers to understand the exam format and manage time effectively.
*   **Create Concise Notes:** Maintain notes of important concepts, formulas, and key points for quick revision.
*   **Prioritize Well-being:** Ensure you get adequate sleep, maintain a healthy diet, and incorporate light exercise to stay focused and reduce stress.

By following these guidelines and staying updated with official announcements from the MSBSHSE, students can approach their Maharashtra HSC exams with confidence.
