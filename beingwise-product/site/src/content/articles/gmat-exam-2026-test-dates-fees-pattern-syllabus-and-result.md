# GMAT Exam 2026 - Test Dates, Fees, Pattern, Syllabus and Result

The Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT) is a globally recognized computer-adaptive examination for students aspiring to pursue master's programs in business and management, including MBA degrees, at leading institutions worldwide. Administered by the Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC), the GMAT assesses a candidate's analytical, quantitative, verbal, and data interpretation abilities.

## Key takeaways

*   The GMAT exam evaluates skills crucial for success in business and management programs globally.
*   As of July 1, 2024, the GMAT Focus Edition is now simply called the "GMAT exam," retaining its structure and content.
*   There is no longer a lifetime limit on the number of GMAT attempts, though a rolling 12-month limit of five attempts remains.
*   The exam features three equally weighted sections: Verbal Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, and Data Insights, with a total duration of 2 hours and 15 minutes.

## Understanding the GMAT Exam

The GMAT is designed to meet the demands of business schools by focusing on higher-order critical reasoning and data literacy. It's an efficient and flexible test that can be taken either from home or at an authorized test center. The total score ranges from 205 to 805.

### Significant Changes to the GMAT

GMAC has introduced important updates to the GMAT exam. Notably, the previous lifetime attempt limit has been removed. However, candidates can still take the GMAT a maximum of five times within any rolling 12-month period. These changes aim to provide greater flexibility for test-takers.

## GMAT Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The GMAT exam features a streamlined pattern compared to previous versions. It comprises three distinct sections, each 45 minutes long, with no essay component. Candidates have the flexibility to attempt these sections in any preferred order. Additionally, test-takers can bookmark and review questions within a section.

### Exam Structure:

*   **Quantitative Reasoning:** Measures problem-solving skills and the ability to interpret graphical data and analyze information.
*   **Verbal Reasoning:** Assesses comprehension, critical reasoning, and the ability to correct written material for grammar and style.
*   **Data Insights:** A new section that evaluates a candidate's ability to analyze and interpret data from various sources to solve complex problems. This section replaces the earlier Sentence Correction questions.

All three sections contribute equally to the overall score, emphasizing a balanced assessment of a candidate's strengths.

## GMAT Exam Dates and Booking

The GMAT is available year-round, offering numerous test dates across various cities in India. It is advisable for candidates to book their test center and date at least two to three months in advance to secure their preferred slot. The official GMAT portal provides up-to-date information on available dates and test center locations.

For example, in major metropolitan areas such as New Delhi & NCR (including Noida, Gurugram, and Faridabad), numerous dates are typically available throughout March and April 2026, often on most days of the month. Candidates should check the official GMAT website for the most current schedule and to locate the nearest test center.

## GMAT Exam Fees

The GMAT exam fee is USD 275. It's important to note that this fee may vary slightly depending on the region and any applicable taxes. Additional fees may apply for rescheduling or cancelling an exam. Candidates should refer to the official GMAT website for the most accurate and current fee structure.

## GMAT Result and Score Reporting

After completing the GMAT exam, candidates will receive their unofficial scores immediately. Official scores, which include a detailed breakdown and percentile rankings, are typically available within a few days. These scores can then be sent to the business schools of your choice. The GMAT score is valid for five years.

The GMAT is a crucial step for many aspiring business leaders, opening doors to a wide array of management programs globally. We wish you the best in your preparation!
