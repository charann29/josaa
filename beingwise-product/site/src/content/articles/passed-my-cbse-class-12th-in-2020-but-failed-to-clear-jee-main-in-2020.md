# I passed my CBSE Class 12th in 2020 but failed to clear JEE MAIN in 2020 as I met an accident in 2021 so this year I will give board exam again with NIOS so my doubt is which marksheet should I use for BITSAT and JEE MAIN ?? As I have not appeared in BITSAT in 2020 but appeared for JEE MAIN 2020 ??

It's completely understandable to feel confused when navigating admission processes after a gap year and reappearing for board exams. Let's clarify which mark sheet you should use for your upcoming BITSAT and JEE Main applications.

## Key takeaways

*   You can generally use either your 2020 CBSE mark sheet or your 2022 NIOS mark sheet, provided both meet the eligibility criteria.
*   It is often advisable to use the mark sheet from your most recent Class 12th examination (NIOS 2022) for consistency and to reflect your latest academic standing.
*   For JEE Advanced, specific rules apply regarding the number of attempts and the year of Class 12th appearance.
*   Always refer to the latest official information bulletins from NTA for JEE Main and BITS Pilani for BITSAT.

## Choosing Your Mark Sheet for JEE Main

The National Testing Agency (NTA), which conducts JEE Main, requires candidates to have passed Class 12th or an equivalent examination. Since you have passed Class 12th twice – once with CBSE in 2020 and again with NIOS in 2022 – both mark sheets technically prove your qualification. However, the NTA information bulletin generally asks for details of the qualifying examination. Using your 2022 NIOS mark sheet would be logical as it is your most recent qualification. Ensure that all subjects required for JEE Main (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) are present on the NIOS mark sheet with the necessary passing grades.

## BITSAT Mark Sheet Considerations

For BITSAT, conducted by BITS Pilani, the eligibility criteria also mandate passing Class 12th with specific subject combinations and minimum aggregate marks. Similar to JEE Main, you have two valid Class 12th qualifications. Opting for your 2022 NIOS mark sheet is generally recommended. This helps present a consistent academic record, especially since you are reappearing for competitive exams this year. Double-check the BITSAT information brochure for any specific clauses regarding multiple Class 12th qualifications or gap years, though typically, the most recent valid qualification is accepted.

## JEE Advanced Eligibility After Reappearing for Class 12th

This is a crucial point. JEE Advanced has strict rules regarding the year of Class 12th examination and the number of attempts. Generally, a candidate can attempt JEE Advanced a maximum of two times in two consecutive years. If you passed Class 12th in 2020, your eligibility for JEE Advanced would typically have been for 2020 and 2021. Reappearing for Class 12th through NIOS in 2022 might not automatically grant you a fresh attempt at JEE Advanced for 2022 if your previous attempts have been exhausted based on your 2020 Class 12th pass. It is absolutely essential to consult the official JEE Advanced information brochure carefully to understand the precise rules for candidates who have passed Class 12th in previous years and are reappearing, as this can be a complex area. The official JoSAA portal also provides detailed eligibility criteria.

## Preparing for Your Exams

Regardless of which mark sheet you use, focused preparation is key. Here are some general tips:

*   **Understand the Syllabus:** Thoroughly review the latest JEE Main and BITSAT syllabi. They are largely based on the Class 11th and 12th NCERT curriculum, but specific topics and weightage can vary.
*   **Familiarise with Exam Pattern:** Know the number of questions, marking scheme, duration, and section-wise distribution for both exams. This helps in strategic time management.
*   **Create a Study Plan:** Develop a realistic study schedule that covers all subjects, allocates time for revision, and incorporates regular practice.
*   **Practice with Mock Tests:** Regularly attempt mock tests and previous year's papers. This helps you get accustomed to the exam environment, identify your weak areas, and improve speed and accuracy. Many resources are available for this, including official NTA practice papers.

Wishing you the very best for your exams and future academic journey! Always rely on official sources for the most accurate and up-to-date information.
