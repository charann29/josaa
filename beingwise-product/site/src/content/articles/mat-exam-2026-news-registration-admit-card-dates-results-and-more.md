# MAT Exam 2026 News: Registration, Admit Card, Dates, Results & more

The Management Aptitude Test (MAT) is a widely recognized entrance examination for admission to MBA and other management programs across numerous business schools in India. Conducted by the All India Management Association (AIMA), MAT offers flexibility by being held four times a year in three different formats: Computer Based Test (CBT), Paper Based Test (PBT), and Internet Based Test (IBT).

## Key takeaways

*   **Multiple attempts:** MAT is conducted multiple times a year, allowing candidates several opportunities to take the exam and improve their scores.
*   **Flexible formats:** Aspirants can choose between Computer Based Testing (CBT), Paper Based Testing (PBT), or Internet Based Testing (IBT) modes based on their preference and convenience.
*   **Widespread acceptance:** Over 1,400 business schools across India accept MAT scores for their MBA/PGDM programs.
*   **February 2026 dates announced:** The examination dates for the February 2026 MAT session have been released, and registration is currently open.

Some institutions also have their application windows open. For instance, ISBR Business School's application deadline for its 2026 MBA/PGDM programs is April 21, 2026. Applicants should verify specific institutional deadlines in addition to the MAT exam dates.

## Understanding MAT Exam Modes

MAT is unique in its offering of three distinct examination modes, catering to different candidate needs and logistical preferences:

*   **Computer Based Test (CBT):** This mode involves taking the exam at designated test centers using computers. It offers a standardized environment and is often preferred by those comfortable with digital interfaces.
*   **Paper Based Test (PBT):** For candidates who prefer traditional pen-and-paper examinations, the PBT mode is available at various test centers. This format might be more familiar to some test-takers.
*   **Internet Based Test (IBT):** This allows candidates to take the exam from the convenience of their home or any suitable location with a stable internet connection. AIMA implements strict proctoring measures to maintain exam integrity in this mode.

Candidates should carefully consider which mode suits them best when registering for the MAT exam.

## Institutions Accepting MAT Scores

A significant number of management institutes and universities throughout India consider MAT scores for admission to their MBA and PGDM programs. This broad acceptance makes MAT a valuable option for many aspiring management professionals.

Some of the prominent institutions that accept MAT scores include:

*   **Guwahati University (GU):** A public university in Guwahati, offering MBA programs.
*   **Savitribai Phule Pune University (SPPU):** Located in Pune, this public university is a well-known name in higher education.
*   **University of Mumbai (MU):** A large public university based in Fort, Mumbai.
*   **Delhi School of Management, DTU:** Situated in Rohini, Delhi, this public institution's MBA program has a fee of approximately ₹3.12 Lakhs for a two-year duration.

These are just a few examples; the official AIMA portal provides a comprehensive list of over 1,400 institutes that accept MAT scores. Candidates are encouraged to check the specific admission criteria of their target business schools, as some may also require scores from other exams like CAT, XAT, CMAT, or ATMA.
