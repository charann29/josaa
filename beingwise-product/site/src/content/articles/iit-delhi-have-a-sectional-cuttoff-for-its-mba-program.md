# Does IIT Delhi Have a Sectional Cutoff for its MBA Program?

Aspiring for an MBA from a prestigious institution like IIT Delhi's Department of Management Studies (DMS) is a common goal for many Indian students. Understanding the admission criteria, particularly regarding CAT scores and cutoffs, is crucial for effective preparation.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIT Delhi DMS primarily uses a Shortlisting Index (SI) for MBA admissions, not just a raw CAT percentile.
*   While not officially published for every year, past trends suggest minimum sectional CAT percentiles are considered.
*   The SI incorporates your CAT score, academic record (10th, 12th, graduation), additional qualifications, and work experience.
*   There are no specified minimum CAT percentiles for SC, ST, and PWD categories.

## Understanding the Shortlisting Process

IIT Delhi's DMS evaluates MBA applicants through a comprehensive process that goes beyond just the overall CAT percentile. Instead, they use a Shortlisting Index (SI) to identify candidates for the subsequent Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds. This index ensures a holistic assessment of each applicant's profile.

## Minimum CAT Percentiles (Based on Past Trends)

Although IIT Delhi DMS does not always release official cutoff percentiles for each academic session, we can look at previous shortlisting criteria to understand the likely expectations. These figures represent the minimal percentiles considered necessary for different categories:

| Category | Quantitative Ability (QA) | Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC) | Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR) | Overall CAT Percentile |
| :------- | :----------------------- | :-------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- | :---------------------- |
| General  | 60                       | 60                                            | 60                                             | 95                      |
| OBC      | 50                       | 50                                            | 50                                             | 85                      |

It's important to note that for candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disabilities (PWD) categories, there is typically no specified minimum CAT percentile requirement.

## How the Shortlisting Index (SI) is Calculated

The Shortlisting Index (SI) is a weighted score designed to give a complete picture of an applicant's potential. The formula for SI takes into account several factors:

`SI = 80 x (Overall CAT Score / Maximum CAT Score of the given year) + Weights for Bachelor's Degree Marks + Weights for Class 12 Marks + Weights for Class 10th Marks + Weights for Additional Qualifications (e.g., Master's degree, Diploma) + Weights for Work Experience (if any)`

This formula highlights that your CAT performance, while significant, is just one component. Your academic history and any relevant professional experience also play a crucial role in determining your eligibility for the next stages of the admission process.

## Academic and Professional Weightage

Beyond your CAT score, IIT Delhi DMS assigns specific weightage to your academic performance in Class 10, Class 12, and your undergraduate degree. Additionally, any postgraduate qualifications like a Master's degree or a diploma, along with relevant work experience, can add valuable points to your Shortlisting Index. Detailed information on how these additional points are calculated is usually available on the official DMS IIT Delhi admissions portal.

Securing admission to IIT Delhi's MBA program requires a strong overall profile, encompassing not just your CAT performance but also your academic consistency and relevant experience.
