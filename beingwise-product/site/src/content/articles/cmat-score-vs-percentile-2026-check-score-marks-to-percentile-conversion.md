# CMAT Score vs Percentile 2026: Check Score/ Marks to Percentile Conversion

Understanding how your raw score in the CMAT exam translates into a percentile is crucial for MBA admissions. While your score reflects the total marks you've achieved, your percentile indicates how well you've performed relative to all other test-takers. This conversion is key to strategizing your college applications.

## Key takeaways

*   CMAT results are always declared in percentiles, not raw scores.
*   A higher raw score generally leads to a higher percentile, but the exact conversion depends on factors like the total number of test-takers and the overall difficulty of the exam.
*   For admission to top MBA programs, aiming for a CMAT percentile between 95 and 99 is often considered desirable.
*   Your CMAT percentile will directly influence which colleges you can apply to, as admissions are based on percentile cut-offs.

## Understanding CMAT Scores and Percentiles

Your CMAT score is simply the sum of marks you obtain across all sections of the test. Each correct answer typically earns you four marks, while an incorrect answer usually results in a deduction of one mark. Unanswered questions do not carry any penalty.

On the other hand, your CMAT percentile is a comparative measure. If you achieve a 90 percentile, it means that 90% of the candidates who took the CMAT exam scored less than you. The higher your percentile, the better your relative performance.

## How CMAT Percentiles are Calculated

NTA, the body responsible for conducting CMAT, calculates percentiles using a specific formula. This formula considers the total number of candidates who appeared for the exam. The percentile for a candidate is determined by taking the total number of candidates who scored less than or equal to that candidate, dividing it by the total number of candidates, and then multiplying by 100. This ensures a fair comparison across all test sessions, if the exam is held in multiple shifts.

For admission to highly sought-after MBA programs, colleges typically look for CMAT percentiles in the higher ranges. Institutes like JBIMS, SIMSREE, KJ Somaiya, Welingkar, and PUMBA often have cut-offs upwards of the 95th percentile.
