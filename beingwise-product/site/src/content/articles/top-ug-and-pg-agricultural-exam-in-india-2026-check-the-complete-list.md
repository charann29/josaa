# Top UG & PG Agricultural Exam in India 2026: Check the Complete List

For students aspiring to build a career in agriculture, understanding the various entrance examinations is crucial. These exams open doors to prestigious undergraduate and postgraduate programs across India, leading to diverse opportunities in a vital sector.

## Key Takeaways

*   Admission to most agricultural degree programs in India relies on entrance exam scores.
*   The Common University Entrance Test (CUET) is a major pathway for both UG and PG admissions to many agricultural universities, including those approved by ICAR.
*   Several state-level examinations also provide access to agricultural colleges within specific regions.
*   Preparing for these exams requires a clear understanding of their structure, syllabus, and important dates.

## Major Agricultural Entrance Exams

India offers a range of entrance examinations for students aiming for UG, PG, and even PhD programs in agriculture and allied fields. Here are some of the prominent ones:

### CUET (ICAR UG)

The National Testing Agency (NTA) conducts the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) for undergraduate admissions, which includes the ICAR UG component. This exam is the gateway to Bachelor's Degree programs in Agriculture and Allied Sciences (excluding Veterinary Sciences) at Agricultural Universities. It covers 20% of university seats, and 100% of seats in specific institutions like RLBCAU, Jhansi; Dr. RPCAU Pusa, Bihar; ICAR-NDRI, Karnal; and ICAR-IARI, New Delhi and its hubs. Successful candidates can apply for 13 Bachelor's degrees within the ICAR-AU system. Prospective students must check the detailed eligibility criteria on the official NTA portal before applying online.

**Courses Offered via CUET (ICAR UG) include:**

*   BSc (Hons.) Agriculture
*   BSc (Hons.) Horticulture
*   BSc (Hons.) Community Science
*   BSc (Hons.) Food Nutrition and Dietetics
*   BTech. Dairy Technology
*   BTech. Food Technology
*   BFSc (Bachelor of Fisheries Science)
*   BTech. Agricultural Engineering
*   BTech. Biotechnology
*   BSc (Hons.) Forestry

### CUET PG Exam

For postgraduate studies, the CUET PG exam is a significant route. Also conducted by NTA, this examination facilitates admission to various Master's degree programs across central and participating universities, including those offering agricultural and allied science courses.

### ICAR AIEEA PG / AICE JRF Exam

The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) conducts the All India Entrance Examination for Admission (AIEEA) for postgraduate programs. Simultaneously, the All India Competitive Examination (AICE) is held for Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and Senior Research Fellowship (SRF) leading to PhD admissions. These exams are crucial for students targeting advanced research and academic careers in agriculture.

### State-Level Agricultural Entrance Exams

Beyond the national-level exams, several states conduct their own entrance tests for admission to agricultural universities and colleges within their jurisdiction. Some notable examples include:

*   **Rajasthan JET Exam:** The Joint Entrance Test (JET) is for admissions to various undergraduate agriculture and allied science courses in Rajasthan.
*   **UPCATET Entrance Exam:** The Uttar Pradesh Combined Agricultural and Technology Entrance Test (UPCATET) is conducted for admissions to UG and PG programs in agricultural universities across Uttar Pradesh.
*   **SHIATS Entrance Exam:** Sam Higginbottom University of Agriculture, Technology and Sciences (SHUATS) conducts its own entrance exam for various courses.
*   **MCAER CET:** The Maharashtra Council of Agricultural Education and Research Common Entrance Test (MCAER CET) is for admissions to UG and PG agricultural courses in Maharashtra.

Choosing the right examination depends on your desired course, the institutions you wish to attend, and your eligibility. Always refer to the official examination bodies for precise details and application procedures.
