# Best Time to Start CAT 2026 Preparation: Dates, Timetable, Schedule, Prep Videos

Starting your preparation for the Common Admission Test (CAT) 2026 at the right moment can significantly boost your chances of success. This guide will help you understand the ideal timelines and strategies for your CAT journey.

## Key Takeaways

*   CAT 2026 is scheduled for November 29, 2026, making March/April 2026 an excellent time to begin your preparation.
*   A focused 6-month plan, even starting in May-June, can still lead to success if you're dedicated.
*   Aim to complete the core syllabus for Data Interpretation, Logical Reasoning, Verbal Ability, and Quantitative Aptitude by August 2026.
*   Consistency and regular practice, including mock tests, are crucial regardless of when you start.

## When Should You Start CAT 2026 Preparation?

The optimal time to begin preparing for the CAT 2026 exam largely depends on your individual strengths, weaknesses, and how much time you can commit daily. While some candidates might start as early as January or February, and others as late as August, a common recommendation is to kick off your studies by March or April 2026.

Considering CAT 2026 will be held on November 29, 2026, starting in March or April gives you a solid 7-8 months of preparation time. This duration allows for comprehensive coverage of the syllabus, ample practice, and sufficient time for revision and mock tests.

Even if you begin a bit later, in May or June 2026, a well-structured and focused 6-month plan can still be highly effective. The key is consistent effort and a clear strategy.

## Expert Advice on CAT Preparation Timelines

According to prominent MBA experts, completing the entire CAT syllabus by August is a strategic goal. This means candidates should ideally start their preparation no later than May. Stretching the syllabus completion too close to the exam date can leave insufficient time for crucial practice and revision.

During June, July, and August, the focus should be on thoroughly covering the core CAT syllabus, which includes Data Interpretation, Logical Reasoning, Verbal Ability, and Quantitative Aptitude. After this foundational phase, aspirants can transition to building speed and accuracy, eventually moving on to full-length mock tests.

## CAT Preparation 2026: A Month-Wise Timetable

Here’s a general month-wise timetable to guide your CAT 2026 preparation, assuming an ideal start in March or April:

| **Timeline**         | **Focus Areas**                                                                                                                              |
| :------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **March - May 2026** | **Foundation Building:** Understand basic concepts, cover fundamental topics in all sections (Quant, VARC, DILR). Focus on accuracy.      |
| **June - August 2026** | **Syllabus Completion & Practice:** Finish remaining syllabus. Start practicing topic-wise questions. Begin taking sectional tests.          |
| **September - October 2026** | **Intensive Practice & Mock Tests:** Focus on speed and accuracy. Take full-length mock tests regularly. Analyze performance and identify weak areas. |
| **November 2026**    | **Revision & Strategy:** Revise key concepts. Work on test-taking strategies. Solve previous year's papers. Maintain a positive mindset.         |

If you start later, for instance, in July 2026, you will need to adopt a more intensive, short-term plan, likely condensing the initial phases and focusing heavily on efficiency and smart study techniques over four months.

## The Role of Toppers' Experiences

Many CAT toppers emphasize the importance of consistent effort over an extended period. While some may have a natural aptitude, nearly all successful candidates dedicate significant time to preparation. Their experiences often highlight that a longer preparation window allows for a more relaxed and thorough understanding of concepts, reducing last-minute stress. They also stress the value of regular mock tests to simulate exam conditions and refine strategy.

Ultimately, the best time to start is when you can commit consistently. Good luck with your CAT 2026 journey!
