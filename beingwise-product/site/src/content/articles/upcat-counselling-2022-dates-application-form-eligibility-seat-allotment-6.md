# UPCAT Counselling 2022: Dates, Application Form, Eligibility, Seat Allotment

For students aspiring to join professional courses in Uttar Pradesh, the Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test (UPCET), previously known as UPSEE, was a key gateway. This guide outlines important details about the counselling process for 2022 admissions.

## Key takeaways

*   **Changes in 2022:** The UPCET exam was discontinued for 2022 admissions and replaced by national-level entrance tests like CUET, JEE Main, and NATA.
*   **Applicable Universities:** Admissions through these national exams are for colleges affiliated with Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU), Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT), and Harcourt Butler Technical University (HBTU).
*   **Course Coverage:** Various undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) courses, including B.Tech, B.Pharm, BHMCT, B.Des, BFAD/BFA, M.Tech, MBA, MCA, M.Pharm, M.Arch, M.Des, and MURP, are covered.
*   **Eligibility:** General eligibility criteria typically require passing a qualifying exam, often with a domicile connection to Uttar Pradesh.

## Evolution of UPCET for 2022 Admissions

In a significant shift for the 2022 academic year, the Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test (UPCET) was discontinued. Admissions to various undergraduate and postgraduate programs across universities like AKTU, MMMUT, and HBTU are now facilitated through other established national entrance examinations. For undergraduate courses such as B.Tech and B.Arch, JEE Main and NATA are the primary routes, respectively. For other UG programs, including integrated courses like Integrated MBA and Integrated MCA, the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) is used. Similarly, postgraduate admissions for courses like MBA, MCA, M.Tech, M.Pharm, M.Arch, M.Des, and MURP are conducted via CUET PG.

## Courses Offered

The counselling process, whether through UPCET in previous years or the current national exams, covers a wide array of programs. These include:

*   BHMCT
*   MURP
*   B.Tech (Lateral Entry)
*   B.Des
*   BFAD/BFA
*   MCA/MCA (Integrated)/MCA (Lateral Entry)
*   M.Tech
*   M.Des
*   B.Pharm/B.Pharm (Lateral)
*   M.Arch
*   M.Pharm
*   MBA/MBA (Integrated)

## Eligibility Criteria

While specific eligibility can vary by course and university, some general requirements for admissions through the counselling process are typically consistent:

*   **Age Limit:** There is generally no upper age limit for most courses.
*   **Academic Qualification:** Candidates must have successfully passed their qualifying examination. The minimum percentage often varies by program and category.
*   **Domicile Requirement:** Usually, candidates need to have passed their qualifying examination from an institution located in Uttar Pradesh. Alternatively, if the qualifying exam was from outside UP, parents must hold a Uttar Pradesh domicile certificate. However, exceptions can apply, allowing candidates who meet other criteria but not the domicile requirements to still be eligible.

## Counselling and Seat Allotment Process

The counselling process for admissions into UP state universities involves several steps, similar to those managed by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for various national exams. It typically includes online registration, choice filling for preferred courses and institutions, document verification, and subsequent seat allotment rounds. Candidates are allocated seats based on their merit rank in the respective entrance exam (e.g., JEE Main, CUET, NATA), their preferences, and the availability of seats. Further details on the exact schedule and procedures are usually released on the official university portals or the relevant counselling authority's website.

For the latest and most accurate information regarding admissions and counselling, students are always advised to refer to the official websites of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU), Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT), and Harcourt Butler Technical University (HBTU).
