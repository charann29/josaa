# CAT 2024 Topper Interview: Know How Vedant Chandewar Achieved 99.98 Percentile

Vedant Chandewar, a final-year Mechanical Engineering student from IIT Bombay, made an impressive mark in the CAT 2024 examination, securing a 99.98 percentile on his very first attempt. BeingWise brings you an exclusive chat with Vedant, where he shares his preparation journey, strategies, and key insights for aspiring CAT candidates.

## Key Takeaways

*   **First Attempt Success:** Vedant achieved this remarkable percentile in his debut CAT appearance, highlighting the effectiveness of his focused strategy.
*   **Self-Study Focus:** Despite joining a coaching institute, his preparation primarily revolved around self-study and an extensive number of mock tests.
*   **Strong Foundation:** His background in mathematics and prior competitive exam successes (NTSE, KVPY, MHT CET topper, INMO) provided a solid base.
*   **Balanced Approach:** While strong in QA and DILR, he actively worked on improving VARC through consistent reading.

## Vedant's CAT 2024 Scorecard

Vedant's sectional and overall scores demonstrate a strong performance across all areas:

| Section | Score | Percentile |
| :------ | :---- | :--------- |
| DILR    | 52.24 | 99.94      |
| VARC    | 32.92 | 96.66      |
| QA      | 53.15 | 99.99      |
| **Overall** | **-** | **99.98** |

Vedant initially aimed for a 99.99 percentile, noting that in previous years, a score of 132 sometimes equated to a perfect 100 percentile. However, he expressed satisfaction with his 99.98 percentile, especially considering it was his first time taking the exam.

## Academic Background and Achievements

Vedant is currently pursuing Mechanical Engineering at IIT Bombay. Hailing from Nagpur, his academic journey is marked by several significant achievements:

*   NTSE Scholar
*   KVPY Qualifier
*   MHT CET 2021 Maharashtra Topper (100 percentile)
*   Selected for the Indian National Mathematical Olympiad (INMO)

His father is an engineer, and his mother is a homemaker. Outside academics, Vedant enjoys playing cricket and listening to music.

## Preparation Strategy: When and How

Vedant started his CAT preparation in August 2024. Given his strong aptitude in mathematics, he found the Quantitative Aptitude (QA) and Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR) sections relatively easier to tackle. His overall strategy was heavily reliant on:

*   **Consistent Practice:** Solving daily practice questions provided by his coaching institute.
*   **Mock Tests:** He focused almost entirely on self-study and taking a large number of mock tests, rather than extensive classroom coaching or concept videos.

## Section-Wise Approach

Vedant adopted specific strategies for each section:

*   **VARC (Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension):** To enhance his reading comprehension, he regularly read articles from reputable international news sources like The New York Times and The Guardian.
*   **DILR (Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning):** He sharpened his analytical skills by consistently practicing puzzles and engaging in games like Sudoku.
*   **QA (Quantitative Aptitude):** His preparation for this section primarily involved solving questions from mock tests and practice materials provided by his coaching institute.

## The Role of Mock Tests

Vedant emphasized the critical importance of mock tests in his preparation. He attempted approximately 40 CAT mock tests, stating that they are invaluable for:

*   Understanding the exam pattern thoroughly.
*   Identifying and addressing weak areas effectively.
*   Developing crucial time management skills under exam conditions.

While he joined a coaching institute, it was primarily for access to their mock test series. His conceptual learning was largely self-directed.

## Exam Day Strategy

On the day of the exam, Vedant focused on maintaining his composure. He ensured he had adequate rest the night before, consumed a light breakfast, and arrived at the CAT exam centre on time. Before the exam commenced, he took five minutes to meditate, helping him to focus and remain calm throughout the test.

Vedant's journey is a testament to focused preparation, self-discipline, and the strategic use of mock tests. His success offers valuable lessons for all aspiring CAT candidates.
