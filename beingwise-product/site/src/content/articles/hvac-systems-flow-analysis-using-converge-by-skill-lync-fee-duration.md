# HVAC Systems Flow Analysis using Converge by Skill Lync: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This certification program offers a deep dive into computational fluid dynamics (CFD) specifically for HVAC applications, using industry-standard software. It's designed to equip participants with practical skills in simulating and analyzing fluid flow within various heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems.

## Key Takeaways

*   Focuses on applying CFD theory to real-world HVAC scenarios like data center cooling and heat exchangers.
*   Provides hands-on experience with CONVERGE STUDIO, ParaView, and command-line tools for simulations and post-processing.
*   Features expert-led instruction and opportunities for practical projects to reinforce learning.
*   Offers flexible learning options, including self-study and virtual classrooms, with comprehensive technical support.

## Course Overview

The program is structured to provide a thorough understanding of how computational fluid dynamics can be used to analyze and optimize HVAC systems. It covers critical aspects of internal flow, including phenomena like recirculation zones, cavitation, and various turbulence models. Participants will learn to set up and run complex simulations, interpret results, and apply their knowledge to components such as superchargers and turbochargers.

Beyond theoretical concepts, the course emphasizes practical application. Students will gain proficiency in using CONVERGE STUDIO for setting up simulations, ParaView for visualizing and analyzing results, and command-line interfaces (Cygwin/CMD) for executing simulations. The curriculum is enriched with projects that allow learners to apply their newfound skills in analyzing HVAC system flows.

## What You Will Learn

Upon successful completion of this program, participants are expected to have a robust understanding of CFD applications in HVAC and possess problem-solving capabilities in this domain. Key learning outcomes include:

*   Setting up internal flow problems, including defining moving boundaries and cleaning up complex geometries.
*   Initializing parameters like temperature, species concentration, and pressure across different flow regions.
*   Implementing turbulence modeling and simulations using advanced CFD solvers.
*   Understanding the mathematical underpinnings of RANS (Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes) equations and their derivations.
*   Selecting appropriate turbulence models for specific HVAC applications.
*   Simulating cavitation using the VOID FRACTION method.
*   Extracting crucial output data relevant to compressors and pumps.
*   Proficiently setting up CFD simulations using CONVERGE CFD software.

## Eligibility and Certification

To enroll in this certification course, candidates must hold a B.E. or B.Tech degree in Mechanical Engineering, Automotive Engineering, or Aerospace Engineering. Successful completion of the course leads to a 'Certificate of Completion'. Additionally, the top 5% of performers in each batch may be awarded a 'Certificate of Merit'.

## Course Fees and Learning Format

The program fee for the HVAC Systems Flow Analysis using Converge certification is Rs. 40,000. The course is delivered through a combination of video and text-based content, supporting both self-study and virtual classroom learning. The medium of instruction is English. Participants also benefit from live webinars, workshops, and career counseling services.
