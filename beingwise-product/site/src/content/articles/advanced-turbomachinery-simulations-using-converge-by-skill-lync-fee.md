# Advanced Turbomachinery Simulations using CONVERGE by Skill Lync: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

This certification course offers in-depth training on computational fluid dynamics (CFD) theory, specifically applied to internal flows within turbomachinery. It's designed to equip you with practical skills in simulating devices like superchargers, centrifugal pumps, and turbochargers using advanced software.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Comprehensive Skill Development:** Gain expertise in CFD theory and its practical application to turbomachinery simulations.
*   **Hands-on Software Experience:** Learn to use CONVERGE software, known for its automated meshing capabilities, to conduct complex simulations.
*   **Flexible Learning:** The course offers self-paced study options, virtual classrooms, and access to video and text-based materials.
*   **Industry-Relevant Curriculum:** Focuses on real-world problems, including fluid separation, recirculation zones, and performance curve analysis for various applications.

## Course Overview and Learning Experience

The Advanced Turbomachinery Simulations course spans three months and is delivered in English. It combines self-study with virtual classroom sessions, providing flexibility in how you learn. The curriculum delves into critical concepts such as fluid flow through turbomachines, identifying fluid separation points, and understanding the generation of recirculation zones. You'll also explore how blade and outlet runner shapes influence simulation results and learn to plot performance curves for different applications.

A significant advantage of this program is the opportunity to work with CONVERGE software. Its automated meshing feature can save considerable time and effort in the simulation process. Beyond the core content, the course covers important theoretical aspects like Cavitation, Turbulence (RANS), and Conjugate Heat Transfer (CHT).

## What You Will Learn

By the end of this program, you will develop a strong understanding of turbomachinery simulations and practical expertise in using CONVERGE CFD. Key learning outcomes include:

*   Setting up CFD simulations effectively using CONVERGE CFD.
*   Tackling complex problems involving multiple zones and defining thermodynamic properties like temperature and pressure.
*   Understanding and discussing the shock tube problem.
*   Simulating cavitation processes.
*   Creating and managing volumetric regions within your models.
*   Initializing temperature, pressure, and species concentration in various flow regions.
*   Selecting appropriate turbulence and combustion models.
*   Inputting valve timing for accurate simulations.
*   Running simulation cases in a parallel computing environment.
*   Decomposing models into distinct boundaries and volumetric regions.

## Eligibility and Certification

To enroll in the Advanced Turbomachinery Simulations using CONVERGE course, candidates should have a foundational understanding of turbomachinery and fluid dynamics. A B.E. or B.Tech degree in mechanical, aerospace, or automotive engineering is required.

Upon successful completion of the course, all participants will receive a 'Certificate of Completion'. For those who demonstrate exceptional performance, specifically the top 5% of performers, a 'Certificate of Merit' will be awarded. The certificate is issued directly by Skill Lync.
