# AIIMS PG Exam Replaced by INI CET

For many years, the AIIMS PG examination was the gateway for aspiring medical professionals to pursue postgraduate studies at the prestigious All India Institute of Medical Sciences. However, this standalone exam has now been integrated into a larger, unified entrance test. Students aiming for postgraduate medical courses at AIIMS and other top institutions will now take the INI CET.

## Key Takeaways

*   The AIIMS PG exam is no longer conducted separately; it has been replaced by the Institute of National Importance Combined Entrance Test (INI CET).
*   INI CET serves as the single entrance exam for admission to MD, MS, DM, M.Ch, and MDS courses at all AIIMS branches, along with other Institutes of National Importance.
*   The examination is conducted twice a year, offering opportunities for admission in both January and July sessions.
*   INI CET is an online, objective-type examination that includes a negative marking system.

## What is INI CET?

INI CET, or the Institute of National Importance Combined Entrance Test, is the new standard for admission to postgraduate medical and dental courses across several premier institutions in India. This includes all branches of AIIMS (such as New Delhi, Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Jodhpur, Patna, Raipur, and Rishikesh), as well as PGIMER Chandigarh, NIMHANS Bengaluru, and JIPMER Puducherry. The exam streamlines the admission process, replacing individual entrance tests previously conducted by these institutes, including the AIIMS PG exam.

## Courses Offered Through INI CET

INI CET facilitates admissions to a comprehensive range of postgraduate medical and dental programs. Successful candidates can pursue:

*   Doctor of Medicine (MD)
*   Master of Surgery (MS)
*   Magister Chirurgiae (M.Ch - 6 years)
*   Doctorate of Medicine (DM - 6 years)
*   Master of Dental Surgery (MDS)

These courses are highly sought after, offering advanced specialisation and training in various medical and surgical disciplines at some of the country's leading institutions.

## Examination Structure and Pattern

INI CET is conducted in an online, computer-based format. The test consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) designed to assess a candidate's comprehensive knowledge of medical sciences. It's important for aspirants to be aware that the examination incorporates a negative marking scheme for incorrect answers. The duration of the examination is typically three hours, allowing ample time to attempt the questions. Candidates should refer to the official INI CET information brochure, released by AIIMS New Delhi, for the most accurate and up-to-date details regarding the exam pattern and syllabus.

## Admission Process and Counselling

Following the INI CET examination, qualified candidates participate in an online counselling process. This multi-round counselling allocates seats based on merit, candidate preferences, and the availability of seats in various postgraduate courses and institutions. The counselling typically includes mock rounds, followed by several official rounds and sometimes an open round, to ensure all available seats are filled. Candidates are advised to regularly check the official AIIMS New Delhi website for counselling schedules, seat allotment lists, and important instructions.

The transition from AIIMS PG to INI CET marks a significant change in postgraduate medical admissions, aiming for a unified and efficient selection process for India's future medical specialists.
