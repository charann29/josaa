# Circles in Maths: Definition, Formulas, Properties and Examples

Circles are fundamental geometric shapes that you'll encounter frequently in mathematics, especially in coordinate geometry. Understanding circles is not just crucial for your board exams, but also for competitive entrance tests like JEE Main, SRMJEE, BITSAT, WBJEE, and BCECE.

## Key takeaways

*   A circle is defined by all points that are an equal distance from a central point.
*   The standard equation of a circle depends on its center coordinates and radius.
*   The general form of a circle's equation allows us to find its center and radius easily.
*   The nature of a circle (real, point, or imaginary) is determined by the value under the square root in its radius formula.

## What is a Circle?

Imagine a single fixed point. Now, think about all the other points that are exactly the same distance away from that fixed point. If you connect all these points, you form a circle!

In mathematical terms:

A circle is the path (locus) of a point that moves in such a way that its distance from a fixed point remains constant.

*   The **fixed point** is known as the **center** of the circle (often denoted as O or C).
*   The **constant distance** is called the **radius** (denoted as 'r').

## Equations of a Circle

There are a couple of common ways to write the equation of a circle, depending on the information you have.

### Centre-Radius Form

This is perhaps the most intuitive form. If you know the center of the circle, say at coordinates (h, k), and its radius 'r', you can write its equation. Let (x, y) be any point on the circle.

Using the distance formula, the distance between (x, y) and (h, k) must be 'r':

√( (x - h)² + (y - k)² ) = r

Squaring both sides gives us the **Centre-Radius Form**:

(x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²

**Special Case: Circle Centered at the Origin**

If the center of the circle is at the origin (0, 0), the equation simplifies to:

x² + y² = r²

### General Form

The general form of a circle's equation comes from expanding the Centre-Radius Form:

(x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²

x² - 2hx + h² + y² - 2ky + k² = r²

Rearranging terms, we get:

x² + y² - 2hx - 2ky + (h² + k² - r²) = 0

This expanded form is typically written as the **General Equation of a Circle**:

x² + y² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0

Here's how to relate 'g', 'f', and 'c' back to the center and radius:

*   The coordinates of the **center** are (-g, -f).
*   The **radius** is given by the formula: r = √(g² + f² - c)

## Nature of a Circle

The value inside the square root for the radius (g² + f² - c) tells us about the nature of the circle. Let's look at the possibilities:

*   **Real Circle:** If g² + f² - c > 0, the radius is a real, positive number. This means we have a standard, visible circle.
*   **Point Circle:** If g² + f² - c = 0, the radius is 0. In this case, the circle essentially shrinks to a single point, which is its center.
*   **Imaginary Circle:** If g² + f² - c < 0, the value inside the square root is negative. This would result in an imaginary radius, meaning there is no real circle that satisfies the equation.

## JEE Main Trends for Circles

Circles are a recurring topic in competitive exams. For instance, looking at past JEE Main exams from 2013 to 2023, a total of twenty-one questions have been asked specifically on the concept of circles. This includes three questions in 2013, one in 2014, one in 2015, two in 2016, one in 2017, one in 2018, six in 2019, two in 2020, two in 2021, and two in 2022. This shows its consistent importance.

Mastering circles will certainly boost your performance in these crucial entrance examinations.
