# MAH CET 2026: Question Paper Analysis (April 6, 7 & 8), Answer Key, Result, Dates, Cutoffs

The Maharashtra Common Entrance Test (MAH CET) for MBA/MMS programs is a crucial state-level exam for those aspiring to pursue management education in Maharashtra. Conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, this exam opens doors to numerous government, university, and unaided management institutes across the state.

## Key Takeaways

*   The MAH CET 2026 exam was held on April 6, 7, and 8, with an additional session scheduled for May 9. Candidates have the unique advantage of attempting the exam twice and using their best score for admissions.
*   Overall, the April sessions were noted as easy to moderate in difficulty but lengthy, particularly the Logical Reasoning and Quantitative Aptitude sections.
*   The Abstract Reasoning and Verbal Ability sections were generally considered straightforward and less time-consuming.
*   Both Maharashtra domicile candidates and those from outside the state (under the All India category) are eligible to appear for the MAH CET.

## Understanding the MAH CET Exam

MAH CET is a computer-based test (CBT) that assesses candidates on various parameters essential for management studies. It is the most sought-after MBA entrance exam in Maharashtra due to its wide acceptance across a range of institutions. The exam scores are accepted by:

*   All Government of Maharashtra Management Education Institutes
*   University Departments of Management Education
*   University Managed Management Education Institutes
*   All Un-Aided Management Education Institutes covered under the relevant Act

Following the entrance exam, a Centralised Admission Process (CAP) is conducted for seat allotment. Candidates applying under the All India category can also use scores from other national-level exams like CAT, GMAT, CMAT, MAT, XAT, or ATMA during the CAP.

## Exam Analysis: April 6, 7 & 8 Sessions

The MAH CET 2026 exam, conducted across five slots on April 6, 7, and 8, presented a consistent pattern. Most slots were perceived as easy but required significant time due to the length of the questions. The fifth slot, however, was slightly more challenging compared to the others.

*   **Logical Reasoning & Quantitative Aptitude:** These sections were generally time-consuming, demanding careful management of the allotted time.
*   **Abstract Reasoning & Verbal Ability:** Candidates found these sections to be more direct and quicker to complete, allowing them to potentially save time for the more intensive sections.

A detailed day-wise analysis is often released by the official State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, allowing candidates to gauge their performance.

## MAH CET 2026 Answer Key, Result, and Cutoffs

After the examination, candidates eagerly await the release of the official answer key, which helps in estimating their scores. The State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, typically publishes this on its official portal. Following this, the MAH CET results are declared, indicating the raw scores and percentiles achieved by candidates.

Cutoffs for MAH CET MBA admissions vary each year and depend on several factors, including the number of applicants, the difficulty level of the exam, and the number of available seats in various colleges. These cutoffs are crucial for candidates to understand their chances of admission into their preferred management institutes during the Centralised Admission Process (CAP).
