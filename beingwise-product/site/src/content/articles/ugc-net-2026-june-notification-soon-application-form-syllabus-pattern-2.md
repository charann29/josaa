# UGC NET 2026: June Notification (Soon), Application Form, Syllabus, Pattern & Eligibility Criteria

The UGC NET is a crucial national-level examination for aspiring Assistant Professors and Junior Research Fellows in Indian higher education. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), this test assesses candidates' eligibility for teaching and research roles across universities and colleges.

## Key Takeaways

*   The NTA is expected to release the UGC NET June 2026 notification and application form in April 2026.
*   The exam determines eligibility for Assistant Professor positions or both Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) and Assistant Professor roles.
*   UGC NET is a Computer Based Test (CBT) covering 85 subjects, with a distinct syllabus for each.
*   Successful JRF candidates receive a stipend for research and are also eligible for Assistant Professor roles.

## Understanding the UGC NET Examination

The University Grants Commission National Eligibility Test (UGC NET) is a benchmark for academic careers in India. It's designed to identify individuals who are well-suited for teaching at the university and college level, or for pursuing advanced research as Junior Research Fellows. The NTA has been responsible for conducting this exam since June 2018, taking over from the CBSE.

## Important Dates and Application Process

Candidates looking to appear for the UGC NET June 2026 session should anticipate the official notification to be released by the NTA in April 2026. This notification, typically in PDF format, will be available on the official NTA UGC NET website. It serves as a comprehensive information bulletin, detailing everything from application procedures and key dates to the syllabus, exam pattern, and eligibility requirements. The application window will open concurrently with the notification's release, allowing candidates a specific period to submit their applications online.

## Eligibility Criteria for UGC NET

To be eligible for the UGC NET, candidates generally need to have completed their Master's degree or equivalent from a recognized university. Specific percentage requirements in the Master's degree apply, and there are age limits for the Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) component, though no upper age limit for Assistant Professor eligibility. Detailed criteria will be outlined in the official notification, so it's essential to review it carefully upon release.

## UGC NET Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The UGC NET is conducted in a Computer Based Test (CBT) format. The exam consists of two papers: Paper 1 and Paper 2. Paper 1 is common for all candidates and assesses teaching/research aptitude, reasoning ability, comprehension, divergent thinking, and general awareness. Paper 2 is subject-specific, chosen from 85 available subjects, and tests the candidate's in-depth knowledge of their chosen discipline. The syllabus for each subject is prescribed by the University Grants Commission (UGC) itself. Both papers consist of multiple-choice questions, and there is no negative marking.

## Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) vs. Assistant Professor Eligibility

Qualifying the UGC NET can lead to two distinct opportunities:

*   **Junior Research Fellowship (JRF):** Candidates who qualify for JRF are eligible to pursue research and receive a stipend. Currently, JRFs are awarded INR 37,000 per month for the first two years, increasing to INR 42,000 per month from the third year onwards, to support their research. JRF qualifiers are also eligible to apply for Assistant Professor positions.
*   **Assistant Professor:** Candidates who qualify solely for Assistant Professor eligibility can apply for teaching positions in Indian universities and colleges. While they can pursue a PhD, they will not receive a government stipend for their doctoral research through this qualification.
