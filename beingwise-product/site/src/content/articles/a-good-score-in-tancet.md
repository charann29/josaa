# What is a Good Score in TANCET?

The Tamil Nadu Common Entrance Test (TANCET) is a crucial state-level examination for students aspiring to pursue postgraduate MBA and MCA programs across various colleges in Tamil Nadu. Understanding what constitutes a "good" score is essential for planning your application strategy.

## Key Takeaways

*   A good TANCET score is generally above the 90th percentile, often leading to admission into top colleges that accept TANCET scores.
*   Anna University, the conducting body, calculates both raw marks and a percentile score to indicate a candidate's comparative performance.
*   Your target score should align with the specific colleges you wish to apply to, as cutoffs vary significantly.
*   A score in the top 10-25% of all test-takers is usually considered a strong performance.

## Understanding TANCET Results and Percentiles

Before determining what a good score is, it's important to understand how TANCET results are compiled. Anna University uses a specific marking scheme: one mark is awarded for each correct answer, and a quarter (0.25) mark is deducted for every incorrect response. The final results include both your raw marks and a percentile score.

The TANCET percentile score is a comparative measure, showing how well you performed relative to other candidates. For example, if you achieve a 97th percentile, it means you scored better than 97% of all test-takers.

## What Defines a 'Good' Score?

A good score in TANCET is not a fixed number; rather, it is relative to the colleges you are targeting. Generally, candidates aiming for top-tier institutions accepting TANCET scores should strive for a percentile above 90. This typically places them in a competitive position for admission.

For many reputable colleges, securing a percentile in the range of 80-90 can also be considered a good score, opening up various options. However, for the most sought-after programs, aiming for the highest possible percentile is always advisable.

Ultimately, a "good" score is one that meets or exceeds the specific cutoff marks set by your desired colleges. It reflects your performance not just in isolation, but in comparison to the entire pool of TANCET candidates.

To understand specific college cutoffs, it's always best to refer to the official TANCET information released by Anna University or the respective college admission portals.
