Welcome to BeingWise! Understanding the XAT exam pattern is crucial for anyone aiming to pursue an MBA through this highly competitive test. This guide breaks down the structure, marking scheme, and duration for XAT 2026.

## Key Takeaways

*   XAT 2026 will likely maintain its established structure, as major changes are uncommon.
*   The exam is divided into two main parts, with a total duration of 180 minutes.
*   It features Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) across four distinct sections.
*   A unique marking scheme includes negative marks for incorrect answers and for too many consecutive unattempted questions.

## XAT Exam Format and Duration

The XAT examination, designed by XLRI Jamshedpur, is an online, computer-based test. The total duration for the exam is three hours (180 minutes). The question paper is structured into two parts. The first part is allotted 170 minutes and covers Decision Making, Verbal & Logical Ability, and Quantitative Ability & Data Interpretation. The second part is dedicated to the General Knowledge section, with a time limit of 10 minutes. Notably, the Essay Writing section is not part of the current XAT pattern.

## Section-Wise Breakdown

The XAT question paper consists of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) distributed across four primary sections. While the exact number of questions can vary slightly each year, the overall structure remains consistent. The General Knowledge section's scores are not factored into the percentile calculation, but it is important for the overall selection process.

## XAT Marking Scheme 2026

The XAT marking scheme is distinctive and requires careful attention from test-takers. Understanding these rules is essential for optimizing your performance and maximizing your score. The key aspects of the marking scheme are:

*   **Correct Answers:** For every question answered correctly, one mark is awarded.
*   **Incorrect Answers:** A deduction of 0.25 marks occurs for each incorrect response.
*   **Unattempted Questions:** A penalty of 0.10 marks is applied if a candidate leaves more than eight consecutive questions unanswered.
*   **General Knowledge Section:** There is no negative marking for incorrect answers within the General Knowledge section. Additionally, the scores from this section do not contribute to the overall XAT percentile calculation, though it is still an important part of the exam.

## Key Features and Considerations

One of the consistent features of XAT is its focus on evaluating a broad range of skills, from analytical and quantitative abilities to decision-making and verbal reasoning. The exam's design, as confirmed by XLRI, aims to assess candidates comprehensively. While the core pattern tends to be stable, candidates should always refer to the official XAT notification for any last-minute updates regarding the 2026 examination.

Preparing effectively for XAT means not just knowing the content but also understanding how your responses will be scored. Good luck with your preparation!
