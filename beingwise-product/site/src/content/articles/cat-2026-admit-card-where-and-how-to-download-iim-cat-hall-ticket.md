# CAT 2026 Admit Card: Where and How to Download IIM CAT Hall Ticket

The CAT admit card is a crucial document for anyone appearing for the Common Admission Test (CAT). This hall ticket is your official pass to the examination centre and contains vital information about your test. It's essential to understand the download process and key details well in advance.

## Key Takeaways

*   The CAT 2026 admit card is expected to be released in the second week of November.
*   You must download your admit card from the official IIMCAT website using your registration ID and password.
*   The admit card is mandatory for entry to the examination centre and should be printed and carried along with a valid photo ID.
*   Check all details on your admit card carefully and contact the CAT help desk immediately if you find any discrepancies.

## When to Expect Your CAT 2026 Admit Card

The release date for the CAT 2026 admit card will be officially announced by the IIM conducting the exam, typically within the CAT 2026 notification. Historically, these admit cards are made available around the second week of November. While you can generally download it until the exam day, it's always advisable to secure your copy as soon as it's released to avoid last-minute issues.

## How to Download Your CAT 2026 Admit Card

Accessing your CAT 2026 hall ticket is a straightforward process through the official IIMCAT portal. Follow these steps to successfully download and print your admit card:

1.  **Visit the Official Website:** Go to the official CAT 2026 website, which is iimcat.ac.in.
2.  **Locate the Login Section:** Look for the 'Login' button or link on the homepage and click on it.
3.  **Enter Your Credentials:** You will need to input your registered application number (or User ID) and password.
4.  **Access Admit Card:** After logging in, find the link specifically for the 'Admit Card' and click on it.
5.  **Download and Save:** Your admit card will appear on the screen. Download it to your computer and then print several copies for safekeeping.

## Documents to Carry Along with CAT 2026 Admit Card

On the day of the CAT exam, you must carry your printed admit card along with a valid photo identification document. Without these, entry to the examination hall will be denied. Acceptable forms of photo ID include:

*   Aadhaar Card
*   Passport
*   Driving License
*   PAN Card
*   Voter ID
*   College ID (must be valid and contain photograph)

The name on your photo ID must exactly match the name on your CAT admit card. Any discrepancy could lead to issues at the test center.

## Important Details on Your CAT Admit Card

Your CAT admit card is more than just an entry pass; it contains critical information about your examination. Please verify all details immediately after downloading. Key information includes:

*   Candidate's Name
*   CAT Registration Number
*   Date of Birth
*   Category (General, SC, ST, OBC, PWD)
*   Exam Date
*   Exam Time/Slot (e.g., Slot 1: 8:30 AM - 10:30 AM, Slot 2: 12:30 PM - 2:30 PM, Slot 3: 4:30 PM - 6:30 PM)
*   Test Centre Name and Address
*   Reporting Time
*   Important Exam Day Instructions

If you notice any errors in your personal details on the admit card, such as your name or registration number, you must contact the CAT help desk immediately for rectification. There is no option for candidates to directly edit the admit card details themselves.

## CAT Exam Day Instructions

Beyond carrying your admit card and ID, there are specific guidelines you must follow on the exam day to ensure a smooth experience. These instructions are typically printed on the admit card itself and include:

*   **Reporting Time:** Arrive at the test centre well before the specified reporting time to complete necessary formalities like security checks and biometric verification.
*   **Prohibited Items:** Do not carry any electronic gadgets (mobile phones, smartwatches, calculators), study material, or any unauthorized items into the examination hall. Only your admit card, ID, and a simple pen (provided at the centre in some cases) are usually allowed.
*   **Rough Work:** All rough work must be done on the scratchpad provided at the test centre. No personal paper is allowed.
*   **Biometric Verification:** Your biometrics (fingerprints) will be captured at the centre, so ensure your hands are clean and free of any mehndi or temporary tattoos that might interfere with the process.
*   **Social Distancing:** Follow any health and safety protocols, including maintaining social distancing and wearing a mask if mandated by the IIMs.

Adhering to these instructions is vital for a hassle-free examination experience. Wishing you the very best for your CAT 2026 preparation!
