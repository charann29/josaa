# XAT 2026 Exam Analysis & Review (Available): Get detailed Paper Analysis Here

The XAT 2026 examination has concluded, and initial reviews from test-takers suggest a moderate overall difficulty level. This analysis provides an in-depth look at the paper's structure, sectional difficulty, question types, and expected cut-offs to help candidates evaluate their performance.

## Key Takeaways

*   The XAT 2026 paper maintained a similar structure and topic composition to the previous year, with no significant changes reported.
*   The Quantitative Aptitude & Data Interpretation (QA-DI) section was generally considered the most challenging part of the examination.
*   Good attempts in the range of 38 to 42 correct answers are estimated to be sufficient for achieving a 99 percentile score.
*   The General Knowledge (GK) section was perceived as relatively easy and quite manageable for most candidates.

## XAT 2026 Paper Structure and Difficulty

The XAT 2026 question paper was administered in a computer-based format and comprised two main parts. Part 1 included the core sections: Decision Making (DM), Verbal Ability & Logical Reasoning (VA-LR), and Quantitative Aptitude & Data Interpretation (QA-DI). Part 2 consisted solely of the General Knowledge (GK) section.

Here's a detailed breakdown of each section's topics and estimated difficulty:

| XAT Section             | Key Topics                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     | Difficulty Level     |
| :---------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------- |
| Verbal Ability & LR     | Reading Comprehension passages, vocabulary questions, grammar rules, fill-in-the-blanks, para jumbles, syllogisms, critical reasoning, identifying the main idea or message from an image, and poetry analysis.                                                                  | Moderate             |
| Decision Making         | Consisted of seven passages, each typically followed by three questions. These passages often presented scenarios requiring ethical judgment or strategic thinking.                                                                                                            | Moderate             |
| Quantitative Aptitude & DI | Questions from Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, and Modern Math. Data Interpretation sets included scatter plots/line graphs, arithmetic-based problems, average calculations, and number-matrix based analyses.                                                                  | Moderate to Difficult |
| General Knowledge       | Covered a broad spectrum of topics including History, Geography, Economics and Business, prominent personalities and awards, Science and Technology, Sports, and Entertainment.                                                                                                | Easy                 |

## Previous Year's XAT Exam Analysis

Understanding past trends can offer valuable context. The XAT 2025 examination also presented a balanced challenge, with some sections being more demanding than others. The Verbal Ability & Logical Reasoning and Decision Making sections were rated as moderate, while Quantitative Aptitude & Data Interpretation proved to be slightly tougher.

Based on the overall difficulty and candidate feedback, the expected cut-offs for XAT 2026 admission to XLRI and other top management institutes are likely to be in a similar range to previous years.
