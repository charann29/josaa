# List of University Entrance Exam: Dates, Results, Cut-Offs, Merit List, Colleges and Universities

Navigating higher education in India often begins with entrance examinations, which are essential gateways to a wide array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across the country. These tests evaluate candidates' skills and knowledge, playing a crucial role in the admissions process for various institutions.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Diverse Pathways:** India offers numerous entrance exams for admissions to central, state, and private universities, covering a broad spectrum of courses.
*   **Eligibility is Key:** Meeting minimum academic requirements, such as completing Class 12 for UG programs or a bachelor's degree for PG programs, is fundamental.
*   **Merit-Based Admissions:** University admissions typically consider entrance exam scores, academic merit, available seats, and reservation policies.
*   **Top Institutions:** Performing well in these exams can open doors to prestigious universities known for quality education and better career prospects.

## Common University Entrance Test (CUET) UG & PG

The Common University Entrance Test (CUET) is a pivotal examination for students aspiring to join undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) programs in India. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), CUET streamlines admissions to numerous central, state, and private universities.

### CUET UG

For undergraduate admissions, CUET UG is mandatory for a vast number of programs including BA, B.Sc, B.Com, and BCA. It facilitates entry into prominent central universities like Delhi University, Jawaharlal Nehru University, Banaras Hindu University, Jamia Millia Islamia, and the University of Hyderabad. After results are declared by NTA, participating universities conduct their own counselling processes based on CUET scores.

### CUET PG

Similarly, CUET PG is the gateway for postgraduate admissions, offering access to approximately 203 universities nationwide. Candidates must fulfill specific eligibility criteria to appear for this exam, which covers a wide range of PG courses.

## IIT JAM (Joint Admission Test for M.Sc.)

The Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) conduct IIT JAM, a national-level entrance examination for admission to various postgraduate science programs. This includes M.Sc. (Two Years), Joint M.Sc.-Ph.D., M.Sc.-Ph.D. Dual Degree, and M.Sc.-M.Tech. programs at the IITs, as well as Integrated PhD programs at IISc Bangalore.

IIT JAM opens doors to over 3000 seats across approximately 90 postgraduate programs at 22 IITs, National Institutes of Technology (NITs), and IISc Bangalore, providing excellent opportunities for science graduates.

## ICAR AIEEA PG (All India Entrance Examination for Admission)

The NTA also conducts the ICAR AIEEA exam, which is essential for students seeking admission to postgraduate and research courses in agricultural sciences. Qualifying this examination makes candidates eligible for admission processes at 75 agricultural universities across India, with admissions based on their scores.

## NEST (National Entrance Screening Test)

NEST is an entrance exam for admission to the integrated M.Sc. programs at the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER Bhubaneswar) and the UM DAE Centre for Excellence in Basic Sciences (CEBS, Mumbai). To be eligible, candidates must have passed Class 12 from a recognized Indian board with at least 60% aggregate marks (55% for SC, ST, and Divyangjan candidates).

## Other Notable University Entrance Exams

Beyond these major examinations, many other universities conduct their own entrance tests. For instance, Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University (GGSIPU) administers the IPU CET for various undergraduate and postgraduate programs, reflecting the diverse admission landscape in Indian higher education.

For the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding specific exam dates, results, cut-offs, and merit lists, always refer to the official portals of NTA, JoSAA, or the respective universities.
