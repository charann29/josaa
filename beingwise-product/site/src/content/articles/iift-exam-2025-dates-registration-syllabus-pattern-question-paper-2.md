# IIFT Exam 2025: Dates, Registration, Syllabus, Pattern, Question Paper, Preparation, Result, Cut Offs

For those aspiring to join the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT) for their MBA programs, it's important to understand the current admission process. Starting from the academic year 2024-25, IIFT no longer conducts its own entrance examination.

## Key takeaways

*   IIFT now accepts CAT scores for admission to its MBA (International Business) and MBA (Business Analytics) programs.
*   The National Testing Agency (NTA) previously conducted the IIFT entrance exam but is no longer involved in the admission process for IIFT's MBA programs.
*   The selection process includes shortlisting based on CAT scores, followed by an extempore and Personal Interview (PI) round.
*   IIFT considers only CAT scores for the initial PI shortlisting, unlike some IIMs which also factor in academic criteria.

## Understanding the IIFT Admission Process

Historically, the IIFT entrance exam was a national-level aptitude test for admission to its MBA programs. This computer-based test, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), assessed candidates on sections like Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension, Quantitative Ability, Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning, and General Awareness. However, this has changed. For admission to the 2025 academic year, IIFT will continue to rely on CAT scores.

Foreign, NRI, and PIO candidates are still considered for admission based on their GMAT scores.

## IIFT 2025 Exam Dates & Schedule

Since IIFT now uses CAT scores for admission, candidates should focus on the CAT examination schedule. The registration and application period for IIFT admission will typically open after the CAT exam, allowing candidates to apply with their valid scores.

## IIFT Application Process 2025

Candidates aspiring for IIFT's MBA programs must first appear for the CAT exam. After receiving their CAT scores, they will need to apply separately to IIFT through the institute's official admission portal. The application process typically involves filling out an online form, submitting relevant documents, and paying an application fee. It is crucial to meet the eligibility criteria set by IIFT before applying.

## IIFT Syllabus and Pattern (CAT-based)

As IIFT utilizes CAT scores, the preparation should primarily focus on the CAT syllabus and exam pattern. The CAT exam generally covers:

*   **Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension (VARC):** This section includes reading comprehension passages, para jumbles, para summaries, and odd-one-out questions.
*   **Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DILR):** This section tests analytical skills through caselets, tables, charts, and logical puzzles.
*   **Quantitative Ability (QA):** This section covers arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and modern math concepts.

Familiarity with the CAT exam structure, including its duration and marking scheme, is essential. Practicing CAT previous year question papers and mock tests will be highly beneficial.

## IIFT Cut Offs for MBA Admission 2025

IIFT determines its cut-offs for shortlisting candidates for the subsequent rounds (extempore and Personal Interview) based on CAT scores. For the 2024-25 admission cycle, the CAT cut-off for PI shortlisting was around the 98 percentile, with an additional two points for gender diversity. It's important to note that IIFT's initial shortlisting primarily considers the CAT score, without factoring in academic records at this stage. The final admission decision is based on a composite score derived from CAT performance and the scores from the extempore and PI rounds.

Aspiring students should aim for a high CAT percentile to maximize their chances of being shortlisted by IIFT.
