# Mass Communication Course, Subjects, Fees, Admission, Eligibility, Scope

Mass Communication is a dynamic field focused on sharing information and messages with a large audience through various media channels. It's a broad discipline covering everything from traditional print to modern digital platforms.

## Key takeaways

*   Mass Communication courses are offered at undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG), and diploma levels.
*   Eligibility for UG courses typically requires completing 10+2, while PG courses need a relevant bachelor's degree.
*   Admission often involves entrance exams like CUET UG, CUET PG, IPU CET, and SET, depending on the institution and course level.
*   Graduates can find diverse roles in journalism, advertising, public relations, and digital media, with average salaries around ₹4.3 LPA, varying with experience and role.

## What is Mass Communication?

This field explores how information is created, disseminated, and received by large groups of people. It encompasses a wide array of subjects, including visual communication, photography, print journalism, electronic media, and broadcasting. Major areas within mass communication include journalism, advertising, and public relations.

## Eligibility and Admission Process

To pursue a Mass Communication course, specific eligibility criteria must be met, which vary by the level of study and the institution.

For undergraduate programs, students generally need to have completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) from a recognized board in India, often with a minimum aggregate score of 50% or as specified by the college.

For postgraduate courses, applicants typically require a bachelor's degree in mass communication or a related discipline. Many institutions conduct their own entrance examinations. Prominent national-level entrance tests include CUET UG for undergraduate admissions and CUET PG for postgraduate admissions, conducted by NTA. Other popular exams are IPU CET, LPU NEST, and SET.

## Specializations and Career Opportunities

Mass Communication is a versatile field offering numerous specializations. Some related and specialized programs include:

*   MA Journalism and Mass Communication
*   BA Journalism and Mass Communication
*   BA Multimedia and Mass Communication
*   PGD in Mass Communication

After successfully completing a course in Mass Communication, graduates can explore a variety of job roles across the media and journalism industries. Popular career paths include:

*   **Journalist:** Reporting news for print, digital, or broadcast media.
*   **News Editor:** Overseeing content and ensuring accuracy and quality.
*   **Content Writer:** Creating engaging content for various platforms.
*   **News Anchor:** Presenting news on television.
*   **Public Relations Officer:** Managing an organization's public image and communication.
*   **Social Media Manager:** Handling an organization's presence on social media platforms.

Starting salaries for these roles can vary based on skills, experience, and the employer, with an average around ₹4.3 LPA.

## Top Colleges for Mass Communication

India has a wide range of institutions offering Mass Communication courses, including both private and government colleges. When choosing, consider factors like fees, infrastructure, faculty, and industry connections. For reliable institutional rankings, you can refer to the annual NIRF rankings.

### Private Colleges

Over 470 private colleges across India offer Mass Communication programs. These institutions are often known for their modern facilities and industry-focused curricula, though their fees can be higher compared to government options. Some notable private colleges include St Joseph's College, Devagiri (fees around ₹3.96 Lakhs) and St Aloysius College Autonomous, Mangaluru (fees around ₹1.32 Lakhs).

### Government Colleges

Approximately 90 government institutions provide Mass Communication courses. They generally offer quality education at more affordable fees. Examples include Farook College, Kozhikode (fees around ₹6,540).

Choosing the right Mass Communication program can open doors to an exciting and impactful career in the ever-evolving world of media.
