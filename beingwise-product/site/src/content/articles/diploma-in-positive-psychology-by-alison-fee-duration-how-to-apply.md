# Diploma in Positive Psychology by Alison: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Exploring the Diploma in Positive Psychology offered by Alison can be a great way to understand the science behind human flourishing and well-being. This program is designed for anyone interested in learning how to cultivate a more positive outlook and apply these principles in various aspects of life.

## Key Takeaways

*   The course is offered completely free of charge, making it accessible to a wide audience.
*   It is delivered through a blend of video and text-based modules, allowing for flexible learning.
*   Upon successful completion, a certificate is available directly from Alison.
*   The curriculum covers fundamental concepts, personality traits, emotional states, and practical applications of positive psychology.

## Course Structure and Delivery

This diploma program is structured into multiple modules, guiding learners through various aspects of positive psychology. The instructional content is provided in English and combines both video lessons and text-based materials, offering a dynamic learning experience that can be accessed at your own pace.

## Understanding the Syllabus

The curriculum is thoughtfully designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of positive psychology. It begins with foundational concepts and progresses to more nuanced topics, including assessments to gauge your learning. Here’s a breakdown of what you can expect to learn:

*   **Module 1: Introduction to Positive Psychology and Research Methods**
    *   Understanding the basics of psychology and the specific field of positive psychology.
    *   Insights into common research methods used in the discipline.
*   **Module 2: Positive Personality Traits**
    *   Exploring different classifications and measurements of character strengths.
    *   Delving into models of virtues and character strengths.
*   **Module 3: Happiness and Wellbeing**
    *   Examining the concepts of happiness and overall well-being.
    *   Understanding how culture influences happiness and methods for testing it.
*   **Module 4: Positive Emotional State and Processes**
    *   Learning about positive affectivity and the importance of emotional intelligence.
*   **Module 5: Diploma in Positive Psychology - First Assessment**
    *   A checkpoint to evaluate your understanding of the initial modules.
*   **Module 6: Intrapersonal Strengths**
    *   Focusing on hope, optimism, self-concept, and resilience.
*   **Module 7: Flow, Mindfulness, and Spirituality**
    *   Exploring states of 'flow', the practice of mindfulness, and the role of spirituality, grit, and mindset.
*   **Module 8: Positive Interpersonal Strengths**
    *   Understanding concepts like compassion, forgiveness, love, empathy, and altruism.
*   **Module 9: Recent Trends and Directions in Positive Psychology**
    *   Insights into positive psychology in an Indian context, ancient Indian literature, and intervention programs.
*   **Module 10: Diploma in Positive Psychology - Second Assessment**
    *   Another assessment to test your progress.
*   **Module 11: Course Assessment**
    *   The final evaluation to complete the diploma program.

One of the most appealing aspects of this program is that the course content itself is entirely free. While the learning is complimentary, Alison, as the certificate-providing authority, offers an official certificate upon successful completion. There may be a fee associated with obtaining this certificate, which is standard for many free online courses.
