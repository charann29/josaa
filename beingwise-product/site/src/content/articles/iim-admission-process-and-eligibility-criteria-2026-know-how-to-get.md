# IIM Admission Process and Eligibility Criteria 2026: Know How to get Admission in IIMs

Securing a spot in an Indian Institute of Management (IIM) is a dream for many aspiring business leaders. The admission process is comprehensive, evaluating candidates on various merits beyond just entrance exam scores.

## Key takeaways

*   Admission to IIMs is a multi-stage process, beginning with the Common Admission Test (CAT).
*   Candidates are evaluated based on their CAT score, academic record, work experience, and performance in the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI).
*   Each IIM has its own specific shortlisting criteria, which can vary slightly from year to year.
*   Diversity in academics and gender is often considered during the selection process.

## Understanding the IIM Admission Timeline

The admission cycle for the IIMs typically kicks off with the CAT examination. For the 2026-28 batch, the CAT 2025 exam was held on November 30, 2025. Following the exam, the IIMs begin to release their individual admission criteria and selection processes. This information outlines the cut-off percentiles, specific parameters, and the shortlisting methodology. It's crucial for prospective students to stay updated with these announcements as they become available.

## Stages of the IIM Selection Process

The path to an IIM involves several distinct stages:

1.  **Common Admission Test (CAT):** This is the primary gateway. Candidates must appear for and achieve a competitive score in the CAT exam.
2.  **Shortlisting for WAT/GD-PI:** Based on CAT scores, academic performance, work experience, and diversity factors, IIMs shortlist candidates for the next round. Each IIM sets its own minimum CAT percentile cut-off for shortlisting.
3.  **Written Ability Test (WAT) / Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI):** Shortlisted candidates participate in a WAT (where they write an essay on a given topic) and/or a GD (where they discuss a topic in a group), followed by a Personal Interview. These rounds assess communication skills, critical thinking, leadership potential, and overall personality.
4.  **Final Merit List:** After the WAT/GD-PI rounds, a composite score is calculated for each candidate. This score typically combines weightage from the CAT score, WAT/GD-PI performance, academic record (10th, 12th, and graduation marks), work experience, and diversity factors. The final admission offers are then made based on this merit list.

## IIM Eligibility Criteria

To be considered for admission to the IIMs, candidates must meet certain basic eligibility requirements, as outlined by the official CAT exam details and individual IIM notifications. Generally, these include:

*   A Bachelor's degree with at least 50% marks or equivalent CGPA (45% for Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disability (PwD) categories) from a university or institution incorporated by an Act of Parliament or State Legislature in India or other educational institutions established by an Act of Parliament or declared to be deemed as a University under Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956, or possess an equivalent qualification recognized by the Ministry of HRD, Government of India.
*   Candidates appearing for the final year of their Bachelor's degree/equivalent qualification examination and those who have completed degree requirements and are awaiting results can also apply, provided they complete their degree requirements with the stipulated percentage of marks by a specific date, usually by June of the admission year. Any candidate who does not meet this condition will be required to withdraw from the program.

## CAT Cut-offs: Qualifying vs. Final

It's important to differentiate between two types of CAT cut-offs:

*   **Qualifying Cut-off:** This is the minimum percentile required in each section and overall in CAT to be considered for shortlisting. Meeting this threshold does not guarantee a call for the WAT/GD-PI round, but failing to meet it disqualifies a candidate.
*   **Final Cut-off:** The actual CAT percentile at which candidates are shortlisted for the WAT/GD-PI rounds is often higher than the qualifying cut-off. This final cut-off varies for each IIM and depends on factors like the number of applicants, their CAT scores, and the specific shortlisting criteria of that particular institute.

### Documents Required for WAT/GD-PI Round

Candidates shortlisted for the Written Ability Test (WAT) / Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds should prepare the following documents:

*   Original and photocopies of academic mark sheets and certificates (10th, 12th, Bachelor's degree).
*   CAT scorecard.
*   Work experience certificates (if applicable).
*   Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC-NCL/EWS/PwD) if applicable, as per the Government of India format.
*   Any other supporting documents mentioned in the interview call letter.

Admission to an IIM is a challenging but rewarding journey that requires meticulous planning and preparation across all stages of the selection process.
