# Sr.Secondary: Psychology (328) by National Institute of Open Schooling via Swayam: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For students keen to explore the fascinating world of the human mind, the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) offers a comprehensive online course in Psychology at the Senior Secondary level. Delivered through the Swayam platform, this program provides a flexible way to gain foundational knowledge in this discipline.

## Key Takeaways

*   This 24-week online course covers core psychological concepts, ideal for Senior Secondary students.
*   It offers 10 academic credit points and a certificate upon successful completion from NIOS and Swayam.
*   The learning is self-paced, utilizing video lectures, reading materials, and discussion forums.
*   No specific fees are mentioned for the course or its certificate, making it an accessible option.

## Course Overview

This Senior Secondary Psychology course (code 328) is a comprehensive online program focusing on various aspects of psychology. Coordinated by Professor Ms. Madhur Bhatia from NIOS, the curriculum is structured into five compulsory modules and one optional module, allowing students some flexibility in their learning path.

The program spans 24 weeks and includes 30 distinct lessons. All course materials, including video lectures, are accessible through the interactive Swayam platform. Upon finishing the course and meeting the evaluation criteria, participants will receive a certificate jointly issued by NIOS and Swayam.

## What You Will Learn

The curriculum is designed to provide a broad understanding of psychology, covering both theoretical foundations and practical applications. Key learning areas include:

*   **Foundations of Psychology:** Understanding the basic principles and historical context.
*   **Key Psychological Processes:** Exploring attention, perception, learning, memory, and thought.
*   **Developmental Processes:** Studying human development across different life stages, including adolescence, adulthood, and aging.
*   **Self and Personality:** Delving into theories of self, personality, and individual differences like intelligence.
*   **Social Psychology:** Examining group dynamics, person perception, and interpersonal relationships.
*   **Applied Psychology:** Learning about psychological disorders, psychotherapy, health psychology, and the application of psychology in education and work environments.
*   **Early Childhood Education:** An optional module that covers developmental patterns in early childhood and the structure of play centers.

## Course Structure and Format

This course is delivered in English, primarily through self-study, utilizing video and text-based resources. It is accredited for 10 credit points at the school level. The interactive Swayam platform facilitates learning through:

*   **Video Lectures:** Engaging video content to explain complex topics.
*   **Reading Materials:** Supplementary texts for in-depth understanding.
*   **Peer Discussion Forums:** Opportunities for students to interact, share insights, and clarify doubts with fellow learners.
*   **Certificate of Completion:** A formal recognition of your achievement from NIOS and Swayam.

## How to Enroll

Enrolling in the Sr. Secondary: Psychology (328) course on Swayam is a straightforward process:

1.  Visit the official Swayam platform for the course.
2.  Locate and click on the "Sign-in" or "Register" option, typically found in the top right corner of the page.
3.  On the new page that appears, you can either sign in with an existing Swayam account or create a new one using your username and password to proceed with enrollment.

## Detailed Syllabus (Week-wise)

The course is structured to guide learners through various psychological concepts progressively:

*   **Weeks 1-3:** Introduction to Psychology, research methods, and biological/cultural influences.
*   **Weeks 4-5:** Sensory processes, attention, and perception.
*   **Weeks 6-8:** Learning, memory, thinking, and reasoning.
*   **Weeks 9-10:** Motivation and emotions.
*   **Weeks 11-14:** Human development from its nature to adolescence, adulthood, and aging.
*   **Weeks 15-18:** Individual differences, self-concept, and personality theories/assessment.
*   **Weeks 19-20:** Psychological disorders, group processes, and human-environment interaction.
*   **Weeks 21-23:** Psychotherapy, health psychology, stress management, and applications in education and career development.
*   **Weeks 24-26:** Early childhood development, objectives, and management of play centers (optional module).

This course offers a valuable opportunity for Senior Secondary students to build a strong foundation in psychology and explore potential career paths in the field.
