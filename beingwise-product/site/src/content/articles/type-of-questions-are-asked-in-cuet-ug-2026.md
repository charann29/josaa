# What Type of Questions are Asked in CUET UG 2026?

The Common University Entrance Test (CUET UG) is a critical step for students aiming for undergraduate admissions in over 250 universities across India. Understanding the types of questions asked in CUET UG 2026 is essential for effective preparation, helping you gauge the difficulty and focus your study efforts.

## Key Takeaways

*   CUET UG 2026 will feature entirely objective-type questions, specifically Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).
*   Each subject paper, including languages, domain subjects, and the General Test, will contain 50 compulsory MCQs.
*   The exam is computer-based, and each correct answer fetches 5 marks, while an incorrect answer leads to a deduction of 1 mark.
*   Questions are designed to assess a candidate's aptitude, analytical skills, and subject-specific knowledge based on the Class 12 NCERT syllabus.

## CUET UG 2026 Exam Structure at a Glance

The National Testing Agency (NTA), the body responsible for CUET UG, has structured the exam into three main sections. This design ensures a comprehensive evaluation of candidates. Each section has a uniform duration and number of questions.

| Section                   | Number of Questions (All compulsory) | Duration   |
| :------------------------ | :----------------------------------- | :--------- |
| Section I - Language      | 50 questions                         | 60 minutes |
| Section II - Domain Specific | 50 questions                         | 60 minutes |
| Section III - General Test | 50 questions                         | 60 minutes |

### CUET UG Marking Scheme

The marking scheme for CUET UG is straightforward:

*   For every correct answer, candidates are awarded five marks.
*   For every incorrect answer, one mark is deducted.
*   There is no penalty for unattempted questions.

## Understanding the Question Format: Objective MCQs

All questions in CUET UG 2026 are objective in nature, presented as Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs). This means for each question, you will be given four options, and you must select the single correct answer. Since the exam is conducted in a Computer-Based Test (CBT) mode, you will use a mouse to click on your chosen option. Accuracy is crucial, as incorrect selections will result in negative marking.

## Types of Questions Asked in Each Section

While all questions are MCQs, the nature of these questions varies across the different sections to test specific skills and knowledge areas:

### Section I: Language

This section tests your proficiency in a chosen language. The questions typically cover:

*   **Reading Comprehension:** Passages followed by questions to assess understanding, inference, vocabulary, and main idea identification.
*   **Verbal Ability:** Questions on sentence correction, sentence completion, synonyms, antonyms, and identifying errors in grammar and usage.
*   **Rearranging Sentences:** Jumbled sentences that need to be put in a logical order to form a coherent paragraph.
*   **Vocabulary:** Direct questions testing knowledge of word meanings.

### Section II: Domain Subjects

This section is designed to assess your in-depth knowledge of the subjects you have chosen (e.g., Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, History, Economics, etc.). The questions will strictly adhere to the Class 12 NCERT syllabus. Expect questions that test:

*   **Conceptual Understanding:** Direct questions based on definitions, principles, and theories.
*   **Application-Based Problems:** Questions requiring you to apply concepts to solve problems or analyze scenarios.
*   **Factual Recall:** Questions testing your memory of specific facts, dates, formulas, and diagrams.
*   **Analytical Skills:** Questions that require you to interpret data, graphs, or statements.

### Section III: General Test

The General Test is designed to evaluate your general aptitude and awareness, irrespective of your academic stream. Questions in this section include:

*   **General Knowledge & Current Affairs:** Questions about national and international events, important personalities, history, geography, and general science.
*   **General Mental Ability:** Questions testing logical reasoning, pattern recognition, series completion, and analogy.
*   **Numerical Ability:** Basic mathematical problems, including arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration, typically up to the Class 8 level.
*   **Quantitative Reasoning:** Data interpretation, tables, graphs, and charts.
*   **Logical & Analytical Reasoning:** Syllogisms, critical reasoning, coding-decoding, and blood relations.

## Preparing Effectively for CUET UG 2026

Given the objective and computer-based nature of the exam, effective preparation involves not just studying the syllabus but also practicing with the correct question format. Regularly attempting mock tests, familiarizing yourself with the NTA's official sample papers, and understanding the time constraints for each section will significantly boost your performance. Focus on strengthening your fundamental concepts from the Class 12 NCERT curriculum for domain subjects, and regularly update your general knowledge for the General Test.

By understanding the types of questions and the exam structure, you can strategize your preparation to achieve your desired scores in CUET UG 2026.
