# MAH MBA CET Cut Offs 2026: Check Round-wise Cut Off for MBA/MMS

To secure admission into MBA and MMS programs at over 300 management institutes across Maharashtra, candidates must meet the MAH MBA CET cut-offs. These cut-offs are released in multiple rounds during the Centralised Admission Process (CAP), which typically takes place in July-August.

## Key takeaways

*   Top management institutes like JBIMS, SIMSREE, Welingkar, and PUMBA are expected to have cut-offs ranging from 97 to 99.99 percentile.
*   The State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, will publish cut-offs round by round for various colleges and categories.
*   Candidates from Maharashtra State (MS) generally face lower cut-offs compared to those from Outside Maharashtra State (OMS).
*   The MAH MBA CET exam will be held on April 6, 7, 8, and May 9, 2026, offering candidates two attempts to achieve their best score.

## Understanding MAH MBA CET Cut-Offs

MAH MBA CET cut-offs are the minimum percentile scores required for admission to participating MBA/MMS colleges. These scores are crucial for candidates aspiring to study management in Maharashtra. The cut-offs are influenced by several dynamic factors each year.

### Factors Determining MAH MBA CET Cut-Offs

Various elements contribute to how the MAH CET cut-offs are set. The State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, considers these factors when finalizing the admission criteria:

*   **Exam Difficulty:** The overall difficulty level of the MAH MBA CET exam for that particular year.
*   **Number of Test Takers:** The total count of candidates who appeared for the exam.
*   **Qualifying Candidates:** The number of candidates who successfully met the minimum eligibility criteria.
*   **Overall Pass Percentage:** The percentage of candidates who passed the examination.
*   **High Percentile Distribution:** The spread and concentration of candidates achieving high percentiles.
*   **Available Seats:** The total number of MBA/MMS seats offered by all participating institutes.
*   **Category-wise Seats:** The distribution of seats across different reservation categories within each institute.
*   **Institute Ranking:** The reputation and ranking of the MAH CET participating institute.
*   **Previous Years' Trends:** The cut-off percentiles at which admissions closed in past years.

### MAH MBA CET Cut-Off Categories

The examination body categorizes MAH CET cut-offs into two main groups: Maharashtra State (MS) and Outside Maharashtra State (OMS). It's important for candidates to understand which category they fall into, as MS candidates typically have different cut-off requirements.

| Category                  | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| :------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Maharashtra State (MS)    | Includes candidates with Maharashtra as their domicile, as well as reserved category students (SC/ST/OBC/PwD) who are residents of Maharashtra. This also covers Minority Seats.                                                                       |
| Outside Maharashtra State (OMS) | Encompasses candidates from outside the state of Maharashtra, referred to as All India Seats. This category also includes candidates from Jammu & Kashmir who have migrated to Maharashtra or other states. |

## MAH MBA CET Exam Dates 2026

Candidates planning to appear for the MAH MBA CET 2026 should note the following key dates:

| Dates       | Exam Schedule        |
| :---------- | :------------------- |
| April 6, 7, 8, 2026 | MAH MBA CET 2026 Exam (Attempt 1) |
| May 9, 2026 | MAH MBA CET 2026 Exam (Attempt 2) |

Candidates can appear for both attempts, and their best score will be considered for admission purposes.

## The Centralised Admission Process (CAP)

The MAH CET Centralised Admission Process (CAP) is the counseling procedure through which MBA/MMS seats are allocated based on MAH MBA CET scores. Conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, the CAP involves multiple rounds where candidates list their preferred colleges and courses, and seats are allotted according to merit, cut-offs, and reservation policies. This process ensures a fair and transparent admission system for all eligible candidates.

We encourage all aspiring candidates to regularly check the official portal for the latest updates on MAH MBA CET cut-offs and the CAP rounds.
