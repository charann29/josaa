# I had got SNQ quota in my 1st round for DSCE but in the 2nd round i got different branch from same clg but i didn't get snq quota what to do

It can be quite confusing and stressful when your admission quota status changes between counselling rounds. Let's explore the possible reasons and what steps you can take if you've lost your SNQ quota for a different branch at DSCE (Dayananda Sagar College of Engineering).

## Key Takeaways

*   **SNQ Quota is Branch-Specific:** Your SNQ (Supernumerary Quota) status might be tied to the specific branch you secured in the first round, and not necessarily guaranteed for all branches in subsequent rounds. Availability can vary.
*   **Act Immediately:** Time is critical. Reach out to the college and counselling authorities without delay, as deadlines for such issues are usually very strict.
*   **Prepare Your Documents:** Have all original SNQ-related documents readily accessible for any verification or appeals process.
*   **Understand Seat Matrix:** The availability of SNQ seats is dynamic and depends on the seat matrix published for each round and branch.

## Understanding SNQ Quota Allocation

The Supernumerary Quota (SNQ) is a special category designed to support students from economically weaker sections, often providing a fee concession. However, it's crucial to understand that these quotas are usually allocated based on a specific seat matrix for each branch and institution. If you secured an SNQ seat in a particular branch in Round 1, it doesn't automatically mean that every other branch will also have SNQ seats available in subsequent rounds, especially if those seats have already been filled or were never allocated to that specific branch in the first place. The availability can change drastically depending on the demand and the official seat distribution for each round.

## Why Your Quota Might Have Changed

There are several reasons why your SNQ quota might not have carried over to a different branch in Round 2 at DSCE:

*   **Branch-Specific Availability:** The most common reason is that SNQ seats for the new branch you received in Round 2 might have already been filled, or there might not have been any SNQ seats designated for that particular branch in that round.
*   **Upgradation Rules:** When you opt for an upgrade, you're essentially re-entering the pool for available seats. If the new branch you received didn't have SNQ seats available at your rank during the second round, the system would allocate you a general seat if one was available.
*   **Systemic Glitch (Less Common):** While rare, sometimes a system error can occur. This is why it's important to cross-check and communicate directly with the authorities.

## Immediate Steps to Take

Given the time-sensitive nature of admissions, here's what you should do right away:

1.  **Contact DSCE Admissions Office:** This is your first and most important step. Reach out to the admissions office at Dayananda Sagar College of Engineering immediately. Explain your situation clearly: that you had SNQ in Round 1 for a specific branch and now have a different branch without SNQ in Round 2. Inquire if it's possible to retain your SNQ quota for the newly allotted branch or if there are any options to revert to your Round 1 allocation with SNQ if that's what you prefer.
2.  **Contact Counselling Authorities:** Simultaneously, try to get in touch with the official counselling authorities responsible for the seat allocation process (e.g., KCET counselling body for Karnataka, if applicable). Explain your scenario and ask if a manual correction is possible, or if there's an appeals process for such discrepancies. Refer to the official information bulletin for their contact details and grievance redressal procedures.
3.  **Gather All Documents:** Make sure all your original documents related to your SNQ eligibility and your Round 1 allotment letter are ready. You might need to submit these as proof during your discussions with the college or counselling body.

## Important Considerations

*   **Deadlines:** Be aware that there are strict deadlines for reporting, fee payment, and grievance resolution after each round of counselling. Missing these deadlines could lead to losing your seat entirely.
*   **Decision Making:** Depending on the responses you receive, you might need to make a quick decision about whether to accept the new branch without SNQ, or explore other options if retaining the SNQ is paramount for you. Understand the fee implications of losing the SNQ benefit.

Navigating such situations requires prompt action and clear communication with the relevant authorities. We wish you the best in resolving this matter swiftly.
