# My Son's Education Across Pune and Bengaluru: Can He Get Government Engineering Colleges Through MHT CET?

It's common for students to move between states during their schooling. If your son has completed his 10th standard in Pune and his 11th and 12th standards in Bengaluru, you might be wondering about his eligibility for government engineering colleges in Maharashtra through the MHT CET.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your son's eligibility for MHT CET largely depends on the board he completed his 12th standard with.
*   To be considered a Maharashtra State Candidature for MHT CET, specific domicile and qualifying examination criteria must be met.
*   Students completing their 12th from outside Maharashtra might still be eligible for certain quotas as All India Candidature.
*   Karnataka government engineering colleges require the Karnataka Common Entrance Test (KCET) for state quota admissions.

## Understanding MHT CET Eligibility for Maharashtra State Candidature

For a student to be eligible for the Maharashtra State Candidature through MHT CET, the primary requirement is usually that they must have passed their 12th standard (or equivalent qualifying examination) from a recognized board within Maharashtra. This is a crucial factor in determining eligibility for the state quota seats in government engineering colleges in Maharashtra.

If your son completed his 11th and 12th standards, and thus his 12th board examination, from a board in Karnataka (e.g., Karnataka Secondary Education Examination Board - KSEEB, or Department of Pre-University Education - DPUE), he would typically not be considered a Maharashtra State Candidature for MHT CET based solely on his 12th standard qualification.

## All India Candidature Through MHT CET

Even if your son completed his 12th standard outside Maharashtra, he might still be eligible to appear for the MHT CET as an 'All India Candidature'. Students who qualify under this category can apply for a percentage of seats (usually around 15-20%) reserved for candidates from all over India in various engineering colleges in Maharashtra, including government institutions. The specific percentage and availability of these seats are detailed in the official MHT CET information brochure published by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra.

For All India Candidature, the 12th standard marks (or equivalent) are still critical, along with the MHT CET score or JEE Main score, depending on the admission process for that specific college and course.

## Admission to Government Engineering Colleges in Karnataka

If your son completed his 12th standard from the Karnataka Board, and his goal is to secure a seat in a government engineering college within Karnataka, he would typically need to appear for the Karnataka Common Entrance Test (KCET). This exam is conducted by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) for admission to various professional courses, including engineering, in colleges across Karnataka. The KCET is the primary gateway for state quota seats in Karnataka's government and government-aided engineering institutions.

## Domicile and Other Criteria

Beyond the board of examination, domicile status also plays a significant role in determining state candidature. The official MHT CET information brochure outlines various types of Maharashtra State Candidature (Type A, B, C, D, E) based on domicile, parents' domicile, or the location of the qualifying examination. It's advisable to refer to the latest official MHT CET brochure for the most accurate and up-to-date eligibility criteria, as these can sometimes have specific nuances.

Navigating admission processes can be complex, especially with studies across different states. Always refer to the official MHT CET information brochure or the relevant state's CET information for the most accurate and current details regarding eligibility and admission procedures.
