# B.Sc. in Humanities & Social Sciences: Course, Full Form, Duration, Admission 2025 Fees, Subjects, Eligibility, Salary & Career Scope

A Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Humanities and Social Sciences is an undergraduate degree program that delves into the intricate study of human culture, societal structures, behavior, and interactions. This three-year course offers a unique blend of scientific inquiry and humanistic exploration.

## Key Takeaways

*   This undergraduate program typically spans three years, focusing on human culture and society.
*   Eligibility generally requires completing Class 12 with at least 50% marks from any stream.
*   Admission often involves entrance exams like CUET, JMI Entrance Exam, or LPU NEST.
*   Graduates can explore diverse career paths, including roles in content creation, civil services, human resources, and law.

## Course Overview and Full Form

The full form, B.Sc. in Humanities and Social Sciences, signifies a Bachelor of Science degree with a focus on subjects traditionally associated with humanities and social sciences. Unlike a pure B.Sc. in a specific science discipline, this program integrates analytical, research, and critical thinking skills across a broader spectrum of humanistic studies. It's designed for students who are curious about understanding human societies, historical contexts, psychological patterns, economic systems, and cultural expressions through a structured, often interdisciplinary, scientific lens.

## Eligibility Criteria and Admission 2025

To be considered for a B.Sc. in Humanities and Social Sciences, prospective students typically need to have successfully completed their Class 12 examinations from a recognized board. Most institutions require a minimum aggregate score, often around 50%. While specific requirements can vary by university, this course is open to students from all streams – Science, Commerce, or Humanities – making it a versatile option.

For admission in 2025, candidates will likely need to appear for various entrance examinations. Prominent national and university-specific tests include the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) conducted by NTA, the Jamia Millia Islamia Entrance Exam, and LPU NEST. Performance in these exams, combined with Class 12 marks, forms the basis for selection.

## Course Duration, Fees, and Subjects

The B.Sc. in Humanities and Social Sciences is a full-time undergraduate program with a standard duration of three years. The fee structure for this course can vary significantly across institutions, ranging approximately from INR 5,000 to INR 1 Lakh per year, depending on whether it's a government, state, or private university. 

The curriculum is broad and interdisciplinary, covering a wide array of subjects. While specific offerings vary, common subjects include:

*   Sociology
*   Psychology
*   Economics
*   Political Science
*   History
*   Anthropology
*   Geography
*   Philosophy
*   Linguistics

Students typically gain a strong foundation in research methodologies, data analysis, and critical interpretation across these disciplines.

## Career Scope and Salary Potential

A B.Sc. in Humanities and Social Sciences opens doors to a diverse range of career opportunities due to the transferable skills developed during the program. Graduates are well-prepared for roles requiring strong analytical abilities, communication skills, and an understanding of human behavior and societal dynamics. Popular job profiles include:

*   Content Writer/Editor
*   Human Resources Manager
*   Civil Servant (through competitive exams)
*   Researcher
*   Social Worker
*   Public Relations Specialist
*   Journalist
*   Lawyer (after further legal education)
*   Defence Services

Entry-level salaries for graduates in India can typically range from INR 4 Lakh to INR 6 Lakh per annum, with potential for significant growth based on experience, further education, and the specific industry. Top recruiters often include government ministries, NGOs, media houses, educational institutions, and corporate sectors like FMCG, pharmaceuticals, and consulting firms.

This degree provides a robust foundation for those interested in understanding the complexities of the human world and contributing meaningfully to society.
