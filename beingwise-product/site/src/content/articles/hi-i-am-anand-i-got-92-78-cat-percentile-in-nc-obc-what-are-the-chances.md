Hi Anand,

It's great you're looking into your chances at IIM Mumbai (formerly NITIE) with your 92.78 CAT percentile in the NC-OBC category. Let's break down what this means for your potential Personal Interview (PI) shortlist.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your 92.78 CAT percentile as an NC-OBC candidate puts you in a strong position for a potential PI shortlist at IIM Mumbai.
*   Meeting minimum sectional CAT cut-offs is essential, in addition to the overall percentile.
*   Shortlisting isn't solely based on CAT scores; other factors like academic profile, work experience, and gender diversity also play a role.
*   The final admission decision considers a composite score from multiple stages, including your PI performance.

## Understanding IIM Mumbai's Shortlisting Criteria

IIM Mumbai, now an Indian Institute of Management, follows a comprehensive approach to shortlisting candidates for the Personal Interview round. While your CAT percentile is a significant factor, it's not the only one. The institute considers a blend of academic achievements, reservation categories, and other diversity parameters.

For NC-OBC category candidates, there are specific minimum percentile requirements for both the overall CAT score and each section. These are the baseline you need to meet to be considered:

*   **Overall:** 75 percentile or higher
*   **VARC (Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension):** 70 percentile or higher
*   **DILR (Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning):** 65 percentile or higher
*   **QA (Quantitative Ability):** 65 percentile or higher

It's important to remember that while these are the minimums, the actual cut-offs for shortlisting can be considerably higher, depending on the applicant pool and other competitive factors each year. Your 92.78 overall percentile is well above the minimum for your category, which is a positive sign, provided you also met all the sectional cut-offs.

## The Role of Gender Diversity

IIM Mumbai, like many top institutes, promotes gender diversity in its cohorts. This means that appropriate weightage in both the overall CAT score and sectional cut-offs may be considered for female candidates. This initiative aims to create a more balanced learning environment.

## Beyond the CAT: The Composite Score

If you are shortlisted and perform well in the Personal Interview, the institute calculates a composite score to determine final admissions. This score is a comprehensive assessment that takes into account various aspects of your profile, including:

*   Your marks in Class 10 and Class 12
*   Your Bachelor's degree marks
*   Any relevant work experience you might have
*   Your CAT percentile
*   Your performance in the Personal Interview
*   Gender diversity (as mentioned earlier)

Each of these components contributes to your overall score, and a strong performance across all stages significantly boosts your chances of securing admission. The final merit list is then prepared based on these composite scores.

## What This Means for You

Given your 92.78 CAT percentile in the NC-OBC category, you certainly have a good chance of receiving a PI shortlist from IIM Mumbai, assuming you've met the sectional minimums. The next crucial steps would be to prepare thoroughly for the Personal Interview and ensure your academic profile and any work experience are well-articulated. Performing strongly in the PI and having a consistent academic record will be key to converting a shortlist into a final admission offer.

We wish you the very best in your application journey to IIM Mumbai!
