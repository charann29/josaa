# BIT Mesra MBA Placement Report 2025-26: Highest & Average CTC; Top Recruiters

The Department of Management Studies at Birla Institute of Technology (BIT), Mesra, has released its placement report for the MBA batch of 2025-26. This report offers valuable insights into the career opportunities and compensation packages secured by recent graduates.

## Key Takeaways

*   The highest salary package offered to an MBA graduate at BIT Mesra reached INR 15.12 LPA.
*   The average salary package stood at INR 7.90 LPA, indicating strong overall placements.
*   A significant majority of the registered students, over 90, successfully secured placements.
*   The batch showcased a diverse academic background and a mix of freshers and experienced professionals.

## Placement Highlights for the 2025-26 MBA Batch

The official placement data for the MBA batch of 2025-26 at BIT Mesra indicates a robust performance. Out of a batch size of 125 candidates, 118 registered for placements, and more than 90 of these students were successfully placed.

Here's a quick look at the key figures:

| Feature                                 | Details       |
| :-------------------------------------- | :------------ |
| BIT Mesra MBA Batch Size                | 125 candidates |
| Students Registered for Placement       | 118           |
| Students Placed                         | > 90          |
| Highest Package                         | INR 15.12 LPA |
| Average Package                         | INR 7.90 LPA  |
| Median Package                          | INR 8 LPA     |

## Batch Profile: Gender Diversity and Work Experience

BIT Mesra's MBA program attracts a diverse group of students. The 2025-26 batch for the main Mesra campus had 72 male students (49%) and 75 female students (51%), making a total of 147. For the Lalpur unit, there were 47 male students (53%) and 41 female students (47%), totaling 88.

In terms of work experience, the batch also presented a blend of fresh graduates and those with prior professional exposure. At the main Mesra campus, 71% (105 students) were freshers, while 29% (42 students) had previous work experience, with an average experience of 16 months. The Lalpur unit saw 77% (68 students) as freshers and 23% (20 students) with experience, averaging 20 months.

## Academic Background of MBA Graduates

The academic foundation of the MBA batch is quite varied, reflecting a multidisciplinary approach to management education. For the main Mesra campus, the breakdown of undergraduate degrees among students was:

*   BBA: 29% (43 students)
*   B.A: 20% (29 students)
*   B.Tech: 6% (9 students)
*   B.Com.: 26% (38 students)
*   B.Sc.: 12% (18 students)
*   BCA: 3% (4 students)
*   B.Pharm.: 1% (2 students)
*   Other degrees (BFMO, Hotel Management, B.Voc., BBM): 0.60-0.68% each (1 student each)

The Lalpur unit also showed a strong representation from BBA and B.Com backgrounds:

*   BBA: 49% (43 students)
*   B.Com.: 30% (26 students)
*   B.A: 9% (8 students)
*   B.Sc.: 7% (6 students)
*   B.Tech: 2% (2 students)

This diverse academic base enriches classroom discussions and provides varied perspectives within the program.

## Top Recruiters

While specific names of top recruiters were not detailed in the provided information, BIT Mesra's Department of Management Studies consistently attracts leading companies from various sectors. These typically include firms from consulting, finance, IT, manufacturing, and fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) industries, all seeking to hire skilled MBA graduates for diverse roles.

The placement report for BIT Mesra's MBA program for 2025-26 highlights the institute's commitment to providing excellent career opportunities and its strong connections with the industry.
