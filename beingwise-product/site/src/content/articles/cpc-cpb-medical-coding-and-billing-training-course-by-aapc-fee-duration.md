# CPC® + CPB®: Medical Coding and Billing Training Course by AAPC: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Medical coding and billing are vital roles in the healthcare industry, ensuring that medical services are accurately documented and properly reimbursed. The AAPC (American Academy of Professional Coders) offers a combined training program for its Certified Professional Coder (CPC®) and Certified Professional Biller (CPB®) credentials, providing a comprehensive foundation for these in-demand careers.

## Key Takeaways

*   This combined program offers in-depth training for both medical coding and billing, covering essential industry standards.
*   The entire course spans 32 weeks, delivered through a flexible online format, allowing for self-paced learning.
*   Successful completion requires achieving at least 70% on all assessments, including chapter and final exams.
*   Participants will gain expertise in critical coding systems like CPT®, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS Level II, alongside a strong understanding of health insurance and regulatory practices.

## Understanding the CPC® + CPB® Program

The CPC® + CPB® training program by AAPC is designed to equip individuals with the knowledge and skills required for both medical coding and medical billing. The course integrates the Certified Professional Coder (CPC®) curriculum, which focuses on accurately translating healthcare services into universal alphanumeric codes, with the Certified Professional Biller (CPB®) curriculum, which covers the processes involved in submitting claims and managing payments. This dual focus provides a holistic understanding of the revenue cycle in healthcare.

### Course Structure and Duration

This comprehensive program has a total duration of 32 weeks. Each component, the CPB® training and the CPC® training, is allocated 16 weeks. The learning is conducted entirely online, offering flexibility through both self-study and virtual classroom modes. Instruction is provided in English.

## Eligibility and Prerequisites

To enroll in the CPC® + CPB® training, candidates should ideally possess a foundational understanding of medical terminology and human anatomy. If you do not have this background, the AAPC recommends completing their introductory courses in these subjects first. The program is suitable for both new aspirants looking to enter the field and working professionals aiming to enhance their existing skills and knowledge in medical administration.

### Who Benefits from This Training?

This course is particularly beneficial for individuals aspiring to roles such as:

*   Medical Coders
*   Medical Billers
*   Data Analysts in healthcare
*   Data Administrators
*   Clinical Research Coordinators

## What You Will Learn

The curriculum is extensive, covering key areas essential for both coding and billing professionals:

### CPC® Preparation Course Syllabus Highlights

*   The Business of Medicine
*   Review of Anatomy
*   Overview of ICD-10-CM and its application guidelines
*   Introduction to CPT® and HCPCS Level II coding systems
*   Modifiers
*   Coding for various body systems (e.g., Integumentary, Musculoskeletal, Cardiovascular, Digestive, Urinary, Reproductive, Nervous, Special Senses)
*   Coding for Anesthesia, Radiology, Pathology & Laboratory, and Evaluation & Management Services

### CPB™ Medical Billing Course Syllabus Highlights

*   Principles of Medical Billing
*   Health Insurance Industry specifics
*   Reimbursement methodologies
*   Legal and regulatory issues in healthcare billing
*   Understanding the business aspects of medicine

Upon successful completion, you will be proficient in the principles of medical coding related to CPT®, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS Level II code sets, alongside a thorough understanding of health insurance, reimbursement, and relevant legal frameworks.

## Certification and Completion Requirements

While this program provides comprehensive training, it's important to note that the AAPC offers completion certificates for the training course itself, not the professional certifications (CPC® and CPB®). To receive a course completion certificate, students must finish the program within the designated timeframe (typically four months for each 16-week segment). Furthermore, a passing score of 70% or higher is required on all chapter exams, the final exam, and for the overall course score.

## How to Apply

To enroll in the CPC® + CPB®: Medical Coding and Billing Training Course, prospective students should visit the official AAPC website. Navigate to the training and events section, locate the specific course, and follow the prompts to enroll. This typically involves adding the course to your cart and proceeding through the sign-in and checkout process on their secure portal.

Embarking on this combined training program can open doors to a rewarding career in the vital field of medical coding and billing, equipping you with the expertise to navigate the complexities of healthcare administration.
