# SBM NMIMS Mumbai Master of Business Administration (MBA) Vs SPJIMR: Compare Fees, Placement, Reviews, Cutoffs

Choosing the right MBA program is a pivotal decision for your career. This guide offers a detailed comparison between the Master of Business Administration (MBA) at SBM NMIMS Mumbai and the Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM) at SPJIMR, highlighting key aspects like fees, placements, student reviews, and more, to help you make an informed choice.

## Key takeaways

*   Both institutions are highly regarded, with SBM NMIMS offering an MBA degree and SPJIMR a PGDM diploma, both recognized as equivalent postgraduate management programs.
*   SPJIMR generally reports higher median and highest placement salaries compared to SBM NMIMS, based on available data.
*   SBM NMIMS has a higher student review count, indicating a larger pool of feedback, though both institutions hold an average rating of 4.2 out of 5.
*   There is a notable difference in the total program fees, with SPJIMR's PGDM being more economical than SBM NMIMS's MBA.

## Overview of the Programs

Both SBM NMIMS Mumbai and SPJIMR are well-established private institutions in Mumbai, both founded in 1981. While SBM NMIMS offers a Master of Business Administration (MBA) which is a degree program, SPJIMR provides a Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM), which is a diploma. Both are full-time, two-year postgraduate courses designed to equip students with essential business acumen and leadership skills.

## Student Ratings and Reviews

Student feedback offers valuable insights into the campus experience. Both SBM NMIMS's MBA and SPJIMR's PGDM programs hold an impressive overall rating of 4.2 out of 5 from verified students. However, the volume of reviews differs significantly:

*   **SBM NMIMS Mumbai MBA:** Rated 4.2/5 based on 91 verified reviews.
    *   Placements: 4.2/5
    *   Infrastructure: 4.4/5
    *   Faculty: 4.5/5
    *   Crowd & Campus: 4.1/5
    *   Value for Money: 3.9/5
*   **SPJIMR PGDM:** Rated 4.2/5 based on 26 verified reviews.
    *   Placements: 4.3/5
    *   Infrastructure: 3.9/5
    *   Faculty: 4.3/5
    *   Crowd & Campus: 4.4/5
    *   Value for Money: 4.3/5

SPJIMR's reviews indicate a slightly higher rating for placements and value for money, while SBM NMIMS scores higher on infrastructure and faculty.

## Fees and Financial Commitment

Understanding the financial investment is crucial. Here's a comparison of the total program fees for the two-year courses:

*   **SBM NMIMS Mumbai MBA:** The total fees for the two-year program are approximately INR 27.00 Lakh.
*   **SPJIMR PGDM:** The total fees for the two-year program are approximately INR 22.50 Lakh.

SPJIMR's PGDM program presents a more budget-friendly option compared to the MBA at SBM NMIMS.

## Placement Opportunities

Placements are a key indicator of a program's success and career prospects. Both institutions boast strong placement records, with SPJIMR reporting 100% batch placement.

### SBM NMIMS Mumbai MBA Placements

*   **Highest Salary:** INR 67.70 Lakh
*   **Median Salary:** INR 32.85 Lakh

### SPJIMR PGDM Placements

*   **Highest Salary:** INR 75.00 Lakh
*   **Percentage Batch Placed:** 100%

While specific median salary data for SPJIMR's latest batch isn't provided in the source, its reported highest salary is higher than SBM NMIMS, and it confirms 100% placement, indicating robust career support.

## Rankings

National rankings provide an external assessment of an institution's quality. While specific year-on-year consistency can vary, both institutions are recognized in prominent rankings.

*   **SBM NMIMS Mumbai:** Ranked 20th in 2024 (as per NIRF '25 data, which often refers to the current year's assessment for the next year's list) and 24th in 2023.
*   **SPJIMR:** Ranked 3rd in 2024 (as per NIRF '25 data) and 8th in 2023.

According to the provided ranking data, SPJIMR consistently holds a higher position than SBM NMIMS.

Both SBM NMIMS and SPJIMR offer excellent postgraduate management programs. Your final decision should align with your career aspirations, financial considerations, and preferred learning environment.
