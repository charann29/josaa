# NEET MDS 2026: Exam Date (May 2), Final Edit Window, City Slip, Admit Card & Syllabus

For aspiring dental professionals aiming for postgraduate studies, the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Masters of Dental Surgery (NEET MDS) is a crucial examination. This guide provides essential details about the NEET MDS 2026 schedule, application process, and exam structure.

## Key Takeaways

*   The NEET MDS 2026 examination is scheduled for May 2, 2026, conducted in a computer-based format.
*   A final correction window for images, signatures, and thumb impressions will open on April 21, 2026.
*   Candidates can expect the NEET MDS city intimation slip on April 22, 2026, to know their exam city.
*   The admit card for the exam will be released on April 29, 2026.

## Understanding NEET MDS

NEET MDS is a national-level entrance and ranking examination conducted annually by the National Board of Examinations (NBE) for admission to Master of Dental Surgery (MDS) courses across India. It serves as the primary gateway for postgraduate dental programs, with scores accepted by most dental colleges nationwide. The exam facilitates admissions to approximately 6,501 MDS seats, covering the 50% All India Quota, 50% state quota seats, and all seats in deemed/central universities and private institutions.

## Application Form Correction and City Slip

Candidates who have registered for NEET MDS 2026 should be aware of the upcoming windows for making necessary corrections. Following the initial correction period, a **final edit window** will be available on April 21, 2026. This specific window is for rectifying issues related to uploaded photographs, signatures, or thumb impressions. It is crucial to ensure these details are correct as per NBE guidelines.

Shortly after, on April 22, 2026, the **NEET MDS city intimation slip 2026** will be released. This slip will inform registered candidates about the city where their examination centre will be located, helping them plan their travel arrangements in advance. The official NEET MDS admit card will then be made available for download on April 29, 2026.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus Overview

The NEET MDS 2026 examination is a computer-based test with a duration of three hours. The paper consists of 240 multiple-choice questions, with each correct answer fetching 4 marks. There is negative marking, so it's important to answer carefully. The total marks for the examination are 960.

The syllabus for NEET MDS covers topics from both BDS Part I and Part II curricula. It is broadly divided into two parts:

*   **Part A (Pre-Clinical & Para-Clinical Subjects):** Includes subjects like General Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry, Pharmacology, Pathology, Microbiology, Dental Anatomy, Histology, Oral Pathology, and more.
*   **Part B (Clinical Subjects):** Covers areas such as General Medicine, General Surgery, Oral Medicine and Radiology, Periodontology, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, Prosthodontics, Conservative Dentistry, Orthodontics, Pedodontics, and Public Health Dentistry.

Candidates are advised to refer to the detailed syllabus provided by the NBE for comprehensive preparation.

## Post-Exam Procedures

After the NEET MDS result declaration, the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) will conduct the counselling process for admission to MDS courses. This involves various rounds of counselling based on the ranks secured by candidates in the examination.

Thorough preparation and attention to these key dates will significantly help candidates in their journey towards securing a postgraduate dental seat.
