# SNAP Answer Key 2025 (Test 1, 2 & 3): Check Unofficial SNAP Questions & Answers

For students who appeared in the Symbiosis National Aptitude Test (SNAP) 2025, understanding the questions and potential answers is a crucial step towards evaluating performance. While the official SNAP examination body does not release answer keys, memory-based questions and solutions compiled from test-takers can offer valuable insights.

## Key takeaways

*   The official SNAP examination body does not publish answer keys or response sheets for the SNAP exam.
*   Memory-based answer keys are compiled from student recollections and online discussions, offering unofficial insights into the exam content.
*   These unofficial answer keys help candidates gauge their performance and estimate potential cut-off scores for various Symbiosis International (Deemed University) institutes.
*   The SNAP 2025 exam was conducted across three sessions on December 6, December 14, and December 20, 2025.

## Understanding Memory-Based SNAP Answer Keys

Unlike many other national-level entrance exams, the SNAP examination body has not released official answer keys since 2020. Before this, when SNAP was a single-attempt examination, answer keys were typically published. However, with the introduction of multiple exam sessions where candidates can attempt the test more than once, the practice of releasing official keys has ceased.

This makes memory-based answer keys particularly important. These are reconstructed from the collective memory of students who appeared for the exam, often shared across various online platforms. While not official, they provide a good reference point for understanding the types of questions asked, their difficulty level, and potential correct answers.

## SNAP 2025 Memory-Based Questions (Test 1)

The first session of SNAP 2025 featured a mix of question types across different sections:

### General English

*   Fill in the blanks
*   Idioms and phrases
*   Assumption-based questions
*   Course of action questions

### Logical Reasoning

*   Coding-decoding
*   Input-output sequences
*   Blood relations
*   Seating arrangement puzzles

### Quantitative Aptitude

*   Simple Interest and Compound Interest
*   Time and distance problems
*   Area calculation (e.g., area of a triangle)
*   Least Common Multiple (LCM) based problems
*   Probability
*   Questions on reflex angles
*   Average calculations

## SNAP 2025 Memory-Based Questions (Test 2)

Test 2 included specific questions where students often recall exact problem statements. Here are a few examples with their probable answers:

*   **Question:** Find the unit digit of 1!+2!+....+100!
    *   **Answer:** 3
*   **Question:** If a man covers 1/3rd of distances at speeds of 10 km/h, 20 km/h, and 60 km/h respectively. Find his average speed.
    *   **Answer:** 18 km/h
*   **Question:** If the HCF of two numbers is 15 and their product is 1800, find their LCM.
    *   **Answer:** 120
*   **Question:** If there are two red and two blue dice, find the probability that the sum of the dice is a prime number, where the number on the red die is greater than the number on the blue die.
    *   **Answer:** 7/15
*   **Question:** If a clock loses 5 minutes every hour and was set correctly at 6 AM on Monday, what will be the time displayed at 6 PM on Tuesday?
    *   **Answer:** 3 PM, Tuesday
*   **Question:** What was the day on August 15, 1947?
    *   **Answer:** Friday
*   **Question:** If a man deposits 100 rupees in the first month and increases his investment by 50 rupees every subsequent month, what will be the total amount in his account by the end of the year?
    *   **Answer:** 4500 rupees
*   **Question:** If A, B, C, D, E, F are 6 people, and A must sit with B, while C does not want to sit with D, find the number of ways they can sit together.
    *   **Answer:** 144
