# CA (Chartered Accountant): Course, Full Form, Duration, Admission 2025, Fees, Subjects

Becoming a Chartered Accountant (CA) in India is a highly respected career path, offering deep expertise in finance, taxation, and auditing. This challenging yet rewarding profession is managed by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI).

## Key takeaways

*   The CA program is administered by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI).
*   The full CA qualification typically takes about 4.5 to 5 years to complete after Class 12, or around 3 years after graduation.
*   Admission to the CA Foundation course is open to students who have passed Class 12, while graduates can directly enter the Intermediate level.
*   The course involves three levels: Foundation, Intermediate, and Final, along with a mandatory practical training period.

## Understanding the CA Full Form and Program Structure

CA stands for Chartered Accountant. This professional designation signifies expertise in accounting, auditing, taxation, and financial management. The ICAI designs and conducts the examinations for the CA program in India. The journey to becoming a CA is structured into three main stages:

1.  **CA Foundation:** This is the entry-level course for students after Class 12.
2.  **CA Intermediate:** Students can enter this level either after clearing CA Foundation or directly after completing graduation in commerce or related fields.
3.  **CA Final:** This is the concluding stage of the CA program, taken after clearing Intermediate and completing practical training.

## Duration of the CA Course

The total duration to become a Chartered Accountant varies depending on your entry point:

*   **After Class 12:** If you start with the CA Foundation course, the entire program, including practical training, generally takes about 4.5 to 5 years.
*   **After Graduation:** If you opt for direct entry into the CA Intermediate course after completing your degree, the program typically takes around 3 to 3.5 years.

## CA Admission 2025: Eligibility and Application Process

Admission to the CA program for 2025 will follow the guidelines set by the ICAI. Here’s a general overview of the eligibility and application process:

### CA Foundation Entry

*   **Eligibility:** You must have passed your Class 12 examinations from a recognized board.
*   **Application:** Registration for the Foundation course is typically open throughout the year, with specific cut-off dates for appearing in the May/June or November/December examinations.

### Direct Entry (After Graduation)

*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Commerce graduates/post-graduates with at least 55% aggregate marks, or
    *   Non-commerce graduates/post-graduates with at least 60% aggregate marks.
    *   Students who have passed the Intermediate level of the Institute of Cost Accountants of India or the Institute of Company Secretaries of India are also eligible.
*   **Application:** Eligible graduates can directly register for the CA Intermediate course.

### Application Procedure

All applications are processed through the official ICAI website. Candidates need to register online, submit the required documents, and pay the stipulated fees.

## CA Course Fees (Approximate)

The fees for the CA program are set by the ICAI and are generally paid at each stage of registration. These figures are approximate and subject to change by the ICAI:

*   **CA Foundation:** Around INR 9,800 (registration and examination fees).
*   **CA Intermediate:** Approximately INR 27,200 (includes registration for both groups and examination fees).
*   **CA Final:** Roughly INR 32,700 (includes registration for both groups and examination fees).
*   **Practical Training (Articleship):** While there isn't a direct fee, students may incur costs for study materials and other expenses during this period. Some firms offer a stipend.

## Key Subjects in the CA Curriculum

The CA curriculum is comprehensive, covering a wide range of subjects essential for a finance professional. Here's a glimpse of the core areas at each level:

### CA Foundation Subjects

*   Principles and Practice of Accounting
*   Business Laws and Business Correspondence and Reporting
*   Business Mathematics and Logical Reasoning & Statistics
*   Business Economics and Business and Commercial Knowledge

### CA Intermediate Subjects

*   **Group I:** Accounting, Corporate and Other Laws, Cost and Management Accounting, Taxation (Income Tax Law and Indirect Tax Law)
*   **Group II:** Advanced Accounting, Auditing and Assurance, Enterprise Information Systems & Strategic Management, Financial Management & Economics for Finance

### CA Final Subjects

*   **Group I:** Financial Reporting, Strategic Financial Management, Advanced Auditing and Professional Ethics, Corporate and Economic Laws
*   **Group II:** Strategic Cost Management and Performance Evaluation, Elective Paper (choose one from various options), Direct Tax Laws & International Taxation, Indirect Tax Laws

## Practical Training (Articleship)

A mandatory component of the CA program is the practical training, also known as articleship. This involves working under a practicing Chartered Accountant for a period of three years. This hands-on experience is crucial for developing practical skills in auditing, taxation, and accounting, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and real-world application.

The journey to becoming a Chartered Accountant is rigorous but equips individuals with invaluable skills and opens doors to a multitude of career opportunities in India and globally.
