# Is SNAP Tougher Than CAT Exam? - Know Differences and Similarities

Many aspiring MBA students wonder about the difficulty of the SNAP and CAT exams. While both are crucial gateways to top management programs in India, they present distinct challenges and opportunities.

## Key Takeaways

*   SNAP is generally considered easier than CAT, though it requires specific preparation strategies.
*   CAT is accepted by a much broader range of institutions, including all IIMs and over 1300 other MBA colleges.
*   SNAP offers three attempts in a single admission cycle, allowing candidates to choose their best score.
*   Both exams share a similar syllabus but differ significantly in question type, pattern, and depth of difficulty.

## Understanding the Difficulty: SNAP vs. CAT

The Common Admission Test (CAT) is widely regarded as one of the most challenging MBA entrance exams in India. Conducted by the IIMs, it serves as the primary admission test for all IIMs and numerous other leading business schools across the country. The high volume of applicants, often exceeding 3 lakh, contributes to its competitive nature and rigorous selection criteria.

In contrast, the Symbiosis National Aptitude Test (SNAP) is typically considered less difficult than CAT. While its syllabus covers similar areas, the questions are generally more direct and less complex. SNAP is the mandatory entrance exam for admission to the 16 constituent institutes of Symbiosis International (Deemed University) (SIU), which offer a diverse range of popular and niche MBA programs.

## Exam Structure and Pattern

Both CAT and SNAP assess candidates on Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), and Quantitative Ability (QA). However, their exam patterns and specific section breakdowns differ.

### CAT Exam Pattern

The CAT exam is a computer-based test conducted for a duration of two hours. It features a mix of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and Type-In-The-Answer (TITA) questions. There is negative marking for incorrect MCQs, but typically not for TITA questions. The official CAT exam structure has seen some adjustments in recent years, including a reduction in the total number of questions, aiming for a more student-friendly approach, as noted by the conducting IIMs.

### SNAP Exam Pattern

SNAP is also a computer-based test, but it is shorter, typically 60 minutes long. It focuses entirely on MCQs, and there is negative marking for incorrect answers. A unique feature of SNAP is that candidates can appear for the test up to three times in a single admission cycle, with their best score considered for admissions. This flexibility can alleviate some pressure compared to the single-attempt CAT.

## Syllabus Overlap and Preparation Strategies

The core syllabus for both CAT and SNAP largely overlaps, covering fundamental concepts in quantitative aptitude, logical reasoning, data interpretation, and verbal ability. Therefore, a significant portion of your preparation for one exam can benefit the other.

However, the depth and type of questions vary considerably. CAT questions often require a deeper understanding of concepts, critical thinking, and advanced problem-solving skills. SNAP, while still requiring a strong conceptual foundation, tends to test speed and accuracy with comparatively simpler questions. For instance, while CAT's QA section might involve complex geometry or number theory problems, SNAP might focus more on arithmetic and basic algebra.

Given that these exams are often conducted within weeks of each other (for example, CAT in late November and SNAP in December), many students prepare for both simultaneously. It's crucial to dedicate the final weeks before each exam to practice specific question types and mock tests tailored to that particular exam's pattern and difficulty level.

## Which Exam Should You Choose?

The choice between CAT and SNAP, or deciding to appear for both, largely depends on your target colleges and career aspirations. If your aim is to get into an IIM or one of the many top-tier business schools that accept CAT scores, then CAT is indispensable. If you are specifically targeting the highly-regarded MBA programs offered by Symbiosis International (Deemed University) institutes, then SNAP is your essential gateway.

Many candidates opt to take both exams to maximize their chances, leveraging the common syllabus while adapting to the unique demands of each test. Ultimately, a clear understanding of your desired institutions will guide your decision.
