Trigonometric functions are a fundamental part of mathematics, essential for understanding relationships between angles and sides in triangles. From calculating building heights to mapping celestial distances, these functions are crucial across science and engineering.

## Key takeaways

*   Trigonometric functions define the ratios of sides in a right-angled triangle, linking angles to lengths.
*   The six core functions are sine, cosine, tangent, cosecant, secant, and cotangent.
*   Understanding their domains, ranges, graphs, and identities is vital for advanced studies in Class 12 and beyond.
*   These functions are extensively applied in physics (waves, motion), engineering (design), and astronomy (navigation).

## What are Trigonometric Functions?

At its heart, trigonometry, derived from Greek words meaning 'three-side measurement,' is the study of triangles. Trigonometric functions are mathematical tools that describe how the angles inside a right-angled triangle relate to the lengths of its sides. There are six primary functions, each representing a specific ratio of two sides:

*   **Sine (sin):** The ratio of the length of the side opposite the angle to the length of the hypotenuse.
*   **Cosine (cos):** The ratio of the length of the side adjacent to the angle to the length of the hypotenuse.
*   **Tangent (tan):** The ratio of the length of the side opposite the angle to the length of the side adjacent to the angle.
*   **Cosecant (csc):** The reciprocal of sine (hypotenuse / opposite side).
*   **Secant (sec):** The reciprocal of cosine (hypotenuse / adjacent side).
*   **Cotangent (cot):** The reciprocal of tangent (adjacent side / opposite side).

These functions allow us to determine unknown angles or side lengths when some information is already known.

## Core Trigonometric Formulas

For a right-angled triangle with an angle 't':

*   `sin(t) = Opposite / Hypotenuse`
*   `cos(t) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse`
*   `tan(t) = Opposite / Adjacent`
*   `csc(t) = 1 / sin(t) = Hypotenuse / Opposite`
*   `sec(t) = 1 / cos(t) = Hypotenuse / Adjacent`
*   `cot(t) = 1 / tan(t) = Adjacent / Opposite`

These foundational formulas are used to solve a wide range of problems, from basic geometry to complex calculus.

## Key Topics in Trigonometric Functions

As students progress from Class 10 to Class 12, the study of trigonometric functions deepens. Key areas of focus include:

*   **Domains and Ranges:** Understanding the possible input values (angles) and output values (ratios) for each function.
*   **Graphs of Trigonometric Functions:** Visualizing the periodic nature of these functions on a coordinate plane.
*   **Trigonometric Identities:** Equations that are true for all valid values of the variables, such as `sin²(t) + cos²(t) = 1`.
*   **Inverse Trigonometric Functions:** Functions that determine the angle when the trigonometric ratio is known (e.g., arcsin, arccos, arctan).
*   **Differentiation and Integration:** Applying calculus principles to trigonometric and inverse trigonometric functions, a crucial part of Class 12 mathematics.
