# Sr.Secondary: Sociology (331) by National Institute of Open Schooling via Swayam: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For students interested in understanding human behavior and societal structures, the Sr. Secondary: Sociology (331) online course, offered by the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) through the Swayam platform, provides a comprehensive introduction. This program is ideal for those at the senior secondary level looking to explore fundamental sociological concepts.

## Key takeaways

*   This is a 26-week, self-paced online course delivered in English through video lectures and text materials.
*   It awards 10 credit points upon successful completion, leading to a certificate from NIOS and Swayam.
*   The curriculum covers core sociological theories, social institutions, change processes, and aspects of Indian society.
*   No course or certificate fees are mentioned, making it an accessible option for learning sociology.

## Course Overview

This specialized online course delves into the study of human behavior within society and the broader implications of social life. Led by Professor Dr. Sukanta Kumar Mahapatra from NIOS, the program aims to equip learners with an understanding of key sociological concepts and research methods. It explores how individuals form groups and interact, shaping the social fabric around them.

The course is structured into five modules and is designed to be completed over 26 weeks. It encourages a self-study approach, allowing students to learn at their own pace. Upon meeting all course requirements and passing evaluations, participants are eligible to receive a joint certification from NIOS and Swayam.

## Learning Experience and Materials

Students enrolled in the Sr. Secondary: Sociology (331) course benefit from a rich learning environment. The program utilizes an interactive platform that includes video lectures and extensive reading materials. To foster a collaborative learning atmosphere, peer discussion forums are also available, allowing students to engage with their classmates and deepen their understanding of the subject matter. The medium of instruction for all content is English.

## What You Will Learn

Upon completing the Sr. Secondary: Sociology (331) certification classes, participants will gain valuable insights into a wide array of sociological topics. The curriculum is designed to provide a holistic view of society and its dynamics, covering:

*   Fundamental concepts within sociology.
*   The nature of social institutions and social stratification.
*   Processes that drive social change.
*   Different segments and structures of Indian society.
*   The historical and contemporary status of women in Indian society.
*   The concept of cultural pluralism.

## Course Syllabus at a Glance

The 26-week program is systematically organized to cover essential sociological themes. Here’s a weekly breakdown of the topics:

| Week(s) | Topics Covered |
| :------ | :------------- |
| 1-4     | Introduction to Sociology, its emergence, relationship with other social sciences, and research methods. |
| 5-9     | Core societal concepts: society, community, association, institution, social groups, structure, system, norms, values, status, and role. |
| 10-14   | Social interactions and relationships: cooperation, competition, conflict, acculturation, assimilation, integration, marriage, family, and kinship. |
| 15-16   | Economic, political, religious institutions, and social stratification. |
| 17-19   | Dynamics of social change: factors, processes, socialization, social control, and deviance. |
| 20-22   | Society and environment, Indian social thinkers, unity and diversity, national integration, and Indian society (tribal, rural, urban, caste system). |
| 23-24   | Major religious communities and social problems in India, including issues faced by Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and other deprived sections. |
| 25-26   | Status of women in Indian society, gender discrimination, equality, women's empowerment, cultural concepts, Indian cultural heritage, cultural pluralism, and media impact on culture. |

## How to Apply

Enrolling in the Sr. Secondary: Sociology (331) course is a straightforward process. Interested individuals can follow these steps to register:

1.  Visit the official Swayam platform for the course.
2.  Locate and click on the "Sign-in" or "Register" option, typically found in the top right corner of the page.
3.  On the subsequent page, you will be prompted to either sign in with an existing account or create a new one using your username and password to proceed with enrollment.

This course offers a valuable opportunity for anyone keen to explore the fascinating field of sociology at the senior secondary level, providing foundational knowledge and a recognized certificate.
