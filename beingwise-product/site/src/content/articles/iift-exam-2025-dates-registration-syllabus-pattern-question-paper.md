# IIFT Exam 2025: Dates, Registration, Syllabus, Pattern, Question Paper, Preparation, Result, Cut Offs

For those aspiring to pursue an MBA in International Business or Business Analytics from the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT), it's crucial to understand the updated admission process. The institute now primarily considers scores from the Common Admission Test (CAT) for its MBA programs.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIFT no longer conducts its own entrance exam; admission is based on CAT scores.
*   The National Testing Agency (NTA) previously administered the IIFT entrance exam but is no longer involved in the current admission cycle.
*   Candidates will need to apply to IIFT separately and also appear for the CAT exam.
*   Shortlisting for the subsequent selection rounds (like Personal Interview and Extempore) will primarily depend on CAT scores.

## The Shift to CAT Scores for IIFT Admissions

Historically, the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT) conducted its own national-level entrance examination for admission to its prestigious MBA (International Business) and MBA (Business Analytics) programs. This exam was managed by the National Testing Agency (NTA), which handled everything from registration to result declaration. However, starting from the 2024-25 academic year, IIFT has transitioned to accepting CAT scores for its admission process. This change means that prospective students no longer need to take a separate IIFT entrance test.

For the upcoming 2025 admissions, candidates must take the CAT exam. IIFT will then use these CAT scores to shortlist applicants for further stages of their selection process, which typically include an Extempore and a Personal Interview (PI). For instance, in a previous cycle, a CAT score of around 98 percentile was a key factor for PI shortlisting, with additional weight for gender diversity.

## IIFT Admission Process and Eligibility

Since the IIFT entrance exam is no longer conducted, the admission process now involves applying to IIFT directly after appearing for the CAT exam. The eligibility criteria for IIFT's MBA programs remain similar to previous years, generally requiring a bachelor's degree with a certain minimum percentage of marks. Specific details on eligibility will be provided by IIFT on their official admissions portal.

The selection process post-CAT score shortlisting will involve rounds like Extempore and Personal Interview. These rounds are designed to assess a candidate's communication skills, general awareness, and suitability for the rigorous MBA programs at IIFT. The institute has historically conducted these rounds online.

## IIFT Application Process

Even though the entrance exam is no longer held, candidates still need to complete the application process for IIFT. This typically involves registering on the official IIFT admission portal, filling out the application form, and paying the required application fee. It is essential to keep track of the application window as announced by IIFT to ensure timely submission. Candidates must also ensure they have successfully registered for and appeared in the CAT exam.

## Understanding IIFT Cut-Offs and Results

With the shift to CAT scores, the IIFT cut-offs will now refer to the minimum CAT percentile required for shortlisting. These cut-offs can vary each year based on factors such as the number of applicants, the difficulty of the CAT exam, and the overall performance of candidates. IIFT will release the list of shortlisted candidates based on their CAT scores for the subsequent selection rounds. Following the interviews and extempore, IIFT will announce the final results and merit list for admission to its MBA programs.
