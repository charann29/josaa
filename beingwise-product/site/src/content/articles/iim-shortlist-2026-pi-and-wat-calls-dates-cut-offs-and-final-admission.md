# IIM Shortlist 2026 - PI & WAT Calls, Dates, Cut Offs & Final Admission Status of all IIMs

Securing a spot in an Indian Institute of Management (IIM) is a dream for many, and the journey begins with the shortlisting process for the Personal Interview (PI) and Written Ability Test (WAT) rounds. This page provides essential information regarding the IIM shortlisting status, key dates, and what to expect for the 2026 MBA admissions cycle.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIMs have begun releasing their shortlist for the PI and WAT rounds for the 2026-28 MBA batch.
*   The shortlisting criteria primarily depend on your CAT score, along with academic performance and other factors.
*   Top IIMs conduct their selection processes individually, while newer IIMs often participate in the Common Admission Process (CAP) or Joint Admission Process (JAP).
*   The final admission lists for all IIMs are expected by the end of May 2026.

## IIM Shortlisting and Selection Process Explained

The admission process at IIMs generally involves three main stages. Initially, candidates are shortlisted based on their CAT scores against the qualifying cut-offs set by each institute. Following this, selected candidates are invited to participate in the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds. Finally, a composite score is calculated for each candidate, considering their performance in CAT, WAT, PI, academic record, and work experience, leading to the final admission offer.

### Stage 1: Initial Shortlisting Based on CAT Scores

After the CAT results are announced, each IIM releases its specific qualifying CAT cut-offs. Candidates who meet these cut-offs are invited to apply for the subsequent WAT/PI rounds through the respective IIM's official website. It's important to note that the *qualifying* cut-offs are often lower than the *final* cut-offs for admission.

### Stage 2: WAT and PI Rounds

Candidates who successfully apply and meet the second-stage shortlisting criteria (which includes CAT score and academic profile) receive invitations for the WAT and PI rounds. These rounds are crucial for evaluating communication skills, general awareness, and suitability for the MBA program. For the 2026 admission cycle, these rounds are generally scheduled for March and April.

### Stage 3: Final Selection and Composite Score Calculation

The final selection is based on a comprehensive evaluation. A composite score is generated, taking into account various components such as your CAT percentile, performance in the WAT and PI, academic background (10th, 12th, and graduation scores), and any relevant work experience. Candidates with the highest composite scores are then offered admission.

## Understanding IIM CAP and JAP

While some of the older and more established IIMs conduct their admissions process independently, many newer IIMs participate in a Common Admission Process (CAP) or Joint Admission Process (JAP). These processes streamline the WAT and PI rounds for multiple participating IIMs, meaning a single WAT/PI performance can be considered by several institutes. For example, IIM Raipur has released its IIM JAP 2026 cut-offs, and IIM Bodhgaya has released its IIM CAP 2026 cut-offs. The newest IIM, IIM Guwahati, is also expected to conduct its admissions individually for its inaugural MBA batch in 2026.

## Expected CAT Cut-Offs for IIM Shortlisting

For the 2026 admissions, the qualifying CAT cut-offs for the General category typically range between 80-90 percentile. However, it is crucial to understand that these are *minimum* qualifying scores. The *actual* cut-offs for receiving a PI call from the top IIMs, and certainly for final admission, are significantly higher, often exceeding the 95th percentile for premier institutes.

## Final Admission Status 2026

After the WAT and PI rounds are concluded, the IIMs will meticulously evaluate all aspects of the candidates' profiles. The final admission offers, including those from IIM CAP participating institutes, are anticipated to be released by the end of May 2026. Keep an eye on the official portals for these crucial announcements.

Preparing thoroughly for the WAT and PI rounds, understanding the specific requirements of each IIM, and staying updated on official announcements are crucial steps towards securing your desired MBA seat.
