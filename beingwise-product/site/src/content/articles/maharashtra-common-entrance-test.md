# Maharashtra Common Entrance Test
The Maharashtra Common Entrance Test (MHT CET) is a crucial state-level examination for students aspiring to pursue professional undergraduate courses in engineering, pharmacy, agriculture, and other fields in Maharashtra.

## Key Takeaways
*   MHT CET is a computer-based test conducted only at designated examination centers, not online from home.
*   The exam covers topics from both Class 11 (20% weightage) and Class 12 (80% weightage) of the Maharashtra board syllabus.
*   Admission to various courses, including B.Tech, B.Pharm, B.Sc (Agriculture), and BPT, can be secured through MHT CET scores.
*   The MHT CET is conducted once a year; there is no second attempt in the same academic cycle.

## What is MHT CET?
MHT CET is an annual entrance examination organized by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra. It serves as a gateway for admissions to a wide range of undergraduate programs offered by government, government-aided, university-managed, and unaided institutions across the state. This includes popular fields like Engineering (B.Tech), Pharmacy (B.Pharm), Agriculture (B.Sc Agri), and Health Sciences programs such as Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT).

## Eligibility and Application for MHT CET
To be eligible for MHT CET, candidates generally need to have passed their 10+2 examination with the required subjects. While students from outside Maharashtra can appear for the exam, they might have limitations in claiming state quota seats during the Centralized Admission Process (CAP) rounds. Such candidates often rely on their JEE Main scores for admissions to engineering courses in Maharashtra, if applicable.

All official information regarding MHT CET, including application forms, important dates, admit cards, answer keys, results, and counseling schedules, is published on the official website: cetcell.mahacet.org. It is vital for all applicants to refer to this portal for accurate and up-to-date details.

## Examination Structure and Syllabus
The MHT CET is a computer-based test (CBT) that assesses a candidate's knowledge in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology. The syllabus is based on the Maharashtra State Board's Class 11 and Class 12 curriculum. As per the official MHT CET syllabus, approximately 20% of the questions are drawn from Class 11 topics, while the remaining 80% come from Class 12 topics. Candidates can choose to appear for the Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM) group or the Physics, Chemistry, Biology (PCB) group, depending on their desired course of study.

## Admission and Counselling Process
After the MHT CET results are declared, the State CET Cell conducts a Centralized Admission Process (CAP) for seat allocation. This multi-round counseling process involves online registration, document verification, choice filling for colleges and courses, and seat allotment based on merit and category. Cut-off lists for various colleges and branches are released during these rounds, indicating the minimum percentile required for admission. Candidates who secure a valid domicile in Maharashtra are eligible to participate in the state quota seats through CAP.

Preparing well for MHT CET opens doors to numerous academic opportunities within Maharashtra's higher education landscape.
