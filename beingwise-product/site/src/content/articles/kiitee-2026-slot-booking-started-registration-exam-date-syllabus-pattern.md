# KIITEE 2026: Slot Booking (Started), Registration, Exam Date, Syllabus, Pattern
The Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology Entrance Examination (KIITEE) is a university-level entrance test for admission to a wide array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs offered by KIIT, a deemed university. This guide provides essential details for prospective students aiming for the 2026 academic year.

## Key takeaways
*   **Registration Deadline:** Phase 1 registration for KIITEE 2026 concludes on April 8, 2026. Make sure to apply online before this date.
*   **Slot Booking:** Slot booking for Phase 1 has already begun on April 8, 2026, allowing candidates to select their preferred exam date and time.
*   **Exam Dates:** The Phase 1 examination is scheduled from April 16 to 20, 2026. Subsequent phases will be held in June and July.
*   **No Application Fee:** There is no fee required to submit the KIITEE application form.

## Understanding KIITEE 2026
KIITEE serves as the gateway for admissions to numerous courses at the Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology, with the exception of MBBS, BDS, and certain postgraduate medical and dental programs. The examination is crucial for securing a spot in the university's diverse academic offerings.

KIITEE is conducted in multiple phases, providing flexibility for applicants. Each course may have a specific syllabus and exam pattern, so it's vital for candidates to refer to the official KIITEE portal for detailed information relevant to their chosen program. Merit lists are prepared separately for each course based on the marks obtained in KIITEE, followed by a counselling and seat allocation process.

## Eligibility Criteria for B.Tech
For aspiring B.Tech students, the eligibility requirements are specific:

*   **Academic Qualification:** Candidates must have passed their 10+2 examination or an equivalent qualification within the last two years, or be appearing for it in the current year.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A minimum aggregate of 60% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) combined is required.

It is important to check the official KIITEE website for detailed eligibility criteria for other courses, as they may vary.

## Application Process and Slot Booking
Applying for KIITEE 2026 is an online process, and the application form is available on the official KIITEE portal: kiitee.kiit.ac.in. As mentioned, there is no application fee, making the initial step of applying accessible to all eligible candidates.

Once registered, candidates must proceed with slot booking. This process allows you to choose your preferred examination date and time from the available slots within the designated exam window. For Phase 1, slot booking commenced on April 8, 2026. Securing your slot early is advisable to get your desired date.

## KIITEE 2026 Exam Pattern (for B.Tech)
For B.Tech admissions, KIITEE is a computer-based test designed to assess a candidate's aptitude in core science subjects. Here's a breakdown of the pattern:

*   **Format:** The exam consists of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).
*   **Total Questions:** There are 120 questions in total.
*   **Sections:** Questions are divided across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
*   **Duration:** The total examination time is 2 hours and 30 minutes.
*   **Marking Scheme:**
    *   Correct Answer: +4 marks
    *   Incorrect Answer: -1 mark
    *   Unattempted Questions: 0 marks

This negative marking scheme is generally applicable to most courses, including B.Tech and B.Arch, so candidates should answer carefully.

Stay updated with the official KIITEE portal for any changes or specific deadlines for Phase 2 and 3 registrations and slot bookings.

Preparing thoroughly according to the specific syllabus for your chosen program, and understanding the exam pattern, will significantly enhance your chances of success in KIITEE 2026. Good luck with your preparation!
