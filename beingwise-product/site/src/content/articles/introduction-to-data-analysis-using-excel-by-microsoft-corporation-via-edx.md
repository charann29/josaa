# Introduction to Data Analysis using Excel by Microsoft Corporation via Edx: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding data is a crucial skill in today's world, and Microsoft Excel remains a powerful, widely used tool for this purpose. This introductory course, offered by Microsoft Corporation through the Edx platform, is designed to equip learners with fundamental data analysis techniques using Excel, making it accessible for anyone looking to build a strong foundation in this area.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn essential Excel functionalities like tables, pivot tables, and formulas for data analysis.
*   The core course content is available for free, allowing broad access to foundational skills.
*   A verified certificate from Microsoft Corporation is available for those who choose to pay a fee.
*   The course is self-paced, requiring approximately 2-4 hours of study per week.

## Course Structure and Learning Experience

This program is delivered entirely in English, utilizing a self-study format with both video and text-based materials. The flexibility of self-paced learning means you can fit your studies around your existing commitments, with an estimated weekly commitment of 2 to 4 hours. This makes it suitable for students, working professionals, or anyone with a busy schedule looking to upskill.

## Certification and Fees

The course itself provides access to all its learning materials without any charge. However, if you wish to receive an official, verified certificate upon completion, there is a fee of ₹8,307. This certificate is issued directly by Microsoft Corporation, adding a valuable credential to your professional profile, which can be useful for career advancement or showcasing your skills to potential employers.

## What You Will Learn: A Week-by-Week Breakdown

The curriculum is structured over four weeks, progressively building your Excel data analysis skills:

### Week 1: Mastering Excel Tables

This initial week focuses on understanding the power of Excel tables. You'll learn how tables offer significant advantages over simple data ranges, enabling more efficient filtering, sorting, and calculating totals. You'll also explore how to add calculated columns to your table data, enhancing your dataset's analytical capabilities.

### Week 2: Introduction to Pivot Tables and Dashboards

Moving into more advanced territory, Week 2 introduces you to the creation of your first pivot table. The course guides you through using multiple pivot tables and pivot charts to construct a basic data dashboard. You'll also learn how to connect various slicers to your pivot tables, making your dashboards interactive and dynamic.

### Week 3: Deep Dive into Pivot Table Functionality

This week expands on your pivot table knowledge, exploring their full potential. You'll discover various methods to filter data within pivots, allowing you to isolate and analyze specific subsets of your information. Furthermore, you'll learn to use calculated fields within pivot tables to compute metrics like profitability and identify data anomalies.

### Week 4: Formulas and Advanced Data Models

The final week presents an alternative to pivot tables by showing how formulas can be used for data aggregation, offering more flexible reporting layouts. You'll also be introduced to how pivot tables can draw data from multiple sources and get an initial understanding of the Excel data model, a concept explored in greater detail in subsequent courses within this series.

This course offers a practical and accessible pathway to developing essential data analysis skills, directly from the creators of Excel, Microsoft Corporation.
