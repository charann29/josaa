# CUET UG 2026: City Intimation Slip, Exam Dates, Admit Card, Syllabus, Expected Cutoff

The Common University Entrance Test (CUET) UG is a pivotal national-level examination for students aspiring to join various undergraduate programs across central and participating state/private universities in India. This guide provides essential information for the 2026 examination cycle, covering key dates, official announcements, and preparation insights.

## Key Takeaways

*   The CUET UG 2026 examination is scheduled to take place between May 11 and May 31, 2026, conducted in a computer-based test (CBT) format.
*   The National Testing Agency (NTA) will release the advance city intimation slip around April 30, 2026, informing candidates about their allotted exam city.
*   Admit cards will be available for download approximately 2-3 days before each candidate's specific exam date.
*   The examination is offered in 13 languages, covers 23 domain-specific subjects, and includes a General Test, allowing students to choose subjects relevant to their desired courses.

## CUET UG 2026 Exam Dates and Schedule

The CUET UG 2026 examinations are slated to be held from May 11 to May 31, 2026. While the detailed subject-wise schedule will be available in the official datesheet, candidates can also check their specific subject timings and slots once the city intimation slip is released. It's crucial for candidates to regularly check the NTA CUET website for the most up-to-date information.

## CUET UG City Intimation Slip 2026

The advance city intimation slip for CUET UG 2026 is expected to be released tentatively by April 30, 2026. NTA typically issues these slips in phases, approximately 10 days before the scheduled examinations. This preliminary document informs candidates of their allotted examination city, the specific dates for their chosen subjects, and slot timings. It is important to remember that this is *not* the admit card and is not required at the examination center.

### How to Download the CUET UG 2026 Advance Intimation Slip

To access your advance city slip, follow these steps:

1.  Visit the official NTA CUET website (cuet.nta.nic.in).
2.  Look for the link labeled 'CUET UG Advance City Slip 2026 Download' and click on it.
3.  Enter your CUET UG 2026 application number and password.
4.  Your intimation slip will appear on the screen as a PDF.
5.  Review all the details carefully.

### Details on the Intimation Slip

The CUET UG intimation slip will contain the following key information:

*   Your name
*   The CUET subjects you have applied for
*   Subject-wise examination dates
*   Subject-wise slot timings
*   The allotted examination city

## CUET UG Admit Card 2026

The CUET UG 2026 admit card will be issued by NTA approximately 2-3 days before each candidate's individual exam date. This is the mandatory document required for entry into the examination center. Candidates will need their CUET application number and password to download their hall ticket from the official NTA CUET website.

## CUET UG Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The CUET UG exam is conducted in a computer-based format. It comprises three sections: Language (Section IA & IB), Domain-Specific Subjects (Section II), and a General Test (Section III). Candidates can choose up to 10 subjects in total. The syllabus for domain subjects is generally based on the NCERT Class 12 curriculum. The General Test assesses general knowledge, current affairs, general mental ability, numerical ability, quantitative reasoning, and logical and analytical reasoning. The exam is administered in 13 languages.

## Expected Cutoff and Participating Universities

CUET UG scores are utilized by numerous accepting universities, including Delhi University, Banaras Hindu University, Allahabad University, Babasaheb Bhimrao Ambedkar University, and others, to prepare their respective cutoff lists, merit lists, and admission lists. The cutoff scores vary significantly each year based on factors like the number of applicants, exam difficulty, and the specific program and university. Candidates should refer to the individual university websites for their past year's cutoffs and admission criteria. The NIRF rankings can also provide an indication of a university's standing and competitiveness.
