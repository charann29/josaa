# All India Counselling for ST Candidates for Government Colleges: Required Marks for MDS

Securing a Master of Dental Surgery (MDS) seat in a government college is a significant goal for many aspiring dentists. For Scheduled Tribe (ST) candidates, understanding the specific qualifying marks and counselling process is crucial for admission through the All India quota.

## Key Takeaways

*   ST candidates generally require a lower qualifying score in NEET MDS compared to the general category.
*   The minimum qualifying percentile for ST/SC/OBC categories is typically around the 20th percentile.
*   This translates to approximately 168 marks out of a total of 960 in the NEET MDS examination.
*   Meeting this cutoff makes you eligible for counselling; final seat allotment depends on your rank, choices, and seat availability.

## Understanding NEET MDS Qualifying Marks for ST Candidates

For ST candidates seeking admission to government dental colleges via the All India 50% quota, the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) MDS is the gateway. The Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) oversees the counselling process for these seats. While the general category has a higher qualifying threshold, reserved categories like ST, SC, and Other Backward Classes (OBC) benefit from a reduced percentile requirement.

Historically, the initial qualifying percentile for ST/SC/OBC candidates was around the 40th percentile. However, to ensure that vacant seats are filled and to provide more opportunities, this cutoff has often been revised downwards. In recent years, this revised minimum qualifying percentile has been approximately 20.137 percentile.

## What Does 20.137 Percentile Mean in Marks?

Translating a percentile into a specific mark helps candidates understand the target score. For ST candidates, this approximately 20.137 percentile typically corresponds to a score of around 168 marks out of the total 960 marks in the NEET MDS exam. It's important to remember that these figures are based on recent trends and can vary slightly each year depending on the overall performance of candidates and official announcements from NTA (National Testing Agency) and MCC.

## Eligibility for All India Quota Counselling

Simply meeting the minimum qualifying marks is the first step. To be eligible to participate in the All India 50% quota counselling for MDS seats in government colleges, you must achieve a score equal to or above this category-specific cutoff. Once you meet this criterion, you can register for the counselling process conducted by the MCC.

During counselling, seats are allocated based on several factors, including your NEET MDS rank within your category, the preferences you submit for colleges and specialities, and the availability of seats under the ST reservation quota. The official MCC portal is the primary source for all counselling-related information, including schedules, seat matrices, and results.

## The Role of Revised Cutoffs

It is not uncommon for the qualifying cutoffs to be revised after the initial declaration. This usually happens if a significant number of seats remain vacant across various categories. Such revisions aim to ensure that eligible candidates have an opportunity to fill these seats, thereby maximizing admissions and reducing wastage of valuable academic positions. These revisions are officially announced by the MCC.

Achieving the required marks in NEET MDS is a crucial milestone for ST candidates aiming for government college MDS seats. Keep a close watch on official announcements from NTA and MCC for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding cutoffs and counselling schedules.
