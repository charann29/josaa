# Sir can we do cardiologist after bams

Many students considering a Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS) wonder about their options for further specialization. It's important to understand the pathways available after completing this traditional Indian medical degree.

## Key takeaways

*   A BAMS degree does not qualify you to practice as a modern allopathic cardiologist.
*   Specializations after BAMS are primarily within various branches of Ayurveda.
*   You cannot pursue postgraduate degrees in allopathic fields like cardiology, radiology, or orthopedics with a BAMS qualification.
*   Opportunities exist for further study in Ayurvedic MD programs, focusing on specific areas of traditional medicine.

## Understanding BAMS and Allopathy

BAMS is a comprehensive undergraduate program that trains students in the principles and practices of Ayurveda, an ancient system of medicine. It covers anatomy, physiology, pharmacology, and clinical methods from an Ayurvedic perspective. Allopathy, on the other hand, is the modern Western system of medicine, which includes specializations like cardiology.

These two systems operate on different foundational principles and diagnostic approaches. As a result, the training received in a BAMS program does not align with the requirements for practicing allopathic specialties such as cardiology, radiology, or anesthesiology. To become an allopathic cardiologist, one must complete an MBBS degree, followed by a Doctor of Medicine (MD) in General Medicine, and then a Doctor of Cardiovascular Medicine (DM) or equivalent specialization.

## Postgraduate options after BAMS

After completing your BAMS degree, you can pursue postgraduate studies, typically leading to an MD (Ayurveda) or MS (Ayurveda) degree. These programs allow you to specialize in various branches of Ayurvedic medicine. These specializations are distinct from allopathic medical fields.

Here are some of the common specializations available for BAMS graduates:

*   **Panchkarma:** Focuses on detoxification and rejuvenation therapies.
*   **Ayurvedic General Medicine (Kayachikitsa):** Deals with internal medicine in Ayurveda.
*   **Ayurvedic Pediatrics (Kaumarbhritya):** Specializes in child health and diseases from an Ayurvedic viewpoint.
*   **Ayurvedic Gynecology and Obstetrics (Prasuti Tantra Evum Stri Roga):** Covers women's health, pregnancy, and childbirth in Ayurveda.
*   **Rasshastra and Bhaisajyakalpana:** Involves Ayurvedic pharmacology and pharmaceutical sciences.
*   **Swasthvrutta:** Focuses on preventive and social medicine, including lifestyle and dietetics.
*   **Shalya Tantra:** Deals with Ayurvedic surgery and para-surgical procedures.
*   **Shalakya Tantra:** Specializes in diseases of the eyes, ears, nose, throat, and head in Ayurveda.
*   **Ayurvedic Pathology (Rog Nidan Evum Vikriti Vigyan):** Focuses on Ayurvedic diagnostics and pathology.

These postgraduate programs deepen your knowledge and clinical skills within the Ayurvedic framework, preparing you for specialized practice, teaching, or research in Ayurveda.

## Can BAMS doctors practice modern medicine?

While BAMS graduates are qualified to practice medicine within the Ayurvedic system, their scope of practice is defined by the regulations governing Ayurvedic medicine in India. They are not licensed to practice modern allopathic medicine, nor can they prescribe allopathic drugs without specific cross-practice training or governmental notifications that may allow limited integration in certain contexts. The Medical Council of India (MCI), now the National Medical Commission (NMC), regulates allopathic medical education and practice, while the National Commission for Indian System of Medicine (NCISM) oversees Ayurvedic education and practice.

## Career paths for BAMS graduates

BAMS graduates have a variety of career opportunities. They can establish private Ayurvedic clinics, work in government or private Ayurvedic hospitals, or join wellness centers. Other avenues include roles in the Ayurvedic pharmaceutical industry, academic positions in Ayurvedic colleges, or pursuing research in Ayurveda. Some also opt for health consulting, medical writing, or public health roles focusing on traditional medicine.

Understanding these distinctions is crucial for making informed decisions about your medical career path after BAMS.
