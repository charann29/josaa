# GUJCET Exam 2026: Answer Key (Out), Result Date, Cutoff, Merit List, Process

The Gujarat Common Entrance Test (GUJCET) is a crucial examination for students aspiring to pursue professional courses in engineering and pharmacy in colleges across Gujarat. This page outlines important details about the GUJCET 2026 process, from application to results and admission.

## Key Takeaways

*   The GUJCET 2026 application window closed on January 16, 2026, including a late fee option.
*   Applicants paid a standard fee of Rs. 350, with an additional Rs. 1000 for late submissions.
*   The application process involved online registration, fee payment, form filling, document uploads, and final submission.
*   A correction window was provided for applicants to amend details, either online or through physical submission.

## GUJCET 2026 Application Process

Applying for GUJCET 2026 required candidates to follow a structured online procedure. The initial registration without a late fee concluded on January 6, 2026, while the final deadline with a late fee was January 16, 2026. Aspiring students needed to ensure they met the stipulated eligibility criteria before proceeding with their applications.

Here's a breakdown of the steps involved:

1.  **Exam Registration:** Candidates first registered by providing essential personal details such as their name, mobile number, and email address. This step activated a unique login portal, which served as the primary access point for all subsequent application stages.
2.  **Fee Payment:** A non-refundable application fee of Rs. 350 was required. This could be paid online via the SBI Epay System, utilizing credit cards, debit cards, or net banking. An alternative option allowed payment through any SBI branch nationwide using the SBI Epay 'SBI Branch Payment' facility.
3.  **Filling the Application Form:** After fee payment, applicants proceeded to fill out the detailed application form, entering personal, academic, and contact information accurately.
4.  **Uploading Documents:** Scanned copies of a photograph and signature were required. These documents needed to adhere to specific size and format guidelines.

    | Document    | Size (KB)  | Format | Dimension (pixels) |
    | :---------- | :--------- | :----- | :----------------- |
    | Photograph  | 5 KB to 50 | JPG    | 120 X 120          |
    | Signature   | 5 KB to 50 | JPG    | 120 X 120          |

5.  **Final Submission:** The final step involved reviewing all entered information for accuracy before submitting the application. Upon successful submission, a unique application number was generated, which candidates should retain for future reference.

## Application Correction Facility

Recognizing the possibility of errors, the Gujarat Secondary & Higher Secondary Education Board (GSEB) provided a facility for applicants to correct details in their GUJCET 2026 application forms. This correction window was available online.

For more complex changes or specific requests, candidates also had the option to submit their correction requests physically. This could be done by mail or in person at the GUJCET cell in Gandhinagar, along with any necessary supporting documents.

## GUJCET 2026 Answer Key, Result, Cutoff, and Merit List (Expected)

While the application process has concluded, future stages of GUJCET 2026 will include the release of the official answer key, examination results, cutoff scores, and the final merit list. Candidates should regularly check the official GUJCET portal for updates on these crucial announcements. The answer key allows candidates to estimate their scores, while the result will formally announce individual performance. Cutoff scores will determine eligibility for various courses and institutions, leading to the preparation of a merit list for admissions counseling.

Stay tuned to the official GUJCET website for all upcoming announcements regarding the answer key, result declaration, cutoff marks, and the merit list, which are vital for the subsequent admission process.
