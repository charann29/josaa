# OPAT: Outpatient Parenteral Antimicrobial Therapy by British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy via Futurelearn: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Outpatient Parenteral Antimicrobial Therapy (OPAT) allows patients to receive intravenous antibiotic treatment at home or in an outpatient setting, rather than staying in a hospital. This course, offered by the British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy (BSAC) through Futurelearn, provides a comprehensive understanding of setting up and managing effective OPAT services.

## Key Takeaways

*   Learn about the foundational principles and benefits of OPAT for patient care.
*   Understand the roles of a multidisciplinary team in delivering OPAT services.
*   Gain insights into patient selection, clinical assessment, and creating robust OPAT plans.
*   Explore governance, antimicrobial stewardship, and monitoring best practices for OPAT.

## Course Structure and Learning Experience

This self-paced online course is delivered through a blend of video and text-based modules. It is designed to be flexible, requiring approximately six hours of study per week. The content is presented in clear, accessible English, making it suitable for a broad audience of healthcare professionals.

The course is divided into two weeks, each focusing on distinct aspects of OPAT:

### Week 1: What is OPAT?

This introductory week defines OPAT, highlights its significance in patient care, and explores the patient's perspective. You'll meet the core OPAT team members, including specialist nurses, antimicrobial pharmacists, and infectious disease physicians, and even gain insights into paediatric OPAT. A major focus is on patient selection, covering assessment techniques, quizzes, and clinical scenarios to help you identify suitable candidates for outpatient therapy.

### Week 2: Management of OPAT

The second week delves into the practical management and governance of OPAT services. Topics include good practice recommendations, an overview of clinical governance, and crucial antimicrobial considerations. You'll learn about the delicate balance between OPAT and antimicrobial stewardship, strategies for antimicrobial selection and administration, and important considerations for vascular devices. The week concludes with guidance on creating effective OPAT plans and monitoring patients.

## Fees and Certification

The course offers various access options. You can purchase the course for a one-off payment of Rs. 1,100. Alternatively, a yearly subscription is available for Rs. 4,450, which provides access to a range of courses. There is also a limited access option that is free of charge.

Upon successful completion, a certificate of achievement is available. This certificate is provided by Futurelearn, affirming your understanding of the course material.

## Expert Instructors

The course content is developed and delivered by experienced professionals in the field of infectious diseases:

*   **Mr. Andrew Seaton:** An Infectious Disease Physician from the University of Glasgow.
*   **Mr. Mark Gilchrist:** A Consultant from Imperial College, London.

Their combined expertise ensures that the course content is current, clinically relevant, and grounded in real-world practice.

This course offers a valuable opportunity for healthcare professionals to enhance their knowledge and skills in delivering high-quality outpatient antimicrobial therapy.
