# I'm an MBA 2nd Year Student. In Sem-3 I Received a KT in One Subject. After the Results, I Applied for Revaluation. However, Two Lists of Revaluation Results Have Been Released So Far and My Name Has Not Appeared in Either of Them. Now the KT Exam Forms Have Been Released. What Should I Do?

Navigating academic challenges can be stressful, especially when your revaluation results are delayed and KT exam forms are already out. It's crucial to make informed decisions to protect your academic progress.

## Key Takeaways

*   Always prioritize filling out the KT exam form as a precautionary measure.
*   Understand that if your revaluation is successful, your KT form will become redundant.
*   Proactively follow up with the university's examination department regarding your revaluation status.
*   Prepare for the KT exam as if you will be taking it, even while awaiting revaluation.

## Fill the KT Exam Form – A Safety Net

The most practical advice in your situation is to go ahead and fill out the KT (Keep Term) exam form. Think of this as an essential safety net. While you're hoping for a positive outcome from your revaluation, there's no guarantee of its timing or result. If you miss the deadline for the KT exam form, and your revaluation doesn't go your way, you could find yourself in a much more difficult position, potentially delaying your graduation.

## What Happens If Your Revaluation is Successful?

Should your revaluation result in a passing grade, the KT exam form you filled out will simply become unnecessary. The university's system will update your academic record, reflecting your cleared status, and you will not be required to appear for the KT exam. You won't face any penalties for having filled the form; it's purely a proactive step to ensure you have options.

## Proactive Follow-Up on Revaluation

While filling the KT form, don't stop actively pursuing your revaluation results. It's advisable to personally visit or contact the university's examination department or the relevant administrative office. Clearly explain your situation, mentioning that two lists have been released without your name. Inquire about the expected timeline for the next list or if there's a specific reason for the delay in your result. Having a clear understanding from official sources can provide peace of mind and help you plan better.

## Prepare for the KT Exam

Even with the revaluation pending, it's wise to start preparing for the KT exam. Treat it as if you will be sitting for it. This approach ensures that you're ready regardless of the revaluation outcome. If your revaluation is successful, the preparation won't be wasted; the knowledge gained will only strengthen your overall understanding of the subject. If you do end up taking the KT exam, you'll be well-prepared, significantly increasing your chances of clearing it.

Taking these steps will help you manage the uncertainty and ensure your academic journey stays on track.
