# SNAP Selection Process 2025: Check GE-PI Dates & Admission Procedure for SIU institutes

For aspiring MBA students eyeing a spot in the prestigious Symbiosis International University (SIU) institutes, understanding the SNAP selection process is crucial. This multi-stage procedure evaluates candidates not just on their entrance exam scores but also on their communication skills and overall personality.

## Key takeaways

*   The SNAP selection process for 2025 admissions includes your SNAP 2025 score, a Group Exercise (GE), and a Personal Interaction (PI).
*   Each SIU institute conducts its own selection rounds and sets individual cut-offs based on its unique admission criteria.
*   Candidates must have selected their preferred MBA programs and institutes within the SNAP application form itself.
*   The final merit list is a composite score, giving significant weight to both your SNAP score and your performance in the GE-PI rounds.

## SNAP Admission Process 2025

The admission journey to SIU MBA programs begins with the SNAP exam, followed by the Group Exercise (GE) and Personal Interaction (PI) rounds for shortlisted candidates. The SNAP 2025 results were announced on January 9, 2026, and institutes like SIBM Pune, SCMHRD, and SIIB have already released their PI shortlists for MBA programs.

Candidates are typically informed about their shortlisting for the GE-PI rounds via email. It's important to remember that if you are shortlisted for multiple programs, you will need to participate in the selection process for each program separately.

### What is Group Exercise (GE)?

The Group Exercise is designed to assess your ability to work in a team and articulate your thoughts effectively. Typically, candidates are divided into groups of 8-10 and given a case study to discuss. These cases often revolve around common business areas such as Marketing, Human Resources, Finance, or Operations. Groups are usually given about 20 minutes to discuss the case and present their collective solutions.

### What is Personal Interaction (PI)?

The Personal Interaction round is a one-on-one discussion with a panel of experts, which usually includes academics, faculty members, and industry professionals. Questions can range from your motivations for pursuing an MBA, your hobbies, future career aspirations, and current affairs. This round aims to evaluate your communication abilities, confidence, problem-solving skills, and overall suitability for a managerial role.

## Final Merit List and Parameters

The final selection of candidates is based on a comprehensive evaluation across all stages of the admission process. A composite score is calculated for each candidate, reflecting their performance in the SNAP exam, Group Exercise, and Personal Interaction. The official weightage for each component in preparing the final merit list is as follows:

| SNAP Selection Round | Weightage | 
| :------------------- | :-------- | 
| SNAP Score           | 50        | 
| Group Exercise (GE)  | 10        | 
| Personal Interaction (PI) | 40        | 
| **Total**            | **100**   |

*Note: The SNAP score, originally out of 60, is scaled down to 50 marks for this calculation.*

## Institute-Wise SNAP Cut-Offs

Each SIU institute independently determines its cut-off scores for shortlisting candidates for the GE-PI rounds. These cut-offs can vary each year based on factors such as the number of applicants, the difficulty of the SNAP exam, and the institute's specific admission requirements. After the GE-PI rounds are concluded, the respective SIU institutes will display their category-wise cut-offs for both merit and wait lists on their official websites.

Navigating the SNAP selection process requires careful preparation and an understanding of what each stage demands. Good luck with your application!
