# List of University Entrance Exam: Dates, Results, Cut-Offs, Merit List, Colleges and Universities

Navigating higher education in India involves understanding a wide array of entrance examinations. These tests are crucial gateways to undergraduate and postgraduate programs across various central, state, and private universities.

## Key takeaways

*   **Eligibility is fundamental:** Most undergraduate programs require Class 12 completion, while postgraduate courses demand a bachelor's degree from a recognized institution.
*   **Diverse opportunities:** Entrance exams open doors to a vast network of universities, including top central institutions like Delhi University, Jawaharlal Nehru University, and Banaras Hindu University.
*   **Merit-based admissions:** University admissions consider entrance exam scores, academic merit, seat availability, and reservation policies.
*   **Official sources are key:** Always refer to official portals for accurate dates, results, cut-offs, and merit lists.

## Common University Entrance Test (CUET) UG and PG

The Common University Entrance Test (CUET) is a pivotal examination for admissions across India. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), CUET streamlines the admission process for numerous central, state, and private universities.

### CUET UG

For students aspiring to undergraduate programs like B.A., B.Sc., B.Com., and B.C.A., qualifying CUET UG is often a prerequisite. This exam facilitates entry into prestigious central universities such as Delhi University, Jawaharlal Nehru University, Banaras Hindu University, Jamia Millia Islamia, and the University of Hyderabad. After results are declared, participating universities conduct their individual counselling processes based on CUET scores.

### CUET PG

Similarly, NTA administers CUET PG for admissions to a wide range of postgraduate programs. This examination serves as a common gateway to around 203 universities nationwide, simplifying the application process for master's degrees.

## Joint Admission Test for M.Sc. (IIT JAM)

IIT JAM is a national-level entrance examination for those seeking admission to postgraduate science programs at the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and integrated Ph.D. programs at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Bangalore. Conducted by the IITs, JAM offers pathways to programs like M.Sc. (Two Years), Joint M.Sc.-Ph.D., M.Sc.-Ph.D. Dual Degree, and M.Sc.-M.Tech. Each year, over 3000 seats are available across 90 postgraduate programs at 22 IITs, in addition to opportunities at NITs and IISc Bangalore.

## ICAR AIEEA PG

For students interested in postgraduate and research courses within agricultural sciences, the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) All India Entrance Examination for Admission (AIEEA) is essential. Conducted by NTA, this exam provides admission opportunities to 75 agricultural universities across the country. Admission is granted based on the marks obtained in the ICAR AIEEA PG exam.

## National Entrance Screening Test (NEST)

NEST is specifically designed for admission to integrated M.Sc. programs at the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER Bhubaneswar) and the UM DAE Centre for Excellence in Basic Sciences (CEBS) Mumbai. To be eligible, candidates must have passed Class XII from a recognized Indian board with a minimum of 60% aggregate marks (55% for SC, ST, and Divyangjan candidates) and successfully cleared the NEST exam.

## Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University Common Entrance Test (IPU CET)

GGSIPU conducts its own entrance examination, IPU CET, for admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate programs offered by the university and its affiliated colleges. This test is crucial for students targeting programs within this specific university system.

Always consult the official websites of NTA, JoSAA, or individual universities for the most current information regarding examination dates, result announcements, cut-off scores, and merit lists.
