# CUET PG 2026: Admit card (OUT), City Slip, Analysis, Exam Date & Question Papers

For students aspiring to pursue postgraduate studies in India, the Common University Entrance Test (CUET PG) is a crucial examination. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), this test opens doors to various Master's programs across numerous participating universities.

## Key takeaways

*   The CUET PG 2026 examination was conducted as a Computer-Based Test (CBT) across the nation for 157 different subjects.
*   NTA will soon release the provisional answer key and individual candidate response sheets on its official portal.
*   Candidates will have an opportunity to challenge the provisional answer key by paying a non-refundable fee for each objection.
*   The final answer key and CUET PG 2026 results will be declared after reviewing all valid challenges.

## Understanding the CUET PG Exam

The CUET PG is a national-level entrance examination designed to streamline admissions into various postgraduate courses. These include popular programs such as MSc, MBA, MA, M.Tech, MCA, and M.Com, among others. The exam is conducted online, ensuring a standardized and efficient testing environment for all candidates.

## CUET PG 2026 Answer Key and Response Sheet

The National Testing Agency (NTA) is expected to release the provisional answer key for CUET PG 2026 shortly. This key, along with individual candidate response sheets, will be accessible through the candidate login on the official CUET PG website. It's important to note that these will not be publicly available; only those who appeared for the exam will be able to view them.

### How to Access Your Answer Key and Response Sheet

To check and download your CUET PG answer key and response sheet, follow these steps:

1.  Visit the official CUET PG NTA website.
2.  Log in using your application number and password.
3.  Navigate to the section for response sheets and answer keys for your respective subjects.

### Challenging the Provisional Answer Key

If candidates identify any discrepancies in the provisional answer key, they can submit objections. Here's how:

1.  Go to the CUET PG portal and click on the 'View/Challenge Answer Key' button.
2.  Select the checkbox next to the option ID you wish to challenge.
3.  Upload supporting documents in PDF format to substantiate your claim.
4.  After selecting the challenged Option IDs, click 'Submit and Review Claims'.
5.  Review your submitted claims and click 'Save Claim and Pay Fee'.
6.  A non-refundable fee of INR 200 per question challenged must be paid online via credit card, debit card, net banking, or UPI.

NTA will review all submitted objections before preparing and releasing the final answer key, which will then be used to calculate the candidates' scores and declare the results.
