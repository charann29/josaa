# Question: Two numbers are in the ratio of 2: 3. If their sum is 125, find the numbers.

This common type of question tests your understanding of ratios and basic algebra. It's a fundamental concept often seen in various competitive exams and entrance tests across India. Let's break down how to solve it effectively.

## Key takeaways

*   Ratios represent a relationship between two or more quantities.
*   You can use a common multiplier (like 'x') to convert a ratio into actual values.
*   Setting up a simple algebraic equation is key to finding the unknown multiplier.
*   Always double-check your answer by plugging the numbers back into the original problem statement.

## Understanding Ratios

A ratio of 2:3 means that for every 2 units of the first number, there are 3 units of the second number. It's a way to express proportions. While the ratio tells us the relationship, it doesn't immediately tell us the actual values of the numbers themselves. For example, 2 and 3 have a ratio of 2:3, but so do 20 and 30, or 50 and 75.

## Setting up the Equation

To find the actual numbers, we introduce a common multiplier. Let's say this multiplier is 'x'.

If the numbers are in the ratio of 2:3, we can represent them as:

*   First number = 2x
*   Second number = 3x

The problem states that their sum is 125. This allows us to form an equation:

2x + 3x = 125

## Solving for 'x'

Now, we combine the 'x' terms:

5x = 125

To find 'x', we divide 125 by 5:

x = 125 / 5
x = 25

This value of 'x' is our common multiplier.

## Finding the Numbers

With 'x' now known, we can find the actual numbers:

*   First number = 2x = 2 * 25 = 50
*   Second number = 3x = 3 * 25 = 75

So, the two numbers are 50 and 75.

## Verifying the Answer

It's always good practice to verify your solution. Do these numbers meet the conditions of the problem?

*   **Ratio:** Is 50:75 equivalent to 2:3? Yes, if you divide both by 25, you get 2:3.
*   **Sum:** Is 50 + 75 equal to 125? Yes, 50 + 75 = 125.

Both conditions are met, confirming our answer.

This step-by-step approach ensures accuracy and builds confidence for similar problems you might encounter in exams like JEE Main, NEET, or even various state-level entrance tests.
