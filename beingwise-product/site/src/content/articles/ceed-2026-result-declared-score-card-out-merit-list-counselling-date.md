# CEED 2026: Result (DECLARED), Score Card (OUT), Merit List, Counselling Date & Admission Process

The Common Entrance Examination for Design (CEED) is a crucial national-level entrance exam for students aspiring to pursue Master of Design (M.Des) programs at various Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and other prestigious design institutes. Conducted by IIT Bombay, CEED assesses a candidate's design aptitude and creative skills.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Results Declared:** The CEED 2026 results were officially released on March 4, 2026, accessible via the candidate login portal.
*   **Scorecard Availability:** Scorecards became available for download from March 10, 2026, and will remain valid until July 31, 2026. No physical copies are dispatched.
*   **New Participating Institute:** IIT Gandhinagar has joined the list of CEED participating institutes, offering an M.Des in Integrated Design & Technology.
*   **Two-Part Evaluation:** Part A marks are shown to all candidates, while Part B marks and ranks are only displayed for those who qualified in Part A.

## CEED 2026 Result and Scorecard

The CEED 2026 results were announced on March 4, 2026. Candidates can view their results by logging in with their credentials on the official CEED portal. Following the result declaration, the CEED 2026 scorecards were made available for download from March 10, 2026. These scorecards are essential for the admission process and will be valid for one year, until July 31, 2026. It's important to note that only digital scorecards are provided; no hard copies are sent to candidates.

For CEED 2026, all candidates who appeared for the examination will see their Part A marks. However, Part B marks are only displayed for candidates who have been shortlisted based on their Part A performance. All shortlisted candidates receive a merit rank.

## Understanding CEED Cutoff

The cutoff for CEED Part A 2026 was released on February 5, 2026, on the official CEED website. This cutoff is presented in terms of mean and standard deviation, which are used to calculate the qualifying marks for Part A. For CEED 2026, the mean for Part A was 39.35, with a standard deviation of 16.89. Meeting or exceeding this cutoff is crucial for candidates to have their Part B evaluated and to be considered for a rank.

## CEED Admission Process and Counselling

After the CEED results are declared and scorecards are issued, the admission process begins. Candidates with a valid CEED merit rank must apply directly to their desired participating institutes. These institutes, which include IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Guwahati, IIT Hyderabad, IIT Indore, IIT Roorkee, IIITDM Jabalpur, and the newly added IIT Gandhinagar, will then shortlist candidates based on their CEED scores and ranks. Institutes may conduct further rounds, such as interviews or portfolio reviews, before finalizing their merit lists for M.Des admissions.

There is no centralized CEED counselling process. Each participating and result-sharing institute manages its own admission procedures independently. It is vital for candidates to regularly check the official websites of their preferred institutes for specific application deadlines, counselling schedules, and detailed admission criteria.

## CEED 2026 Exam Overview

CEED is a single-session, three-hour examination conducted once a year by IIT Bombay. The exam comprises two mandatory parts: Part A and Part B, both conducted offline on the same day. Part A is a computer-based test that evaluates general aptitude, while Part B assesses design and drawing skills. Part B is only evaluated if a candidate qualifies in Part A. The exam is administered in English, and applications are submitted online.
