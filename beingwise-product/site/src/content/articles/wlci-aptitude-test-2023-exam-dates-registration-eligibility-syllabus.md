# WLCI Aptitude Test 2023 Exam: Dates, Registration, Eligibility, Syllabus, Results

For students considering design, media, or advertising programs, the WLCI Aptitude Test was historically a key admission pathway to WLCI's specialized schools. This page provides details about its past structure and important information for those interested in WLCI programs.

## Key takeaways

*   The WLCI Aptitude Test has been discontinued; it is no longer conducted for admissions.
*   Historically, the test offered entry into undergraduate and postgraduate programs in fashion, advertising, graphic design, and media.
*   The application process for the test usually started in August and continued until December.
*   Admit cards, results, and counseling schedules were exclusively available on the official WLCI website.

## What the WLCI Aptitude Test Was

Established in 1996, WLCI previously administered the WLCI Aptitude Test for admissions to its School of Fashion, School of Advertising & Graphic Design, and School of Media & Mass Communication. Unlike many fixed-date examinations, candidates could take this test at their convenience. The online registration for the admission process typically opened in August and concluded in December.

This aptitude test served as the gateway to various fashion technology, graphic design, and media courses offered across WLCI campuses located in cities such as Bangalore, Delhi, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kathmandu, Greater Noida, Mumbai, Pune, Kolkata, and Surat.

## Eligibility Criteria for WLCI Aptitude Test (Historical)

The eligibility requirements for the WLCI Aptitude Test varied for undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) courses. Below are the past criteria for UG programs:

### Eligibility Criteria for UG Courses

To be eligible for undergraduate programs through the WLCI Aptitude Test, candidates typically needed to meet one of the following conditions:

*   Successful completion of Class 12 examinations under the 10+2 system from any recognized Central or State Board of Secondary Education (e.g., CBSE, State Boards, CISCE, New Delhi).
*   Possession of a General Certificate Education (GCE) Examination (London/Cambridge/Sri Lanka) at the Advanced (A) level or an International Baccalaureate (IB) qualification.
*   Passing any Public School/Board/University Examination in India or abroad recognized by the Association of Indian Universities as equivalent to the 10+2 system.
*   A pass grade in the Senior Secondary School Examination conducted by the National Open School, with a minimum of five subjects.
*   A 3 or 4-year Diploma recognized by AICTE or a State Board of Technical Education.

**Note:** Students appearing for their qualifying examination were also eligible to apply, provided they could present the necessary completion documents or marks at the time of counseling or final admissions.

## Important Information Regarding Discontinuation

It is crucial for prospective students to understand that the WLCI Aptitude Test is no longer conducted. Admissions to WLCI's various schools are now managed through alternative processes. Students interested in WLCI programs should directly consult the official WLCI website for the most current admission procedures, eligibility criteria, and application details.

For the latest information on admissions to WLCI's design, advertising, and media programs, please refer to the official WLCI website.
