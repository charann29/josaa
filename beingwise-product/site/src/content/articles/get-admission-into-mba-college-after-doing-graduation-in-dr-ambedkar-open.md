# Can I get admission into MBA college after doing graduation in Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Open University at Telangana state? I want to do MBA in IIT Bombay... can it be possible to do so?

It's a great ambition to pursue an MBA, especially from a prestigious institution like IIT Bombay. Let's break down the possibilities and requirements for your journey.

## Key Takeaways

*   A three-year bachelor's degree alone is typically not enough for IIT Bombay's MBA program.
*   You'll likely need a two-year master's degree after your three-year undergraduate degree to meet the eligibility criteria.
*   The university you graduate from (like Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Open University) needs to be UGC-recognized.
*   Meeting academic percentage requirements and performing well in the CAT exam are crucial.

## Understanding IIT Bombay MBA Eligibility

IIT Bombay's Shailesh J. Mehta School of Management (SJMSOM) has specific requirements for its MBA program. Generally, candidates need either a four-year bachelor's degree or a master's degree from a recognized university. The minimum percentage required is usually 60% (or a CPI/CGPA of 6.5 out of 10). There's a relaxation of 5% for candidates from SC/ST/PwD categories, meaning they would need 55% (or a CPI/CGPA of 6.0 out of 10). This information is consistently available on the official SJMSOM IIT Bombay website.

## Your Three-Year Degree and the Master's Path

Since your graduation from Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Open University is likely a three-year bachelor's degree, it typically won't meet the primary four-year degree requirement for IIT Bombay's MBA. However, this doesn't close the door entirely. If you have a three-year undergraduate degree, you would then need to complete a two-year master's degree from a recognized university. This master's degree, combined with your bachelor's, would then make you eligible to apply, provided you also meet the required aggregate percentages at the postgraduate level.

## The Role of University Recognition

Crucially, for your degrees to be considered valid for admission to top institutions like IIT Bombay, the university from which you pursue your graduation and any subsequent postgraduate studies must be recognized. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Open University is a state open university recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC). As long as the university providing your degrees is UGC-recognized, whether it's an open university or a traditional one, your qualifications should generally be accepted for eligibility purposes by other recognized institutions.

## The CAT Exam and Selection Process

Beyond academic eligibility, admission to IIT Bombay's MBA program is highly competitive and primarily based on your performance in the Common Admission Test (CAT). After clearing the CAT with a high percentile, you would typically go through a selection process that includes a Written Ability Test (WAT) and a Personal Interview (PI). Your academic profile, work experience (if any), and performance in these stages all contribute to the final admission decision.

## Planning Your Path Forward

Given your goal, a clear path would involve: first, confirming the exact duration and recognition of your current degree; second, pursuing a two-year master's degree from a UGC-recognized institution if your bachelor's is three years; and third, preparing rigorously for the CAT exam. Always refer to the official SJMSOM IIT Bombay website for the most up-to-date and precise eligibility criteria before making any decisions.

With careful planning and dedication, achieving your dream of an MBA from IIT Bombay is definitely within reach.
