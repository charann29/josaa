It's understandable to wonder about your prospects for top B-schools with a strong profile. Let's break down your chances for IIM Lucknow and FMS Delhi based on your CAT 2025 raw score, academic background, and work experience.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your expected CAT percentile is likely to be in the 96-97 range, which is very competitive.
*   You comfortably clear the sectional cut-offs for both IIM Lucknow and FMS Delhi.
*   Your strong academics and work experience significantly boost your IIM Lucknow chances.
*   FMS Delhi is a bit more competitive due to its high CAT score emphasis, placing you on the borderline.

## Understanding Your CAT Score and Percentile

A raw score of 84 in CAT 2025, with sectional scores of VARC 34, LRDI 24, and QA 26, is excellent. Based on previous years' trends and paper difficulty, this score typically translates to an overall percentile in the 96-97 range. It's crucial to note that the final percentile depends on the normalization process by NTA, but this range is a strong estimate. Importantly, your sectional scores indicate that you have cleared the minimum requirements for each section, which is a common hurdle for many candidates.

## IIM Lucknow: Strong Chances

IIM Lucknow is a highly sought-after institute, consistently ranked among the top B-schools in India according to NIRF rankings. For the EWS category, the overall CAT percentile cut-off generally hovers around 93-95 percentile, with sectional cut-offs in the 70-75 percentile range. Your expected percentile is above this threshold, placing you in a very favorable position.

Beyond your CAT score, IIM Lucknow considers a holistic profile. Your academic record is outstanding:

*   Class X: 95%
*   Class XII: 94.5%
*   B.Tech: 80.01%

These scores demonstrate consistent academic excellence, which IIM Lucknow values highly. Furthermore, your two years of work experience will be a significant advantage. IIM Lucknow's shortlisting criteria often assign weight to both academic performance and relevant work experience, further enhancing your composite score for interview calls. Given these factors, your probability of receiving an IIM Lucknow interview call is quite good, assuming your final CAT percentile aligns with our estimate.

## FMS Delhi: Competitive but Possible

Faculty of Management Studies (FMS), University of Delhi, is renowned for its excellent return on investment and very high CAT cut-offs. FMS places a very strong emphasis on the CAT score for shortlisting, often making it almost entirely CAT-based. For the EWS category, the overall CAT percentile cut-off typically falls in the 97-98 percentile range. The institute also tends to give significant weight to the VARC section.

Your VARC score of 34 is a positive point, aligning with FMS's preference for strong verbal abilities. However, with an expected overall percentile of 96-97, you are right at the borderline for FMS Delhi. This means that while an interview call is not guaranteed, it is definitely within the realm of possibility. A slight dip in the EWS cut-off for the year, or a favorable final percentile calculation, could work in your favor. Your chances are moderate to borderline, but certainly not out of the question.

## Next Steps: Prepare for WAT-PI

Regardless of the specific institute, if you receive an interview call, the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) stages are critical. It is highly advisable to start preparing for these now. Focus on articulating your work experience, discussing your career goals, and demonstrating your knowledge of current affairs. Your strong academic and professional background provides excellent material for these rounds.

Your profile is very strong, making you a competitive candidate for IIM Lucknow and a plausible one for FMS Delhi. Good luck with your applications and preparations!
