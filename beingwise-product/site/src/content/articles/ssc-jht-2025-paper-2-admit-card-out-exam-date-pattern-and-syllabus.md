# SSC JHT 2025: Paper 2 Admit Card OUT, Exam Date, Pattern & Syllabus

The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) conducts a competitive examination to recruit Junior Hindi Translators (JHT) and Senior Hindi Translators for various government ministries, departments, and organizations across India. This guide provides essential information for candidates aspiring to join these prestigious roles.

## Key Takeaways

*   The Staff Selection Commission has released the admit cards for SSC JHT Paper 2. Candidates can now check their examination city details.
*   Paper 2 of the SSC JHT exam was conducted on December 14, 2025.
*   There are a total of 552 vacancies announced for the current recruitment cycle.
*   Candidates requiring scribe facilities must register for it, which involves Aadhaar authentication.

## Understanding the SSC JHT Examination

The SSC JHT examination is a national-level test designed to select skilled Hindi translators. The recruitment process is thorough, ensuring that only the most capable individuals are chosen for these important linguistic roles within the government. Posts include Junior Translator in the Central Secretariat Official Language Service (CSOLS), with a salary scale at Level-6 (INR 35,400-1,12,400).

## SSC JHT 2025 Important Dates and Application Process

The official notification for SSC JHT 2025 was released on June 5, 2025. The application window opened on the same day and closed on June 26, 2025. The application process is entirely online, consisting of a two-part system: Part I for one-time registration and Part II for filling the detailed application form. The application fee is INR 100, though women, Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Ex-Servicemen (ESM) candidates are exempt from this fee.

## SSC JHT Selection Process: A Step-by-Step Guide

The selection for SSC JHT involves several stages to ensure a comprehensive evaluation of candidates:

### 1. Application Submission

Candidates first complete the online application form as detailed above, ensuring all information is accurate and submitted before the deadline.

### 2. Admit Card Release

Admit cards are issued separately for Paper 1 and Paper 2, typically about two weeks before each examination. These are made available on the respective regional websites of the Staff Selection Commission. Candidates need to enter their login credentials to download their admit cards, which contain crucial details like exam center address.

### 3. Examination Stages (Paper 1 & Paper 2)

The examination is conducted in two stages:

*   **Paper 1:** This initial paper serves as a screening test. Candidates are shortlisted for Paper 2 based on their performance and category-wise cut-offs.
*   **Paper 2:** This is the main examination, assessing the translation and writing skills of the candidates.

### 4. Document Verification

After clearing both Paper 1 and Paper 2, shortlisted candidates proceed to the document verification stage. This involves verifying all academic qualifications, caste certificates, and other relevant documents against the submitted application.

### 5. Final Merit List and Allocation

The final selection is based on the combined performance in Paper 1 and Paper 2, alongside the preferences indicated by candidates. A merit-cum-preference system determines the final allocation to various government departments and ministries.

## SSC JHT Exam Pattern and Syllabus Overview

The SSC JHT examination evaluates candidates on their proficiency in Hindi and English, particularly their translation abilities. While specific details for 2025 are available in the official notification, typically the exam includes sections on general Hindi, general English, and translation tasks. Paper 2, in particular, focuses heavily on translation from English to Hindi and Hindi to English, along with essay writing in both languages.

## Vacancies and Results

For the current recruitment cycle, a total of 552 vacancies have been announced. The final marks for SSC JHT 2024 were released on August 27, 2025, with 272 candidates selected for final appointment. Results are published in PDF format on the official Staff Selection Commission website, listing the roll numbers of qualified candidates.

Aspiring candidates are encouraged to regularly check the official Staff Selection Commission website for the latest updates and detailed information regarding the examination process.
