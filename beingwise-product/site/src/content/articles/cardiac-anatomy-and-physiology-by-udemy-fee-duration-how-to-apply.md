# Cardiac Anatomy & Physiology by Udemy: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

Understanding the human heart is fundamental for anyone pursuing a career in healthcare, from aspiring doctors to nurses and paramedics. This course offers a comprehensive look at the heart's structure and function, providing essential knowledge in an accessible format.

## Key Takeaways

*   **In-Depth Coverage:** The program explores both the structural (anatomy) and functional (physiology) aspects of the adult human heart in detail.
*   **Self-Paced Learning:** Students can progress through the material at their own speed, making it suitable for busy schedules.
*   **Foundational Knowledge:** It lays a strong groundwork for further studies in cardiology or other medical fields.
*   **Practical Insights:** Topics range from basic features to complex electrical activities and contraction mechanisms.

## What the Course Covers

This extensive course is structured to guide learners through every crucial aspect of cardiac science. It begins with the fundamental characteristics of the adult human heart, including its external and internal features, and the intricate network of cardiac arteries and veins that supply it. You'll also delve into the vital role of cardiac valves and the autonomic nervous system's influence on heart function.

The curriculum then progresses to the heart's electrical system, covering the conduction pathways, the histology of cardiac muscle, and the hormones that affect its performance. A significant portion is dedicated to the electrical basis of action potentials, how signals are generated in pacemakers and muscle cells, and their propagation throughout the heart. Understanding excitation-contraction coupling and the mechanisms of cardiac contraction are also key components.

Finally, the course examines advanced physiological concepts such as the autonomic control of heart rate and contraction, the interpretation of electrocardiograms (ECG), and the mean electrical axis. It concludes with an exploration of the cardiac cycle, various cardiac metrics and parameters, and the crucial force-length, force-velocity, and pressure-volume relationships that define heart function.

## Who Is This Course For?

This program is ideal for a wide range of individuals. Medical students, nursing students, and those in allied health professions will find it an invaluable resource for solidifying their understanding of cardiology. It's also well-suited for professionals looking for a refresher, or anyone with a keen interest in human biology and the complexities of the cardiovascular system. No prior deep medical knowledge is strictly required, though a basic understanding of biology would be beneficial.
