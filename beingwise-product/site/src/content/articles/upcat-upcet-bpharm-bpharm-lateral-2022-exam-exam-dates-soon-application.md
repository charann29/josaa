# UPCAT (UPCET) BPharm/BPharm (Lateral) 2022 Exam: Exam Dates (Soon), Application Form (Extended), Eligibility, Syllabus, Results

For students aspiring to pursue BPharm or BPharm (Lateral Entry) in Uttar Pradesh, the Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test (UPCET), previously known as UPSEE, was a key state-level examination. However, for 2022 admissions, significant changes were introduced regarding how admissions to these courses are handled across state universities like Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU) and Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT).

## Key Takeaways

*   **Exam Replaced:** The UPCET exam for BPharm and BPharm (Lateral) was discontinued for 2022 admissions. 
*   **New Admission Pathways:** Admissions are now primarily through national-level entrance exams such as CUET (Common University Entrance Test) and JEE Main.
*   **NTA's Role:** The National Testing Agency (NTA) was previously the conducting body for UPCET and continues to conduct CUET and JEE Main.
*   **Eligibility Remains Key:** While the exam changed, the fundamental eligibility criteria for BPharm/BPharm (Lateral) remained crucial for admission through the new pathways.

## Understanding the Shift from UPCET

Prior to 2022, UPCET served as the common entrance test for various undergraduate and postgraduate programs, including BPharm and BPharm (Lateral), offered by institutions affiliated with AKTU, MMMUT, and HBTU Kanpur. The National Testing Agency (NTA) was responsible for conducting this computer-based test. However, for the 2022 academic session, the Uttar Pradesh government decided to scrap UPCET and instead utilize scores from other national-level examinations for admission to affiliated colleges.

For BPharm programs, admissions are now primarily routed through the CUET (UG) exam. For some technical courses, JEE Main scores are also considered. Similarly, postgraduate courses like MPharm are now covered under CUET (PG).

## Eligibility Criteria for BPharm/BPharm (Lateral Entry)

Even with the change in the entrance exam, the underlying eligibility requirements set by the state authorities and universities remain vital. While specific details would be outlined in the information brochure for CUET or the respective university, general criteria typically include:

*   **Academic Qualification:** Candidates must have passed their 10+2 examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology as compulsory subjects from a recognized board.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A certain minimum percentage (often 45-50% for general category, with relaxations for reserved categories) in the qualifying examination is usually required.
*   **Age Limit:** Generally, there is no upper age limit for appearing in these entrance examinations or for BPharm admissions.
*   **Domicile:** Candidates either need to have passed their qualifying examination from an institution in Uttar Pradesh, or their parents must hold a domicile certificate of Uttar Pradesh. Those not meeting the domicile requirement can still be eligible under certain conditions.

For BPharm (Lateral Entry), candidates typically need to have completed a Diploma in Pharmacy from an institution approved by the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI).

## Application Process and Important Dates

Since UPCET was replaced by CUET for BPharm admissions, the application process and important dates followed the CUET schedule. Students had to apply for CUET (UG) through the official NTA CUET portal. The application period for CUET (UG) 2022 was extended, allowing candidates ample time to register. Key dates for CUET (UG) 2022 generally included:

*   **Application Period:** Typically spanning several weeks in April-May.
*   **Correction Window:** A short window for correcting application details.
*   **Admit Card Release:** Issued a few days before the exam.
*   **Examination Dates:** Conducted over multiple phases, usually in July-August.
*   **Result Declaration:** Announced a few weeks after the exams.

Prospective students should always refer to the official NTA CUET website for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding application forms and deadlines.

## Syllabus and Exam Pattern

With the transition to CUET (UG) for BPharm admissions, the syllabus and exam pattern align with the CUET (UG) structure. The CUET (UG) exam typically consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) and is conducted in a computer-based test (CBT) mode. For BPharm, candidates usually need to attempt specific domain subjects relevant to pharmacy, such as Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics, along with a General Test and Language sections depending on the university's requirements. Each correct answer carries specific marks, and there might be negative marking for incorrect responses.

Students preparing for BPharm admissions through CUET (UG) should consult the detailed syllabus provided on the official NTA CUET website to understand the topics covered in each domain subject.

## Results and Counselling

After the CUET (UG) examination, NTA declares the results on its official portal. Based on these scores, universities like AKTU and MMMUT conduct their respective counselling processes for BPharm admissions. This counselling typically involves online registration, choice filling of colleges and courses, seat allotment, and document verification. Reservation policies, as per state government norms, are applied during the counselling process. Candidates must stay informed by checking the official websites of the respective universities for counselling schedules and procedures.

Navigating higher education admissions can be complex, but staying informed through official channels ensures a smooth application journey.
