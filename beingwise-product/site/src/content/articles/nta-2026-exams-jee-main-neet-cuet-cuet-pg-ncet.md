# NTA 2026 Exams: JEE Main, NEET, CUET, CUET PG, NCET

For Indian students aiming for higher education, the National Testing Agency (NTA) conducts several crucial entrance examinations. Understanding the calendar and requirements for these exams is vital for effective preparation.

## Key takeaways

*   The NTA manages major entrance exams like JEE Main, NEET UG, CUET UG, CUET PG, and NCET, among others.
*   Application dates for JEE Main 2026 Session 1 are set for November 2025, with exams in January 2026.
*   Eligibility criteria vary significantly for each exam, covering academic qualifications, age limits, and minimum marks.
*   Official information, including detailed brochures and the full exam calendar, will be released progressively by the NTA.

## What is the National Testing Agency (NTA)?

Established in 2018, the NTA is the primary body responsible for conducting various national-level entrance examinations in India. Its mandate includes releasing official notifications, defining eligibility criteria, managing application procedures, and providing resources like mock tests. The NTA ensures a standardized and fair testing process for admissions to undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across the country.

## NTA 2026 Exam Schedule and Applications

While the complete NTA exam calendar for 2026 is still awaited, some key dates for JEE Main 2026 Session 1 have been announced. Prospective candidates should note that applications for JEE Main 2026 Session 1 are expected to close by November 27, 2025. The examinations for this session are scheduled to take place from January 21 to January 30, 2026. Details for other examinations, including NEET UG, CUET UG, CUET PG, and NCET, will be released by the NTA on its official portal.

Each examination will have its own detailed information brochure, which will outline the application process, exam pattern, syllabus, and other important guidelines. Students are advised to refer to these individual brochures once they are released.

## Eligibility Criteria for NTA Exams 2026

Eligibility requirements differ significantly for each NTA-conducted examination. Below is a summary of the key criteria for some major exams:

### JEE Main 2026 Eligibility

*   Candidates must have passed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in either 2025 or 2026, or be appearing for their qualifying examination in 2026.
*   There is no upper age limit for applying to JEE Main.

### NEET UG 2026 Eligibility

*   Applicants need to have passed Class 10+2 or an equivalent examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
*   Minimum qualifying marks in Class 12 are generally 50% for the General category, 40% for SC/ST/OBC categories, and 40% for Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD).
*   Candidates must be at least 17 years old by December 31, 2026, to be eligible for admission.

### CUET UG Eligibility Criteria

*   Candidates should have passed Class 12 with a minimum aggregate score of 50%. (Note: Some university-specific criteria may apply).
*   There is no upper age limit for appearing in the CUET UG examination.

### CUET PG Eligibility Criteria

*   Applicants must hold a bachelor's degree or its equivalent from a recognized college or university.
*   The NTA has not set any age restrictions for candidates applying for CUET PG.

### NCET Eligibility Criteria

*   Currently, there is no maximum age limit specified for candidates applying for the NCET examination.

## Preparing for NTA Exams

The NTA often provides mock tests on its official website, which can be invaluable for candidates to familiarize themselves with the exam pattern, duration, and type of questions. Keeping track of official announcements for admit cards, answer keys, and results is also crucial throughout the examination cycle. Regular updates will be available on the NTA's official portal.

Staying informed and prepared by consulting official NTA resources will be key to your success in the 2026 entrance examinations.
