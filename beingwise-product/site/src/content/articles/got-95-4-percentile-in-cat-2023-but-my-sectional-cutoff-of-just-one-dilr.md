# CAT 2023: What Your 95.4 Percentile Means for New and Baby IIMs (Especially IIM Udaipur)

It's fantastic that you achieved a 95.4 percentile in CAT 2023! However, securing an admission call from IIMs involves meeting both overall and sectional cutoff criteria. Let's explore what your DILR percentile of 68 means for your chances.

## Key Takeaways

*   Even with a high overall CAT percentile, failing to meet just one sectional cutoff can prevent an interview call.
*   For IIM Udaipur, as part of the Common Admission Process (CAP), specific sectional cutoffs apply for general category candidates.
*   Your DILR percentile of 68 is likely below the general category sectional cutoff for IIM Udaipur and other CAP IIMs.
*   Reserved category candidates typically have significantly lower sectional cutoffs, which could alter your prospects.

## Understanding IIM Admission Criteria

Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) use a multi-stage process for admissions to their Post Graduate Program (PGP) or MBA courses. The first crucial step is clearing the CAT exam, but it's not just about your total score. Every IIM sets minimum qualifying percentiles, both for your overall performance and for each individual section (Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension - VARC, Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning - DILR, and Quantitative Ability - QA).

If a candidate does not clear even one of these sectional cutoffs, they typically will not be shortlisted for the subsequent rounds, which usually include a Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI), sometimes combined with a Group Discussion (GD).

## Your Situation: General Category and DILR Sectional

Assuming you are a general category candidate, your 95.4 overall percentile is excellent. However, your 68 percentile in the DILR section presents a challenge. For many IIMs, particularly those participating in the Common Admission Process (CAP), the sectional cutoffs for general category candidates are quite stringent.

## IIM Udaipur and the Common Admission Process (CAP)

IIM Udaipur is part of the CAP, which is a unified process for several new and baby IIMs. Historically, for general category candidates, IIMs participating in CAP, including IIM Udaipur, have required an overall percentile of around 92-94 and individual sectional percentiles often in the range of 70-75. Specifically, for IIM Udaipur, official data for past years indicates a general category overall cutoff of approximately 94 percentile, with individual sectional cutoffs typically around 73 percentile for each section. Given your 68 percentile in DILR, it falls below this typical requirement for general category applicants.

## Impact of Reserved Category Status

If you belong to a reserved category (e.g., SC, ST, OBC-NCL, EWS, PwD), the scenario changes considerably. IIMs provide significant relaxations in both overall and sectional cutoffs for reserved category candidates as per government regulations. For instance, sectional cutoffs for these categories can be substantially lower, sometimes in the range of 30-50 percentile. If you are a reserved category candidate, your 68 percentile in DILR might indeed be sufficient to clear the sectional cutoff for IIM Udaipur and other CAP IIMs, making an interview call a strong possibility. It's always advisable to check the specific official admission policy documents for your category on the respective IIM websites.

While your overall CAT score is commendable, the sectional cutoff for DILR is a critical factor for IIM admissions. We hope this clarifies your position.
