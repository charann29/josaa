# MOT: Full Form, Course, Fees, Syllabus, Eligibility, Salary, Scope

The Master of Occupational Therapy (MOT) is a two-year postgraduate program designed for individuals passionate about helping people regain independence and improve their quality of life. This specialized field of medical science focuses on rehabilitating individuals facing physical, mental, or emotional challenges.

## Key takeaways

*   MOT is a two-year postgraduate degree, building upon a Bachelor of Occupational Therapy (BOT).
*   The program equips graduates to work in various healthcare settings, assisting patients with rehabilitation and daily living skills.
*   Career opportunities span roles like Occupational Therapist, Rehabilitation Therapist, and Therapy Manager.
*   Average course fees range widely, typically from around Rs. 54,000 to Rs. 6.10 Lakhs, depending on the institution.

## What is Master of Occupational Therapy (MOT)?

MOT stands for Master of Occupational Therapy. This advanced degree deepens a student's understanding of occupational therapy principles, evidence-based practices, and clinical applications. The curriculum delves into theories and techniques to help patients participate in meaningful daily activities, from self-care to work and leisure. Occupational therapists play a crucial role in empowering individuals to overcome barriers caused by illness, injury, or disability.

## Eligibility Criteria for MOT

To be considered for an MOT program, candidates typically need to meet specific academic requirements. While these can vary slightly between institutions, the general criteria include:

*   **Educational Qualification:** A Bachelor of Occupational Therapy (BOT) degree from a university recognized by the relevant regulatory bodies.
*   **Minimum Marks:** Usually, a minimum aggregate score of 50% in their BOT degree is required. This percentage might be adjusted for candidates from reserved categories, as per government regulations.
*   **Internship:** Many institutions require a minimum of six months of clinical internship experience after completing the BOT program.

## MOT Admission Process and Fees

The admission process for MOT programs can be either merit-based, relying on your performance in your undergraduate degree, or through an entrance examination followed by an interview. Prospective students should always check the specific admission guidelines of their preferred colleges.

Course fees for the Master of Occupational Therapy vary significantly across institutions. On average, you can expect the fees to range from approximately Rs. 54,000 to Rs. 6.10 Lakhs for the entire two-year program. It is highly recommended to review the detailed fee structure of any college before applying.

## Career Scope and Salary After MOT

Graduating with an MOT opens up a fulfilling career path in a growing healthcare sector. Occupational therapists are in demand in various settings, including hospitals, rehabilitation centers, special schools, mental health facilities, and private clinics. The profession offers job stability and the satisfaction of making a tangible difference in people's lives.

Common career roles for MOT graduates include:

*   Occupational Therapist
*   Rehabilitation Therapist
*   Therapy Manager
*   Physiotherapist Assistant (though an MOT is a higher qualification)
*   Professor/Lecturer (after gaining sufficient experience)

Leading healthcare providers like Apollo Hospital, Fortis Hospital, Hosmat Hospital, and organizations such as Help Age India are among the potential employers for MOT professionals. The average annual salary package for an Occupational Therapist in India is around Rs. 4.0 Lakhs, which can increase significantly with experience and specialization.

Choosing an MOT degree is a step towards a rewarding career dedicated to enhancing the functional independence and well-being of individuals.
