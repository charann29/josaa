# Antimicrobial Stewardship for the Gulf, Middle East and North Africa by British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy via Futurelearn: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For healthcare professionals interested in combating antimicrobial resistance, the British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy (BSAC) offers a valuable online course focused on the Gulf, Middle East, and North Africa regions. This program provides essential knowledge and practical skills for implementing effective Antimicrobial Stewardship (AMS) programs.

*   Learn at your own pace through a self-study online format.
*   Understand the specific challenges and action plans for antimicrobial resistance in the MENA region.
*   Gain practical insights into establishing and maintaining AMS programs in various healthcare settings.
*   The course offers flexible access options, including a free limited version and a paid upgrade for a certificate.

## Course Structure and Learning Approach

This program is designed for flexible, self-paced learning. It is delivered through a combination of video and text-based content, allowing you to engage with the material at your convenience. The estimated time commitment is approximately 3 hours per week, making it manageable alongside other professional or personal commitments. The entire course content is provided in English.

## Understanding Antimicrobial Stewardship (AMS)

Antimicrobial Stewardship (AMS) is a coordinated program that promotes the appropriate use of antimicrobials (including antibiotics), improves patient outcomes, reduces microbial resistance, and decreases the spread of infections caused by multidrug-resistant organisms. This course delves into the fundamental principles of AMS, covering both individual practices and hospital-wide strategies. You'll explore what AMS entails and why it's crucial in today's healthcare landscape.

## Regional Focus: Gulf, Middle East, and North Africa

A significant aspect of this course is its specific focus on the Gulf, Middle East, and North Africa (MENA) regions. You will gain an overview of the existing antimicrobial resistance action plans and strategies implemented across these territories. This regional perspective is vital for healthcare professionals working in or planning to work in these areas, as it addresses the unique challenges and contexts prevalent there.
