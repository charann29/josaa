# CMAT Logical Reasoning Questions with Answers: PDF Download

For students aiming to crack the Common Management Admission Test (CMAT), the Logical Reasoning section is a crucial component. This section tests your analytical skills, and performing well here can significantly boost your overall score. To help you prepare, we've compiled some typical Logical Reasoning questions from past CMAT exams, along with their solutions.

## Key takeaways

*   CMAT Logical Reasoning questions generally fall into the moderate difficulty range.
*   Many questions in this section are designed to be solved relatively quickly.
*   Mastering Logical Reasoning primarily requires consistent practice, rather than memorizing formulas.
*   Familiarizing yourself with previous year's questions is an excellent way to understand the exam pattern.

## Understanding the CMAT Logical Reasoning Section

The Logical Reasoning section of CMAT typically features 20 questions. These questions cover a broad spectrum of topics, including Analytical Reasoning, Analogies, Direction Sense, Linear Arrangements, Blood Relations, Ranking, Statement & Assumption, Arguments, Coding-Decoding, Sequencing, Inferences, Number Series, Cause & Effects, and Visual Reasoning. Unlike quantitative sections, there are no specific formulas or theorems to learn. Success in this section is directly proportional to the amount and quality of your practice.

**Question 1:** The river flows from west to east and on the way then turns left. After going some distance it encounters a hill. It goes around the hill counter-clockwise in a quarter circle and then turns right. In which direction is the river finally flowing?

Options:
1.  North
2.  South
3.  East
4.  West

**Question 2:** Based on the statement given below, which of the following option is correct - Whenever Preeti’s father is in town, she abstains from school and goes to her aunt’s house.

Options:
1.  If Preeti has not abstained from school or she has not gone to her aunt’s house, it means that her father is not in town.
2.  If Preeti has not abstained from school but her father is in town, then she will definitely go to her aunt’s house.
3.  If Preeti has abstained from school but she has not gone to her aunt’s house, it means that her father is not in town.
4.  Both (a) and (c).

**Question 3:** Four friends, namely, Liyaqat, Lillian, Lima and Lalit are sitting on a horizontally placed wooden bench, all looking towards the same direction.

If: There is at least one person sitting between Lillian and Lima; Liyaqat is towards the right of Lima but not towards the right of Lalit; Lalit is seated immediately next to Lillian; & Lima is seated at one of the extreme corners of the bench.

Which of the following is definitely true?
1.  Lillian and Liyaqat are seated immediately next to one another.
2.  Liyaqat is seated at one of the extreme corners of the bench.
3.  There is at least one person seated between Lalit and Liyaqat.
4.  There is at least one person seated between Lalit and Lima.

**Question 4:** Ali, Benu, Cutty and Dolly play four different games among Basketball, Cricket, Kabaddi and Hockey. Ali does not play Basketball or Cricket. Benu does not play Kabaddi or Hockey. Cutty plays Hockey and Dolly plays either Basketball or Hockey. Who plays cricket?

Options:
1.  Ali
2.  Benu
3.  Cutty
4.  Dolly

## Tips for Approaching Logical Reasoning Questions

*   **Read Carefully:** Understand all conditions and constraints before attempting to solve. Misinterpreting a single detail can lead to an incorrect answer.
*   **Visualize:** For questions involving directions or arrangements, drawing a diagram can be incredibly helpful.
*   **Eliminate Options:** If you're unsure, try to eliminate obviously incorrect options to narrow down your choices.
*   **Practice Regularly:** Consistency is key. The more you practice, the better you'll become at recognizing patterns and solving different types of logical puzzles.
