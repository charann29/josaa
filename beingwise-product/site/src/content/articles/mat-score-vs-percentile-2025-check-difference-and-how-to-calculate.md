# MAT Score vs Percentile 2025: Check Difference and How to Calculate?

Understanding the difference between your MAT score and percentile is crucial for any aspiring MBA student. While your score reflects your performance on the exam, the percentile indicates how well you performed relative to other test-takers.

## Key takeaways

*   The MAT exam is scaled to ensure fairness across different test sessions, modes, and dates.
*   Your MAT percentile is calculated based on a scaled composite score, not your raw marks.
*   A higher percentile means you performed better than a larger percentage of other candidates.
*   Colleges use MAT percentile cut-offs for admission, with top institutions generally requiring higher percentiles.

## What is the MAT Score?

The MAT score is a numerical value that represents your performance on the Management Aptitude Test. Unlike some other exams that might use raw scores (the direct count of correct answers), the MAT uses a unique scaling process. Because the MAT is conducted in various modes (CBT, PBT, IBT) and on different dates, the difficulty level of the question papers can vary. To ensure that no candidate is unfairly disadvantaged or advantaged by a particular test session, the scores are normalized or 'scaled'. This process brings all scores to a common platform, allowing for a fair comparison of all candidates' abilities, regardless of when or how they took the exam. The official MAT result-cum-scorecard will display your scaled scores for each section and a composite scaled score.

## What is the MAT Percentile?

The MAT percentile is a rank that tells you what percentage of candidates scored less than or equal to you in the exam. For example, if you achieve a 90 percentile, it means that 90% of all candidates who appeared for that MAT session scored less than or equal to your score. The MAT percentile is the primary metric used by participating institutions for admissions. It's a relative measure, reflecting your standing within the entire pool of test-takers for that specific exam window.

## How is the MAT Percentile Calculated?

The All India Management Association (AIMA), the conducting body for MAT, calculates the percentile based on your scaled composite score. It's important to remember that the percentile is not derived directly from your raw score. While AIMA does not publicly release the exact formula for percentile calculation, the general principle is as follows:

1.  **Scaled Composite Score:** Your individual section scores are first scaled, and then combined to form a composite scaled score.
2.  **Ranking:** All candidates are ranked based on their composite scaled scores from highest to lowest.
3.  **Percentile Formula:** The percentile is then calculated using a formula similar to:

    ```
    Percentile = (Number of candidates who scored less than or equal to your score / Total number of candidates who appeared) * 100
    ```

    This means if 10,000 candidates appeared for the MAT and 9,000 candidates scored less than or equal to your score, your percentile would be 90 (9000/10000 * 100).

## Details on the MAT Result-cum-Scorecard

Your official MAT result-cum-scorecard, released by AIMA, will contain several important pieces of information. This typically includes:

*   Your personal details (name, roll number, etc.)
*   Your scaled scores for each of the five sections: Language Comprehension, Mathematical Skills, Data Analysis & Sufficiency, Intelligence & Critical Reasoning, and Indian & Global Environment.
*   Your overall composite scaled score.
*   Your overall MAT percentile.

It's crucial to download and keep a copy of this scorecard, as it's a mandatory document for the MBA/PGDM admission process at various B-schools.

Understanding both your MAT score and percentile will help you assess your performance accurately and strategically apply to management programs that align with your profile.
