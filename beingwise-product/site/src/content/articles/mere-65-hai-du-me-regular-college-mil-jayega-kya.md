# Mere 65% hai DU me regular college mil jayega kya

Many students wonder if their Class 12 marks are enough for admission to Delhi University. While a 65% score might seem challenging for the most competitive courses and colleges, there are definitely pathways to explore at DU.

## Key takeaways

*   Direct admission based solely on 65% in Class 12 is highly unlikely for popular courses and top DU colleges.
*   Your best bet for admission to DU is through the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) scores.
*   Reserved categories (SC/ST/OBC/PWD) and the EWS quota significantly improve admission chances.
*   Consider a wider range of colleges, including off-campus and evening colleges, as they often have lower admission thresholds.

## Understanding DU Admissions Today

Gone are the days when Class 12 marks were the primary or sole determinant for most DU admissions. Since 2022, the majority of undergraduate admissions to Delhi University are based on your performance in the Common University Entrance Test (CUET). This means that while your 65% in Class 12 is noted, your CUET score is what truly matters for most programs.

## The Role of CUET Scores

To apply to any undergraduate course at Delhi University, you must appear for and clear the CUET, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). Your scores in the relevant subjects within CUET will be used to create a merit list. A strong performance in CUET can open doors to various colleges and courses, even if your Class 12 percentage is not exceptionally high. It's crucial to prepare well for CUET and score as high as possible in the subjects required for your chosen program.

## Exploring Reservation Policies

Delhi University adheres to the reservation policies mandated by the Government of India. If you belong to any of the following categories, your chances of admission improve significantly:

*   **Scheduled Castes (SC)**
*   **Scheduled Tribes (ST)**
*   **Other Backward Classes (OBC - Non-Creamy Layer)**
*   **Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)**
*   **Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD)**
*   **Kashmiri Migrants (KM)**
*   **Children/Widows of Armed Forces Personnel (CW)**

Each of these categories has a specific percentage of seats reserved. Make sure you have all the necessary official documentation ready to claim your reservation during the application process, as specified by the official DU admission guidelines.

## Strategic College and Course Selection

With a 65% in Class 12 and a moderate CUET score, it becomes important to be strategic about your college and course choices. Instead of solely aiming for the top-tier colleges like SRCC, Miranda House, or St. Stephen's, which typically have very high cutoffs even through CUET, consider broadening your horizons.

*   **Off-Campus Colleges:** DU has numerous excellent colleges located slightly away from the main North and South Campuses. These colleges offer quality education and often have more accessible admission criteria.
*   **Evening Colleges:** Several DU colleges offer evening shifts. These are regular DU colleges, and their cutoffs can sometimes be lower than their day counterparts, providing a valuable opportunity.
*   **Less Popular Courses:** While you might have a dream course, exploring related or slightly less competitive courses can also be a viable path to securing a seat at Delhi University.

Always refer to the official DU admission bulletin for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding college and course availability and the specific CUET subject combinations required.

## The Application Process is Key

When the DU admission portal opens, ensure you complete your application accurately and on time. Pay close attention to the preference filling stage, where you rank your desired colleges and courses. A well-thought-out preference list, considering your CUET scores and category, can make a real difference.

While a 65% in Class 12 presents a challenge for direct admission to top DU colleges and courses, a strong CUET score, strategic college selection, and leveraging reservation categories significantly enhance your prospects of joining Delhi University.
