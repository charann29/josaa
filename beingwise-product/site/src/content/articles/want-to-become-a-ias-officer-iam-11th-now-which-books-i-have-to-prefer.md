# I want to become an IAS officer, I am in 11th now. Which books should I prefer? Is it the correct time to start preparation? Guide me.

It's wonderful that you're thinking about a career as an IAS officer so early! While it's great to have a long-term goal, the eleventh grade is actually a bit early to dive into dedicated UPSC Civil Services Exam (CSE) preparation. Your primary focus right now should be excelling in your 10+2 examinations and securing admission to a reputable college for your undergraduate degree.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Focus on Academics First:** Prioritize your Class 11 and 12 studies to build a strong foundation and get into a good college.
*   **Start During Graduation:** The ideal time to begin serious UPSC CSE preparation is alongside your undergraduate studies.
*   **Understand the Exam:** Familiarize yourself with the UPSC CSE's three stages: Prelims, Mains, and Interview.
*   **Build Foundational Knowledge:** Read NCERT books from Class 6 to 12 across various subjects to strengthen your general knowledge.

## Understanding the UPSC Civil Services Examination

The UPSC CSE is a highly competitive examination conducted in three distinct stages:

1.  **Preliminary Examination (Prelims):** This is a screening stage. It consists of two objective-type papers.
2.  **Main Examination (Mains):** This stage comprises nine descriptive papers. Your performance here, along with the interview, determines your final ranking.
3.  **Interview (Personality Test):** Candidates who clear the Mains examination are called for an interview.

## Recommended Books to Start With

While you shouldn't dive into specialized UPSC books yet, here are some foundational readings. These books are excellent for building general knowledge and improving comprehension skills.

### For Indian History

*   NCERT Class XI (Ancient & Medieval India)
*   NCERT Class XII (Modern Indian History)

### For Indian Art & Culture

*   NCERT Class XI (An Introduction to Indian Art)

### For Indian Polity

*   NCERT Class IX-XII (Political Science textbooks)

### For Geography

*   NCERT Class VI-X (Old Syllabus, if available, otherwise New Syllabus)
*   NCERT Class XI, XII (New Syllabus)
*   *Certificate Physical Geography* by G.C. Leong (for a slightly advanced but accessible understanding)

### For Economics

*   NCERT Class XI (Indian Economic Development)
*   NCERT Class XII (Introductory Macroeconomics)

### For Environment

*   NCERT Class XII Biology (specifically the last few chapters related to Ecology and Environment)

### For International Relations

*   NCERT Class XII (Contemporary World Politics)

## Cultivating Important Habits

Beyond just academic books, developing certain habits will be immensely beneficial for your future preparation:

*   **Read Newspapers Regularly:** Start reading a quality newspaper like *The Hindu* or *The Indian Express* daily. Focus on understanding current events, national and international news, economic developments, and environmental issues. Don't worry about memorizing facts yet, just aim for comprehension.
*   **Improve Your English:** Strong command over English is crucial for both the written exams and the interview. Read diverse materials, practice writing, and work on your vocabulary and grammar.
*   **Develop Critical Thinking:** As you read, try to form your own opinions and analyze different perspectives on issues. This skill is vital for essay writing and the interview stage.
*   **Stay Curious:** Cultivate a genuine interest in learning about various subjects, from science and technology to history and culture.

## The Right Time to Begin Focused Preparation

The most effective time to start dedicated UPSC CSE preparation is **after your 12th standard, alongside your graduation studies.** This approach offers several advantages:

*   **Maturity:** You'll be more mature and better equipped to handle the rigors of the exam.
*   **Time Management:** You can integrate your preparation with your college coursework, using your graduation subjects to strengthen your general studies knowledge.
*   **Deepen Understanding:** A graduation degree provides specialized knowledge and analytical skills that are highly valuable for the Mains examination.
*   **Syllabus Overlap:** Many undergraduate subjects, especially in humanities, overlap significantly with the UPSC CSE syllabus.

By laying a strong academic foundation now and cultivating good reading habits, you'll be perfectly positioned to begin your UPSC journey when the time is right. Keep working hard and stay focused on your current studies!
