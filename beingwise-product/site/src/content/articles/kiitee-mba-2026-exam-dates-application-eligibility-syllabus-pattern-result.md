# KIITEE MBA 2026: Exam Dates, Application, Eligibility, Syllabus, Pattern, Result

For students aspiring to pursue an MBA, the KIIT School of Management (KSOM) offers admission through its national-level entrance exam, KIITEE Management, or via scores from other major national tests.

## Key takeaways

*   KIITEE Management 2026 was conducted in January, with results released the same month.
*   Applicants with valid scores from CAT, MAT, XAT, CMAT, or GMAT can apply directly without taking the KIITEE Management exam.
*   The application window for all candidates, including those using national-level test scores, closed on April 15, 2026.
*   Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds are scheduled for late April 2026.

## Understanding KIITEE Management

KIITEE Management is a computer-based entrance examination organized by KSOM for admission to its various MBA programs. This university-level test is held across 16 cities in India. It comprises 150 multiple-choice questions, to be completed in 150 minutes, covering sections like Quantitative Aptitude, Analytical Reasoning, General Knowledge, and English. Candidates who have appeared for CAT, MAT, CMAT, or XAT are not required to take the KIITEE Management exam.

## Eligibility Criteria for KIITEE MBA 2026

To be considered for the KIITEE MBA program, candidates must meet the following eligibility requirements:

*   Hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks from a university recognized by the Ministry of HRD, Government of India.
*   Have secured at least 50% aggregate marks (or an equivalent CGPA) in both Class 10 and Class 12 examinations. If grades or CGPA are awarded instead of marks, their equivalent mark conversion will be based on the certification from the issuing university/institution/board. In the absence of specific conversion instructions, KSOM will apply its own equivalence methodology.

## Admission Process

The admission process for KIITEE MBA involves several stages:

1.  **Application:** Candidates can apply either by taking the KIITEE Management exam or by submitting scores from other national-level tests like CAT, MAT, XAT, CMAT, or GMAT.
2.  **Entrance Exam/Score Submission:** Candidates either appear for the KIITEE Management exam or provide their scores from accepted national-level tests.
3.  **Result/Shortlisting:** Results for KIITEE Management are announced, and candidates who qualify are shortlisted for the next round. A separate shortlist is also prepared for those applying through national-level test scores.
4.  **GD & PI Rounds:** Shortlisted candidates participate in Group Discussions (GD) and Personal Interviews (PI). These rounds are conducted in various cities including Bhubaneswar, Kolkata, Jamshedhed, Delhi, Bangalore, and Mumbai.
5.  **Final Selection:** Final admission offers are made based on a comprehensive evaluation of entrance exam scores (or national test scores), academic record, and performance in the GD and PI rounds.

## Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The KIITEE Management exam is a computer-based test designed to assess a candidate's aptitude for management studies.

*   **Exam Mode:** Computer-based
*   **Duration:** 150 minutes
*   **Total Questions:** 150
*   **Sections:** Quantitative Aptitude, Analytical Reasoning, General Knowledge, and English

The syllabus typically covers topics commonly found in MBA entrance exams, focusing on testing analytical skills, language proficiency, numerical ability, and awareness of current affairs.

For more detailed information, always refer to the official KIIT School of Management admissions portal.
