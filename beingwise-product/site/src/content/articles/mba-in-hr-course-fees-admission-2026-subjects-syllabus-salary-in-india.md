# MBA in HR: Course Fees, Admission 2026, Subjects, Syllabus, Salary in India

An MBA in Human Resources (HR) is a postgraduate program designed to equip individuals with the skills needed to manage an organization's most valuable asset: its people. This degree focuses on strategic human resource management, preparing graduates for leadership roles in various industries.

## Key takeaways

*   An MBA in HR is a two-year postgraduate degree focusing on workforce management and development.
*   Admission typically requires a bachelor's degree with at least 50% marks and a valid score in management entrance exams like CAT, MAT, XAT, or CMAT.
*   Course fees can vary widely, from approximately INR 1 lakh to INR 20 lakh, depending on the institution.
*   Graduates can pursue roles such as HR Manager, HR Business Partner, and Senior HR Manager, with salaries typically ranging from INR 4 LPA to INR 8 LPA.

## Understanding MBA in HR Management

This program delves into various aspects of human resources, including talent acquisition, employee relations, compensation and benefits, organizational development, and HR analytics. It aims to develop professionals who can strategically align HR practices with business goals to foster a productive and positive work environment.

### Eligibility Criteria for MBA in HR

To be considered for an MBA in HR, candidates generally need to have completed a bachelor's degree in any discipline (Science, Arts, or Commerce) from a recognized university. A minimum aggregate score of 50% in their undergraduate studies is usually required, though this can vary by college. In addition to academic qualifications, applicants must also clear one of the national or state-level management entrance examinations.

## MBA in HR Course Fees and Top Colleges

The cost of an MBA in HR program can vary significantly. Generally, fees can range from INR 1 lakh at public universities to upwards of INR 20 lakh at private institutions. Some popular colleges offering MBA in HR programs include Symbiosis Institute of Business Management (SIBM), XLRI Xavier School of Management, and Andhra University.

## MBA in HR Syllabus and Subjects

The two-year MBA in HR curriculum is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of human resource functions. While specific subjects may vary by institution, common areas of study include:

*   Organizational Behavior
*   Human Resource Planning
*   Talent Acquisition and Management
*   Compensation and Benefits Management
*   Industrial Relations and Labour Laws
*   Performance Management Systems
*   HR Analytics
*   Training and Development
*   Strategic Human Resource Management
*   Ethics in HR

## Career Scope and Salary after MBA in HR

An MBA in HR opens doors to a variety of rewarding careers across different sectors. Graduates are sought after by companies looking to optimize their human capital. Common job roles include:

*   **HR Manager:** Overseeing all HR functions, developing and implementing HR policies.
*   **Senior HR Manager:** Leading HR teams, strategizing on complex HR issues, and contributing to organizational strategy.
*   **HR Business Partner:** Working closely with business units to align HR strategies with business objectives.
*   **HR Analyst:** Using data to inform HR decisions and improve processes.

Top recruiting companies often include major IT firms, consulting agencies, manufacturing giants, and financial institutions such as TCS, Wipro, Deloitte, Samsung, Nokia, Reliance, and KPMG. The typical starting salary for an MBA in HR graduate in India ranges from INR 4 LPA to INR 8 LPA, depending on the college, individual skills, and the recruiting company.

An MBA in HR is a valuable qualification for those aspiring to make a significant impact on an organization's human capital and overall success.
