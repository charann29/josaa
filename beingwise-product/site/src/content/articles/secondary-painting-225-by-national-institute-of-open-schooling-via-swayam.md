# Secondary: Painting (225) by National Institute of Open Schooling via Swayam: Fee, Duration, How to Apply

For students keen to explore the world of art and painting at the secondary school level, the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) offers a comprehensive course through the Swayam platform. This program, titled "Secondary: Painting (225)," is designed to build artistic skills and an appreciation for art history.

## Key Takeaways

*   This is a 24-week, self-paced online course, perfect for school-level students.
*   It covers both theoretical aspects of art history and practical painting techniques.
*   Upon successful completion, students receive a certificate from NIOS via Swayam.
*   The instruction is provided in English, utilizing video lectures and text materials.

## Course Overview

The Secondary: Painting (225) program is a 24-week online training initiative led by Prof. Ms. Sanchita Bhattacharjee from the National Institute of Open Schooling. It's structured to deliver a thorough understanding of painting and art, combining both theoretical knowledge and hands-on practical skills. The learning journey is supported by interactive study materials and engaging video content.

## What You Will Learn

This course aims to develop your drawing abilities and overall artistic sense. By the end of the program, you will have gained insights into several key areas, including:

*   The evolution of art through different historical periods.
*   Various art forms and significant art movements like Renaissance, Impressionism, Cubism, Surrealism, and Abstract Art.
*   A deep dive into Folk Art of India.
*   An introduction to the pioneers and movements within Contemporary Indian Art.

The practical component focuses on:

*   Understanding tools and materials used in painting.
*   Developing skills in object study, nature study, and human figure drawing.
*   Practicing the study of animals and birds.
*   Learning about composition in art.

## Course Structure and Syllabus

The program is divided into theoretical and practical sections, with a substantial portion dedicated to revision towards the end. Here's a glimpse of the weekly topics:

### Theory

*   **Weeks 1-3:** History and Appreciation of Art, spanning from 3000 BC to the 18th Century AD.
*   **Week 4:** Folk Art of India.
*   **Weeks 5-7:** Major art movements including Renaissance, Impressionism, Cubism, Surrealism, and Abstract Art.
*   **Weeks 8-9:** Pioneers and movements in Contemporary Indian Art.

### Practicals

*   **Week 10:** Tools and Materials.
*   **Week 11:** Object Study.
*   **Week 12:** Nature Study.
*   **Week 13:** Human Figure.
*   **Week 14:** Study of Animals and Birds.
*   **Week 15:** Composition.

**Weeks 16-24:** Dedicated revision sessions to reinforce learning from both theory and practical modules.

## How to Apply

Enrolling in the Secondary: Painting (225) course is a straightforward process through the official Swayam portal. Follow these steps to begin:

1.  Navigate to the specific course page on the Swayam platform.
2.  Look for the "Sign-in" or "Register" option, typically found in the top right corner of the page.
3.  On the new page that appears, you can either sign in with your existing Swayam account credentials or create a new account if you are a first-time user.
4.  Once signed in, you can proceed with the enrollment for the course.

This course is an excellent opportunity for anyone with an interest in art to develop their skills and knowledge from the comfort of their home, guided by experts from NIOS.
