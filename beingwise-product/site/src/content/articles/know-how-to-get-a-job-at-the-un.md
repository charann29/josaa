# Know How to Get a Job at the UN

Many students dream of a career with the United Nations, driven by a desire to contribute to global peace, development, and humanitarian efforts. The UN is a highly influential organisation that recruits individuals from diverse academic backgrounds worldwide, but securing a position requires meeting specific criteria, including education, work experience, international affairs knowledge, and language proficiency.

## Key Takeaways

*   A postgraduate degree in a relevant field is often a prerequisite for most UN roles.
*   Specialised fields like International Relations, Humanitarian Action, Public Policy, and Public Health are highly valued.
*   Strong language skills, particularly in one of the UN's official languages besides English, are a significant advantage.
*   Gaining relevant work experience, even through internships, is crucial for building a UN career.

## About the United Nations

The United Nations is a prestigious organisation, offering a wide array of roles and an extensive global network. Its core mission revolves around achieving peace, dignity, and equality worldwide. This is accomplished through various bodies and specialised agencies such as the World Health Organisation (WHO), the World Food Programme (WFP), the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), UNICEF, and the United Nations High Commission for Refugees (UNHCR). This structure allows for a broad spectrum of professions and skills to be integrated into its workforce. Understanding the qualifications needed can help aspiring individuals identify suitable opportunities within this global organisation.

## Essential Requirements for Working at the UN

For most roles within the UN system, an advanced degree (postgraduate level) in a pertinent field is generally required. The specific course you choose will largely depend on your interests and the particular UN agency you aspire to join. Understanding these requirements early can help you select the most appropriate master's programme and prepare for future internships.

Some of the key academic areas that can lead to a career or internship with the United Nations include:

### International Development and International Relations

A postgraduate degree in International Development provides a strong theoretical foundation for working with agencies like WFP, UNICEF, and UNDP. A master's in International Relations, on the other hand, prepares you for coordinating between governments on complex global issues such as trade, climate change, and public health. These roles often demand significant diplomacy and negotiation skills.

### Humanitarian Action and Disaster Management

Given the UN's role in dispatching relief to disaster-affected regions, degrees in disaster management, human rights, or humanitarian action are highly valuable. Such programmes equip individuals with both the technical skills and an understanding of the social and psychological demands inherent in these challenging careers.

### Public Health or Medicine

Medical doctors and public health specialists frequently join the UN to advance global health initiatives and combat malnutrition and disease, often through affiliations with the WHO. These professionals might also serve as consultants to address specific epidemics or health crises in affected areas. Clarity about the UN's job qualifications is vital for medical professionals looking to align their careers with these global health goals.

### Public Policy

The UN relies heavily on experts in public policy to design, implement, and evaluate programmes and initiatives worldwide. A background in public policy is essential for roles focused on governance, strategic planning, and addressing societal challenges through evidence-based approaches.

## Language Proficiency and Experience

Proficiency in one of the UN's official languages (Arabic, Chinese, English, French, Russian, Spanish) is often a strong requirement, with fluency in English and French being particularly advantageous for many positions. Additionally, gaining relevant work experience, even through internships or volunteer work with international organisations or NGOs, is crucial for building a competitive profile for UN employment.

Embarking on a career with the UN is a journey that requires careful planning, dedicated academic pursuit, and a commitment to global service.
