# What is the facility for a child daughter of an Ex Artillery soldier in collage for MBA and what documents are required for the same.

For daughters of ex-servicemen, pursuing an MBA can come with certain advantages, especially regarding admissions. This guide outlines the key benefits and necessary documentation to help you navigate the application process effectively.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Equal Access:** Daughters of ex-artillery soldiers have full access to all facilities and infrastructure offered by any management school, just like other students.
*   **Reservation Quotas:** Many top B-schools in India offer specific reservation quotas for children of defence personnel, often under categories like 'CW' (Children of War/Wards of Defence Personnel).
*   **Lower Cut-offs:** Admissions under these reserved categories typically have lower cut-off scores compared to general admission categories, increasing the chances of securing a seat.
*   **Document Specificity:** Required documents can vary by institution, but generally include proof of the parent's service and the applicant's academic records.

## Understanding Defence Quotas in MBA Admissions

Many prestigious management institutions in India acknowledge the contributions of defence personnel by reserving a percentage of seats for their children. These reservations are a significant advantage for applicants from ex-servicemen families. For instance, institutions like the Faculty of Management Studies (FMS), Delhi, are known to have specific quotas under the 'CW' category, which includes wards of defence personnel. It's crucial to research each target college thoroughly, as the exact nomenclature and percentage of seats reserved can differ.

## Exploring Colleges with Defence Reservations

While FMS Delhi is a well-known example, many other colleges across India also offer similar defence reservation quotas. When shortlisting institutions for your MBA, it's advisable to check their official admission brochures or websites for details on reserved categories. Look for mentions of 'Wards of Defence Personnel,' 'Children of Ex-Servicemen,' or 'CW' categories. This proactive research will help you identify all potential opportunities and apply strategically.

## Expected Admission Cut-offs

One of the most notable benefits of applying under a defence quota is the typically lower admission cut-off scores. While general category applicants might need very high scores in national-level entrance exams like CAT, XAT, or CMAT, candidates applying under reserved categories often qualify with comparatively lower scores. This doesn't mean the admission process is less rigorous; it simply reflects the policy to provide an accessible pathway for children of those who have served the nation. However, meeting the minimum eligibility criteria for the specific program and institution remains essential.

## Essential Documents for Application

The documentation required for admission under a defence quota can vary significantly from one college to another. However, a general set of documents is usually requested. It's always best to refer to the specific college's admission prospectus for their precise requirements. Here's a list of commonly requested documents:

*   **Proof of Parent's Service:** This is paramount. It could include service record documents, discharge certificates, or pension payment orders (PPO) that confirm the parent's status as an ex-artillery soldier.
*   **Parent's Identity Proof:** A copy of the parent's Aadhar card or other government-issued ID.
*   **Applicant's Identity Proof:** Aadhar card, passport, or other valid government ID of the daughter.
*   **Academic Records:** Mark sheets and certificates for 10th standard, 12th standard, and graduation.
*   **Entrance Exam Scorecard:** The official scorecard of the management entrance exam (e.g., CAT, XAT, CMAT, MAT).
*   **Caste Certificate (if applicable):** If you are also applying under a specific caste reservation (e.g., SC/ST/OBC), the relevant certificate issued by a competent authority will be needed.
*   **Domicile Certificate:** Proof of permanent residency, if required by the state-specific institution.
*   **Photographs:** Recent passport-sized photographs.

### Important Note on Document Verification

Ensure all documents are authentic and readily available. Colleges often conduct thorough verification, and any discrepancy can lead to the cancellation of admission. It's advisable to have both original documents and multiple attested photocopies ready.

By understanding these provisions and preparing diligently, daughters of ex-artillery soldiers can significantly enhance their chances of securing admission to their desired MBA programs.
