  - q: >-
      What is the difference between qualifying CAT cut-off and final CAT
      cut-off?
    a: >-
      The qualifying CAT cut-off percentiles are the minimum scores (sectional
      and overall) required for candidates to be eligible for the Written
      Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds at an IIM. These are
      eligibility scores, not selection guarantees, and are released publicly
      before the exam and result. The final CAT cut-off percentiles are the
      scores based on which a candidate is actually selected for the WAT-PI
      round. These are not publicly released but are estimated based on
      candidate selections. Qualifying cut-offs are category-wise (General,
      NC-OBC, General-EWS, SC/ST, PwD), with General category cut-offs typically
      being higher.
  - q: What CAT cut-off is required for admission to IIM Bangalore?
    a: >-
      The CAT cut-offs for IIM Bangalore generally range between 97-100
      percentile for final admission. For eligibility to the WAT and PI rounds,
      the qualifying CAT cut-off for General category students is typically 85
      percentile overall. Sectional qualifying cut-offs for the General category
      are usually 80 for VARC, 75 for DILR, and 75 for Quantitative Aptitude.
      These cut-offs vary for other categories, for instance, 70 percentile
      overall for SC candidates, 65 for ST, and 60 for PwD.
  - q: What is the CAT cut-off for IIM Lucknow?
    a: >-
      The CAT cut-offs for admission to IIM Lucknow are typically in the range
      of 97-99 percentile.

# CAT 2025 Topper Interview: Aishiki’s Journey from Psychology to 99.18 Percentile in CAT

Preparing for the CAT exam can feel daunting, especially for those from humanities backgrounds or those taking the test for the first time. In this interview, we explore the inspiring journey of Aishiki Sen, a BA (Hons) Psychology student from Banaras Hindu University (BHU), who achieved an impressive 99.18 percentile in CAT 2025.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Embrace Mock Tests:** Aishiki credits her strong performance to consistent mock test practice, highlighting their role in understanding the exam pattern and improving time management.
*   **Strategic Sectional Focus:** Even with a non-mathematics background, Aishiki developed targeted strategies for each section, demonstrating that a well-planned approach can overcome perceived weaknesses.
*   **Balance and Burnout Prevention:** She successfully managed her college studies alongside CAT preparation, emphasizing the importance of preventing burnout through effective scheduling and self-care.
*   **Mindset Matters:** Maintaining a positive and calm mindset on exam day was crucial for her, helping her perform under pressure.

## Aishiki's Profile at a Glance

*   **Name:** Aishiki Sen
*   **Academic Background:** Third-year student pursuing BA (Hons) Psychology from the Faculty of Social Sciences, Banaras Hindu University.
*   **CAT 2025 Overall Percentile:** 99.18 (Overall Score: 87.21)
*   **Sectional Percentiles:**
    *   Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR): 99.30 (Score: 31.66)
    *   Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC): 98.91 (Score: 42.76)
    *   Quantitative Aptitude (QA): 87.04 (Score: 12.80)

## Overcoming Challenges as a Humanities Student

Aishiki's journey is particularly noteworthy given her background in Psychology, a field not traditionally associated with the quantitative demands of the CAT exam. She demonstrates that with dedication and the right strategy, students from any academic stream can excel. Her strong scores in VARC and DILR, coupled with a respectable percentile in QA, show the effectiveness of a balanced preparation approach.

## The Power of Mock Tests and Sectional Strategy

Aishiki consistently highlighted the critical role of mock tests in her preparation. She didn't just take mocks; she analyzed them thoroughly to identify her strengths and weaknesses. This iterative process allowed her to refine her time management and question selection skills. For sections like DILR, she focused on understanding different types of sets and practicing diverse problems. In VARC, consistent reading and comprehension practice were key. While QA presented a challenge, her strategic focus on foundational concepts and selecting solvable questions helped her secure a competitive percentile.

## Managing College and Preventing Burnout

Juggling a demanding university curriculum with CAT preparation can be stressful. Aishiki shared insights into how she managed her time effectively, ensuring that neither her college studies nor her CAT goals suffered. She emphasized the importance of taking breaks and maintaining a balanced lifestyle to prevent burnout, a common pitfall for many aspirants. Her advice underscores that consistent, sustainable effort is more effective than sporadic, intense study sessions.

Aishiki's journey from a psychology student at BHU to a CAT topper with a 99.18 percentile is a testament to perseverance, strategic planning, and a resilient mindset. Her experience offers valuable lessons for all aspiring MBA candidates.
