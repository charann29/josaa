# UPTET 2025: Notification (Soon), Application Form, New Exam Dates (Soon), Syllabus & Latest News

The Uttar Pradesh Teacher Eligibility Test (UPTET) is a crucial state-level examination for aspiring teachers in Uttar Pradesh. This test determines eligibility for teaching positions in primary (Classes 1-5) and upper primary (Classes 6-8) schools across the state.

## Key Takeaways

*   The Uttar Pradesh Education Service Selection Commission (UPESSC) conducts the UPTET annually.
*   UPTET 2025 will be held offline across designated centers in Uttar Pradesh.
*   The official notification, including detailed dates and application forms, will be released shortly by UPESSC.
*   Qualifying UPTET is a prerequisite for appearing in the UP Super TET exam, which leads to recruitment in state schools.

## What is UPTET?

UPTET, or the Uttar Pradesh Teacher Eligibility Test, is a state-level examination administered by the Uttar Pradesh Education Service Selection Commission (UPESSC) on behalf of the Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Board (UPBEB). Its primary purpose is to certify candidates as eligible to teach at various levels within Uttar Pradesh schools.

The examination is structured into two distinct papers:

*   **Paper 1:** Designed for individuals aspiring to teach classes 1 to 5 (Primary Level).
*   **Paper 2:** For those aiming to teach classes 6 to 8 (Upper Primary Level).

Candidates who wish to teach across both levels (Classes 1-8) must appear for and qualify both Paper 1 and Paper 2.

## UPTET Syllabus and Exam Pattern

The UPTET syllabus covers various subjects to assess a candidate's teaching aptitude and knowledge. Paper 1 generally focuses on Child Development and Pedagogy, Language I (Hindi), Language II (English/Urdu/Sanskrit), Mathematics, and Environmental Studies. Paper 2 includes Child Development and Pedagogy, Language I, Language II, and then either Mathematics & Science or Social Studies, depending on the candidate's subject specialization.

Both papers consist of 150 multiple-choice questions, with each question carrying one mark. There is no negative marking for incorrect answers. A thorough understanding of the syllabus and exam pattern is vital for effective preparation.
