# IMS BHU: Does it Offer a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) Course?

Many aspiring students wonder if the Institute of Medical Sciences (IMS) at Banaras Hindu University (BHU) offers a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) program, especially since it might not be immediately visible on their official website. We're here to clarify this for you.

## Key Takeaways

*   IMS BHU **does** indeed offer a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) course.
*   It's a full-time program spanning four and a half years, including a compulsory internship.
*   Admission is through the CUET UG examination, not NEET.
*   The total number of seats for the BPT program is 25.

## Understanding the BPT Program at IMS BHU

The Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) program at the Institute of Medical Sciences, BHU, is a well-established course designed to train future physiotherapy professionals. It's structured as a four-year academic program followed by a mandatory six-month internship, making it a comprehensive four-and-a-half-year commitment.

## Admissions Process: CUET UG is Key

One common point of confusion arises because the BPT course at IMS BHU does not use the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) for admissions. Unlike undergraduate medical (MBBS) and dental (BDS) programs, which rely on NEET scores, entry into the BPT program is determined by performance in the Common University Entrance Test (CUET UG). This distinction is crucial for prospective applicants to understand when preparing for their entrance examinations.

## Seat Matrix and Categories

IMS BHU allocates a total of 25 seats for its BPT program. These seats are distributed across various categories, including General, Other Backward Classes (OBC), Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and Economically Weaker Sections (EWS), in accordance with the reservation policies set by Banaras Hindu University. This ensures a diverse intake of students into the program.

## Course Fees

The total tuition fee for the entire BPT program at IMS BHU is approximately Rs. 72,056. This figure covers the full duration of the course, providing a clear understanding of the financial commitment involved.

## Why It Might Be Hard to Find on the Website

The reason you might not easily find the BPT course listed alongside NEET-related programs on the IMS BHU official website is precisely because its admission process is managed by CUET UG. Websites often categorize courses based on their respective entrance exams, and since BPT falls under the CUET UG umbrella, it may be listed separately from medical and dental courses that use NEET. Always ensure you check all relevant sections or search specifically for 'BPT' on the university's official portals.

We hope this clarifies your questions about the Bachelor of Physiotherapy program at IMS BHU. Wishing you the best in your academic journey!
