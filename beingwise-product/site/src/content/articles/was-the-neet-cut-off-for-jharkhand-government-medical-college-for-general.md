# What Was the NEET Cut-Off for Jharkhand Government Medical Colleges for General Category in 2020?

Understanding past cut-off scores is crucial for students aiming for government MBBS seats in Jharkhand. The NEET 2020 examination results provided a benchmark for admissions into the state's medical colleges.

## Key Takeaways

*   In 2020, three government medical colleges in Jharkhand participated in state-level counselling for MBBS admissions.
*   RIMS Ranchi had the highest cut-off for the General Category.
*   Scores generally ranged above 600 for securing a seat in these institutions.
*   Aspiring students should aim for a score in the 600-620 range for a strong chance at admission.

## Understanding NEET Cut-Offs

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is the sole entrance examination for admission to MBBS and BDS courses in India. The cut-off score represents the minimum marks required for a candidate to be considered for admission to a particular college or category. These cut-offs fluctuate each year based on factors such as the number of applicants, the difficulty level of the exam, and the availability of seats.

## Jharkhand Government Medical Colleges in 2020

During the NEET 2020 admission cycle, three government medical colleges in Jharkhand offered MBBS seats through state counselling. These institutions are highly sought after due to their quality of education and affordable fee structure.

## General Category Cut-Off Scores for 2020

For students belonging to the General Category, the following scores were observed for admission to Jharkhand's government medical colleges in 2020:

| College Name                           | General Category Cut-Off (Marks) |
| :------------------------------------- | :------------------------------- |
| Rajendra Institute of Medical Sciences (RIMS), Ranchi | 617                              |
| Mahatma Gandhi Memorial Medical College (MGM-MC), Jamshedpur | 604                              |
| Shaheed Nirmal Mahto Medical College (SNMMC), Dhanbad (formerly PMCH Dhanbad) | 602                              |

As you can see from the table, RIMS Ranchi consistently maintains a higher cut-off, reflecting its strong reputation and demand among medical aspirants.

## What This Means for Future Aspirants

While cut-off scores can vary from year to year, the 2020 data provides a valuable reference point. Students aiming for a government MBBS seat in Jharkhand in the General Category should target a score comfortably above 600. Preparing thoroughly and consistently performing well in mock tests can help achieve this goal.

Planning your NEET preparation with these target scores in mind can significantly improve your chances of securing a coveted government medical college seat in Jharkhand.
