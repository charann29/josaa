## Understanding IIM Udaipur's Selection Criteria

IIM Udaipur, like other IIMs, uses a comprehensive evaluation process that goes beyond just the CAT percentile. They look at a candidate's complete profile, which includes academic performance, work experience (if any), diversity, and performance in the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds.

For the initial shortlisting, a minimum CAT percentile is set for different categories. For the SC category, the official CAT 2022 cut-offs for IIM Udaipur were:

*   **Quantitative Ability (QA):** 50 percentile
*   **Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR):** 50 percentile
*   **Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VRC):** 50 percentile
*   **Overall Percentile:** 60 percentile

Given your 93 percentile, you comfortably meet and exceed these minimum sectional and overall cut-offs for the SC category. This is a very positive sign for receiving an interview call.

## The Role of Your Academic Profile

While CAT is crucial, your past academic record also plays a significant role in the overall selection process. IIMs assign weights to marks obtained in your 10th standard, 12th standard, and undergraduate degree. Here's a look at your academic percentages:

*   **10th Standard:** 84%
*   **12th Standard:** 75%
*   **Undergraduate Degree:** 60%

These scores will be factored into an Academic Profile score. IIMs often have specific criteria for converting these percentages into a score, sometimes considering the board or university. Your 10th and 12th scores are good, while your undergraduate percentage is moderate. However, for SC candidates, the thresholds and weightages might be applied differently, providing a more favorable consideration.

## Being a Fresher and SC Category Advantage

Being a fresher means you won't have work experience points, but this is common for many applicants. IIMs value a diverse batch, including freshers who bring different perspectives. Your SC category status is a significant advantage, as reservation policies ensure a certain percentage of seats are allocated to Scheduled Caste candidates. This means that for the same CAT score and academic profile, an SC candidate often has a much better chance of admission compared to a General category candidate.

## Next Steps: WAT and PI

If you receive a call for the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds, these will be critical. Your performance in these stages will determine your final admission offer. The WAT assesses your written communication skills, while the PI evaluates your personality, communication, leadership potential, and general awareness. Prepare thoroughly for these rounds by practicing current affairs, common interview questions, and mock WATS.

Considering your strong CAT percentile, especially within the SC category, and your academic profile, you certainly have a good chance of receiving a call from IIM Udaipur. Prepare diligently for the subsequent selection rounds!
