# Hello, my 10th and 12th score is above 80% but my graduation score is 55% only – what business schools for MBA do I stand a chance to get in?

It's common for students to have varying academic performance across their schooling and undergraduate degrees. While your graduation score of 55% might seem concerning for MBA admissions, your strong 10th and 12th scores are definitely a plus.

## Key takeaways

*   A 55% graduation score makes you eligible for the CAT exam and many MBA programs.
*   A high CAT percentile (95-99) can significantly offset a lower undergraduate score.
*   Top business schools consider a holistic profile, including essays, interviews, and work experience.
*   Specific institutions like NMIMS and Symbiosis have their own entrance exams (NMAT and SNAP, respectively).

## Eligibility for MBA Entrance Exams

First, let's address eligibility. For the Common Admission Test (CAT), the primary entrance exam for most top MBA programs in India, the general requirement is a minimum of 50% marks or an equivalent CGPA in your bachelor's degree. This means your 55% makes you fully eligible to appear for the CAT exam. Other exams like NMAT and SNAP also have similar or slightly varying eligibility criteria, which you should confirm on their respective official websites.

## The Holistic Admission Process

Most leading business schools, especially the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs), employ a comprehensive evaluation system. They don't just look at one score in isolation. Your application is assessed based on a weighted average of several factors, which typically include:

*   **Academic Scores:** Your 10th, 12th, and graduation percentages.
*   **Entrance Exam Score:** Your CAT, NMAT, SNAP, or other relevant exam percentile.
*   **Written Ability Test (WAT):** An essay or short writing task.
*   **Personal Interview (PI):** A crucial stage to assess your communication, personality, and motivation.
*   **Work Experience:** If you have any, it adds significant value to your profile.
*   **Diversity Factors:** Some institutes consider academic or gender diversity.

While your graduation score might be lower, excelling in the entrance exam, WAT, and PI can substantially strengthen your overall profile and compensate for it. Your strong 10th and 12th scores will also contribute positively to your academic profile score.

## Strategizing for a Strong Application

Given your academic background, here's how you can boost your chances:

1.  **Ace the Entrance Exam:** This is your biggest opportunity to shine. Aim for a very high percentile (95+ for CAT) to demonstrate your aptitude. Start preparing early, consider joining a coaching institute if it helps your learning style, and practice mock tests regularly. The CAT exam is typically conducted in November each year, so plan your preparation accordingly.
2.  **Focus on WAT and PI:** These components are critical. Practice writing on current affairs and abstract topics for WAT. For PI, prepare common interview questions, research the schools thoroughly, and be ready to articulate your strengths, weaknesses, and career goals clearly.
3.  **Highlight Strengths:** In your application essays and interviews, emphasize your strong 10th and 12th scores, any extracurricular achievements, leadership roles, or unique experiences that showcase your potential beyond just academic percentages.

## Potential Business Schools to Consider

Assuming you perform exceptionally well in the entrance exams and subsequent selection rounds, here are some reputable business schools you could target. Remember that admission depends on a dynamic combination of factors, and cutoff scores can vary each year.

| Exam Accepted | Business Schools (Illustrative List) |
| :------------ | :----------------------------------- |
| CAT           | IIMs (various), FORE School of Management, Goa Institute of Management, BIM Trichy, IPE Hyderabad, IMT Ghaziabad (for some programs), IBS Hyderabad (for some programs) |
| NMAT          | NMIMS University (Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad) |

This list is not exhaustive but provides a starting point. Many other excellent institutions also accept CAT scores or have their own entrance processes. For example, SP Jain School of Global Management Mumbai has a unique selection process that considers diverse profiles.

Your strong performance in entrance exams and subsequent interview rounds will be key to securing a spot in a top MBA program, even with a moderate undergraduate score. Focus on what you can control now: your preparation and performance in the upcoming selection process.
