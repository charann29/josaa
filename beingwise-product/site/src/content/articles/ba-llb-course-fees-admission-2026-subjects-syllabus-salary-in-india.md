# BA LLB: Course Fees, Admission 2026, Subjects, Syllabus, Salary in India

The Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Legislative Law (BA LLB) is a popular integrated undergraduate program that combines the study of social sciences with professional legal education. This five-year course is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in law while also broadening their academic perspective through subjects like history, sociology, and economics.

## Key takeaways

*   The BA LLB is a five-year integrated undergraduate degree combining arts and law disciplines.
*   Eligibility typically requires a Class 12 pass with at least 55% aggregate marks.
*   Admission is primarily entrance-based, with exams like CLAT, AILET, SLAT, and CUET being prominent.
*   Course fees can vary significantly, ranging from approximately INR 3,600 to INR 80 Lakh, depending on the institution.

## Understanding the BA LLB Program

This integrated course offers a comprehensive curriculum that helps students understand the socio-legal aspects of various laws. It's ideal for those who wish to delve deep into legal principles while also gaining insights into the societal context in which laws operate. The program structure ensures that graduates are well-rounded and prepared for diverse legal careers.

### Eligibility Criteria for BA LLB

To be eligible for the BA LLB program, candidates generally need to have successfully completed their Class 12 examinations from a recognized board. Most institutions require a minimum aggregate score, often around 55%, though this can vary by college. It's always advisable to check the specific requirements of your preferred institutions.

## BA LLB Admission 2026

Admission to BA LLB programs in 2026 will largely be through entrance examinations. Many top law universities and colleges conduct their own tests or accept scores from national-level entrance exams. Some key exams to consider include:

*   **CLAT (Common Law Admission Test):** For admission to National Law Universities (NLUs) and other participating institutions.
*   **AILET (All India Law Entrance Test):** Specifically for admission to National Law University, Delhi.
*   **SLAT (Symbiosis Law Admission Test):** For Symbiosis International (Deemed University) law schools.
*   **CUET (Common University Entrance Test):** For admission to various central and state universities.
*   **MHCET Law:** For colleges in Maharashtra.
*   **AP LAWCET & TS LAWCET:** For colleges in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, respectively.

Keep an eye on official notifications from NTA and specific university portals for exact dates and application procedures for 2026 admissions. For example, the MH CET Law 2026 exam is tentatively scheduled for May 8, 2026. Official sources will provide the most up-to-date information.

## BA LLB Subjects and Syllabus

The BA LLB syllabus is multidisciplinary, covering a wide range of subjects from both arts and law streams. Over the five years, students will typically study a blend of foundational arts subjects and core legal principles. Common subjects include:

*   Constitutional Law
*   Criminal Law
*   Civil Law
*   Administrative Law
*   Family Law
*   History
*   Sociology
*   Economics
*   Criminology
*   Environmental Law
*   Human Rights Law

The curriculum is designed to provide a holistic understanding of the legal system and its societal implications.

## BA LLB Course Fees and Top Colleges

The fees for a BA LLB program can vary widely across different institutions, from government-funded universities to private colleges. Based on available data, the annual course fees can range from as low as INR 3,600 to as high as INR 80 Lakh for the entire five-year duration.

Some of the prominent institutions offering BA LLB programs, as recognized by various rankings like the NIRF rankings for law, include the National Law Universities (NLUs), Symbiosis Law School, Saveetha Institute of Medical and Technical Sciences, and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar College of Law.

## Career Scope and Salary after BA LLB

A BA LLB degree opens doors to a diverse range of career opportunities in the legal field and beyond. Graduates can work as advocates, legal advisors, solicitors, legal managers, or law officers. The average starting salary for BA LLB graduates in India is approximately INR 5.3 Lakh per annum, though this can vary significantly based on the role, employer, location, and individual skills.

Top recruiters include law firms, multinational companies, public and private sector banks, government departments, and Legal Process Outsourcing (LPO) firms. Graduates can also choose to appear for judicial services examinations or pursue higher education like an LLM.

Embarking on a BA LLB journey can be a rewarding path for those passionate about law and justice.
