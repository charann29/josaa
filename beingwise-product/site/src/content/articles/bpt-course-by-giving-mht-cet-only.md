# Can I do BPT course by giving MHT CET only?

Yes, aspiring physiotherapists in Maharashtra can absolutely pursue a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) degree by taking the MHT CET examination, provided they meet the required cutoffs. This pathway is a primary route for many students within the state.

## Key Takeaways

*   MHT CET is a valid and common entrance exam for BPT admissions in Maharashtra.
*   Eligibility typically requires Maharashtra domicile for MHT CET BPT admissions.
*   NEET UG is another significant entrance exam for BPT, often used nationwide.
*   Meeting specific cutoff scores in either exam is crucial for securing a seat.

## Understanding BPT Admissions in Maharashtra

For students aiming for a BPT degree within Maharashtra, the MHT CET (Maharashtra Health and Technical Common Entrance Test) is a highly relevant examination. If you hold a valid Maharashtra domicile certificate, you are generally eligible to appear for the MHT CET for admission into various health science courses, including BPT.

Colleges offering BPT programs in Maharashtra consider MHT CET scores as a basis for their admission process. The specific cutoff marks can vary each year depending on factors like the number of applicants, the difficulty of the exam, and the availability of seats in different institutions.

## The Role of NEET UG for BPT

While MHT CET is a strong option for Maharashtra domiciled students, it's important to know that the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET UG) is also a widely accepted entrance examination for BPT courses, both within Maharashtra and across India. Many institutions, particularly private universities and those with national affiliations, may accept NEET UG scores for their BPT programs.

For students who might not qualify for MHT CET or are looking at a broader range of colleges, preparing for NEET UG can open up additional opportunities. It's always advisable to check the specific admission criteria of your desired colleges, as some might prefer or even mandate NEET UG scores, while others accept both.

## Eligibility Criteria for BPT

To be eligible for a BPT program, regardless of the entrance exam, candidates generally need to have completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as core subjects. A minimum aggregate percentage in these subjects is usually required, which can vary by institution. For MHT CET, specific subject combinations and minimum marks are detailed in the official MHT CET information brochure.

Beyond academic qualifications, meeting the domicile requirements for MHT CET is essential for state quota seats. For NEET UG, there are no specific domicile restrictions for general admission, though state counselling bodies might have their own rules for state-funded seats.

## Application Process and Counselling

After taking either the MHT CET or NEET UG, the next step involves the application and counselling process. For MHT CET qualified candidates, the Directorate of Medical Education and Research (DMER) in Maharashtra typically conducts the centralized admission process (CAP) for health science courses. This involves online registration, document verification, and choice filling based on merit.

Similarly, for NEET UG, the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) conducts national-level counselling for certain seats, while state counselling authorities manage admissions for state quota seats. It's crucial to stay updated with the official notifications from these respective bodies to understand the application timelines and procedures.

Choosing the right path for your BPT aspirations involves understanding both MHT CET and NEET UG and aligning them with your academic profile and college preferences.
