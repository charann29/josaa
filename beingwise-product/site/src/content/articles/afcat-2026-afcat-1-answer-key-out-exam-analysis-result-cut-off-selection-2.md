# AFCAT 2026: Answer Key, Exam Analysis, Result, Cut-off, Selection Process & FAQs

The Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT) is a key examination for individuals aspiring to join the Indian Air Force in various roles. This page provides essential information for candidates about the AFCAT 1 2026 cycle, including the recently released answer key and upcoming stages.

## Key Takeaways

*   The official answer key for AFCAT 1 2026 was released on February 1st, allowing candidates to review their performance.
*   Candidates had a specific window to challenge the answer key if they found discrepancies.
*   The AFCAT 1 2026 examination took place on January 31st.
*   A total of 340 vacancies across Flying and Ground Duty branches are expected to be filled through this recruitment drive.

The AFCAT 1 2026 examination was generally considered to be of moderate difficulty. Understanding the exam analysis helps candidates gauge their performance and provides insights into the types of questions asked.

## AFCAT Selection Process

The selection process for AFCAT involves multiple stages designed to assess a candidate's suitability for a role in the Indian Air Force. The primary stages are:

1.  **Online Written Examination:** This is the first stage, where candidates appear for the AFCAT computer-based test covering General Awareness, Verbal Ability, Numerical Ability, and Reasoning & Military Aptitude.
2.  **Air Force Selection Board (AFSB) Interview:** Candidates who successfully clear the written examination are called for an AFSB interview. This stage includes psychological tests, group tasks, and a personal interview to evaluate leadership qualities, communication skills, and overall personality.
3.  **Medical Examination:** After clearing the AFSB interview, candidates undergo a thorough medical examination to ensure they meet the stringent physical and medical standards required by the Indian Air Force.
4.  **Final Merit List:** A final merit list is prepared based on the combined scores from the written exam and the AFSB interview, subject to medical fitness and the number of available vacancies.

## AFCAT Result and Cut-off

The results for AFCAT 1 2026 are anticipated to be announced in February. Candidates can check their results on the official AFCAT portal. Along with the results, the Indian Air Force also releases the cut-off marks. The cut-off is the minimum score required to qualify for the next stage of the selection process, which is the AFSB interview. The cut-off marks can vary each year based on factors such as the number of applicants, the difficulty level of the exam, and the total vacancies.

For those aspiring to join the Indian Air Force, diligent preparation and a clear understanding of each stage of the AFCAT process are essential.
