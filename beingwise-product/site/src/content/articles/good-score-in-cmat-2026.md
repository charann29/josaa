# What is a Good Score in CMAT 2026?

Understanding what constitutes a strong score in the CMAT exam is crucial for aspiring MBA students. With thousands of candidates appearing each year, knowing your target can significantly guide your preparation and college choices.

## Key Takeaways

*   A CMAT score above the 99th percentile is typically required for admission to the most sought-after management institutes in India.
*   For a good selection of Tier 2 colleges, aiming for an 85th percentile or higher is a safe bet.
*   The CMAT exam is scored out of 400 marks, and a raw score between 200 and 250 is generally considered decent.
*   Final admissions are based on percentiles, not just raw scores, making percentile a key metric to focus on.

## Understanding CMAT Scores and Percentiles

CMAT, or the Common Management Admission Test, is a nationwide entrance exam for MBA programs. While candidates receive a raw score based on their correct and incorrect answers, the ultimate result that matters for admissions is the percentile. This percentile indicates how many candidates you have scored better than. For example, a 99th percentile means you scored better than 99% of all test-takers.

Approximately 75,000 candidates take the CMAT exam annually. The score is accepted by over 1300 AICTE-approved institutions across India, including well-known names like Jamnalal Bajaj Institute of Management Studies (JBIMS), Sydenham Institute of Management Studies, Research and Entrepreneurship Education (SIMSREE), PUMBA, Welingkar Institute of Management Development & Research, Great Lakes Institute of Management, and N. L. Dalmia Institute of Management Studies and Research.

## What Constitutes a 'Good' Raw Score?

The CMAT exam is graded out of a total of 400 marks. While the percentile is the final determinant, understanding what raw score corresponds to a competitive percentile can be helpful for setting preparation milestones. Historically, a raw score in the range of 200 to 250 marks is often seen as a decent performance. However, for top-tier institutions, you would typically need a significantly higher raw score to achieve a 99th percentile or above.

## CMAT Score vs. Percentile: Historical Trends

To better understand what a good score means, it's useful to look at past trends where raw scores translate into percentiles. While NTA, the organizing body, provides the official results in percentiles, various analyses of previous CMAT exams show a consistent pattern:

*Please note these are estimated ranges based on previous years' exam patterns and difficulty, and actual figures for CMAT 2026 may vary.* Achieving a 99th percentile means you are among the very top performers, which is essential for the most competitive programs.

## Expected CMAT 2026 Cut-offs for Top B-Schools

For the most sought-after management institutes, the CMAT cut-offs are consistently high. These institutes often look for candidates with a percentile of 99 or above. While not every candidate can achieve such a high percentile, aiming for it significantly increases your chances of securing a seat in these premier institutions. For other reputable colleges, a percentile in the 85-95 range can still open doors to quality MBA programs. It's always advisable to check the specific cut-offs released by individual colleges after the CMAT results are declared.

Ultimately, a good score in CMAT 2026 is one that aligns with your target colleges and helps you secure admission to your preferred MBA program.
