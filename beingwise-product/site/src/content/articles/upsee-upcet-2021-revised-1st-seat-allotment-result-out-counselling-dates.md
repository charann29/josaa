# UPSEE/ UPCET 2021: Revised 1st Seat Allotment Result (Out), Counselling, Dates

For students aspiring to pursue higher education in Uttar Pradesh, the Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test (UPCET), previously known as UPSEE, has been a crucial stepping stone. This guide provides an overview of the 2021 counselling process, focusing on the first seat allotment results and related essential information.

## Key takeaways

*   **UPCET (formerly UPSEE)** served as the gateway for admissions to various undergraduate and postgraduate programs in institutions affiliated with Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU), Lucknow, Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT), Gorakhpur, and Harcourt Butler Technical University (HBTU), Kanpur.
*   **The National Testing Agency (NTA)** was responsible for conducting the UPCET examination.
*   **Seat allotment results** for various rounds, including the first, were released online, guiding candidates through the admission process.
*   **For current admissions (2022 onwards)**, UPCET has been replaced by national-level exams like CUET, JEE Main, and NATA for most courses.

## Understanding UPCET

UPCET was a state-level entrance examination designed for admissions into a wide array of professional courses. These included undergraduate programs like BHMCT, B.Tech (Lateral Entry), B.Des, B.FAD/BFA, B.Pharm, and postgraduate programs such as MCA, M.Tech, M.Des, M.Arch, M.Pharma, and MBA. The exam was conducted in a computer-based format across multiple cities.

## UPCET 2021 Counselling and Seat Allotment

The counselling process for UPCET 2021 involved multiple rounds of seat allotment. Candidates who appeared for the exam and met the eligibility criteria participated in the online counselling, which led to the allocation of seats based on their rank and preferences. It's important for candidates to regularly check the official portal for updates on allotment results and subsequent steps.

## Eligibility Criteria for UPCET

To be eligible for UPCET, candidates generally had to meet certain criteria:

*   There was no specific age limit for appearing in the exam.
*   Candidates needed to have passed their qualifying examination from an institution located in Uttar Pradesh, or their parents must possess a domicile certificate of Uttar Pradesh.
*   Even if candidates did not pass their qualifying exam from an institution in UP, they could still be eligible if they provided their parent's domicile certificate for Uttar Pradesh.
*   Students in the final year of their qualifying examination were also permitted to appear for UPCET, provided they met other stipulated criteria.

## UPCET's Evolution: Replaced by National Exams

It is crucial for prospective students to note that the UPCET examination has been discontinued. For admissions into colleges affiliated with AKTU, MMMUT, and other state universities in Uttar Pradesh from 2022 onwards, candidates must now qualify through other national-level entrance examinations. Specifically, undergraduate courses are primarily offered through CUET, JEE Main, and NATA. For postgraduate courses such as MBA, MCA, M.Tech, MURP, M.Pharm, M.Des, and M.Arch, admissions are now facilitated through the CUET PG exam. Integrated MBA and Integrated MCA courses are offered through the CUET exam.

Staying informed about these changes is vital for planning your higher education journey in Uttar Pradesh.
