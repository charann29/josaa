# ATMA February Exam Result 2026: Release Date (OUT), Download AIMS ATMA Scorecard

The Association of Indian Management Schools (AIMS) has officially announced the results for the February 2026 ATMA examination. Students who participated in this session can now access and download their scorecards online from the official ATMA AIMS website.

## Key Takeaways

*   The ATMA February 2026 exam results were released on **February 28, 2026**.
*   The examination for this session was conducted on **February 22, 2026**.
*   Candidates need their ATMA PID number and date of birth to download their scorecard.
*   ATMA scores are accepted by numerous management institutions for MBA/PGDM and MCA programs for the 2026-28 academic year.

## How to Access Your ATMA 2026 Scorecard

Retrieving your ATMA scorecard is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to download your result:

1.  Visit the official ATMA AIMS website.
2.  Locate and click on the 'Candidate Login' section.
3.  From the dropdown menu, select the relevant ATMA exam date (February 22, 2026).
4.  Enter your unique ATMA PID number and your password (typically your date of birth).
5.  Click the 'Login' button.

Upon successful login, your ATMA scorecard will appear on the screen. It is highly recommended to download and print a copy of your scorecard for future reference and admissions processes.

## Understanding Your ATMA Scorecard

The ATMA scorecard is a crucial document that provides a comprehensive overview of your performance in the examination. It typically includes the following details:

*   Candidate's Name
*   Test Date
*   Roll Number
*   Section-wise Scaled Scores and Percentiles
*   Total Scaled Score and Overall Percentile
*   Result Validation Key
*   Valid Admission Year

Ensure all personal details on your scorecard are accurate. If you notice any discrepancies, contact AIMS immediately.

## What Happens After the ATMA Result?

Securing a good ATMA score is an important first step, but it does not guarantee direct admission. After the results are announced, participating management institutes will begin their individual selection processes. These processes typically involve:

*   **Shortlisting:** Institutes will shortlist candidates based on their ATMA scores, along with other factors like academic performance in Class 10, 12, and graduation.
*   **Further Selection Rounds:** Shortlisted candidates will usually participate in a Written Ability Test (WAT), Group Discussion (GD), and Personal Interview (PI).
*   **Final Admission:** The final admission decision is made considering a holistic profile, which includes ATMA scores, performance in WAT/GD/PI, past academic records, work experience (if any), and sometimes academic and gender diversity.

It is essential to research the specific admission criteria and cutoff scores of the institutes you are interested in applying to. The ATMA scores are valid for admissions for the 2026-28 academic session.
