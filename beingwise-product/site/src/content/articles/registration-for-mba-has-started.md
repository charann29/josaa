# When Registration for MBA Has Started?

Planning your MBA journey in India involves keeping track of various entrance exams and their registration timelines. This guide focuses on the Odisha Joint Entrance Examination (OJEE) for MBA admissions, providing clarity on when you can expect to register.

## Key Takeaways

*   OJEE is a state-level entrance exam for various postgraduate programs, including MBA/PGDM.
*   Registration for OJEE 2024 is anticipated to commence in the second week of April 2024.
*   The OJEE 2024 examination is expected to be conducted in the last week of June 2024.
*   The exam is administered in a computer-based format across different papers.

## Understanding OJEE for MBA Admissions

The Odisha Joint Entrance Examination (OJEE) is a significant state-level entrance test in India. It is conducted by the OJEE Cell to facilitate admissions into a range of professional courses. For those aspiring to pursue an MBA or PGDM, OJEE is one of the pathways to various colleges within Odisha.

## OJEE Exam Format and Courses Offered

OJEE is a computer-based test (CBT) designed to assess candidates for different programs. Beyond MBA/PGDM, the examination also caters to admissions for B.Pharm, MCA, and M.Tech courses. The specific papers vary depending on the course a candidate is applying for, ensuring a tailored evaluation process.

## Anticipated OJEE Registration and Exam Dates

While the official OJEE Cell portal will release the precise dates, based on previous trends, prospective MBA students can expect the registration window for OJEE 2024 to open around the second week of April 2024. Following the registration, the examination itself is typically scheduled towards the last week of June 2024. It is always advisable to regularly check the official OJEE portal for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding application deadlines and exam schedules.

Staying informed about these timelines is crucial for a smooth application process and to ensure you don't miss out on an opportunity to pursue your postgraduate studies.
