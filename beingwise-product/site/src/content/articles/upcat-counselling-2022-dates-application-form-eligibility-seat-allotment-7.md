# UPCAT Counselling 2022: Dates, Application Form, Eligibility, Seat Allotment

The Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test (UPCET), previously known as UPSEE, was a state-level examination for admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate programs across universities in Uttar Pradesh. However, for 2022 admissions and onwards, the UPCET exam has been discontinued.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Exam Discontinued:** The UPCET exam (formerly UPSEE) is no longer conducted for admissions in Uttar Pradesh.
*   **New Admission Process:** Admissions to programs previously covered by UPCET are now facilitated through national-level exams like CUET, JEE Main, and NATA.
*   **Participating Universities:** Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU), Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology (MMMUT), and Harcourt Butler Technical University (HBTU) are among the key institutions.
*   **Diverse Programs:** A wide range of courses, from B.Tech and B.Pharm to MBA and M.Tech, are offered through these new entrance pathways.

## Understanding the Shift from UPCET

For the 2022 academic year and beyond, the National Testing Agency (NTA) no longer conducts the UPCET examination. Instead, students aspiring for admission to affiliated colleges under AKTU, MMMUT, and other state universities in Uttar Pradesh must apply through other established national entrance tests. This change aims to streamline the admission process and align it with national standards.

### Which Exams Replace UPCET?

*   **Undergraduate Courses:** For programs like B.Tech, B.Des, B.Pharm, BHMCT, and BFA, admissions are now primarily based on scores from JEE Main, CUET (for integrated courses like Integrated MBA and Integrated MCA), and NATA (for B.Arch programs, though not explicitly mentioned for UPCET, it's a common architecture entrance).
*   **Postgraduate Courses:** For master's programs such as MBA, MCA, M.Tech, M.Pharm, M.Arch, M.Des, and MURP, candidates will need to take the CUET PG exam.

## Eligibility Criteria for Uttar Pradesh Admissions

While the UPCET exam itself is no longer held, the general eligibility principles for state admissions remain important. These typically revolve around academic qualifications and domicile status.

*   **Academic Qualification:** Candidates must have passed the relevant qualifying examination (e.g., 10+2 for undergraduate or a bachelor's degree for postgraduate) from a recognized institution. Those in their final year awaiting results are often provisionally eligible.
*   **Domicile Requirement:** Generally, candidates should have passed their qualifying examination from an institution located in Uttar Pradesh. Alternatively, if they studied outside UP, their parents must hold a domicile certificate for Uttar Pradesh. There are often provisions for candidates who do not meet the domicile criteria but fulfill all other requirements.
*   **No Age Limit:** Historically, there has been no upper age limit for appearing in these entrance examinations for admission to various programs.

## Counselling and Seat Allotment Process

With the discontinuation of UPCET, the counselling and seat allotment process now follows the procedures of the respective national-level examinations. For example, for JEE Main, candidates participate in JoSAA counselling or state-level counselling based on their JEE Main scores. Similarly, CUET and CUET PG scores will be used by participating universities to conduct their own counselling rounds for seat allocation.

Students are advised to regularly check the official websites of AKTU, MMMUT, HBTU, and other universities they are interested in, as well as the official portals for CUET, JEE Main, and NATA, for specific counselling schedules, application forms, and seat allotment details.

The range of professional courses for which admissions are now facilitated through CUET, JEE Main, and NATA is extensive. Here's a look at some of the popular programs:

*   B.Des
*   BFAD/BFA
*   BHMCT
*   MCA/MCA (Integrated)/MCA (Lateral Entry)
*   MBA/MBA (Integrated)

Navigating university admissions in Uttar Pradesh now requires attention to national entrance exams. Stay informed by checking official university and exam body websites for the most current information regarding application, eligibility, and counselling procedures.
