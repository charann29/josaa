# AIMA MAT May Exam 2026; Dates (OUT), Application Form (Started), Syllabus, Preparation Tips

The Management Aptitude Test (MAT) is a national-level entrance examination for admission to MBA and allied postgraduate management programs. Conducted by the All India Management Association (AIMA), the May 2026 session is now open for applications, offering both Paper-Based Test (PBT) and Computer-Based Test (CBT) options.

## Key takeaways

*   The MAT May 2026 examination will be conducted on May 31 (PBT) and June 14 (CBT).
*   Registration for both modes has commenced, with application forms available on the official AIMA MAT website.
*   The application fee for a single session (PBT or CBT) is INR 2,300, while appearing for both costs INR 4,000.
*   Over 800 MBA colleges across India accept MAT scores for admission.

## Understanding the MAT Exam

MAT is held four times a year – in February, May, September, and December – providing multiple opportunities for aspiring management students. Each phase typically includes both a PBT and a CBT option, allowing candidates to choose the format that suits them best. The exam assesses a candidate's general aptitudes, crucial for success in management studies.

Here's a quick overview of the MAT exam:

| MAT Exam Features | Details |
| :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------ |
| Full Form         | Management Aptitude Test (MAT)                  |
| Conducting Body   | All India Management Association (AIMA)         |
| Exam Level        | National                                        |
| Exam Frequency    | Four times a year (Feb, May, Sept, Dec)         |
| Exam Mode         | Computer-Based Test (CBT) and Paper-Based Test (PBT) |
| Exam Fees         | INR 2,300 (single mode) / INR 4,000 (both modes) |
| Exam Purpose      | Admission to approximately 800 MBA colleges     |

## Eligibility Criteria for MAT May 2026

The MAT exam has straightforward eligibility requirements, making it accessible to a wide range of candidates. To be eligible, applicants must meet the following conditions:

*   Hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university.
*   There is no minimum percentage requirement; candidates with just passing marks in their graduation are eligible.
*   Students in their final year of graduation are also welcome to apply.
*   Professionals holding degrees like CA (Chartered Accountancy), CS (Company Secretaryship), or ICWA (Institute of Cost Accountants of India) are also eligible to appear for the exam.

## MAT Application Process 2026

The application form for the MAT May 2026 session is available exclusively online on the official AIMA MAT website. Follow these steps to complete your registration:

1.  Visit the official AIMA MAT website.
2.  Click on the 'Register' option to begin the process.
3.  Provide your basic details and set a password.
4.  Verify your phone number using the One-Time Password (OTP) received.
5.  Log in with your credentials to access the application form.
6.  Fill in all required details accurately.
7.  Pay the application fee using a debit card, credit card, or net banking. The fee is non-refundable.
8.  Select your preferred exam mode (PBT, CBT, or both) and choose your desired test cities.
9.  Upload your recent photograph and signature as per the specified guidelines.
10. Select up to five management institutes to which you wish your scores to be sent.

## Preparing for MAT May 2026

Effective preparation is key to scoring well in the MAT exam. Candidates should familiarize themselves with the exam pattern and syllabus, which typically covers Language Comprehension, Mathematical Skills, Data Analysis & Sufficiency, Intelligence & Critical Reasoning, and Indian & Global Environment. While the Indian & Global Environment section is not used for percentile calculation, it is important for overall knowledge.

Consider these preparation tips:

*   **Understand the Syllabus:** Go through the detailed syllabus available on the official AIMA MAT website for each section.
*   **Practice Regularly:** Solve previous year's question papers and take mock tests to get accustomed to the exam format and time constraints.
*   **Focus on Weak Areas:** Identify subjects or topics where you struggle and dedicate extra time to improve them.
*   **Time Management:** Develop a strategy to manage your time effectively during the exam, ensuring you attempt all sections.
*   **Stay Updated:** For the Indian & Global Environment section, keep abreast of current affairs, business news, and general knowledge.

By staying informed and preparing diligently, you can maximize your chances of securing admission to a reputable MBA program through the MAT May 2026 exam.
