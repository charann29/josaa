# ATMA Exam Cut off 2026 (Expected): Check Category-wise ATMA Cut off Marks

The ATMA exam is a national-level entrance test for admissions into various postgraduate management programs like MBA and PGDM, as well as MCA courses, across numerous colleges in India. The ATMA cutoff for 2026 will be determined and released independently by each participating institution following the announcement of the ATMA 2026 results.

## Key takeaways

*   Over 500 MBA colleges across India are expected to accept ATMA scores for their 2026 admissions cycle.
*   Each college sets its own specific ATMA cutoff, which can vary by program and category.
*   Meeting the cutoff is a prerequisite for advancing to subsequent selection rounds, typically including Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI).
*   While some colleges consider sectional scores, many base their cutoff solely on the overall ATMA percentile.

## Understanding ATMA Cutoffs

Unlike some other national exams, there isn't a single, uniform ATMA cutoff. Instead, each of the hundreds of colleges that accept ATMA scores publishes its own minimum percentile requirement. These cutoffs are usually made available on the individual college's official website. Candidates who achieve a score equal to or higher than the stated cutoff for their chosen program at a particular institution become eligible for the next stages of the admission process.

## Factors Influencing ATMA Cutoff 2026

Several elements contribute to how ATMA cutoffs are determined each year. Colleges consider a combination of these factors to set their minimum eligibility criteria:

*   **Exam Difficulty:** The overall difficulty level of the ATMA 2026 examination can impact average scores and, consequently, the cutoff percentiles.
*   **Number of Applicants:** A higher number of candidates appearing for the exam generally leads to increased competition and potentially higher cutoffs.
*   **Available Seats:** The total number of seats offered by a participating institute for a specific program plays a crucial role. Fewer seats often result in higher cutoffs.
*   **Past Trends:** Historical cutoff data from previous years is a significant indicator for institutes when setting current year cutoffs.
*   **Marking Scheme:** The scoring pattern and any negative marking in the ATMA exam can influence overall candidate performance.
*   **Reservation Policies:** Institutes adhering to government-mandated reservation policies will have different cutoffs for various categories (e.g., General, SC, ST, OBC, EWS).
*   **Applicant Performance:** The range of scores achieved by all candidates who took the ATMA exam, particularly the lowest and average scores, also helps shape the cutoff.

## ATMA 2026 Exam Dates and Schedule

It's essential for aspiring candidates to be aware of the key dates for the ATMA exam sessions. For the 2026 cycle, the ATMA result for the second session is scheduled to be announced on **May 9, 2026**. The result for the first session was declared on February 28, 2026. Keep an eye on the official ATMA website for any updates or further session announcements.

## Expected ATMA Cutoffs for Top Colleges

While specific 2026 cutoffs will only be released by the institutes, based on past trends, top-tier colleges typically look for higher ATMA percentiles. For instance, renowned institutes like PUMBA, Pune, often have very competitive cutoffs, generally ranging from 95 to 99 percentile. It's important to note that some highly sought-after institutions, such as JBIMS, Mumbai, do not accept ATMA scores for their MMS admissions, instead preferring scores from exams like CAT, MAH MBA CET, or GMAT.

However, many quality institutions accept ATMA scores in the 70-75 percentile range, particularly private colleges. These institutes sometimes conduct multiple counseling rounds, where cutoffs might adjust. Some examples of colleges that have historically accepted candidates with 70 percentile and above include Fortune Institute of International Business (FIIB), New Delhi; Institute of Public Enterprise (IPE), Hyderabad; XIME, Bangalore; AIMS School of Business; and Kristu Jayanti College (KJC).

Meeting the ATMA cutoff is just the first step towards securing your management education. Prepare thoroughly for both the exam and the subsequent admission rounds to maximize your chances.
