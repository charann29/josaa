# Is it true that the KCET rank cutoff displayed by the colleges are not true and that it varies in extended round because I got 26,315 rank and I want GIT BELGAUM ENC Branch but professors are assuming that I might not get and my friends are telling that I will get it easily.So whom should I trust??

It's completely understandable to feel confused when you're getting different opinions about your KCET admission chances. Let's clear up how KCET cutoffs work, especially in the later rounds, to help you make sense of your situation.

## Key Takeaways

*   KCET cutoff ranks are dynamic and can change, particularly in the extended rounds.
*   Initial cutoff lists reflect seat filling in the main rounds, but withdrawals create new opportunities.
*   The extent of cutoff variation depends on factors like student withdrawals and branch popularity each year.
*   Neither professors nor friends can predict with certainty; rely on official data and understand the process.

## Understanding KCET Cutoffs

When colleges release their initial KCET cutoff ranks, these figures represent the last rank admitted into a particular course and college during the primary counselling rounds. These cutoffs are a snapshot based on the choices made by students who participated early in the process and secured their seats. They are a good indicator of demand and competition at that specific point in time.

## The Role of Extended Rounds

The admission process doesn't always end with the first few rounds. Many students, after securing a seat, might decide to withdraw for various reasons. They might get admission into a different course or college they preferred, perhaps through COMEDK, JEE Main, or even an out-of-state entrance exam. Some might upgrade to a different branch within the same college, or choose another institution altogether. When these students withdraw, their previously held seats become vacant. These vacant seats are then made available in subsequent rounds, often referred to as extended or supplementary rounds.

### How Cutoffs Change

Because new seats open up in the extended rounds, colleges can admit candidates who have ranks lower than the initial cutoff. This means if the initial cutoff for a particular branch was, say, 25,000, it might extend to 27,000 or even lower in a later round, depending on how many students withdraw from that specific branch. This phenomenon is why your friends might be optimistic, having seen it happen in previous years.

## Why Predictions Vary

Your professors are likely cautious because the extent to which cutoffs drop in extended rounds isn't fixed. It varies significantly each year. In some years, a large number of students might withdraw, leading to a substantial drop in cutoffs. In other years, fewer students might withdraw, or the demand for a specific branch might remain exceptionally high, resulting in only a minor change in the cutoff. Professors, having observed this variability over many years, tend to err on the side of caution. Your friends, on the other hand, might be recalling particularly favorable years or specific instances where cutoffs dropped significantly, leading to their optimism.

## Your Chances for GIT Belgaum ENC Branch

With your rank of 26,315 and your interest in the Electronics and Communication Engineering (ENC) branch at GIT Belgaum, it's certainly within the realm of possibility that you could secure a seat in an extended round. The key will be the number of students who withdraw from that specific branch at GIT Belgaum. Keep a close watch on the official KCET counselling updates and participate in all extended rounds. While no one can guarantee an outcome, understanding the process helps you navigate it effectively.

Stay informed by regularly checking the official KCET portal for announcements regarding extended rounds and seat matrix updates. Good luck with your admission journey!
