# I am General Category and No Domicile Certificate. How Many Percentile and Marks Required for VJTI Admission?

Securing a spot at Veermata Jijabai Technological Institute (VJTI), Mumbai, without a Maharashtra domicile certificate and as a general category applicant, primarily relies on your performance in the JEE Main examination. This guide will help you understand the expectations.

## Key Takeaways

*   Admission for non-domicile, general category students at VJTI is highly competitive and primarily through JEE Main scores.
*   Aim for a very high JEE Main percentile, often above 99.5, for branches like Computer Engineering, Information Technology, or Electronics and Communication Engineering.
*   A JEE Main score of 190+ is a good starting point, with 250-270+ marks often needed to achieve the highest percentiles for top branches.
*   Always refer to the official VJTI and Directorate of Technical Education (DTE) Maharashtra websites for the most current admission details and cutoffs.

## Eligibility Criteria

To be considered for admission to VJTI through the All India Quota, you must have successfully completed your 12th standard with a minimum aggregate of 45% in subjects such as Physics and Mathematics. Crucially, you must also possess a valid JEE Main score, as this will be the primary factor for your admission consideration.

## JEE Main Score and Percentile Expectations

Your JEE Main performance is the critical determinant for admission to VJTI as a non-domicile, general category student. Historically, the cutoffs for popular engineering branches have been exceptionally high.

For instance, in 2023, the closing percentile for B.Tech. in Computer Engineering was approximately 99.9, while for Information Technology, it stood around 99.85. For branches like Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE), non-domicile candidates typically need to aim for a JEE Main percentile of 99.5 or higher.

To achieve such high percentiles, a significant score in JEE Main is necessary. While the exact marks-to-percentile conversion varies each year based on exam difficulty and candidate performance, aiming for a score above 190 out of 300 is a good general target. For the most competitive branches and to secure a percentile of 99.5 or above, candidates often need to score in the range of 250-270 marks out of 300.

## Understanding Cutoff Trends

It's important to note that admission cutoffs fluctuate annually. These variations are influenced by several factors, including the total number of applicants, the overall performance of candidates in JEE Main, and the number of seats available under the All India Quota. However, the consistent trend for general category, non-domicile candidates is the requirement for an extremely high percentile.

For context, while VJTI also considers MHT CET scores, the All India Quota admissions are primarily based on JEE Main. If we look at MHT CET closing ranks for ECE in 2023, the 'Other State' (non-domicile) general category had a closing rank around 1,136, which was more competitive than the 'Home State' rank of 1,471. This illustrates the higher bar for non-domicile applicants across different admission channels.

## Next Steps for Aspiring Candidates

1.  **JEE Main Registration and Examination:** Ensure you are registered for the JEE Main exam and prepare thoroughly to achieve your best possible score.
2.  **Post-Result Counseling:** After your JEE Main results are declared, closely monitor the official VJTI website and the Directorate of Technical Education (DTE) Maharashtra portal for announcements regarding the counseling process.
3.  **Document Preparation:** Begin gathering all necessary documents for verification well in advance of the counseling period.

Staying informed by regularly visiting the official VJTI website is crucial, as admission procedures and cutoff marks are subject to change each year.
