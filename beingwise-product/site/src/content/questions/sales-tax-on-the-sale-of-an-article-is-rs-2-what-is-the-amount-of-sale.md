# 4% Sales Tax on an Article is Rs 2: What is the Amount of Sale?

Understanding how sales tax works is a fundamental skill, whether you're a student preparing for exams or a parent managing household finances. This article breaks down a common scenario to help you easily calculate the original sale amount when you know the tax percentage and the tax value.

## Key Takeaways

*   Sales tax is a percentage of the selling price of an item.
*   To find the original sale amount, you can work backward from the tax paid and the tax rate.
*   This calculation is useful in various real-world situations, from shopping to business transactions.
*   Always ensure you're using the correct tax percentage for accurate results.

## Understanding Sales Tax

Sales tax is an indirect tax levied by governments on the sale of goods and services. It's usually added to the price of an item at the point of sale. The percentage varies depending on the region and the type of product. For example, when you buy a new appliance, the final price you pay includes the sales tax calculated on the item's base price.

In our specific problem, we are told that the sales tax on an article is 4%, and this tax amount comes out to be Rs 2. Our goal is to figure out what the original price of the article was before this tax was added.

## Calculating the Amount of Sale

Let's consider the unknown original sale amount as 'X'. We know that 4% of this 'X' is equal to Rs 2. We can set up a simple equation to solve this:

*   **Step 1: Express the percentage as a decimal or fraction.**
    4% can be written as 4/100 or 0.04.

*   **Step 2: Formulate the equation.**
    If 4% of X is Rs 2, then:
    (4/100) * X = 2

*   **Step 3: Solve for X.**
    To isolate X, we multiply both sides of the equation by 100 and then divide by 4:
    X = (2 * 100) / 4
    X = 200 / 4
    X = 50

So, the amount of the sale before tax was Rs 50.

## Verifying Your Answer

It's always a good practice to check your calculation. If the original sale amount was Rs 50, let's calculate 4% of Rs 50:

4% of 50 = (4/100) * 50
= 0.04 * 50
= 2

This matches the given sales tax amount of Rs 2, confirming our calculation is correct. The total price paid for the article, including tax, would be Rs 50 (original price) + Rs 2 (tax) = Rs 52.

## Practical Applications

This type of calculation isn't just for textbooks. For instance, if you see an item with a price tag that says "tax included" and then the receipt shows the tax amount separately, you could use this method to figure out the original base price. Similarly, businesses use such calculations to separate the base price from the tax component for accounting purposes.

By following these steps, you can confidently solve problems involving sales tax and original sale amounts.
