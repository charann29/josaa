# A Shopkeeper's Profit: Understanding the Numbers

When a shopkeeper buys an item and sells it for a higher price, they make a profit. Let's break down a simple example to understand how profit and profit percentage are calculated.

## Key Takeaways

*   Profit is the difference between the selling price and the cost price.
*   A positive profit indicates that the item was sold for more than it was bought.
*   Profit percentage shows the profit as a proportion of the original cost, expressed as a percentage.
*   Understanding these calculations is fundamental for any business, big or small.

## Calculating the Profit

Imagine a shopkeeper buys a pen. This is known as the **Cost Price**. In our example, the cost price of the pen is ₹50.

The shopkeeper then sells this pen to a customer. This is called the **Selling Price**. For this particular pen, the selling price is ₹8,470.

To find the profit, we simply subtract the cost price from the selling price:

*   **Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price**
*   Profit = ₹8,470 - ₹50
*   Profit = ₹8,420

So, the shopkeeper made a profit of ₹8,420 on the sale of this single pen. This is a substantial amount, showing a very successful transaction.

## Calculating the Profit Percentage

While knowing the exact profit amount is useful, understanding the profit as a percentage of the original cost gives a clearer picture of the markup. This is called the **Profit Percentage**.

To calculate the profit percentage, we use the following formula:

*   **Profit Percentage = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100**

Let's plug in our numbers:

*   Profit Percentage = (₹8,420 / ₹50) × 100
*   Profit Percentage = 168.4 × 100
*   Profit Percentage = 16,840%

## What This Percentage Means

A profit percentage of 16,840% is exceptionally high. It means the shopkeeper sold the pen for 168.4 times its original cost! This kind of percentage is unusual for most everyday retail items and suggests either a unique item, a significant value addition, or a very specific market condition that allowed for such a markup.
