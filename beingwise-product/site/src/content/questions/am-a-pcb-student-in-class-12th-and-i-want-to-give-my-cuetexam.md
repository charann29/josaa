As a Class 12 student with a PCB background, understanding the right CUET subject combination is crucial for gaining admission to top universities like Delhi University (DU) and Banaras Hindu University (BHU). Let's explore whether your chosen subjects of Biology, Chemistry, and Agriculture will make you eligible.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your Biology, Chemistry, and Agriculture combination is generally suitable for B.Sc. Agriculture at BHU.
*   For most B.Sc. Biology group courses at BHU, the standard requirement is Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
*   Delhi University (DU) often requires Physics, Chemistry, and Biology for its B.Sc. Life Science programs, along with a language.
*   Adding Physics to your CUET domain subjects, even as a PCB student, can significantly broaden your options for various B.Sc. courses at both DU and BHU.

## Eligibility for Banaras Hindu University (BHU)

BHU offers a range of science courses, and your eligibility depends on how well your CUET subject combination aligns with their specific program requirements. For B.Sc. Agriculture, BHU accepts several combinations, including:

*   Physics + Chemistry + Biology
*   Physics + Chemistry + Agriculture
*   Physics + Chemistry + Mathematics

Given your chosen combination of Biology, Chemistry, and Agriculture, you would indeed be eligible for B.Sc. Agriculture at BHU, as it matches one of their approved subject sets. Remember that meeting the minimum Class 12 marks requirement set by BHU is also essential.

For B.Sc. courses within the Biology group (e.g., B.Sc. Biology, B.Sc. Zoology, Botany), BHU typically requires Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as your domain subjects. Since you already have Biology and Chemistry, including Physics in your CUET combination would make you eligible for these programs as well.

## Eligibility for Delhi University (DU)

Delhi University has specific and often stringent CUET subject combination requirements for its undergraduate programs. For most B.Sc. Life Science courses at DU, the preferred combination is:

*   Physics + Chemistry + Biology

Additionally, DU mandates that you include at least one language, such as English or Hindi, in your CUET examination. Therefore, if your only science domain subjects are Biology, Chemistry, and Agriculture, this might not be sufficient for many B.Sc. programs at DU, as they commonly look for Physics along with Chemistry and Biology.

## Should You Change Your Subject Combination?

As a PCB student, Biology, Chemistry, and Physics are your core subjects. While substituting Physics with Agriculture for CUET can open doors to specific programs like B.Sc. Agriculture at BHU, it might limit your options for other B.Sc. courses, particularly at Delhi University. Many universities consider Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as the standard domain subjects for a wide array of science and life science programs.

If your primary goal is to maximize your eligibility for a broader range of B.Sc. courses at both BHU and DU, it is generally advisable to include Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as your main domain subjects in CUET. You can certainly add Agriculture as an additional domain subject if you have a strong interest in B.Sc. Agriculture programs. This approach ensures you meet the requirements for most biology-related courses while also qualifying for agriculture-specific programs.

Consider the specific course preferences and requirements listed in the official information bulletins released by NTA and the respective universities before finalizing your CUET subject choices.
