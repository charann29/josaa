# I Got Rank 861668 in NEET 2025: What Are My Options for BDS Deemed Colleges?

Understanding your options after NEET 2025, especially with a rank of 861668, is crucial for making informed decisions. While the initial round of counselling might not have yielded an allotment for your preferred BDS deemed colleges, there are still avenues to explore and important considerations for your future.

## Key Takeaways

*   A NEET rank of 861668 makes securing a BDS seat in deemed universities through regular counselling very challenging due to typically high cut-offs.
*   It's wise to explore all available options, including management or NRI quotas, and alternative courses.
*   Planning a backup strategy now is more effective than waiting solely for subsequent counselling rounds.
*   For future admissions, consider registering for counselling in other states to broaden your opportunities.

## Understanding Deemed University Cut-offs for BDS

Deemed universities, while private, participate in central counselling conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC). These institutions often have high cut-offs for BDS programs, even under the general category. With a rank like 861668, it's highly improbable to secure a seat in sought-after deemed colleges such as Pravara Institute of Medical Sciences (Loni), Bharati Vidyapeeth Deemed University (Sangli), Datta Meghe Institute of Medical Sciences (Wardha), or Krishna Institute of Medical Sciences (Karad) through the standard counselling process. The advice from your counsellor, suggesting no chance in Round 2, likely stems from historical cut-off trends and the sheer volume of candidates with higher ranks.

## Exploring Management and NRI Quota Seats

While general counselling might be difficult, some private dental colleges, including certain deemed universities, offer seats under management quota or Non-Resident Indian (NRI) quota. These seats typically have different eligibility criteria and significantly higher fee structures compared to general category seats. If financial considerations permit, it's worth directly contacting the admissions departments of these colleges to inquire about the availability and process for these specific quotas. Be aware that the competition for these seats can also be intense, and the fees are a substantial commitment.

## Planning for Future Counselling and Other States

For future NEET cycles, if you decide to reappear, it is highly advisable to register for counselling in other states. Many states conduct their own counselling processes for private colleges within their jurisdiction, even for candidates from outside the state. By not registering for other state counselling this year, you limited your potential pool of colleges significantly. Broadening your scope to include private colleges in various states can substantially increase your chances of securing a seat in future attempts.

## Considering Alternative Health Sciences Courses

If pursuing a BDS degree this year seems unfeasible, it's a good time to explore other valuable and in-demand health science courses. These programs offer excellent career prospects and can be a fulfilling alternative to dentistry. Some popular options include:

*   **B.Sc. Nursing:** A foundational degree for a career in nursing, with diverse specializations and strong job demand.
*   **Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT):** Focuses on physical rehabilitation, helping patients recover from injuries or illnesses.
*   **B.Sc. Life Sciences:** A broad undergraduate degree that can lead to careers in research, pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, or further studies in various medical fields.

These courses are vital to the healthcare ecosystem and provide rewarding professional paths. Researching their curricula, career prospects, and admission requirements could open up new possibilities for your academic journey.

## Making an Informed Decision

It's important to weigh all your options carefully. While reappearing for NEET is one path, exploring alternative health science courses or considering management/NRI quotas are also valid strategies. Making a well-thought-out decision now will help you move forward confidently.
