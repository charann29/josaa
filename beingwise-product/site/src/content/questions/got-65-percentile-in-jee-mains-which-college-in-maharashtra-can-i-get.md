# I got 65 percentile in JEE Mains. Which college in Maharashtra can I get?

Securing a 65 percentile in JEE Mains can feel a bit uncertain when aiming for top engineering colleges. This guide will help you understand your options for B.Tech admissions in Maharashtra, especially if you belong to the General category, as specific caste information wasn't provided.

## Key Takeaways

*   A 65 percentile in JEE Mains generally places you outside the cutoff for NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.
*   Opportunities are more likely in private engineering colleges across Maharashtra.
*   Consider colleges affiliated with state universities, which may have different admission criteria.
*   Always verify cutoffs for your specific category and preferred branch directly with the college or through official state admission portals.

## Understanding Your JEE Main Score and Admissions

Your JEE Main percentile reflects your performance relative to other candidates. While 65 percentile indicates you've outperformed 65% of test-takers, admission to highly sought-after institutions like the National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), or Centrally Funded Technical Institutes (GFTIs) typically requires a significantly higher percentile, often above 90-95, especially for the General category. For these institutions, admission is handled through the official JoSAA portal.

## Maharashtra State Admissions and JEE Main

Maharashtra has its own state-level engineering admission process, often referred to as MHT CET CAP rounds. While MHT CET is the primary entrance exam for state colleges, a certain percentage of seats (usually 15%) in private unaided engineering colleges are reserved for candidates who have appeared for JEE Main. These are known as All India Seats. Admissions to these seats are based on your JEE Main scores. The Directorate of Technical Education (DTE), Maharashtra, manages this process.

## Potential Colleges in Maharashtra

Given a 65 percentile, your best prospects will likely be in private engineering colleges within Maharashtra. These institutions offer a wide range of B.Tech programs and often have more accessible cutoffs for JEE Main scores. It's important to research each college's specific admission criteria, available branches, and previous year's cutoffs.

Here are some examples of colleges that might be within reach, based on historical trends for General category candidates with similar percentiles. This list is illustrative and not exhaustive, and cutoffs can vary year to year.

*   Anantrao Pawar College of Engineering and Research, Pune
*   Shree Ramchandra College of Engineering, Pune
*   Trinity Academy of Engineering, Pune
*   Sahyadri Valley College of Engineering and Technology, Rajuri
*   Dr. D.Y. Patil College of Engineering and Innovation, Pune

When exploring these and other colleges, look at their affiliation (e.g., Savitribai Phule Pune University, Mumbai University), NIRF rankings if available, and the specific B.Tech programs they offer. Always check the official admission brochures published by the colleges or the DTE Maharashtra for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding cutoffs and eligibility.

## Next Steps for Aspiring Engineers

To maximize your chances, thoroughly research the admission processes for Maharashtra's engineering colleges. Keep an eye on the DTE Maharashtra website for announcements regarding counseling schedules and application procedures for JEE Main qualified candidates. Prepare all necessary documents and apply strategically to colleges where your percentile aligns with their admission criteria. Remember, many good colleges can provide an excellent education and career foundation.
