# I'm from Delhi, I want to get admission in GMC Nagpur for BPT course....can I get it?

It's completely understandable to consider options like Government Medical College (GMC) Nagpur for your Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) degree, even if you're based in Delhi. Let's break down the possibilities and requirements for students from outside Maharashtra.

## Key Takeaways

*   Yes, students from Delhi can apply for BPT at GMC Nagpur.
*   Admission is primarily through the NEET UG score.
*   You'll compete for seats under the All India Quota (AIQ).
*   Meeting the 12th-grade science stream requirement is essential.

## Eligibility for BPT at GMC Nagpur

To be eligible for the BPT course at Government Medical College, Nagpur, you must have successfully completed your 12th standard (or equivalent) from a recognized board. The crucial part is that your 12th-grade subjects must include Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB).

This fundamental academic requirement applies to all applicants, regardless of their state of origin. So, if you've passed your 12th with a science stream from a board recognized in Delhi or anywhere else in India, you meet this initial criterion.

## Understanding Admission Quotas

For a central institution like GMC Nagpur, admission to various courses, including BPT, typically involves different quotas. As a student from Delhi, you would primarily be looking at the All India Quota (AIQ) seats.

*   **All India Quota (AIQ):** A certain percentage (often 15%) of seats in government medical colleges across India are reserved for candidates from all over the country. Admission to these seats is based on your performance in the national entrance examination.
*   **State Quota:** The remaining seats are usually reserved for candidates who fulfill the domicile requirements of Maharashtra. As you are from Delhi, these seats would generally not be available to you unless you meet specific Maharashtra domicile criteria, which is usually not the case for students living in Delhi.

## The Role of NEET UG

Admission to BPT courses in many government institutions, including GMC Nagpur, is increasingly linked to the NEET UG examination. While NEET is primarily known for MBBS and BDS admissions, many allied health science courses, like BPT, also use NEET UG scores for their merit list generation. You will need to appear for NEET UG and achieve a competitive score to be considered for admission under the All India Quota.

Keep an eye on the official admission brochures released by GMC Nagpur or the Maharashtra Common Entrance Test (MHT CET) cell for the specific year you are applying, as they will clarify whether NEET UG scores are mandatory or if there's an alternative entrance exam for BPT.

## Application Process for Out-of-State Candidates

When applying, you will need to follow the specific instructions for All India Quota candidates. This usually involves participating in the national-level counseling process conducted by the Medical Counseling Committee (MCC) or a similar designated authority, depending on how BPT admissions are being managed for that academic year. Ensure you register as an All India Quota candidate during the application and counseling phases.

Make sure to regularly check the official websites of GMC Nagpur and the relevant counseling authorities for the latest updates on application forms, dates, and detailed eligibility criteria for the upcoming academic year. Persistence and attention to detail during the application process are key.
