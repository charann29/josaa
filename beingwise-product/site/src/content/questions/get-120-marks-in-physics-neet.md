# How to Score 120+ in NEET Physics

Cracking the NEET Physics section with a score of 120 or more is a realistic goal with the right strategy and consistent effort. This guide will help you navigate your preparation to achieve that target.

## Key Takeaways

*   Master the foundational concepts and formulas from the NCERT textbooks.
*   Dedicate time daily to practicing a variety of numerical problems.
*   Prioritize high-weightage topics like Mechanics, Modern Physics, and Electrostatics.
*   Regularly analyze your errors and revise concepts to solidify your understanding.

## Understanding the NEET Physics Landscape

Physics often feels like the most challenging section for many NEET aspirants. However, it's also highly scoring if approached systematically. The NEET exam, conducted by the NTA, primarily tests your understanding of fundamental principles and your ability to apply them to solve problems. The questions are generally direct, either concept-based or requiring formula application, making thorough NCERT comprehension crucial.

## Mastering NCERT Concepts and Formulas

The NCERT textbooks are your primary resource for NEET Physics. Every concept, definition, and solved example within these books must be understood thoroughly. Create a dedicated notebook for formulas, derivations, and important points from each chapter. Regularly reviewing this notebook will help you recall information quickly during practice and the actual exam. Don't just memorize; strive to understand the 'why' behind each concept.

## Strategic Practice and Error Analysis

Consistent practice is non-negotiable. Solve a diverse range of numerical problems daily, moving from basic to more complex ones. After attempting a set of problems, critically analyze your mistakes. Was it a conceptual error, a calculation mistake, or a time management issue? Identifying the root cause helps you avoid repeating the same errors. For instance, if you struggle with vector analysis, revisit that specific topic before moving on.

## Focusing on High-Weightage Topics

To optimize your score, it's wise to allocate more time to topics that historically carry higher weightage in the NEET exam. Based on past trends, areas like Mechanics, Modern Physics, and Electrostatics/Current Electricity are frequently tested. While it's important to cover the entire syllabus, dedicating extra effort to these sections can significantly boost your score. Reviewing previous years' question papers can help you identify these recurring themes.

## The Role of Mock Tests and Revision

Regularly taking timed mock tests is vital. They simulate the actual exam environment, helping you improve your speed, accuracy, and time management skills. After each mock test, analyze your performance, paying close attention to questions you answered incorrectly or took too long to solve. Consistent revision of all topics, especially your weak areas, is equally important. Spaced repetition can be an effective technique here.

By following these structured steps, you can significantly enhance your NEET Physics preparation and confidently aim for a score of 120 or more.
