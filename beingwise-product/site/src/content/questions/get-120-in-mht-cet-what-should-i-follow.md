# How to get 120 in MHT CET: What should I follow?

Aiming for a score of 120 in the MHT CET is a commendable goal that can open doors to excellent engineering and pharmacy programs in Maharashtra. Achieving this score requires a strategic and consistent approach to your preparation.

## Key Takeaways

*   Thoroughly understand the MHT CET exam pattern, syllabus, and marking scheme from official sources.
*   Prioritize high-weightage topics across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology to maximize your score.
*   Develop strong time management skills to attempt the entire paper, leveraging the absence of negative marking.
*   Focus on consistent revision of learned material, especially as the exam date approaches, avoiding new topics at the last minute.

## Understand the Exam Inside Out

Before you even begin studying, it's crucial to have a complete grasp of the MHT CET examination. This includes understanding the syllabus for both Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) or Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) groups, the paper pattern, and the marking scheme. Familiarize yourself with details such as the number of questions per subject, the duration of each paper, and the fact that there is no negative marking. This comprehensive understanding, available on the official MHT CET portal, will form the bedrock of your preparation strategy.

## Strategize with High-Weightage Topics

To effectively target a score of 120, you need to be smart about your study plan. Analyze past MHT CET papers to identify topics that frequently carry higher weightage. While it's important to cover the entire syllabus, dedicating extra time and effort to these high-scoring areas can significantly boost your overall marks. For instance, in Mathematics, topics like Calculus and Algebra often have a greater presence. In Physics, Mechanics and Electrostatics are typically significant. For Chemistry, Organic Chemistry and Physical Chemistry principles are vital. By focusing on these key areas, you can ensure you secure maximum marks from the most important sections of the exam.

## Master Time Management and Practice Effectively

The MHT CET is not just about knowledge; it's also about how efficiently you can apply that knowledge under timed conditions. Since there's no negative marking, it's a huge advantage to attempt every single question. This means you need to practice completing the entire paper within the given time limit. Regular practice with mock tests and previous year's question papers is indispensable. This will help you improve your speed, accuracy, and develop a strategy for navigating the paper. Learn to quickly identify questions you can answer immediately and those that might require more time, ensuring you don't get stuck on any single problem.

## Consistent Revision and Calm Approach

As the MHT CET approaches, consistency in revision becomes paramount. Avoid the temptation to learn entirely new topics in the final weeks. Instead, dedicate this time to thoroughly revising everything you've already studied. This reinforces your understanding, helps you recall formulas and concepts quickly, and builds confidence. Staying calm and composed during the final preparation phase and on exam day itself is critical. Trust in your preparation and approach the exam with a positive mindset, knowing you've put in the hard work.

By following these guidelines, you can build a robust preparation strategy to achieve your target score of 120 in the MHT CET and secure admission to your desired program.
