# BITSAT Eligibility for Class 12th Improvement Exam Takers

It's great that you're looking into improving your Class 12th scores! Understanding the eligibility criteria for important entrance exams like BITSAT is crucial. Let's break down whether your situation with a Class 12th improvement exam in a fifth subject makes you eligible for BITSAT 2020, having originally passed in 2019.

## Key Takeaways

*   BITSAT generally considers candidates who passed Class 12th in the preceding year or are appearing in the current year.
*   A minimum aggregate of 75% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) or Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) is required.
*   You must also score at least 60% individually in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology.
*   Proficiency in English is a mandatory requirement.

## BITSAT Eligibility Basics

To be eligible for the BITSAT exam, candidates must meet specific academic requirements set by BITS Pilani. These are designed to ensure students have a strong foundation for their engineering or science studies. The primary conditions include:

*   **Passing Class 12th:** You must have successfully completed your Class 12th examination from a recognized central or state board.
*   **Mandatory Subjects:** Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics are compulsory subjects for most B.E. programs. For certain integrated first-degree programs, candidates with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology may also be eligible.
*   **English Proficiency:** Adequate proficiency in the English language is also a stated requirement.

## Understanding the 75% Aggregate Rule

One of the most important criteria for BITSAT eligibility is the minimum percentage requirement. Candidates must achieve:

*   An aggregate score of at least **75% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM)** combined, or **Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB)** combined for relevant programs.
*   A minimum of **60% marks in each of Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology** individually.

This means that even if your overall aggregate is high, you must still meet the 60% individual subject threshold for PCM/PCB.

## Your Situation: Class 12th in 2019 and Improvement Exam

Since you passed your Class 12th in 2019, you generally fall within the eligible window for BITSAT 2020, as candidates who passed in the preceding year are typically considered. The key question then becomes how your improvement exam results are factored in.

While the official BITSAT information outlines the primary subject requirements (PCM/PCB), the impact of an improvement exam in a *fifth* subject needs careful consideration. If your core PCM scores (and English proficiency) meet the 75% aggregate and 60% individual subject criteria based on your 2019 results or subsequent official updates, your eligibility should be intact. The improvement in a fifth subject would likely not negatively affect your eligibility, provided the core requirements are met. Always refer to the official BITSAT information bulletin for the specific year you are applying to confirm how improvement exam scores are treated, especially concerning the aggregate percentage calculation if your improvement changed your core subject percentages.

## Age and Attempts

Unlike some other national exams, BITSAT generally does not have an upper age limit. However, there are restrictions on the number of attempts and the year of passing Class 12th. As mentioned, candidates who passed Class 12th in the previous year (2019 for BITSAT 2020) or are appearing in the current year (2020 for BITSAT 2020) are eligible. This confirms that your 2019 pass year makes you eligible for BITSAT 2020.

It's always recommended to thoroughly read the official BITSAT information brochure for the specific year you intend to apply, as eligibility criteria can sometimes have minor updates.
