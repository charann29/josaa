# How can 12th PCM students apply for AME Course 2022?

For students who have completed their 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM), an Aircraft Maintenance Engineering (AME) course offers a unique and practical pathway into the aviation industry. This field is distinct from traditional engineering degrees, focusing specifically on the upkeep and safety of aircraft.

## Key takeaways

*   AME is a specialized license program, not a conventional engineering degree.
*   It is regulated by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) in India.
*   The program combines academic training with practical industry experience.
*   A valid AME license is essential for certifying aircraft as airworthy.

## Understanding Aircraft Maintenance Engineering

Aircraft Maintenance Engineering is a vital branch of aviation that deals with the repair, maintenance, and troubleshooting of aircraft. Unlike a general engineering degree, an AME program is designed to equip individuals with the precise skills and knowledge needed to ensure aircraft safety and operational readiness. An aircraft can only fly if it has been certified as safe by an appropriately licensed AME.

## Program Structure and Duration

An AME license program typically spans around four years. This period is divided into two main components:

1.  **Academic Program:** Students must complete a two-year regular academic program at an institution approved by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA). This phase provides the foundational knowledge and theoretical understanding required for aircraft maintenance.
2.  **Practical Training:** Following the academic phase, candidates undergo approximately two years of practical training within an airline or a Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul (MRO) industry setting. This hands-on experience is crucial for developing practical skills and understanding real-world maintenance procedures.

## Eligibility Criteria for PCM Students

Students who have completed their 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) are generally eligible to apply for AME courses. Specific percentage requirements may vary between DGCA-approved institutes, so it's always advisable to check the individual institute's admission criteria. While AME is a technical field, it does not typically require entrance exams like JEE Main that are common for traditional engineering degrees. Admissions are usually based on 12th-grade academic performance and sometimes an institute-level screening process.

## The Role of DGCA

The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA), a regulatory body under the Ministry of Civil Aviation, Government of India, plays a pivotal role in the AME sector. The DGCA is responsible for regulating and overseeing all aspects of civil aviation in India, including the approval of AME institutes and the licensing of AME professionals. This ensures that the training standards and qualifications of AMEs meet national and international safety requirements.

## Career Prospects

Upon successful completion of the program and obtaining the AME license, graduates can pursue rewarding careers in various segments of the aviation industry. These include working with domestic and international airlines, MRO organizations, aircraft manufacturing companies, and even government aviation departments. The demand for skilled AME professionals remains consistent due to the continuous growth of the aviation sector.

Embarking on an AME course after 12th PCM can open doors to a dynamic and responsible career, ensuring the safety of air travel for millions.
