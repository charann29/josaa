Navigating engineering admissions can feel complex, especially when considering specific branches and minority quotas. This guide will help you understand your chances for B.Tech Artificial Intelligence and Data Science (AI & DS) at Thakur College of Engineering and Technology (TCET), Mumbai, based on your MHT CET scores and Hindi Linguistic Minority status.

## Key takeaways

*   An MHT CET percentile of 85-90% is competitive, but AI & DS cutoffs at TCET, even for minority categories, can be quite high.
*   Hindi Linguistic Minority status generally offers a benefit, but the exact cutoff for AI & DS may still be above 90%.
*   Meeting the basic academic eligibility criteria (10+2 marks) is crucial before considering percentiles.
*   Always participate diligently in the MHT CET counseling process, as seat allocation depends on it.

## Understanding MHT CET Percentiles and TCET Admissions

Your MHT CET percentile is a crucial factor in securing admission to engineering colleges in Maharashtra. For TCET, a highly sought-after institution, the cutoffs for popular branches like AI & DS are typically on the higher side. While official specific data for the Hindi Linguistic Minority category isn't always released separately from the general minority quota, it's generally understood that minority cutoffs are more lenient than the general home state category.

## Chances for AI & DS with 85-90 Percentile (Hindi Minority)

Based on past MHT CET data, the closing percentile for the general minority category in AI & DS at TCET has been around 93.4%. With an 85-90 percentile, while you are competitive, securing AI & DS specifically might be challenging under the Hindi Minority quota, as the cutoff for this particular branch tends to be high even within minority reservations. It's important to note that cutoffs can fluctuate each year based on the number of applicants, their scores, and the availability of seats.

## What if you score 80 Percentile for 2025?

If your MHT CET percentile is around 80%, securing admission to AI & DS at TCET, even under the Hindi Minority quota, becomes significantly more difficult. However, an 80 percentile might open doors to other B.Tech branches at TCET that have lower cutoff percentiles. It's always advisable to keep an open mind about other specializations if your primary choice seems out of reach.

## The Admission Process: Your Next Steps

1.  **Eligibility Check:** Ensure you meet the fundamental academic requirements. This typically includes passing your 10+2 examination with a minimum of 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (45% for reserved categories). These are standard requirements set by the Directorate of Technical Education (DTE), Maharashtra.
2.  **MHT CET Counseling:** This is a multi-stage process conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra. You must register, fill out your preferences for colleges and courses (including TCET and AI & DS), and participate in the seat allotment rounds. All updates and announcements will be made on the official counseling portal.
3.  **Document Verification:** Be prepared with all necessary documents, including your MHT CET scorecard, 10th and 12th mark sheets, domicile certificate, and your Hindi Linguistic Minority certificate, for verification during the counseling process.

For the most precise and up-to-date information, always refer to the official TCET website and the DTE Maharashtra's MHT CET counseling portal. They are the definitive sources for cutoffs, eligibility, and the admission schedule.
