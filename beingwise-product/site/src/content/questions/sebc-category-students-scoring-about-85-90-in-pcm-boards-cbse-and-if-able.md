# For SEBC Category Students: DAIICT ICT Branch Chances with 85-90% PCM and 100+ GUJCET

For students from the Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBC) category aiming for the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) branch at DA-IICT (Dhirubhai Ambani Institute of Information and Communication Technology), understanding your admission prospects is crucial. This guide will help you assess your chances with strong PCM board scores and a good GUJCET performance.

## Key Takeaways

*   Admission to DA-IICT's ICT program is highly competitive and considers a combination of GUJCET rank, board examination scores, and your specific category.
*   For SEBC students, scoring 85-90% in PCM in your board exams (like CBSE) and over 100 in GUJCET offers a reasonable possibility, but it's not a guarantee.
*   Final admission hinges on the annual cutoffs, which fluctuate based on the total number of applicants, their scores, and the availability of seats in the SEBC category.
*   It is essential to stay updated with official DA-IICT admission announcements and participate diligently in the counselling process.

## Understanding the Admission Criteria

DA-IICT's admission process for its undergraduate programs primarily relies on two key components: your performance in the Gujarat Common Entrance Test (GUJCET) and your marks in the Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) subjects in your 12th standard board examinations. For reserved categories like SEBC, specific quotas are applied, which can influence the cutoff scores.

### The Role of GUJCET Score

A GUJCET score of 100+ is considered a strong performance, placing you in a competitive bracket. This score is a significant factor in determining your overall GUJCET rank, which is then used in the admission process for institutes participating in GUJCET-based admissions, including DA-IICT.

### Importance of Board Marks (PCM)

Your 12th standard board examination marks in PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) are equally vital. A score range of 85-90% indicates a very good academic foundation, demonstrating consistent performance in core science subjects. DA-IICT considers these marks as part of its comprehensive evaluation, often giving them a specific weightage alongside your GUJCET score.

### Category-Specific Considerations

Being an SEBC category student means you are eligible for reservations as per government norms. These reservations lead to separate cutoff lists for the SEBC category, which are typically lower than the general category cutoffs. This can significantly enhance your chances of securing a seat, provided you meet the minimum eligibility and cross the category-specific cutoff.

## Assessing Your Chances for ICT at DA-IICT

With 85-90% in PCM board exams and a 100+ GUJCET score, you certainly have a plausible chance for the ICT branch at DA-IICT as an SEBC candidate. However, it's crucial to understand that admission is dynamic. The final cutoff for any given year depends on several factors:

*   **Overall Applicant Pool:** The total number of students applying and their score distribution in GUJCET and board exams.
*   **Performance of SEBC Candidates:** The scores achieved by other SEBC category students also vying for seats.
*   **Number of Seats:** The fixed number of seats available for the ICT branch under the SEBC category.

While previous year cutoffs can offer an indication, they should only be used as a guide. The official DA-IICT admission portal or the Admissions Committee for Professional Courses (ACPC) website are the best places to look for historical cutoff data, which can help you gauge the competitiveness.

## The Counselling Process

After GUJCET results are declared, you will need to participate in the counselling process conducted by the relevant admission authority (often ACPC for institutes in Gujarat). This involves registration, choice filling (listing DA-IICT ICT as a preferred option), and seat allotment based on your merit rank within your category. It is imperative to follow all instructions precisely and submit all required documents for verification. Missing any step can lead to disqualification, even with excellent scores.

Keep a close watch on official announcements from DA-IICT and the counselling authority to ensure you are well-informed throughout the admission cycle.
