# TFWS Seat at DA-IICT Gandhinagar with Strong Scores

Considering your excellent performance – 90 percentile in JEE Main, over 100 marks in GUJCET, and more than 98 percentile in your board exams, coupled with an OBC category and interest in a TFWS (Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme) seat at DA-IICT Gandhinagar – it's natural to wonder about your admission prospects.

## Key Takeaways

*   Admission to DA-IICT, especially for TFWS seats, is highly competitive due to limited availability.
*   Both JEE Main and GUJCET scores are crucial for admissions, with specific weightage applied.
*   Your OBC category provides reservation benefits, but TFWS seats are often open to all categories based on merit.
*   Thoroughly understand the DA-IICT admission process and cut-offs for TFWS seats.

## Understanding DA-IICT Admissions and TFWS

DA-IICT (Dhirubhai Ambani Institute of Information and Communication Technology) is a highly respected institution. Admissions for their B.Tech programs primarily consider two main entrance exams: JEE Main and GUJCET. For students from Gujarat, 50% of the seats are typically reserved for those admitted through GUJCET, while the remaining 50% are filled via JEE Main scores, drawing students from all over India.

The Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) is a government initiative aimed at supporting meritorious students from economically weaker sections. TFWS seats waive the tuition fee component, making higher education more accessible. These seats are limited in number and are often highly sought after, making them extremely competitive.

## Your Scores and Competitiveness

With a 90 percentile in JEE Main, you've achieved a good score, but for top-tier institutes like DA-IICT, particularly for TFWS seats, the competition is intense. Cut-offs for TFWS seats are generally higher than for general seats because they are awarded purely on merit, irrespective of category, from the top-ranked students who apply.

Your 100+ marks in GUJCET are also strong and will be a significant factor if you are applying through the Gujarat quota. The 98+ percentile in your board exams demonstrates a strong academic foundation, which is always a positive indicator, though direct board marks usually don't factor into the primary admission score for engineering colleges unless there's a tie-breaking situation.

## OBC Category Benefits and TFWS

Being in the OBC category provides reservation benefits for admission to engineering colleges across India. However, it's important to clarify how this interacts with TFWS seats. TFWS seats are generally allotted based on a separate merit list, often irrespective of category. This means that while your OBC category is beneficial for general admission, the TFWS seat allocation will primarily depend on your rank among all TFWS applicants.

It is crucial to check the specific information bulletin released by DA-IICT for the current academic year to understand the exact weightage given to JEE Main vs. GUJCET, the reservation policy for OBC candidates, and the specific criteria for TFWS seats.

## Next Steps: Official Resources and Application

To get a clear picture, your next step should be to consult the official DA-IICT admission brochure or information bulletin for the upcoming academic year. This document will detail the exact admission process, cut-offs, seat matrix (including TFWS), and category-wise reservations. You should also refer to official counseling portals like the Admission Committee for Professional Courses (ACPC) in Gujarat, which handles admissions for state-quota seats, including those for DA-IICT through GUJCET.

Always rely on official sources for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding cut-offs and admission criteria.
