## Understanding Cutoffs and Ranks

Cutoff ranks represent the last rank at which admission was granted to a particular course and college in a specific category during the counseling process. These ranks can fluctuate each year based on several factors, including the number of applicants, their performance in entrance exams, the total number of seats available, and the preferences of students. For COEP, both MHT-CET and JEE Main scores are considered for admissions, with specific quotas for each.

## 2024 Trends for AI & Robotics at COEP

For the academic year 2024-25, the Artificial Intelligence and Robotics branch at COEP Pune saw a very competitive closing rank. For the General Category under the Home State quota, the last admitted candidate had a rank of approximately 369, corresponding to a percentile of around 99.16 in the MHT-CET examination. This gives a strong indication of the high academic performance expected for admission to this sought-after program.

## Home State Quota and Caste Categories

Students applying under the Home State quota compete for a significant portion of seats reserved for candidates from Maharashtra. Within this quota, various caste categories like NT-B (Nomadic Tribes - B) have reservations as per government norms. However, it's important to note that when reviewing official cutoff lists, especially those derived from JEE Main rounds, detailed breakdowns for every sub-category like NT-B might not always be explicitly published. Often, these categories are managed during the centralized admission process (CAP rounds) conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, where specific reserved seats are allocated based on category-wise merit lists.

## Predicting 2025 Cutoffs

While precise 2025 cutoffs cannot be predicted, based on the strong performance required in 2024, it is reasonable to expect that admission to COEP's AI & Robotics program will continue to be highly competitive. Candidates aiming for this branch, including those from the NT-B category, should strive for excellent scores in their MHT-CET or JEE Main exams. Historical trends suggest that specialized branches like AI and Robotics consistently demand ranks in the low thousands or even hundreds for the Home State quota, especially for open/general categories.

Aspiring students should regularly check the official websites of the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, and COEP Pune for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding cutoffs and the admission process once the 2025 cycle begins.
