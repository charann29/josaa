# Ive paid for the kcet but when i try to download the application form i cant,it shows i havent paid

It can be quite frustrating to complete your KCET payment only to find the system still shows an outstanding balance, preventing you from downloading your application form. Don't worry, this is a common issue with clear steps to resolve it.

## Key takeaways

*   Always verify if the payment amount has actually left your bank account.
*   System updates can take a few hours, so patience is key after payment.
*   Logging out and back in often refreshes the application status.
*   The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) helpline is your best resource for persistent issues, especially with payment proof ready.

## Verify Your Bank Transaction

The very first step is to confirm whether the payment was successfully debited from your bank account. Check your bank statement, online banking portal, or transaction history. Sometimes, a payment might appear to go through, but it could be pending or have failed without an immediate notification from the bank. If the amount is still in your account, the transaction likely didn't complete, and you might need to attempt the payment again.

## Allow Time for System Updates

Payment gateways and application portals don't always update instantly. After making a payment for KCET, it's common for the system to take a few hours, or even up to 24 hours in some cases, to process and reflect the payment status. During peak application times, these delays can be more pronounced. It's advisable to wait for a reasonable period before concluding there's an issue.

## Try Re-logging and Checking Email/SMS

Often, a simple refresh of the system can resolve the problem. Log out of your KCET application portal completely and then log back in after some time. This action forces the system to re-check your details and update the payment status. Simultaneously, check your registered email inbox and SMS messages. The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) often sends payment confirmation messages or emails once a transaction is successfully recorded in their system.

## Contact the KEA Helpline

If you've waited, re-logged, and confirmed the payment was debited but the issue persists, it's time to reach out to the official support channel. The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) provides a dedicated helpline for such technical and payment-related queries. When you contact them, make sure to have all your payment details handy, including:

*   Your application number
*   The transaction ID or reference number from your bank
*   The date and time of the payment
*   The exact amount paid
*   Proof of payment (a screenshot of your bank statement or transaction confirmation)

Providing this information will help the KEA team quickly locate your transaction and resolve the discrepancy. They are the authoritative source for rectifying such payment-related issues.

Hopefully, with these steps, you'll be able to quickly resolve the payment discrepancy and successfully download your KCET application form. Good luck with your application!
