# What is the Syllabus for MHT CET 2021?

The MHT CET (Maharashtra Health and Technical Common Entrance Test) is a crucial examination for students seeking admission to various undergraduate professional courses in Maharashtra. Understanding the syllabus is the first step towards effective preparation, ensuring you cover all the necessary topics.

## Key Takeaways

*   The MHT CET 2021 syllabus primarily draws from the Maharashtra State Board's curriculum for classes 11 and 12.
*   It covers three main subject groups: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) or Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB).
*   While the core content remained consistent, it's always wise to refer to the official State Common Entrance Test Cell website for the most precise details.
*   The exam structure generally allocates 20% weightage to Class 11 topics and 80% to Class 12 topics.

## Overview of the MHT CET Syllabus

The MHT CET is conducted annually by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Government of Maharashtra. For the 2021 examination, the syllabus was primarily based on the curriculum prescribed by the Maharashtra State Board for the 11th and 12th standards. Students preparing for this exam should focus on building a strong foundation in these subjects.

## Subject-wise Syllabus Breakdown

To help you organize your study plan, here's a general classification of topics for each subject. For the most detailed and official syllabus, always check the State Common Entrance Test Cell's official portal.

### Physics

The Physics section typically covers fundamental concepts and their applications. Key areas include:

*   **Mechanics:** Circular motion, Gravitation, Rotational motion, Oscillations, Elasticity.
*   **Thermodynamics & Waves:** Surface tension, Wave motion, Stationary waves, Kinetic theory, Wave theory, Interference and diffraction.
*   **Electromagnetism & Modern Physics:** Electrostatics, Current electricity, Magnetic effects of electric current, Magnetism, Electromagnetic induction, Electrons and Photons, Atoms, Molecules and Nuclei, Semiconductors, Communication systems.

### Chemistry

Chemistry topics span across physical, inorganic, and organic chemistry. Important chapters include:

*   **Physical Chemistry:** Solid State, Solutions and colligative properties, Chemical Thermodynamics and energetic, Electrochemistry, Chemical Kinetics.
*   **Inorganic Chemistry:** General Principles and Processes of Isolation of Elements, p-Block Elements, d and f Block Elements, Coordination Compounds.
*   **Organic Chemistry:** Halogen derivatives of alkanes (and arenes), Alcohols, Phenols and Ethers, Aldehydes, Ketones and Carboxylic Acids, Organic Compounds Containing Nitrogen, Biomolecules, Polymers, Chemistry in Everyday Life.

### Mathematics

The Mathematics syllabus is comprehensive, testing both conceptual understanding and problem-solving skills. Key topics are:

*   **Algebra:** Logic, Matrices, Probability distribution, Bernoulli trials and Binomial distribution.
*   **Trigonometry:** Trigonometric functions.
*   **Coordinate Geometry:** Pair of straight lines, Circle, Conics, Vectors, Three-dimensional geometry, Line, Plane.
*   **Calculus:** Continuity, Differentiation, Application of derivative, Integration, Applications of definite integral, Differential equation.
*   **Other:** Linear programming problems, Statistics.

### Biology (Botany & Zoology)

For students opting for the PCB group, Biology is divided into Botany and Zoology, covering a wide range of biological principles.

**Botany:**

*   Genetic Basis of Inheritance, Gene: its nature, expression and regulation.
*   Biotechnology: Process and Application.
*   Enhancement in Food Production, Microbes in Human Welfare.
*   Photosynthesis, Respiration, Reproduction in Plants.
*   Organisms and Environment -I.

**Zoology:**

*   Origin and the Evolution of Life, Chromosomal Basis of Inheritance.
*   Genetic Engineering and Genomics.
*   Human Health and Diseases, Animal Husbandry.
*   Circulation, Excretion and osmoregulation, Control and Co-ordination.
*   Human Reproduction.
*   Organisms and Environment.

## Important Note on Syllabus Changes

It is important to remember that entrance exam syllabi, including MHT CET, generally align with the academic curriculum of the respective board. If there are any changes in the Class 11th and 12th syllabus for a given academic year, these changes are typically reflected in the entrance exam. For the 2021 examination, candidates were advised to stick to the Class 12th syllabus for their chosen subjects, along with relevant Class 11th topics, and to always refer to the official State Common Entrance Test Cell website for any updates or specific notifications regarding the syllabus.

Thorough preparation based on these topics will significantly enhance your chances of success in the MHT CET.
