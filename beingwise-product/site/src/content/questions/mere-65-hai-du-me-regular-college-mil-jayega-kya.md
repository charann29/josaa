# Mere 65% hai DU me regular college mil jayega kya?

Many students wonder about their chances of getting into a regular Delhi University (DU) college with a Class 12 score of around 65%. While securing a spot in top-tier colleges or highly competitive courses can be difficult, there are still avenues to explore.

## Key Takeaways

*   Direct admission based solely on Class 12 marks at 65% for popular DU courses is highly unlikely.
*   Your primary path to DU admission is through the Common University Entrance Test (CUET).
*   Reserved categories (SC/ST/OBC/EWS/PwBD) offer significant relaxation in admission criteria.
*   Consider less competitive courses or colleges that might have lower cutoff requirements.

## Understanding DU Admissions Today

Gone are the days when Class 12 marks alone determined entry into Delhi University. Since 2022, admission to most undergraduate programs at DU is primarily based on your performance in the Common University Entrance Test (CUET), conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). Your Class 12 percentage is now a basic eligibility criterion, but your CUET score is what truly counts for merit-based admissions.

## The Role of CUET Scores

Even with 65% in Class 12, a strong performance in CUET can significantly boost your chances. Each college and course at DU will declare its admission criteria based on CUET scores, not directly on Class 12 percentages. It's crucial to prepare thoroughly for CUET, focusing on the subjects relevant to your desired program. The official CUET information bulletin provides details on the test structure and syllabus.

## Exploring Reserved Categories and Quotas

Delhi University adheres to the reservation policies set by the Government of India. If you belong to any of the following categories, you might find your admission prospects improve significantly:

*   **Scheduled Castes (SC)**
*   **Scheduled Tribes (ST)**
*   **Other Backward Classes (OBC - Non-Creamy Layer)**
*   **Economically Weaker Section (EWS)**
*   **Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD)**
*   **Kashmiri Migrants (KM)**
*   **Children/Widows of Armed Forces Personnel (CW)**

These categories often have significantly lower cutoff requirements compared to the general category. Make sure you have all the necessary and valid documentation ready if you plan to apply under any of these quotas, as specified by DU's admission guidelines.

## Strategic College and Course Selection

Not all DU colleges and courses have the same level of competition. While top colleges like Miranda House or St. Stephen's often have very high CUET score requirements, many other excellent colleges might be more accessible. Consider the following:

*   **Off-Campus Colleges:** Colleges located slightly further from the main campus often have slightly lower cutoffs. They still offer the same DU degree and quality education.
*   **Evening Colleges:** DU's evening colleges provide a flexible learning environment and sometimes have different admission trends compared to their day counterparts.
*   **Less Popular Courses:** Explore courses that are not in extremely high demand. While B.A. (Hons.) English or B.Com (Hons.) are highly sought after, programs like B.A. (Hons.) Sanskrit, B.A. (Hons.) Punjabi, or certain vocational courses might have lower entry barriers.

Consult the official DU admission bulletin and individual college websites during the application period to understand specific course offerings and their respective CUET score expectations.

## The Application Process

Once CUET results are out, DU will open its Common Seat Allocation System (CSAS) portal. This is where you will apply to specific colleges and courses based on your CUET scores. It's a multi-stage process involving preference filling, seat allocation, and document verification. Pay close attention to the deadlines and instructions provided on the official DU admission portal.

While a 65% Class 12 score might not open every door, strategic planning, a strong CUET performance, and exploring all available options can certainly lead to a regular college seat in Delhi University.
