# With 380 Marks in NEET: Your Chances at Army College of Dental Sciences

Understanding your admission prospects for a Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) program, especially at a specific institution like the Army College of Dental Sciences (ACDS), requires looking at several factors. Let's break down what your NEET score of 380 might mean for your application, keeping in mind your background as an OBC candidate from Haryana with a father in the army.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your NEET score of 380 is likely to result in a rank that makes admission to ACDS for BDS very challenging.
*   ACDS primarily admits students based on NEET merit, with specific considerations for children of army personnel.
*   Cut-off ranks for ACDS BDS have historically been much higher than what a 380 score typically achieves.
*   It's crucial to explore other potential colleges and consider all available admission quotas.

## Understanding ACDS Admissions

ACDS offers undergraduate (BDS) and postgraduate (MDS) dental programs. Admission to the BDS course is strictly based on a candidate's performance in the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET). While ACDS is specifically for children of serving and retired Indian Army personnel, admission within this category is still determined by NEET merit. The college's annual fees for the BDS program are approximately Rs. 3.55 Lakhs.

## Predicting Your NEET Rank

Based on a NEET score of 380, your predicted percentile would generally fall in the range of 85.97 to 86.47. This percentile typically corresponds to an overall predicted rank between 212,050 and 252,200. It's important to remember that these are estimations, and the actual rank can vary slightly based on the difficulty of the exam, the number of candidates, and other factors determined by the National Testing Agency (NTA).

## ACDS Cut-off Ranks: Historical Data

To assess your chances, it's helpful to look at previous years' closing ranks for BDS admissions at ACDS. For instance, in 2018, the closing rank for BDS at ACDS was approximately 41,646. In 2019, this figure shifted to around 56,448. While cut-offs fluctuate each year due to various factors like the number of applicants, exam difficulty, and seat availability, these past trends provide a strong indication.

### Comparing Your Predicted Rank to ACDS Cut-offs

When we compare your predicted rank range (212,050 - 252,200) with the historical closing ranks for ACDS (41,646 in 2018 and 56,448 in 2019), it becomes clear that your score of 380 would likely place you significantly below the required merit for admission to this institution. Even with the specific quota for children of army personnel, the competition within that category is still very high, and successful candidates typically have much higher NEET scores and ranks.

## Implications for Your Admission Chances

Given the historical cut-off trends and your predicted NEET rank, securing a BDS seat at the Army College of Dental Sciences with a score of 380 marks appears to be very difficult. Your category (OBC from Haryana) and your father's army service are important factors that might offer some advantages in other colleges or state quotas, but for ACDS, the NEET merit remains the primary hurdle. It would be wise to explore other colleges where your score might be more competitive, possibly through state-level counselling for Haryana or other private institutions.

We encourage you to carefully review the official information provided by JoSAA or the respective state counselling authorities for detailed admission guidelines and cut-offs for all relevant colleges.
