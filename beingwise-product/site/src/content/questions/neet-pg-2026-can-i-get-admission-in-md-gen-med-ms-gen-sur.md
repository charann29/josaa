# NEET PG 2026: Can I Get Admission in MD General Medicine or MS General Surgery?

Aspiring doctors often set their sights on highly competitive specialisations like MD General Medicine and MS General Surgery. Understanding the path to these programs through NEET PG 2026 is crucial for effective preparation.

## Key Takeaways

*   NEET PG is the gateway for admission to MD, MS, and PG Diploma courses across India.
*   Admission to popular specialisations like General Medicine and General Surgery is highly competitive, requiring excellent scores.
*   A strong understanding of the eligibility criteria and the counselling process is essential for all applicants.
*   Preparation should begin early, focusing on the comprehensive NEET PG syllabus.

## Understanding NEET PG 2026

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Postgraduate), or NEET PG, is the single entrance examination for admission to various postgraduate medical courses in India. This includes Doctor of Medicine (MD), Master of Surgery (MS), and PG Diploma programs offered by government and private medical colleges, deemed universities, and Armed Forces Medical Services institutions. The only exceptions are AIIMS, JIPMER, PGIMER, and NIMHANS, which conduct their own entrance exams.

## Eligibility Criteria for NEET PG 2026

Before you can even think about admission, you must meet the fundamental eligibility requirements set by the National Board of Examinations in Medical Sciences (NBEMS). Generally, these include:

*   **MBBS Degree:** Possessing a valid MBBS degree from a university or institution recognised by the National Medical Commission (NMC).
*   **Internship:** Completion of a compulsory one-year rotating internship by the cut-off date specified in the official NEET PG information bulletin.
*   **Provisional/Permanent Registration:** Holding provisional or permanent registration with the NMC or a State Medical Council.
*   **Citizenship:** Generally, Indian citizens are eligible. Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) and Persons of Indian Origin (PIO) may also be eligible under specific rules, as detailed by the official NEET PG guidelines.

It's vital to refer to the official NEET PG 2026 information bulletin, released by NBEMS, for the exact and most up-to-date eligibility conditions, as these can sometimes have minor adjustments year to year.

## The Admission Process: Counselling

After the NEET PG results are declared, the admission process moves to counselling. The Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) conducts counselling for 50% All India Quota (AIQ) seats and all seats in Deemed/Central Universities, ESIC, and AFMS institutions. The remaining 50% of state quota seats are filled by the respective state counselling authorities. For MD General Medicine and MS General Surgery, demand is very high, meaning you'll need a top rank to secure a seat, especially in government colleges.

The counselling process typically involves:

1.  **Registration:** Candidates register online on the MCC portal or respective state portals.
2.  **Choice Filling and Locking:** Applicants select their preferred colleges and specialisations in order of priority.
3.  **Seat Allotment:** Based on NEET PG rank, choices filled, and availability of seats, seats are provisionally allotted.
4.  **Reporting to College:** Allotted candidates must report to their assigned college for document verification and admission formalities.

Multiple rounds of counselling are usually conducted, including a mop-up round, to fill any vacant seats.

## Preparing for MD General Medicine and MS General Surgery

Securing a seat in MD General Medicine or MS General Surgery requires dedicated and strategic preparation for NEET PG. These branches are among the most sought-after, demanding a high level of proficiency across all subjects taught during MBBS. Your preparation should cover the entire NEET PG syllabus, which broadly aligns with the MBBS curriculum. Focus on conceptual understanding, clinical application, and extensive practice with mock tests and previous year's question papers. Aim for a consistently high score to improve your chances in these competitive fields.

With thorough preparation and a clear understanding of the admission process, your goal of pursuing MD General Medicine or MS General Surgery through NEET PG 2026 is within reach.
