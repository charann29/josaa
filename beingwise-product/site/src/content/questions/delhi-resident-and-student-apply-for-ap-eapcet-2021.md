# Can a Delhi Resident and Student Apply for AP EAPCET 2021?

Many students from outside Andhra Pradesh wonder if they can apply for the AP EAPCET (Engineering, Agriculture, and Pharmacy Common Entrance Test). While it is possible, there are specific eligibility conditions that need to be met.

## Key Takeaways

*   Delhi residents can apply for AP EAPCET if they meet specific academic criteria related to their intermediate education.
*   The primary condition involves completing 10+2 or an equivalent qualification from a recognized board in Andhra Pradesh or Telangana.
*   Holders of OCI/PIO cards may also be eligible under certain rules.
*   Understanding these rules is crucial before proceeding with an application.

## Understanding AP EAPCET Eligibility for Non-Locals

The Andhra Pradesh State Council of Higher Education (APSCHE) sets the eligibility criteria for AP EAPCET. These criteria primarily focus on the academic background and, in some cases, the residential status of the applicant. For students residing outside Andhra Pradesh, like those from Delhi, the rules are quite specific.

## The Crucial Academic Qualification

The most important condition for a Delhi resident to be eligible for AP EAPCET is linked to their intermediate (10+2) education. To qualify, a candidate must have successfully completed their 10+2 or an equivalent examination from a recognized educational board located within either Andhra Pradesh or Telangana. This means that simply having passed 10+2 from a Delhi-based board, such as CBSE or ISC, would not typically make a student eligible for AP EAPCET, unless that particular school or board is formally recognized as being within AP/Telangana's purview for this specific purpose.

## Eligibility for OCI/PIO Card Holders

There is an exception for individuals holding Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) cards or Persons of Indian Origin (PIO) cards. If you fall into either of these categories, you may be eligible to apply for AP EAPCET, even if your 10+2 education was not completed in Andhra Pradesh or Telangana. It's always advisable to check the official AP EAPCET information bulletin for the specific guidelines pertaining to OCI/PIO candidates, as these can sometimes have additional requirements or documentation needs.

## How to Confirm Your Eligibility

Before starting the application process, it is highly recommended to carefully review the official AP EAPCET information brochure for 2021. This document, released by APSCHE, contains all the precise and up-to-date eligibility criteria, including details for non-local candidates and OCI/PIO cardholders. Cross-referencing your academic history and personal status with these official guidelines will ensure you meet all necessary conditions.

If you meet these specific conditions, then a Delhi resident can indeed apply for AP EAPCET 2021.
