# Can I Get Ramdeobaba College of Engineering Nagpur with MHTCET 2021 Score 96.52 CS/IT Branch? (OBC, Home State Maharashtra)

With an MHT-CET score of 96.52 percentile and an OBC category status from Maharashtra, you're in a good position to explore your options at Shri Ramdeobaba College of Engineering and Management (SRCOEM) in Nagpur, particularly for Computer Science (CS) or Information Technology (IT) branches.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your 96.52 percentile is competitive, but CS/IT at SRCOEM can be highly sought after.
*   Previous year cutoffs suggest a possibility, but admission trends can fluctuate.
*   Being an OBC candidate from Maharashtra provides reservation benefits that could be advantageous.
*   Keep an open mind about other engineering branches at SRCOEM, as they might be more accessible.

## Understanding MHT-CET Percentiles and Admissions

The MHT-CET percentile score indicates your performance relative to all other test-takers. A 96.52 percentile is a strong score, placing you among the top performers. However, securing a seat in highly competitive branches like CS and IT at a reputable institution like SRCOEM depends on several factors, including the total number of applicants, their scores, and the specific cutoffs for your category in that particular year.

Admission to engineering colleges in Maharashtra is managed through a Centralized Admission Process (CAP) conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell (CET Cell), Maharashtra. This process considers your MHT-CET score, category, and home state status.

## Analyzing Previous Year Cutoffs for SRCOEM

While official MHT-CET ranks for 2021 were not yet released at the time of your query, we can look at past years' cutoff trends to get an idea. Historically, CS and IT branches at SRCOEM have high demand, leading to higher cutoff percentiles. For OBC candidates from Maharashtra, the cutoffs are generally lower than for the General category, but they still remain competitive for core branches.

Based on previous years' data, a 96.52 percentile for an OBC home-state candidate might put you on the borderline for CS or IT at SRCOEM. There's a chance, but it's not a guaranteed admission. Cutoffs are dynamic and can change annually due to factors like the difficulty of the exam, the number of students appearing, and the preferences of top-scoring candidates.

## The Role of Your Category and Home State

As an OBC candidate from Maharashtra, you are eligible for reservations as per government policies. This means a certain percentage of seats are reserved for your category, which improves your chances compared to general category candidates with similar scores. Your 'Home State' status is also crucial, as a significant portion of seats in Maharashtra's engineering colleges are reserved for candidates who have completed their schooling in the state.

## Exploring Other Branches and Colleges

While your focus is on CS/IT, it's wise to consider other excellent engineering branches offered at SRCOEM. Branches like Electronics and Telecommunication, Mechanical Engineering, or Civil Engineering are also highly regarded and might have slightly lower cutoffs, making them more accessible with your percentile. If your primary goal is to study at SRCOEM, being flexible with your branch choice could be a strategic move.

It's also beneficial to explore other reputable engineering colleges in Maharashtra. The official CAP admission brochures, released by the CET Cell, provide detailed information on participating colleges, available seats, and reservation policies, which can help you make informed decisions.

Keep a close watch on the official MHT-CET CAP rounds and counseling schedules. Applying with an open mind and exploring all your options will maximize your chances of securing a good engineering seat.
