# Hi, I am Anand, I got 92.78 CAT percentile in NC-OBC. What are the chances that I get a call from NITIE?

Anand, with your 92.78 CAT percentile in the NC-OBC category, you've achieved a strong score. Let's explore your prospects for receiving a Personal Interview (PI) shortlist from IIM Mumbai, formerly known as NITIE.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your CAT percentile of 92.78 in the NC-OBC category places you in a competitive position for a PI call.
*   Meeting the minimum sectional and overall CAT percentiles is a prerequisite, but actual cut-offs are typically higher.
*   IIM Mumbai considers a range of factors beyond just CAT scores for shortlisting, including your reservation category and gender diversity.
*   Final admission depends on a composite score calculated after the PI round, incorporating academic performance and work experience.

## Understanding the Shortlisting Process

IIM Mumbai's shortlisting for the Personal Interview round is a multi-faceted process. While your CAT percentile is a crucial component, it's not the sole determinant. The institute also takes into account your reservation category, such as NC-OBC, and aspects like gender diversity, which can influence the final cut-offs.

## Minimum CAT Percentile Requirements for NC-OBC

For candidates belonging to the NC-OBC category, IIM Mumbai has specified minimum percentile requirements for both overall and individual sections of the CAT exam. It's important to meet or exceed these thresholds to be considered for a PI shortlist.

| Section | Minimum Percentile |
| :------ | :----------------- |
| Overall | ≥ 75               |
| VARC    | ≥ 70               |
| DILR    | ≥ 65               |
| QA      | ≥ 65               |

It's crucial to understand that these are *minimum* requirements. The actual percentiles for shortlisting can be significantly higher, as they depend on factors like the total number of applicants, their scores, and the institute's specific cohort requirements for that year.

## Beyond the CAT Score: What Else Matters?

Once you've been shortlisted and successfully complete the Personal Interview, your final admission to IIM Mumbai will be based on a comprehensive composite score. This score is calculated by giving weightage to various elements of your application. These typically include:

*   **Academic Performance:** Your marks in Class 10, Class 12, and your Bachelor's degree are significant.
*   **Work Experience:** Relevant work experience, if any, is also factored into the evaluation.
*   **CAT Percentile:** Your performance in the CAT exam continues to hold weight.
*   **Personal Interview (PI):** Your performance in the interview round is a critical component.
*   **Gender Diversity:** The institute may consider gender diversity to ensure a balanced cohort.

## What This Means for Your Chances

Given your 92.78 CAT percentile in the NC-OBC category, you have a good chance of receiving a PI call from IIM Mumbai. Your score is well above the minimum specified for your category. However, remember that the final shortlisting will depend on the overall applicant pool for the current admission cycle. If you do receive a PI call, focus on preparing thoroughly for the interview and ensuring your academic record and any work experience are well-articulated to maximize your composite score and secure a spot.

We wish you the very best in your admission journey to IIM Mumbai!
