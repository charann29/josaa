# I have studied Hindi as language subject in my 10th standard. But actually I am weak in Hindi. Whereas I am more stronger in Tamil(which I studied upto my 8th standard). So, could I choose Tamil as my compulsory Indian language in UPSC exam?

It's common for students to have varying strengths in different languages. When it comes to the UPSC Civil Services Examination (CSE), understanding the language requirements is crucial for effective preparation.

## Key takeaways

*   You can choose any language listed in the Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution for your compulsory Indian Language paper.
*   There is no requirement to have studied a particular language up to Class 10 or 12 to choose it for the UPSC exam.
*   It's highly advisable to select a language you are genuinely proficient in, allowing you to focus on other core subjects.
*   Tamil is an official Scheduled Language, making it a valid option for the compulsory language paper.

## Understanding the Compulsory Indian Language Paper

The UPSC Civil Services Examination includes a compulsory Indian Language paper. This paper is qualifying in nature, meaning your marks from it aren't added to your final merit score, but you must achieve a minimum score to proceed in the examination process. The primary purpose of this paper is to assess your ability to read, comprehend, and express yourself in an Indian language.

## Choosing Your Language: Flexibility is Key

The good news is that the UPSC provides significant flexibility in choosing your compulsory Indian language. The official UPSC notification clearly states that candidates can select any one of the languages included in the Eighth Schedule to the Constitution of India. This list includes 22 recognized languages, such as Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Punjabi, Telugu, Urdu, and many others.

Crucially, there are no stipulations that you must have studied a particular language up to a specific academic level (like Class 10 or 12) to be eligible to choose it for the UPSC exam. Your past academic history with a language is not a barrier to selecting it, as long as it's on the official list.

## Why Proficiency Matters

While you have the freedom to choose, it's strongly recommended that you opt for a language in which you are genuinely strong. The compulsory Indian Language paper requires you to read, write, and understand the language well. If you choose a language you are not proficient in, you would need to dedicate a substantial amount of time to learning it from scratch. This could divert valuable preparation time from other crucial General Studies papers and your optional subject, which are directly counted towards your final merit.

Given your situation, where you feel weaker in Hindi despite having studied it up to Class 10, but are stronger in Tamil (which you studied up to Class 8), choosing Tamil appears to be a very sensible decision. Tamil is indeed one of the languages listed in the Eighth Schedule, making it a perfectly valid and acceptable choice for your UPSC compulsory Indian Language paper.

Focus on leveraging your existing strengths to ensure you comfortably clear this qualifying paper, allowing you to devote maximum energy to the merit-scoring sections of the exam. All the best for your preparations!
