# Understanding Simple Interest: A Practical Example

Many competitive exams, including those for higher education admissions in India, feature questions on fundamental financial concepts like simple interest. Let's break down a typical problem to see how these concepts apply.

## Key Takeaways

*   Simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal amount.
*   Understanding how to set up and solve simultaneous equations is crucial for these types of problems.
*   Careful calculation of interest earned from each investment scheme is essential.
*   This problem demonstrates a common application of basic algebra in real-world financial scenarios.

## The Investment Scenario

Imagine Rajesh has INR 10,000 to invest. He decides to split this amount between two different schemes, A and B. Scheme A offers a simple interest rate of 8% per annum, while Scheme B offers a slightly higher rate of 10% per annum. After two years, Rajesh earns a total of INR 1,680 in interest from both investments combined. The question asks us to find out how much Rajesh initially invested in Scheme A.

This is a classic problem that tests your understanding of simple interest formulas and your ability to solve linear equations.

## Breaking Down the Problem

To solve this, we need to represent the unknown amounts with variables. Let's say the amount invested in Scheme A is 'x' rupees. Since the total investment is INR 10,000, the amount invested in Scheme B must be the remaining portion, which is (10,000 - x) rupees.

Now, let's consider the interest earned from each scheme:

### Interest from Scheme A

The formula for simple interest is Principal × Rate × Time. For Scheme A:

*   **Principal:** x
*   **Rate:** 8% or 0.08 (as a decimal)
*   **Time:** 2 years

So, the interest from Scheme A = x × 0.08 × 2 = 0.16x

### Interest from Scheme B

For Scheme B:

*   **Principal:** (10,000 - x)
*   **Rate:** 10% or 0.10 (as a decimal)
*   **Time:** 2 years

So, the interest from Scheme B = (10,000 - x) × 0.10 × 2 = 0.20(10,000 - x)

## Combining the Interests

We know that the total interest earned from both schemes is INR 1,680. Therefore, we can set up an equation by adding the interest from Scheme A and Scheme B:

Interest from Scheme A + Interest from Scheme B = Total Interest

0.16x + 0.20(10,000 - x) = 1,680

## Solving for 'x'

Now, let's solve this equation step-by-step:

1.  **Distribute:** Multiply 0.20 by both terms inside the parenthesis:
    0.16x + (0.20 × 10,000) - (0.20 × x) = 1,680
    0.16x + 2,000 - 0.20x = 1,680

2.  **Combine like terms:** Group the 'x' terms together:
    (0.16x - 0.20x) + 2,000 = 1,680
    -0.04x + 2,000 = 1,680

3.  **Isolate the 'x' term:** Subtract 2,000 from both sides of the equation:
    -0.04x = 1,680 - 2,000
    -0.04x = -320

4.  **Solve for 'x':** Divide both sides by -0.04:
    x = -320 / -0.04
    x = 8,000

So, the amount invested in Scheme A is INR 8,000.

## Verifying the Answer

It's always a good practice to verify your answer. If INR 8,000 was invested in Scheme A, then INR 2,000 (10,000 - 8,000) was invested in Scheme B.

*   Interest from Scheme A: 8,000 × 0.08 × 2 = 1,280
*   Interest from Scheme B: 2,000 × 0.10 × 2 = 400
*   Total Interest: 1,280 + 400 = 1,680

This matches the given total interest, confirming our calculation is correct.
