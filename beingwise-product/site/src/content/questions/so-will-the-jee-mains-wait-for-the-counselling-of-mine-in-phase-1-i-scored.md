# So Will JEE Main Wait for My Counselling?

It's common to wonder how your JEE Main results in one phase might impact your opportunities, especially if you're planning to take the exam again. Let's clarify how the different phases of JEE Main and the subsequent counselling process, like JoSAA, work together.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your best percentile from all JEE Main attempts will be considered for ranking.
*   There is typically a time gap between JEE Main sessions and the start of counselling.
*   JoSAA counselling covers admissions to IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other GFTIs based on JEE Main and Advanced ranks.
*   Even if you don't qualify for JEE Advanced, JoSAA still offers pathways to various engineering colleges.

## Understanding JEE Main Multiple Sessions

The National Testing Agency (NTA) conducts JEE Main in multiple sessions each year, often referred to as Phase 1 and Phase 2. This structure is designed to give students more than one opportunity to perform their best. If you take both sessions, the NTA will consider your *best* percentile score out of all your attempts when calculating your final All India Rank (AIR) and Category Rank. This means your 76 percentile from Phase 1 is a good starting point, and improving it in Phase 2 could significantly enhance your prospects.

## The Link Between JEE Main and JoSAA Counselling

After all sessions of JEE Main are completed and the final ranks are declared, the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) begins its centralized counselling process. JoSAA is the official body responsible for allocating seats to eligible candidates for admission to 118 institutes, which include 23 IITs, 31 NITs, 26 IIITs, and 38 other Government Funded Technical Institutes (GFTIs). This process is crucial for anyone aspiring to these premier engineering colleges in India.

## Will Counselling Wait for Phase 2 Results?

Yes, the JoSAA counselling process always commences *after* all JEE Main sessions are concluded and the final ranks are announced. This ensures that every student, regardless of which session they appeared in, has their best possible rank considered for seat allocation. So, if you are giving Phase 2, your results from that session will be factored in before counselling begins. There is a planned schedule that provides a window for all exam processes to complete before the allocation rounds start.

## What if I Don't Qualify for JEE Advanced?

It's important not to be disheartened if your JEE Main rank doesn't meet the cutoff for JEE Advanced. While JEE Advanced is the gateway to the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), a significant number of other highly respected institutions, including the National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), and other GFTIs, admit students solely based on their JEE Main ranks through JoSAA counselling. These institutions offer excellent engineering programs and are highly sought after. JoSAA's comprehensive process ensures that you have multiple options based on your JEE Main performance.

Keep focused on your preparation for Phase 2, knowing that your best effort will be recognized in the upcoming counselling rounds.
