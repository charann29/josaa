# I got CAT score 65 % ican admission international business management or other branches

Scoring 65 percentile on the Common Admission Test (CAT) is a good starting point, and while it might be challenging for the absolute top-tier B-schools, it certainly opens doors to various reputable management programs, including International Business Management. Your admission prospects will ultimately depend on a holistic evaluation of your profile.

## Key Takeaways

*   A 65 percentile in CAT positions you for admission to many good management institutes, though likely not the very top-ranked ones.
*   Beyond CAT, your academic history, work experience, and performance in personal interviews are crucial.
*   It's wise to research colleges thoroughly, looking beyond just the CAT cutoff.
*   Consider applying to a diverse range of institutions, including those with moderate cutoffs.

## Understanding CAT Percentiles and Admissions

The CAT percentile is a significant component of the admission process for MBA and PGDM programs in India. However, it's rarely the sole criterion. Most business schools employ a comprehensive selection process that considers multiple aspects of an applicant's profile. A 65 percentile indicates that you performed better than 65% of the test-takers, which is a respectable score. While many of the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) and other top-tier institutions might have higher cutoff expectations, numerous excellent colleges accept scores in this range.

## Beyond the CAT Score: What Colleges Look For

Management institutes typically evaluate candidates on several parameters:

*   **Academic Record:** Your performance in 10th, 12th grade, and your undergraduate degree holds considerable weight. A strong academic background can significantly boost your application.
*   **Work Experience:** Relevant work experience, especially in a professional setting, is highly valued. It demonstrates maturity, practical skills, and a clearer understanding of career goals. The quality and duration of your work experience can often compensate for a slightly lower CAT score.
*   **Personal Interview (PI) and Written Ability Test (WAT):** Most colleges conduct PIs and often a WAT to assess your communication skills, general awareness, problem-solving abilities, and suitability for their program. Excelling in these rounds can be a game-changer.
*   **Extracurricular Activities and Achievements:** Participation in sports, clubs, volunteering, or any significant achievements outside academics can showcase leadership qualities, teamwork, and a well-rounded personality.

## Exploring International Business Management and Other Specializations

With a 65 percentile, pursuing an MBA or PGDM in International Business Management is definitely a viable option. Many institutes offer specialized programs in this area, focusing on global trade, cross-cultural management, and international finance. When researching, look for colleges that have a strong faculty, industry connections, and placement record specifically for their International Business programs. Besides International Business, you might also consider specializations like Marketing, Finance, Human Resources, Operations, or Information Technology, depending on your interests and career aspirations. It's important to align your chosen specialization with your long-term professional goals.

## Researching and Applying to Colleges

Begin by thoroughly researching various management institutes. Don't just focus on the overall NIRF rankings; instead, look into specific program details, faculty profiles, and placement reports for your desired specialization. Many institutes release their admission policies and cutoff trends on their official websites. Create a list of colleges that align with your profile and aspirations. It's advisable to apply to a mix of institutions: some where your score is competitive, and others where you might be slightly below the typical cutoff but have a strong overall profile. Always check the application deadlines and requirements carefully.

Your CAT score is an important piece of the puzzle, but a well-rounded application and strong performance in subsequent rounds can significantly improve your chances of securing admission to a good management program.
