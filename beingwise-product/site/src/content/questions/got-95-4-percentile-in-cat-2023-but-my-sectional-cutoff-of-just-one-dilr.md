# Can I Expect an IIM Call with 95.4 Percentile Overall but 68 Percentile in DILR?

It's common for aspiring MBA students to wonder about their chances with specific CAT scores, especially when one section might be a concern. Let's explore what your 95.4 percentile overall and 68 percentile in DILR could mean for admissions to newer IIMs like IIM Udaipur.

## Key Takeaways

*   Meeting both overall and sectional cutoffs is crucial for IIM shortlisting.
*   A strong overall percentile may not compensate for missing even one sectional cutoff.
*   IIM Udaipur, as part of the Common Admission Process (CAP), has specific sectional requirements.
*   Reserved category candidates typically have lower cutoffs, which could improve your chances.

## Understanding IIM Cutoffs

For admission to the Post Graduate Program (PGP) or MBA at any Indian Institute of Management (IIM), candidates must satisfy two main criteria: the overall CAT percentile and the individual sectional percentiles. These cutoffs are minimum thresholds set by each IIM to shortlist candidates for the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds, sometimes referred to as Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI).

Even if your overall percentile is very high, failing to meet the minimum cutoff in even one section, such as Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), or Quantitative Ability (QA), can prevent you from being shortlisted. This is a strict rule across most IIMs, including the newer ones.

## Your Situation: General Category Perspective

Assuming you are a General Category candidate, your 95.4 percentile overall is indeed excellent. However, your 68 percentile in the DILR section is likely to be a hurdle. Many IIMs, particularly those participating in the Common Admission Process (CAP), have sectional cutoffs that are higher than 68 percentile for the General Category.

For instance, if we consider IIM Udaipur, which is part of the CAP, their official criteria for General Category candidates typically require a high overall percentile (often around 94 percentile) and individual sectional percentiles that are also quite competitive, often around 73 percentile or higher for each section. Since your DILR percentile falls below this typical range, it's improbable that you would receive a shortlist call from IIM Udaipur or other CAP IIMs under the General Category.

## What About Reserved Categories?

If you belong to a reserved category (such as SC, ST, OBC-NCL, EWS, or PwD), the scenario changes significantly. IIMs provide considerable relaxation in both overall and sectional cutoffs for reserved category candidates. The cutoffs for these categories are substantially lower compared to the General Category.

For example, while a General Category candidate might need 73 percentile in DILR, a reserved category candidate could potentially be shortlisted with a much lower sectional percentile, possibly even below 68 percentile, depending on the specific IIM and the category. Therefore, if you are a reserved category candidate, your 68 percentile in DILR, combined with your strong overall 95.4 percentile, could very well put you in contention for a WAT-PI shortlist from IIM Udaipur and other newer or baby IIMs.

## Next Steps

It's important to carefully review the official admission policies and cutoff criteria published by each IIM you are interested in. These details are usually available on their respective official websites. For CAP IIMs, the Common Admission Process portal will also provide consolidated information.

Understanding these specific requirements for your category is key to assessing your actual chances for a call.
