## Understanding IIM Eligibility

For admission to the prestigious Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs), there are several stages. The first step is appearing for the Common Admission Test (CAT). Following the CAT, candidates are shortlisted for the next stages: the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI). These later stages are critical in the final selection process.

**Academic Requirements:**

One key criterion for IIMs is the minimum percentage in your Bachelor's degree. For candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Persons with Disability (PWD/DA) categories, the minimum requirement is 45% in their graduation. For general category candidates, it is 50%. Since you have 68% in your BSc, you comfortably meet this eligibility criterion for IIMs under the SC category.

It's also worth noting that final-year Bachelor's degree students awaiting results can also apply, provided they meet the criteria when their results are declared.

## The Role of CAT and Shortlisting

While meeting the academic percentage is the first hurdle, a strong performance in the CAT exam is paramount. IIMs set specific CAT cut-offs for shortlisting candidates for the WAT and PI rounds. These cut-offs can vary significantly between IIMs and also depend on the candidate's category.

Your 10th and 12th scores (68% and 47.8% respectively) are part of your academic profile. While some IIMs place a certain weight on past academics, the CAT score, followed by your performance in the WAT and PI, often carries substantial weight in the final admission decision.

## Navigating the Selection Process

The final admission to an IIM is not solely based on your CAT score. It's a holistic assessment that considers multiple factors:

*   **CAT Score:** Your percentile in the CAT exam.
*   **Academic Profile:** Your scores in 10th, 12th, and Bachelor's degree.
*   **Work Experience:** If applicable, the duration and quality of your work experience.
*   **Category:** Your SC category will be considered as per government regulations.
*   **WAT & PI Performance:** Your performance in the Written Ability Test and Personal Interview rounds. These are crucial for demonstrating your communication skills, critical thinking, and suitability for an MBA program.

Therefore, even after clearing the CAT cut-off, excelling in the WAT and PI rounds is vital for securing a final admission offer.

## IIMs and Other Top B-Schools to Consider

Given your eligibility, you can aim for a wide range of IIMs. The older, more established IIMs like IIM Ahmedabad, IIM Bangalore, IIM Calcutta, IIM Lucknow, IIM Indore, and IIM Kozhikode are highly sought after. There are also many other excellent IIMs, including the newer and third-generation ones, which offer fantastic management education. With a strong CAT score, you could be eligible for shortlisting at many of these institutes.

Beyond the IIMs, several other highly-ranked B-schools in India accept CAT scores. Some notable examples include Faculty of Management Studies (FMS) Delhi, SP Jain Institute of Management and Research (SPJIMR) Mumbai, Management Development Institute (MDI) Gurgaon, and Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT). These institutions also have their own selection processes and criteria, but a good CAT score is generally the entry point.

Your best strategy is to focus on preparing thoroughly for the CAT exam, aiming for the highest possible percentile. Simultaneously, start honing your communication and analytical skills for the WAT and PI rounds. With dedication, you have a strong chance of getting into a top B-school.
