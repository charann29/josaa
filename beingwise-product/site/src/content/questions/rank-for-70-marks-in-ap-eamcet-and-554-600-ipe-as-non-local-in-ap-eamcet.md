# What will be my rank for 70 marks in AP EAMCET and 554/600 IPE as a non-local in AP EAMCET?

Understanding your potential rank in AP EAMCET is a crucial step for planning your engineering admissions. This guide will help you estimate your standing based on the scores you've provided and explore the possibilities for non-local candidates.

## Key Takeaways

*   With 70 marks in AP EAMCET and 554/600 in IPE (MPC stream) as an OC candidate, your rank could be in the 10,000-12,000 range.
*   As a non-local applicant, you still have good opportunities for admission to reputable engineering colleges in Andhra Pradesh.
*   Final ranks are influenced by the total number of candidates, their performance, and the difficulty of the exam each year.
*   Always refer to the official AP EAMCET information for the most accurate and up-to-date details.

## Understanding Your AP EAMCET Rank Calculation

AP EAMCET ranks are not solely determined by your entrance exam score. The Andhra Pradesh State Council of Higher Education (APSCHE) considers both your AP EAMCET marks and your Intermediate Public Examination (IPE) marks. Specifically, 75% weightage is given to your AP EAMCET score, and 25% weightage is given to your IPE marks in the relevant subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics for engineering streams). This combined score then determines your overall rank.

For your scores of 70 in AP EAMCET and 554/600 in IPE (MPC), assuming you are an Open Category (OC) candidate, an estimated rank would likely fall in the range of 10,000 to 12,000. This is an approximate figure based on previous years' trends and the competitive nature of the exam.

## Non-Local Candidate Status and Seat Allotment

As a non-local candidate, it's important to understand the seat reservation policies in Andhra Pradesh. A certain percentage of seats in unreserved categories are open to both local and non-local candidates. While local candidates generally have more reserved seats, your estimated rank of 10,000-12,000 is often competitive enough to secure a seat in many good institutions, even as a non-local.

The key is to participate actively in the counselling process and make informed choices during seat selection. Keep an eye on the official AP EAMCET counselling schedule and guidelines for non-local candidates.

## Potential Colleges for Your Rank

With a rank in the 10,000-12,000 range, several well-regarded engineering colleges in Andhra Pradesh could be accessible to you. Based on past admission trends, institutions like JNTUK Vizianagaram, Aditya College of Engineering and Technology, Sri Sai Institute of Technology and Sciences, and RVR & JC College of Engineering are examples of colleges where candidates with similar ranks have secured admission. These colleges offer various engineering disciplines, and specific branch availability will depend on cut-offs for that particular year.

It is always advisable to review the previous years' cut-off ranks published by the official AP EAMCET authority during the counselling process to get a more precise idea of your chances for specific branches and colleges.

## Counselling and Seat Allocation Process

The AP EAMCET counselling process typically involves online registration, document verification, choice filling, seat allotment, and self-reporting/reporting to the allotted college. During the choice filling stage, you will be able to select colleges and branches based on your preference. It's crucial to research colleges thoroughly and prioritize your choices effectively. The official AP EAMCET portal will provide detailed instructions and schedules for each stage of counselling.

Your journey to an engineering college in Andhra Pradesh looks promising with your scores. Stay informed through official channels and prepare diligently for the counselling process.
