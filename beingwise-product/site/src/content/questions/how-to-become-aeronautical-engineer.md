# How to become aeronautical engineer

Aeronautical engineering is an exciting field for those passionate about aircraft and space technology. It involves the design, development, testing, and maintenance of aircraft and related systems, playing a crucial role in both the aviation and defense industries.

## Key takeaways

*   You'll need a strong foundation in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics from your 12th standard.
*   Admission to top B.Tech programs typically requires competitive scores in JEE Main and JEE Advanced.
*   Aeronautical engineers work on designing aircraft, propulsion systems, and researching new materials.
*   The field offers diverse career paths in aviation, defense, and space exploration.

## What does an Aeronautical Engineer do?

Aeronautical engineers are at the forefront of aviation innovation. Their primary responsibilities include designing various types of aircraft, from commercial planes to helicopters and drones, and developing advanced propulsion systems that power them. They also conduct research into the aerodynamic properties of materials and structures used in aircraft construction, always aiming to improve efficiency, safety, and performance. This field is vital for advancing both civilian air travel and national defense capabilities.

## Educational qualifications

To pursue a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) in Aeronautical Engineering, aspiring students must meet specific academic criteria. You need to have successfully completed your Class 12th examination, or its equivalent, with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) as core subjects. Most institutions require a minimum aggregate score, typically ranging from 70% to 75% in these subjects, though this can vary by college and program. A strong grasp of these foundational sciences is essential for the rigorous engineering curriculum ahead.

## Admission process

Admission to undergraduate aeronautical engineering programs in India's leading colleges is highly competitive. The primary route for most institutions involves entrance examinations. You will generally need to appear for and achieve good scores in the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main. For admission to the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and other top-tier engineering colleges, a successful score in JEE Advanced is also mandatory. Prospective students should regularly check the official JoSAA portal for detailed information on counseling and seat allocation processes based on these exam scores.

## Top institutions and career prospects

Many reputable engineering colleges across India offer B.Tech programs in Aeronautical Engineering. When researching institutions, it's advisable to consult official sources like the NIRF rankings to understand their standing and academic reputation. Graduates in this field find opportunities in a wide range of sectors, including aircraft manufacturing companies, airlines, defense organizations (such as DRDO), space research organizations (like ISRO), and even in companies developing drones and advanced air mobility solutions. The demand for skilled aeronautical engineers remains consistent, driven by advancements in aerospace technology and the expanding aviation industry.

Embarking on a career in aeronautical engineering promises a challenging yet rewarding journey for those ready to shape the future of flight.
