# I am getting 140 marks in Part A in UCEED 2025. Can anybody guide if there are any chances of getting IIT?

Scoring 140 marks in Part A of UCEED 2025 is a strong performance, and it's natural to wonder about your prospects for admission to an IIT. While the final picture depends on your Part B (drawing) score, we can look at past trends and the structure of UCEED to give you some guidance.

## Key Takeaways

*   A score of 140 in UCEED Part A is significantly above average, placing you in a strong position even before Part B scores are known.
*   IIT admission relies on the combined score of Part A and Part B, with Part B holding substantial weight.
*   Previous years' UCEED ranks for IIT admissions typically fall within the top 100 for the General category.
*   Cut-offs can fluctuate each year based on the difficulty of the exam, the number of applicants, and overall candidate performance.

## Understanding UCEED Scoring and Shortlisting

The UCEED examination is divided into two parts: Part A (computer-based) and Part B (drawing). Your score of 140 is for Part A, which tests your aptitude in areas like visualization, observation, analysis, and general knowledge. The mean score for Part A in UCEED 2025 was 61.50, with a standard deviation of 25.69. Your score of 140 is well above this average, indicating a very strong performance in this section.

Candidates are first shortlisted based on a minimum qualifying mark in Part A. The official UCEED committee calculates these cut-offs using the mean and standard deviation of Part A scores for each category. Only those who meet this Part A cut-off have their Part B answer sheets evaluated. This means your high Part A score already puts you in a good position for this initial shortlisting stage.

## The Crucial Role of Part B (Drawing)

While your Part A score is excellent, the final UCEED merit list is prepared by combining the scores from both Part A and Part B. Part B, the drawing section, carries significant weight and evaluates your ability to sketch, observe, and communicate ideas visually. A strong performance in Part B is absolutely essential to secure a high overall rank, even with an outstanding Part A score.

Without knowing your Part B score, it's impossible to give a definitive answer about IIT admission. However, if your Part B performance is also strong, your chances significantly increase.

## Previous Year IIT Admission Ranks (General Category)

Looking at past years can provide some context, though it's important to remember that these are not guarantees for the current year. For instance, in UCEED 2023, the closing ranks for the General category at various IITs were:

*   **IIT Bombay:** Ranks 1-16
*   **IIT Guwahati:** Ranks 17-59
*   **IIT Hyderabad:** Ranks 21-53
*   **IIITDM Jabalpur:** Ranks 55-93

These figures show that admission to the B.Des programs at IITs is highly competitive, typically requiring a rank within the top 100 for the General category. Your Part A score suggests you have the potential to achieve such a rank if your Part B score is also competitive.

## What to Do Next

Since your Part B score is yet to be revealed, the best course of action is to await the official UCEED results and the release of the final merit list. Once your combined score and rank are available, you can compare them against previous years' trends for a clearer picture. Keep an eye on the official UCEED portal for all announcements regarding results, cut-offs, and counselling schedules. Your strong Part A score gives you a definite advantage, so stay optimistic!
