# IIM Udaipur Admissions: Understanding Your Chances with a 93 Percentile CAT Score

Many aspiring students wonder about their chances of admission to top B-schools like IIM Udaipur, especially with a strong CAT percentile and specific academic backgrounds. Let's break down what your profile might mean for your application.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your 93 percentile in CAT is excellent and well above the typical sectional and overall cut-offs for the SC category at IIM Udaipur.
*   While academic percentages (10th, 12th, and undergraduate) are considered, your CAT score gives you a strong initial advantage.
*   Being a fresher means your profile will be evaluated without work experience, focusing on academic performance and CAT.
*   As an SC category candidate, you benefit from specific reservation policies and lower qualifying cut-offs.

## Understanding IIM Udaipur's Admission Process

IIM Udaipur, like other IIMs, follows a multi-stage admission process. It typically begins with shortlisting candidates based on their CAT scores, academic performance in 10th, 12th, and undergraduate degrees, and sometimes work experience (though not applicable for freshers). For reserved categories, the qualifying cut-offs are generally lower than for the General category.

## Your Profile: A Closer Look

Let's analyze your specifics:

*   **CAT Score:** Your 93 percentile is a significant strength. Looking at past IIM Udaipur cut-offs for the SC category, which were around 50 percentile for each section (QA, DILR, VRC) and 60 percentile overall, your score comfortably exceeds these. This means you are highly likely to be shortlisted for the next stages of the admission process.
*   **Academics:**
    *   10th Standard: 84%
    *   12th Standard: 75%
    *   Undergraduate Degree: 60%

    While your undergraduate percentage of 60% might be a point of consideration, your strong CAT score can often compensate for this, especially given the holistic evaluation process. IIMs typically assign weights to academic performance, but the CAT score usually holds the highest weight for initial shortlisting.
*   **Work Experience:** As a fresher, your application will be evaluated purely on your academic record and CAT performance. IIMs appreciate diversity in their cohorts, including freshers.
*   **Category:** Your SC category status is a significant advantage, as IIMs adhere to government reservation policies, leading to lower qualifying criteria for these categories.

## The Shortlisting and Final Selection Stages

After the initial CAT-based shortlisting, candidates typically proceed to the Personal Interview (PI) and sometimes a Written Ability Test (WAT). Your performance in these stages is crucial for final selection. The final merit list is prepared by combining scores from CAT, academic records, work experience (if any), diversity factors, and performance in the PI/WAT.

### Weightage Example (Illustrative)

While exact weightages vary each year and are officially released by IIM Udaipur, a typical breakdown might look something like this for the final merit list:
