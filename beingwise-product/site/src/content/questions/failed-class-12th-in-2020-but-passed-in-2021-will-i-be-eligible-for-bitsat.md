# I failed class 12th in 2020 but passed in 2021, will I be eligible for BITSAT 2022?

Navigating university admissions can be tricky, especially when your academic journey hasn't followed a straightforward path. If you passed your Class 12 exams in 2021 after an earlier attempt in 2020, you might be wondering about your eligibility for BITSAT 2022.

## Key Takeaways

*   BITSAT 2022 eligibility generally requires passing Class 12 in 2021 or appearing in 2022.
*   If you took multiple attempts for Class 12, only your latest performance is considered.
*   A minimum of 75% aggregate in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (or Biology) is essential.
*   You must also achieve at least 60% in each of these individual subjects.

## Understanding BITSAT 2022 Eligibility

The Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test (BITSAT) has specific criteria for candidates. According to the official BITSAT information, only students who passed their Class 12 examination in 2021 or are appearing for it in 2022 are generally considered eligible. This means that if you passed your Class 12 exams in 2020 or earlier, you would typically not be eligible for BITSAT 2022.

## Multiple Attempts at Class 12

An important point for students who took more than one attempt to clear their Class 12 examination is that BITSAT considers only your latest performance. This rule applies provided your latest attempt covered the full set of subjects or courses required for the examination. So, if you failed in 2020 but successfully passed all subjects in 2021, your 2021 results are what matter.

## Academic Score Requirements

Beyond the year of passing, academic scores are also a crucial part of the BITSAT eligibility. Candidates must have achieved a minimum aggregate of 75% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. If you opted for Biology instead of Mathematics in BITSAT, then the 75% aggregate applies to Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Furthermore, it's not just the aggregate that counts; you must also secure at least 60% marks in each of these individual subjects (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology).

## What This Means for You

Given these guidelines, if you passed your Class 12 in 2021 after an earlier attempt in 2020, and you meet the minimum percentage requirements (75% aggregate in PCM/PCB and 60% in each subject), then your 2021 passing year makes you eligible for BITSAT 2022. The key is that your final passing year falls within the accepted window of 2021 or 2022.

Always refer to the official BITSAT admissions portal for the most up-to-date and precise eligibility details.
