# I passed my CBSE Class 12th in 2020 but failed to clear JEE MAIN in 2020 as I met an accident in 2021 so this year I will give board exam again with NIOS so my doubt is which marksheet should I use for BITSAT and JEE MAIN ?? As I have not appeared in BITSAT in 2020 but appeared for JEE MAIN 2020 ??

It's completely understandable to have questions about which academic records to use when you've appeared for Class 12 exams multiple times. Let's clarify how your NIOS and CBSE mark sheets can be used for BITSAT and JEE Main.

## Key Takeaways

*   You can generally use either your CBSE 2020 or NIOS 2022 Class 12 mark sheet for JEE Main and BITSAT, as both are valid passing certificates.
*   Using your more recent NIOS 2022 mark sheet is often advisable, especially if your scores are better, as it reflects your latest academic qualification.
*   For JEE Main, you are likely in your final eligible attempt window, and eligibility for JEE Advanced is tied to specific criteria, including the year of passing Class 12.
*   Ensure your NIOS subjects align with the requirements for engineering admissions (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics).

## Understanding JEE Main Eligibility and Mark Sheet Usage

The National Testing Agency (NTA), which conducts JEE Main, requires candidates to have passed their Class 12 (or equivalent) examination. Since you passed your CBSE Class 12 in 2020 and are now passing with NIOS in 2022, both mark sheets demonstrate that you meet the fundamental educational qualification.

However, it's crucial to consider the year of passing. JEE Main has specific rules regarding the number of attempts and the years in which Class 12 must have been passed. Typically, candidates can appear for JEE Main in the year they pass Class 12 and the subsequent two years. Having passed in 2020, your 2022 attempt would likely fall within the last eligible window. Always refer to the official JEE Main information bulletin for the most current rules on eligibility criteria and the number of attempts.

When filling out the application form, you will declare your Class 12 details. If you've improved your scores with NIOS, it makes sense to use those details. The system generally allows you to enter the details of the qualifying examination you wish to present.

## BITSAT Requirements and Your Mark Sheets

For BITSAT, conducted by BITS Pilani, similar principles apply. You need to have passed your Class 12 examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, along with adequate proficiency in English. BITSAT also specifies minimum aggregate percentage requirements (typically 75% overall in PCM and at least 60% in each subject). 

Since you did not appear for BITSAT in 2020, your NIOS 2022 mark sheet would be the most relevant and current document to present. As long as your NIOS certificate meets the subject and percentage criteria, it will be perfectly acceptable. The BITSAT information brochure, available on the official BITS admissions portal, will provide the definitive guidelines.

## JEE Advanced Eligibility: A Key Distinction

It's important to differentiate between JEE Main and JEE Advanced eligibility. While passing Class 12 is a prerequisite for both, JEE Advanced has stricter rules concerning the year of passing Class 12. Generally, you are eligible for JEE Advanced only in the year you pass Class 12 and the subsequent year. Since you passed CBSE Class 12 in 2020, your eligibility for JEE Advanced based on that certificate would have expired after the 2021 examination. Even if you pass NIOS in 2022, this typically does not reset your JEE Advanced eligibility clock if you've already passed Class 12 previously. Always consult the official JEE Advanced information brochure for precise details, as these rules are strictly enforced.

## Preparing for Success in Your Final Attempt

Given that this is likely your final attempt for JEE Main, a focused preparation strategy is vital. 

*   **Know the Syllabus:** Thoroughly understand the JEE Main syllabus, which is based on the Class 11 and 12 curriculum.
*   **Understand the Exam Pattern:** Familiarize yourself with the structure, marking scheme, and types of questions asked.
*   **Strategic Planning:** Create a realistic study timetable that covers all subjects, allocating more time to weaker areas.
*   **Practice with Mock Tests:** Regularly taking mock tests and solving previous year's JEE Main papers is crucial. This helps in time management, identifying recurring question patterns, and building exam temperament. Many official resources and reputable platforms offer these practice materials.

Good luck with your preparations and examinations!
