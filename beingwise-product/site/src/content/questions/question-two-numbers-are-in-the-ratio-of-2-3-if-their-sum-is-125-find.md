# Question: Two numbers are in the ratio of 2: 3. If their sum is 125, find the numbers.

This page explores a common type of math problem often found in competitive exams, focusing on ratios and basic algebra. Understanding how to approach such questions is crucial for improving your problem-solving skills.

## Key Takeaways

*   Ratio problems require setting up variables based on the given ratio.
*   Formulating a simple algebraic equation is the next step to solve for the unknown.
*   Always verify your answer by plugging the calculated numbers back into the original problem statement.
*   This type of question tests fundamental arithmetic and algebraic reasoning, which are building blocks for more complex problems.

## Understanding the Problem

The problem states that two numbers have a ratio of 2:3. This means that for every 2 parts of the first number, there are 3 parts of the second number. It also tells us that when these two numbers are added together, their total is 125. Our goal is to find the exact values of these two numbers.

## Setting Up the Equation

To solve this, we can represent the two numbers using a common multiplier, often denoted as 'x'.

*   Let the first number be 2x.
*   Let the second number be 3x.

Since their sum is 125, we can write the equation:

2x + 3x = 125

## Solving for 'x'

Now, we combine the 'x' terms and solve for 'x':

5x = 125

To find 'x', we divide 125 by 5:

x = 125 / 5
x = 25

## Finding the Numbers

Once we have the value of 'x', we can find the two original numbers:

*   First number = 2x = 2 * 25 = 50
*   Second number = 3x = 3 * 25 = 75

## Verifying the Solution

It's always a good practice to check if our calculated numbers satisfy the original conditions:

*   **Ratio:** 50 : 75. If we divide both by 25, we get 2:3. This matches the given ratio.
*   **Sum:** 50 + 75 = 125. This matches the given sum.

Both conditions are met, confirming that 50 and 75 are indeed the correct numbers. Among the given options (50 and 75, 24 and 36, 20 and 30, 32 and 78), Option 1 is the correct choice.
