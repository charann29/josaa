# Can I Get B.Pharmacy at ICT Mumbai with 97.86 Percentile as an OBC-NCL Girl?

Securing a B.Pharmacy seat at the Institute of Chemical Technology (ICT), Mumbai, is highly competitive. Let's explore your prospects with a 97.86 percentile, considering you are an OBC-NCL category female applicant.

## Key Takeaways

*   Admission to ICT Mumbai's B.Pharmacy program is extremely competitive, with high cut-offs even for reserved categories.
*   Historical data for the OBC-NCL (girls) category suggests cut-offs often exceed 99 percentile for B.Pharmacy at ICT Mumbai.
*   While your 97.86 percentile is strong overall, it might be challenging for direct admission in the initial rounds for ICT Mumbai's B.Pharmacy.
*   It is crucial to participate actively in the Centralized Admission Process (CAP) counselling and keep alternative reputable colleges in mind.

## Understanding ICT Mumbai's B.Pharmacy Admissions

ICT Mumbai is renowned for its excellence in chemical technology and pharmacy education. This prestige naturally translates into very high demand for its programs, including B.Pharmacy. Admission is primarily based on MHT CET scores, though JEE Main scores can also be considered for some state-level admissions. The cut-off percentiles are typically among the highest in Maharashtra for all categories, including reserved ones.

## OBC-NCL (Girls) Category Cut-offs for B.Pharmacy

Based on recent years' admission trends, the closing percentiles for B.Pharmacy at ICT Mumbai for the OBC-NCL (girls) category have generally been very high. Many sources indicate these cut-offs often fall in the range of 99.3 to 99.8 percentile. For example, in a recent year, the closing percentile for this category was around 99.63.

Your 97.86 percentile, while excellent, is below these typical closing marks. This suggests that securing a seat in the initial CAP rounds might be difficult.

## The Role of CAP Counselling and Later Rounds

Despite the challenging cut-offs, it is absolutely essential to participate diligently in the CAP counselling process conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra. While direct admission in the first few rounds might be tough, seats can sometimes become available in later rounds, including mop-up rounds, due to various reasons like candidates choosing other colleges or changes in preferences. Always list ICT Mumbai as your first preference if it's your dream college.

## Exploring Alternative Pharmacy Colleges

Given the intense competition for ICT Mumbai, it is a wise strategy to research and identify other highly-regarded pharmacy colleges in Maharashtra. This approach ensures you have strong backup options and increases your overall chances of securing admission to a quality institution. Some excellent alternatives to consider include:

*   Bombay College of Pharmacy, Mumbai
*   SVKM's Dr. Bhanuben Nanavati College of Pharmacy, Mumbai
*   Poona College of Pharmacy, Pune

These colleges also offer excellent B.Pharmacy programs and maintain high academic standards. Carefully research their admission criteria, previous year cut-offs, and facilities to make informed decisions during counselling.

While your percentile is strong, securing a B.Pharmacy seat at ICT Mumbai might be challenging; however, active participation in counselling and exploring other top-tier institutions will maximize your opportunities.
