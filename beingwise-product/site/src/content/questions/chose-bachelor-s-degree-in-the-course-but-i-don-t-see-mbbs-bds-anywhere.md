# I chose bachelor's degree in the course but I don't see mbbs/bds anywhere in the form What should I select

It's perfectly natural to be a little confused when filling out application forms, especially when you're looking for MBBS or BDS but only see a general 'bachelor's degree' option. Let's clarify why this happens and what steps you should take.

## Key Takeaways

*   MBBS and BDS are specialized primary medical degrees, not typically listed under a generic 'bachelor's degree' category in application forms.
*   Admission to MBBS and BDS courses in India is almost exclusively through the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test).
*   If you're aiming for MBBS/BDS, you need to apply directly through the NEET counseling process, not usually through general university application portals.
*   If your primary goal isn't MBBS/BDS, there are numerous excellent Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) courses that offer strong career paths.

## Understanding MBBS and BDS Admissions

MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) and BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery) are indeed bachelor's degrees, but they are highly specialized professional programs. In India, admission to these courses is predominantly regulated by a single national entrance examination: the NEET (UG). 

Colleges offering MBBS and BDS degrees conduct their admissions based on NEET scores through a centralized counseling process, such as that managed by the Medical Counseling Committee (MCC) for All India Quota seats, or state-level counseling authorities for state quota seats. This means you typically won't find MBBS or BDS listed as options on a general application form for a university that offers a wide range of undergraduate programs, unless it's a specific medical university's dedicated application portal.

## The Role of NEET

If you aspire to pursue MBBS or BDS, your primary focus should be on appearing for and qualifying in the NEET (UG) examination. The eligibility criteria for NEET are clearly outlined by the National Testing Agency (NTA), which conducts the exam. Once NEET results are declared, eligible candidates participate in the counseling process. It is during this counseling that you will select your preferred MBBS or BDS colleges and courses based on your rank and seat availability.

## When to Choose 'Bachelor's Degree'

If you've filled out a form and selected 'bachelor's degree' but don't see MBBS or BDS, it likely means the form is for general undergraduate programs, such as Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.), Bachelor of Arts (B.A.), Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com), or Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech). 

If your intention was to apply for a B.Sc. or another non-medical bachelor's program, then selecting 'bachelor's degree' is correct, and you should proceed to choose the specific stream or subject you are interested in (e.g., B.Sc. Physics, B.Sc. Chemistry, B.Sc. Biology, B.Sc. Computer Science, etc.). Many B.Sc. programs, particularly in fields like Biotechnology, Microbiology, or Life Sciences, can lead to excellent career opportunities and further studies.

## What to do Next

1.  **If your goal is MBBS/BDS:** Confirm that you have appeared for NEET (UG) and meet its eligibility criteria. Your application process for these courses will happen through the official NEET counseling portals, not typically through a general university application form. Consult the official MCC website or your respective state's medical counseling authority for details.
2.  **If you are open to other bachelor's degrees:** If you meet the eligibility for the form you are filling and are interested in other undergraduate courses, carefully review the available options under 'bachelor's degree.' Consider courses that align with your interests, strengths, and career aspirations. Research different B.Sc. programs; many offer promising futures in various industries, research, or even lead to postgraduate medical-related fields like Pharmacy or Physiotherapy.

Always ensure you are consulting official sources for admission information, such as the NTA website for NEET details, the MCC portal for medical counseling, or specific university websites for their respective program applications.
