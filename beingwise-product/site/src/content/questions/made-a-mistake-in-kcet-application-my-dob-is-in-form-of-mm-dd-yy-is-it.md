# I made a mistake in KCET application...my DOB is in form of mm/dd/yy is it wrong or if it is wrong can i change it please tell me

It's completely normal to feel a bit anxious if you've spotted an error in your application, especially something as crucial as your date of birth. Let's clarify the correct format for your KCET application and how you can go about fixing it.

## Key takeaways

*   The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) generally requires your Date of Birth (DOB) in the DD/MM/YYYY format for the KCET application.
*   Entering it as MM/DD/YY is incorrect and could cause issues with your application processing.
*   KEA usually provides a specific correction window for applicants to edit details.
*   If the correction window is closed, contact the KEA helpline directly with your supporting documents.

## Understanding the Correct DOB Format for KCET

The official guidelines for the KCET application typically specify that your date of birth must be entered in the DD/MM/YYYY format. This means the day comes first, followed by the month, and then the full four-digit year. If you've entered it as MM/DD/YY (month/day/two-digit year), this is considered an incorrect format and needs to be rectified.

## Why an Incorrect DOB Format Matters

An incorrect date of birth format can lead to several problems. During the automated processing of applications, systems are designed to look for specific data formats. A mismatch can cause your application to be flagged, potentially leading to delays or even rejection if not corrected. It's vital that all your details, especially personal identification like your DOB, match your official documents precisely.

## How to Correct Your Date of Birth

The KEA understands that errors can happen, and they usually facilitate a process for corrections. Here's what you should do:

### During the Official Correction Window

1.  **Check the KEA Website:** Regularly visit the official KEA website. They will announce specific dates for the application correction window. This is your primary opportunity to make changes.
2.  **Log In and Find the Edit Option:** Once the correction window is open, log in to your KCET applicant account. Look for an option that allows you to 'Edit' your application details.
3.  **Update Your DOB:** Navigate to the date of birth field and carefully change it to the correct DD/MM/YYYY format.
4.  **Save and Resubmit:** After making the correction, ensure you save the changes and then formally resubmit your application. Always double-check that the changes have been applied successfully.

### If the Correction Window is Closed

If you discover the error after the official correction window has closed, don't panic, but act quickly:

1.  **Contact the KCET Helpline:** Immediately reach out to the KCET helpline or the support team. You'll find their contact details on the official KEA website. Explain your situation clearly and concisely.
2.  **Prepare Supporting Documents:** Be ready to provide official proof of your correct date of birth. This typically includes your SSLC (10th standard) certificate, passport, or Aadhar card, as these are universally accepted documents for age verification.

## Important Reminders

It is critical to correct your date of birth before the final deadline for application corrections. Failing to do so could unfortunately lead to your application being rejected or significantly delayed in processing. Always refer to the official KEA website for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding application procedures and timelines.

Stay calm and follow these steps diligently. Correcting this detail promptly will ensure your KCET application proceeds smoothly.
