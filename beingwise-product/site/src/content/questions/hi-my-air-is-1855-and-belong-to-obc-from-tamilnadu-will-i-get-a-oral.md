# Hi.. My AIR is 1855 and belong to OBC from Tamilnadu..Will i get a Oral Surgery seat under self finacing quota in tamilnadu or karnataka(General category) state counselling?

It's understandable to be curious about your chances for an Oral Surgery (MDS) seat, especially with an All India Rank (AIR) of 1855 and OBC status from Tamil Nadu. Let's explore the possibilities for self-financing quotas in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka.

## Key Takeaways

*   An AIR of 1855 for OBC candidates presents a moderate chance for an MDS Oral Surgery seat in self-financing categories.
*   State counselling for Tamil Nadu and Karnataka will have distinct reservation policies and seat matrix details.
*   Self-financing quotas often have different cut-offs compared to government-funded seats.
*   Careful participation in counselling, understanding the seat matrix, and preference filling are crucial.

## Understanding Your Rank and Category

Your All India Rank (AIR) of 1855 is a strong performance. As an OBC candidate from Tamil Nadu, you'll benefit from reservation policies during state counselling. However, the specific impact of your rank will vary depending on the total number of seats available, the number of applicants in your category, and the cut-offs in previous years for Oral Surgery specifically.

## Tamil Nadu State Counselling for MDS

Tamil Nadu's Directorate of Medical Education (DME) conducts the state counselling for MDS admissions. For self-financing quotas, the cut-offs can sometimes be lower than for government seats, making them a viable option. When counselling begins, you'll need to carefully review the official prospectus and seat matrix published by the DME. This document will detail:

*   The total number of MDS seats in various specialties, including Oral Surgery, across all colleges.
*   The distribution of seats between government and self-financing institutions.
*   The specific reservation percentages for OBC candidates within the state quota.

It's important to note that while you are an OBC candidate, the self-financing quota might have slightly different dynamics. However, your category will still be considered for state-specific reservations.

## Karnataka State Counselling for MDS (General Category)

Karnataka Examination Authority (KEA) is responsible for conducting the MDS counselling for colleges within Karnataka. Since you're considering the general category here, your OBC status from Tamil Nadu will not apply for reservation benefits in Karnataka's state quota. Your AIR of 1855 will be considered purely against the unreserved (general) seats.

Karnataka also has a significant number of private dental colleges offering MDS courses under self-financing categories. The cut-offs for these seats in the general category will depend on demand and the number of applicants. You should consult the official KEA website for their detailed information bulletin and seat matrix when counselling opens. Pay close attention to the fee structures for self-financing seats, as they can be considerably higher than government-funded places.

## The Role of Counselling

Counselling is a dynamic process. Even with a good rank, strategic participation is key. Here's what to keep in mind:

*   **Stay Updated:** Regularly check the official websites of the DME Tamil Nadu and KEA Karnataka for schedules, notifications, and seat matrix updates.
*   **Preference Filling:** Research colleges thoroughly. List your preferred colleges and specialties (Oral Surgery) in the order of your genuine interest. Ensure you include a mix of colleges with varying popularity to maximize your chances.
*   **Mock Allotments:** If available, participate in any mock allotment rounds to get an idea of where your rank might stand.

While there's no guarantee, an AIR of 1855 for an OBC candidate provides a moderate to good chance, especially when considering self-financing quotas in both states. Diligent preparation for counselling will be your best asset.
