# BSc Cardiac Technology 2023 Cutoff Marks

Aspiring to pursue a Bachelor of Science in Cardiac Technology? Understanding the cutoff marks for admission is a crucial first step. This guide provides an overview of the 2023 admission criteria based on NEET scores.

## Key Takeaways

*   Admission to BSc Cardiac Technology programs primarily relies on NEET entrance examination scores.
*   For general category candidates, the qualifying NEET score typically needs to be around 147.
*   Candidates under the General-PH (Physically Handicapped) category generally require a minimum NEET score of approximately 129.
*   These cutoffs represent the minimum eligibility for consideration, not a guarantee of admission, as actual college cutoffs can be higher.

## Understanding NEET Scores for BSc Cardiac Technology

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is the gateway for many medical and allied health science courses in India, including BSc Cardiac Technology. Your performance in this examination directly impacts your eligibility for various institutions. While specific colleges might have their own merit lists, the initial hurdle is always meeting the minimum qualifying score set by the examination authorities.

## General Category Cutoff

For students applying under the general category, the NEET 2023 qualifying score for allied health programs like Cardiac Technology has historically been around 147. This figure serves as a benchmark, indicating the minimum score needed to be considered eligible for counseling and admission processes across various institutions. It's important to remember that this is a *qualifying* score; actual admission to a competitive program might require significantly higher scores depending on the college and the number of applicants.

## General-PH Category Cutoff

Candidates falling under the General-Physically Handicapped (General-PH) category have a different qualifying score. For 2023, the minimum NEET score for these candidates has typically been set at approximately 129. This adjustment is made to ensure equitable access to medical education for students with disabilities, aligning with reservation policies. As with the general category, meeting this minimum score makes you eligible, but final admission depends on other factors like seat availability and institution-specific merit lists.

## Factors Influencing College-Specific Cutoffs

While the NEET qualifying scores provide a baseline, individual colleges and universities often have their own, higher cutoff marks for admission to BSc Cardiac Technology programs. Several factors contribute to these variations:

*   **Number of Applicants:** A higher number of applicants for a limited number of seats will naturally drive up the cutoff scores.
*   **Institution's Reputation:** Top-ranked institutions, often recognized by bodies like NIRF rankings, tend to have higher cutoffs due to increased demand.
*   **Seat Matrix:** The total number of seats available in a particular college for the Cardiac Technology program plays a significant role.
*   **Previous Year's Trends:** Cutoffs often follow trends from previous academic years, though they can fluctuate.

## How to Check Official Cutoffs

Once NEET results are declared, official cutoff information for various courses and categories is usually released by the National Testing Agency (NTA) or through the respective state counseling authorities. For central universities and all-India quotas, information is often available on portals like the official JoSAA portal or the Medical Counseling Committee (MCC) website. Always refer to these official sources for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding admission cutoffs and counseling schedules.

Understanding these cutoffs is essential for planning your academic path and setting realistic expectations for admission to BSc Cardiac Technology programs.
