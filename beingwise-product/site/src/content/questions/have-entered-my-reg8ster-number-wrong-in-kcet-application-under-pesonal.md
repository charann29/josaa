# I have entered my register number wrong in kcet application under pesonal details dashboard.i cant change it using correction window.it shoes that register number is invalid.what should I do

It can be quite concerning to find an error in your KCET application, especially something as critical as your registration number, and then realize the online correction window isn't letting you fix it. Don't worry, there are steps you can take to rectify this situation.

## Key Takeaways

*   Directly contact the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) as soon as possible.
*   Gather all relevant application details before reaching out to KEA.
*   Be prepared to provide proof of your correct registration number.
*   Act quickly, as delays can complicate the correction process.

## Why Some Fields Are Not Editable Online

The online application correction window, while very helpful for minor changes, often has limitations. Critical fields like your registration number, name, or date of birth might be locked once submitted or after a certain period. This is typically done to maintain the integrity of the application process and prevent fraudulent changes. If the system shows your entered registration number as 'invalid,' it likely means it doesn't match any existing record, preventing you from saving an incorrect entry that would cause further issues.

## Your Immediate Course of Action: Contact KEA Directly

Since the online correction window isn't allowing you to modify your registration number, the only viable solution is to reach out to the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) directly. They are the official body responsible for KCET and are best equipped to handle such specific application errors. Do not delay this step; the sooner you contact them, the better.

### How to Contact KEA

1.  **Phone:** Look for the official KEA contact numbers on their official portal. Make sure you're using the numbers provided specifically for KCET inquiries. Keep trying until you get through.
2.  **Email:** Send a detailed email explaining your situation. Include your application number, the incorrect registration number you entered, and the correct registration number. Attach any supporting documents, such as your admit card or previous registration proofs, if available.
3.  **In-Person (if feasible):** If you are in Bangalore or nearby, and if KEA's official guidelines permit, visiting their office might be an option for urgent matters. Always check their official portal for visitor timings and procedures before planning a visit.

## What Information to Have Ready

When you contact KEA, they will ask for specific details to verify your identity and application. Have the following information at hand:

*   Your KCET Application Number
*   Your full name as per your identity documents
*   Your parent's name
*   Your date of birth
*   The incorrect registration number you entered
*   The correct registration number
*   Any supporting documents that prove your correct registration number (e.g., your 10th/12th mark sheets, previous exam hall tickets, etc.)

## Be Persistent and Follow Up

After your initial contact, make sure to follow up if you don't hear back within a reasonable timeframe. Keep a record of all your communications, including the date and time of calls, the names of officials you spoke with, and copies of emails sent. This documentation can be helpful if further action is needed. KEA will guide you on the specific procedure for rectifying this error, which might involve submitting a written request or visiting their office with original documents.

Addressing application errors promptly and through the correct channels is crucial for a smooth admission process.
