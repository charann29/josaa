It can be frustrating when you're trying to register for an important exam like KCET and run into technical difficulties. If you're seeing a message that your Aadhaar number is already linked to another application, even though you haven't completed or submitted one, here's what you need to know.

## Key Takeaways

*   An 'Aadhaar already tagged' message usually means the system has a record of an incomplete attempt.
*   Technical errors can prevent application number generation, making it seem like no application exists.
*   You won't receive confirmation emails if registration wasn't successful.
*   Directly contacting the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) is the most effective solution.

## Understanding the 'Aadhaar Tagged' Issue

When you begin the KCET registration process, even if you don't complete it, the system might associate your Aadhaar number with that initial attempt. If a technical glitch occurs before an application number is generated or a submission is finalized, it creates a tricky situation. The system 'remembers' your Aadhaar but doesn't provide you with a way to access or continue that incomplete application. This explains why you haven't received any confirmation emails or an application number.

## Why This Happens

This issue typically arises due to an interruption in the registration flow. It could be a browser error, an internet connectivity problem, or a server-side issue on the KEA portal. When such an interruption occurs, your data might be partially saved, leading to your Aadhaar being 'tagged' without a complete application being created. Since no application number is generated, you have no credentials to log in and rectify the situation yourself.

## Steps to Resolve the Problem

The most crucial step is to directly communicate with the official body responsible for KCET – the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA). They are the only ones who can verify the status of your Aadhaar linkage and reset or enable it for a fresh registration attempt.

### Contacting the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA)

Gather all relevant details before you reach out. This includes your Aadhaar number, the exact error message you're seeing, and the date and time of your registration attempts. Clearly explain that you did not complete or submit any previous application and that no application number was generated. Request them to verify the Aadhaar linkage issue and enable your Aadhaar for a new registration.

Here are the contact details for KEA:

*   **Phone:** 080 - 23460460 (Available from 10:30 AM to 5:30 PM on working days)
*   **Address:** Sampige Road, 18th Cross, Malleshwaram, Bangalore-560012

It's advisable to call during their working hours and be prepared to explain your situation clearly and calmly. If possible, consider sending a written communication (email, if available, or a letter to their address) detailing the issue, as this creates a formal record.

## After Contacting KEA

Once you've spoken with KEA, follow any instructions they provide. They might ask you to wait for a certain period or guide you through a specific process. Keep a record of your communication, including the date, time, and name of the person you spoke with, if applicable. This will be helpful if you need to follow up.

Resolving this issue promptly is important to ensure you can complete your KCET application before the deadlines. Be persistent and clear in your communication with the KEA to get this matter sorted.
