# IIM Kashipur MBA Analytics Shortlisting Chances: A Detailed Look

Receiving an email about an MBA Analytics program from IIM Kashipur is an exciting step! Let's break down your profile to see where you stand regarding shortlisting for this program.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your non-engineering background and NC-OBC category are positive factors.
*   Strong academic performance in 10th grade is a plus, but 12th and undergraduate scores might require balancing. 
*   The low CAT percentile, especially in one section, presents a significant hurdle.
*   Your chances, if shortlisted, will heavily rely on exceptional performance in the Group Discussion and Personal Interview rounds.

## Understanding the IIM Kashipur Shortlisting Process

IIM Kashipur, like other IIMs, uses a comprehensive approach for shortlisting candidates. It's not just about your CAT score; your academic record, work experience (if any), diversity (gender, academic background), and category (NC-OBC, SC, ST, EWS, PwD) all play a role. For the MBA Analytics program, there's often an emphasis on quantitative aptitude and analytical skills, which are assessed through various stages.

## Your Academic Profile: A Closer Look

Your academic scores are 94% in 10th grade, 69% in 12th grade, and 74% in your undergraduate degree. The 94% in 10th grade is excellent and will contribute positively to your academic points. However, the 69% in 12th and 74% in your undergraduate degree, while not disqualifying, are on the lower side compared to the profiles of many successful IIM candidates. IIMs typically assign weightage to these scores, and a higher percentage generally means more points. Your non-engineering background is a diversity factor that IIMs often value, as it contributes to a richer learning environment.

## CAT Score and Sectional Cutoffs

Your CAT percentile of 63, combined with sectional percentiles of 77 (Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension), 70 (Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning), and 17 (Quantitative Aptitude), presents a significant challenge. IIM Kashipur, like all IIMs, sets minimum CAT sectional and overall percentile cutoffs for shortlisting. A percentile of 17 in the Quantitative Aptitude section is very low and likely falls below the minimum sectional cutoff for the MBA Analytics program. Even with your NC-OBC category, meeting these minimum cutoffs is usually a prerequisite for consideration. The official IIM Kashipur admissions policy details these specific cutoffs each year.

## The Role of Diversity and Category

As an NC-OBC candidate, you benefit from reservations, which means the cutoffs for your category are generally lower than for the General category. This is a definite advantage. Additionally, your non-engineering background adds to academic diversity, which IIMs often factor into their shortlisting criteria to ensure a varied cohort.

## Group Discussion and Personal Interview (GD-PI)

If, against the odds, you are shortlisted, your performance in the GD-PI rounds will become paramount. This is your opportunity to showcase your analytical thinking, communication skills, and genuine interest in the MBA Analytics program. Strong extracurricular activities, leadership roles, or any relevant projects can be highlighted here to demonstrate a well-rounded profile and a keen interest in the field of analytics.

While your low CAT percentile, particularly in the Quantitative Aptitude section, makes shortlisting highly challenging, the final decision rests with IIM Kashipur's admissions committee, considering all aspects of your profile.
