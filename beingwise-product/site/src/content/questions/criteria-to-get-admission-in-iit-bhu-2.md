# What is the criteria to get admission in IIT BHU?

Securing a spot at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, a highly respected institution, involves meeting specific academic prerequisites and performing well in national-level entrance examinations. This guide outlines the essential steps and criteria for aspiring students.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Class 12 Marks:** General, OBC, and EWS candidates need at least 75% in their Class 12 board exams (or equivalent) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
*   **JEE Main & Advanced:** All undergraduate admissions are primarily based on your performance in JEE Main and then JEE Advanced.
*   **JoSAA Counselling:** Final seat allocation for all IITs, including IIT (BHU) Varanasi, happens through the JoSAA counselling process.
*   **Special Test for B.Arch:** Those aiming for the Bachelor of Architecture program must also clear the Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT) after JEE Advanced.

## Class 12 Academic Requirements

Before you even consider the entrance exams, it's crucial to ensure you meet the minimum academic standards for your Class 12 (or equivalent) examinations. For candidates in the General, Other Backward Classes (OBC), and Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) categories, an aggregate score of at least 75% is mandatory. This percentage must be achieved in subjects including Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

For candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and Persons with Disabilities (PwD) categories, the minimum aggregate requirement is 65% in their Class 12 board exams, also with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

## The Role of JEE Main and JEE Advanced

The primary gateway for undergraduate engineering and technology programs at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, like all other Indian Institutes of Technology, is through the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE).

1.  **JEE Main:** This is the first stage. You must qualify in JEE Main, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), to be eligible to appear for JEE Advanced. Your JEE Main score is also used for admissions to NITs, IIITs, and other centrally funded technical institutions.
2.  **JEE Advanced:** Only candidates who qualify in JEE Main are permitted to sit for JEE Advanced. Your rank in JEE Advanced is the sole criterion for admission to all undergraduate programs at IITs. A strong performance in JEE Advanced is critical for securing a seat.

## JoSAA Counselling and Seat Allocation

After clearing JEE Advanced, the next crucial step is participating in the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) counselling process. JoSAA is responsible for managing and regulating the admissions to 118 central government-funded technical institutions, including all 23 IITs, 31 NITs, IIEST Shibpur, 26 IIITs, and 30 other GFTIs.

During JoSAA counselling, you will register your choices of institutes and courses based on your JEE Advanced rank. The allocation of seats adheres strictly to the reservation policies set by the Government of India for various categories, including GEN-EWS, OBC-NCL, SC, ST, and PwD.

## Special Requirements for B.Arch

If you aspire to pursue the Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch) program at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, there's an additional step. Beyond qualifying in JEE Advanced, candidates must also appear for and pass the Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT). The AAT is usually conducted shortly after JEE Advanced results are declared, and it's a mandatory part of the admission process for B.Arch programs at IITs. Details about the AAT are typically available on the official JoSAA portal.

## Postgraduate Admissions (M.Tech)

For those interested in Master of Technology (M.Tech) programs at IIT (BHU) Varanasi, admission is primarily based on performance in the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE). A competitive GATE score in your relevant engineering discipline is essential for securing a spot in the postgraduate programs.

Meeting these criteria is the first step towards a rewarding academic journey at IIT (BHU) Varanasi.
