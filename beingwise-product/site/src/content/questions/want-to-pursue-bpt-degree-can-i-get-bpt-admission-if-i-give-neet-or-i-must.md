# I want to pursue BPT degree. Can I get BPT admission if I give NEET or I must give MHT CET only?

Embarking on a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) journey is an excellent choice for those passionate about health and wellness. This page will clarify the essential entrance exam requirements for BPT programs across India.

## Key Takeaways

*   NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is not typically required for BPT admissions.
*   Many universities and states conduct their own specific entrance exams for BPT.
*   A BPT degree is a four-year undergraduate program, followed by a mandatory six-month internship.
*   Always check the specific admission criteria for your preferred colleges and universities.

## Understanding BPT Admissions

The Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) is an undergraduate course spanning four academic years, culminating in a compulsory six-month internship. This hands-on experience is crucial for practical skill development and professional readiness. Physiotherapy is a vital healthcare field focused on restoring movement and function, reducing pain, and improving the quality of life for patients.

## Is NEET Required for BPT?

A common misconception among aspiring healthcare students is that NEET is a universal entrance exam for all medical and allied health sciences courses. However, for BPT admissions, this is generally not the case. The National Testing Agency (NTA) conducts NEET primarily for entry into undergraduate medical courses such as MBBS, BDS, BHMS, and BAMS.

Therefore, if your goal is specifically a BPT degree, you typically do not need to appear for NEET. Instead, you will need to focus on other entrance examinations.

## Common BPT Entrance Exams

Since NEET is not the pathway for BPT, various universities and state bodies have their own entrance tests. These exams assess candidates on subjects relevant to the BPT curriculum, often including Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and sometimes English or general aptitude. It's crucial to research the specific requirements of the institutions you are interested in. Some prominent examples of such entrance exams include:

*   **IPU CET:** Conducted by Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University.
*   **IEMJEE:** For admissions to IEM Group of Institutions.
*   **BCECE:** Bihar Combined Entrance Competitive Examination.
*   **LPU NEST:** Lovely Professional University National Entrance and Scholarship Test.
*   **NILD CET:** For colleges affiliated with the National Institute for Locomotor Disabilities.
*   **SRMJEEH:** SRM Joint Entrance Examination for Health Sciences.
*   **DSAT:** Dayananda Sagar Admission Test.

Many other universities, such as Vel's Institute of Science Technology and Advanced Studies, Chennai, and Medical Trust Institute of Medical Sciences, Cochin, also conduct their own university-level entrance exams for BPT courses. Therefore, it is essential to visit the official websites of your target colleges to understand their specific application and examination processes.

## Preparing for BPT Entrance Exams

Preparation for BPT entrance exams should focus on the syllabus typically covered in 10+2 science streams, particularly Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Understanding the exam pattern, including the types of questions and marking scheme, is also beneficial. Many institutions provide detailed syllabi on their official portals, which can guide your study plan.

Choosing the right BPT program is a significant step towards a rewarding career in physiotherapy. By understanding the correct admission pathways, you can effectively prepare and secure your spot.
