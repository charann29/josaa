What shall I do to become airline pilot after doing B.sc in Aviation ??

Many students consider a B.Sc. in Aviation to be a direct path to the cockpit. While it provides a strong foundation, the journey to becoming an airline pilot involves specific steps beyond just this degree.

## Key takeaways

*   A B.Sc. in Aviation is not a mandatory requirement to become an airline pilot.
*   The most crucial step is completing flight training at a recognized flying school.
*   You must pass various examinations, including those set by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA).
*   Eligibility primarily hinges on your 10+2 science background with Physics and Mathematics.

## Understanding the Core Requirements

To become a commercial pilot, your educational background is vital, but not necessarily a B.Sc. in Aviation. The fundamental academic prerequisite is completing your 10+2 (or equivalent) with Physics and Mathematics as core subjects. Typically, a minimum aggregate score of 50% in your 12th board examinations is required. It's worth noting that specific flying schools might have slightly different minimum percentage criteria, so always check with your preferred institute.

Your B.Sc. in Aviation degree can certainly offer valuable theoretical knowledge and a deeper understanding of the aviation industry, which might give you an edge in certain aspects of training or interviews. However, it does not replace the practical flight training and certifications needed.

## The Path Through a Flying School

Once you meet the academic prerequisites, the most direct route to becoming a pilot is enrolling in a reputable flying school. These schools provide the essential flight training required to earn your pilot licenses. This training is intensive and covers both ground theory and actual flying hours.

During your time at a flying school, you will work towards obtaining various licenses and ratings, such as the Private Pilot License (PPL) and the Commercial Pilot License (CPL). The CPL is what you need to be eligible for paid flying jobs, including those with airlines.

## Essential Examinations and Certifications

Becoming an airline pilot involves passing several rigorous examinations. The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) is the primary regulatory body in India that oversees pilot licensing. You will need to clear their theoretical examinations, which cover subjects like Air Navigation, Aviation Meteorology, Air Regulations, and Technical General.

Beyond these theoretical exams, you will also need to complete specific practical flight tests. After obtaining your CPL, an airline will typically require you to undergo 'Type Rating' training for a specific aircraft type (e.g., Airbus A320 or Boeing 737) that they operate. This is an advanced training module that qualifies you to fly that particular aircraft model.

Eventually, to become an airline captain, you will work towards obtaining an Airline Transport Pilot License (ATPL), which is the highest level of aircraft pilot certificate.

## Financial and Selection Considerations

It's important to be aware that pilot training is a significant financial investment. The costs associated with flying schools, examinations, and type ratings can be substantial. Prospective pilots and their families should plan for these expenses carefully.

Furthermore, the selection process for both flying schools and eventually for airline jobs can be competitive. While your B.Sc. in Aviation might demonstrate your commitment and interest in the field, ultimately, your performance in flight training, success in DGCA exams, and suitability during airline interviews will be the deciding factors.

Your B.Sc. in Aviation provides an excellent theoretical base, but the practical journey to the cockpit involves dedicated flight training and passing crucial regulatory examinations.
