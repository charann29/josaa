# Raw Score or Percentile: What Matters for MHT CET Admissions?

Navigating the MHT CET admission process can feel complex, especially when trying to understand how your exam performance translates into college entry. For aspiring students in Maharashtra, the key distinction lies between your raw score and the percentile score.

## Key Takeaways

*   MHT CET admissions are primarily determined by your percentile score, not your raw score.
*   Your raw score is simply the total marks you achieve in the examination.
*   The percentile score indicates how well you performed relative to all other test-takers.
*   Admission decisions also consider your rank, category, preferred courses and colleges, cut-off scores, and seat availability.

## Understanding Your Raw Score

Your raw score in the MHT CET is the direct sum of the marks you obtain across all sections of the examination. For instance, if the total marks for the MHT CET are 200, and you score 150, then 150 is your raw score. While this number reflects your individual performance on the test itself, it's not the final metric used for admissions.

## The Significance of Percentile Score

The MHT CET is conducted in multiple shifts and sessions, meaning different students take slightly different versions of the test. To ensure fairness and standardize results across these variations, the State Common Entrance Test Cell (CET Cell) converts raw scores into percentile scores. A percentile score indicates the percentage of candidates who scored equal to or less than you in the examination. For example, if you achieve a 90th percentile, it means 90% of the students who took the MHT CET scored the same as or lower than you. This is the crucial metric for determining your eligibility for various colleges and programs.

## How Percentile Scores Lead to Rank

Once percentile scores are calculated for all candidates, these scores are then used to determine your overall rank. Your rank is essentially your position among all test-takers, ordered by their percentile scores from highest to lowest. A higher percentile score will naturally lead to a better (lower numerical) rank. This rank, combined with your percentile, becomes a significant factor in the admission process.

## The Admission Decision: Beyond Just Scores

While your percentile score and rank are paramount, they are not the only elements influencing your admission. The official MHT CET counselling process, managed by the CET Cell, takes several other factors into account:

*   **Category:** Your reservation category (e.g., SC, ST, OBC, EWS, PwD) plays a vital role, as specific quotas are allocated for different categories.
*   **Preferences:** The order in which you list your desired courses and colleges during the counselling registration is critical. Your preferences directly guide the seat allocation.
*   **Cut-off Scores:** Each college and program will have a minimum percentile or rank required for admission, known as the cut-off. These vary annually based on demand and performance of candidates.
*   **Seat Availability:** Ultimately, admission depends on the number of seats available in your preferred college and course within your category.

In summary, while your raw score is the initial outcome of your hard work, it's your percentile score and the subsequent rank that truly open doors to higher education through MHT CET.
