# Does IITians join ISRO as an Astronautical Engineer?

Many aspiring engineers dream of contributing to India's space missions, and naturally wonder if a degree from an Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) offers a direct path to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), especially for specialized fields like astronautical engineering.

## Key Takeaways

*   A B.Tech degree with at least 65% marks (or 6.8 CGPA) is a basic requirement for ISRO engineer recruitment.
*   ISRO does not offer direct recruitment; candidates must clear competitive exams and interviews.
*   While IITs provide excellent foundational knowledge, they do not guarantee a direct entry into ISRO.
*   There is no specific direct recruitment for astronautical engineering; candidates with mechanical engineering degrees can apply for relevant ISRO roles.

## ISRO's Recruitment Process for Engineers

ISRO recruits engineers primarily through its Centralised Recruitment Board (ICRB) examinations. These are highly competitive national-level tests designed to identify candidates with strong technical aptitude and problem-solving skills. The process typically involves a written examination followed by an interview for shortlisted candidates.

### Eligibility Criteria for ISRO Engineers

To be eligible for most engineering positions at ISRO, candidates generally need to hold a B.E. or B.Tech degree in a relevant discipline. A crucial academic requirement is a minimum aggregate of 65% marks or a CGPA of 6.8 out of 10 in their undergraduate engineering program. This standard applies to all applicants, regardless of their institution.

### The Role of IITs in ISRO Aspirations

While IITs are renowned for their rigorous engineering education and often rank highly in national surveys like the NIRF rankings, simply being an "IITian" does not provide a direct or preferential entry into ISRO. The selection process is merit-based, focusing on performance in ISRO's specific recruitment examinations and subsequent interviews. An IIT degree undoubtedly equips students with strong fundamentals and analytical skills, which are beneficial in these competitive exams, but it does not bypass the standard selection procedure.

## Astronautical Engineering and ISRO

For those interested in astronautical or aerospace engineering roles, ISRO does not typically have a separate, direct recruitment stream specifically for these degrees. Instead, candidates with degrees in core engineering disciplines like Mechanical Engineering are often considered for roles that involve design, development, and testing of spacecraft components, propulsion systems, and related technologies. If your B.Tech is in Aerospace or Aeronautical Engineering, you would typically apply under a broader category such as Mechanical Engineering, depending on the specific job advertisement released by ISRO.

## Applying to ISRO: What to Expect

ISRO regularly announces vacancies for various engineering disciplines, including Electronics, Mechanical, Computer Science, and Civil Engineering, among others. Interested candidates should regularly check the official ISRO website for recruitment notifications. The application process involves submitting an online application, followed by appearing for the written test if shortlisted, and finally, an interview for those who clear the written exam.

In essence, while an IIT education provides an excellent foundation, success in joining ISRO as an engineer hinges on performing exceptionally well in their rigorous, merit-based selection process.
