# I Want to Be an Aerospace Engineer: What Should I Do?

Aspiring to build rockets, design aircraft, or explore the mysteries of space? Aerospace engineering could be the perfect path for you! This exciting field combines science and technology to develop everything that flies.

## Key Takeaways

*   A Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) in Aerospace Engineering is a four-year undergraduate program.
*   Admission to top engineering colleges in India typically requires clearing the JEE Main entrance examination.
*   You'll need a strong foundation in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics from your Class 12 studies.
*   Academic excellence in Class 12, often with a minimum aggregate percentage, is also a key criterion.

## Understanding Aerospace Engineering

Aerospace engineering is a specialized branch of engineering that focuses on the design, development, testing, and production of aircraft and spacecraft. This vast field encompasses aeronautical engineering (dealing with aircraft within Earth's atmosphere) and astronautical engineering (focused on spacecraft operating beyond Earth's atmosphere). As an aerospace engineer, you could work on everything from commercial airliners and fighter jets to satellites, rockets, and even future space exploration vehicles.

## The Path Through JEE Main

For most leading engineering institutions across India, admission to a B.Tech Aerospace Engineering program is primarily through the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main. This national-level entrance exam is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) and serves as the gateway to various undergraduate engineering courses.

To be eligible for JEE Main, candidates must have either passed or be appearing for their Class 12 examination. The essential subjects for this path are Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Additionally, many institutions, particularly the NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs participating in JoSAA counselling, require candidates to have secured a minimum aggregate of 75% marks in their Class 12 board examination, or be within the top 20 percentile of their respective boards. For SC/ST candidates, this requirement is often relaxed to 65%.

## After JEE Main: JEE Advanced and Counselling

Once you've appeared for JEE Main, your performance will determine your eligibility for JEE Advanced, which is the entrance exam for the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). If you aspire to study Aerospace Engineering at an IIT, clearing JEE Advanced is a must. The official JoSAA portal manages the joint seat allocation process for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other Government Funded Technical Institutes (GFTIs) based on JEE Main and JEE Advanced ranks.

## Top Institutions for Aerospace Engineering

When choosing where to study, consider institutions that consistently rank well in the official NIRF rankings for engineering. While specific rankings for Aerospace Engineering can vary, looking at the overall engineering category gives a good indication of an institution's quality. Some well-regarded institutions that offer Aerospace Engineering or related fields include:

*   Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) suchs as IIT Bombay, IIT Kanpur, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Madras
*   Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST), Thiruvananthapuram
*   PEC Chandigarh
*   RV College of Engineering, Bangalore
