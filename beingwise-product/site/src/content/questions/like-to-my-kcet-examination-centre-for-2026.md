# Your KCET Examination Centre for 2026

Understanding where you'll take your Karnataka Common Entrance Test (KCET) is a crucial part of your preparation. This guide will walk you through how to find your assigned examination centre for the 2026 KCET.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your specific KCET examination centre will be clearly stated on your official admit card.
*   The KCET admit card for 2026 is expected to be released around April 10, 2026.
*   You cannot choose your examination centre; it is allocated by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA).
*   Always download and print your admit card well in advance of the examination date.

## How to Find Your Centre

The most important document for locating your KCET examination centre is your admit card. The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA), the official body responsible for KCET, will issue this card to all registered candidates. Once released, you will need to log in to the official KEA portal using your credentials to download it. Your admit card will contain essential information, including your roll number, photograph, signature, exam date and timings, and, most importantly, the name and address of your assigned examination centre.

## Admit Card Release Date

For the 2026 examination cycle, the KCET admit card is provisionally scheduled for release around April 10, 2026. It is advisable to regularly check the official KEA website closer to this date for any updates or changes. Once the admit card is available, download it immediately and take a printout. Keep multiple copies safe, as you will need to present it at the examination venue.

## Understanding Centre Allocation

The KEA is responsible for allocating examination centres to candidates. This allocation is typically based on the preferences you might have indicated during the application process, if such an option was provided, or based on your residential address. However, it's important to remember that the final decision rests with the KEA, and you cannot request a change of centre after the admit cards have been issued. Therefore, it's wise to plan your travel to the assigned centre well in advance, especially if it's in a different locality or city.

## What to Do Once You Know Your Centre

Once you have your admit card and know your examination centre, consider these steps:

*   **Locate the Centre:** Use online maps or ask for directions to understand the exact location of your centre. If possible, visit the centre a day or two before the exam to familiarise yourself with the route and travel time.
*   **Plan Your Travel:** Determine the best mode of transport and estimate the journey time. Factor in potential traffic or delays to ensure you arrive at the centre well before the reporting time.
*   **Check for Facilities:** While not always possible, knowing if there are basic facilities like water or restrooms nearby can be helpful.
*   **Gather Essentials:** Prepare all necessary items for the exam day, including your admit card, a valid photo ID, pens, and any other permitted materials, as specified by the KEA guidelines.

Being prepared and knowing your examination centre ahead of time will help reduce stress on the big day, allowing you to focus purely on your performance in the KCET.
