# I've got 63744 rank in TS EAMCET and I belong to SC. Can I get a seat in CSE?

It's completely understandable to be curious about your chances of securing a Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) seat with a TS EAMCET rank of 63744, especially when you belong to the SC category. Let's explore the possibilities and understand how the admission process works.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your SC category offers significant reservation benefits, which can greatly improve your admission prospects compared to the general category.
*   While top-tier colleges for CSE often have closing ranks below 20,000 for SC candidates, several other reputable institutions may be within reach with your rank.
*   Consider a broader list of colleges beyond the most competitive ones to maximize your chances.
*   Always refer to the official TS EAMCET counselling guidelines and previous year's cut-offs for the most accurate information.

## Understanding TS EAMCET Ranks and Reservations

TS EAMCET allocates seats based on merit ranks and various reservation categories, including Scheduled Castes (SC). The reservation policy ensures that a certain percentage of seats are reserved for SC candidates, meaning the closing ranks for this category are generally much higher (less competitive) than for the General category.

Computer Science and Engineering remains one of the most sought-after branches in engineering today. This high demand means that even with reservations, the most prestigious colleges tend to have very competitive closing ranks. For instance, a highly reputed institution like MVSR College often sees its SC closing ranks for CSE fall below 20,000.

## Exploring College Options for Your Rank

With a TS EAMCET SC rank of 63744, securing a CSE seat in some well-regarded colleges is a definite possibility. It's crucial to look beyond the absolute top institutions and explore colleges that offer quality education and good placement opportunities within your rank range. ## The Role of Counselling and Choice Filling

The TS EAMCET counselling process is critical. During choice filling, you will have the opportunity to list colleges and branches in your order of preference. Given your rank, it's wise to list a good mix of colleges – some that you aspire to, and several others that are realistically within your reach based on previous year's cut-offs for the SC category. Don't limit your choices to just a few institutions; a broader list increases your chances.

Always refer to the official TS EAMCET counselling portal for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding seat matrix, participating colleges, and category-wise cut-offs from previous years. This data will be your best guide.

With your SC category rank, there are indeed good prospects for securing a CSE seat in a number of engineering colleges. Best of luck with your admissions journey!
