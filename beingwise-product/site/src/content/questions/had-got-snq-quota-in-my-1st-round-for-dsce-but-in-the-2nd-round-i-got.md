# I had got SNQ quota in my 1st round for DSCE but in the 2nd round i got different branch from same clg but i didn't get snq quota what to do

It can be confusing when your admission quota changes between counselling rounds. If you secured an SNQ quota in the first round for a specific branch at DSCE but then received a different branch in the second round without that quota, it's important to understand why this might have happened and what steps you can take.

## Key takeaways

*   SNQ quota availability can vary by branch and round, even within the same college.
*   Immediate communication with the college admissions office is crucial to clarify your situation.
*   Gather all your SNQ-related documents for quick reference and verification.
*   Explore options with counselling authorities if the college cannot resolve the issue.

## Understanding SNQ Quota and Seat Allocation

The SNQ (Supernumerary Quota) is a special category designed to support students from specific backgrounds. While you might have qualified for it in one instance, its application isn't always universal across all branches and rounds within the same institution. Seat allocation is a dynamic process, and the availability of SNQ seats for a particular branch can change based on demand and the number of candidates availing it in earlier rounds. It's possible that by the second round, all SNQ seats for the branch you were allotted were already filled, or perhaps that specific branch had a very limited number of SNQ seats to begin with.

## Your First Step: Contact DSCE Admissions

Your most immediate and effective action is to directly reach out to the admissions office at DSCE. Explain your situation clearly: that you were initially allotted a seat with SNQ quota in the first round, but in the second round, while you received an offer for a different branch in the same college, the SNQ quota is missing. Inquire if there's any possibility to retain your SNQ status for the new branch you've been offered. They are best positioned to explain the college's internal policies regarding quota allocation across different branches and rounds. Be prepared to provide all your application details and any provisional allotment letters you received.

## Preparing Your Documents

Before contacting anyone, ensure all your documents related to the SNQ quota are readily accessible. This includes your original SNQ eligibility certificate, your first-round allotment letter clearly showing the SNQ status, and your second-round allotment letter. Having these handy will help you articulate your case clearly and provide immediate proof if requested. Keep both physical and digital copies.

## Reaching Out to Counselling Authorities

If the college admissions office cannot provide a satisfactory solution or suggests you contact the counselling body, then that should be your next step. The official counselling authorities (e.g., KEA for Karnataka admissions or the body responsible for your specific state/national counselling) manage the overall seat allocation process. Explain your situation to them, detailing the discrepancy in your quota status between the rounds. Ask if a manual correction or a re-evaluation of your SNQ status for the current allotment is possible. Remember that official counselling portals often have dedicated helplines or grievance redressal mechanisms.

## Time is of the Essence

It's crucial to act swiftly. Quota-related issues and changes in admission rounds typically have strict deadlines. Delays can lead to losing out on potential resolutions or even your seat. Make sure to note down the names of people you speak with, the dates, and any reference numbers provided during your communications with both the college and the counselling authorities. This documentation will be valuable if further follow-up is needed.

Navigating admission complexities can be stressful, but taking prompt and informed action can often lead to a resolution.
