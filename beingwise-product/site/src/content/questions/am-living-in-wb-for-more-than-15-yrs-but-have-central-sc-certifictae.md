# I am living in WB for more than 15 yrs but have central sc certifictae of different state can i apply to wb state colleges(CU) as reserved category?

Navigating the nuances of reservation policies for college admissions can be quite complex, especially when you've lived in one state for a long time but hold a reservation certificate from another. Let's clarify how your Scheduled Caste (SC) certificate from a different state applies to admissions in West Bengal state colleges, such as Calcutta University.

## Key Takeaways

*   An SC certificate from another state is typically not valid for claiming reservation in West Bengal state government colleges or universities.
*   Your out-of-state SC certificate *is* valid for admissions to central government educational institutions across India (e.g., IITs, NITs, Central Universities).
*   To claim SC reservation in West Bengal state institutions, you would generally need an SC certificate issued by the West Bengal government.
*   Even with long-term residency in West Bengal, the state's reservation rules usually require a state-specific certificate for state quota benefits.

## Understanding Domicile and Reservation

Reservation policies in India are often categorized into two main types: central and state. A central reservation certificate, like your SC certificate from a different state, makes you eligible for benefits in institutions and jobs controlled by the Central Government, regardless of where they are located. This means you can use it for admission to institutions like the IITs, NITs, or IIMs, as well as Central Universities anywhere in India. The official JoSAA portal, for instance, outlines how central reservations apply to admissions in centrally funded technical institutions.

However, state-level reservations are usually tied to the specific state's domicile and its own caste list. Each state government maintains its list of Scheduled Castes and Tribes, and issues certificates accordingly. To avail reservation benefits in a state-run college or university, such as Calcutta University, you typically need a reservation certificate issued by the government of *that* state.

## Why Your Central SC Certificate Isn't Valid for State Quota

The fundamental reason your SC certificate from another state won't work for West Bengal state colleges is that state reservation quotas are designed for residents who belong to the Scheduled Caste communities as recognized by that specific state government. While you may have lived in West Bengal for over 15 years, your SC status, for the purpose of state-level reservation, is still linked to the state that issued your original certificate.

West Bengal has its own criteria and processes for issuing SC certificates. To be considered for an SC seat in a West Bengal state institution, you would generally need to possess an SC certificate issued by the competent authority in West Bengal itself. Without a West Bengal-issued SC certificate, you would typically be considered under the General Category for admissions to state colleges and universities within West Bengal.

## Applying to West Bengal State Colleges

When applying to state colleges like those affiliated with Calcutta University, you should carefully review the specific admission brochure and information bulletin for the academic year. These documents, usually released by the university or the state's common admission authority, will detail the eligibility criteria for all categories, including domicile requirements and acceptable reservation certificates.

In most cases, for state-specific reserved seats, the requirement is explicitly for a certificate issued by the government of that state. Therefore, based on your current certificate, you would likely apply as a General Category candidate for state quota seats in West Bengal state colleges.

Understanding these distinctions is crucial for planning your higher education journey effectively.
