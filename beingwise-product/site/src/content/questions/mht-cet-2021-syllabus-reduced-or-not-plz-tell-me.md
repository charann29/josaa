# MHT CET 2021 Syllabus: Was it Reduced?

Many students preparing for the MHT CET 2021 exam had questions about potential changes to the syllabus, especially given the academic disruptions of the time. Let's clarify the situation regarding the MHT CET 2021 syllabus.

## Key Takeaways

*   The Maharashtra government announced a 25% reduction in the syllabus for classes 1-12 on July 25, 2020.
*   Initially, there was no official notification from the State Common Entrance Test Cell specifically confirming a syllabus reduction for MHT CET 2021.
*   MHT CET typically draws 20% of its questions from Class 11 topics and 80% from Class 12 topics.
*   Students were advised to refer to the official website of the State Common Entrance Test Cell for the most accurate and up-to-date information.

## Understanding the Syllabus Structure

The MHT CET exam traditionally covers topics from both Class 11 and Class 12 of the Maharashtra State Board curriculum. A significant portion of the exam, specifically 80%, is based on Class 12 topics. The remaining 20% of the questions are drawn from Class 11 subjects. This distribution is important for students to keep in mind when planning their study strategy, ensuring they allocate sufficient time to Class 12 material while still covering the fundamentals from Class 11.

## Government Announcement vs. MHT CET Specifics

On July 25, 2020, the Maharashtra government did announce a general reduction of 25% in the syllabus for all classes from 1 to 12. This led to a lot of speculation and hope among MHT CET aspirants that the entrance exam syllabus would also see a similar reduction. While the general school curriculum was indeed adjusted, a direct and specific announcement from the State Common Entrance Test Cell regarding a reduced syllabus for MHT CET 2021 itself was pending for some time.

## Official Information and Updates

For any critical information regarding the MHT CET syllabus, exam dates, or other important notifications, students should always rely solely on the official website of the State Common Entrance Test Cell, which is responsible for conducting the MHT CET. This is the primary and most reliable source for all official updates. Any information from other sources should be cross-referenced with the official portal to ensure accuracy.

## Preparing for MHT CET 2021

Given the standard pattern, students preparing for MHT CET 2021 were generally advised to focus on the full Class 11 and Class 12 Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) or Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) syllabi as per the Maharashtra State Board. In the absence of a definitive official reduction for the entrance exam, a comprehensive preparation approach was the safest strategy. Any confirmed changes would have been clearly communicated by the State Common Entrance Test Cell.

Staying informed through official channels is always the best approach for any competitive examination.
