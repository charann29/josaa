# I got 147 marks in NEET can I get admission in this college?

Understanding your chances of admission to medical colleges based on your NEET score is crucial for planning your next steps. Let's break down what a score of 147 typically means for aspiring medical students in India.

## Key Takeaways

*   A score of 147 in NEET is generally considered low for securing admission to MBBS, BDS, BAMS, or BHMS programs in government or private medical colleges across India.
*   Admission cut-offs vary significantly based on your category (General, OBC, SC, ST, EWS, PwD) and the specific college and state you are applying to.
*   Without knowing your specific category, it's challenging to give a precise answer, but a score of 147 is unlikely to meet the minimum qualifying criteria for most medical courses.
*   It's important to review the official cut-offs released by counseling authorities after the NEET results for a definitive understanding.

## Understanding NEET Cut-offs

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is the gateway to medical and allied health courses in India. The National Testing Agency (NTA) conducts the exam and declares the results. Following this, various counseling bodies, such as the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) for All India Quota seats and state counseling authorities, release specific cut-off ranks and scores for different categories.

These cut-offs are the minimum marks or ranks required for admission to a particular course and college. They fluctuate each year based on factors like the number of applicants, the difficulty level of the exam, and the availability of seats. Generally, MBBS and BDS courses have significantly higher cut-offs compared to BAMS (Ayurveda) and BHMS (Homeopathy) programs.

## Impact of Your Category

Your admission prospects are heavily influenced by your reservation category. India's reservation system provides quotas for various groups, including Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), Other Backward Classes (OBC), Economically Weaker Sections (EWS), and Persons with Disabilities (PwD).

For instance, a General category candidate will need a much higher score than an SC or ST category candidate to secure a seat in the same college and course. While a score of 147 is generally low across all categories for MBBS/BDS, there might be a very slim chance for certain reserved categories in specific private colleges or less sought-after BAMS/BHMS courses in some states. However, this is rare, and even for these categories, qualifying marks are typically higher.

## What are the typical qualifying marks?

To even be eligible for counseling, candidates must score above the minimum qualifying percentile set by the NTA. For the General category, this is usually the 50th percentile, while for OBC/SC/ST, it's the 40th percentile. The actual scores corresponding to these percentiles vary each year. For example, in recent years, the qualifying score for the General category has often been well above 130-140 marks, and for reserved categories, it has been above 100 marks. A score of 147, while potentially above the *minimum qualifying percentile* for some reserved categories, is still far from the *admission cut-off* for most medical seats.

## Exploring Other Options

If your NEET score of 147 makes admission to traditional medical courses (MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BHMS) challenging, it's wise to consider other valuable pathways in the health sector. There are numerous Allied and Health Sciences programs that offer excellent career opportunities. These include courses in: 

*   **Paramedical Sciences:** Such as Medical Lab Technology, Radiography, Operation Theatre Technology, Optometry, and Physiotherapy.
*   **Nursing:** A highly respected and in-demand profession.
*   **Pharmacy:** Focusing on drug development and dispensing.
*   **Biotechnology:** Research-oriented fields.

Many universities accept applications for these programs based on 10+2 scores or through separate entrance exams, rather than solely on NEET. Researching these alternatives can open up a wide range of fulfilling career paths in healthcare.

Your NEET score is just one step on your academic journey. Evaluate your options carefully and make informed decisions based on official information from NTA and respective counseling authorities.
