# Is there any job for Aerospace engineer at ISRO ? How to apply for ISRO ?

For many aspiring aerospace engineers in India, working at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is a dream. This article explores the opportunities available for aerospace engineers at ISRO and outlines the typical application processes.

## Key takeaways

*   Aerospace engineers are valuable at ISRO, though vacancies can be limited and irregular.
*   A strong GATE score is a primary pathway for many technical positions.
*   ISRO also conducts its own recruitment examinations for various roles.
*   Keeping an eye on the official ISRO website for announcements is crucial.

## Opportunities for Aerospace Engineers at ISRO

ISRO is at the forefront of space research and exploration, requiring a diverse range of engineering talent. Aerospace engineers play a critical role in designing, developing, testing, and maintaining spacecraft, launch vehicles, and related systems. Their expertise is essential in areas like aerodynamics, propulsion, structural analysis, and satellite technology.

While the demand for skilled aerospace engineers within ISRO is high, it's important to note that the number of vacancies for specific roles can be limited. Recruitment drives are often project-specific and may not occur with fixed regularity. However, when positions do open, they offer excellent career prospects and the chance to contribute to India's space missions.

## Primary Application Pathways for ISRO

There are generally two main routes through which aerospace engineers can apply to ISRO:

### 1. Through the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE)

Many technical positions at ISRO, particularly for scientists and engineers, are filled based on GATE scores. The GATE exam is highly competitive and assesses a candidate's comprehensive understanding and technical skills in their respective engineering discipline. A strong performance in GATE can open doors to interviews and further selection processes at ISRO and other public sector undertakings (PSUs).

Candidates who achieve a good GATE score can then apply directly through the official ISRO website when specific recruitment notifications are released that accept GATE scores as a criterion.

### 2. ISRO's Own Recruitment Examinations

ISRO also conducts its independent recruitment examinations and interviews for various technical and non-technical positions. These exams are designed to assess candidates' aptitude, technical knowledge, and problem-solving abilities relevant to the specific roles. These direct recruitment drives are announced on the official ISRO website.

For instance, ISRO occasionally announces recruitment for positions like Scientist/Engineer 'SC' through its own written test followed by an interview. Similarly, positions such as Technician-B, Draughtsman-B, and Technical Assistant may also be filled through dedicated recruitment processes.

## How to Apply for ISRO

The application process for ISRO is primarily online through their official recruitment portal. Here’s a general guide:

*   **Monitor the Official Website:** Regularly check the 'Careers' or 'Recruitment' section on the official ISRO website. All job advertisements, eligibility criteria, application deadlines, and examination schedules are published there.
*   **Review Eligibility:** Carefully read the eligibility criteria for each advertised position, including educational qualifications, age limits, and any specific experience requirements.
*   **Online Application:** Complete the online application form accurately within the specified dates. This typically involves providing personal details, educational background, and uploading necessary documents.
*   **Application Fee:** Pay the prescribed application fee, if any, through the online payment gateway.
*   **Admit Card:** If you are shortlisted for a written test or interview, download your admit card from the official portal.

It's important to apply well before the closing date to avoid last-minute technical issues. For example, in the past, recruitment for roles like Controller in the Human Space Flight Centre (HSFC) in Bengaluru or Technician-B / Draughtsman-B / Technical Assistant roles have been announced with specific application windows.

Working at ISRO offers an unparalleled opportunity to contribute to India's advancements in space technology and research.
