# I got 12853 rank in NEET MDS 2021: Chances for Oral Surgery All India

Understanding your chances for a specific specialty like Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery with a NEET MDS rank of 12853 requires a careful look at past trends and the counseling process. While a definitive answer isn't possible, we can explore the factors involved to give you a clearer picture.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Competition is High:** Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery is a highly sought-after specialty, making admission competitive, especially for General category seats.
*   **Category Matters:** Your chances significantly improve if you belong to a reserved category (SC, ST, OBC, EWS, PwD) due to dedicated seat quotas.
*   **State Quota vs. All India:** State quota counseling often has different cutoff ranks compared to All India Quota, offering more opportunities within your domicile state.
*   **Deemed Universities:** These institutions often have different fee structures and sometimes slightly higher cutoff ranks for admission, which might be an option.

## Understanding NEET MDS Counseling

NEET MDS counseling is conducted by the Medical Counseling Committee (MCC) for All India Quota (AIQ) seats, which comprise 50% of the total seats in government dental colleges and all seats in central and deemed universities. State counseling authorities manage the remaining 50% state quota seats. Your rank of 12853 needs to be considered within the context of these different counseling streams.

## All India Quota and Oral Surgery

For Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery through the All India Quota, a rank of 12853 in NEET MDS 2021 would generally be considered challenging for a General category seat. Historically, the closing ranks for this specialty in government colleges under AIQ have been much lower, often within the triple or low quadruple digits. However, cutoffs can fluctuate each year based on the number of candidates, seat availability, and candidate preferences.

## Role of Reserved Categories

If you belong to a reserved category (SC, ST, OBC, EWS, or PwD), your prospects for Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery improve considerably. Each category has a specific percentage of seats reserved under both All India and State Quota counseling. For example, OBC (NCL) candidates often see closing ranks that are significantly higher than those for the General category. It's crucial to verify your category status and ensure all documentation is in order for counseling.

## Deemed Universities as an Option

Deemed universities offer another pathway to MDS admission. While their fee structures are typically higher than government colleges, they often have slightly different cutoff ranks. It's possible that with a rank of 12853, you might find opportunities in some deemed universities for Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, especially during later rounds of counseling (e.g., mop-up rounds). It's advisable to check the specific cutoff trends for deemed universities from previous years, which are usually published by the MCC.

## State Quota Counseling

Don't overlook your state quota counseling. The cutoff ranks for various specialties, including Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery, can vary significantly from state to state. If your domicile state has a lower cutoff for this specialty compared to the All India Quota, you might have a better chance there. Each state's medical education directorate conducts its own counseling process, so you'll need to monitor their official announcements.

Navigating NEET MDS admissions requires a strategic approach, considering all available quotas and counseling rounds. Keep a close watch on official announcements from the MCC and your state counseling authority for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding seat matrix and cutoffs.
