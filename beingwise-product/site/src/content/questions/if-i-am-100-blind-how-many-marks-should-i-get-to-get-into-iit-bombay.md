# If I am 100% blind how many marks should I get to get into IIT Bombay in JEE Advanced for Computer Science Engineering I am from Economically Weaker Section

Navigating the path to a premier institution like IIT Bombay, especially for a highly sought-after program like Computer Science Engineering (CSE), requires a clear understanding of the admission criteria. For candidates who are 100% blind and belong to the Economically Weaker Section (EWS), there are specific considerations within the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Advanced framework.

## Key Takeaways

*   IIT Bombay CSE is exceptionally competitive, even with reservations.
*   While no specific blind-only category exists, candidates with 100% blindness fall under the Persons with Disability (PwD) category, which has sub-categories.
*   EWS reservation applies alongside PwD status, offering a dual advantage.
*   A score in the range of 108-160+ marks in JEE Advanced is generally considered a competitive target for EWS candidates aiming for IIT Bombay, even with PwD status, given the high demand for CSE.

## Understanding the PwD Category in JEE Advanced

The JEE Advanced examination structure includes provisions for candidates with disabilities. A candidate who is 100% blind would fall under the Persons with Disability (PwD) category. Within this category, various sub-categories exist based on the type and extent of disability. Visually challenged candidates, including those with complete blindness, are specifically accommodated. It's crucial to ensure that your disability certificate is current and issued by a competent authority as per the guidelines provided by the National Testing Agency (NTA) and the official JoSAA portal.

## EWS Reservation and Its Impact

As a candidate from the Economically Weaker Section (EWS), you are eligible for reservation benefits in addition to your PwD status. The EWS category provides a 10% reservation in institutions like IITs for candidates whose family income and assets meet specific criteria. When combined with PwD status, this dual reservation can significantly influence the required marks for admission. The aim of these reservations is to ensure equitable access to higher education for deserving candidates from diverse backgrounds.

## Target Score for IIT Bombay CSE

Admission to Computer Science Engineering at IIT Bombay is consistently among the most competitive across all IITs. While reservation categories do lower the cut-off compared to the General category, the demand for CSE means that even reserved category candidates need to achieve a strong score. Based on past trends and the competitive nature of IIT Bombay CSE, an EWS candidate with PwD status should aim for a score in the range of 108-160+ marks in JEE Advanced. This is a general guideline, and actual cut-offs can vary each year based on factors such as the difficulty of the exam, the number of candidates, and the overall performance of test-takers. It's always advisable to aim for the highest possible score to maximize your chances.

## JoSAA Counselling and Seat Allocation

After clearing JEE Advanced, the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) conducts the counselling process. This is where your JEE Advanced rank, category (EWS and PwD), and preferences for institutes and courses are considered for seat allocation. It's vital to meticulously fill your choices during JoSAA counselling, prioritizing IIT Bombay CSE, and also listing other preferred IITs and courses as backup options. The official JoSAA portal provides detailed information regarding the counselling schedule, required documents, and seat allocation rules, including specific provisions for PwD candidates.

## Preparing for JEE Advanced with a Disability

Preparing for JEE Advanced with 100% blindness requires specific strategies and accommodations. The NTA provides guidelines for scribe assistance and extra time for PwD candidates during the examination. Familiarize yourself with these provisions well in advance. Focus on conceptual clarity, regular practice, and utilizing accessible study materials. Many coaching institutes and online platforms offer resources tailored for visually challenged students. Consistency and a strong problem-solving approach will be key to your success.

Aim high, prepare thoroughly, and understand the admission process to turn your IIT Bombay dream into a reality.
