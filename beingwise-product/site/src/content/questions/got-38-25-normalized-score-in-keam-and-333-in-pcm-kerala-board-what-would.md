# I got 38.25 normalized score in KEAM and 333 in PCM (Kerala Board). What would be my rank?

Understanding your potential rank in KEAM is a crucial step in planning your engineering admissions journey. With your normalized KEAM score of 38.25 and a Kerala Board PCM score of 333 out of 480, let's explore what this might mean for your prospects.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your combined scores suggest an expected rank likely above 50,000, based on trends from previous years.
*   Admission to top government engineering colleges in Kerala might be challenging with this rank.
*   You will likely find opportunities in private or self-financing engineering colleges across Kerala.
*   The official KEAM rank list, once released, will provide your exact position for counseling.

## How KEAM Ranks Are Calculated

KEAM ranks are determined by a specific calculation that combines your performance in the KEAM entrance examination and your marks in the qualifying examination (Class 12th PCM). For engineering admissions, the CEE (Commissioner for Entrance Examinations) typically gives equal weightage to both components: 50% from your KEAM normalized score and 50% from your Class 12th PCM marks. This weighted approach ensures a comprehensive assessment of a candidate's academic and entrance test performance.

## Understanding Your Scores

Your normalized KEAM score of 38.25 is one half of the equation. The other half comes from your Class 12th PCM marks. You've secured 333 out of 480 in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics from the Kerala Board. To get a clearer picture, this percentage (333/480 * 100) is also factored in. While the exact normalization process for Class 12th marks across different boards can be complex, for Kerala Board students, it's generally straightforward. The combined score derived from these two components is what ultimately dictates your position on the KEAM rank list.

## Expected Rank and College Prospects

Based on historical data and trends from previous KEAM examinations, a combined score leading to a normalized KEAM component of 38.25, alongside your Class 12th marks, typically places candidates in a rank range above 50,000. It's important to remember that this is an estimation. While securing a seat in highly sought-after government engineering colleges might be difficult at this rank, there are numerous reputable private and self-financing engineering colleges in Kerala that you could consider. These institutions often have a wider range of admission possibilities.

## The Official KEAM Rank List

The most definitive information regarding your rank will come with the release of the official KEAM rank list. As per the typical schedule, this list is often published around July 10th. Once the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE) publishes the official list, you will be able to check your precise rank. This exact rank is what you will use to participate in the KEAM counseling process and make your college choices.

Stay prepared for the counseling process by researching colleges and courses that align with your interests and potential rank.
