# Is there NCC in this college? - VRSEC

For many students, college isn't just about academics; it's also about holistic development and contributing to society. The National Cadet Corps (NCC) offers a fantastic opportunity for students at Velagapudi Ramakrishna Siddhartha Engineering College (VRSEC) to cultivate leadership, discipline, and a spirit of service.

## Key Takeaways

*   VRSEC proudly offers the National Cadet Corps (NCC) program for its students.
*   The NCC unit at VRSEC, known as 1 (A) EME COY NCC, has a long history, established in 1982.
*   Students can pursue B and C Certificate courses, enhancing their skill sets and future prospects.
*   The program emphasizes the core NCC motto: "Unity and Discipline."

## Understanding NCC at VRSEC

Yes, VRSEC provides an active NCC unit for its students. This program is designed to instill values of patriotism, selflessness, and teamwork, aligning with the NCC's national objective of developing character and leadership qualities among the youth. Participating in NCC can be a transformative experience, offering unique training and exposure that goes beyond the typical classroom environment.

## The 1 (A) EME COY NCC Unit

VRSEC's specific NCC unit is designated as 1 (A) EME COY NCC. This unit has been a cornerstone of student development at the college for decades, having been established on August 16, 1982. Its long-standing presence signifies the college's commitment to providing comprehensive opportunities for its students. The unit is well-structured, comprising a Commanding Officer, an Associate NCC Officer (ANO), and a team of Permanent Instructional (PI) Staff, all dedicated to guiding cadets.

## NCC Certificate Courses and Eligibility

Students at VRSEC have the opportunity to undertake the B and C Certificate courses through the NCC program. These certificates are nationally recognized and can be beneficial for higher education admissions, career opportunities, and even entry into armed forces. Typically, these courses involve a blend of theoretical instruction, drill, and practical training, including camps. At VRSEC, the NCC program is specifically offered to students in their second semester of the B.Tech program.

## The Motto: Unity and Discipline

The guiding principle of the NCC, and therefore of the unit at VRSEC, is "Unity and Discipline." This motto encapsulates the core values that the program seeks to impart. Cadets learn the importance of working together as a cohesive unit, adhering to strict standards of conduct, and developing a disciplined approach to all tasks. These are invaluable life skills that extend far beyond their time in college.

Joining the NCC at VRSEC can be a fulfilling journey, offering personal growth and a chance to contribute positively to both the college community and the nation at large.
