# Can I get Chemical Engineering at NIT Jalandhar with 96.78 Percentile in JEE Main? 

Scoring 96.78 percentile in the JEE Main January session is a commendable achievement. Let's explore your prospects for securing a Chemical Engineering seat at NIT Jalandhar, considering you are a General category candidate from Uttar Pradesh.

## Key Takeaways

*   A 96.78 percentile offers a decent chance for admission to an NIT, but Chemical Engineering at NIT Jalandhar specifically might be competitive.
*   Admission cutoffs are dynamic and depend on various factors each year, including the number of applicants and their scores.
*   Appearing for the JEE Main Session 2 exam is highly recommended to potentially improve your percentile and enhance your admission chances.
*   Utilize official resources like the JoSAA portal for the most accurate and up-to-date cutoff information during counselling.

## Understanding JEE Main Percentile and NIT Admissions

Your JEE Main percentile indicates how many candidates you have performed better than. For instance, 96.78 percentile means you scored better than 96.78% of the candidates who appeared in that session. Admission to NITs is highly sought after, and seats are allocated through the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) counselling process based on your All India Rank (AIR), which is derived from your best JEE Main percentile score.

NIT Jalandhar, officially known as Dr. B. R. Ambedkar National Institute of Technology Jalandhar, is a well-regarded institution. Admission to its various engineering branches, including Chemical Engineering, is competitive, especially for the General category.

## Cutoff Trends for Chemical Engineering at NIT Jalandhar

Historically, the closing ranks for Chemical Engineering at NIT Jalandhar for General category candidates have been relatively high, meaning a higher percentile is often required. While 96.78 percentile is strong, the specific branch and institute combination can be challenging. Cutoffs are not static; they fluctuate annually based on factors such as:

*   The total number of candidates appearing for JEE Main.
*   The difficulty level of the exam sessions.
*   The number of seats available in each branch.
*   The preferences of candidates during counselling.

It's important to remember that your State of Domicile (Uttar Pradesh in your case) can also play a role, as a certain percentage of seats are reserved for Home State candidates. However, for branches like Chemical Engineering at NIT Jalandhar, competition remains significant for both Home State and Other State General category applicants.

## The Importance of JEE Main Session 2

Given the competitive nature of NIT admissions, especially for specific branches like Chemical Engineering, appearing for the JEE Main Session 2 exam is a strategic move. The National Testing Agency (NTA) considers your best percentile score from either session for calculating your final All India Rank. Improving your percentile in the second session could significantly boost your rank and open up better admission possibilities. Even a slight increase in percentile can make a substantial difference in your rank, potentially changing your eligibility for desired branches and institutes.

## Navigating the JoSAA Counselling Process

Once both JEE Main sessions are complete and ranks are declared, the JoSAA counselling process will begin. This is where you will register, fill your choices of institutes and branches in order of preference, and participate in the seat allocation rounds. It's crucial to research previous years' closing ranks for Chemical Engineering at NIT Jalandhar for both Home State and Other State General categories, which are often available on the official JoSAA portal. This will give you a clearer picture of your chances. However, always treat past cutoffs as a guide, not a guarantee.
