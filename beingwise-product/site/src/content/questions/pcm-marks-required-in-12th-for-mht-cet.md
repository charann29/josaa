# PCM Marks Required in 12th for MHT CET

Understanding the academic qualifications needed for the MHT CET is a crucial first step for aspiring engineering students in Maharashtra. This guide breaks down the essential 12th-grade marks and subject combinations required to be eligible for this important state-level entrance exam.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Mandatory Subjects:** Physics and Mathematics are compulsory subjects in your 12th-grade examination.
*   **Third Subject Choice:** You must have one additional subject from a specified list, including Chemistry, Biology, Biotechnology, or a relevant vocational/technical subject.
*   **Minimum Marks (General Category):** A combined score of at least 45% in your three core subjects (Physics, Mathematics, and the third optional subject) is required.
*   **Minimum Marks (Reserved Categories):** Candidates from Backward Class categories, Economically Weaker Section (EWS), and Persons with Disability (PwD) in Maharashtra State need a minimum of 40% in the three core subjects.

## Core Subject Requirements for MHT CET Eligibility

To be eligible for the MHT CET, all candidates must have successfully completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) examination. The cornerstone of this eligibility lies in the specific subjects you studied. Physics and Mathematics are non-negotiable; they must be among your subjects in the 12th grade.

Alongside Physics and Mathematics, you need to have studied one additional subject from a broad list. This list includes:

*   Chemistry
*   Biotechnology
*   Biology
*   Technical Vocational Subject
*   Computer Science
*   Information Technology
*   Informatics Practices
*   Agriculture
*   Engineering Graphics
*   Business Studies
*   Electronics
*   Entrepreneurship

This flexibility allows students from various academic backgrounds to pursue engineering through MHT CET, provided they meet the core PCM (or equivalent) structure.

## Minimum Aggregate Marks in 12th Grade

Beyond the specific subjects, your performance in these subjects also determines your eligibility. The MHT CET requires a minimum aggregate percentage in the compulsory subjects (Physics, Mathematics, and your chosen third subject).

### For General Category Candidates

If you belong to the General Category, you must have secured a minimum of **45% marks** when combining the scores of Physics, Mathematics, and your third eligible subject (e.g., Chemistry, Biology, etc.). This rule applies to all candidates, regardless of whether they are from Maharashtra or other states.

### For Reserved Category Candidates from Maharashtra State

For candidates belonging to Backward Class categories, Economically Weaker Section (EWS), and Persons with Disability (PwD) from Maharashtra State, there is a relaxation in the minimum marks. These candidates need to achieve a minimum of **40% marks** in the aggregate of Physics, Mathematics, and their third eligible subject.

It's important to note that this relaxation is specifically for Maharashtra State candidates who fall into these reserved categories. Candidates from other states, even if they belong to reserved categories in their home state, are generally considered under the General Category criteria for MHT CET eligibility.

## Diploma Holders Eligibility

Candidates who have completed a Diploma in Engineering and Technology are also eligible for MHT CET. Similar to 12th-grade qualifiers, they need to meet specific percentage criteria:

*   **General Category:** A minimum of 45% marks in their Diploma.
*   **Backward Class, EWS, and PwD (Maharashtra State):** A minimum of 40% marks in their Diploma.

This pathway provides an alternative route for vocational students to enter engineering degree programs.

## Nationality and Age Criteria

To apply for MHT CET, candidates must be Indian nationals. Interestingly, there is no upper age limit specified for appearing in the MHT CET examination, which means candidates of any age can pursue their engineering aspirations through this route, provided they meet the academic qualifications.

Always refer to the official MHT CET information brochure for the most current and detailed eligibility criteria, which is released annually by the relevant authorities.
