# I want to study medicine in India

Aspiring to become a doctor in India is a noble goal, and understanding the path to achieving it is the first crucial step. This guide will walk you through the essential information for pursuing medical education in the country.

## Key takeaways

*   The NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is the single gateway for most medical and allied health courses in India.
*   Eligibility for NEET includes specific academic percentages in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology, along with a minimum age requirement.
*   After clearing NEET, admission to colleges is determined through a counselling process based on your NEET rank.
*   Beyond MBBS and BDS, NEET also covers admission to AYUSH (Ayurveda, Unani, Siddha, Homoeopathy) and Veterinary Science programs.

## The NEET Examination: Your Gateway to Medical Studies

For nearly all medical and allied health courses in India, whether in government or private institutions, the NEET is the mandatory entrance examination. This includes popular programs like Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) and Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS).

Beyond these, NEET also serves as the entrance for:

*   **AYUSH Courses:** Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS), Bachelor of Unani Medicine and Surgery (BUMS), Bachelor of Siddha Medicine and Surgery (BSMS), and Bachelor of Homoeopathic Medicine and Surgery (BHMS).
*   **Veterinary Science:** Bachelor of Veterinary Science (BVSc). It's worth noting that for state quota seats in BVSc, some states might conduct their own entrance exams in addition to or instead of NEET for specific allocations.

In certain states, like Maharashtra, NEET scores are also accepted for admission into Bachelor of Science (Nursing) programs.

## Eligibility Criteria for NEET

Meeting the NEET eligibility criteria, as set by the National Testing Agency (NTA), is fundamental for appearing in the exam. Generally, candidates must either be appearing for their 12th board examination or have successfully passed it with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as subjects.

Specific percentage requirements for the aggregate of Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology (PCB) in the 12th examination are:

*   **General Category:** At least 50%
*   **SC/ST/OBC Categories:** At least 40%
*   **Persons with Disability (PwD) Category:** At least 45%

Additionally, candidates must be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year. For the most precise and up-to-date information, always refer to the official NEET information bulletin released by NTA.

## The Admission Process After NEET

Once you have successfully qualified the NEET examination, the next crucial step is participating in the counselling process. This is where seats in MBBS, BDS, and other medical courses are allotted based on your rank in NEET. The counselling is typically conducted by central and state authorities, aligning with the guidelines provided by regulatory bodies.

## Top Medical Colleges in India

While specific rankings can vary year to year, the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) provides an official annual ranking of institutions across various disciplines, including medicine. Consulting the latest NIRF rankings can give you an idea of the top medical colleges in India, helping you make informed choices during the counselling process.

Embarking on a medical career is a challenging yet deeply rewarding journey, and understanding these foundational steps will set you on the right path.
