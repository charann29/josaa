It's natural to wonder about your chances at top B-schools like IIM Lucknow and FMS Delhi, especially with a solid profile and an EWS category advantage. Let's break down what your CAT 2025 raw score, academic record, and work experience mean for your aspirations.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your expected CAT percentile of 96-97% puts you in a strong position for IIM Lucknow, especially with your EWS status.
*   Excellent academic scores (X, XII, B.Tech) and two years of work experience significantly boost your IIM Lucknow prospects.
*   For FMS Delhi, your chances are borderline but not impossible, as their cut-offs are exceptionally high, focusing heavily on CAT scores.
*   Thorough preparation for the Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds is crucial for both institutes.

## Understanding Your CAT Performance

Your CAT 2025 raw score of 84 is promising. Based on historical trends and depending on the paper's difficulty and normalization process, this score is likely to translate into an overall percentile in the 96-97% range. More importantly, your sectional scores—VARC 34, LRDI 24, and QA 26—are well above the typical minimum sectional cut-offs for both IIM Lucknow and FMS Delhi. This is a significant advantage, as many candidates struggle to clear all sectional hurdles.

## IIM Lucknow: A Strong Contender

IIM Lucknow considers a holistic profile, giving weight to CAT scores, academic performance, and work experience. For the EWS category, the overall CAT percentile cut-off has generally been around 93-95% in recent years, with sectional cut-offs typically in the 70-75% range. Your expected overall percentile of 96-97% comfortably surpasses this.

### Academic and Work Experience Advantage

Your academic record is outstanding: 95% in Class X, 94.5% in Class XII, and 80.01% in B.Tech. IIM Lucknow places considerable emphasis on consistent academic excellence. Furthermore, your two years of relevant work experience will add valuable points to your composite score, further enhancing your chances during the shortlisting process. Given all these factors, your probability of receiving an IIM Lucknow interview call appears quite good.

## FMS Delhi: A Competitive Edge

FMS Delhi is known for its highly competitive admissions process, with an extremely high weightage given to the CAT score during shortlisting. The overall CAT percentile cut-off for the EWS category at FMS Delhi typically falls within the 97-98% range. While your expected 96-97% is at the lower end of this range, it's not entirely out of contention.

### VARC Score and Shortlisting

FMS Delhi often places a strong emphasis on the VARC section. Your VARC score of 34 is a positive point in this regard. While an FMS Delhi call isn't guaranteed due to the very high cut-offs, it remains a possibility. If the final percentile calculation works slightly in your favour, or if the EWS cut-off sees a minor dip this year, you could still receive a call. Be aware that FMS Delhi's shortlisting is predominantly CAT-score driven, making every decimal point in your percentile crucial.

## The Next Steps: WAT and PI Preparation

Regardless of whether your chances are strong or borderline, receiving an interview call means the real work begins. Both IIM Lucknow and FMS Delhi have rigorous Written Ability Test (WAT) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds. Start preparing diligently for these stages. Focus on current affairs, general knowledge, your work experience, and your career aspirations. Being well-prepared for WAT-PI can make all the difference in converting a call into a final admission offer.

With a strong profile like yours, it's also wise to consider other top-tier IIMs and highly reputed non-IIM institutions where your application would be very competitive.
