# How Many Subjects in MHT CET?

The MHT CET (Maharashtra Health and Technical Common Entrance Test) is a crucial examination for students aspiring to pursue professional undergraduate courses in engineering, pharmacy, and agriculture in Maharashtra. Understanding the subjects covered in this exam is the first step toward effective preparation.

## Key Takeaways

*   The MHT CET primarily assesses candidates in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology.
*   The syllabus draws from both Class 11 and Class 12 topics.
*   Approximately 20% of the questions come from the Class 11 syllabus, while 80% are from the Class 12 syllabus.
*   Eligibility generally requires passing 10+2 with specific science subjects.

## Core Subjects Assessed

For students targeting engineering and pharmacy, the MHT CET covers Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM). For those aiming for health sciences and certain agricultural courses, the exam includes Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB). Some aspirants may choose to appear for all four subjects (PCMB) to keep their options open for a wider range of courses.

## Syllabus Distribution: Class 11 vs. Class 12

It's important for candidates to note the weightage given to different parts of the syllabus. The MHT CET allocates a significant portion of its questions to the Class 12 curriculum. Specifically, about 80% of the questions in the exam are based on topics from the Class 12 syllabus. The remaining 20% of the questions are derived from the Class 11 syllabus. This distribution highlights the critical importance of a strong grasp of Class 12 concepts, while still requiring a foundational understanding of Class 11 material.

## Eligibility for MHT CET

To be eligible for the MHT CET, candidates must have successfully completed their 10+2 or an equivalent examination from a recognized board or university. The specific subject requirements for eligibility depend on the chosen course. Generally, for engineering and pharmacy, candidates must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. For health science courses, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology are typically mandatory. Always refer to the official MHT CET information brochure for the most precise and up-to-date eligibility criteria.

## Exam Pattern Overview

The MHT CET is conducted in multiple shifts and typically consists of three papers:

*   **Paper 1: Mathematics**
*   **Paper 2: Physics and Chemistry**
*   **Paper 3: Biology (Botany and Zoology)**

Candidates appearing for PCM will take Paper 1 and Paper 2. Those appearing for PCB will take Paper 2 and Paper 3. Students opting for PCMB will appear for all three papers. Each paper has a specific duration and marking scheme, which candidates should review in the official MHT CET guidelines.

Understanding the subjects and their respective weightage is fundamental to crafting an effective study plan and maximizing your chances of success in the MHT CET.
