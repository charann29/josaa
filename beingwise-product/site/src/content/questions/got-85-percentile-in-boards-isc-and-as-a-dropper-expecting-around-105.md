# Got 85 percentile in boards ISC and as a dropper expecting around 105+ in GUJCET which college should i get

Navigating college admissions can be a little overwhelming, especially when you're combining board exam results with a state-level entrance test and also taking a gap year. Let's break down your options and help you understand what might be a good fit.

## Key takeaways

*   Your ISC board percentile of 85% is a solid foundation for many admissions processes.
*   An expected GUJCET score of 105+ is competitive for various engineering programs in Gujarat.
*   Being a dropper doesn't negatively impact your GUJCET or board exam eligibility for most colleges.
*   Focus on institutes that weigh both board performance and GUJCET scores appropriately.

## Understanding your scores: ISC and GUJCET

Your 85th percentile in the ISC boards is a good academic achievement. Many colleges, especially in Gujarat, consider a minimum percentage in 12th grade for eligibility, and 85% generally meets or exceeds these cutoffs. For GUJCET, an expected score of 105+ is quite respectable. This score range typically opens doors to good engineering branches in various government, grant-in-aid, and self-financed institutions across Gujarat. It's important to remember that GUJCET scores are often combined with 12th board results (typically in a 60:40 or 50:50 ratio) to form a merit score for admissions through the Admission Committee for Professional Courses (ACPC).

## Exploring colleges based on your profile

With your profile, you have a good chance at several reputable institutions. Here are some types of colleges and specific examples to consider:

*   **Government and Grant-in-Aid Colleges:** Institutions like Government Engineering College (GEC) various locations (e.g., Gandhinagar, Bhuj, Modasa) and Sardar Vallabhbhai National Institute of Technology (SVNIT) Surat (though for SVNIT, you'd typically need JEE Main scores, not GUJCET) are highly sought after. For state-level GECs, your GUJCET score will be crucial.
*   **Self-Financed Institutions (SFIs):** Many well-regarded private universities and colleges offer a wide range of engineering programs. Look into universities recognized as Centres of Excellence, as mentioned by official bodies, or those with high NAAC grades like 'A'. Ahmedabad University is one such example often noted for its B.Tech programs.
*   **Specialized Institutes:** Consider institutions like IITRAM, Gujarat (Institute of Infrastructure Technology Research and Management), which focuses on specific engineering disciplines related to infrastructure. These can be excellent options if their specializations align with your interests.

## The Admission Process: ACPC and direct admissions

Most engineering admissions in Gujarat, particularly for government and grant-in-aid colleges, are conducted through the Admission Committee for Professional Courses (ACPC). They will publish detailed merit lists based on combined 12th board and GUJCET scores. You'll need to register on their official portal and participate in their online counseling process. For private universities, some may participate in ACPC counseling, while others conduct their own direct admission processes. Always check the specific admission criteria for each university you are interested in directly from their official websites.

## Important considerations as a dropper

Being a dropper does not disadvantage you in the GUJCET or most college admissions processes in India. Your most recent academic performance (12th boards and GUJCET) is what matters. Ensure all your documentation is up-to-date and accurately reflects your academic history. Focus on demonstrating your continued dedication and readiness for higher education.

Thorough research into each college's specific admission criteria, including their weighting of board marks versus GUJCET scores, will be your next crucial step.
