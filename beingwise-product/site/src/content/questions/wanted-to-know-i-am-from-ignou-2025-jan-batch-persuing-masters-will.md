# I wanted to know i am from ignou 2025 jan batch persuing masters. Will i be able to get into mphil programme by the time i finish masters

If you're starting your Master's degree with IGNOU in January 2025, you're likely looking ahead to your academic future and wondering about further studies like M.Phil. It's important to understand the latest changes in higher education policy that affect these programs.

## Key Takeaways

*   The M.Phil. degree has been discontinued in India under the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.
*   Most universities, including IGNOU, have stopped offering M.Phil. courses.
*   After completing your Master's, you can directly apply for Ph.D. programs if you meet the eligibility criteria.
*   Eligibility for Ph.D. typically requires a minimum of 55% marks in your Master's, with some relaxation for reserved categories.

## The Discontinuation of M.Phil. Programs

The National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 has brought about significant reforms in India's education system. One of the key changes relevant to your query is the abolition of the M.Phil. (Master of Philosophy) degree. This means that by the time you complete your Master's program, M.Phil. will no longer be an option for further study in Indian universities.

This policy change aims to streamline the academic progression towards doctoral research, encouraging direct entry into Ph.D. programs after a Master's degree. Universities across the country have been aligning their offerings with NEP 2020, and the vast majority, including institutions like IGNOU, have already ceased M.Phil. admissions and programs.

## Direct Entry to Ph.D. After Your Master's

With the M.Phil. pathway removed, the next step after your Master's degree is direct entry into a Ph.D. (Doctor of Philosophy) program. This is a significant shift, offering a more direct route into advanced research.

To be eligible for Ph.D. admission, you will generally need to have completed your Master's degree with a minimum of 55% aggregate marks. For candidates belonging to reserved categories (such as SC/ST/OBC-NCL/PwD), there is typically a relaxation of 5%, meaning a minimum of 50% marks would make you eligible. These criteria are consistent across most universities as per regulatory guidelines.

## The Ph.D. Application Process

Once you meet the academic eligibility criteria, the application process for a Ph.D. usually involves two main stages:

1.  **Entrance Test:** Most universities conduct their own entrance examinations for Ph.D. admissions. Some national-level tests might also be accepted, depending on the university's specific requirements. These tests assess your research aptitude, subject knowledge, and analytical skills.
2.  **Interview:** Candidates who qualify in the entrance test are then invited for an interview. This stage is crucial as it allows you to discuss your proposed research area, demonstrate your passion for research, and for the university faculty to assess your suitability for doctoral studies.

It's advisable to regularly check the official admission notifications of the universities you are interested in for specific dates, eligibility criteria, and application procedures.

## Planning Your Academic Future

As you progress through your Master's degree from IGNOU, focus on achieving strong academic results, especially maintaining a good percentage. This will be key for your eligibility for Ph.D. programs. You might also start thinking about potential research interests or areas you'd like to explore in a doctoral study. While M.Phil. is no longer an option, the path to advanced research and a Ph.D. remains open and direct.

By the time you complete your Master's, the academic landscape will be well-adjusted to the NEP 2020 guidelines, offering clear pathways for direct Ph.D. enrollment.
