# I got 74.65 percentile in XAT. I belong to OBC category. Can I apply in KJ Somaiya and Welingkar Institute for HR specialization? Is there any girls quota in these colleges?

It's great you're looking into HR specializations and exploring your options! Let's break down your chances for admission at KJ Somaiya and Welingkar, and discuss other potential institutes.

## Key Takeaways

*   KJ Somaiya generally requires a higher XAT percentile, typically 85 and above, with no separate cutoff for OBC candidates.
*   Welingkar Institute, especially for its PGDM HCM program, often considers a holistic profile beyond just XAT scores.
*   There is generally no specific 'girls quota' in these private management institutes; admissions are typically merit-based.
*   Several other reputable institutions could be a good fit for your XAT score and HR specialization interest.

## KJ Somaiya Institute of Management

For KJ Somaiya, based on past admission trends, a XAT percentile of 85 or higher is usually expected for admission to their management programs. This benchmark applies across all categories, meaning there isn't a distinct, lower cutoff for OBC candidates. While your 74.65 percentile is a decent score overall, it might fall short of KJ Somaiya's typical requirements.

## Welingkar Institute of Management Development and Research

Welingkar Institute, with campuses in Mumbai and Bangalore, offers a more profile-centric admission process for some of its specialized programs, such as PGDM Human Resources (HR) or PGDM Healthcare Management (HCM). This means they look at your overall academic record, work experience (if any), extracurricular activities, and performance in group discussions and personal interviews, in addition to your XAT score. Therefore, your 74.65 XAT percentile, combined with a strong profile, could make you a viable candidate for Welingkar. The fee for Welingkar's PGDM programs is approximately ₹11 lakhs.

## Girls Quota in Management Institutes

Private management institutes like KJ Somaiya and Welingkar typically do not have a specific 'girls quota' or reserved seats for female candidates. Admissions are usually based on merit, entrance exam scores, and overall profile, irrespective of gender. However, many institutes strive for gender diversity in their cohorts, which might indirectly benefit female applicants with strong profiles.
