# I registered twice for KEAM – What now?

It can be worrying if you've accidentally submitted multiple applications for KEAM. Let's look at what this might mean for your application and how you can try to resolve it.

## Key Takeaways

*   KEAM generally permits only one application per candidate.
*   Multiple applications might lead to the cancellation of all your submissions.
*   It's good that you've already emailed the authorities; keep records of this communication.
*   Stay vigilant and regularly check the official KEAM portal for updates.

## Understanding KEAM Application Rules

When applying for the Kerala Engineering, Architecture, and Medical (KEAM) entrance examination, candidates are typically expected to submit a single application form. This is a standard procedure across many competitive exams to ensure fairness and streamline the application process. Submitting more than one application, even if for different courses, can often be flagged during the scrutiny phase by the Office of the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE), Kerala.

## Why Multiple Applications Can Be an Issue

The primary concern with multiple applications is that the system is designed to identify and process a unique candidate. When two different application IDs are generated for the same individual, it can be interpreted as an attempt to bypass rules or create confusion. During the verification process, such discrepancies can lead to one or both applications being cancelled or rejected. The CEE, Kerala, aims to maintain a transparent and equitable admissions process, and multiple applications can complicate this.

## What You Can Do Now

1.  **Keep Records of Your Communication:** Since you've already sent an email to the KEAM authorities explaining your situation, ensure you have a copy of that email, including the date and time it was sent. This serves as proof that you proactively informed them of the error.
2.  **Monitor the Official KEAM Portal:** Regularly check the official KEAM website. Look for any announcements, notifications, or a section where you can check the status of your application(s). Sometimes, specific instructions are provided for candidates who have made errors in their applications.
3.  **Look for Contact Information:** If your email hasn't received a response after a reasonable period, consider other official contact methods listed on the KEAM portal. This might include a helpline number or a different email address for technical support or application queries. When contacting them again, reference your previous email and provide both application IDs.
4.  **Prepare for the Worst, Hope for the Best:** While there's a possibility of cancellation, your proactive communication might be viewed favorably. Be prepared for the scenario where you might need to re-apply if allowed, or if one of your applications is deemed valid. However, do not attempt to create a third application unless explicitly instructed to do so by the authorities.

## Preventing Future Issues

Always double-check all instructions and guidelines provided in the official KEAM prospectus before starting your application. When filling out forms, take your time, review all entries before final submission, and ensure you understand the terms and conditions regarding single applications. If you encounter technical glitches, it's often better to contact support before attempting to create a new application.

Staying informed and proactive is key when dealing with application discrepancies like this. We hope for a positive resolution for you.
