# What was the NEET Cut-off for Jharkhand Government Medical Colleges for General Category in 2020?

Understanding past NEET cut-offs can offer valuable insights for aspiring medical students. This guide looks at the scores required for admission to government MBBS seats in Jharkhand for the general category during the 2020 admission cycle.

## Key Takeaways

*   In 2020, three government medical colleges in Jharkhand participated in state counselling for MBBS admissions.
*   Admission scores for the general category ranged from 602 to 617 marks.
*   RIMS Ranchi had the highest cut-off among these institutions.
*   Aiming for a score in the 600-620 range is generally recommended for a strong chance at a government MBBS seat in Jharkhand.

## Jharkhand Government Medical Colleges in 2020

During the NEET 2020 admission process, three government medical colleges in Jharkhand offered MBBS seats through state counselling. These institutions are highly sought after by students aiming for a medical career due to their quality education and government affiliation.

## General Category Cut-off Scores for 2020

For students applying under the general category, the cut-off marks for these government medical colleges in Jharkhand were as follows:

*   **Rajendra Institute of Medical Sciences (RIMS), Ranchi:** 617 Marks
*   **Mahatma Gandhi Memorial Medical College (MGM-MC), Jamshedpur:** 604 Marks
*   **Shaheed Nirmal Mahto Medical College (SNMMC), Dhanbad (formerly PMCH Dhanbad):** 602 Marks

These scores represent the minimum marks required for a general category candidate to secure a seat in these respective colleges during the 2020 counselling rounds. It's important to remember that cut-offs can fluctuate each year based on factors like the number of applicants, exam difficulty, and available seats.

## Understanding Cut-off Trends

Analyzing historical cut-off data, such as the 2020 figures, helps in setting realistic goals. The higher the demand for a particular college and the better the performance of candidates in the NEET exam, the higher the cut-off scores tend to be. RIMS Ranchi, being a premier institution in the state, consistently sees higher cut-offs.

## Planning for Future Admissions

Based on these 2020 figures, if you are a general category student aspiring for a government MBBS seat in Jharkhand, aiming for a NEET score of approximately 600-620 marks or above would provide a good buffer. While past data is a guide, preparing thoroughly and striving for the highest possible score is always the best approach. Keep an eye on official announcements from the National Testing Agency (NTA) for NEET exam details and the Jharkhand Combined Entrance Competitive Examination Board (JCECEB) for state counselling information.

We hope this information helps you understand the admission landscape for government medical colleges in Jharkhand for the general category in 2020.
