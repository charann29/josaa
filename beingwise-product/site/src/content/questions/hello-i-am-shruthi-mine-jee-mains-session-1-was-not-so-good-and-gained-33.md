Hi Shruthi, it's completely understandable to feel a bit disheartened after your JEE Main Session 1 results. Many students find themselves in a similar position, and the good news is that you have multiple avenues to explore for engineering admissions. Let's break down your options.

## Key Takeaways

*   Your JEE Main Session 1 percentile of 33.6899 is low for top-tier NITs/IIITs, but many other colleges accept JEE scores.
*   Appearing for JEE Main Session 2, KCET, CUET UG, and COMEDK UGET significantly broadens your college choices.
*   Admission criteria vary greatly by college; always check the specific requirements for each institution you're interested in.
*   Focus on preparing well for your upcoming exams to maximize your chances across different entrance tests.

## Understanding Your JEE Main Score

Your JEE Main Session 1 percentile indicates that you scored better than approximately 33.68% of the candidates who appeared for that session. While this percentile might be challenging for securing a seat in the most sought-after National Institutes of Technology (NITs) or Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), it doesn't close the door to all engineering colleges. Many private and state-level engineering colleges consider JEE Main scores, even at lower percentiles, especially for less competitive branches or through management quotas.

For a clearer picture of your standing, you can refer to the official NTA website for previous year's cut-offs, though these can fluctuate annually.

## Exploring Multiple Entrance Exams

It's excellent that you are planning to appear for JEE Main Session 2, KCET, CUET UG, and COMEDK UGET. This strategy is highly recommended as it diversifies your opportunities. Each of these exams caters to admissions in different sets of colleges:

*   **JEE Main Session 2:** This is a crucial second chance to improve your percentile for NITs, IIITs, and various centrally funded technical institutions, as well as many state and private colleges that accept JEE scores.
*   **KCET (Karnataka Common Entrance Test):** Essential for admission to government and private engineering colleges in Karnataka, provided you meet the domicile requirements.
*   **CUET UG (Common University Entrance Test Undergraduate):** This exam is increasingly being adopted by central, state, deemed, and private universities across India for various undergraduate programs, including engineering.
*   **COMEDK UGET (Consortium of Medical, Engineering and Dental Colleges of Karnataka Undergraduate Entrance Test):** This is vital for admission to private unaided engineering colleges in Karnataka, particularly for students who do not have Karnataka domicile.

## Identifying Suitable Colleges

Since you haven't mentioned a specific college, it's difficult to say if you can get into "your college." The key is to research colleges that accept scores from the exams you are taking. Here's how you can approach this:

*   **For JEE Main:** Look for state-level engineering colleges and private universities that accept JEE Main scores. Many institutions publish their cut-offs on their official websites or through their admission brochures.
*   **For KCET & COMEDK UGET:** Focus on engineering colleges located in Karnataka. Official portals for KCET and COMEDK will list participating institutions and their previous year's closing ranks.
*   **For CUET UG:** Check the official CUET website for the list of participating universities and the specific engineering programs they offer through CUET scores.

When researching, pay attention to factors like course availability, fee structure, location, and, if available, their NIRF rankings, which can give an indication of their overall quality.

Your proactive approach in attempting multiple entrance exams is a smart move. Focus on preparing diligently for each one, as a strong performance in any of these can open up many doors for your engineering aspirations.
