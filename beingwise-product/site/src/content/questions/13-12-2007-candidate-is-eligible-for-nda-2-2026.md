# Is a December 13, 2007 Candidate Eligible for NDA 2 2026?

Understanding the eligibility criteria for the National Defence Academy (NDA) examination is crucial for any aspiring candidate. This guide will clarify the age requirements, specifically for those born on December 13, 2007, looking towards the NDA 2 2026 exam.

## Key Takeaways

*   The NDA exam has strict age limits set by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC).
*   Candidates must be unmarried Indian citizens to be eligible.
*   For NDA 2 2026, the age window typically falls between July 2, 2007, and July 1, 2010 (inclusive).
*   A candidate born on December 13, 2007, generally meets the age criteria for NDA 1, but likely not for NDA 2 2026.

## Understanding NDA Eligibility

The UPSC, the body responsible for conducting the NDA examination, sets out clear eligibility conditions for all applicants. These include nationality, marital status, educational qualifications, and, most importantly, age. The age criteria are very precise, defining both the earliest and latest birth dates for candidates to be considered eligible for a particular examination cycle.

## Age Criteria for NDA

The NDA exam is conducted twice a year, known as NDA 1 and NDA 2. Each examination has a specific age window that candidates must fall within. Generally, candidates need to be between 16.5 and 19.5 years old at the time the course commences. This means that for any given NDA exam, there's a fixed range of birth dates that are considered eligible.

### NDA 2 2026 Age Window

Based on past trends and the typical age requirements, the eligibility window for NDA 2 2026 is expected to be for candidates born between **July 2, 2007, and July 1, 2010 (inclusive)**. It's important to always refer to the official notification released by the UPSC for the most accurate and up-to-date information regarding specific exam dates and eligibility cut-offs.

## Eligibility for a December 13, 2007 Candidate

Let's apply this to a candidate born on December 13, 2007. If the age window for NDA 2 2026 is indeed July 2, 2007, to July 1, 2010, then a candidate born on December 13, 2007, would fall outside this specific range. They would be considered too old for the NDA 2 2026 examination. Such a candidate would typically have been eligible for earlier NDA examinations, specifically NDA 1 and possibly NDA 2 in 2025.

## Checking Official Notifications

While this guide provides general information based on established patterns, the definitive source for all eligibility criteria is the official notification released by the UPSC for each NDA examination. Candidates are strongly advised to meticulously review these documents as soon as they are published to confirm their eligibility for any particular attempt. Always refer to the official UPSC website for these announcements.
