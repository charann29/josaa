The TS EAMCET examination is a crucial step for students aiming for engineering and agriculture programs in Telangana. Understanding the chapter-wise weightage can help you strategize your preparation effectively, especially for the 2021 exam cycle.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Mathematics:** Calculus, Vectors, and Probability generally carry significant weight, making them high-priority topics for focused study.
*   **Physics:** Thermodynamics, Laws of Motion, and Magnetic Effects of Current are consistently important, alongside foundational concepts like Work, Energy, and Power.
*   **Chemistry:** Atomic Structure, Chemical Bonding, Solutions, and p-block elements often account for a larger share of marks, requiring thorough revision.
*   **Strategic Preparation:** While exact weightage can vary slightly year to year, focusing on these high-weightage chapters can optimize your study time and boost your score.

## Understanding Chapter Weightage

While the official syllabus provides a comprehensive list of topics, the distribution of marks across chapters (known as weightage) can guide your study plan. For TS EAMCET 2021, given potential syllabus adjustments, these estimations are based on past trends and expert analysis. Remember, these are approximate figures designed to help you prioritize.

## Mathematics: Key Chapters and Weightage

Mathematics often forms a significant portion of the EAMCET score. Chapters with higher weightage deserve more dedicated practice.

*   **Calculus:** Approximately 6%
*   **Vectors:** Around 10%
*   **Probability:** Roughly 15%
*   **Matrices and Determinants:** Often around 5%
*   **Sets, Relations, and Functions:** Typically about 5%
*   **Limits, Indefinite and Definite Integration:** Each contributing around 5%

Students should ensure a strong grasp of these areas, as they frequently appear in the examination.

Chemistry in TS EAMCET covers Physical, Inorganic, and Organic Chemistry. Some chapters are consistently more prominent.

*   **Atomic Structure and Chemical Bonding:** Approximately 8%
*   **Solutions:** Around 7%
*   **Classification of Elements and Periodicity:** Roughly 5%
*   **p-block elements:** Often around 5-9%
*   **Thermodynamics:** About 4%
