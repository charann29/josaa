# What shall I do to become an airline pilot after 12th from PCM??

Dreaming of soaring through the skies as an airline pilot after completing your 12th with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM)? It's a fantastic career choice, and there are clear pathways to achieve it.

## Key Takeaways

*   You have two primary routes: direct flight training or pursuing an aviation-related bachelor's degree first.
*   Direct flight training at a Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) approved school is often the quickest path to obtaining your Commercial Pilot License (CPL).
*   Aviation-focused engineering degrees can provide a strong theoretical foundation and alternative career options within the aerospace industry.
*   Both paths require significant dedication, rigorous training, and meeting strict medical and academic standards.

## Path 1: Direct Flight Training After 12th

This is often the most straightforward and fastest way to become a pilot. After finishing your 12th grade with PCM, you can enroll in a recognized flying school. These schools provide the intensive training required to obtain various pilot licenses, with the Commercial Pilot License (CPL) being the key credential for aspiring airline pilots.

### Choosing a Flying School

It's crucial to select a flying school approved by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) in India. These schools adhere to specific training standards and curricula. The training typically involves both ground classes, covering subjects like air navigation, aviation meteorology, and air regulations, and extensive practical flying hours. You'll progress through different aircraft types and gain experience in various flying conditions.

### Licenses and Ratings

Your primary goal will be to earn your CPL. Along the way, you'll also acquire other essential ratings, such as an Instrument Rating (IR) and a Multi-Engine Rating (ME). These ratings are vital for flying modern commercial aircraft under different weather conditions and operating multi-engine planes. After obtaining your CPL and relevant ratings, you'll be eligible to apply for first officer positions with airlines.

## Path 2: Bachelor's Degree Followed by Flight Training

Another viable option is to first complete a bachelor's degree in a relevant field before embarking on flight training. This path offers a deeper academic foundation and can provide a fallback or alternative career within the aviation sector if direct piloting doesn't materialize immediately.

### Relevant Bachelor's Degrees

Consider degrees such as:

*   **Bachelor of Science in Aviation:** These programs often integrate some flight theory and can sometimes be combined with flight training modules.
*   **Aeronautical Science:** Focuses on the science and technology of flight.
*   **Aeronautical Engineering:** Deals with the design, construction, and study of aircraft.
*   **Aerospace Engineering:** A broader field encompassing aircraft and spacecraft.
*   **Avionics Engineering:** Specializes in the electronic systems used in aircraft.

Many reputable engineering colleges and universities offer these programs. You can check the official NIRF rankings for top institutions in engineering. Admission to these programs typically involves entrance exams like JEE Main, conducted by NTA, or state-level engineering entrance tests, with counseling often managed through platforms like the official JoSAA portal.

### Advantages of a Degree Path

*   **Strong Foundation:** Provides in-depth knowledge of aircraft systems, aerodynamics, and aviation principles.
*   **Alternative Careers:** Opens doors to roles in aircraft maintenance, design, research, or management within the aviation industry.
*   **Higher Education:** A degree can be a stepping stone to a Master's degree, further specializing your knowledge.

After completing your bachelor's degree, you would then enroll in a DGCA-approved flying school, similar to Path 1, to acquire your CPL and other necessary ratings.

Whichever path you choose, becoming an airline pilot demands commitment, rigorous training, and a passion for aviation. Best of luck with your journey!
