# Closing Ranks for MD Physiology at Bangalore Medical College

Understanding the previous year's closing ranks can offer valuable insights for aspiring MD Physiology students hoping to join Bangalore Medical College and Research Institute (BMCRI). This information helps candidates gauge their chances based on their NEET PG scores and plan their counselling strategy.

## Key Takeaways

*   For the General Category (All India Quota), the closing rank for MD Physiology at BMCRI in the previous year was approximately between 49,600 and 50,600.
*   This suggests that a rank below 50,000 significantly improves your prospects for admission to this program at BMCRI.
*   Counselling for MD courses typically involves multiple rounds, with ranks often extending further in later rounds.
*   Always refer to the official Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) website for the most accurate and up-to-date counselling schedules and results.

## Understanding Closing Ranks

Closing ranks represent the rank of the last candidate admitted to a specific course at a particular institution under a given category and quota. These ranks fluctuate each year due to various factors, including the number of applicants, their performance in the NEET PG exam, the availability of seats, and candidate preferences during counselling.

## Previous Year's Rank Trend (General Category, All India Quota)

Based on historical data, the closing rank for MD Physiology at BMCRI for the General Category under the All India Quota has typically fallen within a specific range. In the previous admission cycle, the final rank for admission was approximately between 49,600 and 50,600. This range serves as a benchmark for prospective candidates.

## Counselling Process and Rounds

Admissions to MD programs, including Physiology at BMCRI, are conducted through a centralized counselling process managed by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC). This process usually involves several rounds. Candidates are allotted seats based on their NEET PG rank, choices filled, and seat availability. As counselling progresses, the closing ranks can sometimes extend in later rounds as seats become available due to candidates upgrading to other colleges or courses.

For example, based on recent trends, the round-by-round closing ranks for MD Physiology (General Category, All India Quota) at BMCRI have shown the following pattern:

*   **Round 1:** Approximately 54,493
*   **Round 2:** Approximately 57,292
*   **Round 3:** Approximately 84,538
*   **Round 4 (Final Round):** Approximately 110,373

It's important to note that these are illustrative figures from a recent cycle and actual ranks for future admissions may vary. Always refer to the official MCC portal for the definitive closing ranks for each counselling round.

## Factors Influencing Rank Variation

Several factors contribute to the year-on-year variation in closing ranks:

*   **NEET PG Performance:** A higher number of top-scoring candidates can push ranks higher (numerically lower).
*   **Number of Seats:** Any change in the number of available MD Physiology seats at BMCRI can impact the closing ranks.
*   **Candidate Preferences:** The choices filled by candidates during the counselling process play a crucial role. If more high-ranking candidates prefer BMCRI for Physiology, the closing rank will be lower.

Considering these aspects can help you prepare for the competitive admission landscape.
