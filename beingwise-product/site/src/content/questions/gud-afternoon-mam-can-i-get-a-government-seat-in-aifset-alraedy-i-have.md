# Gud afternoon mam, can I get a government seat in AIFSET..ALRAEDY I HAVE COMPLETED TO 10+2 AFTER DMLT (DIPLOMA IN MEDICAL LAB TECHNICIAN) I AM ELIGIBLE TO STUDY FOR BSC FORENSIC SCIENCE IN GOVERNMENT QUOTA..

Many students wonder about their eligibility for Bachelor of Science (B.Sc) in Forensic Science, especially after completing a diploma like DMLT. Let's clarify the requirements for securing a government quota seat through the All India Forensic Science Entrance Test (AIFSET).

## Key Takeaways

*   AIFSET is a national-level entrance exam for B.Sc and M.Sc Forensic Science programs.
*   Having a DMLT after 10+2 does not automatically grant eligibility for government quota seats; core 10+2 science subjects are crucial.
*   Eligibility for B.Sc Forensic Science generally requires a minimum of 50% in 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and either Mathematics or Biology.
*   AIFSET is an offline, multiple-choice question exam with 100 questions, and cut-offs vary annually.

## Understanding AIFSET

AIFSET, or the All India Forensic Science Entrance Test, is a significant national examination designed to facilitate admissions into B.Sc and M.Sc Forensic Science courses across various institutions. It serves as a common platform for aspiring forensic scientists to gain entry into these specialized programs.

## B.Sc Forensic Science Eligibility: The Core Requirements

For B.Sc Forensic Science, the fundamental eligibility criteria focus on your academic performance in your 10+2 (or equivalent) examination. Candidates must have successfully passed their 10+2 with a minimum aggregate score of 50%. Critically, this qualification must be in the science stream, specifically with Physics, Chemistry, and either Mathematics or Biology as core subjects. While your DMLT is a valuable qualification, the primary assessment for B.Sc Forensic Science eligibility often reverts to your 10+2 science subject combination and marks.

## AIFSET Exam Structure and Attempts

The AIFSET examination consists of 100 multiple-choice questions. It is conducted offline, requiring candidates to be physically present at designated centers. The qualifying cut-off scores for AIFSET are not fixed; they fluctuate each year and also depend on the specific category of the applicant. Therefore, it's challenging to predict an exact score needed for admission. Candidates are generally allowed a maximum of three attempts to clear the AIFSET exam.

## Government Quota Seats

Securing a government quota seat typically depends on a combination of factors: meeting the basic eligibility criteria, performing well in the entrance exam (like AIFSET), and the specific reservation policies of the institutions you are applying to. Your DMLT qualification might be beneficial for certain diploma-entry programs or lateral entries in some cases, but for direct B.Sc Forensic Science government quota seats, the 10+2 science background is usually the primary determinant. Always check the specific prospectus of the government institutions you are interested in for precise details on their quota allocations and eligibility nuances.
