Lady Irwin College, a distinguished institution affiliated with the University of Delhi, is a popular choice for students seeking quality education, particularly in Home Science and Education. This guide offers a clear overview of its academic programs, fee structure, admission procedures, cutoff trends, and placement opportunities.

## Courses Offered and Fees

Lady Irwin College provides a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. The flagship courses often include B.Sc. in Home Science and B.Ed., alongside various postgraduate specialisations. While specific fee details can vary by program and year, here’s a general idea:

*   **Undergraduate Programs (B.Sc., B.Ed.):** Annual fees typically range from INR 15,000 to INR 20,000.
*   **Postgraduate Programs (M.Sc., M.Ed.):** Annual fees usually fall between INR 18,000 to INR 25,000.

It's always recommended to check the official Delhi University and college websites for the most current fee information for your chosen course.

## Admission Process

Admission to Lady Irwin College, for both undergraduate and postgraduate courses, is entirely entrance-based. The college follows a centralized admission system managed by the University of Delhi.

### Undergraduate Admissions

For undergraduate programs like B.Sc. and B.Ed., admission is based on the Common University Entrance Test (CUET UG). Students must appear for CUET UG in subjects relevant to their desired course and their Class 12 curriculum.

### Postgraduate Admissions

For postgraduate programs, admission is determined by performance in the Common University Entrance Test (CUET PG).

### Application Steps

1.  **CUET Application:** Register and apply for the CUET UG or CUET PG exam through its official website. You can select multiple courses and universities, up to a maximum of ten programs per university. The application fee varies based on the number of programs chosen.
2.  **Appear for CUET:** Take the entrance examination in your chosen subjects. Delhi University guidelines require students to take CUET in subjects they have studied in Class 12.
3.  **CSAS Registration:** After CUET results are declared, eligible candidates must register on the Common Seat Allocation System (CSAS) portal of Delhi University. This is a crucial step for final admission and seat allocation.

### CSAS Registration Process

*   Visit the official CSAS portal (UG or PG, as applicable) and create a new user account.
*   Fill in your personal details and academic information.
*   Crucially, select Lady Irwin College and your preferred course combinations.
*   Pay the non-refundable CSAS application fee:
    *   **INR 250** for UR/OBC-NCL/EWS categories.
    *   **INR 100** for SC/ST/PwBD categories.

**Note:** All application and admission processes are conducted online. There is no offline application method.

## Cutoff Scores

Cutoff scores for admission to Lady Irwin College are highly competitive and vary each year based on the number of applicants, their CUET scores, and the availability of seats. Generally, students aiming for popular courses like B.Sc. Home Science or B.Ed. should target high scores in their respective CUET examinations. The cutoffs are released in multiple rounds through the CSAS portal.

## Placements

Lady Irwin College has a dedicated placement cell that works to connect students with various organizations. While specific placement statistics can fluctuate, the college generally sees decent placement opportunities, especially for its professional courses.

Based on student feedback and verified reviews, the college has received a rating of approximately 3.18 out of 5 for its placement facilities. Graduates find opportunities in sectors such as education, nutrition, food technology, fashion, textile design, and research. Many students also pursue higher education or entrepreneurship after completing their degrees.
