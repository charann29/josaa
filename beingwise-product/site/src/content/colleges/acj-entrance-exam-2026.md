Considering a career in journalism or financial media? The Asian College of Journalism (ACJ) offers respected postgraduate diploma programs. This guide from BeingWise will walk you through everything you need to know about the ACJ Entrance Exam for 2026 admissions, covering courses, fees, the admission process, expected cutoffs, and career opportunities.

## ACJ Postgraduate Diploma Programs

ACJ provides specialized one-year postgraduate diploma courses designed to equip students with practical skills and theoretical knowledge for the media industry. The primary offerings are:

*   **PG Diploma in Journalism:** This program focuses on core journalism skills across various platforms, including print, broadcast, and digital media. It's ideal for aspiring reporters, editors, and content creators.
*   **PG Diploma in Business and Financial Journalism:** Tailored for those interested in economic and financial reporting, this course covers business concepts, market analysis, and specialized financial communication.

## ACJ Entrance Exam 2026: Key Dates (Tentative)

The official dates for the ACJ Entrance Exam 2026 are yet to be announced by the college. However, based on previous years, you can expect the examinations to follow a similar schedule, typically spread across multiple phases:

*   **Phase 1:** Likely in the second week of April 2026
*   **Phase 2:** Likely in the third week of May 2026
*   **Phase 3:** Likely in the second week of June 2026

It's crucial to submit your application on time, as only registered candidates will receive notifications about their specific exam and interview schedules via their registered email addresses.

## Admission Process for ACJ 2026

The selection process for ACJ is rigorous and involves two main stages:

### Stage 1: Online Entrance Test

This is a two-hour online examination conducted in two slots – one for Section I (morning) and one for Section II (afternoon). Candidates applying for either or both diploma programs will be notified of their specific test date and time. The test assesses candidates on:

*   **English Language:** This section evaluates your grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and writing abilities.
*   **General Knowledge & Current Affairs:** This tests your awareness of national and international events, as well as general factual knowledge.

There is no negative marking for unanswered questions, so you can skip questions if you are unsure.

### Stage 2: Personal Interview (PI)

Candidates who successfully clear the online entrance test will be invited for an online Personal Interview. This round is critical for evaluating your suitability for the chosen PG diploma program. The interview panel will assess:

*   Your understanding of the media landscape
*   Your motivation for pursuing journalism or financial journalism
*   Your communication skills and personality
*   Your analytical abilities and general awareness

The interviews are typically held on the same day as the online test or the day immediately following it. You will receive your interview schedule at least three days prior to the date.

## Expected Cutoffs

ACJ does not publicly release specific cutoff scores for its entrance exam. Admission is highly competitive, and cutoffs depend on several factors, including the overall performance of applicants in a given year, the number of available seats, and the quality of candidates in the interview round. A strong performance in both the online test and the personal interview is essential for securing admission.

## Placement Opportunities

ACJ has a strong reputation in the media industry, and its graduates are highly sought after. The college has a dedicated placement cell that assists students in securing internships and full-time positions. Alumni often find roles in leading media organizations, including:

*   National and international news channels
*   Major newspapers and magazines
*   Digital media platforms
*   Business news outlets
*   Content creation agencies

The college maintains excellent industry connections, which significantly aid in student placements. Graduates from both the Journalism and Business & Financial Journalism programs have gone on to build successful careers across various media domains.

## Preparing for the ACJ Entrance Exam

To maximize your chances of success, consider these preparation tips:

*   **Stay Updated:** Regularly read newspapers, magazines, and reputable online news sources to build your general knowledge and current affairs awareness.
*   **Improve English Skills:** Focus on grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and writing practice. Reading diverse texts can be very helpful.
*   **Practice Mock Tests:** Familiarize yourself with the exam pattern and time constraints.
*   **Prepare for Interview:** Research ACJ, understand current media trends, and be ready to articulate your passion for journalism or financial reporting. Practice answering common interview questions.

By understanding the process and preparing diligently, you can significantly enhance your prospects of joining the Asian College of Journalism for the 2026 academic year.
