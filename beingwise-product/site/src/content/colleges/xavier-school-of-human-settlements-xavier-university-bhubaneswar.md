The Xavier School of Human Settlements (XSHS), part of Xavier University Bhubaneswar, is a distinguished institution focusing on the complex challenges of urban development and human habitats. It offers specialised postgraduate and doctoral programmes designed to cultivate experts in creating sustainable and well-managed communities. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of its courses, fee structure, admission process, and career prospects.

## Courses Offered at XSHS

XSHS provides advanced academic programmes at both the postgraduate and doctoral levels, primarily in the domain of Urban Management and Governance. These programmes are structured to equip students with deep knowledge and practical skills for addressing modern urban issues.

### Master's Programme in Urban Management and Governance (MBA-UMG)

This is a two-year full-time Master's degree tailored for individuals aspiring to lead in urban planning and management. The curriculum is rigorous, spanning three academic terms, each lasting 12-14 weeks. It is an intensive programme, with students typically dedicating around 70 hours per week to their academic pursuits. The entire programme carries a total of 113 credits, encompassing a blend of core and elective coursework, alongside other experiential learning components.

### Doctoral Programmes (PhD)

XSHS also offers both full-time and part-time research programmes at the doctoral level. These programmes are designed for aspiring researchers and academics who wish to delve into critical areas of human settlements, contributing to new knowledge and solutions.

## Fees Structure

Specific fee details for the academic year are typically updated on the official XSHS website. Prospective students are advised to check the university's admissions portal for the most current information regarding tuition and other associated costs for their chosen programme.

## Admission Process

Admission to programmes at XSHS is competitive and entrance-exam based. There is no provision for direct admission to any of the postgraduate or doctoral programmes.

### Eligibility Criteria for MBA-UMG

Candidates applying for the MBA-UMG programme must possess a Bachelor's degree of at least three years' duration from a recognised university, with a minimum aggregate score of 50%.

### Entrance Examinations

Applicants for the MBA-UMG programme are required to appear for national-level management aptitude tests. XSHS primarily accepts scores from:

*   **CAT (Common Admission Test)**
*   **XAT (Xavier Aptitude Test)**
*   **MAT (Management Aptitude Test)**

Admission is granted based on a merit-based selection process, considering the scores from these entrance exams, academic record, and other selection rounds like group discussions and personal interviews.

## Cutoff

Cutoff scores for admission to XSHS programmes vary each year based on the applicant pool and the performance in the entrance examinations. While specific cutoff percentages are not publicly disclosed, candidates are encouraged to perform strongly in the accepted entrance tests to enhance their chances of admission.

## Placements and Career Opportunities

Xavier School of Human Settlements is committed to ensuring strong career outcomes for its graduates, offering 100% placement assistance. The institution attracts leading organisations for recruitment drives.

### Career Paths for MBA-UMG Graduates

Graduates of the MBA-UMG programme are well-prepared for diverse roles across various sectors. The curriculum equips them with skills applicable in areas such as:

*   **Finance** within urban development projects
*   **Operations** management in municipal bodies or urban infrastructure companies
*   **Human Resources** for development organisations
*   **Marketing** and communication for urban initiatives
*   **Consultancy** in urban planning and policy

Year after year, XSHS has demonstrated an improving placement record, with graduates securing positions in a variety of organisations, including government bodies, NGOs, consulting firms, and private sector companies involved in urban development, infrastructure, and real estate. The average placement packages have shown a consistent upward trend, reflecting the growing demand for skilled professionals in human settlements.
