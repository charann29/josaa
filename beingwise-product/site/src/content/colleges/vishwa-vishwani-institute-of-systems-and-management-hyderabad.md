Vishwa Vishwani Institute of Systems and Management (VVISM) in Hyderabad is a well-regarded institution offering various management and undergraduate programs. If you're considering VVISM for your higher education, understanding its courses, fees, admission process, and placement opportunities is key to making an informed decision.

## Programs Offered at VVISM Hyderabad

VVISM primarily focuses on management education, with its flagship Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM) program. The institute also offers undergraduate (UG) courses. These programs are designed to equip students with practical skills and theoretical knowledge relevant to today's industry.

### Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM)

The PGDM is VVISM's premier offering, known for its industry-aligned curriculum. It is a full-time program that prepares students for leadership roles in various sectors.

### Undergraduate (UG) Programs

VVISM also provides undergraduate courses. While specific program names are not detailed, these typically include Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) or similar management-focused degrees, laying a foundation for further studies or entry-level roles.

## Fees Structure

The application fee for the PGDM program at VVISM is INR 1,200. This fee is non-refundable and must be submitted along with the application documents. For online applicants, payment can be made through the institute's official website's secure payment gateway. Offline applications allow for cash or other accepted payment methods like card or UPI.

## Admission Process

Admissions at VVISM Hyderabad are a blend of merit-based selection and entrance exam performance. The process varies slightly between postgraduate and undergraduate programs.

### PGDM Admission

For PGDM admissions, VVISM considers scores from several national-level management entrance exams. Accepted exams include:

*   CAT
*   MAT
*   XAT
*   CMAT
*   GMAT
*   ATMA

Candidates who meet the entrance exam criteria are then invited for a Group Discussion (GD) and a Personal Interview (PI) round. The final selection is based on a comprehensive evaluation of academic record, entrance exam score, and performance in the GD-PI rounds.

### Undergraduate Admission

Admission to undergraduate courses is primarily merit-based, considering the applicant's performance in their last qualifying examination. While merit is key, candidates with scores in relevant entrance exams may also apply. Shortlisted candidates for UG programs will also undergo a Personal Interview for final admission.

### Application Timeline

VVISM typically conducts its application and admission process between January and May each year. Classes for most programs usually commence in the first week of July. While specific deadlines for undergraduate admissions are usually announced on the official website, it's advisable to check regularly for the latest updates.
