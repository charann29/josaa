Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), located in Indore, is a well-regarded institution offering a wide array of programs. This page provides a comprehensive overview of its courses, fee structures, admission procedures, cutoff trends, and placement opportunities to help you make an informed decision.

## Courses and Fees at DAVV

DAVV offers diverse undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across various disciplines, including engineering, management, science, arts, and law. The fee structure varies significantly depending on the course and the institute within DAVV. For instance, engineering programs typically have different fees compared to management or science courses. It's always advisable to check the specific fee details for your chosen program on the official DAVV website.

## Admission Process

Admission to most undergraduate and postgraduate programs at DAVV is primarily through national-level entrance examinations. For many courses, especially at the UG level, the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) is a key requirement. However, some specialized postgraduate courses, such as LL.M., might accept CUET-PG or have their own specific entrance criteria.

*   **For B.Tech and other UG courses:** CUET scores are generally mandatory.
*   **For PG courses:** CUET-PG or other relevant entrance exams might be required.
*   **Direct Admission:** While CUET is crucial for popular programs, some specific courses might offer alternative admission routes. However, it's always best to be prepared for the relevant entrance exams to secure a seat in your preferred program, as competition is high.

### Important Admission Tips:

*   Thoroughly research the admission criteria for your desired course.
*   Prepare well in advance for CUET or other required entrance exams.
*   Keep an eye on official notification dates for applications and counseling.

## Cutoff Information

Cutoffs for admission to DAVV vary each year based on factors like the number of applicants, the difficulty of the entrance exam, and the availability of seats. For popular programs like B.E. in Computer Science and Engineering, the cutoff ranks are typically higher. For example, a general AI category candidate might need a rank around 58,973 for B.E. Computer Science and Engineering, while a course like B.E. Civil Engineering might have a lower cutoff, around 157,297.

These figures are indicative and can change. Aspiring students should aim for the best possible scores in their respective entrance exams to enhance their chances of admission to competitive branches.
