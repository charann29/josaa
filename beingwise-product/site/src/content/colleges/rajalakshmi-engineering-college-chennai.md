## Courses Offered at REC

REC provides a diverse selection of Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) and Master of Technology (M.Tech.) programs, along with a Master of Business Administration (MBA). The undergraduate engineering courses cover various specializations, allowing students to choose a field that aligns with their interests and career aspirations.

### Popular B.E. Specializations:

*   Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)
*   Mechanical Engineering
*   Automobile Engineering
*   Civil Engineering
*   Electronics and Communication Engineering
*   Electrical and Electronics Engineering
*   Information Technology
*   Biomedical Engineering
*   Aeronautical Engineering

For postgraduate studies, students can pursue M.Tech degrees in various engineering disciplines or an MBA program.

## Admission Process

Admission to REC programs is primarily based on entrance examination scores and counselling processes.

### B.E. Admissions:

Admission to undergraduate B.E. programs is conducted through the Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA) counselling. Eligibility requires candidates to have passed or be appearing for their 10+2 examination. Admission scores are calculated based on marks obtained in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry, which are then normalized to a total of 200 (Mathematics: 100, Physics: 50, Chemistry: 50).

### MBA Admissions:

For the MBA program, admission is typically through the Tamil Nadu Common Entrance Test (TANCET). Candidates must register for and appear in the TANCET exam. Eligibility also requires candidates to hold an appropriate degree from Anna University or an equivalent qualification recognized by Anna University's Syndicate.

## Cutoff Scores

Rajalakshmi Engineering College publishes TNEA cutoff ranks annually for various B.E. specializations. These cutoffs indicate the minimum rank required for admission to a particular course for candidates under the General AI (All India) category.

For the 2024 academic year, the TNEA cutoff ranks for B.E. programs at REC for General AI candidates ranged broadly from 6193 to 46008. Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) was observed to be the most competitive course, while Civil Engineering was comparatively less competitive.
