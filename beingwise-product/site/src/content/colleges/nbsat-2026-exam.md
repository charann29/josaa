Planning your higher education journey in management? The NBSAT 2026 exam is a key gateway to various MBA and PGDM programs. This guide from BeingWise will walk you through everything you need to know about the exam, from courses and fees to the admission process, cutoffs, and placement opportunities.

## NBSAT 2026 Overview

The Narayana Business School Aptitude Test (NBSAT) is an entrance examination designed to assess candidates' readiness for postgraduate management studies. It's a crucial step for those aiming for MBA and PGDM programs at Narayana Business School. The exam is conducted online, allowing candidates to take it remotely from their preferred location.

## Programs and Eligibility

NBSAT is specifically required for admission to all MBA and PGDM programs offered by Narayana Business School. However, candidates also have the flexibility to apply using scores from other national-level management entrance exams like CAT, XAT, NMAT, and CMAT.

To be eligible for the MBA program through NBSAT, candidates must be at least 19 years old. The maximum age limit for applicants is 28 years.

## Application and Exam Process

The registration for NBSAT 2026 is currently open. Once you submit your application form, you will be assigned an exam date and time slot. The test is administered in a remote proctored mode, meaning you can take it from home under supervision. You'll need to log in about 15 minutes before your scheduled test time.

### Exam Format and Difficulty

The NBSAT question paper is divided into four main sections:

*   Data Interpretation & Data Adequacy
*   Vocabulary & Analytical Reasoning
*   Reading Comprehension
*   Quantitative Techniques

The exam generally has an easy difficulty level. A significant advantage is the absence of negative marking, which can reduce test-taking pressure. A solid grasp of basic quantitative techniques, data interpretation, analytical reasoning, vocabulary, and reading comprehension is usually sufficient to achieve a good score.

### Key Preparation Topics

To prepare effectively for NBSAT, focus on these areas:

*   **Quantitative Techniques & Data Interpretation:** Essential for problem-solving and understanding data.
*   **Logical & Analytical Reasoning:** For critical thinking and deduction.
*   **Business & Economic Awareness:** To test your general knowledge of the business world.
*   **Communication & Language Proficiency:** For assessing verbal ability and comprehension.
*   **Creativity & Innovation:** To evaluate problem-solving skills beyond conventional methods.
*   **Decision-Making & Ethical Reasoning:** For assessing judgment and ethical considerations.

## Admission Process and Cutoffs

The NBSAT cutoff for selection is 50%. This means candidates must score at least 50% in the exam to be considered eligible for the next stages of admission.

After the NBSAT results are declared (typically within 24 hours of completing your test), shortlisted candidates will proceed to the Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds. These are mandatory for final selection. The institute will share details for booking your PI slot via email after the results.

### Evaluation Criteria for MBA Admission

The final merit list is compiled after the GD/PI rounds, and offer letters are sent to selected candidates. The comprehensive evaluation process considers several factors, as detailed below:

| Admission Parameter                 | Weightage (out of 100) |
| :---------------------------------- | :--------------------- |
| NBSAT Score (normalized to 40 marks) | 40                     |
| Academic Performance & Work Experience | 20                     |
| Statement of Purpose                | 20                     |
| Personal Interview                  | 20                     |
| **Total**                           | **100**                |
