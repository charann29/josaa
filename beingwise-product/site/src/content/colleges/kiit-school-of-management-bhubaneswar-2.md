The KIIT School of Management (KSOM) in Bhubaneswar is a well-regarded institution for business education. It offers a range of programs designed to prepare students for the corporate world. Let's explore its courses, fees, admission process, typical cutoffs, and placement opportunities.

## Programs Offered at KIIT School of Management

KSOM provides full-time programs at undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral levels. These include:

*   **Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)**: A three-year undergraduate program with various specializations such as Human Resource Management, Finance Management, Marketing Management, International Business, and Operations and Analytics.
*   **Master of Business Administration (MBA)**: A two-year postgraduate program.
*   **Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.)**: For those interested in advanced research.

## Admission Process

Admission to KSOM programs is entrance-based, followed by selection rounds. Here’s a general overview:

### For BBA Admission

1.  **Entrance Exam**: Candidates must appear for the KIIT Entrance Examination (KIITEE), an online test conducted by KIIT.
2.  **Application**: Register for the KIITEE and apply for the BBA program through the official website.
3.  **Counselling**: After the exam, qualified candidates participate in a counselling process.

### For MBA Admission

1.  **Entrance Exams**: Applicants need to take national-level management entrance tests such as CAT, MAT, XAT, or CMAT. KSOM also accepts scores from its own KIITEE Management exam.
2.  **Application**: Apply for the MBA program on the institute's official website, submitting your entrance exam scores.
3.  **Shortlisting**: Candidates are shortlisted based on their application and entrance exam performance.
4.  **Group Discussion (GD) & Personal Interview (PI)**: Shortlisted candidates must attend and qualify these rounds.
5.  **Admission Offer**: Successful candidates receive an admission offer, which they confirm by paying the fees.

## Understanding Cutoffs

Cutoffs are an important indicator of the required performance in entrance exams. For MBA admissions, KSOM considers scores from various national exams. In recent years, typical percentile cutoffs for the open quota have been around:

*   **CMAT / MAT**: Approximately 80 percentile
*   **XAT**: Approximately 70 percentile

While a 70 percentile in XAT might be sufficient, a similar score in CAT is generally not enough for admission to KSOM's MBA program. The KIITEE Management exam is generally considered to have an easy to moderate difficulty level, making it achievable with focused preparation of three to four months.
