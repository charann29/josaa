D.D. Goverdhan Doss Vaishnav College, located in Chennai, is a well-regarded institution offering a wide array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. If you're considering higher education in Chennai, this college might be a great option for you.

## Overview of Programs and Student Life

Vaishnav College is known for its diverse course offerings, including Master of Arts (M.A.), Master of Science (M.Sc.), Master of Social Work (MSW), Master of Commerce (M.Com), Master of Computer Applications (MCA), Master of Business Administration (MBA/PGDM), Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.), Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com), Bachelor of Arts (B.A.), Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA), and Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA). These programs are taught by an experienced faculty committed to student success.

Students frequently praise the college experience. Based on student feedback, the college has received an average rating of 4.31 out of 5 for its overall offerings. Campus life is particularly highlighted, scoring 4.59. Faculty quality is rated 4.23, while infrastructure and placements also receive strong ratings of 4.25 and 4.15 respectively.

## Admission Process

Admissions to Vaishnav College typically open after the results of Class 12 examinations are announced. The process generally involves applying directly to the college. For specific dates and detailed application procedures, it is always best to check the official college website or contact their admissions office directly.

## Courses Offered and Fees

Vaishnav College provides a comprehensive selection of courses across various disciplines. Here's a look at some of the popular programs and their approximate fee structures. Please note that these fees are subject to change and should be confirmed with the college.

### Undergraduate Programs

*   **B.A. (Bachelor of Arts):** Specializations often include English, Economics, and History. The fee for the full course is typically around INR 75,000.
*   **B.Sc. (Bachelor of Science):** Covers subjects like Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Computer Science, and Biotechnology. The total fee generally ranges from INR 75,000 to INR 1.2 Lakhs.
*   **B.Com. (Bachelor of Commerce):** Offers various specializations such as General, Accounting & Finance, and Corporate Secretaryship. The course fee is usually around INR 75,000.
*   **BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration):** Focuses on management principles and business administration. The total fee is approximately INR 90,000.
*   **BCA (Bachelor of Computer Applications):** Aims to provide a strong foundation in computer applications. The fee for the entire program is generally around INR 90,000.

### Postgraduate Programs

*   **M.A. (Master of Arts):** Available in subjects like English and Economics. The fee for the full course is typically around INR 50,000.
*   **M.Sc. (Master of Science):** Includes specializations such as Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Computer Science. The total fee generally ranges from INR 60,000 to INR 1 Lakh.
*   **M.Com. (Master of Commerce):** Focuses on advanced commerce and business studies. The course fee is usually around INR 50,000.
*   **MSW (Master of Social Work):** Prepares students for careers in social work. The total fee is approximately INR 70,000.
*   **MCA (Master of Computer Applications):** A professional master's degree in computer applications. The fee for the entire program is generally around INR 1.5 Lakhs.
*   **MBA/PGDM (Master of Business Administration/Post Graduate Diploma in Management):** A comprehensive program for management professionals. The total fee is approximately INR 2 Lakhs.

## Intake Capacity

The college maintains a manageable student-to-faculty ratio, with the total seat availability for various offered courses generally going up to 60 students per program. This allows for focused attention and a more personalized learning experience.

Vaishnav College has a commendable track record for student placements. Specific cutoff marks for admission can vary each year based on the number of applicants, the difficulty of the qualifying examinations, and the overall academic performance of the applicant pool.
