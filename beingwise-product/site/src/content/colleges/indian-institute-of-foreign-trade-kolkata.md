The Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT), Kolkata, is a well-regarded institution for those aiming for a career in international business. This page provides a comprehensive overview of its courses, fee structure, admission process, cutoff trends, and placement outcomes to help you make an informed decision.

## Programs Offered and Fees

IIFT Kolkata primarily focuses on postgraduate management education, with its flagship MBA in International Business (MBA-IB) program. It also offers doctoral programs for those interested in advanced research.

### MBA in International Business (MBA-IB)

This is a full-time, two-year residential program designed to equip students with expertise in global trade and management. The curriculum covers various aspects of international business, trade policy, finance, marketing, and supply chain management.

**Eligibility for MBA-IB:**

*   A Bachelor’s degree of at least three years' duration in any discipline with a minimum of 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD candidates) from a recognized university.
*   Candidates appearing for their final year bachelor’s degree examination are also eligible to apply, provided they complete their degree requirements by a specified date.

**MBA-IB Fees:**

The total fee for the MBA-IB program is approximately INR 21.77 Lakhs. This generally includes tuition and other academic charges for the two-year duration.

### PhD Programs

IIFT Kolkata offers doctoral degrees in two specializations: Management and Economics. These programs are suitable for individuals seeking to contribute to academic research and industry knowledge.

**PhD in Management:**

*   **Eligibility:** A Master's degree or MPhil degree in any discipline (including IIFT's MPIB) from a recognized university with at least 55% marks or an equivalent CGPA. Alternatively, a diploma course equivalent to a Master's degree (like PGDIM from IIMs) with 55% aggregate, recognized by AICTE/UGC. For part-time mode, a minimum of three years of industry work experience or one year of teaching experience is required. Final year Master's degree candidates can also apply.

**PhD in Economics:**

*   **Eligibility:** A Master's degree in Economics from a recognized university with at least 55% marks or an equivalent grade. A 5% relaxation in marks is provided for SC/ST/OBC (NCL)/differently-abled categories. Final year Master's degree candidates can also apply.

## Admission Process and Cutoffs

Admission to IIFT Kolkata's flagship MBA-IB program has seen a significant change recently. While it previously conducted its own entrance exam, it now primarily accepts CAT scores.

### MBA-IB Admission Process:

1.  **Entrance Exam:** For admissions from the 2024 academic year onwards, IIFT will consider valid scores from the Common Admission Test (CAT). Candidates must register for the CAT exam separately through its official website.
2.  **Application:** Candidates must also fill out the application form for IIFT Kolkata through its official website (iift.ac.in).
3.  **Shortlisting:** Applicants are shortlisted based on their CAT score, academic record (Class 10 and 12 marks), and diversity factors (academic and gender diversity).
    *   **Shortlisting Criteria Weightage:**
        *   CAT Score: 73 marks
        *   Class 10 Marks: 10 marks
        *   Class 12 Marks: 10 marks
        *   Academic Diversity (Non-Engineering): 5 marks
        *   Gender Diversity (Female): 2 marks
        *   **Total:** 100 marks
4.  **Final Selection Rounds:** Shortlisted candidates undergo further evaluation, which includes:
    *   Writing Skills Assessment (WAT)
    *   Group Discussion (GD)
    *   Personal Interview (PI)
5.  **Final Merit List:** The final selection is based on a comprehensive assessment of the CAT score, performance in WAT, GD, PI, and work experience.

**Minimum CAT Percentile:**

While the exact cutoff can vary each year, a CAT percentile of around 60 is generally considered for shortlisting, though higher scores are advisable for stronger chances.

### PhD Admission Process:

Admission to the PhD programs involves a rigorous selection process, typically including an entrance test, research proposal presentation, and a personal interview.
