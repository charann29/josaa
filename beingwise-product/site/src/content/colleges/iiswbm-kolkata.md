Welcome to BeingWise, your guide to higher education in India. Today, we're exploring the Indian Institute of Social Welfare and Business Management (IISWBM) in Kolkata. Established as a pioneering management institute, IISWBM offers various postgraduate programs designed to equip students with essential business and social welfare skills. Let's delve into its offerings, admission process, and more.

## Programs Offered at IISWBM Kolkata

IISWBM provides a range of postgraduate courses, primarily focusing on management and social welfare. These programs are structured to cater to different career aspirations and schedules.

*   **Master of Business Administration (MBA)**: This flagship program comes in several variants:
    *   MBA (Day): A full-time, comprehensive program.
    *   MBA (Evening): Designed for working professionals, allowing them to pursue an MBA without interrupting their careers.
    *   MBA (Human Resource Management - HRM): Specializes in human resource development and management.
    *   MBA (Public Systems - PS): Focuses on management principles applied to public sector organizations.
*   **Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM)**: A popular choice for those seeking a robust management education.
*   **M.Phil. in Social Work**: A research-oriented program for those interested in advanced studies in social welfare.

## Admission Process and Eligibility

Admission to IISWBM's postgraduate programs is primarily entrance-based, followed by a rigorous selection process.

### Eligibility Criteria

For most MBA and PGDM programs, candidates must hold a bachelor's degree from a recognized university. Specific programs may have additional requirements, such as work experience for the MBA (Evening) program.

### Entrance Exams Accepted

IISWBM accepts scores from several national-level management entrance examinations:

*   **For MBA (Day) and MBA (HRM)**: CAT (Common Admission Test)
*   **For MBA (Evening)**: MAT (Management Aptitude Test), CMAT (Common Management Admission Test), or JEMAT (Joint Entrance Management Aptitude Test)
*   **For MBA (PS)**: CAT, XAT (Xavier Aptitude Test), MAT, CMAT, JEMAT, GATE (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering), or ATMA (AIMS Test for Management Admissions)

### Application Procedure

1.  **Application Form Submission**: Candidates can obtain the application form and brochure from the IISWBM reception counter or download it online. The application fee varies by program, typically around INR 900 for most MBA courses and INR 500 for programs like EPGPM. This fee is generally non-refundable.
2.  **Payment and Submission**: If applying offline, submit the filled form along with payment at the institute. For online applications, payment can be made digitally, and a copy of the completed form and payment slip, along with supporting documents, should be couriered to the college address.
3.  **Document Checklist**: Ensure all required documents (photocopies initially, originals for verification) are submitted with your application.

### Selection Rounds

Shortlisted candidates, based on their entrance exam scores, are invited for further evaluation:

*   **Group Discussion (GD)**: Assesses teamwork, communication, and problem-solving skills.
*   **Personal Interview (PI)**: Evaluates a candidate's aptitude, motivation, and suitability for the program.

Final admission offers are extended to candidates who successfully clear all selection rounds. A seat acceptance fee is required to confirm admission.

IISWBM considers competitive scores from various entrance exams. The specific cutoff for each program can fluctuate year to year based on the applicant pool and the overall performance in the entrance exams. Candidates should aim for strong scores in their respective entrance exams to increase their chances of being shortlisted for GDPI rounds.

IISWBM has a dedicated placement cell that assists students in securing internships and final placements. The institute aims to provide good career opportunities across various sectors. While specific average package figures from the provided text are limited, the MBA (Evening) program, for instance, has received positive reviews regarding its placement assistance and outcomes for working professionals.
