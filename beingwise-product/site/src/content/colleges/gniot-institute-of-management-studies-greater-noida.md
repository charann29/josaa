GNIOT Institute of Management Studies (GIMS), located in Greater Noida, is a prominent institution for management education. This page provides a clear overview of its programs, fee structure, admission process, cutoff details, and placement opportunities, helping you make an informed decision.

## Programs Offered at GIMS

GIMS primarily focuses on postgraduate management programs. Their flagship offering is the Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM), designed to equip students with comprehensive business knowledge and practical skills for the corporate world.

## Fee Structure

The fee for the PGDM program at GNIOT Institute of Management Studies is ₹3.14 Lakhs. This amount typically covers tuition and other academic charges for the entire program duration.

## Admission Process

Admission to the PGDM program at GIMS is a multi-stage process, primarily relying on national-level entrance exam scores. Here's a breakdown:

### Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university. Specific percentage requirements may apply.

### Entrance Exams

Applicants are required to appear for one of the following management entrance examinations and secure a valid score:

*   CAT (Common Admission Test)
*   MAT (Management Aptitude Test)
*   CMAT (Common Management Admission Test)
*   GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test)
*   XAT (Xavier Aptitude Test)
*   ATMA (AIMS Test for Management Admissions)

### Selection Rounds

Shortlisted candidates based on their entrance exam scores will undergo further evaluation, which typically includes:

*   **Group Discussion (GD):** To assess teamwork, communication, and problem-solving abilities.
*   **Essay Writing:** To evaluate written communication skills and clarity of thought.
*   **Personal Interview (PI):** To gauge personality, motivation, and suitability for the program.

Final selection is based on a cumulative assessment of entrance exam scores and performance in the GD, essay, and PI rounds.

## Cutoff Information

GIMS considers scores from various national-level management entrance exams for admission. While specific cutoff percentages can vary each year based on the applicant pool and exam difficulty, a strong score in any of the accepted exams (CAT, MAT, CMAT, GMAT, XAT, ATMA) is crucial for shortlisting.
