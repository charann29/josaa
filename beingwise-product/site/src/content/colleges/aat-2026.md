The Architecture Aptitude Test (AAT) is a crucial step for students aiming to pursue a Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch) degree at the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). This guide from BeingWise provides a clear overview of AAT 2026, covering courses, fees, the admission process, cutoffs, and placement opportunities.

## Understanding AAT 2026

AAT 2026 is specifically designed for candidates who have already qualified in JEE Advanced and wish to join the B.Arch program at an IIT. It's an additional aptitude test that assesses specific skills relevant to architecture.

### Eligibility for AAT 2026

To be eligible for AAT 2026, you *must* have appeared for and successfully qualified in JEE Advanced. Only candidates who meet the minimum passing marks in JEE Advanced will be able to register for AAT. There are no provisions for candidates who have not qualified JEE Advanced to apply for AAT.

### Key Dates for AAT 2026

Here are the important dates to keep in mind for AAT 2026:

| Event                       | Date(s)                 |
| :-------------------------- | :---------------------- |
| Online Application Form     | June 1 and 2, 2026      |
| AAT 2026 Examination Date   | June 4, 2026            |
| AAT 2026 Examination Time   | 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM     |

It's important to note the short window for application. Candidates should complete their registration as soon as the portal opens.

## AAT 2026 Application and Exam Process

### How to Apply

The AAT 2026 application form will be available online on the official JEE Advanced website, jeeadv.ac.in. You will need your JEE Advanced registration number and date of birth to log in and register. Please remember that only online applications are accepted; there is no offline application process.

### AAT 2026 Examination Details

The AAT is a written test conducted for three hours. The examination will be entirely in English and aims to evaluate your proficiency in several key areas:

*   **General Knowledge:** Awareness of current affairs and general facts.
*   **Geometry Drawing:** Ability to understand and represent geometric concepts.
*   **3D Aspects:** Comprehension of three-dimensional forms and spaces.
*   **Freehand Drawing:** Skill in sketching and visual representation without aids.
*   **Imagination:** Creativity and ability to conceptualize.
*   **Aesthetic Sensitivity:** Understanding and appreciation of beauty and design principles.

### What to Bring to the Exam Centre

When you report to the AAT exam centre, you must carry your JEE Advanced admit card. Additionally, you will need a valid photo identity proof. Without both these documents, you will not be allowed to take the examination. Make sure to review all guidelines carefully before the exam day.

### Exam Centre Information

Unlike JEE Advanced, there is no separate admit card for AAT. Instead, the authorities will communicate your allotted exam centre details directly. You can expect to receive an email on your registered ID with information regarding the centre address, date, specific guidelines, and any other crucial details.

## JoSAA Counselling for B.Arch Admissions

Admission to the B.Arch program at IITs, based on AAT results, is conducted through the JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) counselling process. JoSAA also manages admissions for BE/B.Tech courses across IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.

### JoSAA Counselling Steps

The JoSAA counselling process typically involves several online stages:

1.  **Registration:** Creating your profile on the JoSAA portal.
2.  **Choice Filling:** Selecting your preferred branches and colleges in order of priority.
3.  **Seat Allocation:** Seats are allotted based on your JEE Advanced rank (for IITs) or JEE Main rank (for NITs, IIITs, GFTIs) and your filled choices.
4.  **Online Reporting:** Accepting or floating the allotted seat and completing the necessary admission formalities.

### Filling Choices After Registration

After registering for JoSAA, you will need to carefully fill in your choices of academic programs and institutions. The system will display available options based on your qualifications. Once you've entered your choices, you can arrange them in your desired order of preference using the 'Arrange Filled Choices' option. This step is critical, as seat allocation heavily depends on your chosen preferences.

### Number of JoSAA Rounds

JoSAA counselling usually takes place over five to six rounds. These rounds facilitate admissions to 23 IITs, 31 NITs, IIEST Shibpur, 26 IIITs, and 40 other Government-Funded Technical Institutes (GFTIs). In each round, seats are allocated based on your rank and the preferences you submitted during registration. Following an allocation, candidates can either accept the seat or choose to 'float' if they wish to be considered for higher preferences in subsequent rounds.

## Courses, Fees, Cutoffs & Placements (General)

While specific details for AAT 2026 B.Arch courses, fees, and cutoffs will vary slightly by IIT, the general structure remains consistent. The B.Arch program is a five-year undergraduate degree. Fees typically include tuition, hostel, and other charges, which can range from INR 2 to 3 lakhs per year, subject to institutional policies and category. Cutoffs are determined by the performance in JEE Advanced and AAT, varying each year based on applicant pool and seat availability. IIT graduates, including B.Arch students, generally enjoy excellent placement opportunities in top architectural firms, design studios, urban planning agencies, and construction companies, both in India and abroad. Many also choose to pursue higher studies or entrepreneurship in related fields.
