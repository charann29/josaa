The University of Mysore, a distinguished institution in Karnataka, offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. Established with a rich legacy, it focuses on providing quality education across various disciplines. This guide covers essential information about its courses, fees, admission process, cutoffs, and placement opportunities.

## University of Mysore: Courses and Fees

The university provides diverse programs, with fees structured into components like tuition, caution deposits, and examination fees. Some charges are one-time, while others are annual or semester-based. Here's an overview of the approximate tuition fees for some popular courses:

*   **M.Tech:** ₹55,660 - ₹66,550
*   **MBA:** ₹8,059 - ₹1.16 Lakhs
*   **BCA:** ₹17,203
*   **M.Com:** ₹21,000 - ₹1.8 Lakhs
*   **M.Ed:** ₹21,000
*   **B.Sc:** ₹8,160 - ₹1.67 Lakhs
*   **M.Sc:** ₹4,818 - ₹2.38 Lakhs

*(Please note: These figures are for tuition only and can vary. It's always best to confirm the latest fee structure directly with the university.)*

## Admission Process and Eligibility

Admissions at the University of Mysore are based on a combination of merit and entrance examinations. For most postgraduate courses, the university conducts its own Post Graduate Entrance Examination (PGEE).

Additionally, the university accepts scores from various national and state-level entrance tests for specific programs. These may include:

*   **MAT**
*   **CAT**
*   **Karnataka PGCET**
*   **KCET**

After evaluating applications and entrance exam scores, the university releases a merit list to announce the names of shortlisted candidates.

## Cutoff Information

Specific cutoff details for various courses are typically released by the university after entrance exam results are declared. These cutoffs depend on factors such as the number of applicants, the difficulty of the entrance exam, and the availability of seats. Candidates are advised to check the official university website for the most up-to-date cutoff information for their desired programs.

## Placement Opportunities

The University of Mysore has a dedicated placement cell that works to connect students with potential employers. While specific data for individual courses like MBA might not always be separately published, overall placement statistics offer a good indication.

According to the NIRF report for 2024, the median package offered to students completing two-year postgraduate programs in 2023 was ₹3.75 Lakhs per annum. Here’s a look at the median package trends for two-year PG programs:

| Course        | Median Package (2021) | Median Package (2022) | Median Package (2023) |
| :------------ | :-------------------- | :-------------------- | :-------------------- |
| PG (2-year)   | ₹3.85 LPA             | ₹2.05 LPA             | ₹3.75 LPA             |

## Faculty and Learning Environment

The University of Mysore, as a centenarian institution, boasts a diverse and experienced academic community. The faculty members are generally regarded as highly qualified and supportive, with many reviews highlighting their excellent teaching quality and approachable nature. The teaching approach often combines traditional lectures with research-focused guidance, aiming to develop students' analytical, problem-solving, and professional skills. While the quality can vary slightly by department, the overall emphasis is on providing a strong theoretical foundation.

## Campus Facilities and Student Life

To ensure holistic student development, the university features a dedicated Directorate of Physical Education. This department oversees various sports and recreational activities. The campus is well-equipped with impressive sports facilities, including:

*   Three cricket grounds
*   Four tennis courts
*   A cinder track
*   Three basketball courts
*   Nalwadi Krishnaraja Wadiyar and Gangotri Glades stadiums
*   Three indoor stadiums
*   A large, well-equipped gymnasium
