The Delhi School of Economics (DSE), a well-known institution in Delhi, offers a range of postgraduate and undergraduate programs. This guide provides a clear overview of its courses, fees, admission process, and placement opportunities, helping you make an informed decision for your higher education journey.

## Programs Offered

DSE is primarily famous for its postgraduate programs, but it also provides undergraduate options. The main courses available include:

*   **Master of Arts (MA):** Specializations are available in various fields, with Economics being particularly prominent.
*   **Master of Business Administration (MBA):** This program offers specializations in Human Resource Development (HRD) and International Business (IB).
*   **Bachelor of Arts (BA):** While DSE is renowned for its postgraduate studies, it also offers a BA program.

## Admission Process

Admission to Delhi School of Economics follows the Common University Entrance Test (CUET) for both undergraduate and postgraduate programs, as mandated by the University of Delhi.

### For Undergraduate (UG) Courses (like BA):

1.  **CUET UG Examination:** Aspirants must appear for the CUET UG exam and select the University of Delhi as a preferred institution during the application process.
2.  **DU CSAS Portal Registration:** After CUET UG results are declared, candidates need to register on the University of Delhi's Common Seat Allocation System (CSAS) portal.
3.  **Preference Submission:** On the CSAS dashboard, submit your preferred course and college combinations, prioritizing DSE and your chosen BA program.
4.  **Seat Allocation:** The University releases multiple seat allocation lists based on merit and preferences.
5.  **Acceptance & Verification:** If provisionally allocated a seat, you must 'Accept' it. DSE will then verify your eligibility and uploaded documents online.
6.  **Fee Payment:** Upon successful verification and approval from DSE, pay the admission fee to confirm your seat.

### For Postgraduate (PG) Courses (like MA, MBA):

Admission to PG courses at DSE is based on the CUET PG examination. Similar to UG admissions, candidates will need to register on the DU CSAS portal (or equivalent PG admission portal) after CUET PG results and follow a similar seat allocation and verification process.

## Cutoff Information

DSE, like other colleges under the University of Delhi, releases cutoffs in the form of rank lists for admission. Candidates who meet or exceed the minimum cutoff ranks for their desired courses will be eligible for subsequent counseling rounds and seat allocation. These cutoffs are determined by factors such as the number of applicants, CUET scores, and seat availability.
