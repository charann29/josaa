Alliance University's Alliance Management Aptitude Test (AMAT) is a key entrance exam for those aspiring to pursue an MBA at the Alliance School of Business. This guide provides a clear overview of the AMAT 2026 exam, including course details, fees, admission process, cutoffs, and placement opportunities.

## Understanding the Alliance-AMAT 2026 Exam

AMAT is specifically designed for admission to MBA programs at Alliance University. It's a crucial step in the application journey, conducted multiple times a year.

### Exam Format and Structure

The Alliance AMAT is typically an offline, paper-based test, though it has also been conducted as a computer-based test at designated centers. The exam lasts for one hour. Here’s a breakdown:

*   **Duration:** 60 minutes
*   **Total Questions:** 56 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
*   **Marking Scheme:** Each correct answer earns 1 mark, while 0.25 marks are deducted for every incorrect answer.
*   **Sections:** The test generally covers Quantitative Aptitude, General Knowledge, General English, Comprehension, and a Conceptual Section.
*   **Language:** English

### Key Dates and Registration

Alliance AMAT is usually held twice a year to accommodate different admission cycles. For 2026, the application window for the January batch typically closes around January 31st, and for the July batch, it usually closes around April 15th. It's essential to check the official Alliance University website for the most current dates.

To register, candidates need to:

1.  Visit the official Alliance University website.
2.  Create a login ID and password.
3.  Complete the online application form with all required details.
4.  Upload necessary documents.
5.  Pay the application fee, which is INR 1,000 for MBA courses.

When attending the exam, remember to carry a coloured passport-size photograph, a valid photo ID, and a coloured copy of your Alliance AMAT admit card.

## Eligibility and Admission Process for MBA

To be considered for an MBA at Alliance University, candidates must meet specific academic and entrance exam criteria.

### Eligibility Criteria

*   **Academic Qualification:** A bachelor’s degree or an equivalent qualification with at least 50% aggregate marks (45% for SC/ST candidates).
*   **Final Year Students:** Those in the final year of their undergraduate studies can apply provisionally, provided they meet the aggregate marks requirement upon graduation.
*   **Entrance Exams:** In addition to AMAT, applicants must also have valid scores from other national-level management entrance exams such as CAT, MAT, XAT, NMAT, CMAT, KMAT, or GMAT. The university considers a combination of these scores.
*   **No Age Limit:** There is no upper age limit for appearing in the Alliance AMAT.

### Selection Rounds

The admission process is comprehensive and includes several stages:

1.  **Application Submission:** Online application and fee payment.
2.  **Entrance Exam Scores:** Submission of scores from AMAT and other accepted management entrance tests.
3.  **Oral Extempore Speech:** Candidates will participate in an extempore speech round.
4.  **Personal Interview (PI):** A personal interview to assess communication skills, aptitude, and overall fit.
5.  **Academic Performance Review:** The university also considers past academic records.

### Merit List and Counselling

Based on performance in the selection rounds, a merit list is published. Shortlisted candidates are then invited for a counselling session. During counselling, applicants need to pay the admission fee to confirm their seat in the program.
