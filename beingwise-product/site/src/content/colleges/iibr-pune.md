The Indian Institute of Business Research (IIBR) in Pune, part of the well-established ASM Group of Institutes, offers a strong foundation for postgraduate management studies. Approved by AICTE, it focuses on equipping students with practical skills and industry exposure.

## About IIBR Pune

Established in 1995 by the ASM Group in collaboration with MCCIA, IIBR Pune has been a significant player in management education. The ASM Group itself was founded in 1983 and oversees more than seven educational institutions, all dedicated to quality learning.

## Courses Offered

IIBR Pune primarily focuses on postgraduate management programs. The flagship offering is the Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM).

## Admission Process

Admission to IIBR Pune's PGDM program is a structured, multi-stage process designed to identify well-rounded candidates. Direct admissions are generally not offered.

### Eligibility Criteria

To be considered for admission, applicants must meet the following basic requirements:

*   Hold an Indian nationality.
*   Have a Bachelor's degree from a recognized university with at least 50% aggregate marks (45% for candidates from reserved categories).

### Selection Rounds

Once the eligibility criteria are met, candidates proceed through a comprehensive selection process:

1.  **Entrance Exam Scores:** Applicants must provide valid scores from any of the following national-level management entrance exams: CAT, XAT, CMAT, ATMA, MAT, or GMAT.
2.  **Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI):** Shortlisted candidates will participate in a Group Discussion, followed by a Personal Interview round.
3.  **Academic Performance:** Your scores from Class 10, Class 12, and graduation are considered.
4.  **Additional Factors:** Participation in sports, extracurricular activities, academic diversity, and gender diversity are also taken into account during the final selection.

## Fees Structure

Specific fee details are best confirmed directly with the institute's admissions office, as they can vary by program and academic year.
