The Indian Institute of Management Mumbai, formerly known as NITIE, is a leading institution in India's financial capital, offering comprehensive management education. Renowned for its focus on industrial management and supply chain, it provides a vibrant learning environment for aspiring business leaders.

## Courses Offered at IIM Mumbai

IIM Mumbai offers a range of postgraduate programs designed to equip students with essential management skills. The flagship program is the Post Graduate Diploma in Industrial Management (PGDIM), a two-year full-time residential course that emphasizes core business functions, strategic thinking, and practical application.

### Key Programs

*   **Post Graduate Diploma in Industrial Management (PGDIM):** This is the primary two-year program, covering a broad spectrum of management disciplines with a strong focus on industrial operations and supply chain. It's designed for fresh graduates and those with limited work experience.
*   **Post Graduate Diploma in Industrial Engineering (PGDIE):** Another significant two-year program, PGDIE focuses on engineering principles applied to industrial management, catering to candidates with an engineering background.
*   **Fellow Programme in Management (FPM):** A doctoral-level program for those interested in academic research and teaching.
*   **Executive Programs:** IIM Mumbai also offers various executive courses tailored for working professionals seeking to enhance their skills and career prospects.

## Fee Structure

The fees at IIM Mumbai cover tuition, hostel accommodation, and other academic expenses. It's important for prospective students to plan their finances accordingly. The fee structure is subject to periodic revisions by the institute.

For the PGDIM and PGDIE programs, the total course fee is approximately around INR 14.50 Lakhs. This figure is an estimate and can change.

## Admission Process

Admission to IIM Mumbai's flagship PGDIM and PGDIE programs is highly competitive and primarily based on the Common Admission Test (CAT) scores, followed by a rigorous selection process.

### Steps for Indian Candidates

1.  **CAT Examination:** Candidates must appear for the CAT exam, which is a mandatory entrance test for admission to IIMs.
2.  **Shortlisting:** Based on valid CAT scores and meeting minimum eligibility criteria (such as academic percentages), candidates are shortlisted for the next stages.
3.  **Group Discussion (GD) & Personal Interview (PI):** Shortlisted candidates participate in a Group Discussion and a Personal Interview. These rounds assess communication skills, leadership potential, general awareness, and suitability for the program.
4.  **Final Selection:** The final admission offer is made based on a composite score that considers CAT score, performance in GD/PI, academic record, and work experience (if any).

### Steps for International Candidates

International applicants are typically considered based on their GMAT scores. They also need to provide IELTS/TOEFL scores to demonstrate English proficiency and must have a minimum of 60% aggregate marks in their qualifying degree. Shortlisted international candidates also undergo a Group Discussion and Personal Interview.

## CAT Cutoff

IIM Mumbai establishes both a qualifying CAT cutoff and a final CAT cutoff for admissions. The qualifying cutoff is the minimum percentile required to be considered for the GD/PI rounds, while the final cutoff determines admission offers.

For General category candidates, the qualifying CAT cutoff for IIM Mumbai admissions is generally at or above the 85 percentile overall, with sectional cutoffs typically between 75 and 80 percentile. These cutoffs vary for different categories:

## Placements

IIM Mumbai boasts a strong placement record, attracting top companies from various sectors. The institute consistently achieves good placement statistics, reflecting the quality of its programs and graduates.

### Placement Highlights

*   **Median Package:** The median package offered during placements has shown a positive trend. For example, in 2024, the median package for Pre-Placement Offers (PPOs) was INR 28 LPA. In 2022, the median package for PGP placements was INR 22 LPA.
*   **Recruiting Sectors:** A diverse range of sectors recruit from IIM Mumbai. In recent placements, the Manufacturing sector emerged as a leading recruiter, employing 19% of the students. Other prominent sectors include Consulting, FMCG, IT/Product Management, and BFSI.
