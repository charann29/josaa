Tecnia Institute of Advanced Studies (TIAS) in Delhi is a well-regarded institution offering a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. It's a popular choice for students aiming for careers in business, IT, and media. This guide covers everything you need to know about its courses, fees, admission process, and career opportunities.

## Programs Offered at Tecnia Institute of Advanced Studies

TIAS provides a variety of courses approved by AICTE, catering to different academic interests and career aspirations. These programs are designed to equip students with practical skills and theoretical knowledge.

### Undergraduate Programs

*   **Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA):** A three-year program focusing on core business principles, management, and entrepreneurial skills.
*   **Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA):** A three-year course for students interested in software development, computer science, and IT applications.
*   **Bachelor of Journalism and Mass Communication (BJMC):** This three-year program covers various aspects of media, journalism, and communication studies.

### Postgraduate Programs

*   **Master of Business Administration (MBA)/Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM):** A two-year program offering advanced management education with specializations.
*   **Master of Computer Applications (MCA):** A two-year program for further specialization in computer applications and advanced IT concepts.

Across all programs, the total student intake capacity at Tecnia Institute of Advanced Studies is up to 1020 seats.

## Tecnia Institute of Advanced Studies Fee Structure

The fee range for courses at Tecnia Institute of Advanced Studies typically falls between INR 2,01,000 and INR 3,35,100. It's always advisable to check the official TIAS website or contact their admissions office for the most current and specific fee details for your chosen program.

## Admission Process and Eligibility

Admission to Tecnia Institute of Advanced Studies is based on specific eligibility criteria for each program. Generally, for undergraduate courses, candidates need to have completed their 10+2 education from a recognized board. For postgraduate programs like MBA/PGDM and MCA, a bachelor's degree from a recognized university is usually required.

The selection process often involves reviewing academic qualifications and, for some programs, may include entrance exam scores or an interview. Prospective students should carefully review the detailed admission guidelines available on the TIAS website and ensure all necessary documents are prepared for application.
