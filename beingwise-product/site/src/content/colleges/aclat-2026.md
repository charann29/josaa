ACLAT 2026 is an essential entrance exam for those aspiring to pursue a Master of Laws (LL.M.) at Alliance University. This guide provides a clear overview of the program, admission process, fees, and career prospects to help you make an informed decision.

## Understanding ACLAT 2026

The Alliance Common Law Admission Test (ACLAT) is specifically designed for admission to the LL.M. program offered by Alliance University. It's important to note that this exam is exclusively for postgraduate law studies; undergraduate law admissions (LL.B.) at Alliance University are handled through the AUSAT exam.

### Key Facts about ACLAT:

*   **Purpose:** Exclusively for LL.M. admissions at Alliance University.
*   **Conducted By:** Alliance University.
*   **Mode:** Online (computer-based test).
*   **Frequency:** Generally conducted annually.

## Eligibility Criteria for LL.M. through ACLAT

To be eligible for the LL.M. program via ACLAT, candidates must meet specific academic requirements:

*   **Educational Qualification:** A Bachelor of Law degree (LL.B.) from a university recognized by the Bar Council of India.
*   **Minimum Marks:** A minimum aggregate score of 45% in their undergraduate law degree. For SC/ST candidates, this requirement is typically 40%.
*   **Age Limit:** There is no upper age limit for applying to the LL.M. program through ACLAT, provided other eligibility criteria are met.

## ACLAT 2026 Admission Process

The admission journey through ACLAT involves several steps:

1.  **Application:** Candidates must complete and submit the online application form for ACLAT. It's crucial to fill in all details accurately as there is no provision for correction windows after submission. If an error occurs, candidates should immediately contact the Alliance University Admission Office.
2.  **Entrance Exam (ACLAT):** Eligible candidates will appear for the online ACLAT exam.
3.  **Personal Interview (PI):** Shortlisted candidates based on their ACLAT scores will be invited for a Personal Interview.
4.  **Final Merit List:** A final merit list for admission is prepared considering both ACLAT scores and performance in the Personal Interview.

## ACLAT 2026 Exam Pattern

The ACLAT exam is a computer-based test consisting of multiple-choice questions. Here's a breakdown of the sections and their weightage:

| Section                          | Number of Questions |
| :------------------------------- | :------------------ |
| Language Proficiency             | 40                  |
| General Awareness                | 50                  |
| Logical Reasoning & Analytical Ability | 45                  |
| Numerical Ability                | 25                  |
| Legal Aptitude                   | 45                  |

*   **Total Questions:** 205
*   **Marking Scheme:** Each correct answer typically fetches 1 mark. There is **negative marking**, with 0.25 marks deducted for each incorrect answer. Candidates can mark questions for review and revisit them before submitting the exam.

## LL.M. Courses Offered and Fees

Alliance University offers a comprehensive Master of Laws (LL.M.) program. While specific specializations might vary, the program generally aims to deepen understanding in various legal domains. For the most accurate and current fee structure for the LL.M. program, it is always best to refer to the official Alliance University admissions brochure or website, as fees can be subject to revision.

## Cutoff and Placements

### ACLAT Cutoff

The cutoff scores for ACLAT vary each year based on factors such as the number of applicants, the difficulty of the exam, and the availability of seats. Candidates need to secure marks equal to or above the declared cutoff to be considered for the Personal Interview round and subsequent admission.

### Placements

Alliance University has a dedicated career advancement and networking cell that assists LL.M. graduates with placement opportunities. While specific placement statistics for the LL.M. program are best obtained directly from the university, law graduates typically find roles in:

*   Law firms
*   Corporate legal departments
*   Judiciary services
*   Government agencies
*   Academia and research
*   Non-governmental organizations (NGOs)

The university strives to connect students with reputable employers, facilitating internships and final placements to launch their legal careers effectively.
