O.P. Jindal Global University (JGU), located in Sonepat, is a well-regarded private institution known for its diverse academic programs. This page provides a clear overview of its courses, fee structure, admission process, cutoffs, and placement opportunities to help you make an informed decision.

## Why Choose O.P. Jindal Global University?

JGU stands out as a premier private university, especially for students eyeing careers in Management and Law. The University Grants Commission (UGC) and the Government of India have recognized it as an 'Institution of Eminence,' a testament to its academic quality. International rankings, such as those by QS World University Rankings, consistently place JGU as India's top private university in fields like Management, Legal Studies, and Arts & Humanities.

## Courses and Fees

JGU offers a wide array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across various disciplines. While specific fees vary by course, the university is committed to providing quality education. Some of the prominent schools within JGU include the Jindal School of Banking and Finance and the Jindal Global Business School.

### Jindal Global Business School (JGBS) - MBA/PGDM

The MBA/PGDM program at JGBS has a substantial intake, offering 220 seats. This ample capacity allows a diverse group of students to benefit from the institution's resources and quality education.

## Admission Process and Eligibility

Admission to O.P. Jindal Global University is comprehensive and involves several stages, including academic performance, entrance exam scores, and interviews. It's important to note that admission to any program at JGU requires a valid entrance exam score; direct admissions without an exam are not possible.

### Undergraduate Admissions (e.g., Jindal School of Banking and Finance)

For bachelor's programs, candidates generally need to have successfully completed their Class XII examination or an equivalent from a recognized board (CBSE, ISC, IB, Cambridge, etc.). A holistic admission policy considers:

*   Scores from 10th and 12th grade.
*   Performance in an online personal interview.
*   Entrance Exam Scores from one of the following:
    *   Jindal Scholastic Aptitude Test (JSAT): Minimum 50% overall.
    *   UGAT/CUET: 60 percentile or higher.
    *   ACT: 27 points.
    *   SAT: 1100 and above.

### Postgraduate Admissions (e.g., MBA/PGDM, Master in Finance)

For master's programs, candidates typically need an undergraduate degree in any field with a minimum of 50% marks. For some finance-related programs, a background in mathematics (in high school or an undergraduate degree) might be preferred. Final-year graduation students are also eligible to apply.

Entrance exam requirements for postgraduate programs include:

*   **For MBA/PGDM:**
    *   CAT/MAT/XAT/CMAT/ATMA: 50 percentile or higher.
    *   GRE: 295 or higher.
    *   GMAT: 450 or higher.
    *   NMAT: 160 or higher.
*   **For Master in Finance:**
    *   Master in Finance Aptitude Test (MFAT) conducted by the Jindal School of Banking & Finance.
    *   Alternatively, scores from CAT/MAT/XAT/CMAT/ATMA (50+ percentile), GRE (295+), GMAT (450+), or NMAT (160+).

## Placements

JGU is dedicated to supporting its students in their career journeys. While official, comprehensive placement statistics are often updated, insights from recent years provide a picture of opportunities.

For the MBA Public Policy program in 2024, the highest package reported was INR 11 LPA. Student reviews have also indicated a highest package of INR 21 LPA across various programs in recent placements, with the lowest package being around INR 3 LPA. The average package over the past few years has generally ranged between INR 6 LPA and INR 7 LPA.

It's important to note that official, detailed statistics for MBA placements are not always released publicly, but the university strives to connect students with reputable employers across various sectors.
