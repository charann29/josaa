## I Business Institute, Greater Noida: An Overview

I Business Institute, located in Greater Noida, focuses on delivering a comprehensive management education. The institute aims to blend theoretical knowledge with practical application, preparing students for the demands of the modern business world. Their flagship offering is the PGDM program.

## Programs and Fees

IBI primarily offers a Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM). This program is structured to provide a holistic understanding of business functions and management principles. Specific fee details are typically available directly from the institute or on their official website, as these can change annually. It's always best to confirm the latest fee structure directly with the admissions office.

## Admission Process

Admission to the PGDM program at IBI is generally based on a candidate's academic record, performance in entrance exams, and subsequent interview rounds. Here's a typical outline of the process:

*   **Eligibility:** Applicants usually need a bachelor's degree from a recognized university with a minimum aggregate score (often around 50%).
*   **Entrance Exams:** Candidates are expected to have appeared for national-level management entrance exams such as CAT, XAT, CMAT, MAT, GMAT, or ATMA.
*   **Application:** Submit an online application form through the institute's official portal.
*   **Selection Rounds:** Shortlisted candidates will typically undergo a Group Discussion (GD) and a Personal Interview (PI) round.
*   **Final Admission:** Admission is granted based on a composite score considering academic performance, entrance exam scores, and GD/PI results.

## Cutoffs

While specific cutoff percentages for entrance exams can vary each year depending on the applicant pool and exam difficulty, IBI considers scores from major management entrance tests. Generally, a competitive score in exams like CAT, XAT, CMAT, or MAT is essential for consideration. It's advisable to aim for a strong percentile to enhance your chances of admission.
