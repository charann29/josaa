The Panjab University Management Entrance Test (PU MET) is a key examination for students aspiring to pursue management programs at Panjab University. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the courses, fees, admission process, expected cutoffs, and placement opportunities, helping you make informed decisions for your academic journey.

## PU MET 2026: Key Dates and Overview

For those planning to take the PU MET in 2026, mark your calendars. The examination is scheduled for **April 12, 2026**. It will be a paper-based test (PBT) conducted at various designated examination centers. This means you'll need to physically attend a center and mark your answers on an OMR sheet. The application window for PU MET 2026 opened on **December 3, 2025**, and the final date to register was **April 5, 2026**.

### Application Process

Applying for PU MET involves filling out the application form, which is typically available on the official Panjab University website. Candidates can usually complete this process either online or offline, depending on the specific instructions provided for the year. Ensure you complete your application well before the deadline to avoid any last-minute issues.

## Understanding the PU MET Exam Pattern

The PU MET is designed to assess a candidate's aptitude for management studies. Here’s a breakdown of what to expect:

*   **Mode:** Paper-Based Test (PBT)
*   **Duration:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
*   **Sections:** 6 distinct sections
*   **Total Questions:** 200 multiple-choice questions (MCQs)
*   **Questions per Section:** Each of the six sections contains an equal number of MCQs.
*   **Marking Scheme:** Each question carries one mark. There is **no negative marking** for incorrect answers.
*   **Total Marks:** 200 marks

The difficulty level of the PU MET is generally considered to be easy to moderate, making it accessible for well-prepared candidates.

## Admission Process and Cutoffs

Admission to management programs through PU MET is a multi-stage process:

1.  **PU MET Examination:** Candidates first appear for the entrance test.
2.  **Merit List:** Based on PU MET scores, a merit list is prepared.
3.  **Group Discussion (GD) & Personal Interview (PI):** Shortlisted candidates are then invited for a Group Discussion and Personal Interview. These rounds are crucial for assessing communication skills, leadership potential, and overall suitability for a management program.
4.  **Final Selection:** The final selection is based on a composite score, usually combining PU MET scores, GD, PI performance, and academic record.

**Cutoffs:** The cutoff scores for admission can fluctuate each year depending on factors like the number of applicants, the difficulty of the exam, and the available seats. While specific cutoffs are not released in advance, candidates should aim for a strong performance in the PU MET to maximize their chances of being shortlisted for subsequent rounds.

Panjab University has a dedicated placement cell that facilitates career opportunities for its management graduates. The university maintains strong ties with various industries, leading to decent placement records. Graduates find opportunities in sectors such as:

*   Finance

Reputed national and multinational companies participate in campus recruitment drives. While specific salary packages vary based on individual skills, experience, and market conditions, graduates generally secure roles with competitive compensation packages, reflecting the quality of education and training received at Panjab University.
