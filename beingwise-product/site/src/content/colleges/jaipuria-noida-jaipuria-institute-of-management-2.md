Jaipuria Institute of Management in Noida is a well-regarded institution for postgraduate management education. This page offers a clear overview of its programs, fee structure, admission requirements, cutoff scores, and placement statistics to help you make an informed decision.

## Programs Offered

Jaipuria Noida primarily focuses on postgraduate management programs, including its flagship PGDM (Post Graduate Diploma in Management) and a Fellowship Program in Management (FPM).

### PGDM Program

The PGDM is a comprehensive management program designed to equip students with essential business skills and knowledge. It is equivalent to an MBA and is a popular choice for those looking to advance their careers in various sectors.

### Fellowship Program in Management (FPM)

The FPM is a doctoral-level program for individuals aspiring to careers in research and academia. It offers an in-depth opportunity to contribute to management thought and practice through rigorous study and research.

## Admission Process

Admission to Jaipuria Noida's programs is competitive and based on a combination of entrance exam scores, academic performance, and personal interviews.

### PGDM Admission

To be considered for the PGDM program, candidates must have a bachelor's degree with at least 50% aggregate marks. The selection process involves:

*   **Entrance Exam Scores:** Valid scores from national-level management entrance exams such as CAT, MAT, XAT, CMAT, or GMAT are mandatory.
*   **Personal Interview (PI):** Shortlisted candidates will undergo a Personal Interview to assess their communication skills, general awareness, and suitability for the program.

There is no provision for direct admission to the PGDM program; entrance exam scores are compulsory.

### FPM Admission

For the FPM program, candidates need to apply through the institute's website with a prescribed fee. Key components of the FPM application and selection include:

*   **Application Submission:** Complete the application form and pay the INR 1050 application fee.
*   **Research Proposal:** Submit a research proposal of 500-1000 words outlining your area of interest.
*   **Selection Criteria:** The selection process considers several factors with assigned weightages:
    *   Written Test: 25%
    *   Work Experience: 15%
    *   Academic Achievements & Prior Research Experience: 20%
    *   Research Proposal & Interview: 40%

The number of scholars admitted each year depends on the institute's available resources, with selections typically made once annually.
