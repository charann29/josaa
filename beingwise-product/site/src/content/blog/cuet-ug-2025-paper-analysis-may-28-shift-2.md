## CUET UG 2025 GAT Paper Analysis

Students found the GK & Current Affairs paper well-balanced and manageable, with around 11-12 history questions focusing on battles like Chausa and Haldighati, and several questions from Books & Authors such as *Unhappy India*, *Half a Life*, and *Wings of Fire*. However, many questions have appeared in match-the-column format, which students recognized from previous shifts. However, questions on probability, HCF-LCM, and mensuration were simpler than before, while reasoning questions focused on non-verbal topics like mirror images and direction sense. Overall, students from both Arts and Science streams found the paper familiar, not too difficult, and fairly distributed across sections.

## CUET UG 2025 Physics Paper Analysis

Students found the Physics paper generally easy to moderate, with a clear emphasis on numerical questions that were mostly formula-based or tricky but familiar. Many felt the paper was a bit lengthy but manageable, attempting around 45-46+ questions. Topics like AC Circuits, Electrostatic Potential & Capacitance, Moving Charge & Magnetism, and Nuclear Physics had good weightage, while Magnetism & Matter was noticeably absent, making students expect it in future exams. Moreover, Ray Optics had limited questions, and some unexpected topics like the Meter Bridge and the Potentiometer appeared. Overall, students found this shift easier than the previous day’s tougher paper, appreciating the focus on calculations and the introduction of the first graph-based question in CUET Physics.

## CUET UG 2025 English Paper Analysis

## Student Reviews For CUET UG 2025 May 28th Shift 2 Exam

Most students from different states found CUET UG 2025 Shift 2, which was held on May 28th, was easy to moderate. Here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

**General Aptitude Test (GAT):** Most students felt the GAT section was moderately easy to moderate. The language comprehension questions were clear, but some found the quantitative aptitude questions a bit time-consuming, requiring careful calculation and speed.

**Physics:** Students at the centre seemed somewhat troubled by the Physics paper. However, some students later reported that the paper was easier compared to other days. Some questions were outside the explicitly mentioned syllabus. Many students mentioned that questions were conceptually driven, especially from topics like Thermodynamics and Modern Physics, with a mix of numerical problems that tested application skills.

**Chemistry:** Chemistry was challenging for many aspirants. The paper had a strong focus on problem-solving, particularly in physical chemistry, and required solid understanding of concepts. Some students noted tricky numerical questions that demanded extra effort.

**English:** The English section was generally rated as easy to moderate. Vocabulary and grammar questions were straightforward, but a few comprehension passages were lengthy, requiring good reading speed and focus.

## CUET UG 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

Given below are a few CUET UG 2025 exam day guidelines that students need to follow.

- **Carry Only Required Items:** Only take your printed admit card (with passport-size photograph), your valid photo ID (Aadhaar or PAN card), a transparent ballpoint pen, and a transparent water bottle. Candidates can check all the CUET exam details on the admit card and are advised to adhere to the instructions.
- **Be On Time:** Report to the exam center well in advance relative to your reporting time (7:00 AM for Shift 1 and 1:00 PM for Shift 2). If you are late, you will not be allowed to enter.
- **Prohibited Items:** Do not bring mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, bags, books, or food if not medically required (with documentation).
- **Maintain Discipline:** Be seated in your assigned seat, only do rough work on the sheets provided, and do not perform any unfair practice or make any noise in the exam hall.
- **Time Management:** It is a critical challenge across subjects, especially with lengthy sections like RC or Quant. It’s advised to tackle easy questions first.
- **The Normalisation Factor:** This factor will account for variations in difficulty across shifts. The focus should be on attempting as many questions with 100% accuracy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ********Is there any question outside the syllabus in the CUET UG 2025 Physics paper?********

In the Physics paper, some questions were outside the explicitly mentioned syllabus, such as the Meter Bridge and the Potentiometer. The paper maintained the usual weightage for Nuclear Physics and Modern Physics.

### **2. **********************Is there any repetition in the 2nd shift of the 28th May CUET UG 2025 English paper?************************

Yes, passages in the Reading Comprehension section were repeated from the previous set. The paper had 3+ passages with approx. 12 questions total were there, along with the 4-5 indirect questions.

### **3. ****How did students perceive the overall balance between theory and application questions in the second shift of 28th May CUET UG 2025 Physics and Chemistry paper?******

In the Physics paper, the emphasis is more on numericals, mostly formula-based, or tricky but familiar. However, in Chemistry, there were more theory-based questions than numericals ones. It was a mix of conceptual problems like SN2 rate, coupling reaction, and aniline strength, and application-based questions like T50 & T100, alum formula.

### 4. **********What were some of the synonyms, antonyms, and homophones that have appeared in the CUET UG 2025 English paper?**********

### 5. ****What is the CUET exam syllabus?****

CUET exam syllabus has a language subject which has various types of passages, such as factual, literary, and narrative, etc., 5 domain subjects, and a section of GAT which assesses General Knowledge, Current Affairs, General Mental Ability, Numerical Ability, etc.
