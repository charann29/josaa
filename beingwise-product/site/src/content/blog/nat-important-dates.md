## What is NAT Exam?

NAT stands for NxtWave Assessment Test. It is an assessment conducted by NxtWave as part of the NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) upskilling program process.

However, it is not an entrance examination conducted by the university. The collaborating Universities with NIAT independently conduct their own entrance examinations for admission into their degree programs. NAT does not guarantee admission into any collaborating university. NxtWave uses it as part of its own internal process

## NAT Application Process 2026

For enrollment in the NIAT upskilling program with specialisations in AI, ML, and DS, students need to qualify for the Nxtwave Assessment Test (NAT). The [NAT application process](/blog/engineering-colleges/how-to-register-for-nat-exam-complete-process/) is through the official website of NIAT. Students need to pay INR 1200 as the application fee to apply for the NIAT enrollment 2026. Check the steps given below to know the NAT application process.

**Step 1:** Visit the official website of NIAT: <https://www.niatindia.com/>

**Step 2:** Click on “Apply Now” in the top right corner of the homepage. The students can also go to the “Admissions Counselling” tab and click on “[Apply for Counselling](https://apply.niatindia.com/login?utm_source=CollegeDost-SEO-Blogs&utm_medium=nat-important-dates-2025&utm_campaign=CollegeDost)”.

**Step 3:** Give your mobile number and verify it with an OTP to proceed with the registration process.

**Step 4:** The application form is available for both the Parents and the Students.

**Step 5:** Carefully fill out the form and make sure the information given is correct and without any errors.

**Step 6:** Students can proceed to book the exam slot.

**Step 7:** Students can opt whether they want to appear for the test “ Right Now” or “Later”. If later, then they can select the date and time of the exam.

**Step 8:** Next, pay the non-refundable application fee of INR 1200 through UPI modes and NetBanking through ICICI and Axis Bank. You can also use your UPI ID and online wallets to make the payment.

**Step 9:** Click on “Continue to Pay” to book your Nxtwave Assessment Test slot.

## NAT Test Pattern

The Nxtwave Assessment Test (NAT) is a 90-minute online assessment test for enrollment into the NIAT upskilling program. Students need to have a 10+2 level to be eligible for NAT. The test focuses on the candidate’s cognitive abilities and potential as a future tech innovator. It consists of 3 sections that assess different skills and competencies. The evaluation covers personality and emotional traits, analytical and adaptive skills, and practical problem-solving abilities.

Check the format of the NAT in the table below, including three sections, the number of questions, and the time limit allotted for each section.

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Test Format** | **Number of Questions** | **Time Limit** | **Focus Area** |
| Psychometric Test | 44 | 25 minutes | Personality and emotional traits |
| Critical Thinking | 20 | 30 minutes | Analytical and adaptive skills |
| Mathematics | 17 | 35 minutes | Practical and problem-solving |

## Frequently Asked Questions

#### 1. What is the NAT application deadline?

Important dates 2026 for the NAT application process are going to close soon. Students are recommended to visit the official website and register as soon as possible.

#### 2. What are the eligibility criteria to appear for the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT)?

Students need to have 10+2 level with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as the core subjects to be eligible for NAT 2026.

#### 3. What is the NAT test pattern?

NAT 2026 is a 90-minute-long assessment test for enrollment into NIAT’s upskilling program. The test has 3 sections that assess different skills and competencies. The evaluation covers personality and emotional traits, analytical and adaptive skills, and practical problem-solving abilities.

#### 4. What is the NAT registration fee?

The NAT registration fee is INR 1200. This is a one-time non-refundable fee, which won’t be returned even if the student does not appear for the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT).

#### 5. How much does a student need to pay for the BTech course at the collaborating university of NIAT?

A student needs to pay INR 2 Lakhs to INR 4.5 Lakhs as the first-year fee for the BTech course at the universities where NIAT’s upskilling program is offered. However, the total fee for the course is INR 8 Lakhs to 18 Lakhs.

#### 6. Can a student choose their preferred date and time for NAT?

Ies. During the registration process, students have the option to either appear for the test immediately or schedule it for a later date and time of their choice, based on available slots on the official NIAT website.

#### 7. Can parents also fill out the NAT application form on behalf of the student?

Yes. The NAT application form is available for both students and parents. Parents filling out the form are required to provide the student’s details along with their own information, including name, phone number, family income, and occupation category.

#### 8. Does NIAT decide which university a student gets admitted to?

No. NIAT does not decide university admission. Based on the student’s NAT performance and counselling session outcome, NxtWave shares the student’s details with a suitable collaborating university, strictly with the student’s written consent. The university independently evaluates the student and grants admission based on its own policies.

#### 9. What is the total number of questions in NAT?

NAT consists of a total of 81 questions across three sections — 44 questions in the Psychometric Test, 20 questions in the Critical Thinking section, and 17 questions in the Mathematics section.

#### 10. What is the NAT application deadline?

Important dates 2026 for the NAT application process are going to close soon. Students are recommended to visit the official website and register as soon as possible.

#### 11. Do I need to give NAT mock test?

It depends on you and your preparation. Giving a mock test always helps you in getting prepared. You can also practice the **[CollegeDost NAT mock test](/mock-test/nat)**. However, getting your basics clear will also help you in qualifying NAT.
