## Subject-wise JEE Mains 2026 April 4 Shift 2 Paper Analysis

Here’s a comprehensive subject-wise JEE Mains Paper Analysis April 4th shift 2 to give you a clear idea of the marking pattern and difficulty level of the questions.

### JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

The Chemistry section of JEE Mains 2026 April 4 Shift 2 Paper was easy. Students completed this section quickly, often in 35 to 45 minutes, with many attempting 20+ questions.

 This section was mostly NCERT-based, especially the Inorganic Chemistry segment. Inorganic Chemistry was the easiest part, while the Physical Chemistry section comprised basic calculations. Check the Key topics where questions are asked in this shift below.

- Organic Chemistry (GOC, Reaction Mechanism)
- Inorganic Chemistry (p-block, d-block)
- Physical Chemistry (Electrochemistry, Chemical Kinetics)
- Biomolecules
- Salt Analysis

### JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Physics Section

The Physics section of the JEE Main 2026 April 4 Shift 1 Question Paper was formula-based, and the calculative and difficulty Level was Easy to Moderate. Students also added that they were able to attempt 20 to 22 questions within 40–50 minutes. Take a look at the Key topics where questions are asked in this shift

- Units and dimensions.
- Kinematics
- Mechanical Properties of Solid.
- Ray Optics.
- Electromagnetism
- Modern Physics
- Semiconductor

### JEE Main Paper Analysis for Maths Section

In the JEE Main Analysis, the Mathematics part was Lengthy and Tricky, and the Difficulty Level was Moderate, but extremely long. Although the mathematics questions were manageable, the average time spent on each problem was long (approximately 5-6 minutes), limiting the number of questions pupils could try. Students could only answer an average of 8-12 questions. Key themes where questions are asked during this shift

- Coordinate Geometry
- Conic Section
- Vectors & 3D Geometry
- Calculus (Limits, Definite Integration)
- Matrices & Determinants
- Sequence & Series
- Algebra: Binomial Theorem, Sequence & Series, and P&C

## JEE Mains 2026 April 4 Shift 2 Paper Analysis by Students

After the exam was over, we talked to the students coming out of the exam center. We have received a mixed reaction from them; however, most of the students said the JEE Main 2026 April 4 Shift 1 Question Paper was easy to moderate in difficulty level.

- **Rohan Mishra** tried around 65 questions, which was substantially higher than his January attempt. He considered Chemistry to be the game-changer due to its simplicity. However, he found Mathematics to be too difficult, calling it “Bhayankar”. Considering the simplicity of the paper, Rohan feels that even 99th percentile students can score up to 220 marks.
- NEET aspirant **Ankit Rai** found that the Physics paper was simpler than the NEET standard. He tried 21 questions in Physics and 12 in Chemistry. But Mathematics appeared too difficult, as he tried only seven questions in Mathematics.
- **Priya Gupta** made substantial progress, attempting 60 questions as compared to 42 questions in the previous attempt. She found that Physics was quite simple and direct, including even such lesser-known areas like Fluid Mechanics, which was a scoring area.
- Partial dropper **Sneha Das** scored 84% in Class 12 and 97.5th percentile in the January attempt. Consistent practice and familiarity with the exam pattern can really make a lot of difference for a relatively simpler shift like this one.

The NTA Score or the JEE Main Percentile Score is the process through which the National Testing Agency evaluates the scores of all examinees who appeared in different exam sessions. Candidates’ rankings are determined not on the basis of the actual scores but on the basis of their percentiles.
