## WBJEE Marking Scheme 2026

WBJEE follows a **category-based marking system**. Each category follows a different marking rule for correct and incorrect answers. Candidates should be aware of the negative marking rules while attempting the questions to avoid unnecessary loss of marks. Let’s have a look:

| **Category** | **Correct Answer** | **Incorrect Answer** | **Unattempted** | **Special Case** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Category I | +1 mark | -0.25 mark | 0 | If a question has more than one option marked, even if one of them is correct, the candidate will lose ¼ mark. |
| Category II | +2 marks | −0.5 mark | 0 | If a candidate marks more than one option, even if one of them is correct, ½ mark will be deducted. |
| Category III | +2 marks | No negative marking | 0 | If a candidate selects some of the correct options but not all, marks will be awarded proportionally. The marks are calculated as 2 × (number of correct options marked ÷ total number of correct options). There is no negative marking for these questions. |

## WBJEE OMR Sheet Instructions

WBJEE is conducted as an **OMR-based test**, and candidates must carefully follow the instructions while marking answers.

- Answers must be marked on the **OMR sheet** provided in the exam hall.
- Candidates should **darken the appropriate circle completely** using a **blue or black ballpoint pen**.
- Tick marks, cross marks, or incomplete circles may lead to incorrect reading of answers.
- Responses **cannot be changed once marked on the OMR sheet**.

Practising with **sample OMR sheets** before the exam can help improve speed and accuracy.

## WBJEE Preparation Tips

Preparing for WBJEE 2026 requires a strategic approach that focuses on conceptual clarity, consistent practice, and effective time management. Candidates should first understand the syllabus and exam pattern so that they can plan their preparation efficiently. - **Create a study schedule:** Prepare a realistic timetable that covers all subjects and allows enough time for revision and practice.

- **Attempt mock tests regularly:** Taking full-length mock tests helps candidates evaluate their preparation level and manage time effectively during the exam.

- **Revise regularly:** Preparing short notes and revising important formulas, reactions, and concepts frequently can help retain information better.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How many papers are there in the WBJEE exam?

WBJEE consists of two papers conducted on the same day. Paper I includes Mathematics, while Paper II includes Physics and Chemistry. Candidates who want admission to engineering courses must appear for both papers.

### 2. How many questions are asked in WBJEE 2026?

The WBJEE exam includes a total of 155 multiple-choice questions. Out of these, 75 questions are from Mathematics, while Physics and Chemistry contain 40 questions each. All questions are objective type and must be answered on an OMR sheet.

### 3. Which subject has the highest weightage in WBJEE?

Among all subjects, Mathematics carries the highest weightage in the WBJEE exam. It includes 75 questions with a total of 100 marks, making it the most important section for scoring well. Physics and Chemistry each contribute 50 marks.

### 4. What subjects are included in the WBJEE syllabus?

The WBJEE syllabus mainly covers Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry topics from Class 11 and Class 12. Most concepts are based on the NCERT curriculum, along with some application-based problems. Candidates should thoroughly revise these subjects for better performance.

### 5. Is there negative marking in WBJEE 2026?

Yes, negative marking is applied in WBJEE, but it depends on the question category. Category I questions deduct 0.25 marks, while Category II questions deduct 0.5 marks for incorrect answers. However, Category III questions do not have negative marking.

### 6. What is the duration of the WBJEE exam?

The total duration of the WBJEE exam is 4 hours. The exam is divided into two sessions of 2 hours each, one for Mathematics and the other for Physics and Chemistry. Candidates must complete each paper within the allotted time.
