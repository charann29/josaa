The National Testing Agency has released the CUET UG exam city intimation slip on May 7th on its official website, [cuet.nta.nic.in](http://cuet.nta.nic.in). Candidates can check the CUET 2025 exam slip by logging in to the official website. The city intimation slip contains the date of examination, slot, allotted exam city, state, personal information, applied subjects, and medium of the examination. In this article, we have discussed how to download the city intimation slip, exam pattern, and participating universities. Check the details below:

The CUET UG exam is initially announced to be conducted from May 8th onwards. However, the NTA has announced that the exam will commence on May 13th. With 13 lakh students appearing every year, the CUET UG is India’s second-largest examination. Students are advised to follow the official website to stay updated regarding the exam details.

## Guide to Download the CUET UG City Intimation Slip

The CUET UG city intimation slip released by the NTA allows the students to know their allotted exam city, shift, and date of the examination in advance. This can help students plan their travel accordingly so as to reach the examination centre on time. Candidates have to note that the City intimation slip is not the admit card, but a document released before the admit card is released. Follow the steps mentioned below to download the CUET admit card:

1. Go to the official website: [cuet.nta.nic.in](http://cuet.nta.nic.in)
2. Access the link showing ‘CUET UG City intimation slip’
3. Enter your credentials: Application number, Security pin, and DOB.
4. Select the login button.
5. View your city intimation slip on the screen.

## CUET City Intimation Slip 2025: Changes Made This Year

As per the latest UGC guidelines, candidates appearing for CUET UG 2025 can now choose any subject for the exam, regardless of their Class 12 stream. This update offers students greater flexibility in selecting domain-specific subjects. The City Intimation Slip will continue to display the candidate’s chosen subjects, examination city, date, and time slot, consistent with the previous years.

## About CUET UG Entrance Examination 2025

The Common University Entrance Test – Undergraduate, conducted by the National Testing Agency, is a national-level entrance examination. The CUET offers a chance for students to secure admissions in several Central, State, and private universities across India. Students can reduce their burden of appearing for multiple entrance examinations, as CUET provides a unified platform for students seeking admission to undergraduate courses.

Candidates are tested on their language knowledge, general aptitude and the domain-specific subjects. This ensures the fairness of the examination and allows educational institutions to evaluate the standards of the individuals. Students who completed their 12th class or are in their final year can appear for the CUET exam.

## CUET UG 2025 Eligibility Criteria

Although there is no restriction on the age limit to apply for CUET UG 2025 examination, Candidates must have passed class 12th from a recognised board. Candidates must note that they have to fulfil the age criteria set by the university/institution to which they are allotted for admission.

## CUET UG Admit Card 2025

The [CUET UG 2025 admit card](/blog/cuet/cuet-ug-2025-paper-analysis-may-31-shift-1/) is a crucial document that every candidate must carry to the examination centre. Issued by the National Testing Agency (NTA), the admit card is expected to be released approximately one week before the exam period, which is scheduled from May 8 to June 1, 2025. Candidates can download the admit card from the official CUET website: [https://cuet.nta.nic.in](https://cuet.nta.nic.in/). To access the admit card, candidates need to log in using their application number and date of birth. It is advised to check the website regularly for updates and notifications regarding the release.

The admit card contains essential details such as the candidate’s name, roll number, exam date, reporting time, and exam centre address. It also includes important instructions that must be followed on the day of the examination. Candidates are required to carry a printed copy of the admit card along with a valid photo ID proof (such as Aadhaar card, PAN card, voter ID, or passport) to the exam hall. Failure to present the admit card and ID may lead to disqualification from appearing in the exam. Therefore, it is strongly recommended that candidates download and print their admit cards well in advance to avoid last-minute issues
