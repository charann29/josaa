## MHT CET Counselling 2025

MHT CET counselling process 2025 is conducted online. Below are the steps candidates need to follow for MHT CET 2025 counselling:

- **Registration:** Enter your personal, contact, and security details on the MHT CET official website to start the registration process. Once these details have been entered, you will need to create a password. After setting the password, fill in the other required fields. After that, applicants should submit their qualifications, including their overall and subject-specific grades from grades 10 and 12, along with the names of the relevant boards.

- **Uploading Scanned Documents:** In the second step of MHT CET counselling, candidates are required to upload the given documents:
  - 10th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - 12th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - MHT CET Scorecard 2025
  - Migration Certificate
  - Category Certificate (if applicable)
  - Character Certificate
  - Domicile Certificate (if applicable)
  - Print out the MHT CET Counselling registration Form

## MHT CET PCM Exam Analysis: Student Reviews For April 25th Shift 1 and Shift 2

According to student reviews from the MHT CET 2025, April 25th – Shift 1 & Shift 2, here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

**Mathematics**: Students found MHT CET paper pattern of Mathematics to be the hardest and usually consists of lengthy and complicated problems, particularly in the topics of Integration, 3D Geometry, and Vectors.

**Physics:** The MHT CET question paper of Physics was of moderate difficulty, questions will usually be application-based concepts such as Rotational Dynamics, Current Electricity, and Capacitors.

**Chemistry:** Typically, the easiest of the sections. MHT CET paper pattern of Chemistry is the most common section where direct questions come from the Maharashtra HSC textbook. The topics to focus on are Redox Reactions, IUPAC Nomenclature, and Coordination Compounds.

## Conclusion

The MHT CET 2025 PCM exam held on 25th April was a fair but moderately difficult paper for aspirants in Shift 1 and Shift 2. The Mathematics was lengthy, which tested the speed and problem-solving ability of students. Physics required conceptual clarity and application-based thinking, especially for Shift 2 candidates. Chemistry remained a scoring domain with mostly direct and theory-based questions.

Overall, the MHT CET question paper of all subjects was maintained according to the Maharashtra State Board syllabus, and there were no shocks or deviations from the syllabus. Candidates who focused on the concepts and applied time management while also revising important formulas and derivations were well-positioned to score better.
