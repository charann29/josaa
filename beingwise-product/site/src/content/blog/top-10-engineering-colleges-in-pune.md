### Entrance Examination:

- **MHT CET:** MHT-CET (Maharashtra Health and Technical Common Entrance Test) is a state-level exam conducted for admission into engineering, pharmacy, and agriculture courses in Maharashtra. It’s a computer-based test with PCM and PCB streams, based on the Class 11 and 12 syllabus, and has no negative marking.
- **JEE Main:** JEE Main (Joint Entrance Examination Main) is a national-level exam conducted by the NTA for admission into engineering, architecture, and planning courses in institutions like NITs, IIITs, and other centrally funded colleges. It’s also the qualifying exam for JEE Advanced and some other colleges.

**Institute Specific Entrance Examinations:**

- **NAT:** The NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT) is an online exam conducted by the NIAT Pune that evaluates reasoning, quantitative, and problem-solving skills for admission into NxtWave’s tech and upskilling programs. It’s the first step before counselling or enrollment.
- **MIT-WPU CET:** MIT-WPU CET (Common Entrance Test) is the university’s online exam for admission to various UG and PG programs. It includes a computer-based test and a personal interview, assessing reasoning, English, and subject knowledge depending on the course.
- **BVP CET:** BVP CET is Bharati Vidyapeeth’s entrance exam for UG and PG courses, including B.Tech. It tests Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics through a 3-hour computer-based test.
- **SITEEE:** SITEEE (Symbiosis Institute of Technology Engineering Entrance Exam) is a computer-based test for admission into B.Tech programs at Symbiosis Institute of Technology campuses. It has 60 questions from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, with no negative marking, and is based on the Class 12 PCM syllabus.
- **ACET:** ACET (Ajeenkya DY Patil University Common Entrance Test) in Pune is an online exam for admission to undergraduate and postgraduate programs like B.Tech, BBA, and B.Des. Admission is based on the ACET score, followed by a personal interview or studio test for certain courses.

### Admission Process for the Top 10 Engineering Colleges in Pune

Admissions to the Top 10 Engineering colleges in Pune are offered based on the entrance examinations such as MHT CET, JEE, and some institute-specific entrance examinations like NAT, SITEEE, ACET, BVP CET, etc. Based on the Specific college criteria, the seats are filled in a 70:30 ratio for the Entrance examination-based and management quota seats. Admissions are offered based on:

- Those who appeared for the MHT CET and JEE exam are required to appear for the counselling rounds and based on their preferences and rank, the college is allotted.
- Those who appear for the institute-specific entrance examination have to qualify for the same, in order to secure admission in the college.
