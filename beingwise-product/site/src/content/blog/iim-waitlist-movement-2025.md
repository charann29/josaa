## Table of Contents

Aspiring for admission into an IIM is a major goal for MBA candidates in India. Preparing for the CAT exam demands consistent, year-round effort to clear cutoffs and secure a seat in the prestigious MBA/PGDM programs offered by these central government-run business schools. Despite their dedication, many candidates are unable to get admission to an IIM. Some candidates, even after being placed on the IIM waitlist movement, ultimately do not get a seat.

Many students successfully qualify and clear the WAT-GD/PI rounds at IIMs, but due to their final composite scores falling short compared to others, they unfortunately miss out on admission.

**IIM Selection Process**

India is home to 21 IIMs, each consistently ranked among the top 100 business schools by NIRF. The selection process at these institutes is highly competitive and comprehensive. Apart from achieving a strong CAT score, candidates must also present an impressive overall profile. Most IIMs offer final admission based on the performance of the candidates in Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI) rounds. However, some institutes like IIM Ahmedabad have replaced the GD round with a combination of Personal Interview (PI) and Academic Writing Test (AWT), alongside other criteria.The admission process for the Post Graduate Programme (PGP) at IIMs follows a structured approach:

- Initial screening is done based on CAT exam scores, including sectional and overall cutoff ranks.
- Shortlisted candidates are then required to participate in AWT/WAT, GD, and Personal Interview rounds.
- The final admission decision depends on category-wise shortlisting and the candidate’s Final Composite Score (FCS).

## What is waitlist movement in IIM?

Each year, hundreds of thousands of students compete for admission to MBA programs at the IIMs. With a total seat capacity of around 4,000 across all 20 IIMs, only a limited number of candidates can be admitted. After the initial selection list is announced, there is often a group of candidates who remain on a waitlist, waiting for any selected candidate to decline their seat. When a seat is vacated, it is then offered to the next eligible candidate on the IIM waitlist.

**The IIM waitlist reflects the number of seats that become available for waitlisted candidates.** Applicants often wait anxiously for IIM waitlist movement, especially at top institutes such as IIM Ahmedabad, IIM Bangalore, and IIM Calcutta, where admission chances improve as seats are released. Recently, the IIM waitlist movement at baby IIMs has also gained attention.

Waitlist movement at IIMs and other top MBA colleges varies based on categories. It tends to be highly competitive and strict for the general category, while reserved categories like SC, ST, OBC, and PwD may experience somewhat relaxed criteria.

Unlike many other IIMs, IIM Lucknow waitlist movement is released in the form of a merit list. The Institute releases a list of candidates if the previously admitted candidates drop out, creating a vacant seat.

## Important List For IIM Waitlisted Students

If you find yourself on the IIM waitlist, here are some steps you can take to stay prepared and improve your chances:

Be Prepared to Respond Quickly: Offers from the waitlist might come with tight deadlines. Stay mentally and practically ready to make swift decisions, as other candidates are also waiting for their turn.

Keep a Positive Mindset: It’s natural to feel disappointed when waitlisted, but don’t lose hope. Many opportunities can still arise, and maintaining confidence and optimism will help you handle the situation better.

Organize Your Documents in Advance: Stay ahead by arranging all necessary paperwork like your CAT scorecard, academic transcripts, and recommendation letters. Having these ready will allow you to respond quickly if called.

Maintain Contact with Admissions: Politely reach out to the admissions team via email or phone to express your continued interest. Staying on their radar, without being overly persistent, can work in your favor.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What is waitlist movement in IIM?

The IIM waitlist consists of candidates who have qualified but have not been offered admission initially due to limited seats. If selected candidates decline their seats, waitlisted candidates move up in order based on their ranks and are offered admission during the IIM waitlist movement.

### 2. When does the IIM waitlist movement 2025 usually begin?

Typically, the IIM waitlist movement starts soon after the initial selection lists are released, around May-June, and can continue through the summer months as seats get vacated.

### 3. Is the IIM waitlist movement 2025 the same for all IIMs?

No, the waitlist movement varies across different IIMs depending on seat availability, category-wise reservations, and candidate decisions. Some IIMs may have multiple rounds, while others have limited or no waitlist movement.

### 4.  Can candidates from the General category expect different waitlist movement compared to reserved categories in the IIM waitlist?

Yes, waitlist movement tends to be more stringent in the General category. Reserved categories like SC, ST, OBC, and PwD generally see more movement due to relaxed cutoffs and reservation policies.

### 5. What should candidates do while waiting for the IIM waitlist movement 2025?

Candidates should stay positive, keep their documents ready, maintain communication with the admission office, and be prepared to respond quickly to any admission offer during the waitlist movement.
