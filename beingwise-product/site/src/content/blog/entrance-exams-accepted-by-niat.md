## How to Qualify for Universities Where the NIAT Upskilling Program is Offered?

To be considered for a university where NIAT’s upskilling program is offered, students need to go through the following steps:

**Step 1: Meet the Basic Eligibility Criteria.** Students must have completed or be appearing for Class 12 (10+2) with PCM (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics) as their core subjects, with a minimum of 50% marks. It is important to note that each collaborating university has its own eligibility criteria, and admission is granted solely based on the university’s policies.

**Step 2: Register and Appear for NAT** Students must register on the official NIAT website at niatindia.com and pay the non-refundable registration fee of INR 1,200. After registration, students appear for the NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) — a 90-minute online CBT test that evaluates cognitive abilities, problem-solving skills, and logical reasoning. NAT is not a university entrance exam. It is conducted by NxtWave as part of the enrolment process for NIAT’s upskilling program.

**Step 3: Attend the 1:1 Counselling Session** Students who qualify NAT are invited for a one-on-one counselling session. This session helps understand the student’s goals, interests, and career aspirations, and assesses how NIAT’s upskilling pathways can align with them. NAT performance and past academic records are also reviewed during this session for fee concession eligibility. Attending this session is mandatory — students who skip it will not be considered for enrolment in NIAT’s upskilling program.

**Step 4: NIAT Shares Details with the University** Based on the student’s NAT performance and counselling session, NxtWave shares the student’s details with a suitable collaborating university, strictly with the student’s written consent. NxtWave acts only as a facilitator in this process.

**Step 5: University Confirms Admission** The final admission decision rests entirely with the collaborating university. The university evaluates the student based on its own eligibility criteria and admission policies. Clearing NAT or completing the counselling session does not guarantee admission to any university.

### Are the NAT Entrance Exam and NIAT Exam same?

Yes, both the NAT and NIAT exam are the same. However, NAT is not an entrance exam for any university, but rather is a qualifying test for NIAT’s Upskilling program. Also, NIAT doesn’t have an entrance exam, as it is not a university; it is an Upskilling centre where students can enrol after clearing the NAT, also known as the NIAT qualifying test.

After the NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) qualifying test, students need to attend a mandatory 1:1 counselling program. With the written consent of the students, the info about them will be sent to the partner university.

### How to Prepare For NAT?

NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) is a 90-minute online test conducted by NxtWave as part of the enrolment process for NIAT’s industry-ready upskilling program. It evaluates a student’s cognitive abilities, problem-solving skills, and overall readiness for NIAT’s industry-aligned training modules. Students can go through the key pointers below to understand how to prepare effectively for NAT.

- NAT consists of three sections: Psychometric Test, Critical Thinking, and Mathematics.
- Each section has a specific time limit, so practising within time constraints is essential.
- The Mathematics section covers topics such as Number Theory, Ratios, Probability, Sets, and Coordinate Geometry.
- The Critical Thinking section tests logical reasoning skills through topics like Coding and Decoding, Blood Relations, and pattern recognition.
- The Psychometric Test assesses personality and emotional traits; students are advised to answer honestly and consistently.
- Attempting mock tests regularly helps students get familiar with the test format.
- Consistent daily preparation of one to two hours is more effective than irregular study sessions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

#### 1. How do I join a university offering the NIAT upskilling program?

Students must have passed class 12th, must have qualified for the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT), and must appear for a 1:1 counselling session conducted by NIAT. Students will be offered admission based on their performance in these three stages.

#### 2. What if I fail to attend the NIAT counselling session after qualifying for NAT?

NAT is the assessment test accepted by universities collaborating with NIAT. Still, if you fail to attend the counselling session even after qualifying for NAT, then your candidature will be cancelled, and you will not be eligible for enrollment in the NIAT’s upskilling program. The counselling session is vital to confirm your admission to NIAT.

#### 3. What is the NAT registration fee? Can I get a refund if I do not appear for NAT?

The NAT is the assessment test accepted by universities collaborating with NIAT, which charges an INR 1200 registration fee. No, you can not get a refund of the registration fee.

#### 4. How do I join a university offering the NIAT upskilling program?

To get started, students need to register on the official NIAT website at niatindia.com and appear for the NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test). Based on the NAT performance and 1:1 counselling session, NxtWave shares the student’s details with a suitable collaborating university, with the student’s written consent. The final admission is granted solely by the university based on its own eligibility criteria and admission policies.

#### 5. What is the NxtWave NIAT fee structure?

The fee for the NIAT upskilling program offered through collaborating universities ranges from INR 8 Lakhs to 18 Lakhs, depending on the location and the specialisation of the upskilling program.

#### 6. Can I appear for NAT from home?

Yes, NAT is conducted in an online Computer-Based Test (CBT) format, which allows students to take the test remotely using a compatible device and a stable internet connection.

#### 7. What happens after completing the NAT?

After attempting the NAT, students need to go through a 1:1 counselling session where their performance, interests, and goals are discussed to guide them toward suitable opportunities.

#### 8. What is the NIAT exam syllabus 2026?

NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) covers three sections: Psychometric Test, Critical Thinking, and Mathematics. The Critical Thinking section includes topics such as Coding and Decoding, Blood Relations, and Logical Reasoning, while the Mathematics section covers Number Theory, Ratios, Probability, Sets, and Coordinate Geometry. The Psychometric Test assesses a student’s personality traits and emotional tendencies.

#### 9. What happens if a student qualifies NAT but does not attend the counselling session?

If a student qualifies NAT but fails to attend the 1:1 counselling session, their candidature for enrolment in NIAT’s upskilling program will be cancelled. The counselling session is a mandatory step in the enrolment process and cannot be skipped.

#### 10. Does NAT require memorisation or rote learning?

No. NAT is designed to assess a student’s cognitive abilities, logical reasoning, and problem-solving skills. It does not test memorised content, making it accessible to students who are strong in analytical and critical thinking.

#### 11. What is NAT exam date 2026?

The NAT exam date 2026 is on 24th May. However, NAT takes place every Sunday. So students can do NAT exam date 2026 registration until Saturday of that week.
