## How to Prepare For The Last KEAM 2026 Exam?

With the final KEAM 2026 shifts just around the corner, this is the time to sharpen your strategy rather than start anything new. Based on the KEAM 2026 difficulty level seen in the April 21 paper, particularly the lengthy Mathematics section and moderate Physics, candidates must focus on high-weightage topics, speed, and accuracy. Here are the key preparation tips to make the most of the time left:

- Prioritise Calculus and Algebra in Mathematics; these two topics together account for over 50% of the Maths section and are the biggest scoring opportunity in the KEAM Maths question paper.
- Revise Electromagnetism for Physics: it carried the highest weightage in Physics at around 27%, covering Current Electricity, Electrostatics, and Magnetism.
- Consolidate NCERT for Chemistry: Chemistry is the most accessible section of the KEAM entrance exam; a strong command of Organic, Physical, and Inorganic Chemistry from NCERT can give your overall score a meaningful boost.
- Practice from KEAM model question papers: solving KEAM previous year question papers under timed conditions will help you identify weak areas and improve your accuracy before the final shift.
- Attempt full-length CBT mock tests: since the KEAM exam pattern is computer-based, practising in CBT mode helps simulate real exam conditions and reduces last-minute anxiety.
- Work on time management: students who appeared on the KEAM 2026 April 21 exam have consistently flagged time as the biggest challenge, especially in Mathematics. Train yourself to attempt easier questions first and return to lengthy ones later.
- Students can also practice the KEAM exam model question paper, which is available on the [**KEAM CollegeDost Mock Test**](/mock-test/keam) platform.

Yes, 470 is widely considered an above-average score in KEAM for engineering aspirants. The KEAM 2026 April 21 paper is designed for engineering aspirants and consists of a total of 150 questions. The paper is divided across three subjects: 75 questions from Mathematics, 45 from Physics, and 30 from Chemistry.

### 4. What are KEAM 2026 exam day guidelines?

Candidates appearing for the remaining KEAM 2026 exam shifts should keep the following guidelines in mind. Carry your admit card and a valid photo ID to the exam centre without fail. Report at least 30 minutes before the scheduled start time to avoid last-minute complications. Do not carry electronic devices, calculators, or study materials inside the examination hall. Read each question carefully before marking, as the KEAM exam pattern includes a negative marking of 1 for every incorrect response. Prioritise accuracy alongside speed, particularly in the Mathematics section, where calculation errors can be costly.

### 5. What was the difficulty level of KEAM 2026 on April 21?

According to the KEAM Paper Analysis, the difficulty level for the April 21 exam was rated overall moderate by students. Chemistry was the easiest section, Physics was moderate to slightly lengthy, and Mathematics was the most challenging due to its lengthy, multi-step, calculation-based problems.

### 6. Is KEAM 2026 tough or easy?

Based on the KEAM 2026 exam analysis conducted across multiple shifts, the overall difficulty level has been rated moderate. Chemistry has been consistently manageable and NCERT-based, Physics has been moderate with a mix of conceptual and numerical questions, while Mathematics has been the most demanding section due to its lengthy and calculation-intensive nature.

### 8. Is there negative marking in KEAM 2026?

Yes, the KEAM entrance exam 2026 follows a negative marking scheme. Candidates receive +4 marks for every correct answer and lose 1 mark for every incorrect response. Unattempted questions carry no penalty, so strategic skipping of uncertain questions is advisable to protect your overall score.

### 9. What is the validity of KEAM 2026 score?

The KEAM 2026 score and rank are valid only for the current academic year’s admission cycle. Candidates cannot carry forward their rank or score to the following year, making it essential to participate in all rounds of counselling conducted by CEE Kerala to maximise their chances of securing a preferred college and branch.
