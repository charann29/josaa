## Table of Contents

The CAT Exam Pattern and Syllabus are guides for managing one of India’s leading management entrance exams and gaining a competitive edge. Knowing the CAT Exam Pattern and Syllabus makes it much easier to understand the marking scheme, total marks available, and types of questions asked in each section. This complete overview gives aspirants the strategy to maximize their scores and perform well.

The CAT exam 2025 is for 120 minutes, with 40 minutes allotted to each 3 sections. There are MCQs and Non-MCQs types of questions. In the Non-MCQ questions, you have to directly type the answer.  3 marks are awarded for every correct answer, whereas 1 mark is deducted for each incorrect answer in the mCQ section.

You can also view detailed information on the marking scheme, the total marks available, download the most current CAT Syllabus PDF, and plan your study schedule around it for your convenience.

- **Sectional Time Limit:** Each section must be completed within a strict 40-minute window. Once the time ends, the test will automatically proceed to the next section.
- **Fixed Section Order:** The exam must be attempted in the prescribed sequence, starting with VARC, followed by DILR, and ending with Quantitative Aptitude. Candidates are not allowed to start with a section of their choice.
- **No Section Switching:** Due to the fixed order format, moving back and forth between sections is not permitted. Each section must be attempted in the given sequence.
- **On-Screen Calculator:** A digital calculator will be available on the screen for rough calculations during the test.
- **Answer Review Within Section:** Candidates may mark answers for review, but only within the current section. All reviews and submissions must be completed within the section’s time limit; otherwise, answers will not be recorded.
- **Negative Marking:** Incorrect answers will result in a penalty of 1 mark. Candidates should select their responses carefully to avoid unnecessary loss of marks.
