## CUET UG 2025 Exam Overview

The CUET exam is conducted across three sections, where candidates are allotted 60 minutes for each subject. Section I of the CUET exam pattern assesses the student’s language skills, while Section II and Section III assess the candidate’s abilities in the domain-specific subjects and general aptitude. Here is the exam overview of the CUET exam pattern 2025:

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Section** | **No of Questions** | **Duration** | **Questions Type** |
| Section IA- Language | 50 Questions | 60 Minutes | Language skills will be assessed through reading comprehension based on various types of passages such as factual, literary and narrative, along with evaluation of literary aptitude and vocabulary. |
| Section II- Domain Specific | 50 Questions | MCQS Based on Class 12 Syllabus |
| Section III- General Test | 50 Questions | The section will assess General Knowledge, Current Affairs, General Mental Ability, Numerical Ability, Quantitative Reasoning (based on simple applications of basic mathematical concepts from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration taught up to Grade 8), as well as Logical and Analytical Reasoning. |

## Student Reviews for CUET UG 2025 May 13th Shift 1 Exam

Most students from different states found CUET UG 2025 Shift 1 (held on May 13th) to be easier to moderate compared to last year. Here are some student reviews for reference:

### **Aman (from Gurgaon):**

“The overall paper was easier than expected. GAT was slightly lengthy with a lot of Reasoning questions, but manageable. English was very easy, especially vocabulary and RCS. Chemistry was NCERT-based and straightforward. Physics had a few tricky numericals, but nothing too tough.”

### **Ritika (from Rourkela):**

“GAT had some time-consuming questions, especially in Quant, but Reasoning and GK were easier. English was full of synonyms, antonyms, and rearrangements, very doable. Chemistry was scoring with direct NCERT questions. Physics felt moderate, with twisted concepts but fewer calculations.”

### **Soham (from Bhubaneswar):**

“English was the easiest section, with a focus on vocabulary and phrasal verbs. Chemistry was balanced across Physical and Organic topics, mostly direct questions. GAT was moderate; Quant was a bit lengthy, but Reasoning saved time. Physics was slightly tricky with conceptual twists.”

### **Sneha (from Delhi):**

“I found Chemistry very scoring, especially Physical and Organic parts. GAT was calculative, Quant had fewer but trickier questions. English has a lot of rearrangement and vocabulary, making it easy to attempt. Physics was fine, some numericals were a bit confusing, but overall okay.”

### **Vikram (from Cuttack):**

“The paper was mostly easy. English was simple with lots of vocabulary-based questions. Chemistry was NCERT-centric, so revision helped. GAT was slightly time-consuming because of the lengthy Quant questions. Physics had a few numericals that needed careful reading, but were manageable.”
