## Mistakes to Avoid in JEE Main Preparation Strategy

The following are factors that can negatively impact your ability to adequately prepare for the JEE Main:

- Not following the prescribed format and syllabus for the JEE Main exam
- Using more than one or two guidebooks, rather than selecting commonly used or accepted guidebooks
- Not establishing a comprehensive yet practical timetable for studying
- Not maintaining a consistent schedule and taking frequent breaks from study
- Not reviewing past subjects studied
- Not taking practice exams, and/or when taking practice exams, analysing them to understand performance
- Focusing only on your strong subject areas, instead of improving upon your weaker subject areas
- Not being able to control your time effectively when taking practice exams, as well as when completing timed mock exams
- Answering with your best guess, which results in an increased number of answers marked incorrect (eg, negative marks)
- Starting to study new material only days before the actual exam date
- Not having access to the prior year’s JEE Main question papers
- Letting anxiety and/or stress related to exams negatively affect the aspects of the study routine leading up to your exam.
