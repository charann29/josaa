Every year, lakhs of students appear for the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination with one goal in mind: securing a good rank. But what happens after the exam? The WBJEE merit list is the official document that decides who moves forward to counselling and who does not.

The West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board (WBJEEB) releases the WBJEE merit list on its official website, wbjeeb.nic.in. It is published along with the WBJEE result declaration. The merit list contains the names and ranks of all candidates who have qualified in the exam. Only those whose names appear in the WBJEE merit list are eligible to participate in the WBJEE counselling process and apply for admission to engineering, architecture, and pharmacy courses in West Bengal colleges.

This guide covers everything you need to know about the WBJEE merit list 2026, from important dates and download steps to rank calculation, category-wise lists, and what to do after results are announced.

## How Is the WBJEE Merit List Prepared?

The WBJEE merit list is not prepared arbitrarily. There is a clear and transparent process that WBJEEB follows every year to ensure fair ranking. Understanding this process helps you know exactly where you stand and what score you need.

The merit list preparation involves the following steps. First, WBJEEB collects all candidates’ raw scores from the OMR sheets after the exam. Then, objections raised against the provisional answer key are reviewed. After review, the final answer key is published. Marks are then recalculated based on the final answer key. Finally, ranks are assigned in descending order of total marks. Key factors in merit list preparation are as follows.

- Ranks are based entirely on the candidate’s performance in the WBJEE entrance exam.
- Class 12 board marks are not considered at all for the merit list.
- Two separate merit lists are prepared: General Merit Rank (GMR) and Pharmacy Merit Rank (PMR).
- Category-wise reserved merit positions are also indicated on the rank card.
- In the case of equal marks, WBJEEB applies its tie-breaking policy.
- A separate merit list is prepared for JEE Main qualified candidates for 10% of seats in self-financed engineering colleges in West Bengal.

### What Is GMR in WBJEE?

GMR stands for General Merit Rank. It is the primary rank used for all admissions through the WBJEE counselling process. Every student who appears in both Paper 1 (Mathematics) and Paper 2 (Physics and Chemistry) receives a GMR.

The GMR is calculated based on the total marks scored in Paper 1 and Paper 2 combined. The total marks for GMR calculation are out of 200. This rank is used for admission to all engineering, technology, and architecture degree courses in West Bengal colleges. It is also used for admission to the pharmacy course at Jadavpur University specifically.

One important thing to remember: GMR is the only rank used during all rounds of counselling and seat allotment. Your category rank appears on your rank card, but it does not decide your seat. GMR does.

### What Is PMR in WBJEE?

PMR stands for Pharmacy Merit Rank. It is a separate rank prepared for candidates who are seeking admission to pharmacy courses in West Bengal.

The PMR is based only on marks scored in Paper 2, which covers Physics and Chemistry. The total marks for PMR are out of 100. This rank is valid for pharmacy admissions in all enlisted pharmacy colleges and universities in West Bengal, except for Jadavpur University. Jadavpur University uses GMR for pharmacy admissions, not PMR. Students who appear only for Paper 2 get a PMR but not a GMR.

## WBJEE Merit List 2026: GMR vs PMR at a Glance

Many students confuse GMR and PMR. The table below explains the difference clearly:

| Parameter | GMR (General Merit Rank) | PMR (Pharmacy Merit Rank) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Full Form** | General Merit Rank | Pharmacy Merit Rank |
| **Based On** | Paper 1 + Paper 2 combined | Paper 2 only |
| **Total Marks** | Out of 200 | Out of 100 |
| **Used For** | Engineering, Technology, Architecture + Pharmacy at JU | Pharmacy at all colleges except JU |
| **Who Gets It** | Students appearing for both Paper 1 and Paper 2 | Students appearing for Paper 2 |
| **Used in Counselling** | Yes, for all rounds | Yes, for pharmacy admissions |
| **Appears on Rank Card** | Yes | Yes (if applicable) |

## How to Download the WBJEE Merit List 2026?

WBJEEB does not release a separate public PDF with all candidates listed. Instead, your individual rank card serves as your personal merit list document. You need to log in to the WBJEE portal and download your rank card. The steps below explain how to download the WBJEE merit list 2026.

- Step 1: Visit the official WBJEE website at wbjeeb.nic.in.
- Step 2: Look for the WBJEE 2026 Result or Merit List link on the homepage. Click on it.
- Step 3: You will be taken to the WBJEE candidate portal login page.
- Step 4: Enter your Application Number and Password used during registration.
- Step 5: Click Submit. Your WBJEE 2026 rank card will appear on the screen.
- Step 6: Check all details carefully, including your name, roll number, marks, GMR, and category rank.
- Step 7: Download the rank card as a PDF and save it on your device.
- Step 8: Take a printout and keep it safe. It is a mandatory document for WBJEE 2026 counselling.

**Note:** If you have forgotten your password, use the Forgot Password option on the portal. You can reset it using your registered email ID or mobile number. For any discrepancy in your rank card, contact WBJEEB at info@wbjeeb.in within 24 hours of result declaration.

## WBJEE Tie-Breaking Rules for Merit List 2026

When two or more students score exactly the same total marks, WBJEEB cannot assign the same rank to both. To handle this, the board follows a structured tie-breaking policy. This policy is applied in a fixed order of priority until the tie is resolved. **The tie-breaking rules are different for GMR and PMR.** Read both sections carefully if you are appearing for either or both.

### WBJEE Tie-Breaking Rules for GMR (General Merit Rank)

The following criteria are applied in the order listed below. If the first criterion resolves the tie, the remaining criteria are not used. If not, the board moves to the next criterion.

| Priority | Tie-Breaking Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| **1** | Student with fewer negative marks in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry combined gets a higher rank |
| **2** | Student with more positive marks in Mathematics and Physics combined gets a higher rank |
| **3** | Student with more positive marks in Mathematics and Chemistry combined gets a higher rank |
| **4** | Student with fewer negative marks in Mathematics and Physics combined gets a higher rank |
| **5** | Student with fewer negative marks in Mathematics and Chemistry combined gets a higher rank |
| **6** | Student with more positive marks in Mathematics for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **7** | Student with more positive marks in Physics for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **8** | Student with more positive marks in Chemistry for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **9** | Student with fewer negative marks in Mathematics for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **10** | Student with fewer negative marks in Physics for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **11** | Older student (earlier date of birth) gets a higher rank |
| **12** | Student with a lower application number (registered earlier) gets a higher rank |

### WBJEE Tie-Breaking Rules for PMR (Pharmacy Merit Rank)

The tie-breaking rules for PMR are based only on Paper 2 marks since PMR uses Paper 2 scores alone.

| Priority | Tie-Breaking Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| **1** | Student with fewer negative marks in Physics and Chemistry combined gets a higher rank |
| **2** | Student with more positive marks in Physics for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **3** | Student with more positive marks in Chemistry for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **4** | Student with fewer negative marks in Physics for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **5** | Student with fewer negative marks in Chemistry for 2-mark questions only gets a higher rank |
| **6** | Older student (earlier date of birth) gets a higher rank |
| **7** | Student with a lower application number (registered earlier) gets a higher rank |

If none of the subject-based criteria resolves the tie, the older student gets the higher rank. If that is also a tie, the student with the lower application number is ranked higher.

## WBJEE Merit List 2026: Role of Negative Marking

WBJEE has a negative marking scheme. This directly affects not just your total score but also your tie-breaking outcome. Students who attempt fewer questions incorrectly have an advantage during tie-breaking over students who score the same total but with more wrong answers.

Paper 1 and Paper 2 have three categories of questions. Category 1 questions carry 1 mark each, with 1 mark deducted for each wrong answer. Category 2 questions carry 2 marks each, with 1 mark deducted for each wrong answer. Category 3 questions carry 2 marks each, but there is no negative marking for these. **This is why the tie-breaking rules focus specifically on 2-mark questions.** Negative marks in 2-mark questions reflect a higher level of incorrect risk-taking.

## WBJEE Category Rank: What It Means and When It Is Used

WBJEEB prepares a category-wise merit list for SC, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B, EWS, PwD, and TFW categories. Your category rank appears alongside your GMR on the rank card.

During counselling, however, only GMR is used for seat allotment across all rounds. Your category rank is not used to determine your turn in counselling. Category ranks matter in one specific scenario. If your category claim is found to be invalid during document verification at the college level, your reserved category rank is cancelled. You are then treated as a General category student and your GMR alone applies. Always keep your caste or category certificate ready during counselling. Ensure it is from the appropriate authority and is valid as per West Bengal government norms.

### What to Do After the WBJEE Merit List Is Released?

Once the WBJEE merit list 2026 is out, the admission process moves quickly. Missing a deadline can cost you a seat. Here is what you need to do after the merit list is released.

- First, download your rank card immediately from wbjeeb.nic.in as soon as it is available. Check all details and report errors within 24 hours.
- Second, check the WBJEE 2026 counselling schedule published on the official website. Mark all important dates for registration, choice filling, and seat allotment.
- Third, prepare all required documents for counselling. These typically include the WBJEE rank card, Class 10 certificate for date of birth proof, Class 12 mark sheet and certificate, category certificate if applicable, domicile or residential proof, passport-size photographs, and a valid photo ID.
- Fourth, register for counselling within the specified window and fill in your college and course preferences carefully.
- Fifth, once the seat allotment result is out, pay the seat acceptance fee of Rs 5,000 online to confirm your seat.
- Sixth, report to the allotted college with original documents for verification and tuition fee payment within the given deadline.

## WBJEE Merit List 2026: Common Mistakes to Avoid

Students often make avoidable errors during and after the merit list phase. Here are the most common mistakes and how to avoid them:

- Not downloading the rank card promptly is a major mistake. The portal can get slow after result announcement due to heavy traffic. Download your rank card as soon as possible.
- Ignoring errors on the rank card is another issue. Even a minor discrepancy in name, date of birth, or category can cause problems during document verification. Report errors to WBJEEB immediately.
- Missing the counselling registration window is a critical error. The window is short, often just a few days. Register for counselling as soon as the schedule is announced.
- Not having documents ready is a frequent problem. Gather all required documents before counselling begins. Scrambling at the last minute can cause delays.
- Filling in the wrong college preferences during choice filling is hard to undo. Research each college thoroughly before the choice-filling window opens. You cannot change preferences once submitted in most cases.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **When will the WBJEE merit list 2026 be released?**
   The WBJEE merit list 2026 is expected to be released in July or August 2026, along with the WBJEE 2026 result. The exact date will be announced by WBJEEB after the final answer key is out. Check the official website wbjeeb.nic.in for updates.
2. **What login credentials are needed to download the WBJEE merit list?**
   You need your WBJEE 2026 Application Number and Password to log in to the candidate portal and download your rank card.
3. **Is the WBJEE merit list the same as the rank card?**
   Yes. WBJEEB does not publish a separate public PDF listing all candidates. Your individual rank card serves as your personal merit list document. It contains your GMR, PMR (if applicable), and category rank.
4. **Will the WBJEE 2026 merit list be released offline?**
   No. The WBJEE merit list 2026 is only released online on the official website wbjeeb.nic.in. No offline or postal copies are issued.
5. **What is the difference between GMR and PMR in WBJEE?**
   GMR (General Merit Rank) is based on total scores in Paper 1 and Paper 2 combined (out of 200) and is used for engineering, technology, and architecture admissions. PMR (Pharmacy Merit Rank) is based on Paper 2 marks only (out of 100) and is used for pharmacy admissions in all colleges except Jadavpur University.
6. **Can I participate in counselling if my name is not in the merit list?**
   No. Only students who qualify and receive a GMR or PMR are eligible for WBJEE 2026 counselling. If your name does not appear in the merit list, you cannot participate in the admission process.
7. **Is re-evaluation or rechecking of WBJEE answer sheets allowed?**
   No. WBJEEB does not allow re-evaluation or rechecking. WBJEE OMR sheets are machine-evaluated. However, if you find a discrepancy in your rank card, you can raise a grievance at info@wbjeeb.in within 24 hours of result declaration with copies of your OMR, rank card, question booklet number, and series code.
8. **Will my category rank be used during counselling?**
   No. Category ranks such as SC Rank, ST Rank, OBC-A Rank, and others appear on the rank card for reference only. All rounds of counselling and seat allotment are conducted strictly based on GMR.
9. **What happens if two students have the same WBJEE score?**
   WBJEEB applies a multi-step tie-breaking policy. The criteria include fewer negative marks across subjects, more positive marks in specific combinations, performance in 2-mark questions, date of birth (older student gets preference), and finally the application number (lower number gets preference).
10. **What is the TFW category in WBJEE?**
    TFW stands for Tuition Fee Waiver. It is a category under which eligible students can receive a waiver on tuition fees in self-financed colleges. Students in the TFW category receive a separate TFW rank on their rank card in addition to their GMR.
11. **Does Jadavpur University accept WBJEE for B.Tech admissions?**
    Yes. Jadavpur University accepts WBJEE scores for B.Tech admissions. For engineering and technology courses, GMR is used. For pharmacy, GMR is also used at Jadavpur University specifically, unlike all other colleges that use PMR for pharmacy.
12. **How many rounds of WBJEE counselling are held?**
    WBJEEB typically conducts two rounds of counselling for WBJEE admissions. The number of rounds may vary in a given year. Check the official WBJEE counselling notification for the latest information.
