### MHT CET Paper Analysis April 13 Shift 1: Physics

Based on the MHT CET student’ reaction, today’s shift 1 Physics section was rated as moderate to tough. As per the MHTCET Paper Analysis by Experts, Many students often struggled, scoring between 15-20, largely due to fear and poor time management. However, they pointed out that there were actually 30 to 35 easy questions available if students remained calm. Take a look at the key topics asked in today’s shift below

- **Rotational Dynamics:** Torque (specifically for a disc)
- **Oscillations & Waves:** Time period in SHM and Young’s Double Slit Experiment (YDSE), which has appeared consistently across multiple shifts
- **Electrodynamics:** Ohm’s Law (VI graphs), capacitance, Wheatstone bridge, and EMI (magnetic flux and EMF)
- **Optics:** Mirror and lens calculations

### MHT CET Paper Analysis April 13 Shift 1: Maths

As per the MHTCET paper Analysis, the maths section was medium to tough, but it was noted by experts that more than 50% of the questions were easy and solvable with clear concepts. About 4 to 5 questions were tough, and the remaining questions were of medium level.  Some common topics asked in today’s shift include

- **Vectors and 3D Geometry –** Direction cosines, Unit vectors, and Angle between two vectors
- **Calculus –**Maxima, Minima of a function and solving differential equations
- **Matrices –**Inverting matrices using the Adjoint technique
- **Other Topics –**Determinants, Conditional Probability, 3D Lines

### MHT CET Paper Analysis April 13 Shift 1: Physics

As per the MHT CET PCM Paper, the subject ‘Chemistry’ can be considered the easiest subject for this shift. Students who have prepared themselves adequately could easily score more than 35 marks in this paper. But there were around 4 or 5 theoretical questions which could be considered ‘new’, but were taken from the textbook itself. Look at the key topics asked in today’s shift.

- **Physical Chemistry:** Order of reaction (Chemical Kinetics), Nernst equation, and colligative properties
- **Inorganic Chemistry:** Hybridisation (e.g., NH3​ , BF 4 ), oxidation states (e.g., finding Phosphorus in H 3PO 4 )
- **OrganicChemistry:** IUPAC nomenclature, and coordination compounds

## Expert Strategy for Upcoming Shifts to Score High

The right strategy can help you a lot in acing your MHT CET exam. Taking some inputs from the MHTCET Paper Analysis April 13 Shift 1 helps you to score high in the upcoming shifts:

- Manage Your Time: Try to answer the questions of Chemistry within 20 minutes to have enough time left to attempt the lengthy sections like Mathematics.
- Fast Begin with Chemistry: Attempt pure theoretical questions from Chemistry in the beginning within 10 minutes, scoring at least 25+ marks.
- Take Some Mental Breaks: After completing Section 1 of the test, take a short break of 5 minutes before starting with Mathematics.
- Choose Questions Wisely: Do not waste time on complex numerical questions and skip them for now, and attempt them if there is any time left.
- Right Source of Preparation: Do practice through MHT CET PYQs as well as through some other competitive exams and some easy questions of JEE Main.
- Do Not Panic: You need to remember that it is normal that some initial questions will be difficult; however, they will include some easier questions too.
- Practice with Mock Tests: Do practice with some mock tests rather than watching lengthy solution videos.
