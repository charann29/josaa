## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What makes NIAT Training Centre in Bhopal different from traditional engineering colleges?

NIAT is not a standalone university; it is an upskilling program that runs at a Training Centre provided by Scope Global Skills University (SGSU). Unlike traditional engineering programs that rely heavily on theory and rote learning, NIAT focuses on industry-relevant skills through project-based learning, mentorship from professionals at top tech companies, and access to a 3000+ hiring partner network. Students get a UGC-recognised B.Tech degree from SGSU and an Industry Ready Certificate (IRC) from NxtWave.

### 2. What is the fee structure for NIAT in Bhopal?

The combined cost, covering NIAT’s upskilling program fee and SGSU’s B.Tech degree fees, ranges from approximately INR 8–18 lakhs in total (INR 2–4.5 lakhs per year). NIAT charges a separate upskilling/enrollment fee, while SGSU independently collects degree-related fees.

### 3. Is NIAT an accredited university or institution?

No. NIAT is not a university, college, or academic institution. It is an upskilling program by NxtWave, endorsed by NSDC, NASSCOM, and Startup India. Students receive their UGC-recognised, AICTE-approved B.Tech degree from Scope Global Skills University (SGSU), while NIAT provides an Industry Ready Certificate (IRC).

### 4. What are the SGSU University on-campus facilities like?

SGSU offers a 26-acre green campus with hi-tech laboratories, smart classrooms, an open-air theatre, a canteen, health and counselling services, a library with digital resources, fitness facilities, and hostel accommodation with basic medical support.

### 5. What does NIAT mean?

NIAT stands for NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies. It is an upskilling program that collaborates with over 20 universities across India and provides practical training in AI & ML, Data Science, Cyber Security, and Full Stack Development.

### 6. I’m confused. Do I pay the fees to SGSU or NIAT?

Both entities collect fees independently. SGSU collects fees related to the B.Tech degree program, while NIAT charges a separate fee for its industry-focused upskilling program. These are two distinct payments made to two separate organisations.

### 7. How can I get into the NIAT Upskilling Program in Bhopal?

Students need to register for the NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test), a 90-minute online assessment. After qualifying, they participate in a counselling session. Based on performance and preferences, NxtWave may recommend SGSU Bhopal as a collaborating university. Final admission to SGSU is solely determined by the university.

### 8. Does NIAT guarantee SGSU admission or admission to any other university?

No. NIAT only acts as a facilitator. It can share student details with SGSU after obtaining written consent, but admission decisions are made exclusively by the university based on its own eligibility criteria and policies.

### 9. What support does NIAT provide beyond the training modules?

NIAT provides mentorship, soft-skills training, career counselling, coding practice sessions, resume-building assistance, mock interviews, hackathon support, access to student clubs, and a 3000+ company hiring network to improve career readiness.

### 10. How good is Scope Global Skills University (SGSU) for B.Tech CSE?

SGSU is Central India’s first NEP and NSQF-aligned skills university, recognised by UGC and AICTE. Its industry partnerships, skill-focused curriculum, and collaboration with NIAT provide B.Tech CSE students with a practical and industry-oriented learning experience.
