## **JEE Mains Counselling 2025: Step-by-Step Process**

Before the announcement of JEE Mains Counselling Date 2025, two mock rounds were conducted to help candidates gain a better understanding of the counselling process. This process involved several important stages, including registration, selection of colleges and courses, fee payment, and locking of choices. A detailed explanation of each step in JEE Main Counselling 2025 will be provided below:

### **How to Participate in JEE Main Counselling 2025?**

JEE Mains Counselling 2025 process consists of 5 stages.  To participate in the process, candidates must register through the official website. To check details, refer to the section below:

Candidates must register on the JoSAA official portal to participate in counselling. Those who appeared for JEE Main 2025 or JEE Advanced 2025 can log in and complete the registration process.

#### 1. Registration

Candidates must register on the JoSAA official portal to participate in counselling. Those who appeared for JEE Main 2025 or JEE Advanced 2025 can log in and complete the registration process.

##### **How to Register for JEE Main Counselling 2025?**

- Visit the JoSAA website.
- Log in using your JEE Main 2025 application number, password, and security pin
- Read and accept the instructions by clicking ‘I Agree’
- Fill in the counselling form and click on ‘Confirm Registration’

#### 2. Choice Filling & Choice Locking

You need to select their desired institutes and B.Tech branches. Seat allotment will depend on the rank, seat availability, and submitted choices.

- Select preferred courses and institutions
- Edit, rearrange, or delete choices if needed
- Verify and lock the final choices
- Choices can be locked after each counselling round

#### 3. Seat Allotment

Seat allocation will be based on the JEE Main/JEE Advanced rank. Once a seat is allotted, candidates must choose one of the following:

- Freeze: Accept the allotted seat and do not participate in further rounds
- Float: Accept the seat and stay eligible for better options in later rounds
- Slide: Accept the institute, but wait for a preferred branch within the same institute

#### 4. Payment of Seat Confirmation Fee

Candidates who choose Freeze or Float must pay the required seat acceptance fee online and upload scanned documents for verification. Upon successful verification, a provisional admission letter will be issued with details about the remaining fee payment and reporting schedule

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Fee (INR)** |
| SC / ST / ST-PwD / SC-PwD / OBC-NCL-PwD / GEN-PwD | 20,000 |
| Other Candidates | 45,000 |

#### 5. Reporting to the Allotted Institute

Candidates must visit the allotted college as per the date mentioned in the allotment letter. They need to pay the remaining fees and present original documents for final admission formalities.

## **About JoSAA Counselling 2025**

The Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) regulates the admission process to prestigious institutes such as IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other government-funded technical institutes. JoSAA operates under the Ministry of Education and regulates admissions to 121 institutes across India.

## **JEE Main Counselling 2025:Two Major Processes**

The JEE Main counselling 2025 process comprises three major phases. JoSAA conducts the first phase for admissions into **IITs**, **NITs**, **IIITs**, and **GFITs.** CSAB conducts the second phase of the counselling process in two modes: i) CSAB NEUT and CSAB SFTI.  CSAB NEUT fills additional seats reserved for candidates from the North-Eastern states and Union Territories. On the other hand, CSAB SFTI, which covers 18 Self-Financing Technical Institutes (SFTIs), assigns seats according to the applicant’s JEE Main AIR and preference order.

### **JoSAA Counselling 2025 vs CSAB Counselling 2025**

Students often get confused between these two counselling processes. To make it easier to understand, a detailed comparison has been provided below:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **JoSAA** | **CSAB** |
| Conducts six rounds for seat allocation | Conducts two rounds for seat allocation |
| Candidates who qualified for JEE Mains & Advanced are eligible to participate | Seats may remain vacant after JoSAA counselling |
| Seats may remain vacant after JoSAA counseling | Eligible candidates are those who failed to appear for JoSAA counseling. Candidates who want to upgrade the seat / didn’t receive any seat in JoSAA counseling. |

## **JEE Main Counselling 2025: Eligibility Criteria for Admissions**

Candidates appearing for JEE Mains Counselling 2025 must meet the eligibility criteria. Here is the eligibility criteria for admission:

- Based on the All India Rank, admissions to undergraduate courses will be made.
- For admissions into B. Tech candidates have to secure 75% in their 12th class (65% for SC/ST).
- To apply for B. Arch, candidates must score at least 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, and 50% overall in the qualifying exam.
- This applies to all technical institutes participating in the JEE Main 2024 Counselling through JoSAA/CSAB.
- State-affiliated engineering colleges will create separate rank lists based on state-specific eligibility criteria.

# JEE Main Counselling 2025: Document Format and Verification Guidelines

Proper documentation is one of the most important parts of JoSAA counselling 2025. Each certificate submitted during JEE Main 2025 counselling must strictly follow JoSAA’s prescribed formats and validity rules. Even small mistakes, such as incorrect format, incorrect issuance date, or inconsistencies in personal details, can lead to rejection, a category change, or the cancellation of the allotted seat. This guide explains all document-related rules candidates must follow to ensure a smooth and error-free counselling process.

### **JoSAA counselling 2025: Issuance Date Requirements for Category Certificates**

JoSAA is very strict about the issuance date of certain certificates. If a certificate does not adhere to the specified date requirement, the category benefit is revoked even if the candidate technically belongs to that category.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Certificate Type** | **Required Issuance Date** | **Notes** |
| **OBC–NCL Certificate** | On or after **1 April 2025** | Mandatory; older certificates are automatically invalid |
| **GEN–EWS Certificate** | On or after **1 April 2025** | Older certificates result in category shift to General |
| **SC/ST Certificate** | No annual date restriction | Must be issued by competent authority; must be in the Central List |
| **PwD Certificate** | As per RPwD Act | Must clearly mention type and percentage of disability |

Incorrect issuance dates are one of the top reasons candidates lose OBC–NCL and EWS reservation benefits.

### **JoSAA counselling 2025: Requirements for PwD Certificate**

Candidates applying under the PwD category must provide a certificate issued in accordance with the **Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act, 2016**. The certificate must clearly specify:

- Type of disability
- Percentage of disability
- Permanent or temporary condition
- Authorised medical signature and stamp

Only the official JoSAA format is accepted. Any deviation can lead to the denial of PwD reservation benefits.

### **JoSAA counselling 2025: Medical Fitness Certificate Requirements**

All candidates must submit a **Medical Fitness Certificate** in JoSAA’s official format. This certificate verifies the student’s physical fitness and includes:

- Height and weight
- Vision status
- General health status
- Doctor’s stamp, signature, and medical registration number

The certificate is again verified at the time of reporting at the allotted institute.

### **JoSAA counselling 2025: Domicile Certificate Requirements**

A Domicile Certificate may be required depending on the counselling type:

- CSAB Special Rounds
- State quota allocations
- Certain NITs and GFTIs with Home State quotas

The appropriate district authority must issue the certificate and must match the personal details submitted in the JEE application.

### **JoSAA Counselling 2025: Uploading Scanned Copies and Verification of Originals**

During the online process, students must upload digitally scanned copies of the **original versions** of their documents. The uploaded copies must be clear, readable, and free from distortion.

Later, at the institute reporting stage, candidates must show:

- Original certificates
- Original category documents
- Medical certificate
- Academic certificates

Any mismatch between the scanned copy and the physical original may lead to cancellation of the seat.
