## CAT 2025 Eligibility Criteria

Candidates need a graduation degree with 50% minimum marks from a recognized university to appear for the CAT exam 2025. Moreover, Professionals with CA, CS, ICWA (CMA), or Fellow of the Institute of Actuaries of India (FIAI) can also take the exam if they meet these percentage requirements. The minimum eligibility marks needed are given below in the table.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Minimum Required Percentage** |
| General/EWS/NC-OBC | 50% or equivalent CGPA |
| SC/ST/PwD | 45% or equivalent CGPA |

### CAT 2025 Eligibility Criteria For Final-Year Students

Final-year students can apply for CAT 2025 if they finish their graduation by June 2026. Selected candidates can join the programme temporarily if their Principal/Registrar gives them a certificate showing they’ve completed all bachelor’s degree requirements.

### CAT 2025 Eligibility Criteria For Candidates With Backlogs

Students with backlogs in their bachelor’s degrees can take CAT 2025. They need to clear all backlogs before starting the admission process at their chosen management institutes. This includes students with pending supplementary re-examinations, who must eventually score a minimum of 50% for the general category and 45% for the reserved categories.

### CAT 2025 Eligibility Criteria For Reserved Categories

The reservation policy for CAT 2025 follows government guidelines:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Reservation Percentage** |
| Scheduled Caste (SC) | 15% |
| Scheduled Tribe (ST) | 7.5% |
| Other Backward Classes (NC-OBC) | 27% |
| Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) | Up to 10% |
| Persons with Disability (PwD) | 5% |

Students from reserved categories should upload valid category certificates during registration. SC/ST candidates must belong to a caste/tribe listed in the Government of India schedule. PwD candidates should get a UDID card and include it with their application.

### CAT 2025 Eligibility Criteria For International/NRI Candidates

All the eligible NRIs and International candidates can apply through CAT 2025. Although some IIMs accept GMAT or GRE scores.

Indian nationals applying as international candidates must have lived outside India for at least 18 months as of January 1, 2025. These candidates should be outside India on the day of the exam. They need a certificate from their local Indian Embassy/High Commission/Consulate confirming this.

Foreign nationals must have a valid passport/travel document from a country other than India.

## CAT 2025 Registration Process

Students can register for CAT 2025 from August 1 until September 13, 2025, on the official website. Candidates must submit their applications by 5:00 pm on the closing date to take the examination scheduled for November 30, 2025.

Here’s how you can register for CAT 2025:

1. Go to the official CAT website ([https://iimcat.ac.in](https://iimcat.ac.in/)).
2. Under the New Candidate Registration section, click on the “Register” button.
3. Candidates need to fill out the information like name, date of birth, email address, and mobile number.
4. Candidates will receive an OTP on the registered mobile number and email. Just verify it to proceed. If you’re an overseas candidate, you’ll get the OTP only via your email.
5. After verification, candidates will get User ID by email and the Password by SMS. Overseas candidates receive both through email.
6. Use these credentials to log in and complete the application form. The form has six sections: Personal Details, Academics, Work Experience, Programmes, Test City, and Payment.
7. Pick up to six test cities where you’d like to take the exam.
8. Double-check all information before paying – you can’t make changes later.
9. Pay the application fee online:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Fee Amount** |
| General/EWS/NC-OBC | INR 2,600 |
| SC/ST/PwD | INR 1,300 |

### **Common mistakes to avoid during CAT 2025 Registration**

Students often make mistakes during registration. Here’s what you should watch out for:

- Your personal information must be accurate. You can’t change details like your name, date of birth, or contact information after submission.
- Keep your academic documents handy while filling in percentages and graduation details. Students often make mistakes in these sections.
- Many applications get rejected because of poor document uploads. Make sure your scanned documents are clear and meet the size and format requirements.
- Take your time with the form. You can save it multiple times before the final submission. Review everything carefully before making the payment.
- Use recommended browsers like Microsoft Edge (80-103), Google Chrome (59+), or Mozilla Firefox (56+) to avoid technical issues while applying.

## **CAT 2025 Admission & Selection Process**

The CAT 2025 admission process goes beyond entrance exam scores. Candidates must clear multiple stages that assess them on different parameters.

### **CAT exam and result declaration**

CAT 2025 has three main sections: **Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), and Quantitative Ability (QA)**. IIM Kozhikode will announce results in January 2026. Candidates will receive email notifications and can check their results on the official website. The results show sectional and overall percentile scores that are the foundations of the next selection stages.

### **Shortlisting based on CAT cutoff**

Each IIM and B-school sets its own cutoffs to shortlist candidates. These cutoffs change based on institution and category. IIM Mumbai’s minimum percentile requirements serve as a good example:

|  |  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Category** | **VARC** | **DILR** | **QA** | **Overall** |
| General | ≥80 | ≥80 | ≥75 | ≥85 |
| NC-OBC/EWS | ≥70 | ≥65 | ≥65 | ≥75 |
| SC | ≥65 | ≥60 | ≥60 | ≥70 |
| ST | ≥55 | ≥55 | ≥55 | ≥65 |
| PwD | ≥45 | ≥45 | ≥45 | ≥55 |

The actual cutoffs might be higher depending on the quality of applicants.

### **WAT, GD, and PI rounds**

Candidates who clear the cutoffs move to the next evaluation rounds. Most IIMs use a Written Ability Test (WAT) to check writing skills and clarity of thought. A Personal Interview (PI) follows to assess communication, personality, and domain knowledge. Some institutes like IIM Ahmedabad, IIM Lucknow, and JBIMS Mumbai also include Group Discussions (GD) to evaluate leadership and team skills.

### **Final merit list and composite score**

Each institution creates its final merit list using a composite score from multiple parameters. IIM Mumbai’s scoring pattern looks like this:

- CAT 2024 Score: 60%
- Personal Interview: 20%
- Academic Performance & Work Experience: 20%

### **Weightage of Academic and Work profile**

Academic records and professional experience play a vital role in final selection. Most IIMs give 5-15% weightage to academics (10th, 12th, and graduation marks) and 5-10% to work experience. IIM Bangalore allocates 15% to academics and 10% to work experience. They multiply the quality of experience by 0.25-2x based on relevance. Newer IIMs such as Sirmaur and Mumbai give up to 20 marks for work experience. This makes these institutes particularly attractive to candidates with exceptional professional backgrounds.

## **CAT **Common Admission Process (CAP) for new IIMs****

CAT 2025 results will be announced in January 2026. Shortlisted candidates must complete a multi-stage counselling process to get admission to their chosen institutions.

CAT doesn’t have a centralised counselling system. Each institute has its own process that starts with shortlisting based on CAT percentile. Candidates get call letters through email with WAT/GD/PI dates, venue details, and registration links. The next steps include document verification, registration on the institute’s portal, selection of priorities, and evaluation rounds.

IIM Bodh Gaya coordinates CAP 2025, which brings together the admission process for nine newer IIMs including Jammu, Sirmaur, and Visakhapatnam. Students need to pick four cities they want for their Personal Interview and Written Ability Test. Cities are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis. CAP requires students to upload several documents including their photograph, identity proof, CAT scorecard, academic certificates, and work experience papers.

### **Supplementary Admission Process (SAP)**

SAP 2025 gives a second chance to candidates with lower percentiles who couldn’t make it through CAP shortlisting. IIMs Bodh Gaya, Jammu, and Sirmaur run SAP with lower cut-offs – 92 percentile for General category compared to 95 for CAP. Students can attend interviews in Delhi, Kolkata, Bengaluru, Mumbai, and campus locations.

### **Seat allotment and document verification**

Selected candidates must verify their documents. These include academic certificates, identity proof, reservation certificates (if applicable), and work experience documentation. Final-year students need to submit a certificate from their institution that confirms 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD).

### **Admission confirmation and fee payment**

Students must confirm their seat by paying fees before the deadline. Those with provisional admission need to submit their final degree certificates by December 31, 2026. Missing this deadline leads to admission cancellation. Before the programme starts, students must bring their original documents to their assigned institute for final verification.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What are the key dates for CAT 2025?

The CAT 2025 exam is scheduled for 30 November 2025. Registration opens on 1 August 2025 and closes on 13 September 2025. Admit cards will be available for download from 5 November to 30 November 2025.

### 2. What is the eligibility criteria for CAT 2025?

Candidates must have a bachelor’s degree with at least 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD) from a recognised university. There is no age limit, and final-year students can also apply if they complete their degree by June 2026.

### 3. How does the CAT 2025 selection process work?

The process involves multiple stages: the CAT exam, shortlisting based on cutoffs, the Written Ability Test (WAT), the Group Discussion (GD), and the Personal Interview (PI). The final selection is based on a composite score that includes CAT performance, academic background, and work experience.

### 4. What are the expected cutoffs for top IIMs in CAT 2025?

For general category candidates, the cutoffs for top IIMs like Ahmedabad, Bangalore, and Calcutta are expected to range from 85 to 94 percentile. However, actual cutoffs may vary based on the applicant pool and can be higher for some institutes.

### 5. How does the Common Admission Process (CAP) work for new IIMs?

CAP 2025, coordinated by IIM Bodh Gaya, unifies the admission process for nine newer IIMs. Candidates select four preferred cities for their Personal Interview and Written Ability Test. Participation requires uploading the necessary documents, including the CAT scorecard, academic certificates, and work experience proof.
