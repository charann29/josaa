## JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Maths

In the JEE Mains Question Paper, the difficulty level of mathematics was Medium to Hard. Following previous shift trends, Mathematics remained the toughest and most time-consuming section.

The section involved a lot of mathematical calculations.  The questions were described by some experts as “a trap,” appearing simple at first sight but needing 5 to 6 minutes each to solve.

**Some of the** **High weightage chapters for JEE Mains 2026** **covered in the Mathematics paper** included Vectors and 3d, Conic Sections, Matrices, Integration, Quadratic Equations, Calculus, Limits, Functions, GP, and conic sections, vectors, and 3D geometry and calculus (definite integrals).

## JEE Mains Chemistry Paper Analysis

The level of Difficulty of the Chemistry section was moderately easy compared to Mathematics, but more difficult than Physics. There were around 6 to 8 statement-type questions, and the paper appeared moderately lengthy. Although most of the questions were taken directly from NCERT, some numerical questions related to physical chemistry were stated to have been difficult and calculative

**The topics included in this section primarily include organic and physical chemistry**

Particularly noteworthy High weightage chapters for JEE Mains covered were Chemical bonding, structure of molecules, coordination compounds, and biomolecules.

## JEE Mains Physics Paper Analysis

As per the JEE Main Analysis for the Physics section, the level of difficulty is Easy to Medium. This section was considered to be the easiest of all. The questions asked in this section are mostly formula-based questions. All candidates had no problem solving this section, which took only 40-45 minutes to complete.

**Some of the High weightage chapters for JEE Mains covered in the Physics paper were electrostatics, capacitors, and the magnetic effect of current.**

### What is NTA JEE Mains Percentile Score?

JEE Mains percentile score is an adjusted score that is calculated by NTA to maintain consistency in scores across various test sessions. This score indicates the percentage of candidates who have performed worse than or at the same level as the candidate scoring a specific score in the test.

This process helps mitigate discrepancies arising from varying levels of difficulty in different test sessions and facilitates a consistent assessment of performance.
