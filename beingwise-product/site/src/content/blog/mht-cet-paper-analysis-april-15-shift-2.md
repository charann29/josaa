## Subject-wise MHT CET April 15 Shift 2 Paper Analysis 2026

The MHT CET Question Paper consists of 150 questions, 50 each from Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry. Each correct answer is worth one mark, and there is no negative grading for incorrect responses. Now, take a look at the subject-wise MHT CET exam analysis for April 15 shift 2, below

### MHT CET 2026 Chemistry Paper Analysis

Chemistry was one of the easiest and highest-scoring subjects among other subjects. Most questions were direct and NCERT-based. A huge portion of the questions belonged to the topic of Organic & Inorganic Chemistry.

Students who appeared in this shift said that most of the questions were theoretical and quite direct, requiring few calculations. Thus, it turned out to be the quickest subject, which enabled candidates to make maximum attempts. The major topics included:

- Organic Chemistry (named reactions, basic mechanisms)
- Chemical Kinetics (important physical chemistry topic)
- Chemical Bonding
- Basic Inorganic Concepts

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Physics Section

As per the MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis. Physics was rated moderately easy by candidates of shift 2; however, relatively lengthy. There were a number of formula-based numericals. Although questions were not too difficult, candidates required several steps to solve them, and hence, it became relatively lengthy. The key topics asked in this shift include:

- Mechanics (Laws of Motion, Work, Power, Energy)
- Oscillations & Waves
- Magnetism
- Modern Physics

### MHT CET Analysis for Maths Section

Mathematics proved to be the toughest and most time-consuming in this shift. This subject was moderately difficult and lengthy. Candidates also found some questions slightly tough, equivalent to JEE type. Mathematics has a strong dominance of standard high-weightage chapters. Key topics include:

- Calculus (Differentiation & Integration)
- Vectors & 3D Geometry
- Algebra (Matrices, Determinants)
- Probability

3. Next, carefully compare your marked responses with the correct answers provided in the key. For every correct answer, add marks according to the official marking scheme: +1 mark for Physics and Chemistry, and +2 marks for Mathematics.
4. Note that there is no negative marking in case of wrong answers, and unattempted answers are allotted zero marks.
