## April 13 Shift 2 MHT CET Paper Analysis Subject-wise

Based on the MHT CET paper analysis for the past two days, it is observed that the mathematics portion in the test has always been relatively tough and lengthy, involving many calculations. It is suggested that the candidates should thoroughly analyze the MHT CET Paper analysis to make their preparations more exam-centric.

### MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 April 12 Shift 1 Maths

According to the MHT CET PCM paper analysis, Mathematics was considered the toughest section in the Shift 2 examination. Questions involved many steps, formulas, and calculations. It was highly calculative and time-consuming, thus requiring the students to have good conceptual clarity and fast-solving skills. However, the majority of the students faced problems mainly because of their poor time management ability. Take a look at the key topics of today’s shift 2 given below.

- Vectors & 3D Geometry: Direction ratios, angles, equations of lines
- Calculus: Integration, Differential Equations, Maxima & Minima
- Matrices & Determinants: Inverse using adjoint, properties
- Probability: Conditional probability, Bayes theorem basics
- Coordinate Geometry: Straight lines, circles

### MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis April 12 Shift 1 Chemistry

As evident from the MHT CET Paper Analysis, Chemistry is considered the easiest and most scoring subject of the three subjects in Shift 2. The majority of questions in the section were directly based on concepts and theories. They were based mainly on PYQs of NCERT books. Therefore, students were able to score 35-40+ marks if they were well-prepared.

- Physical Chemistry: Chemical Kinetics, Electrochemistry (Nernst equation)
- Inorganic Chemistry: Chemical bonding, coordination compounds, periodic trends
- Organic Chemistry: IUPAC nomenclature, basic reaction mechanisms

### MHT CET Paper Analysis April 12 Shift 1 Physics

In accordance with the MHT CET Analysis, Physics was considered a moderate-level section with a mix of theoretical and formula-based questions. Numerical required high levels of calculations and accurate answers to be attempted by students. Check out the key  topics asked in the physics section

- Current Electricity: Ohm’s Law, circuit-based numericals
- Electrostatics: Electric field, potential, capacitance
- Modern Physics: Photoelectric effect, atomic structure
- Optics: Ray optics (mirror & lens formulas)
- Oscillations & Waves:  SHM, wave optics basics

## MHT CET Counselling and Admission Process

The MHT CET counseling and admission procedure follows the CAP procedure, which allows admission based on the candidate’s percentile score. After the MHT CET result declaration, the candidates have to register online, get documents verified, and select colleges of their choice along with branches.

Seats are allotted in various CAP rounds based on the percentile scored by the candidates, along with their choice of the course. Candidates should accept the allotted seat and report to the college for admission. Proper planning will help ensure that the best college is secured.
