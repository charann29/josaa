West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination is a state-level entrance examination for admissions into undergraduate courses like engineering, pharmacy, architecture, etc. WBJEE is conducted by the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination Board. The frequency of the exam is once a year. WBJEE Consists of three subjects namely physics, chemistry, & Mathematics.

WBJEE Consists of two papers of which Paper 1 consists of mathematics, and Paper 2 consists of physics and chemistry. Candidates with proper schedules for preparation, revision, and mock tests will have an edge over other candidates. While it is important to complete the syllabus to score high in the exam, candidates need to focus more on the important topics from which most of the questions are asked. This article will highlight the important topics for WBJEE to help you prepare effectively.

## WBJEE Important Topics for Physics

Physics accounts for 50 marks in WBJEE Paper 2 and is one of the important subjects to decide your final rank. The physics section is considered moderate by most of the candidates and is a scoring subject if given importance.

## Preparation Tips for WBJEE 2025

WBJEE is scheduled for April 27th, 2025. Candidates should prepare a strict schedule and stick to it to complete the syllabus and revise effectively. Here are some tips to prepare for the WBJEE 2025 exam.

Ensure you get enough sleep, eat nutritious food, and stay hydrated. A healthy mind and body are essential for focused preparation and peak performance on exam day.

- Candidates should prefer focusing on revising the important topics rather than focusing on studying new topics as it consumes a lot of time.
- Questions directly based on the formulas are often asked in the exam and candidates are advised to be well versed with the formulas.
- Attempting the previous year’s question papers will help in understanding the toughness of the questions.
- Simulate exam-like conditions by taking mock tests which helps you boost your confidence and in managing your time
