## JEE Mains 2026 Registration Update

Over the past few years, exam authorities have faced growing challenges related to fake registrations, impersonation, and document tampering. Instances where proxy candidates attempted to sit for exams have raised serious concerns about exam security.

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has therefore prioritised enhancing the JEE Mains registration process to safeguard candidate data and uphold examination transparency. Tightening registration security ensures fair competition and builds trust among test-takers and institutions.

### Live Photo Capture Feature

The newly introduced live photo capture feature during JEE Mains 2026 registration marks a vital shift in digital exam security**. Instead of uploading pre-edited or outdated photos, candidates must now capture a live, real-time image using their device camera.**

This measure ensures that the submitted image accurately reflects the candidate’s current appearance, thereby eliminating the possibility of photo manipulation or impersonation. The feature also helps accurately link biometric and demographic data, ensuring that each candidate’s identity remains unique and verifiable. Check the steps below to know how to capture a live photo.

- After completing the JEE Mains 2026 Registration process and filling out the application form, you will reach the Live Photo Capture section.
- This section appears once you fill in your emergency contact details.

- Connect your webcam to the device on which you are filling the form. Your live image will appear in the “Live Camera” box.
- Ensure there is a light-coloured background and adequate lighting. Your face should be clearly visible (around 80%), including your ears, and you should not wear a mask.
- Click on the “Capture Photograph” button to take your photo.
- If the photo is clear and correct, select “Upload Photograph.” If not, retake the photo and upload it again.
- If your device does not support a webcam, scan the QR code shown on the screen using your mobile phone. Capture and upload your photo from your mobile device. Once uploaded, click “Refresh Captured Photo” on your screen.

**Note:** A timer will start as soon as the live capture section opens. Ensure that you complete the live photo capture before the timer expires.

**Live Camera Instructions**

- Switch on your device’s built-in camera or connect an external webcam.
- Check that the lighting and background are proper.
- Ensure your face is well within the frame and clearly visible.
- When ready, press “Capture Photograph.”
- Check the preview of your captured image. If you are satisfied, submit it.

**Using a Mobile Phone or Tablet**

- Candidates can also capture their live photo for JEE Mains registration using a mobile phone or tablet.
- Scan the QR code shown on the screen to open the photo capture link.
- Use your device’s camera to take the photograph.
- After clicking the photo, upload it through the same tab.
- Once uploaded, select ‘Refresh Captured Photo’.
- The live photo will then appear on the application screen.
- If the image looks correct, proceed to submit the form.

### e-KYC Verification System

The e-KYC (Electronic Know Your Customer) system utilises Aadhaar details, with the candidate’s permission, to automatically fill and verify personal information, such as name, date of birth, gender, and address, during registration. This digital process helps minimise manual errors and makes verification faster. Candidates who do not have an Aadhaar card can still apply by providing valid ID proof and completing physical verification.

## Are students facing difficulties with live photo capture due to Aadhaar e-KYC during JEE Mains 2026?

During [**JEE Mains 2026 Registration**](/blog/jee-main/jee-main-2026-registration/), students’ live photos are captured using the laptop camera and automatically verified with the Aadhaar database to confirm identity. However, many candidates have faced difficulties completing the application form because of this new process.

If the live photo does not match the image in the Aadhaar database, the system stops the application from moving to the next step. This issue has affected several students applying for the JEE Main 2026 exam, as no alternate option has been provided to fix the mismatch.

The only **available solution** is to update the photo linked with the Aadhaar card. Students can visit an Aadhaar update centre to change their photograph. Once the update is completed, their Aadhaar image will match the live photo captured during registration, allowing them to proceed with the application successfully.

## Mistakes To Avoid While While Capturing Live Photo for JEE Mains 2026

The live photo capture during JEE Mains 2026 registration is a mandatory step introduced to ensure the candidate’s identity verification is accurate and secure. The photo captured must meet strict guidelines concerning the environment, lighting, facial visibility, and format to avoid rejection or issues in the exam process.

- **Insufficient Face Visibility:** Ensure your face is about 80% visible, including both ears, without wearing a mask or any facial covering at the time of capturing the live photo.
- **Inadequate Lighting or Background:** Use a well-lit area with a plain, light-colored background (preferably white). Avoid dark, shadowy, or cluttered backgrounds that obscure facial features.
- **Using Incorrect Devices or Camera Setup:** Use a proper webcam or the device camera as prescribed. Avoid using poor-quality cameras or unstable setups that result in blurry or pixelated images.
- **Ignoring Photo Specifications:** The capture must be in JPEG/JPG format with clear visibility and proper resolution. Avoid uploading images that are too small, too large, or not within prescribed dimensions (3.5 cm x 4.5 cm).
- **Wearing Obstructive Accessories:** Do not wear hats, caps, glasses, or other face-covering accessories unless medically necessary (and documentation may be required).
- **Trying to Upload a Selfie or Digital Photo:** The photo must be live captured through the application interface and not uploaded as a selfie or previously captured photo.
- **Not Checking Image Clarity Before Submission:** Before submitting, verify that the image is not blurry, overexposed, or pixelated. Retake the photo if necessary.
- **Not Following Instructions for Signature Upload:** Alongside the live photo, a clear, scanned signature in black ink on white paper must also be uploaded, as per the guidelines. Both files must be in the proper format and adhere to the file size limits.

**Additional Considerations During Live Photo Capture**

- Ensure the device has a good internet connection to prevent upload failures.
- Use the QR code option if capturing the photo from a mobile device, ensuring the camera is steady and focused.
- Do not rush during capture to avoid a blurry picture. Take time to adjust your position, lighting, and expression for an optimal photo.
- The same photo will be used in admit cards and for identity verification during exams, so correctness is critical.
