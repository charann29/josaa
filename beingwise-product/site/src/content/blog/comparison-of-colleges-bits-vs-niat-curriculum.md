## **Does NIAT Skill Map Actually Get Updated Every Six Months?**

NIAT continuously evolves its upskilling content by drawing on real-time insights gathered from its expansive ecosystem of 3,000+ hiring companies, 10,000+ tech professionals, and dedicated in-house research and development. These insights are carefully synthesised into structured reports that are shared with partner universities on a regular basis.

However, it is important to understand the distinction between NIAT’s upskilling content and the university’s academic curriculum. When it comes to the university’s degree program, it is entirely the university’s academic bodies, such as the Board of Studies, that review, approve, and update the curriculum through their own formal processes, in accordance with UGC and AICTE guidelines. The management and delivery of university courses, lectures, seminars, and academic sessions remain fully within the university’s jurisdiction.

Running parallel to this, NxtWave independently conducts its own skill-building sessions, hands-on workshops, practical exercises, and industry-aligned training modules as part of NIAT’s upskilling program. This ensures that students benefit from consistently fresh, market-relevant training content without any dependency on the university’s academic update cycle.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ******************What is the upskilling enrollment fee at NIAT, and how does it compare to the university fee?******************

The combined first-year cost of NIAT’s upskilling enrollment fee and the collaborating university’s fee for the B.Tech CSE program typically ranges between INR 2 Lakhs and INR 2.5 Lakhs. The total 4-year upskilling enrollment fee ranges from INR 8 Lakhs to INR 18 Lakhs.

### 2. **Does NIAT’s upskilling program offer hands-on, practical training?**

Yes. NIAT’s upskilling program places a strong emphasis on practical, project-based learning. Through its industry-aligned training modules and skill map, students work on real-world projects, hands-on coding exercises, and application-based assignments from the very beginning of their program, building the kind of job-ready competencies that today’s top employers actively look for.

### 3. ******************Where can I get the NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies skill map?******************

You can explore NIAT’s detailed skill map by visiting the official NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies website at <https://www.niatindia.com/skillmap>. The skill map outlines the training modules, industry-aligned content, and project-based learning tracks covered across the upskilling program.

### 4. How can I get started with NIAT’s upskilling program enrollment?

[**NIAT Upskilling enrollment 2026**](https://collegedost.in/blog/engineering-colleges/niat-admission-process-2025/) is based on the NAT (Nxtwave Assessment Test) score, followed by a counselling session. All the eligible students can visit the official website of NIAT to register themselves and apply for the test. Following the assessment, a counselling session will walk you through the program details, fee structure, and the collaborating university’s offerings. Please note that the final admission to the B.Tech degree program is governed entirely by the collaborating university’s own eligibility criteria and admission policy, and enrolling with NIAT does not guarantee a university seat.

### 5. What is the difference between NIAT’s IRC and a university degree?

The Industry Ready Certificate (IRC) is a skill-focused credential issued by NxtWave upon successful completion of NIAT’s upskilling program. It validates a student’s hands-on, job-market-ready competencies in areas such as AI, Data Science, and Full Stack Development. It is not equivalent to, nor a replacement for, a university degree or diploma. The B.Tech degree is independently awarded solely by the collaborating university.

### 6. Is NIAT’s skill map the same as BITS Pilani’s curriculum?

No. BITS Pilani’s curriculum is an academic syllabus independently designed and managed by the university, covering a broad range of theoretical and core engineering subjects. NIAT’s skill map is an independently developed, industry-aligned training framework built in consultation with 3,000+ companies and 10,000+ tech professionals. It is not part of any university’s academic curriculum and is updated regularly to reflect evolving industry demands and emerging technology trends.

### 7. Does NIAT replace a university degree?

No. NIAT does not offer or replace a university degree. The B.Tech degree is independently awarded by the collaborating university upon meeting its academic eligibility criteria. NIAT’s upskilling program runs alongside the university degree, and students receive an Industry Ready Certificate (IRC) from NxtWave upon completing the program, which is a skill-focused credential, not equivalent to a university degree or diploma.

### 8. Is NIAT the same as BITS Pilani?

No. BITS Pilani is an autonomous university that independently awards degrees and conducts its own academic programs. NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) is an upskilling program that operates through Training Centres within collaborating universities. While BITS focuses on theoretical academic depth, NIAT’s role is complementary, delivering industry-aligned training modules, project-based learning, and skill-building sessions that prepare students for real-world tech careers alongside their university degree.
