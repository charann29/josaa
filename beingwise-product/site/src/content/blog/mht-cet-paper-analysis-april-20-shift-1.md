Subject-wise MHT CET Paper Analysis April 20 shift 1

### April 20 shift 1 MHT CET Paper Analysis for Math

In the MHT CET Paper Analysis, April 20 shift 1, **mathematics is generally perceived as hard or lengthy.** While some students felt the questions themselves were easy, the time management required for the lengthy calculations made it difficult to complete the section.  Now, take a look at the Key Topics from where questions are asked in this shift

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Topic** | **Key Highlights** |
| Calculus (Integration Focus) | A major portion of the paper was based on Integration and core Calculus concepts |
| Differential Equations | Questions on Order & Degree, Variable Separable form, and finding Particular Solutions |
| Vectors & 3D Geometry | Problems on Volume of a Tetrahedron (given value 11/6 to find x), angle between a line and a plane, and perpendicular vectors using dot product |
| Matrices | Focus on Inverse, Invertibility, Cofactors, and Transpose operations |
| Trigonometry | Questions from inverse trigonometric functions and identities (e.g., cos⁻¹ + sin⁻¹ type expressions) |
| Differentiation | Finding dy/dx for implicit functions using shortcut methods (e.g., result like y/x) |
| Other Topics | Area between parabola & line, Mathematical Logic, and Linear Programming Problems (LPP) |

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for April 20 shift 1 Physics

The MHT CET Analysis suggests the physics section is rated as easy to moderate. The following are the key Topics where questions are asked in this shift

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Topic** | **Key Highlights** |
| Current Electricity & Electrostatics | Basic questions like Ohm’s Law (V = IR) and force on a charge in an electric field (F = qE) |
