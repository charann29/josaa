## Factors Influencing JEE Advanced Cutoff 2026 Scores

JEE Advanced Cutoff Marks for 2026 are not pre-decided and keep changing from year to year based on certain important factors. These factors decide the cutoff marks required for the qualifying criteria as well as admission:

**1. Difficulty Level of the Examination**

If the difficulty level of the paper is high, then the cutoff is usually lowered. If the paper is easy, then the cutoff is usually higher.

**2. Number of Students Taking the Test**

If the number of students is higher, then the cutoff is usually higher. If the number of students is less, then the cutoff is usually lower.

**3. Seats Available in IITs**

The total number of seats provided in IITs is an important factor. High seats could marginally decrease the cutoff, whereas fewer seats result in a higher cutoff.

**4. Performance Level of Candidates**

In case many students perform well and obtain good scores, then the cutoff will be increased. A low level of performance reduces the cutoff.

**5. Reservation Policy**

The reservation process for various categories, such as General, OBC-NCL, EWS, SC, ST, PwD, affects the cutoff for each category.

**6. Previous Year’s Cutoff Trends**

To ensure consistency, authorities frequently examine previous trends when determining the current year’s cutoff.

**7. JoSAA Counselling Trends**

The final admission cutoff is based on seat allocation trends during JoSAA Counselling, which include student preferences for institutes and branches.

## Difference between Qualifying vs Admission Cutoff

It should be remembered here that there is a difference between the qualifying JEE Advanced cutoff and the admission cutoff. While the former is released in the result, the IIT Admission Cutoff is announced later in JoSAA Counseling through opening and closing ranks.

| Feature | Qualifying Cutoff | Admission Cutoff |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Definition | Minimum marks required to pass the exam | Minimum rank required to get admission into IITs |
| Purpose | To include candidates in the rank list | To allot seats in IITs and other institutes |
| Released By | JEE Advanced | JoSAA Counselling |
| Format | Marks (subject-wise + aggregate) | Opening & Closing Ranks |
| When Released | Along with the result | During counselling rounds |
| Category Variation | Yes (General, OBC-NCL, EWS, SC, ST, PwD) | Yes (varies by category, IIT, and branch) |
| Role in Admission | Eligibility step only | Final seat allotment decision |
| Guarantee of IIT Seat | No | Yes (if within cutoff rank) |

**2. What will be the rank requirement for IIT Delhi CSE?**

**3. When will the IIT JEE Advanced result cutoff be released?**

**4. What is the JEE Advanced Cutoff 2026?**

Ans. The JEE Advanced Cutoff 2026 refers to the minimum marks required to qualify for ranking. It includes subject-wise and overall qualifying marks for different categories and is announced with the results.

Ans. No, the qualifying cutoff and admission cutoff are different. The qualifying cutoff is the minimum score required to be included in the rank list, while the admission cutoff refers to the opening and closing ranks for admissions during counselling.
