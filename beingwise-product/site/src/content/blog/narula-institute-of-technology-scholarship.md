Narula Institute of Technology, Agarpara, Kolkata, is a private autonomous engineering college affiliated with MAKAUT, WB and approved by AICTE. For students planning to join B.Tech at NIT, the cost of education is a key factor, and the institute actively lists scholarship options that can reduce the effective fee significantly.

NIT scholarship options for B.Tech students fall into three categories: institute-level schemes run by the college, government scholarship programmes from the West Bengal government and central bodies, and external foundation scholarships. This article covers all three categories. The institute-level schemes are sourced from [www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php](http://www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php). Government and external scholarships are sourced from official notices and PDFs published on the institute website.

## Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: Institute-Level NIT Scholarship for B.Tech Students

The institute runs its own scholarship and freeship schemes for B.Tech students. These are separate from government and external scholarships. The table below lists all institute-level schemes along with their criteria, as published at [www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php](http://www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php).

| Scholarship Name | Criteria | Benefit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sibling Scholarship | Immediate sibling of an existing or former NIT student | 20% on tuition fee |
| Employee Scholarship | Children or immediate siblings of permanent employees on JIS Group payroll | As applicable |
| Alumni Scholarship | Students who completed a degree or diploma from any JIS Group institution and are joining a second programme | 20% on tuition fee |
| Sikh Scholarship | Students from the Sikh community | 50% freeship on tuition fees |
| WBFS (West Bengal Freeship Scheme) | Names sent directly by the Higher Education Department, Govt. of West Bengal | 50% freeship on tuition fees |
| Freeship (Financial Hardship) | Students from poor financial backgrounds; applicable from 2nd semester onwards | Percentage decided case-by-case |
| Tripura Lateral Entry Scholarship | Students from Tripura admitted through Lateral Entry into B.Tech | As applicable |

Students who lose their primary earning member during the course are eligible for 50% freeship on tuition fees as per MAKAUT rules. Freeship cases are reviewed based on academic performance, attendance, and conduct. Scholarship applications are separate from the main admission process. Contact the admission cell at 8902496651 for institute scholarship queries.

## Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: WBFS Scholarship for NIT B.Tech Students

Among all the scholarship options available to NIT B.Tech students, the West Bengal Freeship Scheme (WBFS) stands out for its direct benefit and straightforward process. Unlike most schemes, students do not need to apply through the institute. The freeship is applied automatically for those who qualify.

- The West Bengal Freeship Scheme (WBFS) is one of the most significant scholarship options for NIT B.Tech students from West Bengal. Eligible students receive a 50% freeship on tuition fees under this scheme.
- Student names under WBFS are sent directly by the Higher Education Department, Government of West Bengal, to the institute. Students do not apply to the institute separately for this scheme.
- The scheme covers both General and other category students who meet the state government’s financial criteria. The B.Tech tuition component forms a significant part of the semester fee. A 50% freeship on this amount results in a substantial reduction in total course cost over four years.
- For current eligibility criteria and application procedures, students should check with the West Bengal Higher Education Department or contact the NIT admission cell.

## Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: Scholarship for NIT SC Students and OBC Students

SC/ST and OBC students at Narula Institute of Technology can apply under the OASIS scholarship scheme run by the West Bengal government. OASIS covers post-matric scholarships for students from SC, ST, and OBC categories who are domiciled in West Bengal. Applications are submitted at oasis.wb.gov.in.

For OBC students specifically, both OASIS and the NSP portal list central OBC scholarship schemes. Students should check current eligibility criteria and income limits on the respective portals, as these are updated annually.

## Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: AICTE Pragati and Saksham Scholarship

Narula Institute of Technology has officially notified students about both AICTE schemes. Since the institute is AICTE-approved, all eligible B.Tech students can apply.

The AICTE Pragati Scholarship is for girl students pursuing technical education at AICTE-approved institutions. Fresh applications are submitted at scholarships.gov.in, while renewal applications go through the AICTE web portal. The AICTE Saksham Scholarship is for specially-abled students at AICTE-approved institutions, following the same portal structure.

Both applications must be submitted online only. Offline or incomplete applications are not accepted. The institute verifies submitted applications at its level before they are forwarded to the state nodal officer.

## Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: Swami Vivekananda Merit-cum-Means Scholarship

The SVMCM is a West Bengal government scholarship open to students domiciled in West Bengal on a merit-cum-means basis. Narula Institute of Technology has issued an official notice confirming that the scheme is open for both fresh and renewal applications for NIT students. Fresh and renewal applications are submitted at svmcm.wbhed.gov.in. For current award amounts and eligibility, students should check the SVMCM portal directly, as figures are notified each academic year.

### Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: External Scholarships for NIT B.Tech Students

Beyond institute-level and government schemes, Narula Institute of Technology also lists a set of external foundation scholarships on its official scholarship page. These are independently run programmes from private foundations and central platforms. The Sitaram Jindal Foundation scholarship is the most detailed among these, with specific eligibility criteria, monthly amounts, and a defined application process for West Bengal students.

### Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: Sitaram Jindal Scholarship

The Sitaram Jindal Foundation scholarship is one of the few external scholarships with detailed eligibility criteria available for NIT B.Tech students. It is listed on the official NIT scholarship page at [www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php](http://www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php) and administered through the foundation’s website at [www.sitaramjindalfoundation.org](http://www.sitaramjindalfoundation.org/).

#### Scholarship Amount

B.Tech and engineering students fall under Category E of the Sitaram Jindal Foundation’s classification. The monthly scholarship amounts under this category differ for boys and girls, with an additional allowance for students residing in hostels.

| Category | Scholarship Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Girl Students | ₹1,700 per month |
| Boy Students | ₹1,500 per month |
| Additional Hostel Allowance | ₹1,200 per month (for hostel residents) |

#### Eligibility Criteria

The scholarship has defined conditions around academic performance, family income, age, and existing financial support. Students must meet all of the following criteria to be considered eligible.

| Parameter | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Minimum Marks – Boys | 65% and above in last examination |
| Minimum Marks – Girls | 60% and above in last examination |
| Family Income Limit (Salaried) | Up to ₹4,00,000 per year |
| Family Income Limit (Others) | Up to ₹2,50,000 per year |
| Other Scholarship | Student must not be receiving any other stipend or scholarship |
| Not Applicable For | Final year students (for courses of 3 years or more) |
| Age Limit | Scholarship discontinued after 30 years of age |

#### How to Apply?

West Bengal students fall under the Northern and Eastern India region for the Sitaram Jindal Foundation. Applications must be sent by post to:

**The Trustee, Sitaram Jindal Foundation** “NATURELLE”, 11, Green Avenue, Behind Sector D-III, Vasant Kunj, New Delhi – 110070

Application forms are available free of cost at [www.sitaramjindalfoundation.org](http://www.sitaramjindalfoundation.org/). Printed forms sourced from press shops are not accepted. For queries, students can write to scholarship.del@sitaramjindalfoundation.org.

#### Documents Required

Applicants must submit a set of documents along with the completed application form. The list below covers the standard requirements, with an additional certificate needed for students living in hostels.

- Marksheet of the last examination passed
- SSLC/HSC marksheet
- Income certificate from a competent authority (not older than 3 years)
- Certificate regarding annual fees from the college principal
- Declaration of not receiving any other scholarship or stipend
- For hostel residents: certificate from the warden or accommodation owner

### Narula Institute of Technology Scholarship: Other External Scholarships Listed on NIT Website

The following external scholarship schemes are also listed on the official NIT scholarship page at[www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php](http://www.nit.ac.in/institute-scholarship.php). Eligibility criteria, award amounts, and application deadlines vary each academic year. Students are advised to visit the respective official portals for current information.

- **Priyamvada Birla Scholarship:** A merit-based scholarship from the M.P. Birla Group for students studying in West Bengal. Eligibility details and application procedures are available on the official M.P. Birla Group website.
- **Vidyasaarathi Scholarship:** A scholarship aggregator platform backed by NSDL. It lists multiple scholarship opportunities from corporates and foundations in one place. Students can browse and apply for relevant schemes at vidyasaarathi.co.in.
- **Vidya Lakshmi / Vidyalaxmi Education Loan:** A central government portal that connects students to education loan schemes from multiple banks. Some of these loans are linked to scholarship benefits. Students looking for financial assistance beyond direct scholarships can explore options at vidyalakshmi.co.in.
- **NSP National Scholarship Portal:** A central government portal that aggregates scholarship schemes from central ministries and state governments. Students from SC, ST, OBC, minority, merit-based, and differently-abled categories can find and apply for applicable schemes at scholarships.gov.in.

## Conclusion

Narula Institute of Technology lists over 14 scholarship and freeship options for B.Tech students across institute-level, government, and external categories. The institute-level WBFS gives 50% freeship on tuition. The Sibling and Alumni scholarships give 20% on tuition. The Sikh community scholarship and financial hardship freeship go up to 50%.

For government scholarships, NIT B.Tech students can apply under SVMCM, OASIS (for SC/ST/OBC students), Aikyashree (for minority students), Kanyashree k2 (for girl students), and AICTE Pragati and Saksham through scholarships.gov.in. The Sitaram Jindal Foundation scholarship for B.Tech students provides ₹1,700/month for girls and ₹1,500/month for boys, with an additional ₹1,200/month for hostel residents.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is there any scholarship at Narula Institute of Technology for B.Tech students?

Yes, Narula Institute of Technology offers over 14 scholarship and freeship options, including institute-level schemes and government/external scholarships like SVMCM, OASIS, AICTE Pragati, AICTE Saksham, and Sitaram Jindal Foundation.

### 2. What is the WBFS scholarship for B.Tech students?

The West Bengal Freeship Scheme (WBFS) provides a 50% freeship on tuition fees for eligible students. Beneficiaries are selected by the Higher Education Department, Government of West Bengal.

### 3. Is there a scholarship for OBC students?

Yes, OBC students can apply through the OASIS scholarship portal and also through central government schemes available on the NSP portal.

### 4. Is there a scholarship for SC students?

Yes, SC students can apply for the OASIS post-matric scholarship and also explore centrally-sponsored schemes through the NSP portal.

### 5. What scholarships are available for general category students?

General category students can apply for scholarships like SVMCM and Sitaram Jindal Foundation. Institute-level schemes such as Sibling, Alumni, and Financial Hardship freeships are also available.

### 6. What is the ₹60,000 scholarship for NIT students?

The Swami Vivekananda Merit-cum-Means Scholarship (SVMCM) is often associated with higher annual benefits for engineering students. The exact amount varies each year as per official notifications.

### 7. What is the Sitaram Jindal scholarship amount for B.Tech students?

B.Tech students receive ₹1,700 per month for girls and ₹1,500 per month for boys. Hostel residents get an additional ₹1,200 per month. Eligibility includes academic performance and income criteria.

### 8. What is the highest package of Narula Institute of Technology?

The highest package for the 2024 batch was INR 47.88 LPA, as per official WBJEEB placement data.
