KEA declared the KCET Results 2026 on June 6, 2026. Along with the result, KEA released the official KCET 2026 Toppers List across all streams.

The numbers from this year’s Karnataka Common Entrance Test tell an interesting story. **Of the 3,09,014 candidates who appeared for KCET 2026, a total of 2,92,782 qualified.** **That is a qualification rate of over 94%.** In simple terms, **roughly 9 out of every 10 candidates who sat for the exam are now eligible for admission.** Despite that high eligibility rate, the competition for top seats remains extremely narrow. The top 1,000 engineering ranks are separated by very small score margins, which means even a single mark can shift a candidate’s rank by hundreds of positions.

**Tanisha Karthik of RV PU College, Jayanagar, Bengaluru secured Rank 1 in the engineering stream.** Navana Gopi of Base PU College, Bengaluru topped three separate streams. In the Engineering stream, 2,82,603 out of 3,09,014 appeared candidates were declared eligible for admission.

## KCET 2026 Toppers List: What the Qualification Rate Actually Means

A 94.7% qualification rate can be misread. Qualifying in KCET does not mean getting into a top college. It simply means a candidate is eligible to participate in counselling. The actual seat allotment depends on rank.

In Engineering, only the top few thousand ranks can expect seats in top government and autonomous colleges like RVCE, MSRIT, BMS, NMIT, and PES University Bengaluru. Based on historical KCET cutoff data, a rank below 5,000 in the General Merit category is typically needed for CSE or ECE at premier colleges. A rank between 5,000 and 20,000 still gives access to mid-tier government colleges and reputed private colleges.

Of the 2,82,603 candidates eligible in Engineering, a rough breakdown based on historical rank-to-marks patterns looks like this:

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |

| 20,001 – 50,000 | 100 – 130 | Regional and district-level government colleges |
| 50,001+ | Below 100 | Remaining eligible candidates, limited top-college options |

Note: These score ranges are indicative. Official cutoffs will be released after each round of KCET 2026 Counselling.

### About the KCET 2026 Exam

KEA conducted KCET 2026 in offline, pen-and-paper mode on April 22, 23, and 24, 2026. The exam covered Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (for engineering) and Biology (for medical streams). Each correct answer carried one mark. There was no negative marking.

A total of 11,51,367 OMR sheets across all subjects were uploaded to the KEA website within five days of the exam for public review. The final answer key was released after the challenge window closed. KEA then compiled PUC marks, ran technical scrutiny, and declared the result on June 6.

How to Check KCET Result 2026: Visit cetonline.karnataka.gov.in or karresults.nic.in. Click on the UGCET 2026 Result link. Enter your application number and date of birth. Download your scorecard.

## KCET 2026 Rank Card: What It Contains

The KCET Rank Card 2026 available on the official KEA portal contains the following details.

- Candidate’s name and application number
- Subject-wise scores (Physics, Chemistry, Maths or Biology)
- CET total score
- PUC / qualifying exam marks
- Combined merit percentage
- Stream-wise rank
- Category and sub-category rank

Candidates need their rank card for document verification and for option entry during KCET 2026 Counselling.

### KCET 2026 Counselling: What Comes Next

KEA has announced that KCET 2026 Counselling will begin on June 10, 2026. Mock results will be announced four days after the rank list is published. The counselling process involves document verification, option entry and locking, mock allotment, final seat allotment, fee payment, and reporting to the allotted institution.

Candidates who qualified in Engineering, Pharmacy, Agriculture, Veterinary Sciences, Nursing, BNYS, and related courses are eligible to participate in counselling based on their rank and category.
