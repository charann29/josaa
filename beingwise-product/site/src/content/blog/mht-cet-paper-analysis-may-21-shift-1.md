## May 21 Shift 1 MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis 2026

The subject-wise MHT CET 2026 exam analysis not only helps you be aware of the difficulty level, but it also allows you to identify the MHT CET chapter wise weightage​ and the most crucial areas.

### MHT CET 2026 Exam Analysis for Chemistry Section

Following the trends, the Chemistry Section was the easiest and most scoring part in the MHT CET May 21 Question Paper. As per the MHT CET Paper Analysis.  There were many questions based directly on NCERT concepts. Thus, students believed that chemistry improved their overall attempts and accuracy.

The physical chemistry numericals were easy to moderate, whereas the organic chemistry had reaction-based direct questions. Key topics where questions are asked in this shift

- Coordination Compounds
- Chemical Kinetics
- Thermodynamics
- Biomolecules
- Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers
- p-Block Elements
- Polymers
- Surface Chemistry
- Electrochemistry

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Physics Section

The Physics Section was also considered moderate. The numerical questions were direct, but proper formula use was needed. A majority of questions were formula-based, with some conceptual applications. Students who had solved PYQs found the physics section easy. Some students found the Physics section a little long due to its calculative aspect. Key topics where questions are asked in this shift

- Current Electricity
- Electrostatics
- Magnetic Effects of Current
- Modern Physics
- Semiconductor Devices
- SHM & Waves
- Thermodynamics
- Ray Optics
- Rotational Motion

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Maths Section

Again, the mathematics paper was the lengthiest section, as per the MHT CET Paper Analysis. Though the paper comprised questions based mostly on formulas, the calculation involved was time-consuming. Calculus had more importance than other topics; however, look at the key themes where questions are asked during this shift.

- Integration
- Differentiation
- Probability
- Matrices & Determinants
- Vector Algebra
- 3D Geometry
- Application of Derivatives
- Line & Plane
