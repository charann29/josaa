## Student’s Reaction to JEE Main 2026 23 Jan Shift 2 Paper Analysis

After the shift is over, we have talked to the students to know their reaction to the JEE mains question paper of Jan 23 evening shift.  Many students noted that the paper was “Moderate to Hard” and one of the most difficult shifts observed thus far. Take a quick look at the JEE Mains students’ reaction shift 2

- Several students spent too much time on Physics, with one stating it took more than an hour. Another kid spent 45 minutes on Physics, just to answer 7-8 questions. A third student took an hour to answer only nine questions.
- One student noted that Maths felt easier than Physics during this shift, as they were able to complete 11 questions in Section A.
- One student attempted 11 Chemistry, 9 Physics, and just 5-6 Maths, labelling Physics as the “toughest”.
- Students stated that Chemistry was “badly designed” for the time. One student mentioned that they met four questions with five statement possibilities (A, B, C, D, E) and three assertion-reasoning questions, which slowed them down significantly.
- Many students reported between 26 and 40 total questions due to the unexpected complexity of the Physics and Chemistry formats.
- One student reported that he attempted 18 questions in Chemistry, 15 in Physics, and 15 in Maths, for a total of 48 questions, which was deemed a successful attempt for this shift.

The JEE Main paper pattern comprises three subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Each subject consists of 20 multiple-choice questions and 5 numerical questions. The paper is delivered online (CBT).
