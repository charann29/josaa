## What is NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies (NIAT)?

NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) is an upskilling program established in 2023, dedicated to equipping students with industry-relevant skills in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Data Science. NIAT conducts its upskilling programs across more than 20 universities across various states in India, including Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Delhi NCR, and more.

NIAT has a dedicated placement support team that helps students explore job opportunities. So far, NIAT has partnered with 3000+ companies to connect students with potential career paths. NIAT also has a team of world-class Trainers and Mentors from leading companies such as Microsoft, Google, and Amazon, as well as top IITs. NIAT also hosts masterclasses led by CEOs and CXOs to strengthen students’ conceptual understanding and technical skills.

## Does NIAT’s Skill Map Get Updated Every Six Months?

NIAT gathers real-time industry insights from a network of 3000+ companies, 10,000+ tech professionals, and in-house research. These insights are compiled into structured reports and shared with the respective partnered university on a regular basis. However, it is entirely the university’s decision to review and update its academic curriculum through its own formal bodies, such as the Board of Studies, in accordance with their internal processes.
However, NIAT continuously updates its own training modules, skill-building sessions, workshops, and industry-aligned content to ensure students are always learning skills relevant to the current job market.

## Who are the Trainers at NIAT?

NIAT has its own Trainers, Mentors, and Success Coaches who deliver the upskilling program. They are not part of the university’s academic staff or faculty. At NIAT, Student Mentors, known as **SuperMentors**, come from top-tier companies like Google, Amazon, Meta, Microsoft, and Apple. These industry experts provide invaluable guidance to students, helping them not only master technical skills but also develop an innovative and leadership-oriented mindset. Their real-world experience ensures that students gain insights into industry expectations and career-building strategies.

#### Q6. Does NIAT provide a degree or diploma?

No. NIAT is an upskilling program and does not offer any degree or diploma. Students earn their academic degree from their respective collaborating university. NIAT awards an IRC that reflects a student’s industry-relevant skills and achievements in the upskilling program.

#### Q7. What kind of Learning Module is covered under NIAT’s upskilling program?

NIAT’s program consists of learning modules, training modules, industry-aligned content, project-based learning, and a structured skill map. This content is not part of any university curriculum or academic syllabus; it is designed purely to build industry-relevant competencies alongside a student’s university education.

#### Q8. What is the difference between NAT and a university entrance exam?

NAT is an internal assessment conducted by NIAT as part of the enrollment process for the upskilling program. It is completely separate from any entrance examination conducted by the university. Each university independently manages its own admission and entrance process.

#### Q9. What fees does NIAT charge?

NIAT charges a program fee (also referred to as an upskilling fee or enrollment fee) for its upskilling courses. NIAT does not charge any admission fee, tuition fee, semester fee, or seat reservation fee, as these are entirely handled by the respective university. Students need to pay a fee range of INR 8 to 18 Lakhs for a 4-year duration.

#### Q10. Does NIAT’s upskilling skill map cover the B.Tech curriculum?

No. NIAT’s skill map is not designed to cover or replicate any university’s B.Tech curriculum. Since NIAT is an upskilling program, its learning modules and training modules are exclusively focused on building industry-relevant skills and real-world competencies. The academic curriculum remains entirely under the purview of the respective collaborating universities.
