## Normalization Procedure Based on Percentile Score

The normalization process is a standardized method used to compare the performance of candidates who appear for exams held in multiple shifts. This approach aligns with the methods followed in other major educational selection tests in India. For shift-wise normalization, the NTA applies the percentile equivalence method.

NTA usually conducts JEE Main 2026 exam in different shifts. Its quiet possible that, a set of student will get a tougher question paper in a particular shift than the other one. So, NTA uses “Normalization procedure based on Percentile Score” to ensure that candidates are neither benefitted nor disadvantaged due to the difficulty level of the Examination.

**JEE Main Raw Score**

**Percentile Scores** represent a candidate’s performance compared to others who appeared in the same exam shift. The raw marks obtained by each candidate are converted into a scale ranging from 0 to 100 for every shift.

A Percentile Score shows the percentage of candidates who scored equal to or below the marks of a particular student in that specific shift. Hence, the highest scorer of each shift always secures a Percentile Score of 100. **Scores between the highest and lowest marks are proportionately assigned appropriate percentile values.**

**The final merit list is prepared after these percentile scores are treated as the normalized scores of the examination**. Percentile scores are calculated up to seven decimal places to minimize tie situations and ensure accuracy.

## Step-By-Step JEE Main Normalisation Procedure And Preparation Of Result

Normalization is a statistical process used in competitive exams conducted in multiple shifts to ensure fairness across sessions that may have varying difficulty levels. The step-wise method below outlines how candidates are distributed across shifts and how their results are processed and normalized to maintain equality in evaluation.

**Step 1: Allocation of Examinees into Shifts**
The JEE Main 2026 exam will be held in two shifts: Day 1 Shift 1 and Day 1 Shift 2.  Candidates are assigned to different shifts randomly to ensure that each shift includes nearly the same number of participants
This randomized allocation prevents bias and ensures a fair representation of candidates from across the country.

**Step 2: Result Compilation for Each Shift**
For every shift, results are generated in two forms:

- Raw scores obtained by candidates
- Percentile scores based on these raw scores

The percentile score for each examinee in a particular shift, denoted as TP1, is calculated using the **total raw marks of that candidate in relation to the overall performance of all candidates in that shift**.

**Step 3: Compilation of NTA Score and Result Preparation**

The percentile scores obtained for each shift are combined to create the NTA scores. These scores serve as the basis for preparing the final results and determining further processes such as merit ranking and seat allocation.

If the percentile values corresponding to the qualifying (cut-off) marks vary across different shifts, the lowest percentile among them will be taken as the qualifying benchmark for that particular category across all shifts.

## JEE Main 2026 Tie Breaking Criteria

In cases where two or more candidates secure identical total NTA scores, the tie will be settled using the following sequence of criteria:

- Higher NTA score in Mathematics.
- If still tied, higher NTA score in Physics.
- If the tie persists, higher NTA score in Chemistry.
- Candidate with a lower ratio of incorrect to correct responses across all subjects.
- If required, candidate with a lower ratio of incorrect to correct answers in Mathematics.
- Next, candidate with a lower ratio of incorrect to correct responses in Physics.
- Thereafter, candidate with a lower ratio of incorrect to correct answers in Chemistry.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ****What is JEE Main Marks vs Percentile 2026?****

JEE Main Marks vs Percentile 2026 represents the relationship between candidates’ raw marks and their percentile scores after normalization. It helps students estimate their rank and admission chances in top engineering colleges like NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.

### ************2. ******How is the JEE Main Normalisation Procedure takes place?******************

The JEE Main normalisation procedure is used by the NTA to adjust scores across multiple shifts of the exam. It ensures fairness by converting raw marks into percentile scores, comparing a candidate’s performance relative to others in the same shift.

### **3.********What is the JEE Main Normalisation Formula?**********

The JEE Main normalisation formula calculates the percentile score as:
Percentile = (100 × Number of candidates who scored equal to or below a candidate in a shift) ÷ Total candidates in that shift. This helps in preparing the final NTA score accurately.

### 4. ********Why is normalization important in JEE Main 2026?********

Since JEE Main 2026 is conducted in multiple shifts with varying difficulty levels, normalization ensures no student is advantaged or disadvantaged based on the paper’s difficulty. It provides a fair comparison across all shifts.

### 5. What is a good score in JEE Main 2026?

A good score in JEE Main 2026 is generally between 250 and 279 marks, which corresponds to a 97–98.9 percentile. Such scores usually qualify for top NITs and IIITs, especially in competitive branches like Computer Science or Mechanical Engineering.

### 6. How many marks are needed for 99 percentile in JEE Main 2026?

To score around the 99 percentile, candidates should aim for 280-300 marks in JEE Main 2026. This range places students in the top 1,000 ranks, making them eligible for admission to top NITs and some IITs through JEE Advanced.

### 7. What is the difference between JEE Main Marks and Percentile?

Marks are the actual raw scores obtained by candidates.
Percentile reflects how a candidate performed relative to others in their shift after normalization.
A higher percentile means better performance even if raw marks differ across shifts.

### 8. How does NTA prepare the JEE Main result using percentile scores?

NTA compiles percentile scores from all shifts and combines them to create the final NTA score. These normalized scores are used to determine ranks, merit lists, and eligibility for JEE Advanced or college admissions.

### 9. Can I get admission with a low percentile in JEE Main 2026?

Yes. Even with a lower JEE Main percentile, students can secure seats in several NITs with lower cutoffs, state government colleges, and private universities through CSAB and JoSAA counselling rounds.
