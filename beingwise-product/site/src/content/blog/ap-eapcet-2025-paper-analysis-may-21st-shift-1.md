## Student Reviews For AP EAMCET 2025 May 21st Shift 1 Exam

The first shift of the AP EAMCET 2025, held on May 21, was considered moderately challenging. There was a noticeable increase in application-oriented questions in both Mathematics and Physics, while Chemistry provided a bit of relief amidst the more demanding sections. Successfully navigating the exam required effective time management and a strong understanding of concepts. Many students noted that although the paper remained within the expected syllabus, the format necessitated a balanced approach to both problem-solving speed and accuracy.

### Physics

Difficulty Level: Moderate to Difficult
Feedback:
The Physics section was reported to be intellectually demanding and required solid understanding of fundamental principles. Questions from areas like Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Oscillations, and Electrostatics involved layered calculations and multi-concept reasoning. Several problems were time-consuming due to the depth of conceptual application needed. Students found that while the questions aligned with the syllabus, the approach needed to solve them was more analytical, making time management a challenge.

### Chemistry

Difficulty Level: Easy to Moderate
Feedback:
Chemistry was the most manageable section in this shift. Questions were largely direct and closely aligned with textbook concepts, especially from NCERT. Physical Chemistry included simple numerical problems, while Organic and Inorganic Chemistry had predictable patterns and familiar reaction mechanisms. Most students felt confident with this section as it was less time-consuming and more scoring compared to Physics and Mathematics.

### Mathematics

Difficulty Level: Moderate to Difficult
Feedback:
The Mathematics section posed significant challenges due to lengthy and tricky questions. Topics such as Calculus (including Integration and Application of Derivatives), Coordinate Geometry, and Probability required precise logic and speed. Many problems involved multi-step reasoning, making it essential to maintain accuracy under time pressure. Students highlighted the need for strong practice and clarity in fundamental algebraic techniques to perform well.

## AP EAMCET 2025 Result Details

The AP EAMCET (AP EAPCET) 2025 results are expected to be released in June 2025, following the conclusion of the engineering exams held from May 21 to May 27, 2025. Before the results, the preliminary answer key will be published on May 28, allowing candidates to raise objections until June 1. The final answer key is expected around June 5. Once results are declared, candidates can check their scores and download their rank cards from the official website [cets.apsche.ap.gov.in/](http://cets.apsche.ap.gov.in/)  using their hall ticket and registration numbers. The scorecard will display key details such as the candidate’s name, subject-wise marks, total score, qualifying status, rank, and category. Based on the results, qualified students will be called for the counselling process, anticipated to begin in July 2025. During counselling, candidates must undergo document verification and choose their preferred colleges and courses. Seat allotment will follow based on rank, category, and availability.

## AP EAMCET Counselling

The **AP EAMCET 2025 counselling process** is conducted online by APSCHE to facilitate admissions into engineering, agriculture, and pharmacy courses across participating colleges in Andhra Pradesh. After the results are declared, eligible candidates must register on the official website and pay the counselling fee. The next step is **document verification**, where candidates upload scanned copies of their required documents such as the AP EAMCET rank card, hall ticket, 10th and 12th marksheets, caste certificate (if applicable), and income certificate. Once verified, candidates can proceed to **web options entry**, where they select their preferred colleges and courses in order of priority. After the choice filling deadline, the system will process the data and release the **seat allotment results** based on the candidate’s rank, category, and preferences. Candidates must then download the **allotment order**, report to the allotted college, and complete the **admission formalities** within the given timeframe.
