## JEE Advanced Exam Pattern (Paper 1 & 2)

JEE Advanced is meant to assess a candidate’s level of conceptual understanding as well as their ability to solve advanced problems. For 2026, the JEE Advanced exam pattern will have all three subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, which will likely have equal weightage for each of the three subjects.

As per the JEE Advanced Exam Pattern, each paper may have multiple sections, and each section will have a different question type and marking scheme:

Section I – MCQ with one answer

Section II – MCQ with more than one answer

Section III – Numerical Answer Type (NAT)

Section IV – Matching or comprehension-based questions

This  JEE Advanced Exam pattern aligns with the distribution of marks for all sections because both theoretical and analytical skills will be tested. The different marking schemes (i.e., partial, negative) emphasise that accuracy will be necessary to achieve a good score across all sections when attempting the maximum marks for JEE Advanced.

## JEE Advanced 2026 Exam Mode

The JEE Advanced 2026 exam mode is consistent with previous years, allowing applicants to become accustomed to it. **The test will be administered in a Computer-Based Test (CBT) style,** often known as the JEE Advanced CBT pattern. It provides a consistent and smooth experience across all testing centres via an online mode.

## JEE Advanced 2026 Paper Medium

Candidates can register for the JEE Advanced exam in their preferred language, **either English or Hind**i, ensuring that the exam is accessible to students with diverse linguistic backgrounds.

## JEE Advanced Question Paper Duration

**According to the JEE Advanced Exam Pattern, each Paper is typically completed in three hours.** However, PwD candidates are allowed an extra hour for each paper. Such students will get four hours for each paper to create a comfortable and fair test atmosphere.

Yes, JEE Advanced typically includes negative marking for certain questions. Candidates should carefully read the instructions provided in the question paper before answering.
