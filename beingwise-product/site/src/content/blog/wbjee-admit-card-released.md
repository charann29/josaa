## WBJEE Dress Code and Guidelines

Candidates appearing for WBJEE must follow a simple dress code and strict exam-day protocols to ensure a smooth and fair examination process. They should avoid wearing heavy clothing, accessories, or prohibited items and reach the centre well in advance.

Here are the [Dress code](/blog/wbjee/wbjee-dress-code/) guidelines

- Wear light and comfortable clothes (avoid bulky or layered clothing).
- Simple footwear only — avoid boots, high heels, or shoes with thick soles.
- Do not wear any jewellery, including rings, chains, earrings, or bracelets.
- Watches and electronic accessories like smartwatches or fitness bands are strictly prohibited.
- Avoid wearing clothes with pockets that may appear suspicious or could hide items.
- Candidates with medical needs (e.g., insulin pumps) should inform authorities in advance.

## Rules on Filling the OMR Sheet

The WBJEE Exam is in pen-and-paper mode, so candidates need to follow the instructions to complete the OMR sheet. Here are the [WBJEE rules](https://collegedost.in/blog/wbjee/wbjee-rules-and-regulations/) for filling the OMR Sheet.

- Submit the OMR sheet to the invigilator before leaving the exam hall.
- As instructed in the exam guidelines, use only a black or blue ballpoint pen—do not use gel or ink pens.
- Fill in the question booklet number, roll number, and other required details carefully in the spaces provided.
- Darken the correct circle completely — do not tick, cross, or partially fill the bubble.
- Mark only one option per question — multiple markings will be treated as incorrect even if one option is correct.
- Do not fold, staple, or tear the OMR sheet — any damage may lead to disqualification.
- Avoid overwriting or correction fluid — once marked, an answer cannot be changed.
- Match the question number carefully with the corresponding circle on the OMR to avoid mismatches.
- Leave the OMR sheet neat and clean — avoid any stray marks as it can affect scanning.
- Sign the OMR sheet where required, in the candidate’s designated space.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. **************What should I do if there is an error in my admit card details?**************

Contact the WBJEEB helpline immediately for corrections before the exam date.
Helpline: **1800 1234 782( Extn. No-2)**

### 2. **********Do I need to carry a photo ID along with the admit card?**********

Yes, it is mandatory to carry a valid photo ID (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, etc.) along with the printed admit card.

### **3. **Can I show the digital copy of the admit card on my phone?****

No, only a **printed copy** of the admit card is accepted at the exam center.

### 4. ******What should I do if I can’t download my admit card?******

Check your login details, browser compatibility, or try again later. If the problem persists, contact the WBJEEB support.

### ********5.****Do I need to preserve the admit card after the exam?************

Yes, keep it safe even after the exam — it may be required during counselling and admission.

### 6. **Will the admit card be sent by post?**

No, the WBJEE admit card is available **only in online mode** and will **not be sent via post or email**.
