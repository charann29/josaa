## **CAT Score Vs Percentile** Overview

CAT score vs percentile 2025 serves as the foundation of MBA programme admissions throughout India. Your preparation strategy will work better when you understand these metrics well.

### **CAT Score vs Percentile Calculation**

A well-structured process determines your standing among test-takers through the calculation of your CAT score. The raw score for CAT 2025 follows a specific marking scheme. Each correct answer earned +3 marks, while incorrect MCQ attempts lost 1 mark. Non-MCQs didn’t have negative marking.

Raw scores then go through a mathematical transformation to give all exam slots a fair chance. Here’s the percentile calculation formula:

**Percentile = [(N – R)/N] × 100**

N shows the total test-takers, while R represents your rank based on the scaled score. To cite an instance, see a case where 300,000 candidates took the CAT 2024. With a rank of 6,000, your percentile would be 98.

### **CAT Score Vs Percentile: What is CAT Percentile?**

Your relative performance against other candidates shows up in CAT percentile. The score tells you what percentage of test-takers scored equal to or below you. A 99 percentile means you outperformed 99% of all candidates.

This metric proves valuable because it:

- Makes fair comparisons possible across exam slots
- Acts as the main criterion to shortlist candidates
- Gives a standard measure despite of the exam’s difficulty level

The percentile calculation happens in these steps:

1. Find the total number of CAT candidates (N)
2. Get a rank (r) from scaled scores
3. Use the formula: P = ((N-r)/N) x 100
4. Round the percentile to two decimal points

### **CAT Score Vs Percentile: Normalisation Method**

Normalisation creates an adjusted score called the scaled score. CAT happens in multiple slots with different difficulty levels. The normalisation process gives everyone a level playing field.

Slot discrepancies get handled through normalisation. Without normalisation, candidates from different slots would face unfair evaluation.

## **How to Calculate CAT Percentile**

The mechanics of CAT score calculation give valuable insights to students who want top percentiles. Several stages work together to evaluate candidates fairly across different exam slots.

### **Marking Scheme and Raw Score Formula**

A clear marking pattern serves as the foundation of CAT’s scoring system. Students earn +3 marks for each correct answer, while wrong MCQ attempts cost them -1 mark. Type In The Answer (TITA) questions don’t have negative marking, which makes them valuable from a strategy perspective.

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Question Type** | **Correct Answer** | **Incorrect Answer** | **Unattempted** |
| MCQs | +3 marks | -1 mark | 0 marks |
| TITA | +3 marks | 0 marks | 0 marks |

Raw score calculation follows a simple formula:

**Raw Score = (3 × Number of correct answers) – (1 × Number of incorrect MCQ answers)**

### **Scaled Score and Normalisation Process**

CAT takes place in multiple slots with different difficulty levels, so normalisation becomes vital. The official process works like this:

Each slot’s statistical parameters get calculated first. These include mean scores (M1, M2, M3) and standard deviation (S1, S2, S3). VARC, DILR, and QA sections each get their own calculations.

The process also looks at the mean raw score of each slot’s top 0.1% candidates. This data helps compare performance across different exam sessions accurately.

A normalisation formula then uses these statistical values to adjust raw scores. This ensures the allocated slot doesn’t give any unfair advantage or disadvantage to candidates.

### **Slot-wise difficulty adjustment**

Slot-wise adjustments tackle variations in exam complexity. Small differences in difficulty show up between slots despite careful planning.

The normalisation algorithm spots these differences effectively. CAT 2024’s first slot was easier than others, which led to a 4-mark reduction in raw scores. Other slots received a 3-mark boost.

Mathematical transformations help compare performance across slots fairly. The statistical method looks at both average and high-end performance metrics. This ensures fairness at every score level.

Raw score calculation, scaled score determination, and slot-wise adjustment are the life-blood of CAT’s evaluation system. These three steps directly affect how cat scores relate to percentiles.
