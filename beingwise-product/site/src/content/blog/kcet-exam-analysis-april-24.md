### KCET 2026 Exam Pattern

Each paper consists of 60 MCQs to be attempted in 80 minutes. The marking scheme awards plus 1 for every correct answer. There is no negative marking for wrong answers. Total marks across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics come to 180. Biology is evaluated separately for medical admissions. KCET weightage in the final rank calculation is 50 percent. The remaining 50 percent comes from 2nd PUC (Class 12) theory marks in the relevant subjects. Understanding KCET weightage is essential before estimating rank from raw scores alone.

## KCET Exam Analysis April 24 Mathematics Paper Review

KCET 2026 paper analysis for Maths is not about conceptual difficulty. Questions were within reach. The problem was that solving them properly required multi-step working. With 60 questions and 80 minutes, many students ran out of time before they ran out of knowledge.

Probability was the section that frustrated students the most. Multiple students described it as tedious and time-consuming rather than difficult. It slowed candidates down and cut into their target scores. Calculus gave some relief. Integration questions were structured in a way that allowed students to spot the answer halfway through the solution.

The paper leaned heavily on Class 12 topics. Compared to KCET 2025, the 2026 Maths paper was lengthier and more execution-heavy. Several students said it felt like a board exam in terms of the working each question demanded.

### KCET Exam Analysis April 24: Memory-Based Maths Questions

These are recalled questions compiled from student feedback and coaching institute sources on exam day.

**Calculus**

1. Find the derivative of sin x with respect to log x, and of log x minus 1/x. (Parametric differentiation)
2. Evaluate: integral of dx divided by x(x to the power 10 + 1). (Integration using partial fractions)
3. Evaluate: integral of e raised to x multiplied by (tan x + sec squared x) dx. (Standard e raised to x [f(x) + f'(x)] form)
4. Evaluate: integral of 1 divided by (x minus 3)(x minus 7) dx. (Partial fractions, direct)
5. Identify whether f(x) = tan x minus x is increasing or decreasing. (Function analysis)

**Algebra and Matrices**

6. For a 3 by 3 matrix A where the absolute value of A equals 5, find the absolute value of adj(A). (Matrix property)
7. A relation defined by a = b- 2 where b is greater than 6. Identify its properties. (Reflexive, symmetric, transitive check)

**Probability**

8. Conditional probability question involving dice outcomes. (P of A given B type)
9. Set theory-based probability involving cricket and tennis preferences within a group. (Inclusion-exclusion using Venn diagram)

**Geometry and Others**

10. Find the latus rectum of the ellipse x squared + 3y squared = 12. (Conic sections, standard form conversion)
11. Find the degree of a given differential equation. (Direct definition application)
12. Counting problem involving arrangement of words or numbers. (Permutations and Combinations)

## KCET Exam Analysis April 24 Biology Paper Review

Biology in KCET Exam Analysis April 24 stayed close to NCERT. It rewarded students who read the textbook carefully, including boxed text and side notes. The afternoon session ended with most students feeling relieved after the morning’s Maths paper.

Questions were mostly factual. Some application-level recall came from Biotechnology and Evolution. Students who relied on short notes without going back to NCERT found roughly 6 to 10 questions harder to answer.

### KCET Biology Paper Pattern

- Class 12 chapters made up approximately 70 to 75 percent of questions.
- Reproduction units (Human Reproduction, Reproductive Health, Reproduction in Organisms) were most heavily represented.
- Molecular Biology, Genetics, and Evolution together covered 15 to 18 questions.
- Biotechnology chapters contributed 5 to 6 questions. Some were process-specific.
- Class 11 topics (Cell Biology, Morphology, Plant Physiology) filled the remaining 25 to 30 percent.
- Diagram-based questions from Human Physiology appeared as MCQ hooks.

### KCET Exam Analysis April 24: Memory-Based Biology Questions

1. Which enzyme unwinds the DNA double helix during replication? (Helicase, Molecular Basis of Inheritance)
2. What is the role of the corpus luteum after fertilisation? (Human Reproduction)
3. Which type of placenta is found in humans? (Reproductive Structures)
4. During which phase of meiosis does crossing over occur? (Prophase I, Cell Division)
5. Match the organism to its specific mode of reproduction. (Reproduction in Organisms)
6. Which of the following is NOT a greenhouse gas? (Environmental Biology, factual)
7. Humulin is produced using which host organism in recombinant DNA technology? (E. coli, Biotechnology Applications)

### How KCET Rank Is Calculated?

KCET marks vs rank cannot be read from entrance test score alone. KEA uses a composite formula.

- 50 per cent weightage: KCET entrance test marks (out of 180)
- 50 per cent weightage: 2nd PUC (Class 12) theory marks in PCM

Two students with identical KCET scores can land at very different ranks based on board performance. Every percentage point drop in board marks is roughly equal to losing 1.5 composite marks.

## How to Get Rank Under 1000 in KCET

Getting a rank under 1000 in KCET needs a strong entrance score, good board marks, and smart exam strategy. Based on exam trends and student patterns, here is what matters most.

### Composite Score Target

To target a top rank, students need a strong combined performance in KCET and board exams.

- Aim for **130 to 150+ KCET marks** with **90%+ in 2nd PUC PCM**.
- If board marks are around **80 to 85%**, the KCET score may need to reach **155 to 160** to balance it.

### What This Year’s KCET Paper Means for Top Ranks

KCET 2026 paper, especially Maths, was tougher than last year. This may lower high scores overall and impact rank trends.

- Harder Maths may reduce the score ceiling.
- Students who did well in Maths may gain a bigger rank advantage.
- Scoring around **48 correct in Maths** could be a strong edge this year.

### KCET Exam Strategies That Top-1000 Rank Holders Follow

Top rankers usually focus on speed, accuracy, and smart question selection. These strategies can help:

- Attempt Biology and Chemistry fully before opening Maths. Both are more time-efficient.
- In Maths, pick all direct questions first. Return to Probability and lengthy integration problems only after simpler questions are done.
- No single question should get more than 90 seconds on the first pass. Skip and return.
- With no negative marking, never leave an OMR bubble blank. A guess has a 25 percent chance of being right. A blank guarantees zero.
- Accuracy above 90 percent on attempted questions matters more than total attempt count.
