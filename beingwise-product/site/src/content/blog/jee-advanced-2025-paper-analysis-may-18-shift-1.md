## JEE Advanced 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

Given below are a few JEE Advanced 2025 exam day guidelines that students need to follow.

- Carry a printed copy of your JEE Advanced 2025 admit card. Entry without it is strictly prohibited.
- Bring a government-issued photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN card, Driving License, Passport, or School ID).
- Reach the exam center **at least 90 minutes before** the start of the exam.

- Simple wristwatch (digital watches not allowed)
- Listen carefully to the instructions provided by the invigilators.

- Do not attempt to log in until instructed.
- Use only the on-screen calculator provided during the exam.

- Any malpractice will result in disqualification.
- No re-entry is allowed once you leave the exam hall.
- Maintain silence and avoid any suspicious behaviour.

## JEE Advanced 2025 Dress Code

Candidates appearing for JEE Advanced 2025 must follow a strict dress code to ensure security and fairness during the exam. The attire should be simple, and accessories should be avoided. Here are a few essential points for the JEE Advanced 2025 Dress code.

- Wear plain, light-colored T-shirts or shirts without elaborate designs or pockets.
- Only half-sleeve tops are permitted; full sleeves are strictly not allowed
- Avoid layered clothing like jackets, coats, hoodies, or shawls
- Clothing must not have big buttons, metallic threads, or inner pockets
- Open footwear like slippers or sandals is allowed.
- Shoes, boots, and closed footwear are not permitted.
- Watches (digital or analogue) are banned inside the exam hall.
- Jewellery—including earrings, rings, bracelets, chains—is prohibited.
- Carry only essential items: admit card, ID proof, and stationery.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ********How was the JEE Advanced 2025 Paper?********

The **JEE Advanced 2025** exam was reportedly of **moderate to high difficulty**. While **Paper 1** was relatively manageable with straightforward questions in Chemistry and familiar problem types in Maths and Physics, **Paper 2** challenged students with more conceptual questions and lengthier calculations, especially in Physics. Chemistry continued to be the highest-scoring subject overall.

### ************2. What is the JEE Main 2025 qualifying percentile for JEE Advanced?************

To be eligible for **JEE Advanced 2025**, candidates must meet the minimum percentile set in **JEE Main 2025**. Based on recent trends and the 2024 benchmark, the qualifying percentile for the **General category** is expected to be around the **93+ percentile**. For **OBC-NCL**, it’s usually in the **79–80 percentile** range, while **EWS** candidates typically need **around 80 percentile**. SC and ST candidates generally qualify with percentiles around **60–62** and **47–48**, respectively.

### **3. ********How many attempts are allowed for JEE Advanced?**********

Candidates are allowed a **maximum of two attempts** at **JEE Advanced**, which must be made in **two consecutive years**. For example, a candidate who first appeared in 2024 can retake the exam in 2025, but not in 2026. These limits apply regardless of the number of JEE Main attempts.

### 4. ********What is the minimum score required in JEE Advanced to get into an IIT for the General category?********

While qualifying cutoffs vary each year, a **General category** candidate typically needs at least **35% of the total marks** to be eligible for inclusion in the common rank list. Based on a full score of 360, this translates to **around 125–130 marks**. However, gaining admission to a top IIT usually requires a significantly higher score—often **over 150**, depending on the branch and institute.

### ********5. **************What minimum score is required in JEE Advanced to get into an IIT for the OBC category?**********************

For **OBC-NCL** candidates, the qualifying marks are typically around **31.5% aggregate**, with at least **9% in each subject**. This means a total score of **approximately 113–115 marks** (based on 360) is required to make it to the OBC rank list. However, getting into competitive IIT branches like Computer Science or Electrical Engineering may require scores above **130–140 marks,** depending on the year’s cutoff.

### 6. **What is the Cutoff for JEE Advanced 2025?**

As of now, the **official cutoff for JEE Advanced 2025** will be announced along with the results on **June 2, 2025**. However, based on trends from previous years, the expected cutoff for the **General (CRL) category** could be around **30% to 35%** of the total marks. As per the standard pattern, cutoffs for reserved categories like OBC, SC, ST, and EWS will likely be lower. Final cutoffs may vary depending on paper difficulty and overall student performance.

### 7. **Was JEE Advanced 2025 Paper Tougher than JEE Advanced 2024 Paper?**

Many candidates and experts believe that the **JEE Advanced 2025 paper** was tougher than the **JEE Advanced 2024 paper**. While the overall structure was similar, Paper 2 in 2025 featured more concept-driven and time-intensive problems. This was especially noticeable in Physics and Mathematics, which required deeper analytical thinking than the previous year. The difficulty level spiked compared to 2024, which had a slightly more balanced and predictable format

### 8. **When will the JEE Advanced 2025 answer key be released?**

JEE Advanced **authorities are** expected to release the **JEE Advanced provisional answer key** **on May 26th, 2025**, followed by the **JEE Advanced Final Answer key** on June 2nd  after reviewing objections.
