## Overview of the KIRF College Rankings in Kerala

The Kerala Institutional Ranking Framework is a wing under the KSHEC and is responsible for ranking the colleges in the Kerala state based on specific parameters. KIRF ranks the higher educational institutions based on five parameters, and all higher educational institutions in the state of Kerala are eligible to participate in the KIRF College rankings. Let’s understand the parameters in the KIRF college rankings in Kerala State.

### Parameters of KIRF College Rankings

Here are the five parameters of KIRF College rankings that are considered while ranking a college in Kerala State:

**1. Teaching, Learning & Resources(TLR):** This also has several other factors such as Student strength, Faculty-Student Ratio, Faculty Qualification and Experience, Online Education, Subscription to e-journals, Financial Resources, and their utilization. TLR focuses on the teaching, learning outcomes, and the resources of the institution/college that support students’ learning and ranks the higher institution accordingly.

**2. Knowledge Dissemination and Research Excellence(KDRE):** This parameter is considered for measuring the impact of knowledge dissemination and excellence of the institution in research. This section includes parameters like Research Productivity, Research Impact, Intellectual Property Rights, and Research Footprint.

**3. Graduation Outcome(GO):** This section measures the institution’s excellence in preparing students for their future endeavours upon their graduation. This section includes parameters like Metric for University Examinations (GUE), Metric for Entrance Examinations UGC-NET/JRF/CSIR/GATE/GRE (GEE), Placement and Higher Studies (PH).

**4. Outreach and Inclusivity(OI):** This Parameter measures the inclusivity and outreach of the college by parameters such as the Percentage of students from outside the state/country, the Percentage of first-generation learners, Facilities for the differently abled, and Social Inclusiveness.

**5. Scientific Temper and Secular Outlook(STSO):** This parameter measures the commitment of the institution to promote scientific temper and secular outlook among students. This includes parameters like Initiatives/programmes organised/participated by students and faculty of the institutions reflective of scientific temper and secular outlook (STSO Ql), Adoption of Green Technology (GT), and Accreditation and Ranking (AR).

## How to Use KIRF College Rankings for College Selection?

KIRF College rankings are an important source to consider when choosing colleges. However, it’s important to understand that rankings alone might not land you in the best college. So students and parents have to consider several other factors as well while selecting a college for higher education. Here’s how you can decide:

**1. Use KIRF Rankings as a Starting point:** Shortlist the top 5–100 colleges in KIRF rankings, and also check for other rankings such as NIRF rankings. List out those colleges that are common in both the rankings.

**2. Consider fees and ROI:** Consider how much you are paying to the college and placement opportunities in the college, which can help you estimate your Return on Investment approximately.

**3. Consider location and accessibility:** Consider proximity to your home, exposure, and networking opportunities. This is important for those who can’t afford the cost of living in cities.

**4. Consider faculty and academic environment:** Students can go through the faculty profiles and their qualifications while choosing a college. This is important as experienced faculty can support and enhance your learning.

**5. Branch/Stream over College:** Consider preferring the branch over the college reputation, as it is the branch that can upskill you and not the college.
