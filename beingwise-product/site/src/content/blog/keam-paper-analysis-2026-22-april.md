## What are the Students’ Reactions of KEAM 2026 April 22nd Exam?

The Kerala Engineering Architecture Medical Entrance Examination (KEAM) 2026 wrapped up on April 22, concluding a six-day exam window conducted by the CEE, Kerala. The final engineering shift ran from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM in CBT mode. As student reactions began pouring in, the April 22 paper proved consistent with the trends seen throughout the week — moderate in difficulty, NCERT-heavy, and demanding on time management.

**Overall Difficulty**: Students described the overall paper as moderate to easy compared to the previous year, with no major surprises reported, as the paper remained predictable for well-prepared students.

**Chemistry:** Students found Chemistry in the KEAM entrance exam to be the easiest section, with most questions direct and NCERT-based. Many chose to attempt it first College Dost, using it as a confidence booster.

**Physics**: Students found Physics simple and manageable, easier than their mock tests, with mostly formula-based, direct application questions and no assertion-reasoning type questions.

**Mathematics:** Mathematics caught many aspirants off guard, with students reporting it as quite lengthy and calculation-based, making time management crucial. Topics like Integration were considered especially difficult, alongside questions from Vector 3D and Cramer’s Rule.

## What is the KEAM 2026 Difficulty Level Compared to JEE Mains?

The difficulty level of the KEAM exam is considered to be significantly easier than JEE Main. While JEE Mains is known for its unpredictability and highly complex questions, KEAM is generally viewed as being closer to a standard board exam or NCERT level. Given below are the key differences in difficulty and exam style compared to JEE Main:

**Direct and Familiar Questions:** Unlike JEE Mains, which often features deep application-level problems that students are forced to skip, the questions in KEAM are mostly direct. Teachers note that in KEAM, an average prepared student will likely know how to solve almost all the questions on the paper.

**A Test of Speed Rather Than Depth:** The primary challenge of KEAM is not the conceptual difficulty, but rather time management. KEAM requires students to answer roughly double the number of questions (150 questions) in the same amount of time it takes to sit a JEE Main paper. Therefore, success in KEAM relies far more on speed and accuracy than on deep, exhaustive subject learning.

**Predictable Patterns:** KEAM tends to stick closely to standard formulas and NCERT textbook data without deviating much into highly complex, out-of-syllabus conceptual twists.

Because of this comparatively manageable difficulty level, KEAM is often seen as a highly achievable target and a reliable safety net for students who may not have reached their expected scores in the JEE Main exams.

## How Does KEAM 2026 Compare To KEAM 2025’s Difficulty?

Overall, the difficulty level of the KEAM entrance exam 2026 was very similar to the KEAM 2025. The standard of the questions remained comparable to a standard board exam and stuck closely to the NCERT syllabus. However, students and teachers noted a few minor differences in specific subjects compared to the previous year:

**Mathematics**: While the questions themselves were simple and balanced, students felt that they were slightly more lengthy and time-consuming than last year’s paper.

**Chemistry:** Although the section was described as “easy as usual,” there was a slight increase in application-level questions that required careful thinking. Unlike previous years, the Inorganic Chemistry portion specifically tested minute, data-based information directly from the NCERT textbook.

## What’s Next After KEAM 2026?

Once the KEAM 2026 exam wraps up, the real wait begins. CEE Kerala will move quickly through the post-exam process, and staying updated on each step is crucial so you don’t miss any deadlines. Here is what you can expect in the coming weeks:

- Score Standardisation: Since KEAM is conducted across multiple shifts, scores are standardised to ensure fairness. Your final rank is based on this standardised score combined with your board exam marks.

- KEAM Counselling 2026: Qualified students will be called for centralised counselling conducted by CEE Kerala. You will need to register, fill in college and branch preferences, and upload documents.

- College Reporting: After seat allotment, selected students must report to their allotted college within the specified deadline with all original documents to confirm admission.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1.How can students avoid negative marks in the KEAM exam?

To avoid negative marks, students must actively work to minimize careless mistakes, which are actually more common when an exam is perceived as easy. It is crucial to develop the skill of knowing which questions to skip; do not hold onto difficult or time-consuming problems, and instead skip them quickly to move forward. Students are advised to answer calmly and avoid the urge to greedily attempt questions without accuracy.

### 2.Which specific NCERT chapters should I focus on for Physics?

You should aim to cover all chapters because the exam uniformly distributes questions, ensuring at least one or two questions appear from every topic. However, the paper analysis specifically highlighted that questions were prominently featured from Modern Physics, Electrostatics, and Current Electricity.

### 3.Is the KEAM syllabus mostly based on NCERT?

Yes, the KEAM exam is heavily based on the NCERT syllabus, with a difficulty level comparable to a standard board exam. Teachers emphasize that the NCERT textbook should never be set aside, and preparation should be entirely centred around its concepts. This is especially true for subjects like Chemistry, which frequently test minute details, specific information, and exact data directly from the NCERT text. Even in subjects like Physics and Mathematics, the questions remain very basic and do not go outside the bounds of the NCERT curriculum.

### 4.What was the overall difficulty level of KEAM 2026 on April 22?

The overall difficulty level was moderate. Physics was easy, Chemistry was NCERT-based and simple, and Mathematics was time-consuming but not conceptually hard. The paper was comparable to a standard board exam.

### 5.Which subject was the hardest in KEAM 2026 April 22 paper?

Mathematics was the most difficult section, not because of conceptual complexity, but because of its length and time demands. Students found it challenging to solve all 75 questions within the given time.

### 6.What is the safe score for KEAM 2026?

Based on expected marks and KEAM 2025 cutoff trends, a score of 350+ after standardisation is considered safe for getting a seat. For top government colleges like CET Thiruvananthapuram, aim for 500+.

### 7.Were there any questions from topics outside the standard KEAM syllabus in the April 22 paper?

No, the KEAM entrance exam April 22 paper strictly adhered to the standard KEAM syllabus and NCERT curriculum. Students and teachers confirmed that there were no out-of-syllabus surprises. All questions, even the more time-consuming Mathematics problems, were rooted in standard textbook concepts, making thorough NCERT preparation the most reliable strategy.
