IIEST Shibpur is one of the oldest engineering colleges in India. Set up in 1856, it has been running for over 170 years. The institute holds a NIRF rank of 54 in Engineering and 4th in Architecture and Planning as of 2025. It is also an Institute of National Importance, a status it received in 2014.

The IIEST Shibpur admission process is based on the JEE Main 2026 Exam. Apart from this, fees at IIEST Shibpur depend on the candidate’s family income. A student from a higher-income household pays around ₹96,600 at the time of admission for B.Tech. A student with family income below ₹1 lakh pays ₹34,100 for the same semester. SC/ST and PwD students get a full tuition fee waiver by default. This article on IIEST Shibpur admission 2026 covers admission process eligibility, fees, cutoff, documents, and placements.

## **IIEST Shibpur Admission Criteria for B.Tech**

B.Tech admission at IIEST Shibpur is based on JEE Main All India Rank. There is no separate entrance test by the institute. Seats are allocated through JoSAA counselling after JEE Main results are out.

### **Academic Eligibility**

Candidates must have passed Class 12 or an equivalent qualifying examination with the following subjects:

- Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects
- Apart from that, Chemistry, Biotechnology, Biology, or a Technical Vocational subject

Minimum marks required:

- **General / OBC / EWS:** 75% aggregate in Class 12, or within the top 20th percentile of their respective board
- **SC / ST / PwD:** 65% aggregate in Class 12

### **Qualifying Exam Accepted By IIEST Shibpur**

These are the academic qualifications (Class 12 or equivalent) that are accepted:

1. 10+2 final examination by any recognised Central or State Board (CBSE, CISCE, etc.)
2. Intermediate or two-year Pre-University examination by a recognised Board or University
3. Final examination of the two-year course of the Joint Services Wing of the National Defence Academy
4. Senior Secondary School Examination by NIOS with a minimum of five subjects
5. Any Board / University examination in India or abroad recognised as equivalent to 10+2 by AIU
6. Higher Secondary Certificate Vocational Examination
7. AICTE-recognised Diploma or State Board of Technical Education Diploma of at least 3 years
8. GCE examination (London / Cambridge / Sri Lanka) at Advanced (A) level
9. Cambridge High School Certificate or International Baccalaureate Diploma from IBO, Geneva

### **Entrance Exam Accepted By IIEST Shibpur**

**JEE Main is the only entrance exam accepted**. Candidates who clear JEE Main and meet the eligibility criteria above are considered for seats through JoSAA counselling. IIEST Shibpur is one of 127 institutes under JoSAA, alongside IITs, NITs, and IIITs.

## **IIEST Shibpur Admission Criteria: B.Arch**

B.Arch admission also goes through JEE Main, specifically Paper 2 (Architecture), then followed by JoSAA counselling. The required subjects for B.Arch are Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry. Minimum marks criteria are the same: **75% for General / OBC / EWS and 65% for SC / ST / PwD.** The same qualifying examination list applies. Seats are allocated through the JoSAA seat matrix.

## IIEST Shibpur Admission Process: Step by Step

The admission process for IIEST Shibpur is conducted through JoSAA counselling based on JEE Main ranks. Here is a simple and clear breakdown of the process for 2026:

- **Step 1: Appear for JEE Main 2026 (January & April 2026)** Candidates need to register and appear for JEE Main. Admission to IIEST Shibpur is based on the **All India Rank**, not the raw score.
- **Step 2: Check Result and Analyse Rank (April 2026)** After the results are declared, candidates should check whether their rank falls within the expected cutoff range. Most branches close between **50,000–70,000**, while CSE closes around **13,000–15,000**.
- **Step 3: Register for JoSAA Counselling (June 2026)** Candidates must register on the JoSAA portal, as there is **no separate application form** for IIEST Shibpur.
- **Step 4: Fill and Lock Choices (June 2026)** Candidates need to select their preferred institutes and branches in order of priority. Seat allotment depends on **rank, category, choices, and availability**.
- **Step 5: Seat Allotment Rounds (June to July 2026)** JoSAA conducts multiple rounds of seat allotment. Seats are allotted category-wise, including the **Home State quota (West Bengal)**.
- **Step 6: Accept Seat and Pay Fee** Once a seat is allotted, candidates must accept it within the deadline and pay the **seat acceptance fee**.
- **Step 7: Document Verification** Candidates must upload and verify documents such as **Class 10 & 12 mark sheets, JEE Main scorecard, category certificate (if applicable), and ID proof**.
- **Step 8: Report to the Institute (August 2026 – Tentative)** Candidates need to report to IIEST Shibpur as per the schedule. Admission is confirmed after **document verification and fee payment**.

### CSAB Special Round

Candidates who do not secure a seat through JoSAA can participate in CSAB Special Rounds. These rounds usually have higher closing ranks, offering another opportunity for admission.

### DASA Counselling Round

Admissions for NRI, PIO, and foreign candidates are conducted through the **DASA portal**. Candidates must have a valid JEE Main rank and a minimum of **60% aggregate in Class 12 (or equivalent)**.

### Tuition Fee Waiver

SC/ST and PwD students get a full tuition fee waiver automatically. Reserved candidates need no income certificate. The deadline for submitting this certificate is notified by the Office of the Dean (Student Welfare) every year.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is there any direct admission at IIEST Shibpur?

No. All B.Tech and B.Arch seats go through JEE Main and JoSAA/CSAB counselling. There is no management quota or direct admission for Indian nationals. The only alternate route is DASA for NRIs and foreign nationals.

### 2.**Can West Bengal students get a preference?**

Yes. There is a Home State Quota for West Bengal students in the JoSAA seat matrix. Some seats are reserved for candidates from West Bengal, which gives local students an advantage in specific categories.

### 3. **Is IIEST Shibpur part of JoSAA?**

Yes. IIEST Shibpur is one of 127 institutes under JoSAA 2025, alongside 23 IITs, 31 NITs, and 26 IIITs.

### 4. **What is the last rank to get into IIEST Shibpur?**

For the General category, ranks up to around 70,000–75,000 give you a chance at some branch in JoSAA. CSAB special rounds sometimes extend this slightly. For CST, you would need a rank under 14,000–15,000 based on 2025 data.

### 5. **Is WBJEE accepted at IIEST Shibpur?**

No. IIEST Shibpur only accepts JEE Main ranks processed through JoSAA. WBJEE is for West Bengal state engineering colleges like Jadavpur University or MAKAUT-affiliated institutes.

### 6. Does IIEST Shibpur offer scholarships?

Yes. Income-based tuition fee waivers apply for UG students with family income below ₹5 LPA. Government scholarship schemes for SC/ST students are also applicable, and merit-based scholarships exist for high performers.

### 7. How to get admission in IIEST Shibpur?

To get admission in IIEST Shibpur, you need to qualify JEE Main and take part in JoSAA counselling. Seats are allotted based on your rank, category, and the choices you make during counselling.
