During the CUET UG exam process, candidates must be mindful of the inevitable **common mistakes** to avoid during cuet 2025 exam that can affect their performance. One key area is the list of essential documents to carry. The admit card is the most important document and must be presented at the exam centre without fail. Additionally, students should exercise extra caution in their **exam preparation strategy**, ensuring they follow the correct syllabus, manage time efficiently, and practice regularly.

## CUET 2025 Exam: Mistakes To Avoid On The Exam Day

Check out some of the common mistakes candidates should not make on the day of the CUET exam 2025.

- **Forgetting the Admit Card and ID:** If you arrive at the exam hall without your printed CUET UG admit card and a government-issued photo ID, you may be denied entry. Students are advised to pack their exam bags the day before the exam to avoid any last-minute rush.
- **Arriving Late to the Exam Center:** Arriving late to the CUET UG 2025 exam can gain result in denied entry. Always check the reporting time in advance and familiarize yourself with the exam center. It is advisable to plan to arrive at least 30 to 45 minutes early.
- **Maintain Dress Code:** The CUET UG has a strict dress code to ensure fairness and security during the examination. Candidates are not allowed to wear clothing with large buttons, metal zippers, or badges. Shoes with thick soles are prohibited unless specifically permitted at the exam center. Additionally, items such as jewellery, watches, and belts are not allowed. It is recommended to wear simple, thin clothing without complex designs.
- **Double Check Map Location and Transport:** Make sure to double-check the location of your exam center on the map and plan your mode of transportation. Calculate the travel time and allow extra time for potential traffic jams.

### CUET UG 2025 Exam: Mistakes To Avoid During Exam

Check out some of the common mistakes candidates should not make during the CUET exam 2025.

- **Ignoring the Instructions on the Screen:** Sometimes students miss reading the on-screen guidelines and then skip vital steps. Always make sure you read all instructions that are given on the screen before you begin the test.
- **Not Understanding the Sequence of Sections:** If you have chosen Slot 1 for your Phase 1 exam, you will face four sections: two domain-based, one General Knowledge, and one language section. An important point to remember is that **once the time for a particular section ends, the system will automatically move you to the next section**. Therefore, do not panic during the exam and make sure to start with the section you are most confident in. There is no strict rule that you must begin with the language section just because you selected it first during registration.
- **Spending Too Much Time on Difficult Questions:** You should avoid spending too much time on one question, which could lead to negatively impact your overall score. Instead, if you feel stuck, skip the question and move on. Timing ( or clock management) is the key; if you manage your time wisely, you should be able to attempt more questions correctly.
- **Guessing Blindly Without Eliminating Choices:** Remember that CUET has negative marking, so do not guess blindly. You can make educated guesses if you can narrow down the choices to at least one or two wrong answer options.
- **Not Reading Questions Properly:** Because students are often in such a rush to finish the exam, they misread the details of the question. Make sure to always read the question at least twice, so that you truly understand what is being asked of you before you choose your answer.
- **Getting Anxious During System Issues:** When your system lags or freezes, do not get anxious. Calmly inform the invigilator. Your answers will usually have been auto-saved to date, and you will receive assistance with the system issue.
- **Forgetting to Use ‘Mark for Review’:** The mark for review option allows you to go back to questions you are unsure about later. Use “mark for review” wisely, as you do not want to waste valuable time on difficult questions during the first time around.
- **Do not rely on last-minute answer marking:** Candidates are advised not to keep any questions for the last minute during CUET UG 2025. Keep 10 minutes in your hand for revision of your answers. Check if you have missed any sections. Practice time management during your mock test.

This is for all the freshers who are going to attempt competitive exams for the first time. Till now, candidates have only appeared for subjective-type question papers, but here the scenario changes.

- MCQs are always designed to look easy, but it is tricky when candidates attempt them.
- The identical options may confuse the candidates which resulting in picking the wrong ones even after knowing the answer.
- Be careful when choosing any answer, and check before proceeding to the next one.
- Even while flaunting, make sure to use the logical elimination method.

## CUET UG 2025 Exam: Mistakes To Avoid During the Final Days of Preparation

Check out some of the common mistakes candidates should not make during the final days of preparation for the CUET exam 2025.

1. **Do Not Start New Topics**
   Don’t start new topics or chapters in the last few days, as it can lead to confusion and unnecessary anxiety.
2. **Do Not Forget To Revise**
   Don’t ignore the revision of important formulas, key concepts, and shortcuts you have gone through. Don’t do only difficult problems and ignore the basics, as both are equally important in the exam.
3. **Overloading on Study Material**
   Overloading yourself with study materials is a common mistake among students. While having high-quality resources is important, many students believe that their preparation will improve simply by accumulating more books. This often leads to confusion, distraction, and difficulty in grasping fundamental concepts. Instead, it’s better to limit yourself to a select few reliable and well-known resources, such as NCERT textbooks, study guides, and previous years’ questions (PYQS). Take a structured approach to your studies and focus on understanding the concepts, rather than bouncing between different books and notes.
4. **Avoid pulling an all-nighter on the last day**.
   Don’t do an all-nighter because not having proper sleep will negatively impact memory and attention.
5. **Skipping Mock Tests** 
   Do not avoid mock tests in your final days. If you avoid mocks, you have not practised time management or how your actual exam will be. Students need to take at least 3-4 mock tests and go through previous year papers to be more confident.
6. **Not Reviewing all Errors**
   Many students overlook the various ways they can make mistakes in their mock tests. It is important to review the mistakes you made, understand the concepts behind each one, and revise the relevant topics thoroughly.
7. **Not Taking Breaks.**
   As the exam approaches, make sure to take short breaks between study sessions. If you don’t take breaks, you may experience burnout or exhaustion on exam day.
8. **Double Check The Exam Day Rules**
   Always adhere to exam day rules, guidelines, and requirements. Familiarise yourself with the exam centre location and ensure all documents are ready. The rules and regulations are outlined on the admit card itself.
9. **Poor Health Management**
   Sleeping poorly, eating poorly, and taking no breaks can negatively affect performance. Students should maintain a healthy diet, get enough rest, and take short breaks to stay mentally and physically fit while studying.

Check out some of the mistakes candidates should avoid if they want to score high on the CUET exam 2025.

1. **Underestimating CUET Syllabus and Exam Pattern:** Students often underestimate the CUET syllabus and exam pattern. Many begin their preparation without fully understanding the official syllabus, leading them to study irrelevant topics or overlook critical areas with higher weightage.

2. **Skipping Mock Tests and Previous Year Papers:** Neglecting mock tests is one of the worst mistakes you can make. It can negatively impact your time management, weaken your understanding of different types of questions, and increase anxiety on test day. Regularly take timed mock tests and evaluate your performance afterwards to identify areas where you need improvement.

**Time Mismanagement During Exams**: Some candidates spend too much time on difficult questions and neglect the ones they could have easily answered, resulting in lower scores. Candidates should utilize the mark for review option and move on.

Careless guessing can significantly affect scores. To improve accuracy, it’s essential to use elimination techniques. Begin by eliminating obviously wrong options before attempting to answer the question. If you cannot eliminate at least two options confidently, it is wiser to skip that question altogether.

## CUET Exam 2025: Things to Carry

Candidates must bring the following documents to the CUET exam centre. Failure to bring the required documents may result in not being allowed to sit for the exam.

- CUET Admit Card
- CUET Self-Declaration Form to be downloaded from the NTA website.
- 2 to 3 passport-size photographs (same as one uploaded on the application form)
- Any of the following authorized photo IDs:
  - PAN Card
  - Passport
  - Aadhaar Card
  - Bank Passbook
- A transparent ballpoint pen
- A transparent water bottle
- PwD/PwDB certificate (if applicable)

### Instructions For PwD Candidates at Exam Centres

- Eligible PwD candidates classified with 40% or more disability are provided extra time during the examination.
- Candidates with disabilities are allowed to use a scribe if they are eligible and request the exam authority. Their scribe should not come from the same academic background as the candidate to ensure fairness in the exam.
- Usually, candidates for PwD had separate sitting arrangements to make it more comfortable. PwD sitting provisions are often made on the ground floor.
- Most examination centers have wheelchair ramps and other mobility supports to assist candidates to their designated rooms.
- Designated support staff are available at the exam centres to help PwD candidates navigate the premises before and after the exam, but not during the exam itself.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ****What are the common mistakes made by students before, during, and after the CUET UG 2025 exam?****

Common mistakes made by candidates include forgetting to carry their admit card, not reading the instructions carefully, getting to the exam center late, mismanaging time, and making mistakes in filling up the OMR form or online examination form.

### **2. **************Can candidates appear for the CUET UG 2025 exam without the admit card?****************

No, candidates cannot enter the exam hall without an admit card. Candidates must carry their admit card in printed format, along with a valid photo ID.

### **3. ****************How early must one reach the exam center on the CUET UG 2025 exam day?******************

Candidates are advised to reach the exam center at least 1.5 to 2 hours before the reporting time stated in the admit card to avoid any last-minute chaos.

### **4. ****************What documents do I need to carry to the CUET UG 2025 exam center******************

Candidates are required to carry their CUET 2025 admit card, a valid government-issued photo identity (Aadhaar/PAN/Passport), and other items mentioned in the examination guidelines.

### 5. ******What is the next step after the CUET UG 2025 exam?******

After the CUET UG 2025 exam, the next step is to wait for the provisional answer key so you can get a rough score and raise any concerns you might have. Once the final answer key is published, the CUET results will follow. After the results, candidates need to appear for a counselling session and seat allotment based on the cutoff score.

### 6. ****When is the CUET exam 2025?****

The CUET exam date 2025 is conducted from 13th May to 3rd June. The admit card has already been released. Candidates can visit the official website to download the CUET hall ticket 2025.
