## How Does Lateral Entry for Diploma Students Work with NIAT Training Centre?

The collaborating Universities where the NIAT upskilling program is offered don’t provide lateral entry for diploma holders.

All students are required to join the collaborating Universities with NIAT in the first year to ensure the skill map is delivered. This is a strategic decision since diploma students only possess skills that are closely aligned with traditional engineering programs. The **NIAT-trained first-year skill map is not only foundational but also future-oriented**, equipping students with the skills and experiences necessary to succeed in the tech industry. In their first year, students are introduced to core programming, problem-solving techniques, and practical computing concepts.

## **NIAT Upskilling Program Enrollment through NAT**

The NIAT Upskilling Program enrollment is based on the NxtWave Assessment Test. To gain access to the NIAT upskilling program offered at collaborating universities, candidates need to attempt the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT), and the next steps involve a personal counselling session to understand the candidate’s requirements. Here is how you can apply for NAT:

- Enter your phone number and enter the OTP sent to your registered mobile.
- After this, you will be redirected to the entry form
- Fill in your details either as a student or as a parent.
- Register for the NAT.
- Upon successful registration, candidates will receive a confirmation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Can diploma students join NIAT’s upskilling program?

Yes, diploma students can join NIAT’s upskilling program. However, all students must start from the first year, as collaborating universities do not offer lateral entry into the second year.

### 2. Why can’t diploma students get lateral entry into the second year?

This ensures all students complete NIAT’s foundational first-year skill map, which includes programming, problem-solving, product thinking, and exposure to AI, ML, and Data Science.

### 3. What do diploma students gain by starting from the first year?

Diploma students gain alignment with NIAT’s skill map, early exposure to advanced technologies, mentorship, and opportunities for internships with stipends up to INR 30,000 per month.

### 4. Is NAT a university entrance exam for diploma students?

No, NAT is not a university entrance exam. It is an assessment conducted by NxtWave for enrollment into NIAT. Admission to universities is handled separately.

### 5. What is the eligibility criteria for diploma students to appear for NAT?

Candidates must have completed Class 12 and meet the collaborating university’s eligibility criteria. All diploma students must begin from the first year.

### 6. Will diploma students study alongside Class 12 students?

Yes, diploma holders will study alongside first-year students from a Class 12 background to ensure equal access to learning and opportunities.

### 7. Does NIAT award a degree or diploma to diploma students?

No, NIAT does not award degrees or diplomas. The B.Tech degree is provided by the collaborating university, while NIAT offers an Industry Ready Certificate (IRC).

### 8. Why is NIAT a better choice for diploma students compared to a regular B.Tech route?

NIAT offers industry-aligned training, mentorship, project-based learning, early internships, and exposure to emerging technologies, providing a more practical and career-focused approach than traditional programs.

### 9. Is NAT mandatory for diploma students?

Yes, NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) is mandatory for all students seeking admission into NIAT. It evaluates logical reasoning, quantitative skills, and communication ability.
