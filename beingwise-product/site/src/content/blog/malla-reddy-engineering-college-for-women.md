Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women was an autonomous institution established in the year 2008. MRECW is a part of the Malla Reddy Group of Institutions, which includes Malla Reddy University, MREC, MRECW, etc. MRECW was approved by AICTE and affiliated with JNTUH. It also holds a grade ‘A+’ from the NAAC. The college has received national recognition through NIRF and ARIIA rankings and has consistently been ranked among the top emerging engineering colleges in India. Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women offers B.Tech programs in CSE, ECE, IT, and specialised branches like AI & ML, Cyber Security, IoT, and Data Science, along with M.Tech and MBA courses.

With its strong placement training through industry tie-ups, MRECW provides excellent placement opportunities for their students. The college promotes innovation through technical clubs, Hackathons, and its own Technology Business Incubation (TBI) Centre. With national and international conferences, funded research projects, and active student chapters like IEEE and ISTE, MRECW focuses on building leadership, innovation, and real-world skills among young women engineers. In this blog, you will understand the courses offered, the admission process, and the fees for each course at Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women.

## Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women Admission Process

Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women offers admissions through various entrance exams and management quotas. Students who appear and qualify for the entrance exams have to attend the counselling process, and based on merit, they will be allotted admission into MRECW. Check the table below to understand the admission process for MRECW.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Course Level** | **Admission Mode** | **Criteria** |
| B.Tech (UG) | EAMCET (Convener Quota) | Based on the EAMCET rank conducted by the State Board. |
| Management/NRI Quota | Minimum 60% in overall or group subjects (or equivalent) |
| ECET (Lateral Entry) | 10% of seats through ECET rank for direct admission into the 2nd year |
| MBA (PG) | ICET (Convener Quota) | Based on the ICET rank conducted by the State Board. |
| Management/NRI Quota | Preferably ICET rank holders; else, candidates with 55%+ in the qualifying exam |
| M.Tech (PG) | GATE / PGECET | Based on GATE/PGECET rank for Embedded Systems, CSE, and related branches |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. **********What are the Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women’s fees?**********

Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women’s fees are ₹95,000 per year for all the B.Tech courses. Students who receive scholarship aid from the government might get a tuition fee waiver or a concession in fees.

### **2. ******Are the Malla Reddy Women’s College and Malla Reddy University the same?********

No, they are part of the Malla Reddy Group of Institutions, but are not the same.

### 3. ************************************Are placements at Malla Reddy Women’s College good?************************************

Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women offers good placement opportunities for students. With an average package of 6-7 LPA, MRECW provides decent opportunities for students.

### 4. ****************Do Malla Reddy Women’s College have hostel facilities?****************

Yes, Malla Reddy Engineering College for Women have a hostel facility that supports 1500 students. It offers a secure environment for students who are staying at hostels.

### 5. ************What are the scholarship opportunities at MRECW?************

Students at MRECW can avail of scholarships provided by the Government of Telangana, Government of India (for SC/ST/Minority categories), as well as in-house merit scholarships. Top academic performers receive cash rewards up to ₹5,000 annually.

### 6. ******What entrance exams are accepted for admission to MRECW?******

MRECW accepts EAMCET scores for B.Tech admissions, and ICET for MBA. For M.Tech, GATE or PGECET scores are accepted.
