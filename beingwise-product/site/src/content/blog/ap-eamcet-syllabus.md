## Table of Contents

AP EAMCET syllabus 2026 is based on the Class 11 and 12 curriculum prescribed by the Andhra Pradesh Board of Intermediate Education. The exam is conducted for two streams, i.e., Engineering (MPC) and Agriculture & Pharmacy (BiPC). In the Engineering stream, Mathematics carries the highest weightage with 80 questions. Physics and Chemistry carry equal weight. On the other hand, in the Agriculture & Pharmacy stream, Botany and Zoology together account for 80 questions. Physics and Chemistry contribute 40 questions each. This blog on AP EAMCET Syllabus 2026 will discuss the subject-wise syllabus, subject weightage, preparation, books, etc.

## **AP EAMCET 2026 Preparation**

Now that you know the syllabus and chapter-wise weightage, the next step is to plan your preparation properly. Instead of covering topics randomly, focus more on high-weightage areas first and then move to the rest.

### Engineering (MPC) Preparation Strategy

For MPC students, Mathematics should be your main focus since it carries the highest weightage and often decides your rank.

- Start with Coordinate Geometry and Calculus (highest weightage)
- Then move to Trigonometry and Algebra
- Cover Probability and Vectors after that

For Physics:

- Focus on Mechanics (laws of motion, work, energy)
- Give time to Current Electricity and Optics
- Keep Modern Physics for quick scoring

For Chemistry:

- Organic Chemistry: reactions and mechanisms
- Physical Chemistry: numericals (thermodynamics, equilibrium)
- Inorganic Chemistry: periodic trends and bonding
- Stick to NCERT-level preparation, as most questions are direct

### Agriculture & Pharmacy (BiPC) Preparation Strategy

For BiPC students, Biology is the most important subject and should be your priority.

For Botany:

- Focus on Plant Physiology and Genetics
- Cover Ecology and Cell Biology
- Revise concepts regularly (theory and application)

For Zoology:

- Prioritise Human Physiology (most important)
- Then cover Reproduction and Animal Diversity
- Don’t skip Evolution and basic concepts

For Physics & Chemistry:

- Follow the same approach as MPC
- Give slightly less time compared to Biology
- Focus on important and scoring topics only

### How to Approach Preparation?

- Keep your preparation simple and consistent, no matter which stream you choose.
- Start by focusing on high-weightage topics since they carry more marks.
- Study theory and solve questions together instead of doing them separately.
- Make sure you practice previous year questions regularly to understand the pattern.
- Revise topics again and again instead of rushing to finish new ones.
- Don’t try to cover everything; focus on important topics and do them properly.
