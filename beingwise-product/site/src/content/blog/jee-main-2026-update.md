## Aadhaar / UDID / Category Certificate Advisory

The NTA will use Aadhaar-based electronic KYC to automatically verify candidate details such as name, photograph, gender, and date of birth. This digital verification system reduces manual data errors and speeds up the approval process by around 25 to 30 per cent. Candidates are advised to ensure the following before applying:

- **Aadhaar Card:** Candidates must have the correct name, date of birth (as per their Class 10 certificate), a recent photograph, their current address, and the name of their father.
- **UDID Card (for Persons with Disabilities):** Candidates should have a valid, updated and renewed as required.
- **Category Certificate (EWS / SC / ST / OBC-NCL):** Candidates should ensure that their category certificate is updated, valid, and consistent with the information entered in the application form.

## NTA Introduces Live Photo Capture and e-KYC Verification System for JEE Mains 2026

Applicants will now be required to take a live photograph using a webcam while completing the application form. This step enables instant identity verification and helps prevent the use of edited or duplicate images.
After completing JEE Main 2026 registration and entering emergency contact details, you’ll reach the Live Photo Capture section.

### Steps to Capture Live Photo

- Connect your webcam and check that your live image appears in the preview box.
- Use a light-colored background and proper lighting. Ensure your face (about 80% visible) and ears are clearly shown. Avoid wearing a mask.
- Click Capture Photograph and, if satisfied, choose Upload Photograph. Retake if needed.
- If your system lacks a webcam, scan the on-screen QR code using your mobile device. Capture and upload your photo, then click Refresh Captured Photo on your computer.
- Complete the process before the timer ends.

### Live Camera Tips

- Turn on your webcam or built-in camera.
- Check lighting and background clarity.
- Keep your face fully within the frame and visible.
- Press Capture Photograph and preview the image before final submission.

### Using a Mobile or Tablet

- Scan the shown QR code to open the capture link.
- Take your photo using your device’s camera.
- Upload it in the same window and select Refresh Captured Photo.
- Once the image displays correctly on your form, proceed to submit.

### Enhanced Accessibility for PwD Candidates

To make the test more inclusive, NTA has introduced several assistive features for students with disabilities, including dark mode, magnifier tools, adjustable font sizes, and navigation using keyboard keys. These improvements are expected to benefit around 2,000 to 2,500 PwD candidates across India.

- Person with Benchmark Disability: At least 40% of a specified disability, certified by an authorised authority.
- Person with Disability: Long-term physical, mental, intellectual, or sensory impairment hindering full participation in society.
- A scribe/Reader/Lab Assistant facility will be available for benchmark disability candidates with writing limitations.
- Mandatory for blindness, bilateral-arms locomotor disability (BA), and cerebral palsy cases (if desired).
- Other disability cases require a medical certificate that proves the limitation in writing.
- The candidate may use their own scribe after submitting the details.
- Applies to candidates with *less than 40%* disability but who have difficulty writing.
- Scribe and/or compensatory time granted only on a certificate from a competent medical authority (multi-member board).
- The board includes a CMO/Civil Surgeon, an Orthopaedist/PMR specialist, a Neurologist (if available), a Psychologist/Special Educator, and an Occupational Therapist (if available).

**Scribe and Compensatory Time Guidelines (NTA)**

- The candidate can choose their own scribe or request that the NTA provide one.
- Scribes to be verified before the exam.
- Minimum compensatory time: 20 minutes/hour (1 hour for a 3-hour exam).
- Extra time applies even if the scribe facility is not used.
- Candidates cannot misuse scribe/compensatory time; misuse leads to cancellation of evaluation/admission.
- PwD certificate (UDID) must be uploaded in the JEE Mains 2026 application form.

## CBSE Registration Number in JEE Mains 2026

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has decided to collect the[**CBSE registration number**](/blog/jee-main/why-nta-needs-cbse-registration-number-in-jee-main-2026-registration/) of Class 11 students while filling out the JEE Main 2026 application form. This step ensures that the schedules of JEE Mains and CBSE board exams do not clash. Therefore, schools must give the CBSE registration number to all Class 11 students applying for JEE Main.

This new rule was introduced to streamline the registration and verification process. In earlier years, many students faced problems because their details in JEE Main forms, board exam records, and counselling documents did not match. Such mismatches often caused delays and extra verification steps.

By entering the registration number in CBSE 10th marksheet and continuing with the same number in Class 11 and JEE Main forms, students will now have a single verified record used for all future exams and admissions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. **When will the JEE Main 2026 registration begin?**

The JEE Main 2026 registration NTA window for Session 1 is from October 21st to November 27th, 2025, while Session 2 will start in January 2026.

### ************2. ******What are the new Aadhaar requirements for JEE Main 2026?******************

The JEE Main 2026 Aadhaar update mandates live Aadhaar-based e-KYC verification. Ensure that your Aadhaar card contains the correct name, date of birth, gender, and photograph before submitting your application.

### **3.********How do I correct Aadhaar details before JEE Main 2026 Aadhar registration?**********

Visit the official UIDAI portal or a nearby enrollment center to update Aadhaar details such as name, photo, and address, as these directly affect JEE Main 2026 registration.

### 4. ********How to find the CBSE registration number for JEE Main registration?********

Log in to your school portal or CBSE results website to check your CBSE registration number. It is also printed on your Class 10 CBSE registration certificate and your Class 12 CBSE registration certificate.

### 5. What are the new JEE Main 2026 exam centres?

The total JEE Main 2026 exam centres have increased to 323 across India to ensure broader access. Centres will be allocated based on the Aadhaar address.

### 6. Has NTA removed the on-screen calculator for JEE Mains 2026?

Yes, according to the JEE Mains 2026 new update, both physical and on-screen calculators are no longer allowed during the exam.

### 7. Where can I find the latest JEE Mains update?

Visit the NTA JEE Main 2026 official website, **<https://jeemain.nta.nic.in/>** for the JEE Mains 2026 update notifications regarding forms, syllabus, and exam schedules.

### 8. Can I apply for both sessions during one JEE Main 2026 registration?

No. As per the JEE Mains 2026 registration update, you must first apply only for Session 1 and later reapply for Session 2 using the same application number.
