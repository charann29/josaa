## **JEE Mains 2025 Exam Date: Highlights**

The Joint Entrance exam, widely known as the JEE exam, is a national-level entrance test, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) twice a year. The JEE exam allows aspirants to land in prestigious institutions like IITs and NITs for bachelor courses like B.tech/B.E, B.Arch, and B.Planning. This exam consists of two stages, JEE Main and JEE Advanced. JEE Main is the first stage, and qualifying for JEE Main will open doors for admissions in NITs and IIITs across the country.

Students aspiring to pursue studies at IITs must take the JEE Advanced. One has to qualify for the JEE Mains exam to appear for JEE Advanced. The difficulty level of JEE Advanced is higher than that of JEE Mains. Competition for JEE Advanced can be very high, but to take you there, JEE Mains is the first and most important step. Candidates aspiring to gain admission to top-tier institutions through the JEE Main 2025 examination must meet the eligibility criteria before applying. You can check the eligibility criteria below:

- Candidates who completed Class 12 in 2022, 2023, or 2024 and those appearing for Class 12 in 2025 are eligible to fill out the JEE Mains 2025 application form.
- Candidates who have passed class 12 before 2022 are not eligible to apply for JEE Mains 2025.
- Candidates must have completed Class 12 with Physics and Mathematics, plus one of the following: Chemistry, Biotechnology, Biology, or a Technical Vocational subject.

## **JEE Main 2025 Exam Pattern and Syllabus**

Understanding the exam pattern and syllabus is crucial for effective preparation and strategic planning. The JEE Main 2025 exam comprises multiple papers, each tailored for different courses: BE/BTech (Paper 1), B.Arch (Paper 2A), and B.Planning (Paper 2B). Here are the key details:

### **Exam Structure**

- **Mode of Exam:** Computer-Based Test (CBT) for all papers except the Drawing section of B.Arch, which is pen-and-paper based.
- **Duration:** 3 hours for each paper (3.5 hours for candidates appearing for both B.Arch and B.Planning).
- **Languages:** The exam is conducted in English, Hindi, and various regional languages.

### **Types of Questions**

- **B.E./B.Tech (Paper 1):**
  - Subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics
  - Each subject: 25 questions (20 MCQs + 5 Numerical Value Questions)
  - Total Questions: 75
  - Total Marks: 300
- **B.Arch (Paper 2A):**
  - Mathematics: 20 MCQs + 5 Numerical Value Questions
  - Aptitude Test: 50 MCQs
  - Drawing Test: 2 questions (pen-and-paper)
  - Total Questions: 77
  - Total Marks: 400
- **B.Planning (Paper 2B):**
  - Mathematics: 20 MCQs + 5 Numerical Value Questions
  - Aptitude Test: 50 MCQs
  - Planning-Based Questions: 25 MCQs
  - Total Questions: 100
  - Total Marks: 400

### **Marking Scheme**

- **Correct Answer:** +4 marks
- **Incorrect Answer (MCQ/Numerical):** -1 mark
- **Unattempted/Non-MCQ:** 0 marks (no penalty)

### **Subjects and Syllabus Coverage**

- **Physics:** Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electricity & Magnetism, Optics, Modern Physics, and more.
- **Chemistry:** Physical, Organic, and Inorganic Chemistry, including topics like Atomic Structure, Chemical Bonding, Coordination Compounds, and Environmental Chemistry.
- **Mathematics:** Algebra, Calculus, Coordinate Geometry, Trigonometry, Probability, and more.
- **Aptitude & Planning (for B.Arch/B.Planning):** Spatial ability, architectural awareness, planning skills, etc.
