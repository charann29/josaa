The NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT) is a 90-minute online test designed to help students with high potential to thrive in emerging tech careers. For registration for the 2026 NAT Exam, the application is available online. Candidates must visit the official website and pay a non-refundable registration fee of INR 1200 to apply for the NAT Exam. The NAT registration includes a  few simple steps that will help in understanding the student and their goals.

## Will NAT Registration Give Me Admission To The NIAT Collaborating Universities?

No. NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) registration does not guarantee admission to any of NIAT’s collaborating universities. Registering and qualifying for NAT will get you into NIAT’s upskilling program journey, not a guaranteed university seat. The university decides admissions independently, and NxtWave simply helps connect interested students with collaborating institutions.

- The NAT is an internal assessment conducted by NxtWave to evaluate a student’s aptitude and potential for its upskilling programs. It is not an entrance examination conducted by any university.
- Each collaborating university has its own independent admission policy. Admission is granted only if a student satisfies that university’s eligibility criteria; this is entirely the university’s decision.
- NxtWave’s role is that of a facilitator only. After a student shows interest, NIAT can share the student’s details with the prospective collaborating university, but only with the student’s written consent.
- The collaborating university will then evaluate the student based on its own process and grant admission if the eligibility criteria are met.

## How to Register for the NAT Exam 2026?

To apply for NAT 2026, candidates need to visit the official website, complete the registration form with accurate personal and academic details, and pay the application fee online. After successful submission, it’s important to download and save the confirmation page for future reference.

**🚨 LAST CHANCE to Join NIAT’s Industry-Ready Upskilling Program!

APPLY NOW! 🚨**

**Students can check the steps below for registration for NAT Exam 2026:**

- Visit the official website of NIAT: [NIAT India](https://www.niatindia.com/).
- Click on “Apply for counselling” in the top right corner of the homepage.
- Give your mobile number and verify it with an OTP to proceed with the application process.

Now you will have two options in front of you!

- **For Students:** Add your name, address, phone, and mail ID, along with your academic details and parent details.

- **For Parents:** Give name, number, family income, and occupation category, along with all the student details.

- Next, book an exam and a slot to pay.
- Pay the non-refundable application fee of INR 1200.

## What is **NIAT Syllabus?**

To prepare for the NAT 2026, focus more on developing problem-solving skills and logical reasoning. The test covers topics such as number theory, time and work, ratios, coding and decoding, and blood relations. Along with these, you also need to prepare quantitative aptitude topics like sets, probability, and polynomials.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Topics & Description** |
| **Logical Reasoning & Problem Solving** | |
| Number Theory | Understand concepts like divisibility, prime numbers, factors, and remainders. |
| Ratios & Proportions | Solve problems involving ratios, proportions, and percentages. |
| Time & Work | Calculate work done, time taken, and efficiency in related problems. |
| Coding & Decoding | Learn to decode and encode alphabets and numbers using various patterns. |
| Blood Relations | Understand family relationships and solve problems based on them. |
| **Quantitative Aptitude** | |
| Sets | Understand set operations, Venn diagrams, and related problems. |
| Polynomials | Learn about different types of polynomials, operations, and solving equations. |
| Coordinate Geometry | Understand concepts like lines, points, and shapes in a coordinate plane. |
| Probability | Understand probability concepts and solve problems related to events and outcomes. |

## Frequently Asked Questions

#### 1. What is the registration fee for the NIAT application form?

Both the NIAT application form and the NAT exam form are the same. You don’t need to register twice. However, registration for the NAT Exam 2026 requires candidates to pay a Non-Refundable fee of INR 1200.

#### 2. What should I do after appearing for NAT?

After you appear for the NAT exam and qualify, there will be a 1:1 counselling session that will help in understanding the students.

#### 3. How many questions are there in NAT?

There are a total of 81 questions divided into Psychometric Tests, Critical Thinking, and Mathematics.

#### 4. What is the NAT syllabus that I need to prepare for?

To prepare for the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT) 2026, you need to focus on problem-solving skills, logical reasoning, and quantitative aptitude topics. Some of the topics are number theory, time & work, ratios, sets, polynomials, probabilities, etc.

#### 5. Who delivers the upskilling sessions at NIAT’s training centres?

NxtWave has its own Trainers, Mentors, and Success Coaches to deliver the upskilling program content. These individuals are entirely separate from the university’s academic faculty and staff. They focus exclusively on industry-aligned skill-building and student success.

#### 6. How many students have joined NIAT at this point of time?

There are about 7500+ students studying with NIAT across universities in india.

#### 7. What do I receive after completing NIAT’s upskilling program?

Upon successful completion of NIAT’s upskilling program, students receive an IRC issued by NxtWave. It is not equivalent to a university degree or diploma, the academic degree is awarded by the collaborating university separately.
