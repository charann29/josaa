## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Is NIAT a college or university that I get admitted to?

No. NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) is an upskilling program, not a college or university. Students pursue their degree at a collaborating university, which independently awards the degree.

### Q2. Who teaches the upskilling sessions at NIAT — are they university professors?

No. NIAT sessions are conducted by NxtWave’s trainers, mentors, and success coaches, who are separate from university faculty and bring industry experience.

### Q3. What exactly will I learn through NIAT’s upskilling program?

You will learn industry-relevant skills such as Full Stack Development (HTML, CSS, Python), SQL, Data Structures and Algorithms, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Data Science through project-based and hands-on learning.

### Q4. What certification do I receive after completing NIAT’s upskilling program?

You will receive an IRC (Industry Ready Certificate) from NxtWave, which validates your practical skills. It is not equivalent to a degree or diploma.

### Q5. Are the projects done at NIAT part of the university’s degree curriculum?

No. NIAT projects and assignments are part of its independent upskilling program and are not included in the university’s academic curriculum.

### Q6. Students mentioned going to events like Mumbai Tech Week — is that organized by NIAT?

Such events are facilitated through NxtWave’s industry connections as part of exposure and career readiness, and are separate from university activities.

### Q7. What fees are charged by NIAT, and are they the same as college tuition fees?

NIAT charges an upskilling or program fee for its training services. This is separate from university tuition, admission, or semester fees.

### Q8. I heard NIAT students get internships in their first year. Does NIAT guarantee placements or internships?

NIAT prepares students for internships and jobs through training and industry exposure, but placements depend on individual performance and market demand. There is no guarantee.

### Q9. Does NIAT have its own physical campus with classrooms and infrastructure?

No. NIAT operates within training centres provided by collaborating universities and does not have its own campuses.

### Q10. Is NIAT recognized by NSDC and NASSCOM? Does that make it a government-approved institution?

NIAT is recognized by NSDC and NASSCOM for its skill development initiatives. However, this does not make it a government-approved university or academic institution, as it is not a degree-granting body.
