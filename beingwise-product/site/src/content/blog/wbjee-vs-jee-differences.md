Students have multiple career options like engineering, medicine, and management after class 12th. **Engineering** is the most common career choice among candidates, and many colleges offer admissions based on several entrance exams in India. Joint Entrance Examination and West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination are some popular examinations. The former is conducted by the National Testing Agency, which is a central authority responsible for entrance examinations into central institutes. **WBJEE** is a state-level examination conducted by WBJEEB for entrance into engineering, pharmacy, architecture, etc. This article will help you understand the key differences between JEE and WBJEE.

## WBJEE vs JEE Exam Details

WBJEE is a state-level examination conducted by WBJEEB for entrance into undergraduate courses like pharmacy, architecture, and engineering in the state of West Bengal. **JEE** is a National level examination for entrance into prestigious institutes like [IITs](https://collegedost.in/blog/engineering-colleges/top-iits-in-india/), [NITs](https://collegedost.in/blog/engineering-colleges/top-nits-in-india/), etc, in undergraduate courses like Engineering, Architecture, and Planning.

Here are the details of the examination for [JEE](https://collegedost.in/blog/jee-main/) and [WBJEE](https://collegedost.in/blog/wbjee/).

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Particulars** | **JEE** | **WBJEE** |
| **Level of Examination** | National level | State Level |
| **Mode of Examination** | Computer Based Test | Pen and paper-based |
| **Courses Eligible** | B.Tech B.Plan B.Arch | B.Tech B.Pharma B.Arch |
| **Exam Duration** | 3 Hours | 4 Hours |
| **Papers** | **Paper 1:** Mathematics **Paper 2:** Physics & Chemistry | **Paper 1:** BE/B.Tech **Paper 2:** B.Arch **Paper 3:** B.Plan |
| **Medium of Examination** | Bengali and English | Hindi, English, Bengali, Assamese, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Odia, Punjabi, Tamil, Marathi,  Telugu, and Urdu. |

## Exam Pattern: WBJEE vs JEE

### WBJEE Exam Pattern

[**WBJEE Exam** **Pattern**](https://collegedost.in/blog/wbjee/wbjee-exam-pattern/) consists of 155 questions in total. Mathematics accounts for a total number of 100 marks, and physics, chemistry accounts for 50 marks each. There is a negative marking in the examination, and based on the category of questions, marks are deducted for incorrect answers.

|  |  |  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Subject** | **Category-1** | **Category-2** | **Category-3** | **Total Number of Questions** | **WBJEE Total Marks** |
| **Mathematics** | 50 | 15 | 10 | 75 | 100 |
| **Physics** | 30 | 5 | 5 | 40 | 50 |
| **Chemistry** | 30 | 5 | 5 | 40 | 50 |

There are some key differences between WBJEE and JEE, and here area  few of them:

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ******Is WBJEE and JEE Same?******

No, the WBJEE examination is a state-level examination, and JEE is a national-level entrance examination.

### 2. **********************Am I eligible for admission into IITs through WBJEE?**********************

No, IITs won’t accept admissions based on WBJEE scores.

### **3. ********************Can I get Jadavpur University with WBJEE?**********************

Yes, candidates need good scores to get Jadavpur university with WBJEE.

### 4.******************What is a good score in WBJEE?******************

A score above 130+ is considered good in WBJEE.
