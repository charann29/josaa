## **Guidelines for Attempting JEE Mains 2025 Mock Tests**

Attempting JEE Mains 2025 mock tests effectively is not just about practice, but about practising at the right time, in the right way, and through the right channels. Here’s how to plan your mock test journey for JEE Mains 2025:

### **1. When to Start Attempting JEE Mains Mock Tests**

- **Early Foundation (6–8 Months Before Exam):**Begin with occasional mock tests or chapter-wise tests to build familiarity with the exam format and question types. This phase helps you identify initial weak areas and adapt your study plan accordingly.
- **Midway Milestone (3–5 Months Before Exam):**Increase the frequency of full-length mock tests. Use these to assess your overall readiness, fine-tune your time management skills, and adjust your revision strategies based on your performance.
- **Final Countdown (1–2 Months Before Exam):**Attempt full-length mock tests regularly—ideally two or more per week. Simulate real exam conditions as closely as possible to build stamina, reduce anxiety, and optimise your test-taking strategy.

### **2. How to Structure Your JEE Mains 2025 Mock Tests Practice**

- **Start with Chapter-wise/Subject-wise Tests:**Build your basics and confidence by focusing on smaller units before moving to full-length tests.
- **Gradually Move to Full-Length Mocks:**As your preparation matures, transition to full-length mock exams to build exam endurance and develop holistic time management skills.
- **Simulate Real Exam Conditions:**Take each mock at the same time of day as your actual exam, in a quiet environment, with no interruptions.
- **Review and Analyse:**After each test, thoroughly analyse your mistakes, timing, and accuracy. Adjust your study plan and attempt re-tests if available.
