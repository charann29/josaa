The first shift was from 09.00 AM to 12.00 PM, whereas the second shift was from 03.00 PM to 06.00 PM. It is one of the most important examinations for engineering aspirants in Telangana, assessing fundamental aptitude in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry.

CollegeDost has analysed both Shift 1 and Shift 2 for the May 2nd exam and noted several differences in difficulty and subjects tested in terms of difficulty level and question pattern. Below is a summary of the two shifts compared in terms of difficulty level, weightage, and number of questions from each topic.

## TG EAPCET 2025 2nd May Shift 1 Paper Analysis

The TS EAMCET 2025, conducted on May 2, Shift 1, was received in a moderate to difficult manner overall. Candidates have considered a score of 80+ as positive in light of the overall performance. Candidates stated that Physics and Maths on the day were particularly difficult, with Physics having approximately 14 difficult questions, **whilst Maths had over 25 difficult questions, with some even at JEE Mains level problems**. However, 12 to 13 questions were easy from Maths. Chemistry was relatively easy with over 25 straightforward questions (hard with few, difficult). **Candidates are also looking for 11 grace marks due to a number of incorrect questions and options.**

## TG EAPCET Exam Analysis 2025: Student Reviews For May 2nd Paper 1 and Paper 2

According to student reviews from the TS EAMCET 2025, May 2nd – Paper 1 & Paper 2, here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

### **Shift 1**

**Toughest Paper: Mathematics**

According to student feedback, the first shift of the TS EAMCET exam on May 2nd was on the tougher side. However, the overall take was that the Mathematics section of the exam was tougher for several reasons. Firstly, many candidates faced time constraints while solving 20-25 difficult problems. **Some said that the maths questions resembled the JEE Main Questions.** This made the physics questions difficult, with about 14 questions on the exam, particularly from the areas of Thermodynamics, Wave Optics, and Current Electricity. The chemistry portion of the test was on the easier side in that more than 25 questions were direct questions, and many of the questions were rated as easy to moderate, particularly Organic and Physical Chemistry questions. A few students expressed their frustration over the paper due to a few discrepancies and absurd facts in some of the problems. This might be enough for them to expect grace marks regarding the incorrect questions, approximately 11 marks in total.

### **Shift 2**

Overall, students rated the TS EAMCET 2025 Shift 2 exam as moderate. The Mathematics section, despite being manageable in terms of question difficulty, was lengthy, leading to time constraints. Physics posed challenges due to its conceptual nature. Chemistry was the easiest section, with direct and theory-based questions, especially from Biomolecules, Polymers, and Atomic Structure. Overall, students felt this shift was better balanced, and most did not face issues with ambiguous questions.

## TS EAMCET 2025: Answer Key and Response Sheet Details

**TG EAPCET 2025 answer key will be released today (5th May 2025) at 5:00 PM.** Candidates need to visit the official website, <https://eapcet.tgche.ac.in/>, to download the response sheet. Check the steps below to download the TS EAMCET response sheet 2025.

### Steps to Download the Answer Key

- Go to the official TG EAPCET 2025 website.
- Select the TS EAMCET 2025 “Download Master Question Paper With Key (E)” link or “Download Response Sheet (E)”.
- Next, give your hall ticket number, registration number, and Date of Birth.

- Click on “Get Candidate Response Sheet”.
- The answer key will appear on your screen.
- Candidates should download the answer key for later reference.

Candidates can use TG EAPCET answer key to compare with their answers for estimating their likely scores.

## TG EAPCET 2025 Answer Key: How To Raise Objections

Candidates can follow the steps below to raise an objection to the answer key of TG EAPCET 2025.

- Go to the TG EAPCET official website.
- Click on the “Objections on Preliminary Key (E)”.
  - **Note:** Candidates should only use the Master Question Paper to raise objections on the Preliminary Key.
- Read the instructions carefully and click on “Continue”.

- Next, give your hall ticket number, registration number, and Date of Birth.
- Click on “Proceed”.
- **To object to any question or preliminary key, the candidate must pay a fee of INR 500 per question.** The money will be returned within seven days of the results’ publication date if the objection to a question or preliminary key is legitimate.
- Click on “Get Print of Already Raised Objections on Preliminary Key (E)” by giving your “Payment Reference ID”.

## TG EAPCET Exam Analysis: 2025 Vs 2024

Candidates can check and compare the [**TG EAPCET exam analysis 2024**](https://collegedost.in/blog/ts-eamcet/ts-eamcet-paper-analysis/)with the analysis of the current year.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Subject** | **TS EAMCET 2025** | **TS EAMCET 2024** |
| **Mathematics** | Most challenging subject. Lengthy and calculation-intensive. Around 40–45% of questions required complex computations. | Moderate to difficult. Emphasis on Matrices, Complex Numbers, Probability, and 3D Geometry. |
| **Physics** | Challenging subject. About 60% of questions were numerical, requiring deep conceptual clarity. | Moderate to difficult. Mostly formula-based; key topics: Gravitation, Modern Physics, Semiconductors. |
| **Chemistry** | Easiest section. Approx 75% of questions are from NCERT, making it highly scoring. | Easy to moderate. Balanced mix from Organic, Inorganic, and Physical Chemistry, mostly NCERT-based. |

### **Observations**

**2025:** The emphasis on application-based and numerical problems in the Mathematics and Physics sections has increased substantially, where Maths being the hardest on 2nd May, Shift 1. Chemistry remained fairly simple, which is great for students who had studied the NCERT.

**2024:** The hardest shift was identified as May 9, Shift 2. Students even said it was as tough as JEE Mains. Mathematics was consistently lengthy, and Physics required a strong understanding of formulas. Chemistry was seen as the simplest topic in both years.

## Conclusion

TS EAMCET 2025 May 2 papers were of a different level of difficulty in both shifts, so it gave students different exam experiences. Shift 1 was generally regarded as slightly more difficult, given the complicated Mathematics section and Physics-based questions grounded in concepts. Chemistry, however, was accessible in both shifts. In contrast, Shift 2 was on the easier side of the spectrum and was a more balanced version of Shift 1 by having straightforward questions across subjects, which would have been a relief to have as a candidate. Students aiming for 80+ marks in both shifts were seen as being in a good position.
