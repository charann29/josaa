## AP EAMCET 2025 Mathematics Paper Analysis

The Mathematics paper in the AP EAMCET 2025 exam (23rd May, Shift 1) was **moderate to tough** overall, with many students finding it lengthy and time-consuming. The most challenging topics included **Calculus** (especially Definite Integration and Differential Equations), **Matrices**, and **Coordinate Geometry** (particularly Circles and Straight Lines). Moderate-level questions were seen in **Vectors**, **Probability**, and **Complex Numbers**, while relatively easier topics included **Sequences and Series**, **Trigonometry**, and **Binomial Theorem**. Key topics such as **Limits**, **Continuity**, and **Determinants** were commonly tested across the paper. Students reported that the paper was quite lengthy, requiring careful time management, and many struggled to complete all questions within the time limit.

## AP EAMCET 2025 Physics Paper Analysis

The **Physics** paper in AP EAMCET 2025 (23rd May, Shift 1) was considered **easy to moderate** by most students, with a majority of questions being formula-based and direct. The most challenging topics included **Electrostatics**, **Current Electricity**, and **Laws of Motion**, where conceptual clarity was essential to solve tricky numerical problems. **Thermodynamics**, **Kinematics**, and **Rotational Motion** were moderate in difficulty, requiring careful application of concepts. The easiest questions were from topics like **Units & Measurements**, **Oscillations**, and **Semiconductors**, which were more straightforward and scoring. The paper was **not overly lengthy**, and most students were able to attempt the majority of questions within the time limit. ## Student Reviews For AP EAMCET 2025 May 23rd Shift 1 Exam

Most students found the May 23rd, Shift 1 paper of AP EAMCET 2025 to be moderate overall, with Mathematics being the most challenging and time-consuming. The lengthy and calculation-heavy nature of the Maths section made time management difficult, leaving some students struggling to complete the paper. On the other hand, Physics and Chemistry were considered easier and more straightforward, with formula-based questions dominating Physics and fact-based questions making Chemistry more manageable. While a few tricky questions, especially in Organic Chemistry and Electrostatics, tested deeper understanding, students generally felt the paper was fair and aligned with the syllabus, though a bit lengthy due to the Maths section.
