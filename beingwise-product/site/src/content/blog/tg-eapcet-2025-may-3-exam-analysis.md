The second day of the TS-EAPCET/TS-EAMCET exam was held on May 3rd. The exam is conducted by JNTUH on behalf of the TSCHE. The exam for Engineering students is held from May 2nd to May 4th, i.e. across three days and two shifts. Qualifying Candidates are eligible for admission into undergraduate courses like B.Tech, B.Pharm, etc. The first shift was conducted from 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM, and the second shift from 3:00 PM to 6:00 PM both the shifts are perceived as easy by most of the students. ## TG EAPCET 2025 3rd May Shift 2 Paper Analysis

The TS EAMCET 2025 May 3rd Shift 2 paper was generally considered **easy by students**. In Maths, about 45 questions were easy, with key topics including differentiation, integration, trigonometric transformations, and probability. Physics had a moderate difficulty level, with emphasis on thermodynamics, magnetism, and current electricity. Chemistry was balanced, with organic compounds, acids and bases, and equilibrium carrying high weightage. Students noted that strong basics, formula recall, and time management were essential. Since there was no negative marking, using tricks in Physics was recommended. Attempting Maths and Chemistry first was suggested to maximise scores effectively during this shift.

## TG EAPCET Exam Analysis 2025: Student Reviews For May 3rd Paper 1 and Paper 2

Check the student’s reviews of the shifts 1 and 2 held on May 3rd:

### Student Reviews for Shift 1

THE TS EAMCET Shift 1 exam held on May 3rd was considered easy to moderate level based on the paper analysis. However, students expressed mixed opinions on the paper’s toughness. Some students felt the first shift exam was harder than the previous day’s papers. They felt the exam was lengthy, and the mathematics section was hard and time-consuming. Although some students said that the models they had prepared in the mock tests appeared in the exam, they felt the questions in the exam were lengthy, posing a challenge to complete the paper on time. Students who haven’t practised enough found the exam a bit challenging and hard to finish.

### Student Reviews for Shift 2

Despite initial reactions labelling the TS EAMCET 2025 May 3rd Shift 2 paper as generally easy, broader feedback from students and experts revealed a mixed opinion. While Maths was largely considered easy, with many familiar and formula-based questions, some found the section slightly lengthy. Students still described the evening shift as harder than the morning one. Overall, the paper was seen as time-consuming, especially with complex questions that combined topics like matrices and probability. Physics was moderately difficult, and Chemistry had a balanced weightage. Overall, the shift demanded strong fundamentals, speed, and smart strategy, making it moderate to difficult for most candidates.

## TS EAMCET 2025: Answer Key and Response Sheet Details

**TG EAPCET 2025 answer key will be released today (5th May 2025) at 5:00 PM.** To download the response sheet, candidates need to visit the official website, <https://eapcet.tgche.ac.in/>. Check the steps below to download the TS EAMCET response sheet 2025.

### Steps to Download the Answer Key

- Go to the official TG EAPCET 2025 website.
- Select the TS EAMCET 2025 “Download Master Question Paper With Key (E)” link or “Download Response Sheet (E)”.
- Next, give your hall ticket number, registration number, and Date of Birth.

- Click on “Get Candidate Response Sheet”.
- The answer key will appear on your screen.
- Candidates should download the answer key for later reference.

Candidates can use the TG EAPCET answer key to compare with their answers for estimating their likely scores.

## TG EAPCET 2025 Answer Key: How To Raise Objections

Candidates can follow the steps below to raise an objection to the answer key of TG EAPCET 2025.

- Go to the TG EAPCET official website.
- Click on the “Objections on Preliminary Key (E)”.
  - **Note:** Candidates should only use the Master Question Paper to raise objections on the Preliminary Key.
- Read the instructions carefully and click on “Continue”.

- Next, give your hall ticket number, registration number, and Date of Birth.
- Click on “Proceed”.
- **To object to any question or preliminary key, the candidate must pay a fee of INR 500 per question. If the objection is legitimate, the money will be refunded within seven days of the results’ publication date**.
- Click on “Get Print of Already Raised Objections on Preliminary Key (E)” by giving your “Payment Reference ID”.

| **Mathematics** | Challenging and lengthy, and considered moderate to hard in almost all the shifts. | Moderate to difficult. |

### **Observations**

**2025:** The emphasis on application-based and numerical problems in the Mathematics and Physics sections has increased substantially, with Maths being the hardest on 2nd May, Shift 1. Chemistry remained fairly simple, which is great for students who had studied the NCERT.

**2024:** The hardest shift was identified as May 9, Shift 2. Students even said it was as tough as JEE Mains. Mathematics was consistently lengthy, and Physics required a strong understanding of formulas. Chemistry was seen as the simplest topic in both years.

## Conclusion

While both shifts on May 3rd received different opinions, experts considered the exam to be of a moderate level. Shift 1 was considered difficult by many students as the questions were a bit lengthy and time-consuming. Shift 2 was considered easy to moderate by most of the candidates. The Chemistry section of both shifts was a relief for the students as the questions were straightforward and balanced.
