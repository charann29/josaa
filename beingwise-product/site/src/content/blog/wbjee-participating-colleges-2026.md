## What are WBJEE Participating Colleges?

WBJEE participating colleges are institutes that offer admission to B.Tech and related engineering courses based on WBJEE scores. These include:

- Government engineering colleges
- Private engineering colleges
- Universities and university departments

## WBJEE Participating Colleges 2026: Highlights

## **How to Choose the Right WBJEE College**?

Choosing a college is not just about rank, it’s about making a decision that works for you in the long run. Here’s a simple way to approach it:

- Step 1: Check previous year cutoffs
  Start by looking at past cutoff trends to understand which colleges and branches are possible with your rank.
- Step 2: Shortlist colleges based on your rank
  Divide your list into three parts: dream, realistic, and safe options. This helps in balanced choice filling.
- Step 3: Compare placement records and fees
  Look at average packages, top recruiters, and total fees to judge the return on investment.
- Step 4: Consider location
  Colleges in Kolkata usually offer better exposure, internships, and connectivity compared to other regions.
- Step 5: Focus on branch preference
  If placements are your priority, branches like CSE and IT generally offer more opportunities. Core branches can be considered based on interest.
- Step 6: Check infrastructure and faculty
  Basic factors like labs, campus facilities, and teaching quality also matter in your overall experience.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1.  How can I check WBJEE participating colleges for 2026?

Candidates can check the list of participating colleges during WBJEE counselling by logging into the official portal. The complete list of institutes and courses is available while filling in choices.

### Q2. How many colleges accept WBJEE scores?

Around 115–120 colleges, including government, private, and university institutes in West Bengal, accept WBJEE scores for B.Tech admissions.

### Q3. Which are the top government colleges under WBJEE?

Jadavpur University, Kalyani Government Engineering College, Jalpaiguri Government Engineering College, and Government College of Engineering & Ceramic Technology are among the top choices.

### Q4. **What is the fee range of WBJEE participating colleges?**

Government colleges usually have lower fees (around ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh for the full course), while private colleges may range between ₹3 lakh to ₹5 lakh, depending on the institute.
