Selecting a suitable engineering college with the help of
the Kerala Engineering and Management Entrance Test (KEAM)

colleges.

NIRF, or the National Institutional Ranking Framework, is a
ranking mechanism used by the government to assess Indian
colleges on the basis of teaching quality, research output,
graduation rates, outreach, and perception of the
college.  Its ranking is important as it enables
students to compare institutes on a standardised national
level.

## Why does the NIRF Ranking matter?

The reason why the NIRF (National Institutional Ranking
Framework) ranking is important is that it provides a
standard and a government-sponsored benchmark for colleges
all over India.

It assesses institutes on the basis of various factors such
as teaching standards, research output, passing out results,
placement rates, and other reputations. This helps students
to make a more informed assessment of the college besides
its name and popularity.

**The importance of the NIRF ranking is that it enables you
to:**

- Make equal comparisons between colleges nationally
- Research institutes with good academics and placements
- Recognise long-term benefits of college, beyond admission.
- During counselling and admissions, ensure better choices
  are made in KEAM.

## Factors to Consider Before Selecting a KEAM College

There are numerous factors students should consider before
choosing the best KEAM colleges for themselves.

**KEAM Rank and Eligibility:** These are the
colleges and branches that can be secured in the counselling
process based on your KEAM rank. Your rank is compared with
the previous year’s rank of KEAM for better planning with
the college cutoff.

**College Ranking and Reputation:** To know the
overall quality and performance of an institute, check the
KEAM colleges ranking. Positive ratings are associated with
better academic performance and student outcomes.

**NIRF Ranking Status:** The KEAM NIRF Colleges
are more reliable as they are ranked nationally. NIRF
enables you to make a comparison between institutes with
respect to teaching, research and placements.

**Placement Records:** Placement history
indicates how effective a college is in placing students.
Always look into average salary packages and the best
recruiting firms.

**Fees and Affordability:** When comparing
government and private colleges, for instance, the fee
structure is an important consideration. Find an affordable
college, but still of high quality.

**Infrastructure and Facilities:** Lab, library
and classrooms, etc., create good infrastructure, thus
affecting the learning environment. Campus facilities also
impact the overall life and comfort of the students.

**Location and Accessibility:** The location of
a college has an impact on travel, safety and living
expenses. Students’ daily lives can be made easier by the
well-connected location.

**Job Opportunities After Degree:** Think
long-term career progression while making your college and
branch choice. Some colleges provide more industry exposure
and more job opportunities.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Q1. Does a higher NIRF rank always mean a better
   college?**
   Not necessarily. While NIRF is a significant factor,
   students must also research placements, branch locations,
   fees and campus facilities before making their decision.
2. **Q2. Can I get admission to a good college with a
   mid-range KEAM rank?**
   Yes. Some colleges give quality education and excellent
   placement opportunities in various ranks.
3. **Q3. Should I prioritise branch preference or college
   reputation?**
   It is generally best to take an even approach. Select a
   branch as per interest, as well as the quality of the
   college.
4. **Q4. Are private KEAM colleges good for
   placements?**
   Kerala has several private colleges that have good
   placement records and industry linkages. Results of
   placement may differ from institute to institute and
   branch to branch.
5. **Q5. How many colleges should I include during KEAM
   option registration?**
   A combination of a dream college and achievable and
   realistic goals is recommended. The broader your choice
   list, the better your chances are of getting a desired
   seat.
