## What is the KCET Cutoff 2026?

KCET Cutoff is the minimum qualifying marks and closing ranks for each college, branch, category, and counselling round in the Karnataka engineering admission process conducted through KCET. This is required for candidates to secure admission to any KCET colleges.

KCET Cutoff is released in two forms:

- Opening Rank: The first rank at which the admissions through KCET start for an individual college and branch is known as the opening rank.

- Closing Rank: It is the last rank at which a seat is allotted in a particular branch and category.

These KCET Cutoffs depend on several factors, which include the total number of students appearing for KCET, the difficulty level of the exam, seat availability, branch popularity, reservation criteria, and previous years’ cutoff trends.

## B.Tech Government College KCET Cutoff – Reservation Criteria

Reservation Criteria determines how engineering seats are distributed across different categories during the KCET counselling process conducted by KEA. It significantly affects the government college KCET cutoff as each of the categories has different closing ranks and seat allocations. KCET Reservation categories are divided into vertical reservation, horizontal reservation, special category reservation, and regional quota reservation. ## Factors Affecting Government College KCET Cutoff

The KCET College Cutoff for the government college varies every year based on several academic, counselling, and seat allocation factors. B.Tech admission in government colleges and the government college KCET cutoff are affected by the following factors:

- The total number of KCET Applicants impacts the Government College KCET Cutoff. The higher the number of candidates, the higher the cutoffs.
- The difficulty level of the KCET Exams and the KCET college cutoffs are lower than a higher difficulty level.
- Total seats available in the government college, as more seats may increase admission chances.
- The popularity of Engineering branches affects the government college KCET Cutoff.
- Overall, college reputation and placements strongly affect the KCET college cutoff.
- KCET College Cutoff changes across rounds 1, 2, and the extended round for counselling.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Ques. Is a 1 lakh rank in KCET good or bad?**

Ans. A 1 lakh rank in KCET is considered an average to low rank for highly competitive branches like CSE, AI/ML, EEE, and ECE.

**2. Ques. What are the government engineering colleges for a 40000 rank in KCET?**

Ans. Students with a 40000 rank in KCET can get admission into branches like ECE, EEE, Mechanical, and Civil at colleges such as GEC Hassan, S K S J T Institute of Engineering, GEC Haveri, GEC Raichur, and others.

**3. Ques. What are the top 10 engineering colleges in Karnataka through KCET in the government sector?**

Ans. The top government engineering colleges in Karnataka through KCET include UVCE, S K S J T Institute of Engineering, GEC Hassan, GEC Ramanagara, GEC Haveri, GEC Raichur, GEC Koppal, and GEC Chamarajanagar.

**4. Ques. Can I get a government engineering college with a 50,000 rank in KCET?**

Ans. Yes, with a 50,000 KCET rank, students can get admission into government B.Tech colleges such as GEC Hassan, Ramanagara, Haveri, or Raichur in core engineering branches.

**5. Ques. Are KCET government college fees lower than private colleges?**

Ans. Yes, government colleges through KCET generally have much lower annual fees compared to private engineering colleges.
