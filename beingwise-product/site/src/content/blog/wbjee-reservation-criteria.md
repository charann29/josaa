## Table of Contents

WBJEE reservation criteria decide how seats are distributed during counselling across different categories. In WBJEE 2026, a significant number of seats are reserved for categories like SC, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B, EWS, and PwD, along with special provisions such as the TFW scheme and Defence quota.

Along with your rank, it’s important to understand how these reservations work. Your eligibility depends on things like domicile, income, and valid documents, not just your category. Having clarity on these rules beforehand can make the counselling process easier to handle.

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria 2026: Overview

Before getting into category-wise details, candidates must understand how reservations actually work in WBJEE. WBJEE Reservation doesn’t change your marks or rank, but affects the pool of seats you compete for. Here are some important points that define the reservation system:

- Reservation is applied only during counselling and seat allotment
- OBC-A and OBC-B candidates must belong to the Non-Creamy Layer
- Most reservation benefits are limited to West Bengal domicile candidates
- The final number of reserved seats is announced before counselling

The following categories are covered under WBJEE reservation:

- Scheduled Castes (SC)
- Scheduled Tribes (ST)
- OBC-A and OBC-B
- Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)
- Persons with Disabilities (PwD)
- Tuition Fee Waiver (TFW)
- Defence Quota
- JEE Main quota (for private colleges)

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria 2026: Category-Wise Seat Matrix

[WBJEE 2026](/blog/wbjee/wbjee-2026/) follows reservation rules set by the West Bengal government. SC candidates get 22% seats, and ST candidates get 6% seats. OBC-A has 10%, and OBC-B has 7% reservation for non-creamy layer candidates. EWS candidates get 10% seats if their family income is up to ₹8 lakh. PwD candidates have 5% reservation for those with at least 40% disability. The TFW scheme offers extra seats for students with an income of up to ₹2.5 lakh. There are 13 seats for the defence quota with WB domicile. Also, up to 10% seats in private colleges are available through JEE Main.

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria for SC Category

This category is meant for candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes as recognised by the state government. The benefit is available only if the candidate satisfies the domicile conditions.

### Eligibility

To meet the SC reservation criteria:

- The candidate must belong to the SC category recognised in West Bengal
- The candidate must meet WB domicile requirements

### Documents Required

At the time of counselling, candidates need to provide:

- A valid caste certificate issued by a WB authority
- Domicile proof

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria for ST Category

Reservation under the ST category support candidates from tribal communities listed in West Bengal. Similar to SC, domicile plays an important role here.

### Eligibility

If you’re applying under ST, make sure you:

- The candidate must belong to an ST category recognised in West Bengal
- WB domicile is mandatory

### Documents Required

Candidates must submit:

- Caste certificate
- Domicile certificate

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria for OBC-A and OBC-B

OBC reservation is divided into two subcategories, i.e. i) OBC-A and ii) OBC-B. One important condition here is the Non-Creamy Layer requirement.

### Eligibility

Candidates must meet the following conditions:

- Belong to OBC-A or OBC-B category listed in West Bengal
- Fall under the Non-Creamy Layer (NCL)
- Fulfil domicile requirements

### Documents Required

Candidates are required to submit:

- Caste certificate
- Non-creamy layer certificate
- Domicile proof

**Note:** The NCL certificate should be valid and updated at the time of counselling. Outdated certificates are not accepted.

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria for EWS

The EWS category is designed for candidates from economically weaker backgrounds who do not fall under SC, ST, or OBC categories.

### Eligibility

EWS eligibility mainly depends on your category and family income. If you’re applying under EWS, here’s what you should check:

- The candidate must belong to the General category
- Annual family income should not exceed ₹8 lakh

### Documents Required

You’ll need the following document during counselling:

- A valid EWS certificate issued by a competent authority

### Important Points To Remember

There are a couple of things you should keep in mind here:

- EWS is not applicable to candidates from reserved categories
- The benefit is granted based on income criteria set by the government

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria for PwD

The PwD category helps candidates with disabilities get equal access to education and exam facilities.

### Eligibility

To apply under this category:

- The candidate must have at least 40% benchmark disability
- The disability must be recognised under the RPwD Act, 2016
- It includes locomotor, visual, hearing, intellectual, and multiple disabilities
  wbjee reservation

### Documents Required

PwD Candidates need to submit disability certificate issued by a government medical board to avail WBJEE Reservation under the PwD category.

### Special Facilities for PwD Candidates

Apart from the reservation, PwD candidates also receive certain exam-related support:

- 40% concession in application fees (with prior request)
- Extra time of 20 minutes per hour of exam
- Scribe/reader facility for eligible candidates
- Special arrangements may be made at the Kolkata centre if required
  wbjee reservation

## WBJEE TFW (Tuition Fee Waiver) Scheme

The TFW scheme is meant to support students from financially weaker backgrounds by reducing their tuition burden.

### Eligibility

To apply under TFW you need to meet the following eligibility criteria:

- Annual family income must be less than ₹2.5 lakh
- The candidate must be a WB domicile
  wbjee reservation

### Important Points To Remember

To avail the reservation under WBJEE TFW (Tuition Fee Waiver) Scheme, you need to keep the following things in mind:

- Seats under TFW are limited
- These are supernumerary seats (extra seats beyond regular intake)

## WBJEE Defence Quota

This quota is reserved for candidates who are wards of defence personnel. The process here is slightly different from other categories.

### Eligibility

To be eligible for this quota, candidates need to meet the following conditions:

- Be a ward of serving, retired, or deceased defence personnel
- Apply through the Rajya Sainik Board

### Important Points To Remember

Some important points that you need to keep in mind:

- A total of 13 supernumerary seats are available
- A separate merit list is prepared
- WBJEE General Merit Rank is required
  wbjee reservation

### Documents Required

To claim the Defence quota, candidates need to submit a few supporting documents during counselling.

- Defence certificate
- Service-related documents
- Domicile proof

## WBJEE Reservation Criteria Through JEE Main

In addition to WBJEE rank, candidates with a valid JEE Main score can also be considered for admission through WBJEE counselling. For more details, candidates can refer to the section below:

- Up to 10% of seats in private self-financed colleges are filled through JEE Main
- Candidates must have a valid JEE Main rank
- Applicable only to participating private institutions

## WBJEE Domicile Criteria for Reservation

Domicile is one of the most important factors in determining eligibility for reservation in WBJEE. In most cases:

- Reservation benefits are available only to WB domicile candidates
- Candidates from other states are treated under the general category
- OCI candidates are eligible only for unreserved seats

## Documents Required for WBJEE Reservation 2026

During counselling, candidates must present valid documents for verification. Missing or invalid documents can affect seat allotment. You should keep the following documents ready:

- Caste certificate (SC/ST/OBC)
- Domicile certificate
- Non-creamy layer certificate (for OBC)
- EWS certificate
- Income certificate (for TFW)
- PwD certificate
- Defence certificate
- JEE Main rank card (if applicable)

## Important Points About WBJEE Reservation

Some small details are easy to miss during counselling, but they can affect your admission. Keep these points in mind:

- Reservation is applied only during counselling
- Documents are verified at the allotted institute
- Incorrect or false information can lead to cancellation
- Category may be changed if the documents are not valid
- Candidates may still be considered in later rounds if eligible

## WBJEE Reservation vs All India Reservation

WBJEE follows a state-level system, which is different from national-level exams like JEE Main.

- Most benefits are limited to WB candidates
- Domicile plays an important role
- Rules differ from exams like JEE Main

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Is WBJEE reservation available for candidates from other states?

No, most reservation benefits in WBJEE are only available to candidates with West Bengal domicile. Candidates from other states are usually considered under the general category.

### Q2. **What documents are required to claim reservation in WBJEE 2026?**

Candidates need to submit valid documents such as caste certificate, domicile certificate, EWS certificate, income certificate (for TFW), PwD certificate, or defence certificate, depending on the category.

### Q3.  Is there reservation in WBJEE for BTech courses?

Yes, reservation in WBJEE applies to BTech and other courses offered through participating colleges, as per category-wise seat allocation.

### Q4. Can I apply for multiple reservation categories in WBJEE?

A candidate can apply under multiple categories if eligible (like PwD along with SC/ST/OBC), but must provide valid documents for each during counselling.

### Q5. **Is domicile mandatory for WBJEE reservation?**

Yes, for most reserved categories like SC, ST, OBC, EWS, and TFW, West Bengal domicile is required to claim reservation benefits.
