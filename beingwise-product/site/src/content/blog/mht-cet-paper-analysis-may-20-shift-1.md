## Subject-wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis 2026

The MHT CET PCM exam consists of 2 sections, Paper 1 and Paper 2. The first 90 minutes of testing are for Physics and Chemistry combined, and the second 90 minutes of testing are for Mathematics. Let’s now go through the detailed subject wise MHT CET 2026 May 20 exam analysis for the morning shift below.

### MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis for Physics Section

From the MHT CET 2026 Shift 1 Paper Analysis, it is seen that the Physics paper was of a moderate level. Mostly, questions are asked about formulas and numerical calculations. Tricky conceptual questions are asked in Class 12.

- Current Electricity
- Electrostatics
- Semiconductor
- Ray Optics
- Thermodynamics
- Modern Physics
- Magnetic Effects of Current
- Oscillations

### MHT CET 2026 Exam Analysis for Chemistry Section

Chemistry was relatively easier compared to other sections of the May 20 Shift 1 exam paper. As per the reports, many questions asked were directly related to theories and points from the NCERT textbooks. Organic chemistry had good weightage, along with physical chemistry numericals.  Take a look at the MHT CET high-weightage​ discussed in Chemistry:

- Chemical Bonding
- Coordination Compounds
- Amines
- Electrochemistry
- Biomolecules
- Thermodynamics
- p-Block Elements
- Solutions

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Maths Section

Mathematics was relatively more challenging in terms of calculation and time. Many students found the calculations tricky in some integration- and coordinate-geometry-related questions. Important topics discussed from Mathematics:

- Integration
- Differential Equations
- Probability
- Vectors
- Matrices and Determinants
- Application of Derivatives
- Straight Line and Plane
- Trigonometry

## Student Reactions on May 20 MHT CET Shift 1 paper​

Students who have taken the MHT CET 2026 May 20 Shift 1 PCM exam had mixed reactions after the exam.

- Rohit felt that the Physics paper was balanced. He stated that there were no difficult topics, but some numerical problems took more time to solve, which made the questions tricky.
- For Ananya Deshpande, the easiest part of the examination paper was Chemistry. As per her observations, the majority of the questions were very close to the NCERT concepts and quite easily attempted.
- As far as the Mathematics portion is concerned, Siddharth Patil informed that the Mathematics part was quite lengthy and included several questions requiring calculations. He added that the sections related to Integration and Probability required quite some time for their completion.
- On the other hand, Priyanka Joshi observed that the pattern of this paper was not very different from the previous MHT CET examination papers. Hence, she thought that candidates having prepared through mock and previous year question papers must have easily handled the test.
