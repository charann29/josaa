## Dos and Don’ts to Ace MBA Interview Questions

To crack your MBA interview, you need the right mix of preparation, Confidence, and smart strategy. Here are a few simple do’s and don’ts that will help you share your story in a way that excites the admissions team and increases your chances of getting selected.

### Dos For Program-Specific MBA Interview Questions

It is important to show the panel why their program is right for you instead of why you need to enrol in their program. It is advised to prepare a program-specific set of MBA questions and answers to easily tackle their questions regarding the curriculum, values, culture, teaching style, etc. You can also include the specifics and unique learning opportunities that you like in their program. Prepare program-specific MBA Questions and practice them, as this shows them you’ve done your homework and are deliberately choosing their MBA program.

### Don’ts for Program-Specific MBA Interview Questions

The interview panel sitting before you has interviewed many individuals and can easily sense rehearsed, generic answers and answers with genuine interest. Don’t try to give generic responses such as ‘I want to enrol in this program because I strongly believe this enhances my career prospects’. Instead, tell them how you can add value to their ecosystem, for example, you can mention your recent trips, adventures, book fairs, etc. The admission panel wants someone who can take the lead in organising trips and planning events, so customise your responses accordingly.

### Dos For Situational MBA Interview Questions

Candidates are advised to structure their answers in a storytelling format rather than giving plain or mechanical answers. Consider Situation, Task, Action, Result(STAR Method) as this helps in giving a structured approach for you to answer. Practice storytelling as it makes a lasting impact on the admission panel and can increase your chances of admission. Inculcate situations where you’ve shown impactful results in your team or driven results. This helps in showcasing your abilities.

### Don’ts For Situational MBA Interview Questions

Don’t panic and answer their question confidently. While it is very common to feel anxious for some questions take a moment and answer the interview panel confidently. Stories are great, but stick to the relevance instead of rambling.

### Dos For Skill-Related MBA Interview Questions and Answers

Business schools welcome students who can thrive in teams and can actively lead the teams. Share examples from your experience where your leadership skills and collaborative skills brought team success. This can enhance your chances of securing admissions as you are well prepared for the real-world business challenges.

**Ex:** You can talk about how you’ve come up with ideas to tackle a challenging project, etc.

### Don’ts For Skill-Related MBA Interview Questions and Answers

Although it’s crucial to emphasise your skills, it is advised not to completely focus on your individual achievements. MBA programs foster teamwork, collaboration and leadership, so it is important to demonstrate that you fit well in the ecosystem and contribute to the mutual success. Emphasising individual success can trigger the admission committee about your ability to work with teams.

### Dos for Behavioral MBA Interview Questions

Be prepared for behavioural questions based on your past experiences, as they can reflect qualities such as leadership, flexibility, and problem-solving skills. Prepare your responses beforehand and use the STAR method for clear answers. Hypothetical questions such as “How would you manage a conflict in your team?” can showcase your decision-making ability and ability to respond under pressure. Ensure you answer these questions logically by breaking down the problem and asking the follow-up questions if the situation is unclear.

### Don’ts for Behavioral MBA Interview Questions and Answers

Admission committees can sense rehearsed answers, so avoid scripted answers to maintain the flow of the interview. Be authentic and give genuine responses instead of robotic answers. Sounds like you are genuinely interested in enrolling on the program and enthusiastic to contribute to the business ecosystem.

## Consider Following up With a Thankyou Note

A thank-you note is a formal response to the admission panel’s time and consideration. Thank them for providing you with an opportunity to discuss about your qualifications and experience with them. A Thank-you note showcases professionalism and attention to detail. It showcases your genuine interest in the MBA program, and it creates a positive impression among the admission panel.

**Note: Keep the Thankyou note short and don’t try to include anything that you’ve discussed in the interview here. The purpose here is to showcase your interest and not to sell yourself.**

## Bonus Tips to Ace an MBA Interview: Do’s and Don’ts

It is important to consider some points while appearing for an interview for admission into the business school. Keep the following things in mind while appearing for the interview:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Do’s** | **Don’ts** |
| Dress professionally, as it creates a positive impression among the admission panel | Be on time, ensure you plan your travel accordingly. |
| If allowed, offer a firm handshake instead of loose or tight ones. This creates a sense of professionalism. | Carry your file professionally instead of holding it in awkward ways. |
| Maintain eye contact with panellists but don’t stare at them. This showcases your confidence. | Don’t sound aggressive or arrogant. Maintain a low pitch yet sound confident in your answers. |
| Understand your program thoroughly and prepare for the questions related to it. This shows your genuine interest towards the program. | Maintain your energy throughout the interview and don’t lose confidence for those answers you’ll not be able to answer. |
| In case of tricky questions you are unsure about, ask permission and take a moment to structure your answer. | Don’t emphasise your individual achievements; instead, focus more on how you contributed to the group’s success. |
| Do showcase your academic excellence and emphasise your projects about how they shaped you, etc. | Don’t go off track while answering the question. Stick to what is relevant and answer confidently. |
| Present yourself as a well-rounded individual who can work efficiently with teams and collaborate with team members to deal with challenging projects. | Don’t be judgmental about your interview performance, as it can affect your flow and might negatively impact it. |
