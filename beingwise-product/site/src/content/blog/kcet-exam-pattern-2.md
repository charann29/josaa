## Table of Contents

KEA has released **KCET Exam Pattern 2026 on** [**cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea**](http://cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea)**.** Following the exam pattern, KEA will conduct the exam in offline mode. Candidates will have a total of 3 hours and 20 minutes to complete the paper. There will be 3 papers, i.e., Physics, Chemistry, Math/Biology.  Each paper will have 60 Marks. The exam authority will conduct the exam in 4 sessions. Candidates will have a total duration of 3 hours and 20 minutes to complete all the papers. On the first day, the Mathematics and Biology papers will be held in two separate sessions, while on the second day, the Physics and Chemistry exams will be conducted in two sessions.

## KCET Exam Pattern 2026: Marking Scheme

Candidates appearing for KCET 2026 exam pattern must be aware of the marking scheme. For more details related to KCET Marking scheme, you can refer to the section below:

- Each **correct answer carries 1 mark**.
- There is **no negative marking**.
- No marks are awarded for unanswered questions.
- If multiple answers are marked for one question, no marks will be given.

## KCET Exam Pattern 2026: Important Points to Remember

For KCET 2026 aspirants, understanding a few important insights about the exam pattern and recent trends can help in preparing more effectively. Based on previous years’ analysis, the following key points should be kept in mind:

- The KCET exam is conducted in offline (pen-and-paper) mode using an OMR answer sheet.
- Each subject paper is conducted for 80 minutes.
- There are 60 questions in each subject, making a total of 180 questions in the exam.
- The total marks for KCET are 180, with 1 mark awarded for each correct answer.
- There is no negative marking, which leads to a highe**r attempt rate of around 90–95% per paper**, compared to exams like JEE where the attempt rate is lower.
- Questions from 1st PUC syllabus are generally straightforward, while 2nd PUC questions are usually more application-based.
- In Physics, topics like **Gravitation have appeared frequently** in recent papers.
- In Mathematics, topics such as **Vectors and 3D Geometry often show higher error** rates among candidates.

## Conclusion

KCET Exam Pattern 2026 is simple and student-friendly. The exam includes three papers i) Physics, ii) Chemistry, and iii) Mathematics or Biology, with 60 questions in each subject and a total of 180 marks. Since there is no negative marking, candidates can attempt more questions without worrying about losing marks.

To perform well in KCET 2026, students should clearly understand the exam pattern, marking scheme, and important chapters. Regular practice, focusing on high-weightage topics, and solving previous year papers can help improve both speed and accuracy in the exam

## Frequently Asked Questions

### **1. What is the KCET exam pattern 2026?**

The KCET Exam Pattern 2026 includes three papers: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology. Each paper has 60 multiple-choice questions and carries 60 marks. The exam is conducted in offline (pen-and-paper) mode, and each paper lasts for 80 minutes.

### 2. Is there negative marking in KCET 2026?

No, there is no negative marking in KCET 2026. Candidates receive 1 mark for each correct answer, and no marks are deducted for incorrect responses.

### 3. What are the total marks in KCET 2026?

The total marks in KCET 2026 are 180. Each subject carries 60 marks, and the total score is calculated based on the combination of subjects relevant to the course stream.

### 4. What is the duration of the KCET exam?

Each paper in the KCET exam is conducted for 1 hour 20 minutes (80 minutes). Since there are three papers, the total exam duration is 3 hours and 20 minutes.

### 5. What is the KCET exam pattern and syllabus?

The KCET exam pattern and syllabus are based on the First-Year and Second-Year PUC curriculum of Karnataka. The exam covers topics from Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology.

### 6. What is the official website for KCET exam pattern 2026?

The official website for checking the KCET exam pattern 2026 is cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea, where candidates can also find updates on syllabus, admit card, and exam dates.

### 7. When will KCET 2026 be conducted?

According to the latest schedule, the KCET 2026 exam will be conducted on April 23 and April 24, 2026 in multiple sessions.

### 8. What is the subject-wise paper pattern in KCET 2026?

In KCET 2026, each subject paper includes 60 objective questions, each carrying 1 mark. The subjects include Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (for engineering aspirants), and Biology (for pharmacy/agriculture aspirants).
