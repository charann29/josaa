## JEE Main Session 2 Applications Procedure

NTA manages the entire JEE Main 2026 registration process online via the JEE Main Session 2 applications process, also known as JEE Mains Session 2 registration.

The official NTA exam schedule instructs candidates to perform all the required tasks on time to prevent disqualification during JEE Main Session 2. Here is a very simple step-by-step process.

**Step 1: Visit the Official Website.**

To begin the JEE Mains Session 2 registration process, visit the official JEE Main website. The official JEE Main website contains all the updates regarding JEE Main Session 2, including the JEE Main Session 2 exam date, correction window, admit card, and result of Session 2.

**Step 2: New Registration**

Click on the registration link and enter basic details such as name, date of birth, mobile number, and email ID. This step is mandatory for JEE Mains Session 2 registration, even if you are a new candidate applying only for JEE Main Session 2.

**Step 3: Fill the Application Form**

After registration, log in and submit the JEE Main Session 2 application form by entering personal details, academic details, and exam choices. Be cautious while selecting exam cities because the selected city will be displayed later on the JEE Main Session 2 exam city intimation slip.

**Step 4: Upload Documents**

Scan your photo, signature, and other necessary documents and upload them in the required format. Any mistake in the upload process may lead to the rejection of your JEE Main Session 2 applications.

**Step 5: Pay the Application Fee**

Pay the application fee through debit card, credit card, net banking, or UPI. The form will be deemed complete only when the payment is successful, before the JEE Mains Session 2 registration last date.

**Step 6: Download the Confirmation Page**

After successful submission, download the confirmation page. This will come in handy later for downloading the JEE Mains Session 2 admit card, checking the JEE Main Session 2 exam date, tracking the JEE Main Session 2 admit card release date, viewing the JEE Main Session 2 answer key date, and checking the JEE Mains Session 2 result date.

## Who Can Appear For JEE Mains Session 2?

Many students have this doubt when applying for JEE Mains session 2: Can I appear for the 2nd session if I appeared in the 1st one as well? The answer is Yes, students can take part in all the sessions of JEE Mains. Refer to the following to know some of the highlights of JEE Mains session 2 eligibility criteria.

- Must have passed Class 12 in 2024 or 2025, or be appearing in 2026
- No age limit to appear in JEE Main
- Required subjects in Class 12 depend on the course (Maths compulsory for BTech)
- There are no limits as to the number of times you can appear in both Session 1 and Session 2.
- In case you were present in Session 1, you are required to fill in another form, as well as pay the fee for Session 2.
- In case you did not turn up or enrol in Session 1, you are free to apply and complete Session 2 on your own.
- The maximum score and percentage attained in the sessions conducted by NTA will be taken into consideration for the final merit list.
- As a result, there is no need to finish session 1 before being able to finish session 2, since they are two separate opportunities to be a better performer.

## JEE Mains Counselling and Admission Process Overview

After the declaration of the JEE Main results, eligible candidates can participate in the counselling and admission procedure, which is organised largely by JoSAA and CSAB. Candidates register for counselling based on the rank obtained in JEE Main, enter their choices of colleges and streams, and undergo multiple sessions of seat allocation.

The seats are assigned to students according to their ranking, the choice they make, category, and the availability of the seat. Once the allocation of seats is done, candidates have to confirm their seat allocation by submitting the fees for their seat allocation and physically reporting themselves to the given institute, where their documents are to be verified and the final admission confirmed.
