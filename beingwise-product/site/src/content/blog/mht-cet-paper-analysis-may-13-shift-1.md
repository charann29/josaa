## Subject-wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis 2026

The MHT CET 2nd attempt exam for the PCM group was held from May 12 to May 21, 2026. The first 90 minutes were planned for Physics and Chemistry, while the remaining 90 minutes were for Mathematics. Let’s take a look at the subject-wise MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 for PCM groups below.

### MHT CET 2026 Exam Analysis for Physics Section

As per the MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 May 13 Shift 1 Paper, the Physics section was relatively moderate in terms of difficulty, which was primarily based on understanding concepts and formula applications. A few numerical problems were comparatively lengthy, thus posing challenges for students in time management. But candidates prepared with formulas and PYQs did well.

**Have a look at the areas where questions are asked in the shift.**

- Electrostatics and Current Electricity covered high weightage in the paper.
- Modern Physics & Semiconductor Devices were frequently asked.
- SHM, Waves & Oscillations were often asked in the test paper.
- Conceptual questions of Magnetic Effects of Current & Electromagnetic Induction were asked.

## MHT CET 2026 Maths Section Analysis

The mathematics section of the MHT CET 2026 May 13 Shift 1 examination was moderate and lengthy. Though mostly formula-based, questions were lengthy, thus requiring speedy calculation skills. Candidates well-versed with PYQs and mock tests did well in the section.

**Check out the important areas where questions are asked during the shift.**

- The topic Calculus had the maximum weightage in the exam.
- Various questions were asked on Integration and Differentiation topics.
- Topics Vector Algebra, 3D Geometry, and Line & Plane were frequently tested.
- Moderate weightage was given to Probability and Matrices topics.
- Several questions required extensive calculation and application-based knowledge.

### MHT CET 2026 Exam Analysis for the Chemistry Section

The Chemistry section was considered the easiest and scoring part of the MHT CET 2026 May 13 Shift 1 exam. Most of the Inorganic Chemistry questions were conceptual. The majority of questions in the section were NCERT-based and theory-based.

**Check out the important Chemistry topics covered in the shift.**

- A significant weightage was given to Organic Chemistry.
- Frequent questions were asked on topics such as Coordination Compounds and Chemical Bonding.
- Formula-based Physical Chemistry numerical problems were simple.

## MHT CET Counselling 2026

The MHT CET 2026 Centralized Admission Process (CAP). It will be an online process conducted by the Maharashtra State CET Cell. Admission for engineering and pharmacy programmes will be granted after completion of three rounds of CAP. Take a key highlight of MHT CET Counselling 2026

- **Online Registration & Fees Submission:**

The applicants will have to perform online registration and pay the counselling fees. The counselling fees for the candidates of the General category will be ₹1,000, while the candidates in the reserved categories need to pay ₹800.

- **Filling Choices for CAP Rounds:**

The candidates will have to fill in choices and lock them individually for each counselling round.

- **Publication of Merit List:**

The provisional merit list will be released first by the State CET Cell, followed by the release of the final merit list in July 2026.

- **Process of Seat Allotment:**

Seats will be allotted depending on criteria like percentile in MHT CET, merit rank, category, available seats, and chosen colleges.

- **Expected Cut Off for Top Colleges:**

To get admission into reputed institutes such as COEP & VJTI, applicants require scoring 90–95+ percentiles. For those scoring about 70-80 percentiles, college predictor tools can help select colleges.
