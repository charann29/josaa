Jadavpur University  is a public state university located in Kolkata, West Bengal. It was founded on 24 December 1955 under Jadavpur University. The university is self-affiliated, recognised by the University Grants Commission (UGC), and approved by AICTE, NCTE, and the Council of Architecture (COA). It operates through four faculties across multiple departments, offering programmes in Engineering and Technology, Science, Arts, and Interdisciplinary Studies.

Jadavpur University Kolkata is a government institution. It holds NAAC accreditation with grade A+ and CGPA 3.46 in the fourth cycle. As per NIRF 2025, it is ranked 1st among State Public Universities, 9th in the University category, and 18th in Engineering. The QS World University Rankings 2025 placed it at 721–730 globally. The BE/BTech total fee for most engineering branches is approximately INR 10,000–12,000 for four years, making it one of the most affordable engineering universities in India. In the 2024 placement season, 830 out of 913 registered engineering students were placed, with an average package of INR 11 LPA.

### Jadavpur University: Is It Government or Private?

Jadavpur University is a government university. It is a state-funded public institution established under the Jadavpur University Act, 1955. The Government of West Bengal funds the university. The Chancellor is the Governor of West Bengal. As of 2025, the Vice-Chancellor is Dr. Chiranjib Bhattacharjee. It is not a private or deemed university, nor is it affiliated to any other university. It is self-affiliated and awards its own degrees.

### How to reach the main campus?

- **By Rail**: Jadavpur Railway Station is on the Sealdah-Diamond Harbour suburban line. It is 8 km from Sealdah Station.
- **By Metro**: The nearest Metro stations are Rabindra Sarobar and Mahanayak Uttam Kumar on the Green Line. Auto-rickshaws and buses connect both stations to the campus.
- **By Road:** The campus is 15 km from Howrah Station and 25 km from Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport.
- **By Bus**: Buses E1, AC-1, and S-5 from Howrah Station stop at Bengal Lamp, which is a 5-minute walk from the campus.

### Jadavpur University Campus Area and Infrastructure

The main campus covers 58 acres in Jadavpur. The Salt Lake campus covers 26 acres. Together, the two campuses house the majority of BTech and engineering programmes. The Salt Lake campus is used for several BTech specialisations including Information Technology, Construction Engineering, Instrumentation Engineering, Power Engineering, and Printing Engineering.

**Academic Buildings:** The main campus has separate department buildings for Civil, Electrical, Mechanical, Chemical, Computer Science, Electronics and Telecommunication, and other engineering branches. The campus also has a dedicated UG Science Building, PG Science Building, and the main administrative block, Aurobindo Bhavan.

**Central Library:** The Jadavpur University Central Library holds over 6.4 lakh books and 80,000 journals. It provides access to digital databases including Scopus, e-books, and online research resources. It is located on the main campus and is open to students, faculty, and research scholars across all departments.

**Sports Facilities:** The campus has a 6-acre sports complex. Facilities include cricket and football grounds, badminton courts, tennis courts, kabaddi courts, a gymnasium, and an indoor stadium.

**Other Campus Facilities:**

- Free Wi-Fi is available across the campus, though connectivity may vary by area.
- The campus has a health centre, a daycare centre, a guest house, canteens, a post office, a bank, and book shops.
- There are 7 auditoriums on the main campus, including Gandhi Bhavan and Triguna Sen Auditorium.
- An Open Air Theatre with a seating capacity of approximately 3,000 is also on campus.
- A CAD Lab with 58 PCs and 14 workstations is located in the Production Engineering Building.

### Jadavpur University Hostel

Jadavpur University has 13 hostels with a combined capacity of approximately 1,600 students. There are 9 hostels for boys and 4 for girls. A separate hostel is available for research scholars. The main hostel complex is located just outside the main campus. The hostel mess is run by the student mess committee under the supervision of the hostel superintendent. Canteens are available at the main hostel complex, the ladies hostel complex, and beside the old PG hostel.

## Jadavpur University Admission Process

Admission to BE/BTech at Jadavpur University is based entirely on WBJEE rank. The university does not conduct a separate entrance test for engineering admissions. Seat allocation happens through the centralised counselling conducted by the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board (WBJEEB). The steps below describe the standard admission process for BTech.

1. Appear for WBJEE and obtain a valid rank.
2. Register on the WBJEEB counselling portal at wbjeeb.nic.in.
3. Fill in choices for college and branch in order of preference.
4. WBJEEB releases the seat allotment result based on rank and choices.
5. Report to the allotted institution with required documents for verification.
6. Pay the admission fee to confirm the seat.

Note: Candidates must visit jadavpuruniversity.in for the official admission schedule, application links, and course-specific notifications.

## Jadavpur University: Why Choose It?

Jadavpur University offers a combination of nationally ranked academics, extremely low fees, strong placements, and research output that very few government engineering universities in India can match. Here is a factual summary of the key reasons students choose this university.

- The total B.Tech fee for most engineering branches is INR 10,000–12,000 for four years. This is among the lowest at any ranked engineering university in India. For IT, the fee is approximately INR 1 lakh for four years — still far lower than most private colleges.
- NIRF 2025 ranks JU 1st among all State Public Universities in India, 9th in the University category, and 18th in Engineering. These are official, verifiable figures from the Ministry of Education.
- The 2024 engineering placement rate was approximately 91%. The average package was INR 11 LPA. These figures are sourced from NIRF 2025 and Shiksha placement data.
- JU is one of only a few state universities to feature in the QS World University Rankings and the QS Sustainability World Ranking. Its research output has earned it the UGC “University with Potential for Excellence” status.
- Multiple scholarships are available, including the Swami Vivekananda Merit-Cum-Means scholarship, which offers INR 60,000 per year to eligible students with family income below INR 2.5 lakh.

### Conclusion

Jadavpur University, Kolkata, is a state-funded public research university established in 1955. It has three campuses totalling approximately 94 acres, with the main campus covering 60 acres in Jadavpur. It operates through 36 departments across four faculties and serves over 12,000 students as of 2025.

NIRF 2025 ranks it 1st among State Public Universities and 18th overall in India. The QS World University Rankings 2025 places it at 721–730 globally. It holds NAAC A+ accreditation with CGPA 3.46. The BTech fee for most branches is under INR 12,000 for four years. In 2024, 830 engineering students were placed, with an average package of INR 11 LPA and a highest domestic package of INR 57 LPA.

Admission to BTech is strictly through WBJEE. The CSE branch is the most competitive, with closing ranks under 100 for the General category in recent years. Students from West Bengal occupy 90% of General category seats, with 10% open to All India students.

For students who clear WBJEE with a strong rank, Jadavpur University offers one of the best returns on investment in Indian higher education. The combination of low cost, national ranking, research recognition, and strong industry placement makes it a first-choice destination for engineering aspirants across the state and the country.
