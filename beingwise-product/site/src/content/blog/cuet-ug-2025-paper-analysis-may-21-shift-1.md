## CUET UG Exam Pattern 2025

Students are tested across the language, aptitude, and subject knowledge. For each subject, candidates are given 60 minutes to complete the examination. Check the following table for a more detailed CUET exam pattern:

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Section** | **No of Questions** | **Duration of the Exam** | **Questions Type** |
| Section IA- Language | 50 Questions | 60 minutes for each subject | Questions on reading comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary are asked to test the candidate’s English proficiency. |
| Section II- Domain Specific | 50 Questions | Questions based on the domain-specific subjects are asked to test the candidate’s subject proficiency. |
| Section III- General Test | 50 Questions | This section assesses a candidate’s proficiency in General Knowledge, Current Affairs, Logical and Analytical Reasoning, and Mental Ability. It also evaluates Numerical and Quantitative Reasoning using fundamental concepts from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration, typically taught up to Grade 8. |

## Student Initial Reaction for CUET Exam: May 21st Shift 1 Exam

Check the student’s initial reaction after the exam:

**1. Kunal (from Delhi):** “The overall paper was easy, especially Chemistry and English. I attempted 42 questions in Chemistry because everything felt directly from the NCERT. GAT was tricky, and Quant had too many lengthy questions. Physics was moderate but manageable if you had prepared for the boards well.”

**2. Meera (from Hyderabad):** “Political Science and History were very straightforward. Some questions even came from the NCERT side boxes. I was surprised by the cartoon-based questions, especially in History. GAT was tougher than I expected, especially General Science and GK. Time was a big issue.”

**3. Aditya (from Bhopal):** “Physics had about 10 numericals, most of which were calculation-heavy but doable. The focal length question in optics caught me off guard. Chemistry was super easy; Organic chemistry was dominant. GAT felt hard because of too many math questions. I should’ve practised more on speed.”

**4. Sanika (from Lucknow):** “English was very easy, synonyms, antonyms, and comprehension questions were straightforward. Chemistry was a breeze, too. The GK section in GAT was tough, though, with unexpected questions. Thankfully, Reasoning wasn’t too bad.”

**5. Rohan (from Jaipur):** “History and Political Science felt smooth — everything was from NCERT. Physics was a bit time-consuming, especially the numericals. I attempted 41 questions in Chemistry. GAT was my weak spot — more practice in Quant would’ve helped a lot.”

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. **What is the CUET UG 2025 exam pattern?**

CUET UG 2025 is divided into three sections: Language, Domain-Specific Subjects, and General Test. Each section consists of 50 questions and is timed for 60 minutes.

### 2. ******************When will the CUET UG 2025 result be declared?******************

The CUET result date 2025 is expected to be in the third or fourth week of June. Stay updated through the CUET official website: cuet.nta.nic.in.

### **3. **********************What are the reporting and shift timings for the CUET exam 2025?************************

For Shift 1, reporting starts at 7:00 AM and the exam runs from 9:00 AM to 10:00 AM. Shift 2 begins at 12:00 PM, with reporting at 11:00 AM.

### **4. **************What items are allowed in the CUET exam centre?****************

Students can carry their CUET UG 2025 admit card, a valid photo ID, a passport-sized photograph, a transparent water bottle, a basic pen, and a 50ml hand sanitiser.

### 5. ****Are electronic devices allowed in the CUET exam 2025?****

No. Items such as mobile phones, calculators, smartwatches, and earphones are strictly prohibited inside the examination centre.
