The KEAM 2026 exam will be conducted between April 13 and 25. Before applying, candidates intending to undertake the engineering courses in Kerala must refer to the latest KEAM exam pattern. The engineering entrance examination will be held in the form of one online paper, which will have objective-based questions.

The KEAM exam pattern 2026 shows that the paper will consist of 150 multiple-choice questions, which will be answered in three hours. Questions will be formulated according to the higher secondary syllabus of Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, with Mathematics bearing the maximum marks.

The test will be taken in the KEAM 2026 CBT format to have a transparent evaluation process. According to the KEAM marking scheme, 4 marks will be given whenever the correct response is given, and 1 mark will be taken away whenever the incorrect response is given.

There are no marks deducted or added when questions are not answered. Applicants have to achieve the required minimum KEAM qualifying marks 2026 as stipulated by the authorities in order to be ranked. Refer to the following to learn about the latest KEAM exam pattern 2026.

## KEAM Exam Pattern 2026 | Engineering Paper

Candidates may check out the following to go through the engineering paper pattern of KEAM. The KEAM exam pattern will assist students in becoming more familiar with the exam flow and plan their preparation steps accordingly.

- **Mode of Exam:** Computer-based test (CBT)
- **Number of Papers:** One Paper (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics)
- **Language:** English
- **Total Number of Questions:** 150 questions
- **Question Distribution:** Mathematics: 75, Physics: 45, Chemistry: 30
- **Total Marks:** 600 marks
- **Type of Questions:** Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
- **Time Duration:** 3 hours

## KEAM Syllabus 2026

Students can get a brief overview of the KEAM syllabus 2026 for the engineering exam below. Along with the KEAM exam pattern, students must check out the KEAM 2026 syllabus to plan a proper and wise preparation strategy.

**Physics:** Physical world and measurement, kinematics, laws of motion, work, energy and power, motion of system of particles, gravitation, properties of solids and liquids, thermodynamics, oscillations and waves, electrostatics, current electricity, magnetic effects of current, electromagnetic induction, alternating current, electromagnetic waves, optics, dual nature of radiation and matter, atoms and nuclei, electronic devices.

**Chemistry:** Some basic concepts of chemistry, structure of atom, classification of elements and periodicity, chemical bonding, states of matter, thermodynamics, equilibrium, redox reactions, hydrogen, s-block and p-block elements, organic chemistry basics, hydrocarbons, environmental chemistry, solid state, solutions, electrochemistry, chemical kinetics, surface chemistry, coordination compounds, polymers, biomolecules, chemistry in everyday life.

**Mathematics:** Sets, relations and functions, trigonometric functions, inverse trigonometric functions, matrices and determinants, continuity and differentiability, applications of derivatives, integrals and applications of integrals, differential equations, vector algebra, three-dimensional geometry, linear programming, probability, mathematical reasoning, complex numbers and quadratic equations.

## Calculation of the KEAM Rank 2026

The rank in KEAM 2025 is determined by giving the entrance examination and Class 12 board examination similar weight to the performance of the candidate. In Kerala, the rank list is compiled using a formula of 50:50 weightage of the Commissioner of Entrance Examinations.

It means that the weight of the normalised score obtained in the KEAM entrance test will be equal to the weight obtained in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in the Class 12 board examination. The two elements are integrated once they have been normalised to ensure that efficiency is disseminated to different education boards.

**The following are some important points that should be considered with reference to the KEAM 2025 Rank Calculation:**

- Normalisation of the KEAM entrance examination score by 50 per cent.
- Weights of Class 12 marks in Physics, Math and Chemistry.
- Board marks are normalised so as to bring parity between board marks.
- The outcome is that the final rank list is prepared by adding both the normalised scores.
- The location is one of the factors deciding counselling and seat assignments.
