### Stages of the MHT CET counselling process

The MHT CET counselling process is conducted entirely online and consists of multiple stages.

**Stage 1: Online Registration**

The first stage involves the online registration for MHT CET CAP Counselling 2026 on the official website of the counselling process. It involves giving personal, academic and exam related information of the candidate and creating the counselling profile.

**Stage 2: Counselling Fee Payment**

As part of the application process, candidates must make payment of the prescribed counselling fee for MHT CET CAP 2026. The estimated fee for this is ₹1,000 for general category candidates while ₹800 for other categories.

**Stage 3: Verification of Documents**

The next stage involves uploading and verifying required documents by candidates for their counselling purposes. This helps in ensuring that they have given proper and correct details and are eligible for the counselling process.

**Stage 4: Publishing of MHT CET Merit List**

After processing, the Maharashtra CET Cell would publish the provisional merit list of candidates depending upon the MHT CET percentiles. They can also raise objections and then the final merit list shall be published.

**Stage 5: Choice Filling & Preferences**

Students need to fill their preferences for the courses they wish to pursue as well as the colleges they wish to apply to. It is recommended to include both dream and safe colleges among other options.

**Stage 6: Seat Allotment under CAP Round 1**

The initial process of seat allotment occurs depending upon the criteria likeMHT CET Percentile Score, Rank, Caste Category, College Preference and Branch Preference, Seat Availability. Candidate who gets a seat may accept the same or continue in subsequent rounds for getting better options.

**Stage 7: Seat Allotment under CAP Round 2**

Those candidates who fail to receive an allotment during the first round or those who wish to improve their allotted seats may appear in Round 2 of CAP Process. New seat allotment shall be carried out depending upon the number of vacant seats and preference of candidates.

**Stage 8: Seat Allotment under CAP Round 3**

The last counselling process shall take place to fill the remaining vacant seats in all participating institutes. Those candidates who are still without an admission may apply for this round.

**Stage 9: Seat Acceptance and Fee Payment**

In case of an allotted seat, the candidate needs to accept the allotment within the specified time period and pay the necessary fees.

**Stage 10: Reporting to Allotted College**

The selected candidate must report himself at the allotted college with the original certificates. The candidate must complete the admission process through the verification process by the institute.

Ans. The MHT CET scorecard includes candidate details, application number, subject-wise percentile scores, overall percentile score, qualifying status, and examination session details.

Ans. A percentile above 90 is considered good and offers opportunities in reputed colleges, while above 95 increases chances of admission to top engineering institutes.

Ans. Yes, candidates with around 70 percentile can get admission into various private and regional engineering colleges depending on branch preferences.
