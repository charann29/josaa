## What is the NIAT Upskilling Training Program?

NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies is an upskilling program that operates within Centres of Excellence provided by collaborating universities across India. It is not a university, college, or academic institution, and does not offer degrees or diplomas of any kind.

NIAT is designed to run alongside a student’s university degree journey, bridging the gap between academic education and real industry expectations. While the collaborating university manages admissions, degree coursework, examinations, and degree conferral, NIAT focuses entirely on building students’ industry-ready competencies through:

- Industry-Aligned Training Modules: Skill-focused content built around what today’s job market demands, not a fixed academic syllabus
- Project-Based Learning: Hands-on, real-world projects that build a strong portfolio and practical problem-solving ability
- Structured Skill-Map: A clearly defined progression path taking students from foundational skills all the way to advanced, job-ready capabilities
- Dedicated Mentors, Trainers, and Success Coaches: Deployed by NIAT and entirely separate from the university’s academic faculty, providing personalised guidance and continuous support throughout the program

Upon successful completion, students receive an IRC from NIAT, recognising their achievement in the upskilling program. This complements, but is entirely separate from, the academic degree awarded by the collaborating university.

## Where are the NIAT Upskilling Programs Offered in the Collaborating Universities?

Multiple universities across India combine academic rigour with practical exposure, creating a tech-forward environment designed to prepare students for the demands of today’s industry. These universities collaborate with NIAT to offer NxtWave’s upskilling program within their dedicated Training Centres or Centres of Excellence.

Students enrolled at these universities will receive a **UGC-recognised B.Tech degree** awarded entirely by the collaborating university, upon successfully completing the university’s own coursework, examinations, and fulfilling the necessary academic requirements as per UGC and AICTE guidelines. The degree is conferred solely by the university, not by NIAT.

Alongside their degree journey, students also undergo NIAT’s industry-focused upskilling program and, upon successful completion, receive an **IRC** issued by NIAT. This certification recognises their achievement and participation in NxtWave’s upskilling program and is entirely separate from the university’s academic award.

NIAT’s upskilling program is co-curricular in nature and does not interfere with or affect a student’s degree progression at the university.

## B.Tech CSE

The Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) in Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) is a four-year undergraduate program offered by the university, where the NIAT upskilling program is available. While pursuing this degree, students also gain hands-on training through NIAT’s structured Skill-map, designed to help them build strong fundamentals in computer science and develop industry-relevant skills. With guidance from experienced mentors and real-world project work, students get ready for top tech careers in areas like software development, systems design, and problem-solving.

**Key areas of focus include:**

- Programming languages and software development
- Algorithms and data structures
- Computer networks and security
- Database management systems
- Operating Systems Principles

## Frequently Asked Questions

### **1. What is NIAT?**

NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies is an upskilling program launched in 2023 and run by NxtWave. It operates within Training Centres or Centres of Excellence provided by collaborating universities across India. NIAT is not a university, college, or academic institution and does not award degrees or diplomas of any kind. NIAT is offering its industry-aligned upskilling programs at 20+ universities across India.

### **2. Does the NIAT’s upskilling program offer placement support?**

Yes. NIAT facilitates opportunities from 3,000+ companies; from Fortune 500 giants to fast-growing startups, with a dedicated placement team to assist with job applications, resume building, and interview preparation.

### **3. What are the eligibility criteria for admission to the universities where NIAT’s upskilling program is offered?**

The NIAT team conducts assessments and counselling sessions to understand your skills, interests, and aptitude. Then we recommend the most suitable university where NIAT upskilling programs are offered, based on your preferences.

To get admission to the university, students should meet the eligibility criteria and follow the admission policies established by the university. The admission is solely at the discretion of the university.

### **4. What is the duration of NIAT’s upskilling program?**

NIAT’s upskilling program is designed to provide students with the opportunity to learn advanced technology. The duration of the NIAT’s upskilling program is 4 years.

### **5. Do NIAT provide B.Tech degree?**

No, NIAT does not provide a B.Tech degree. The B.Tech degree is awarded solely by the collaborating university upon successfully completing their coursework, examinations, and academic requirements as per UGC and AICTE guidelines. NIAT only provides an IRC upon completion of its upskilling program.

### 6. What does NIAT provide upon completion of the program?

Upon successful completion of NIAT’s upskilling program, students receive an IRC issued by NxtWave. This is a skill-focused certification that recognises their participation and achievement in the upskilling program. It is entirely separate from and does not substitute for the academic degree awarded by the university.

### 7. What B.Tech specialisations are available at universities collaborated with NIAT’s upskilling program is offered?

The collaborating universities offer B.Tech in CSE with specialisations including Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), Data Science (DS), AI & ML combined, and AI-DS, providing students with a range of in-demand technology-focused degree options

### 8. Can NIAT guarantee admission to a collaborating university?

No. Each collaborating university independently manages its own admissions process based on its eligibility criteria and admission policies. NIAT can act only as a facilitator, sharing your details with the prospective university with your written consent. Admission is solely at the discretion of the university.

### 9. How is NIAT’s training program different from the university’s academic curriculum?

NIAT’s upskilling content is not part of the university’s academic curriculum or syllabus in any way. It consists of industry-aligned training modules, project-based learning, and a structured Skill-Map, all developed independently by NIAT to complement your degree with practical, job-market-relevant skills.
