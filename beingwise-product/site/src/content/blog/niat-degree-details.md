## What are the NIAT Certification & Degree Offered by Collaborating Universities?

**The collaborating universities offer their own degree programs, independent of NIAT. NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) is an upskilling program and does not offer any degrees or diplomas of its own.** The skill map, enriched with real-world projects and capstone experiences, ensures that NIATians are well-equipped to excel in software engineering, AI-driven solutions, and data analytics, making them highly competitive in the global job market.

- Students who enroll in NIAT’s upskilling program and also meet the eligibility criteria of a collaborating university can pursue degree programs, such as B.Tech, offered by that university
- The degree is awarded solely by the collaborating university, not by NIAT.
- NIAT’s role runs **parallel** to the degree program; NxtWave has its own Trainers, Mentors, and Success Coaches to deliver industry-aligned training modules and project-based learning at the university’s Training Centre/Upskilling Centre
- The upskilling program content is not part of the university’s degree curriculum or academic syllabus; it is a skill-focused program designed to make students industry-ready.

**What students receive from NIAT:** An **IRC (Industry Ready Certificate)**, a skill-focused certification issued by NIAT for achievement in the upskilling program. This is not equivalent to a university degree or diploma.

## What is NIAT?

NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies (NIAT) is an upskilling initiative focused on equipping students with industry-relevant skills in emerging technologies. Many universities across India offer NIAT’s upskilling program to equip students with industry-relevant skills. across India to provide students with a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) program in Computer Science Engineering with a specialisation in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science. The B.Tech degree is offered by the collaborating university and not by the NIAT. NIAT aims to bridge the gap between the academy and industry with its skill map designed in collaboration with top professionals from leading tech companies. NIAT emphasises hands-on learning, ensuring that students gain practical experience through real-world projects and capstone initiatives.

Multiple universities across India offer NIAT’s upskilling program. Around 3000+ companies have hired NIATians.

## What is NIAT Skill-Map?

The **[NIAT’s Skill-map](/blog/engineering-colleges/niat-comprehensive-curriculum/)** integrates Computer Science fundamentals, AI, and the MERN Stack, combining theory with hands-on projects to prepare students for top tech roles and global competitions. The Foundational phase covers programming, full-stack development, algorithms, databases, DevOps, and product management. The Specialisation phase focuses on advanced topics like Machine Learning, Data Science, AI/ML production, Generative AI, and Large Language Models (LLMs). Students engage in real-world projects, including capstone projects, system design, big data analytics, and AI model development, while also preparing for industry-leading tech competitions such as Google Summer of Code (GSoC) and ACM ICPC.

## Conclusion

NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) is a comprehensive upskilling program that works alongside collaborating universities across India to prepare students for industry-relevant tech careers. The program is built around an industry-aligned skill map that covers Computer Science fundamentals, AI, the MERN Stack, Machine Learning, Data Science, Generative AI, and Large Language Models, delivered through structured training modules and project-based learning. NxtWave’s own Trainers, Mentors, and Success Coaches conduct these sessions at the Training Centre or Upskilling Centre provided by the collaborating university.

It is important to understand that NIAT does not offer any degree or diploma. The B.Tech degree is awarded solely by the collaborating university, subject to the student meeting that university’s eligibility criteria. NxtWave’s role is complementary, focused entirely on skill-building to help students become industry-ready. Upon successful completion of the upskilling program, students receive an IRC (Industry Ready Certificate) issued by NxtWave, which recognises their hands-on training and achievement in the program. This certificate is not equivalent to a university degree or diploma.

## Frequently Asked Questions

#### 1. What is IRC in NIAT?

The Industry Ready Certificate (IRC) is a prestigious certification awarded to NIATians. Recognised by top tech companies, the IRC boosts job opportunities by validating the industry-relevant skills students acquire during their course. It enhances employability and adds significant value to a student’s resume.

#### 2. Does NIAT offer upskilling programs across multiple cities in India?

Yes. NIAT’s upskilling program is offered at collaborating universities located across multiple cities in India, including Hyderabad, Vijayawada, near Pondicherry, Bangalore, Mangalore, Pune, Jaipur, Delhi NCR, and more. The specific universities and locations can be checked on the official NIAT website.

#### 3. What is the degree offered at the universities offering the NIAT upskilling program?

Universities collaborating with NIAT offer a 4-year B.Tech completion Degree and an Industry certificate for the students.

#### 4. How to apply for the NIAT?

Candidates can navigate to the official website to apply for the NIAT and apply online for their NxtWave Assessment Test by paying the registration fees of INR 1200.

#### 5. Can NIAT guarantee a job after the completion of the upskilling program?

NIAT’s upskilling program is designed to equip students with industry-relevant skills and build competencies aligned with the job market. However, job outcomes depend on individual performance, skills acquired, and market conditions. Students are advised to visit the official NIAT website for accurate placement-related information.

#### 6. Is NIAT approved by UGC, AICTE, or any government body?

NIAT is an upskilling program and not a university, college, or academic institution. Therefore, NIAT is not required to be approved by UGC, AICTE, or any government regulatory body. The collaborating universities where NIAT’s upskilling program is offered may have their own respective approvals and accreditations.

#### 7. Does NxtWave charge a tuition fee or semester fee for NIAT’s upskilling program?

No. NxtWave does not charge any tuition fee, semester fee, or admission fee. NxtWave charges an upskilling fee for its skill-building courses. University-related fees such as tuition and semester fees are charged independently by the respective collaborating university.

#### 8. What is the role of Success Coaches in NIAT’s upskilling program?

Success Coaches are deployed by NIAT as part of the upskilling program to guide and support students throughout their skill-building journey. They help students stay on track with their learning goals, navigate the program effectively, and build the competencies needed for a successful tech career.

#### 9. What does NIAT mean?

NIAT is an acronym that stands for NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies. It is an upskilling program collaborating with 20+ universities across India and widely recognized for its high-quality training, practical learning for specializations like Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning, Data Science, Cyber Security with hands-on experience. NIAT has received global recognition and collaborated with prestigious institutions and universities all over India. Additionally, NxtWave has earned endorsements from NSDC, NASSCOM, Startup India, and recently collaborated with OpenAI Academy.”

#### 10. Are the Trainers at NIAT part of the university’s teaching faculty?

No. NIAT deploys its own Trainers, Mentors, and Success Coaches to deliver the upskilling program. They are entirely separate from the university’s academic faculty and staff and focus exclusively on industry-aligned skill-building and student success.
