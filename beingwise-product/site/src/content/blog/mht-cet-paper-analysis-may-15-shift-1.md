## Subject-wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis

The MHT CET May 15 exam for PCM contains 150 questions, 50 from Physics, Chemistry, and Math. To be successful in the exam, students must perform well in all parts. Let us look at the subject-specific MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 for PCM groups below.

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Maths Section

As per the MHT CET 2026 exam analysis for this shift, the Mathematics Section was reported to be the toughest. Students mentioned that some questions involved complex calculations and multi-step solutions, thus increasing the time required for completing the section. Take a look at the key highlights of this section below

| Mathematics Topic | Important Subtopics Asked |
| --- | --- |
| Calculus & Integration | Integration, Application of Derivatives, Limits, Continuity, Differentiation |
| Vectors & 3D Geometry | Direction Ratios, Coplanarity, Equation of Plane, Distance Problems |
| Trigonometry | Trigonometric Identities, Inverse Trigonometric Functions, General Solutions |
| Algebra | Matrices, Determinants, Complex Numbers, Mathematical Logic |
| Probability | Conditional Probability and Bayes Theorem-based questions |
| Coordinate Geometry | Straight Lines, Circle Equations, Pair of Lines |
| Differential Equations | Formation and solving of differential equations |

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

Once again, Chemistry emerged as the easiest section in the paper. Chemistry was the highest-scoring section. Students following the NCERT lines for their preparations could answer many questions easily. Take a look at the important topics covered in this in chemistry section.

| Chemistry Topic | Type of Questions Asked |

| Organic Chemistry | Name reactions, reaction mechanisms, and direct conceptual MCQs |

| Coordination Compounds | Theory-based and compound identification questions directly from textbook concepts |
| Thermodynamics | Numerical and conceptual questions involving enthalpy and energy changes |

| Solutions | Concentration, molarity, and colligative property-based numerical questions |
| Biomolecules | Direct, factual, and statement-based MCQs |

## MHT CET May 15 Question Paper Pattern

On May 15, the MHT CET examination is held for the PCM group in both shifts. The MHT CET exam paper​ for the PCM includes a total of 150 questions with 50 questions from each subject. Take a look at the detailed MHT CET May 15  question paper pattern below

| Exam Particulars | Information |
| --- | --- |
| Examination Mode | Computer-Based Test (CBT) format |
| MHT CET PCM Paper Structure | Two Sections:  Section 1: Physics & Chemistry  Section 2: Mathematics |
| Total Exam Duration | 180 minutes in total, with 90 minutes allotted to each section |
| Question Format | All questions are Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) |
| Total Questions | 150 questions overall, with 50 questions from each subject |
| Maximum Marks | The paper carries a total of 200 marks |
| Section-wise Marks Distribution | Physics & Chemistry section: 100 marks;  Mathematics section: 100 marks |
| Medium of Examination | Mathematics is available only in English, whereas Physics & Chemistry are offered in English, Urdu, and Marathi |
| Marking Pattern | Each correct answer in Physics and Chemistry carries 1 mark, while every correct Mathematics answer carries 2 marks |
| Negative Marking | No marks are deducted for incorrect answers |

## MHT CET Counselling & Admissions Process

After the MHT CET result declaration, the process of counseling and admission commences for the eligible candidates. Candidates have to register themselves, mention the preferred colleges and courses, and be a part of the CAP (Centralized Admission Process) seat allocation rounds.

In the process of seat allocation, there are various things that need to be considered by the candidate, such as the percentile, category, reservation criteria, and availability of seats in the colleges taking part in the CAP process. Once the candidate is allotted a seat, they need to do the document verification and pay the admission fees within the specified time limit.
