## What are the Students’ Reactions to the AP EAPCET 2026 Exam?

Students who appeared for AP EAMCET 2026 May 13 Shift 1 shared a range of reactions after walking out of the exam hall. Here’s a summary of the general sentiment:

Physics: Students reported that the Physics section was moderate. The questions were direct and formula-based, with a heavy focus on core concepts. Key topics included Thermodynamics, Electrostatics, and Kinematics, and students feel that an average score of 30 to 35 out of 40 marks is very achievable.

Chemistry: Students found the Chemistry section to be very easy. The questions were largely drawn directly from NCERT concepts. Topics covered included Organic and Inorganic Chemistry, Hydrocarbons, Alcohols, P-block elements, and Coordination. Because of its ease, students expect to comfortably score between 34 and 37 out of 40 marks.

Mathematics: Students reacted to this section as being moderate to difficult and quite lengthy. Solving some of the multi-step problems could take anywhere from 5 to 10 minutes each, making time management extremely crucial for this section. The questions mainly came from 3D Geometry, Vectors, Integration, and Differential Equations.

Overall, the complete paper was considered moderate, and achieving a score between 110 and 125 marks is estimated to be a good score for securing a seat in top colleges.

## AP EAMCET 2026 Exam Day Guidelines

Candidates appearing for AP EAMCET should carefully follow all the exam day instructions issued by APSCHE and JNTU Kakinada to avoid any last-minute issues at the examination centre. Following the proper guidelines helps ensure a smooth entry into the exam hall and prevents unnecessary stress before the examination. Students are advised to carry all required documents, follow the dress code, and reach the exam centre well before the reporting time.

- Carry a printed copy of the AP EAMCET Hall Ticket to the examination centre.
- Bring a valid photo ID proof, such as an Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, School ID, Passport, or Driving License.
- Reach the exam centre at least 1 to 2 hours before the reporting time.
- Verify the exam centre location one day before the examination to avoid delays.
- Do not carry electronic gadgets such as mobile phones, smart watches, Bluetooth devices, calculators, or earphones.
- Avoid carrying study materials, notes, books, or written papers inside the exam hall.
- Candidates should follow the dress code instructions mentioned on the admit card.
- Biometric verification and photograph verification may be conducted at the centre.
- Rough sheets for calculations will be provided inside the examination hall.
- Carefully read all instructions displayed on the computer screen before starting the exam.
- Do not attempt unfair means or communicate with other candidates during the examination.
- Candidates will not be allowed to leave the exam hall before the completion of the exam.
- Ensure that all personal details displayed on the system are correct before starting the test.
- Maintain silence and follow the invigilator’s instructions throughout the examination.
- Keep the hall ticket safe even after the exam, as it may be required during counselling and admission processes.
