### What is the Qualifying Exam Accepted By NIAT?

The NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT) is a 90-minute online assessment conducted by NxtWave to evaluate a student’s cognitive abilities, problem-solving potential, and aptitude for NIAT’s industry-ready upskilling program.

It is important to understand that the NAT is not a university entrance examination. Each collaborating university independently conducts its own admission process based on its own eligibility criteria and admission policy. NxtWave acts only as a facilitator, based on a student’s performance in the NAT and the goals and preferences expressed during the 1:1 counselling session, NxtWave’s team helps suggest a suitable collaborating university offering NIAT’s upskilling program. However, the final admission decision rests solely with the respective university. To be considered, students must meet the following requirements:

- **10+2 level with PCM** as core subjects
- **Qualifying the NAT** (NxtWave Assessment Test)
- **Meeting the respective collaborating university’s own eligibility criteria and admission policy**

Since each collaborating university is an independent academic institution governed by its own regulatory framework, admission norms, including any applicable UGC and AICTE guidelines, are determined and enforced by the university itself, not by NIAT.
