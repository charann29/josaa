## CUET UG 2025 English Paper Analysis

Students found the English section in Shift 2 was tougher than Shift 1, with extensive vocabulary-based questions and Reading Comprehension passages. The paper has difficult words like “Munificent,” “Recalcitrant,” and “Taciturn.” As per the students, the Reading comprehension was easier, featuring mostly direct questions across two passages, including a story-based one. The paper also has Idioms, phrases, and figures of speech that appear in various formats, along with one-word substitutions. Students also reported that the fill-in-the-blanks were tricky, especially without strong vocabulary skills. Overall, some students managed to finish the paper, but the vocabulary required extra focus and preparation.

## Student Reviews For CUET UG 2025 May 21st Shift 2 Exam

Most students from different states found CUET UG 2025 Shift 2, which was held on May 21st, was moderate. Here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

**Physics:** Students found the Physics paper moderate to easy, with questions largely based on NCERT concepts. Numerical problems on Mechanics and Optics were common, and many felt well-prepared if they focused on core topics.

**English:** The English section was reported as difficult by several students, mainly due to challenging vocabulary and indirect comprehension questions. Grammar and idioms added to the complexity, making it tougher for some aspirants.

**Chemistry:** Chemistry was moderate in difficulty, featuring questions on Physical, Organic, and Inorganic Chemistry. Students appreciated the balanced mix of theory and application-based questions, though some numerical problems required careful calculation.

**General Aptitude Test (GAT):** The GAT section had a difficulty level, with a good mix of quantitative aptitude, reasoning, and chronological questions. Time management was essential, as some quantitative questions were lengthy but manageable.

Overall, students felt the exam tested their conceptual clarity and time management skills, with English being the most challenging section for many.

## CUET UG 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

Given below are a few CUET UG 2025 exam day guidelines that students need to follow.

- **Carry Only Required Items:** Only take your printed admit card (with passport-size photograph), your valid photo ID (Aadhaar or PAN card), a transparent ballpoint pen, and a transparent water bottle. Candidates can check all the CUET exam details on the admit card and are advised to adhere to the instructions.
- **Be On Time:** Report to the exam center well in advance relative to your reporting time (7:00 AM for Shift 1 and 1:00 PM for Shift 2). If you are late, you will not be allowed to enter.
- **Prohibited Items:** Do not bring mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, bags, books, or food if not medically required (with documentation).
- **Maintain Discipline:** Be seated in your assigned seat, only do rough work on the sheets provided, and do not perform any unfair practice or make any noise in the exam hall.
- **Time Management:** It is a critical challenge across subjects, especially with lengthy sections like RC or Quant. It’s advised to tackle easy questions first.
- **The Normalisation Factor:** This factor will account for variations in difficulty across shifts. The focus should be on attempting as many questions with 100% accuracy.
