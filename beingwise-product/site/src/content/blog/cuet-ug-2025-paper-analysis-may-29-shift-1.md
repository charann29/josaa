## Student Initial Reaction for CUET Exam: May 29th Shift 1 Exam

Here’s the student’s first reaction after appearing for the CUET exam on May 29th 2025, shift 1:

**Ishita (from Jaipur):**
“The GAT section was really Quant-heavy for me, almost 20 questions, and many were lengthy, especially Coordinate Geometry and Statistics. English had 3 RCs that were time-consuming, but the vocabulary was familiar thanks to our prep. Physics was manageable with just a couple of tricky ones.”

**Raghav (from Lucknow):**
“In my slot, Reasoning had more weight in GAT than Quant or GS. The series and Ranking questions were simple, but that Missing Number question was a brain-twister! English RCs were straightforward. I was glad I revised Antonyms ‘ Complacent’ showed up just like in mocks.”

**Neha (from Hyderabad):**
“English took the most time because of 3 Reading Comprehensions and lots of rearrangement. I remember seeing ‘Frugal’ and ‘Resurrect’, luckily, I knew both. Economics was a breeze with easy numericals and balanced theory. The GST launch year and Marginal Utility numericals were direct picks!”

**Arjun (from Chandigarh):**
“The GAT felt tricky. My paper had more General Studies, Articles, dynasties, and even the word ‘Sorology’ popped up! Quant wasn’t hard, but really lengthy. English had two RCs; questions weren’t direct, so elimination was key. Chemistry was moderate and mostly NCERT-based.”

**Meera (from Bhopal):**
“English was vocabulary-rich in my slot, Phrasal Verbs, Synonyms, and even Homophones like ‘Course/Coarse’. I saw a Spelling Error question, too, which was new. GAT Reasoning was okay—series and diagram questions were doable. Political Science went really well—most questions felt familiar.”

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ************Was the CUET 2025 GAT section more Quant or Reasoning heavy on May 29 Shift 1?************

Based on student feedback, GAT had significant variation; some saw Quant-heavy papers with Time & Work and Statistics, while others saw more Reasoning and GK.

### 2.******************************Did CUET UG 2025 English paper on May 29, Shift 1 have multiple RCs across slots?******************************

Yes, students from multiple cities reported encountering 2–3 Reading Comprehensions, making time management crucial during the English section.

### **3. **********************************Were any out-of-syllabus or unexpected topics seen in CUET 2025 GAT on May 29?************************************

Some students reported unfamiliar terms like “Sorology” in the General Studies part, highlighting the importance of broad awareness in GK.

### **4. **************************How similar were the CUET UG 2025 May 29 English questions to previous years?****************************

The vocabulary and grammar portions repeated many previously asked questions, making previous year papers a helpful resource for prep.

### 5. ****************Which Economics concepts were prioritised in CUET UG 2025 May 29 Shift 1?****************

Focus areas included Marginal Propensity to Consume, GST year, and Investment Multiplier, with case-based questions aligned to NCERT.
