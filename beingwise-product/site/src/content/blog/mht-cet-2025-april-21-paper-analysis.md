## MHT CET Counselling 2025

MHT CET counselling process 2025 is conducted online. Below are the steps candidates need to follow for MHT CET 2025 counselling:

- **Registration:** Enter your personal, contact, and security details on the MHT CET official website to start the registration process. Once these details have been entered, you will need to create a password. After setting the password, fill in the other required fields. After that, applicants should submit their qualifications, including their overall and subject-specific grades from grades 10 and 12, along with the names of the relevant boards.

- **Uploading Scanned Documents:** In the second step of MHT CET counselling, candidates are required to upload the given documents:
  - 10th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - 12th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - MHT CET Scorecard 2025
  - Migration Certificate
  - Category Certificate (if applicable)
  - Character Certificate
  - Domicile Certificate (if applicable)
  - Print out the MHT CET Counselling registration Form

- **Payment of Counselling Fees**– Candidates are required to pay the counselling fees online, depending on their category:-

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Courses** | **Registration Fee for JEE Main Applicants (All India Quota and Institutional-Level Quota)** | **Registration Fee for MHT CET Qualified Candidates** |
| BTech | INR 800 | INR 600 |
| BArch | INR 800 | INR 600 |
| BTech (Lateral Entry) | INR 800 | INR 600 |
| BPharma (Lateral Entry) | INR 800 | INR 600 |

- **Merit List Release:** The committee will make the MHT CET merit list public. The Class 12 and CET scores will be used to determine the marks in a 50:50 ratio. You can submit objections on the website if you find any inconsistencies in the merit list.

- **Choice Filling:** In this step, you must choose your college as well as your preferred branch and specialisations. Seats will be distributed by the committee based on the merit list ranking.

- **Seat Allotment:** Seats will be distributed according to the selections made and the merit positions of the candidates. Candidates must pay the admission fee and show up at the designated college to confirm their seat.

### **Shift 1**

Overall, on average, the students rated the paper in Shift 1 as moderate to tough.

Physics, on the whole, students rated this section as moderate to tough. Most of the questions were in the area of Mechanics and Optics. There were several lengthy numerical questions, and many of the questions were from Heat and Thermodynamics.

Chemistry, students rated this area as moderate.  Most of the questions were from the NCERT; however, there was a focus on Organic Chemistry and Chemical Bonding.

Math students rated this section as easy to moderate. The majority of the questions were directed towards topics within Algebra and Calculus; however, most Math questions were lengthy, so students had to spend a large chunk of time solving the problems.

### **Shift 2**

For the 2nd shift of MHT CET 2025, it was rated overall, moderate in difficulty.

For Physics, students rated this area as moderate, and the students thought there was a good balance of questions from Electricity & Magnetism and Mechanics. The paper included some lengthy and time-consuming numerical questions related to Optics and Thermodynamics.

Chemistry, the majority rated questions easy to moderate, based on the NCERT books. Areas of focus seem to be Organic Chemistry (like Alcohols & Ethers) and Inorganic Chemistry (like Coordination Compounds).

In Chemistry, the students described the questions as easy to moderate. Most of the questions were from the NCERT textbooks, and key areas for the students were Organic Chemistry (Alcohols and Ethers) and Inorganic Chemistry (Coordination Compounds).

The Mathematics section was moderate to difficult. Questions were based on Vectors, Algebra, and Calculus. From a difficulty level, the Integration questions were quite lengthy and difficult to do.

## Conclusion

The MHT CET 2025 April 21st Paper gave an excellent picture of the continuing trends seen this year. Both Shift 1 and Shift 2 demonstrated a balanced paper structure, following the official syllabus and weightage distribution closely. MHT CET paper pattern PCM in **Shift 1** was moderately difficult overall, and Mathematics proved to be the biggest hurdle, especially since time-consuming problems were coming from two areas of calculus and 3D Geometry. MHT CET paper pattern for **Shift 2** was slightly more student-friendly since Physics and Chemistry were largely formula-based, with few tricky concepts for students.

In Physics, there was a significant amount of numerical problems from Modern Physics and Thermodynamics. Chemistry continued to test Organic and Physical Chemistry, with a focus on Electrochemistry and Coordination Compounds. Mathematics, again, had heavy-weighted topics like Integrals, Vectors, and Probability in both shifts.
