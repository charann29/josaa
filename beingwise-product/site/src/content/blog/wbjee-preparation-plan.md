WBJEE is a state-level entrance examination for admissions into undergraduate courses like Engineering, Architecture, and pharmacy. Approximately one lakh students register for the exam and many students prepare for the exam to secure admission to prestigious institutes. A proper preparation and revision plan can help them ace the exam paving the way for a better future. This article will give you a comprehensive preparation plan to help you score high in [WBJEE](https://collegedost.in/blog/category/wbjee/) 2025.

## How to Prepare for WBJEE 2025 in 30 Days?

Although it is recommended that students start their preparation as early as possible, students can still prepare for the exam in 30 days and can gain a competitive edge over others. Candidates who can dedicate a few extra hours of study and stick to the daily schedule can confidently appear for the exam and secure admission to prestigious institutes. Before following the given schedule, you are advised to go through the syllabus and each [subject](https://collegedost.in/blog/wbjee/wbjee-subject-wise-weightage/) at least once, as this can give you a good understanding of the exam pattern and topics asked in the exam.

### Week 1 Preparation Plan

Week 1 is dedicated to revising the basics and understanding the exam pattern and the difficulty level of the questions asked in the exam. The schedule for week 1 is mentioned below.

**Note: Candidates are allowed to start with the subject they wish to study.**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Day** | **Schedule** |
| **Day 1-2: Physics** | Study and revise the basic concepts.  Analyse Previous Year’s Question Papers. Solve Numerical and focus on frequently asked topics. |
| **Day 2-4: Chemistry** | Start with revising the basic concepts. Solve questions based on the learnt concepts. Prepare short notes on the important topics |
| **Day 5-6: Mathematics** | Revise important Formulas Solve previous years’ papers and analyse |
| **Day 7: Mock test** | Give a full-length mock test. Analyse your performance and make a note of your mistakes. |

### Week 2 Preparation Plan

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Day** | **Schedule** |
| **Day 8-9: Physics** | Study important topics like modern physics and electricity. Prepare short notes for important formulas. Solve related questions after studying the theory. |
| **Day 10-11: Chemistry** | Complete topics like organic chemistry. Analyse the types of questions asked in these chapters. |
| **Day 12-13: Mathematics** | Study probability, Permutation & Combinations.Memorise trigonometric formulas and note them. Solve problems from these chapters. |
| **Day 14: Mock test** | Give a full-length test and track your performance. |

### Week 3 Preparation Plan

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Day** | **Schedule** |
| **Day 15-17: Physics** | Focus on solving numerical problems from different sources. Solve previous years’ papers and find out the areas where you struggle Take time to understand the concepts and clarify all your doubts from seniors or peers. |
| **Day 18-19: Chemistry** | Revise/study environmental chemistry. Clear all your theoretical concepts and make a note of difficult concepts. Practice solving problems. |
| **Day 20-21: Mathematics** | Start solving tougher problems. Focus on managing your time, as maths consumes a lot of time during the exam. |
| **Day 22: Mock test** | Solve mock tests and revise all the concepts that you failed to answer. |

### Week 4 Preparation Plan

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Day** | **Schedule** |
| **Day 23-25: Revision** | Ensure you revise all the subjects from short notes. Revise all the important formulas. |
| **Day 26-27: Previous Year Papers** | Glance at the previous year’s questions and make a note of frequently asked questions. |
| **Day 28-29: Complete Revision** | Avoid learning new topics/concepts. Revise every subject once again. |
| **Day 30: Last Minute Tips** | Ensure you get a proper sleep. Stay confident |

## Booklist for WBJEE 2025

Choosing the right sources is important as it will ease our preparation. Right sources provide you with both theoretical and practical knowledge. Stick to the following sources to gain a strong foundation over the topics asked in the exam.
