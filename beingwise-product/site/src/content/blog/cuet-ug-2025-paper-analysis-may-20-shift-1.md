## CUET UG Exam Pattern 2025

Students are tested across the language, aptitude, and subject knowledge. For each subject, candidates are given 60 minutes to complete the examination. Check the following table for a more detailed CUET exam pattern:

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Section** | **No of Questions** | **Duration of the Exam** | **Questions Type** |
| Section IA- Language | 50 Questions | 60 minutes for each subject | Questions on reading comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary are asked to test the candidate’s English proficiency. |
| Section II- Domain Specific | 50 Questions | Questions based on the domain-specific subjects are asked to test the candidate’s subject proficiency. |
| Section III- General Test | 50 Questions | This section assesses a candidate’s proficiency in General Knowledge, Current Affairs, Logical and Analytical Reasoning, and Mental Ability. It also evaluates Numerical and Quantitative Reasoning using fundamental concepts from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration, typically taught up to Grade 8. |

## Student Initial Reaction for CUET Exam: May 20th Shift 1 Exam

Here’s what students felt after the CUET UG 2025 exam, held on May 20th, Shift 1:

**1. Aarav (from Delhi):**
“The English section was manageable, but some vocabulary questions like ‘Relegate’ and ‘Petulant’ were really confusing. Reading passages took time, but they were doable. GAT was moderate, Quant had Ratio and Mensuration questions, and GK covered topics I hadn’t revised, like WTO and Hardy-Weinberg. Overall, it felt moderate to lengthy.”

**2. Sneha (from Muzaffarpur, Bihar):**
“My Maths paper was time-consuming. Questions from Integration and Matrices were tough. GAT was fine, Quant was basic, and Reasoning had predictable topics like Calendar and Number Series. History and Polity came up a lot in GK, especially Constitution-related topics. I used the elimination method a lot. Decent attempt overall.”

**3. Kabir (from Jaipur):**
“GAT felt lengthy, but not too difficult. Time & Work, Probability, and Clock questions were there. I found English easy except for synonyms and foreign words. My Political Science paper had many globalisation-based questions and ASEAN and EU topics. A lot of analysis was required, not just rote learning.”

**4. Aanya (from Bengaluru):**
“I had Fine Arts, and it was super easy, almost everything was from the NCERT 12th book. GT had 3-4 Mensuration questions and some tricky Height & Distance ones. The reasoning was light. GK was unpredictable, but I saw repeat topics like Ministers and Books & Authors. I finished just in time.”

**5. Rishabh (from Lucknow):**
Physics had many statement-based and numerical questions. It was okay, but it needed solid concept clarity. I attempted 48/50. GT had a fair number of GK questions, especially History, dynasties and wars were asked. I also had geography, which was easier, mostly from Book 2. Time management was key today.”

As per the CUET UG exam pattern 2025, the test is divided into Language (Section I), Domain-specific (Section II), and General Test (Section III). Each subject section lasts for 60 minutes.
