## MHT CET Paper Analysis April 17 Shift 2

The second Shift of the MHT CET PCM Exam is held from 02;00 to 5:00 P.M. The MHT CET PCM Paper exam lasted three hours and included 150 questions (50 each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics). Talking to subject-wise MHT CET paper analysis, Physics and Chemistry were easy, while Maths review is ‘doable but lengthy’.  Take a quick overview of the MHT CET difficulty level for the April 17 Shift 2 below

MHT CET Paper Analysis by Students

After the exam had concluded, we talked to the students to know what they thought about the MHT CET Question paper of this shift. We have received mix reaction about the April 17 Shift 2 MHT CET difficulty level as follows

- **Aman Sharma stated that the overall paper is of a moderate level**, but it was Mathematics that created the most problems. Although he felt that the questions were easy from the concept, they took quite some time, making it difficult to answer all the questions. “The key was speed, rather than difficulty, in this transition.”
- **Priya Verma found it easier than other students.** Particularly, Chemistry was found easy and was NCERT-based. She finished her Chemistry part early and gained some additional time for Mathematics. Yet, even then, she felt that the time required in Maths was more than she had thought, adding to her anxiety.
- **Rahul Patil found the Physics section of the paper to be balanced**. There were both formula-based questions and a few concept-based ones as well. He added that the majority of the questions were quite direct and scored too. Anyone who knew his concepts would score well in the Physics paper.

## April 17 Shift 2 MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis Subject-wise Breakdown

The MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 for the April 17 Shift 2 paper reveals that mathematics had questions that were lengthy, while physics questions were balanced. In contrast, the chemistry section was easier and scored more. See below for a subject-wise breakdown of the April 17 Shift 2 MHT CET 2026 paper analysis.

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Maths Section

Based on the MHt CET Paper Analysis, the Mathematics section of April 17, Shift 2, was found moderately tough owing to its lengthy and calculation-intensive nature. Although the concepts involved in the questions were easy.

Many students found the section difficult to answer all the questions because each question had numerous steps. Hence, the crucial elements of the section were time management and accuracy. Key topics for the MHT CET Maths section are

- Differentiation (limits, applications)
- Definite Integration
- Vectors
- 3D Geometry
- Probability
- Matrices & Determinants
- Trigonometry
- Calculus

### MHT CET Exam Analysis for Physics Section

As per the MHT CET Paper Analysis for April 17 shift 2, the Physics section was considered moderately balanced, having a good mix of both formula-based and concept-based questions.

According to the candidates, this section appeared easier than the previous one, with a few direct questions based on formulas. Although a few numerical problems needed some reasoning, almost all questions were quite easy for those who had clear conceptual knowledge. Check key topics from where questions are asked in today’s shift

- Modern Physics
- Current Electricity
- Electrostatics
- Laws of Motion
- Thermodynamics
- Semiconductors

### April 17 Shift 2 MHT CET Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

The Chemistry section turned out to be the easiest of all subjects for students taking MHT CET on April 17, Shift 2. Based on the MHT CET Analysis for the chemistry Section, most of the questions were NCERT-based and could easily be solved within a very short duration of time. Check the most important themes where questions are posed in this shift.

- **Organic Chemistry:** Named Reactions, Basic Organic Chemistry (GOC), Hydrocarbons, Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers
- **Inorganic Chemistry:** p-block Elements, d-block Elements, Coordination Compounds (basic concepts)
- **Physical Chemistry:** Basic Numerical Problems, Mole Concept, Chemical Thermodynamics, Electrochemistry (basic)
