## Factors Affecting CAT Cutoff Scores

There are several factors that affect the CAT cutoff 2025 scores each year. Here’s an overview of these factors:

- College Reputation and Ranking: Premier institutes and those with strong brand value usually maintain higher cutoffs since they attract high-scoring candidates. If a college’s reputation improves, its cutoff benchmark can also rise accordingly.
- Number of Test-Takers: When more aspirants appear for CAT, the level of competition rises. As a result, the cutoff scores generally go up since colleges want to filter out the best talent from a larger applicant pool.
- Exam Difficulty: The toughness of the paper has a direct impact on the cutoff. If the exam is relatively hard, fewer students manage to score high, which usually brings the cutoff down. On the other hand, if the paper is easier, more candidates can secure better marks, pushing the cutoff higher.
- Seat Availability: The number of seats offered by an institute also influences the cutoff. A higher seat intake often leads to lower cutoffs because more students can be accommodated, while limited seats increase competition and, in turn, raise the cutoff.
- Reservation Policies: The application of category-based reservations affects cutoff trends. Relaxation in cutoff marks for reserved categories changes the overall selection range for the institute.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Does work experience impact the CAT Cutoff 2025 shortlisting criteria?

Work experience does not change the raw CAT Cutoff 2025, but it can enhance your composite score, especially in IIMs like Bangalore, Lucknow, and Indore. It can help you clear the final shortlist even with slightly lower percentiles.

### 2. How do baby IIMs and new IIMs define their CAT Cutoff 2025 for each category?

The CAT Cutoff 2025 for new and baby IIMs (like IIM Jammu, IIM Sirmaur) is typically: General: 85–90 percentile, OBC: 75–80 percentile, SC/ST: 60–70 percentile. Their selection process heavily emphasizes PI, academics, and diversity, beyond just CAT scores.

### 3. Can a 90 percentile in CAT 2025 fetch a call from any top MBA college?

Yes, a 90 percentile in CAT Cutoff 2025 can get you into: TISS, IRMA, TAPMI, XIMB. Profile-based calls from SPJIMR, IMT. Reserved category candidates may even receive IIM calls at this percentile.

### 4. How is CAT Cutoff 2025 calculated: score-based or percentile-based?

CAT Cutoff 2025 is based on percentiles, not raw scores. Percentiles depend on:
Normalized scores across all slots. Relative performance of test-takers. Raw score needed for 99 percentile could be around 100–105 marks, but it varies yearly.

### 5. Is it possible to get a call from IIM Ahmedabad with less than 99 percentile in CAT 2025?

Yes, but only under certain conditions:
Reserved category candidates (SC/ST/OBC) can get calls at lower percentiles
Exceptional academic background or diversity profile
Top-5% in 10th, 12th, and graduation. Still, for the General category, the CAT Cutoff 2025 for IIM-A will likely remain above 99 percentile.
