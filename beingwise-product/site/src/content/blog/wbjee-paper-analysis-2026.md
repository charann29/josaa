## WBJEE 2026 Exam Pattern

WBJEE total marks are 200. Paper 1 covers Mathematics for 100 marks. Paper 2 in WBJEE 2026 exam covers Physics and Chemistry. Both subjects carry 40 marks each, for a total of 100 marks.WBJEE timing for each paper is 2 hours. The exam is offline and OMR-based. Questions are divided into three categories across both papers. The table below shows how marks and negative marking work across all three categories:

| Category | Questions per Paper | Marks per Question | Negative Marking |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Category 1 | Varies per section | 1 | Minus 0.25 for wrong answer |
| Category 2 | Varies per section | 2 | Minus 0.5 for wrong answer |

In Mathematics, Category 1 has 50 questions, Category 2 has 15 questions, and Category 3 has 10 questions. In Physics-Chemistry, each subject has 30 questions in Category 1, 5 in Category 2, and 5 in Category 3.

## WBJEE Paper Analysis 2026: Subject-wise Breakdown

WBJEE 2026 had three sections with very different difficulty levels. Physics was the toughest, Chemistry was the most manageable, and Mathematics was long. Here is a section-wise breakdown of topics covered, student observations, and difficulty verdicts:

### WBJEE Paper Analysis: Physics Section

Physics was the section that split the WBJEE paper analysis clearly. Students who had strong conceptual clarity managed it. Students who depended on formula shortcuts found it hard.

The Physics section covered a wide range of chapters. Questions combined multiple concepts in single problems and did not follow an obvious pattern.

#### Topics covered

Optics covered ray optics validity, lens combinations, and photoelectric effect. Electromagnetism had questions on magnetic flux, circular coil magnetic induction, magnetic moment of an arc, AC circuit power, and displacement current. Mechanics included viscous liquid motion, projectile from a tower, fuse wire heating time, and error in mass measurement. Modern physics had radioactive decay and de-Broglie wavelength. Waves covered transverse wave equations and particle velocity conditions.

#### Observations

Category 1 demanded both formula recall and application. Category 2 had heavier numerical work. Category 3 was tricky. **One question on induced EMF across a rod in a circular magnetic field region had an error in the figure.** This may affect the WBJEE answer key 2026 for that question. Several Category 3 questions in vectors and Boolean algebra had closely spaced options that caused confusion.

**Verdict:** Difficult. Students who averaged 25 to 30 out of 40 in Physics did well by this year’s standards.

### WBJEE Paper Analysis: Chemistry Section

Chemistry was the relief section in this WBJEE paper analysis. Students who had their organic mechanisms and physical chemistry numericals in order found it manageable.

#### Topics covered

Organic Chemistry dominated the section. Reaction sequences, Birch reduction, Williamson synthesis, Lindlar catalyst reduction, nucleophilicity order, stereochemistry, and IUPAC nomenclature all appeared. Physical Chemistry covered buffer solutions, van’t Hoff factor, first-order kinetics, rate constants, colligative properties, and van der Waals equation. Inorganic Chemistry included molecular geometry, crystal structure, hybridisation, coordination compounds, magnetic properties of ions, and borazole preparation.

#### Observations

The buffer pH question required careful use of the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation before and after dilution. The coordination compound question on [Co(NH₃)₅SO₄]Br asked for coordination number, oxidation state, d-electrons, and unpaired d-electrons together, which tripped some students. Category 3 questions on Boyle’s Law plots, thermodynamics, and glucose properties were fair and within standard preparation.

Verdict: Moderate. Chemistry was the scoring section. Students targeting 28 to 35 out of 40 could achieve it with solid preparation.

### WBJEE Paper Analysis: Mathematics Section

Mathematics was long. That is the central finding of this WBJEE paper analysis for Paper 1. The questions were not always individually very difficult, but the volume and multi-step nature made time management the real challenge.

#### Topics covered

Calculus had the highest share: limits, definite and indefinite integrals, differential equations, functions and their properties, and monotonicity. Algebra covered complex numbers, quadratic equations, matrices, determinants, sequences and series, probability, and combinatorics. Coordinate Geometry included parabola, ellipse, circles, reflection of points, and intercepts on tangents. Vectors and 3D covered dot products, cross products, and intersection points. Trigonometry covered inverse functions and general solutions.

#### Observations

Category 1 was manageable with solid basics. Category 2 was where time pressure hit hardest. Problems on definite integrals and GP/AP relations required multiple steps. Category 3 questions on vectors, functions, and probability were fair for well-prepared students. Questions on complex numbers, number theory, and determinants rewarded students who had practised diverse problem types from WBJEE question paper archives.

**Verdict:** Moderate to Difficult. A score of 55 to 70 out of 100 was achievable for well-prepared students.

## WBJEE Paper Analysis 2026: Memory-Based Questions

This section of the WBJEE paper analysis lists questions recalled by students and verified against the official WBJEE question paper 2026. These are useful for self-assessment and as WBJEE previous papers reference for future aspirants.

#### WBJEE Physics Memory-Based Questions

Q1. A body of density ρ is dropped slowly on the surface of a lake of depth d. If the lake water density is ρ’ where ρ’ is less than ρ, what is the time taken to reach the bottom?

Answer: √[2dρ / g(ρ − ρ’)]

Q2. Beyond what distance is ray optics valid when the aperture is 6 mm and wavelength is 6000 Å?

Answer: 60 m

Q3. A plano-convex lens fits into a plano-concave lens. Refractive indices are μ₁ and μ₂. Radius of curvature is R. What is the focal length of the combination?

Answer: R / (μ₁ − μ₂)

Q4. A fuse wire of length l and radius r: the heating time for maximum current depends on?

Answer: t is proportional to r⁴l⁰

Q5. Density is 20 ± 4 gm/cm³ and volume is 10 ± 1 cm³. What is the absolute error in mass?

Answer: 60 gm

Q6. A square of side L lies in the x-y plane. Magnetic field is B = B₀(2î + 3ĵ + 4k̂). What is the magnetic flux through the square?

Answer: 4B₀L²

Q7. A circular coil of radius R carries current. At what distance on the axis is the magnetic induction 1/27th of its value at the centre?

Answer: 2√2 R

Q8. From a tower of height H, a particle is thrown upwards with speed u. Time to hit ground is n times the time to reach the highest point. What is the relation between H, u and n?

Answer: 2gH = nu²(n − 2)

Q9. An iron bar has magnetic moment M. It is bent into an arc subtending 60° at the centre. What is the magnetic moment of the arc?

Answer: 3M/π

Q10. A circular region of radius R has a uniform time-varying magnetic field increasing at rate α. A straight conducting rod of length 2R is placed as shown. What is the induced EMF across the rod?

Answer: (1/4)πR²α

### WBJEE Chemistry Memory-Based Questions

Q1. Among N₂O, ClF₂⁻, SO₂ and I₃⁺, which species have linear structures?

Answer: N₂O and ClF₂⁻

Q2. A compound X gives a brown precipitate with CuSO₄ solution. On adding hypo solution, the precipitate turns white. What is X?

Answer: KI

Q3. Three engines A, B and C take steam at 130°C and reject at 20°C, 40°C and 50°C respectively. Which is the most efficient?

Answer: Engine A

Q4. What is the van’t Hoff factor (i) for a dilute aqueous solution of Na₂SO₄?

Answer: 1 + 2α

Q5. In a conductance experiment, aqueous AgNO₃ is added to aqueous KCl. Which plot correctly shows molar conductivity versus volume of AgNO₃?

Answer: Option D

Q6. How many isomers can a compound with molecular formula C₃H₅Br have?

Answer: 5

Q7. Which compound does not lose water even in concentrated H₂SO₄?

Answer: Compound 2

Q8. In a first-order reaction, concentration decreases from 400 mol/L to 50 mol/L in 7.5 × 10³ s. What is the rate constant approximately?

Answer: 2.77 × 10⁻⁴ s⁻¹

WBJEE Mathematics Memory-Based Questions

Q1. If α and β are roots of x² − px + q = 0 with α > 0 and β > 0, then α^(1/4) + β^(1/4) = (p + 6√p + 4q^(1/4)√(p + 2√q))^K. What is K?

Answer: 1/4

Q2. If Σ(r=1 to ∞) tan⁻¹(1/2r²) = a, then tan a equals?

Answer: 1

Q3. A mapping is selected at random from all f: A → A where A = {1, 2, …, n}. If the probability that the mapping is injective is 3/32, what is n?

Answer: 4

Q4. The minimum length of intercept on any tangent to the ellipse x²/4 + y²/9 = 1 cut by the circle x² + y² = 25 is?

Answer: 8

Q5. The total number of polynomials of the form x³ + ax² + bx + c divisible by x² + 1, where a, b, c ∈ {1, 2, …, 10} is?

Answer: 10

Q6. If a = lim(n→∞) cos²ⁿx for x = nπ and b = lim(n→∞) cos²ⁿx for x ≠ nπ, what is the area of the triangle with vertices (a, b), (−2, 1) and (2, 1)?

Answer: 2

The WBJEE answer key 2026 is the first tool students should use after the exam. It allows score estimation before the official WBJEE result 2026 is declared. The official WBJEE answer key 2026 will be released on wbjeeb.nic.in.

### How to Calculate Score Using WBJEE Answer Key?

Following the simple steps below, candidates can easily calculate their score by using WBJEE Answer Key.

- For Category 1: add 1 mark for each correct answer, subtract 0.25 for each wrong answer.
- For Category 2: add 2 marks for correct, subtract 0.5 for wrong.
- For Category 3: add 2 marks only if all correct options are marked. No marks are deducted in Category 3 for wrong or incomplete attempts.

Students can raise objections against the WBJEE answer key 2026 within the objection window announced by WBJEEB after the preliminary key is published. The final answer key is released after reviewing valid objections.
