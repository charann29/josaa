The National Testing Agency (NTA) has announced significant changes to the format of JEE Mains 2025. The NTA issued a notification stating that the option to select questions of one’s choice in Section B of the Exam will be discontinued. This applies to all papers, which include Paper 1(BE/BTech), Paper 2A(BArch), and Paper 2B(BPlan). **Under the new pattern for JEE Mains 2025, Section B of Paper 1 will no longer include optional questions.** Students must answer each of the 5 questions and must select from among those 5.

## **JEE Mains Exam Pattern** 2025

The JEE Mains 2025 exam pattern consists of three papers: Paper 1 (for BTech/BE), Paper 2A (for BArch), and Paper 2B (for BPlan). JEE Mains 2025 exam for Paper 1 consists of 75 MCQs, which carry 300 marks. Each correct answer is awarded 4 marks, and every wrong answer accounts for 1 negative mark.

JEE Mains 2025 Paper 2 consists of two papers, Paper 2A (BArch) and Paper 2B (BPlan). They are held separately. JEE Mains 2025 paper 2A for BArch consists of 77 MCQs accounting for 400 marks. Paper 2B for BPlan consists of 100 MCQs totalling 400 marks.

## JEE Advanced Exam Pattern Overview

JEE Advanced is the second stage of the Joint Entrance Examination and is conducted for candidates who qualify through JEE Main. The structure and format of JEE Advanced differ significantly from JEE Main, both in terms of complexity and exam organisation.

JEE Advanced consists of two mandatory papers: Paper 1 and Paper 2. Both papers are held on the same day, and each is of 3 hours’ duration. Candidates must attempt both papers to be considered for ranking.

Each paper is further divided into three sections: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. The questions are designed to test a deeper understanding of concepts, analytical ability, and problem-solving skills. The question types include multiple-choice questions with single or multiple correct answers, numerical value-based questions, and matrix match type questions. The marking scheme is not uniform; it varies by question type and may include full, partial, or negative marking, which is announced in the exam instructions.

JEE Advanced is conducted in a computer-based (online) mode. Unlike JEE Main, there is no choice in the number of papers or sections: all candidates must attempt all sections in both papers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Are there any Changes in the JEE Mains 2025 Exam?

Yes, NTA has announced a major change removing the optional questions in section B of the JEE Mains 2025 Exam.

### 2. JEE Mains 2025 is for how many marks?

JEE Mains 2025 exam paper 1 consists of 75 questions for 300 marks. Paper 2A and 2B for 400 marks each.

### 3.  Will there be a negative marking for wrong answers in JEE Mains 2025?

Yes, there is a penalty of 1 mark for every wrong answer in Jee Mains 2025.

### 4. JEE Mains 2025 online or offline?

JEE Mains 2025 for all papers is conducted online. However, the Drawing exam for B.Arch is conducted offline.

### 5. How many shifts is the JEE Mains 2025 exam conducted in?

JEE Main is conducted in two shifts each day: the first shift from 9 AM to 12 PM, and the second shift from 3 PM to 5 PM.

### 6. How many questions are there in JEE Mains?

There are 90 questions in total, with 30 questions each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Candidates must attempt 75 questions (25 from each subject).

### 7. What is the exam pattern of JEE Mains 2025?

JEE Mains 2025 Paper 1 has 3 sections: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, each carrying 100 marks. The exam has 90 questions, but candidates answer 75. Each subject has 20 MCQs and 10 numerical questions, with 5 numerical questions to be attempted. Each correct answer awards 4 marks, and each wrong answer has a 1 mark penalty. The exam duration is 3 hours and it is computer-based.
