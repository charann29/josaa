## An allotted seat is not a confirmed seat

This is the one idea to hold onto. JoSAA allots seats based on your rank and choices, but that allotment is only provisional. You keep the seat only after you accept it, pay the fee, report, and clear verification, all inside the deadline.

If something is missing or does not match at verification, your seat can be put on hold or cancelled. None of that is about merit. It is about paperwork you can prepare for in advance.

## The standard JoSAA document set

Below is the well-known standard set most JoSAA candidates need. Treat it as your starting checklist, but always verify it against the current year's business rules and reporting brochure on the official portal, because formats and add-ons change year to year.

### Allotment and JEE documents

- JoSAA seat allotment letter, downloaded after the round result
- Provisional seat acceptance or fee receipt, after you pay the SAF
- JEE Main 2026 admit card
- JEE Main 2026 scorecard or rank card
- JEE Advanced 2026 scorecard, only if you wrote Advanced or are taking an IIT seat

### Academic and identity documents

- Class X marksheet and passing certificate (proof of date of birth)
- Class XII marksheet and passing certificate
- Photo identity proof (Aadhaar, passport, voter ID, or PAN)
- Recent passport-size photographs (keep 6 to 8, ideally the same photo as your JEE form)
- Medical or fitness certificate in the prescribed format, if your institute asks for it

### Category and special-status documents (only if you claim them)

- Category certificate (SC, ST, or OBC-NCL) in the correct central government (GoI) format, issued recently and within validity
- EWS certificate for the current financial year, in the prescribed format
- PwD or disability certificate, valid, from an authorised medical authority, if you claimed a PwD seat
- DS (Defence Service) certificate, if you claimed that quota
- OCI, PIO, or foreign-national documents, if applicable
- Name or DOB correction proof (affidavit, gazette notification, or board certificate), if anything differs across your documents

For how category and home-state quotas actually decide your seat, read [home-state quota and category seats explained](/blog/home-state-quota-category-seats).

Carry originals plus two sets of self-attested photocopies. Verification is done on originals; the centre keeps copies. Keep clear scans on your phone and email as backup.

## How the Seat Acceptance Fee works

The Seat Acceptance Fee (SAF) is the official amount you pay on the JoSAA portal to confirm that you accept an allotted seat. No payment, no seat. It is that simple.

- It is approximately ₹30,000 for general candidates and around ₹15,000 for reserved categories. Confirm the current figure on the official portal before you pay. We never quote it as final.
- You pay it once, online, and it carries across rounds. If you chose Float or Slide and your seat upgrades in a later round, you do not pay it again.
- It is adjusted against your institute admission fee later. It is not money lost and not an extra charge.
- It is paid only on the official portal to JoSAA, never to any agent, consultant, or reporting helper. Anyone asking you to pay a seat fee to them is a red flag.

## Steps after a seat is allotted

After each round result, the process runs in a fixed order. Miss a step and the seat is gone.

### Step 1: Accept and choose Float, Freeze or Slide

Log in, view your allotted seat, and select Float, Freeze, or Slide. This choice decides whether you stay put or try for an upgrade in the next round. If you are unsure which to pick, our [choice-filling mistakes guide](/blog/josaa-choice-filling-mistakes) covers the traps here.

### Step 2: Pay the Seat Acceptance Fee

Pay the SAF on the portal and download the receipt immediately. Until this is done and confirmed, your acceptance is incomplete.

### Step 3: Online reporting and document upload

Upload scanned copies of the required documents in the formats the portal asks for: correct file type, size, and clarity. This is where the system or a verifying officer does an initial check. Watch the file-size and format limits. A rejected upload because the scan was too large is a needless way to miss a deadline.

### Step 4: Physical reporting, when required

When the schedule calls for it, report physically at the reporting centre or your allotted institute with all originals and photocopies. Officials verify your originals against your uploaded copies and your claimed category. Only after this is your admission confirmed for that round.

Steps and deadlines differ by round and by year. Re-check the official schedule every round. Do not assume this round works exactly like the last one.

## Common mismatches that cancel seats

Most cancellations are not about rank or merit. They are small inconsistencies that surface at verification. Catch them before you reach the desk.

### Name and date of birth mismatches

Your name on the Class X certificate, your JEE form, and your ID should read the same way. A middle name on one document but not another, or a date of birth that differs from your Class X certificate, can put your seat on hold. The Class X certificate is usually the accepted date of birth proof, so make every other record agree with it.

### Wrong or expired category certificate

This is the single biggest reason reserved seats fall through. OBC-NCL and EWS certificates often must be issued recently, in the relevant year, and in the exact central format. A state-format or expired certificate may be rejected even if you genuinely belong to the category.

### Photo and upload issues

A blurry photo, a face that does not clearly match your JEE form, or an oversized scan that the portal rejects can all slow you down or cost you a deadline.

### Common mistake to fix: quick reference table

| Common mistake | Why it cancels a seat | How to fix it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Name spelling differs across JEE form, marksheet and ID | Verifier can't confirm you are the same person | Carry a board certificate, affidavit or gazette correction proving the link |
| Date of birth differs between Class X and JEE or ID | DOB is verified from the Class X certificate | Carry the original Class X certificate and a correction affidavit if needed |
| Category certificate in wrong format (state, not GoI) | Reserved seats need the central format | Get a fresh certificate in the correct GoI format before counselling |
| Expired or old category or EWS certificate | Validity (often current year) has lapsed | Apply early for a current-dated certificate |
| Photo mismatch or unclear photo | Identity can't be verified | Use the same photo as your JEE form; carry extra prints |
| Blurred or oversized uploads | Portal rejects the file | Re-scan clearly within the size and format limits |
| EWS criteria not met or wrong year | Claim found invalid at verification | Confirm eligibility and certificate year before claiming |
| Missing original at physical reporting | Verification can't be completed | Build the full checklist; carry originals plus two photocopy sets |

A single mismatch on a reserved-category document can cost you the reserved seat entirely, sometimes pushing you to a general-category cut-off you do not clear. Treat your certificate format and validity as seriously as your rank.

## Prepare early, not at the last minute

You don't need to wait for an allotment to start. Most of this can be done the week your result is expected. Certificates take time to issue, and corrections take longer. The verification window does not wait.

- Before results: get every certificate in the correct format and within validity. Re-issue anything old. Fix name and DOB mismatches now.
- Right after allotment: download the allotment letter. Note the accept-and-pay deadline in your calendar the same hour.
- Same day: accept, choose Float, Freeze or Slide, pay the SAF, save the receipt.
- Within the window: complete online reporting and upload clean scans.
- On the reporting date: carry originals plus two photocopy sets plus extra photos. Reach early; queues are long.

Make a physical folder. One labelled folder with originals, one with photocopies, plus a phone gallery album of scans. When you are tired and the queue is moving, organisation is what saves you.
