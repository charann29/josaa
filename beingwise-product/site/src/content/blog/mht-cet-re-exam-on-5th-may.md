The **MHT CET 2025 PCM re-exam** will be conducted on **May 5, 2025**, for candidates who faced discrepancies during the initial exam held on April 27. The re-test has been announced following reports of **translation errors in 21 questions** of the English-medium Mathematics paper. These mistakes, caused by inaccurate translations from Marathi, resulted in mismatched or incorrect answer options that could significantly impact students’ scores.

According to the official notification, the re-exam is exclusively for the **27,837 candidates** impacted across English, Urdu, and Marathi mediums. Only students whose grievances have been verified and approved by the exam authority are eligible to appear for the re-test. The list of eligible candidates is available on the official CET Cell website (cetcell.mahacet.org), and fresh admit cards must be downloaded prior to the re-exam date. **Candidates who faced no issues in the original exam will not be allowed to appear for the re-exam**.

Students are advised to follow all exam-day guidelines to ensure smooth participation strictly. This includes reaching the exam centre early, carrying the updated admit card and a valid photo ID, and avoiding any prohibited items such as electronic gadgets or study materials. The re-exam is a crucial opportunity for affected candidates, and adhering to official instructions will help avoid disqualification or confusion on the day of the test.

## Eligible Candidates for the MHT CET Re-Exam

These candidates will receive a fresh admit card, which must be downloaded from the official website. ## Reason for MHT CET PCM Re-Exam

The **MHT CET 2025 PCM re-exam**, scheduled for **May 5, 2025**, has been announced due to significant errors identified in the Mathematics paper (English medium) during the MHT CET 2025 exam date  on **April 27, 2025**. The issue involved **translation mistakes in 21 questions**, where the English version did not match the intended meaning from the Marathi original, leading to confusion and incorrect answer options.

## MHT CET 2025: Re-Test Admit Card

The MHT CET 2025 Re-Test Admit Card will be released by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, for candidates eligible to appear in the re-examination. This re-test is being conducted due to the cancellation of the PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) paper held on April 27, 2025, following technical issues during the Mathematics section. Candidates who are found eligible, as listed in the official notification, can download their re-test admit cards from the official website – cetcell.mahacet.org. Candidates must log in using their application number and password to access the admit card. The admit card will contain important details such as the re-test date, time, exam centre, and candidate instructions.

## Important Exam-Day Guidelines for MHT CET Exam

Candidates appearing for the **MHT CET 2025** must strictly adhere to the exam-day instructions issued by the State CET Cell. Proper preparation and compliance with guidelines can help avoid last-minute stress and ensure a smooth exam experience.

#### **Key Guidelines:**

- Carry a **printed copy of the admit card** along with a **valid government-issued photo ID** (Aadhaar card, PAN card, etc.).
- **Report to the exam centre** at least **60 minutes before** the reporting time mentioned on the admit card.
- Ensure all details on your admit card are **correct and legible** before the exam day.
- **Do not carry electronic items** such as mobile phones, smartwatches, calculators, Bluetooth devices, or earphones.
- **Stationery items like pens, pencils, and erasers** will be provided at the center
- **Bags, books, notes, and written materials** are prohibited in the exam hall.
- Candidates must **undergo biometric verification** and **frisking** at the entry point.
- Maintain **silence and discipline** inside the exam hall and follow the invigilator’s instructions carefully.
- Any form of **malpractice or cheating** will lead to disqualification and strict legal action.
- Candidates with disabilities may be allowed **extra time or scribes** as per norms, with prior approval.
- **Carry your own water bottle**, and keep it transparent with no labels.

## MHT CET Results

The **MHT CET results** will be announced soon on the MHT CET official website – cetcell.mahacet.org. Students who appeared for the PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) or PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) group exams can check their results by logging in with their application number and date of birth.

The result will display key details such as subject-wise scores, total marks, percentile rank, and qualification status. Once the results are declared, the **State CET Cell, Maharashtra,** will prepare a merit list based on candidates’ performance. This merit list will be used for the centralised admission process (CAP) for undergraduate courses in Engineering, Pharmacy, and Agriculture across participating colleges in Maharashtra.

## MHT CET Counselling

After the declaration of results, the **MHT CET counselling process** will be conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, through a **Centralised Admission Process (CAP)**. Eligible candidates must register online, fill in their course and college preferences, and upload the required documents. The counselling will be held in multiple rounds, including choice filling, document verification, and seat allotment. Seats will be allotted based on the candidate’s rank, preferences, reservation criteria, and seat availability. Once a seat is allotted, candidates must confirm their admission by paying the fee and reporting to the allotted college within the specified timeline.
