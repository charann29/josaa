## How Students React to Today’s JEE Mains Question Paper?

- **Arjun Patel** said that Physics was mostly formula-based, which made it easier to attempt. He rated Chemistry as balanced. Overall, he attempted around 33-36 questions out of 75. He was quite confident about his performance.
- **Rahul Verma** explained Physics as having direct formula-based questions from topics like Laws of Motion, Units & Measurement, and Rotational Motion. He rated Physics as moderate, but scoreing.
- **Sneha Iyer** rated the Mathematics session as the toughest in the JEE Mains Question Paper. She explained that questions were lengthy, requiring extensive calculations, mostly from topics like Integrals, Determinants, and Sequences & Series.
- **Kunal Shah** explained that Mathematics took the most time, as questions were taking longer than expected. Physics and Chemistry were relatively easier and doable compared to Mathematics.

### How to Calculate JEE Mains Percentile Score?

The National Testing Agency provides JEE Main results in the form of percentiles to facilitate fair results for all students, as there are multiple shifts.

To find your percentile in JEE Main, you need to first download the official answer key and match it with your answer sheet to find the number of correct, incorrect, and unattempted questions. Next, you need to calculate your raw score based on your performance. The raw score is calculated by adding 4 points for every correct answer and subtracting 1 point for every incorrect answer.

After obtaining your estimated raw score based on 300, you can use the [JEE Main percentile predictor](/rank-predictor/jee-main-percentile-predictor/) to estimate your percentile based on past trends. The estimated percentile will give you an idea of your performance, which can further be used to estimate your All India Rank (AIR) in JEE Main.
