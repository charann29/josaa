## **Admit Card Instructions for JEE Mains 2025**

The JEE Mains 2025 admit card is a crucial document that serves as your official entry pass to the exam centre. Without it, you will not be allowed to appear for the exam under any circumstances. Here’s everything you need to know:

**What information does the admit card contain?**

- Candidate’s name, roll number, date of birth, photograph, and signature
- Exam date and time, reporting time, and gate closing time
- Test centre name, address, and city
- Paper(s) you are appearing for
- Category and state code of eligibility
- Important exam day instructions

**Why is the admit card important on exam day?**

- Entry to the exam centre is strictly prohibited without a valid admit card.
- You must also bring a government-issued photo ID (such as PAN card, Driving License, Voter ID, Passport, Aadhaar card with photograph, E-Aadhar, Ration Card, or Aadhaar enrollment number with photo).
- Carry a passport-size photograph (the same as uploaded with your application form) to be pasted on the attendance sheet at the centre.
- Duly fill in the self-declaration form (if required by NTA) and bring it along.
- For candidates appearing for Paper 2 (B.Arch), additional items, such as a geometry box and drawing materials, are required as per the instructions on the admit card.

## JEE Mains 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

The JEE Mains 2025 exam is one of the most crucial milestones for students aiming to pursue engineering at top institutes like the NITs, IIITs, and other centrally funded technical institutions. As the exam day approaches, being aware of the official guidelines and instructions is just as important as academic preparation. From reporting time and dress code to items allowed inside the examination hall, understanding these rules can help you avoid last-minute stress and ensure a smooth test-day experience. Given below are the JEE Mains 2025 guidelines for the candidates to know before the exam.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Criteria** | **Guidelines** |
| **Arrival and Registration** | Arrive at least one hour before the JEE exam time for registration. Bring the admit card and any other required documents; entry without the admit card is not permitted. |
| **Hall Regulations** | Only the admit card and ID proof are allowed inside the exam hall. Follow all instructions provided by the invigilators. Familiarise yourself with the JEE exam rules before entering the hall. |
| **Writing Materials** | Pens, pencils, and blank paper for rough work will be provided. Write your name and roll number on the top of the rough work paper. Return the writing materials before leaving the hall. |
| **Aptitude Test II Requirements** | Bring your geometry box, pencils, crayons, or colored pencils for Aptitude Test II. Watercolors are not allowed as per the new IIT JEE rules. |
| **Attendance Sheet and Thumbprint** | Fill in the required details on the attendance sheet. Sign and affix a photograph in the designated spot. Ensure your thumbprint is clear and does not smudge. |
| **Prohibited Items** | No baggage, textual materials, calculators, slide rules, electronic watches, mobiles, pagers, bits of paper, or any electronic devices. Violating this rule will result in debarring from the examination. |
| **Silence Rule** | Maintain complete silence in the exam hall at all times. |

## Dress Code Guidelines for JEE Mains 2025

Wearing the right attire on exam day is crucial for a smooth entry and avoiding unnecessary delays at the security check. The National Testing Agency (NTA) issues clear dress code instructions to ensure fairness and security during JEE Mains 2025. Here’s what you need to know:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Guidelines** |
| Simple Clothing Recommended | Wear simple, light clothes without elaborate designs or embellishments for faster frisking and fewer checks. |
| Avoid Thick-Soled Shoes & Large Buttons | Thick-soled shoes and clothes with large buttons are not allowed; prefer flat footwear and clothes with small/no buttons. |
| Jewellery & Metal Items Not Allowed | No jewellery (rings, earrings, necklaces, bracelets, anklets, etc.) or metal items allowed; may be asked to remove. |
| Special Attire for Religious Reasons | Religious attire, such as turbans and hijabs, is allowed, but arrive early for thorough screening and frisking. |
| No Accessories | Avoid caps, scarves (except for religious use), sunglasses, or fashion accessories. |
| Avoid Clothing with Pockets | Not banned, but best avoided as clothing with many pockets may be subject to additional scrutiny. |

## **Special Guidelines for Diabetic Candidates**

To accommodate the health requirements of diabetic candidates, the NTA permits certain exceptions to the general rules regarding food and drink inside the examination hall:

- **Permitted Items:** Diabetic candidates may carry sugar tablets and whole fruits such as bananas, apples, or oranges. Additionally, they may bring a transparent water bottle to ensure they remain hydrated and can manage their blood sugar levels during the exam.
- **Prohibited Items:** While diabetic candidates can bring the items listed above, they are not allowed to carry any packed or processed foods, such as chocolates, candies, or sandwiches, into the examination hall.

**Note:** These allowances are strictly for candidates with diabetes and are intended to help them maintain stable blood sugar during the exam. Candidates should ensure that any food items brought comply with the guidelines to avoid any issues during security checks.

Including these details helps diabetic candidates prepare confidently and ensures that all candidates are aware of the special provisions in place.

## **Code of Conduct at the Exam Centre**

To ensure a fair and disciplined environment, all candidates must strictly adhere to the code of conduct set by the National Testing Agency (NTA) during the JEE Mains 2025 examination:

1. **Maintain Silence:** Candidates must remain completely silent throughout the exam. Any conversation, whispering, gestures, or disturbance in the examination hall will be treated as misbehaviour.
2. **Follow the Invigilator Instructions:** All instructions given by the invigilators or the centre superintendent must be followed without argument. Failure to comply may lead to disciplinary action.
3. **No Unfair Means:** Any attempt to use unfair means, such as copying, seeking help, using unauthorised materials, or impersonation, is strictly prohibited. If caught, the candidate’s exam will be cancelled and he/she may be debarred from future examinations, either permanently or for a specified period, depending on the severity of the offence.
4. **No Impersonation:** Impersonating another candidate or allowing someone else to impersonate you is a serious offense and will result in immediate cancellation of candidature and possible legal action.
5. **Attendance and Verification:** Candidates must fill in all required details in the attendance sheet, provide a clear left thumb impression, and paste their photograph as instructed. Any tampering or misrepresentation will be considered a breach of conduct.
6. **Respect Exam Property:** Candidates should not damage or tamper with any property at the exam centre. All rough sheets provided must be returned to the invigilator before leaving the hall.
7. **Consequences of Misconduct:** Any violation of the code of conduct, including causing disturbances, using unfair means, or failing to follow instructions, may result in disqualification from the current exam and being barred from future attempts.

By following the code of conduct, candidates help maintain a fair, secure, and respectful examination atmosphere for everyone.
