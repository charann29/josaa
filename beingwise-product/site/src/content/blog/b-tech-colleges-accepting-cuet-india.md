## CUET B.Tech Admission Process Explained

Admission to B.Tech programs through CUET (UG) is rapidly becoming a strong alternative for students seeking admission in engineering courses other than JEE Main. The CUET B.Tech admission process ensures flexibility, as the students get the chance to apply to multiple B.Tech colleges accepting CUET in India and get admitted to their preferred courses.

Once the CUET examination results have been released by NTA, the concerned universities initiate the admission process, which includes determining cutoff marks, merit list preparation, and counselling sessions. Below is a step-by-step process of the CUET B.Tech admission procedure.

| Stages | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Registration for CUET UG | Candidates register on the official portal, fill in details, and select PCM subjects along with preferred universities |
| Appear for Exam | Students take the CUET exam, which serves as the basis for admission to B.Tech colleges accepting CUET in India |
| Result Declaration | The National Testing Agency releases CUET scorecards and percentiles |
| University Application | Candidates apply to participating universities and choose B.Tech courses and specializations |
| Cutoff Release | Colleges release course-wise cutoffs based on CUET scores |
| Merit List | Universities prepare merit lists according to CUET performance |
| Counselling Process | Students participate in counselling, fill out choices, and lock preferences |
| Seat Allotment | Seats are allocated based on rank, preferences, and availability |
| Document Verification | Candidates submit required documents (marksheets, scorecard, ID proof, etc.) |
| Final Admission | Fee payment and confirmation of admission in the allotted college |

## Eligibility Criteria for B.Tech colleges accepting CUET

Candidates desiring admission into B.Tech colleges accepting CUET in India need to ensure that they fulfill the prescribed eligibility norms laid out by the respective universities. It is imperative to do so since students failing to fulfill these eligibility norms might fail to get admission in B.Tech colleges accepting CUET.

In order to gain admission in B.Tech colleges accepting CUET in India 2026, the candidate needs to:

- Have completed Class 12 or an equivalent examination from a recognised board, with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) as core subjects.
- Obtain a minimum of 50% in Class 12 (relaxation may apply to reserved categories depending on the university).
- Meet the eligibility standards for the Common University Entrance Test (CUET UG) 2026, which typically require candidates to score between 45-50% on their qualifying exam.

It should be noted that the eligibility requirements apply to the majority of B.Tech colleges accepting CUET, while there may be some variances depending on the institution. Candidates are therefore recommended to examine the exact requirements of their selected universities before applying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. How many B.Tech colleges accepting CUET 2026 in India?

At present, there are more than 220+ B.Tech colleges in India that accept CUET scores for admission.

### Q2. Is CUET UG enough for B.Tech admission?

Yes, CUET is accepted by many universities for B.Tech admission. However, top institutions like IITs and NITs still require JEE Main or JEE Advanced scores.

### Q3. What are the eligibility criteria for B.Tech colleges accepting CUET in India?

Candidates must have passed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) and typically need a minimum of 45–50% marks, depending on university norms.

### Q4. What is the fee structure for B.Tech colleges accepting CUET?

Fees vary widely, ranging from ₹50,000 to ₹2 lakh in government colleges and ₹5 lakh to ₹20 lakh in private universities.

### Q5. What are the best B.Tech colleges accepting CUET scores?

Some well-known colleges include BHU, Amity University, Lovely Professional University, and UPES Dehradun.

### Q6. What is the admission process for CUET B.Tech courses?

The process includes CUET registration, appearing for the exam, result declaration, cutoff release, counselling, and final seat allotment.

### Q7. Are CUET B.Tech colleges equal to IITs or NITs?

While CUET-based colleges may not match the prestige of IITs or NITs, many offer good academics and decent placement opportunities.

### Q8. Are there any placements in CUET B.Tech colleges?

Yes, many CUET-accepting colleges offer promising placements, especially in streams like Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence.
