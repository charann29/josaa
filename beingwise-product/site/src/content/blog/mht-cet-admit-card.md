The Maharastra Common Entrance Test (MHT CET) 2025 is for students who are seeking admission to various undergraduate courses in colleges and universities across Maharastra. It covers subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics or Biology. Candidates preparing for MHT CET require a thorough preparation and understanding of the syllabus as well as the paper pattern.

The MHT CET Admit card is issued by the Maharastra State Common Entrance Test Cell to candidates who have successfully registered for the MHT CET examination. The admit card contains all the important details of the candidates. Admit cards can be downloaded from the official website. Only those candidates who have filled out the application form before the deadline can download the admit card.

The MHT CET Admit card for the **PCB group is released on 4th April 2025** and for **PCM group **is released on** 10th April 2025.**

## How to Download the MHT CET Admit Card?

Candidates can download the admit card  from the official website by following the below steps:

**Step 1:** Go to the official website of MHT CET 2025 – [cetcell.mahacet.org](http://cetcell.mahacet.org)

**Step 2:** Click on the MHT CET Admit link

**Step 3:** Enter your login credentials details like Registered Email ID, and Password.

**Step 4:** Now, You will be able to see your admit card on the screen.

**Step 5:** Check all the details like your personal, academic, and other relevant details mentioned on the admit card.

**Step 6:** Finally, Click on the ‘Download’ button to view it offline.

**Step 7:** Make sure you take a soft copy of the Admit card.

## MHT CET Admit Card Details Mentioned on Admit Card

Candidates need to check the details mentioned below on the Admit card:

- Candidate Name
- Father’s Name
- Mother’s Name
- Roll Number
- Application Number
- Type of disability (If Applicable)
- Name and Address of the Exam centre
- Exam Date
- Candidate Photo
- Candidate Signature
- Reporting Time
- Exam Duration

## Documents to Carry for MHT CET Exam Centre?

Candidate must be ready with hard copies of the following documents to carry the MHT CET 2025 Exam Centre:

- Admit Card (At least 2 copies)
- MHT CET Application form
- Passport Size Photograph
- Aadhaar Card or any Other Government ID proof

## Things not to carry MHT CET Exam Centre?

The MHT CET exam center has strictly prohibited from carrying things that are mentioned below:

- Formula tables
- Calculators
- Smart Watches
- Mobiles
- Alarm clocks
- Piece of Personal paper
- Any Other Electronic Gadgets

## MHT CET Exam Day Guidelines

It is important to follow the MHT CET exam day guidelines which are mentioned below:

- Candidates must visit the exam center before the timings mentioned on the admit card.
- The Candidates must bring the documents like Admit card and other relevant documents to the exam hall.
- There is no extra time is given to the candidates for writing the exam.
- No candidate is allowed to leave the exam hall before the exam is done. And, no candidate is allowed inside after completion of the exam.
- No electronic gadgets, calculators, smart watches, or Mobile phones are allowed inside the exam hall.
- Candidates must sit in the allotted seats provided by the exam hall.
- If any candidate is found as unfair, he or she will be debarred from the exam and not allowed for the admission process

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Who is eligible for the MHT CET Exam?

Students who complete their 12th standard in PCM or PCB from the recognized board or university with 45% marks are eligible to take the MHT CET exam.

### 2. Can other state candidates apply for MHT CET?

Yes, the exam is open for all Indian nationals including those from other states. However, reservation policies are not applicable. The reservation policies apply only to candidates belonging to Maharastra.

### 3. Is the MHT CET exam compulsory to get into Engineering?

No, there are many options to get into the engineering. The MHT CET is one of the options.

### 4. When the MHT CET PCM admit card is released?

The MHT CET admit card for the PCM group is released on 10th April 2025.
