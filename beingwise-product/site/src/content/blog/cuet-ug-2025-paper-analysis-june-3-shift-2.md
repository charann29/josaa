## CUET UG 2025 GAT Paper Analysis

Students found the GAT paper to be tougher than previous shifts, especially due to the high number of advanced Mathematics questions, which made it challenging for non-science backgrounds. According to the student’s report, around 18 to 19 questions came from Maths alone, with a heavy focus on topics like Mensuration, Coordinate Geometry, and Interest calculations. While a few questions were easy, many were lengthy and concept-heavy. Students found that the Reasoning section had fewer questions and was moderately difficult, with typical topics like Blood Relation, Calendar, and Coding-Decoding. However, the GK section was relatively easier, with questions from Budget 2024–25, Indian Polity, History, and Current Affairs.

## Student Reviews For CUET UG 2025 June 3rd Shift 2 Exam

Most students from different states found CUET UG 2025 Shift 2, which was held on June 3rd, to be moderate. Here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

**Chemistry**
**Difficulty Level: Easy to Moderate**
**Student Feedback:** Many students found the chemistry paper challenging due to its emphasis on conceptual and numerical questions, particularly in inorganic chemistry. Time management was also a concern for some candidates.

**Biology**
**Difficulty Level: Easy
Student Feedback:** The biology paper was perceived as easier compared to other subjects. However, approximately 30% of students faced challenges with time management.

**English**
**Difficulty Level: Easy**
**Student Feedback:** The English paper included 25 reading comprehension questions divided among four passages. The literary aptitude section covered prepositions and adverbs, while other questions focused on synonyms, antonyms, one-word substitutions, idioms, phrasal verbs, and jumbled phrases. Notably, there were no questions regarding foreign vocabulary, reported speech, or active and passive voice.

**General Aptitude Test (GAT)**
**Difficulty Level: Difficult**
**Student Feedback:** The General Aptitude Test was rated easy to moderate, with reasoning and quantitative aptitude posing some difficulty. The General Knowledge/General Studies section presented a more significant challenge, falling within the moderate to difficult spectrum.

## CUET UG 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

Given below are a few CUET UG 2025 exam day guidelines that students need to follow.

- **Carry Only Required Items:** Only take your printed admit card (with passport-size photograph), your valid photo ID (Aadhaar or PAN card), a transparent ballpoint pen, and a transparent water bottle. Candidates can check all the CUET exam details on the admit card and are advised to adhere to the instructions.
- **Be On Time:** Report to the exam center well in advance relative to your reporting time (7:00 AM for Shift 1 and 1:00 PM for Shift 2). If you are late, you will not be allowed to enter.
- **Prohibited Items:** Do not bring mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, bags, books, or food if not medically required (with documentation).
- **Maintain Discipline:** Be seated in your assigned seat, only do rough work on the sheets provided, and do not perform any unfair practice or make any noise in the exam hall.
- **Time Management:** It is a critical challenge across subjects, especially with lengthy sections like RC or Quant. It’s advised to tackle easy questions first.
- **The Normalisation Factor:** This factor will account for variations in difficulty across shifts. The focus should be on attempting as many questions with 100% accuracy.
