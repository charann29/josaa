## CUET UG 2025: Exam Pattern

The CUET UG exam pattern is designed to test the student’s skills across three major skills, i.e. language, subject knowledge, and general aptitude. The duration of the exam is 60 minutes for each subject. Refer to the following table for the detailed CUET UG exam pattern:

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Section** | **Duration of the Exam** | **No of Questions** | **Questions Type** |
| Section IA- Language | 60 minutes for each subject | 50 Questions | Reading comprehension questions are primarily designed to evaluate a candidate’s proficiency in language and understanding of written text. |
| Section II- Domain Specific | 50 Questions | Questions on domain-specific subjects. |
| Section III- General Test | 50 Questions | This section will assess a candidate’s proficiency in General Knowledge, Current Affairs, and General Mental Ability, along with Numerical Ability and Quantitative Reasoning based on fundamental concepts from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration up to Grade 8 level. Additionally, it will evaluate Logical and Analytical Reasoning skills. |

## Student Reviews for CUET UG 2025 May 15th Shift 1 Exam

Students from different states have reported the CUET UG 2025 exam held on May 15th, shift 1, to be Easy to moderate, compared to the previous day’s papers. Check the student’s reviews below:

**Riya Sharma (from Delhi):**
Economics was moderate but tricky because of the Microeconomics focus. I had revised it, so it felt manageable. However, the General Test was unexpectedly Quant-heavy and time-consuming, making it tough to finish in time.”

**Arjun Patel (from Ahmedabad):**
“Accountancy was tougher than expected. Questions from Debentures and Partnership had confusing theoretical parts. Numerical questions were fine, but some out-of-pattern theory questions were difficult. Business Studies, though, was a relief — very easy with straightforward case studies.”

**Sneha Gupta (from Lucknow):**
“The General Test surprised me. Usually, the reasoning is more, but this time, the Quantitative questions were lengthy and twisted. Time management became a big challenge. Maths was also quite hard, especially questions from Integration and Matrices.”

**Vikram Mehta (from Jaipur):**
Physics was moderate and quite easy overall. Hardly 3-4 tricky questions. English was moderate too, but scrolling up and down for Reading Comprehension wasted a lot of time. Practising on-screen reading helped me a bit.”

**Ananya Reddy (from Hyderabad):**
“Overall, the paper was a mix. Business Studies was very easy, while Accountancy had tough theory parts. The General Test pattern change was shocking, with so much Quant. Watching analysis sessions really helped me tackle unexpected topics.”
