## Table of Contents

KCET Chapter Wise Weightage: KCET chapter wise weightage refers to the number or percentage of questions asked from different chapters in the exam. It helps aspirants identify high-priority topics by analysing trends from previous years’ question papers.

Although the KCET syllabus is based on the 1st and 2nd PUC curriculum, the number of questions asked from each chapter is not distributed equally. Analysis of previous years’ KCET papers shows that the 2nd PUC syllabus contributes more compared to 1st PUC. However, this does not mean that in the final stage of preparation you should revise every high-weightage chapter in detail. During the last phase, preparation needs to be more strategic and focused. Along with chapter-wise weightage, students should also pay attention to topics that repeatedly appear in previous KCET papers. By identifying chapters that have both high weightage and high repetition in PYQs, students can prioritise the most important topics for revision. This blog on KCET Chapter Wise Weightage 2026 is going to discuss subject-wise chapter weightage, previous year trends and More. Let’s begin!

## KCET Chapter Wise Weightage: Subject-Wise Breakdown

The number of questions from each chapter may vary slightly every year. However, analysis of previous KCET papers shows that certain topics consistently carry higher weightage. In general, **second-year PUC chapters contribute slightly more questions than first-year topics**, as they involve advanced concepts and applications. The following sections provide a subject-wise breakdown of important chapters and their expected contribution in the exam.

## How to Use KCET Chapter Wise Weightage in KCET Preparation: Tips

From the sections above, you already got the list of important chapters. Now you have to shortlist the high-weightage chapters to make an efficient study plan. But before proceeding, keep the following points in mind :

- **First focus on chapters with 7-9 questions**, as they contribute the largest share of the paper.
- **Next revise medium-weightage chapters (4-6 questions)** to strengthen overall coverage.
- **Finally review lower-weightage topics** during the last revision phase.
- Practice **previous year KCET questions** from these chapters to understand the exam pattern.
- Attempt **mock tests regularly** to improve time management and accuracy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is KCET chapter wise weightage 2026 PDF available for download?

No, the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) does not release an official **KCET chapter-wise weightage PDF**. KEA only publishes the **KCET syllabus**, which is based on the 1st and 2nd PUC curriculum. The chapter-wise weightage available online is usually prepared by subject experts through analysis of previous year question papers.

### 2. What is the weightage of 11th and 12th syllabus in KCET?

KCET includes topics from both **1st PUC (Class 11)** and **2nd PUC (Class 12)** syllabus. However, analysis of previous exam papers shows that **2nd PUC chapters contribute around 65–70% of the questions**, while **1st PUC topics account for nearly 30–35%** of the paper.

### 3. What is the KCET Mathematics chapter wise weightage?

In KCET Mathematics, **Algebra and Calculus topics usually carry higher weightage**. Chapters such as **Matrices, Permutation and Combination, Binomial Theorem, Integrals, and Probability** frequently appear in the exam and often contribute multiple questions.

### 4. What are the most important chapters for KCET Physics?

Some of the most important chapters for KCET Physics include **Gravitation, Thermodynamics, Oscillations, Current Electricity, Magnetism, and Ray Optics**. These chapters have shown consistent question frequency in previous KCET exams.

### 5. Where can I check KCET Biology and Chemistry chapter wise weightage 2026?

Students can check **KCET Biology and Chemistry chapter-wise weightage** through previous year question paper analysis and exam preparation articles. Important Biology chapters usually include **Genetics, Human Physiology, and Ecology**, while in Chemistry, topics such as **Mole Concept, Organic Reactions, Electrochemistry, and Coordination Compounds** often carry higher weightage.
