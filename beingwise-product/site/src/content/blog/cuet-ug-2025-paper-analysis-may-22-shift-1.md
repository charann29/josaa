## CUET UG Exam Pattern 2025

The CUET UG 2025 exam is conducted to test the candidate across three major areas, i.e. language, subject knowledge, and aptitude. Candidates are allotted 60 minutes for each subject. Candidates can select a maximum of 5 subjects forthe  CUET UG 2025 exam. Check the following table for more details regarding the CUET exam pattern.

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Section** | **No of Questions** | **Duration of the Exam** | **Questions Type** |
| Section IA- Language | 50 Questions | 60 minutes for each subject | Questions on reading comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary are asked to test the candidate’s English proficiency. |
| Section II- Domain Specific | 50 Questions | Questions based on the domain-specific subjects are asked to test the candidate’s subject proficiency. |
| Section III- General Test | 50 Questions | This section assesses a candidate’s proficiency in General Knowledge, Current Affairs, Logical and Analytical Reasoning, and Mental Ability. It also evaluates Numerical and Quantitative Reasoning using fundamental concepts from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration, typically taught up to Grade 8. |

## Student Initial Reaction for CUET Exam: May 22nd Shift 1 Exam

Check the student’s initial reaction after the exam:

**Aayush (from Jaipur):** “The overall paper was moderate, but Maths was quite lengthy and tricky. I spent a lot of time on Calculus and Differential Equations. The General Test had a good mix. Quant was okay if formulas were known, but Reasoning was easier.”

**Sana (from Lucknow):** “English and Hindi were both easy. I found the grammar section in Hindi very scoring. The GK part of the General Test had repeated questions from previous shifts, like City Nicknames and famous battles—so my revision helped a lot.”

**Rohit (from Delhi):** “Physics was manageable with a few numericals that needed quick calculations. Chemistry was mostly theory-based. The General Test had fewer Reasoning questions in my set, but more Quant, especially Mensuration and Profit & Loss.”

**Priya (from Patna):** “Accountancy and Business Studies were surprisingly straightforward, no case studies in my paper. In English, RC was simple, and there were questions on Narration and Clauses that I didn’t expect but could handle.”

**Vikram (from Hyderabad):** “GAT was unpredictable. I had more GK questions than Reasoning. The Clock and Calendar questions in Reasoning were just like what I practised. Maths was tough for me, Probability and Matrices took time.”

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. **What was the overall difficulty level of CUET UG 2025 May 22nd Shift 1?**

The paper was generally rated easy to moderate. While sections like English, Hindi, and General Knowledge were manageable, students found Maths to be lengthy and time-consuming, especially topics like Calculus and Probability

### 2.********************Which topics dominated the General Test section in the CUET UG 2025 exam?********************

Key areas included Mensuration, Percentages, Coding-Decoding, Odd One Out, and Current Affairs. Many students noticed a higher number of Quantitative Aptitude questions, especially from Geometry and Profit & Loss, with fewer Reasoning items in some sets.

### **3. ************************Were there any irrelevant questions in the English section?**************************

Yes, besides the expected comprehension and vocabulary, students reported questions on Narration, Clauses, and Active-Passive Voice, which were less commonly asked in previous shifts but still easy to attempt.

### **4. ****************How did students find the Chemistry paper?******************

The Chemistry section was mostly theory-based and considered easy to moderate. A few numericals appeared in Solid State and Electrochemistry, but case-based questions, especially from D & F Block and Coordination Compounds, tested deeper conceptual clarity.

### 5. ******Was the Hindi section easy to attempt?******

Yes, students found the Hindi section straightforward. It featured simple grammar-based questions, short paragraphs for comprehension, and familiar topics like Idioms and Figures of Speech.

### 6. **Were there any technical issues in Shift 1?**

No major issues reported; entry was smooth with early reporting at 7 AM.

### 7. **How should students use this analysis for the next shift?**

Revise high-weightage topics, practice formulas, and focus on recent GK.
