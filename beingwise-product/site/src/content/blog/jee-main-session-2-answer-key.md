## **JEE Mains 2025 Counselling and Seat Allotment Process**

JEE Main Counselling is a centralised process conducted for admissions into NITs, IIITs, GFTIs, and other participating institutions based on JEE Main scores. Counselling is managed by the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) and typically involves multiple rounds, during which candidates can select their preferred colleges and courses. Seat allotment is done based on rank, category, seat availability, and choices filled by the candidates. After allotment, students must verify their documents and complete the admission formalities. Special rounds will also be conducted for vacant seats by CSAB.

After the declaration of JEE Main results, candidates seeking admission to NITs, IIITs, GFTIs, and other participating institutes must participate in the centralised counselling and seat allotment process conducted by JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority). This process involves several key steps and important deadlines.

**Key Steps in the Counselling and Seat Allotment Process**

- **JoSAA Registration and Choice Filling:** Eligible candidates must register on the JoSAA portal within the specified dates. During registration, candidates fill in their preferred choices of institutes and programs based on their JEE Main rank.
- **Mock Seat Allotment:** JoSAA conducts mock seat allotment rounds to help candidates understand their chances of getting a preferred seat. Candidates can revise their choices after mock allotments are made.
- **Locking Choices:** Choices must be locked before the deadline. If not locked, the system automatically locks the last saved choices.
- **Seat Allotment Rounds:** JoSAA conducts multiple rounds (usually six) of seat allotment. After each round, candidates are allotted seats based on their rank, preferences, and seat availability.
- **Seat Acceptance and Online Reporting:** Candidates who are allotted a seat must accept it by paying the seat acceptance fee and uploading the required documents within the specified deadline. Candidates must also indicate their willingness using the “Freeze,” “Float,” or “Slide” options:
  - **Freeze:** Accept the allotted seat and refrain from participating in further rounds.
  - **Float:** Accept the seat but wish to be considered for higher preferences in subsequent rounds.
  - **Slide:** Accept the seat in the current institute, but wish to be considered for a higher preferred program in the same institute.
- **Document Verification:** Uploaded documents are verified online by the reporting authorities. Candidates must respond promptly to any queries raised during the verification process.
- **Withdrawal and Exit Options:** Candidates can withdraw from the counselling process within certain rounds if they do not wish to continue. After the withdrawal deadline, no further withdrawal is permitted.
- **Final Admission and Reporting to Institutes:** After the final round, candidates must report to the allotted institute for admission and complete all formalities.
