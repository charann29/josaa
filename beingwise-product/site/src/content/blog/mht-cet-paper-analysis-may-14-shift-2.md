## Subject-wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis

The MHT CET 2nd attempt exam for the PCM group lasts three hours. The first 90 minutes were set aside for Physics and Chemistry, with the remaining 90 minutes for Mathematics. Let’s look at the subject-specific MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 for PCM groups below.

### MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 for Physics Section

**As per the MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 May 14 Shift 2, the Physics section had a moderate level of difficulty.** The majority of the questions were formula-based and practical applications; however, some numericals demanded conceptual knowledge and mathematical computation skills.

Students found the Physics section relatively easier compared to Mathematics, even though some questions took a bit more time. Find out the important topics from the Physics section here.

- Current Electricity
- Electrostatics
- Modern Physics
- Magnetism
- Thermodynamics
- Oscillations
- Ray Optics
- Semiconductor

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Mathematics Section

From the initial feedback and MHT CET 2026 paper analysis, the Mathematics section was the toughest and lengthiest part in Shift 2. The majority of the students found it highly calculative and time-consuming. Some questions needed multiple calculations, and proper time management was required to answer all 50 questions. Here are the important topics from the Mathematics section.

- Integration
- Solution of a Triangle
- Vectors
- Line and Plane
- Probability
- Differential Equations
- Matrices and Determinants
- Application of Derivatives

### MHT CET 2026 Exam Analysis for Chemistry Section

Of all the three sections, Chemistry was the easiest and highest-scoring part in the May 14 shift 2 MHT CET May 14 Question paper. Most of the questions were direct, theoretical, and NCERT-based. Candidates having a strong grip on NCERT concepts found the section relatively easier. Have a look at the important Chemistry topics asked in the paper below.
