## Subject-wise JEE Mains 8 April Shift 2 Paper Analysis

In consideration of feedback from both students and experts, the JEE Mains 8 April shift 2 paper analysis can be said to be moderately difficult, where Mathematics was long and time-consuming, whereas Chemistry was a little tricky, while Physics had an even distribution of questions and was scoring too. Let’s now go through subject-wise JEE Mains April 8 paper analysis and key topics below.

### JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

The Chemistry section involved many statement-based questions (sometimes having as many as 5-7 statements per question), making it time-consuming and prone to silly mistakes. Take a look at the key topics asked in the JEE Mains Chemistry Question Paper.

- **Inorganic Chemistry:** There has been an increase in Salt Analysis, Coordination Compounds, and p-blocks, which together can carry around 35-40% of the total marks in the entire portion.
- **Organic Chemistry:** GOC (General Organic Chemistry), Biomolecules, and Naming are important topics; however, students find that these questions have become somewhat difficult now.
- **Physical Chemistry:** Most questions from Physical Chemistry are found in the Numerical Value Type (NVT) portion of the paper and can often be numerical in nature rather than theoretical.

### JEE Mains 2026 Analysis for Maths Section

In the JEE Mains 8 April shift 2 analysis, Mathematics is often termed as the ‘hardest’ section due to its difficult calculation-based nature. The pattern seen in Mathematics, as was expected, was that it was extremely lengthy, with very few candidates attempting more than 12-13 questions.  Take a look at the key topics from where questions are asked in the JEE Mains Maths Section.

- **Vectors & 3D Geometry:** These are regarded as the most important concepts for the 2026 sessions, holding considerable weightage in almost all shifts.
- **Calculus:** This topic is highly focused upon and contains topics like Integration (Definite and Indefinite), Differential Equations, Limits, Functions, and Application of Derivatives.
- **Algebra:** Important topics to be aware of are Matrices & Determinants, Sequence and Series (AP/GP), Quadratic Equations, and Binomial Theorem.
- **Coordinate Geometry:** Important sub-topics include Conic Sections (Ellipse, Parabola, and Hyperbola), Circle, and Straight Lines.

### JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Physics Section

The Physics section is rated moderate and heavily formula-based, allowing students with solid fundamentals to score rapidly. Physics was known for its great uniformity, meaning no particular chapter dominated, forcing students to have a broad knowledge base.

- **Modern Physics:** Units such as the Photoelectric Effect, Dual Nature of Matter, need special attention as they are of high priority
- **Electromagnetism:** This covers weightage on topics like Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetic Effects of Current, and Capacitors
- **Mechanics:** Important chapters include Laws of Motion, Work, Energy, and Power, and Rotational Motion
- **Other Topics:** Other important topics covered in JEE Main analysis include Thermodynamics, Units and Dimensions, Ray Optics, and Semiconductors.
