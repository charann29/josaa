JoSAA Counselling 2026 is the centralised admission process, conducted by the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA), and it ensures a transparent and merit-based allocation of engineering seats across India’s top institutes via a single online platform. The JoSAA Counselling 2026 Dates are announced. As per the JoSAA Counselling Schedule, the candidate registration and choice filling start on June 02, 2026. Eligible students must complete their JoSAA Registration before the last date.

## What is JoSAA Counselling?

The JoSAA counselling process is conducted through an online portal, where qualified applicants are allotted seats according to their rank and preferences. The Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) conducts the counselling process for admission to institutes, including 23 IITs, 31 NITs, 26 IIITs, IIEST Shibpur, and 40 other GFTIs. The candidates qualified for the JEE Advanced 2026 can opt for the IITs, whereas those qualified for the JEE Main can join NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.

## JoSAA Counselling 2026 Eligibility Criteria

Before moving to the JoSAA Counselling 2026 registration process, candidates have to meet the eligibility criteria set by JoSAA to get admitted into participating colleges.

### JoSAA Counselling Eligibility for IIT Admission

- To become eligible for getting admission to IITs through JoSAA Counselling 2026 is that a candidate must qualify JEE Advanced 2026.
- Candidates must have qualified Class 12 (or its equivalent examination) in 2025 or 2026 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as their core subjects.
- Besides, candidates have to either score a minimum of 75% marks in Class 12 (65% marks for SC/ST/PwD candidates) or be included in the top 20 percentile of their respective board.

### JoSAA Counselling Eligibility for NITs, IIITs, IIEST, and GFTIs

- For getting admitted to NITs, IIITs, IIEST Shibpur, and other GFTIs, candidates need to score a valid JEE Main rank in JEE Main 2026.
- They should have given Class 12 (or its equivalent examination) in 2024, 2025, and 2026 according to the requirements of their respective courses.
- Candidates will either be required to score a minimum of 75% marks in Class 12 (65% for SC/ST/PwD candidates) or be among the top 20 percentile of successful candidates in their respective boards.

## Complete Guide to JoSAA Counselling Process

The JEE Counselling process is carried out by the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) for admissions to IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. The JoSAA counselling procedure consists of registration, option filling, mock seat allotment, final allotment, payment and confirmation of allotment, document verification, and reporting to the designated institute.

All the steps in the JoSAA counselling process must be followed to get admitted to the institute of choice.

### Step 1: JoSAA Registration 2026

The [JoSAA Registration 2026](/blog/engineering-colleges/documents-required-for-josaa-counselling/) is the first phase of the whole counselling process. JoSAA Counselling Registration needs to be done via the portal at josaa.nic.in. Candidates who clear JEE Main 2026 or JEE Advanced 2026 are eligible to apply for the counselling process.

#### How to Apply for JoSAA Counselling 2026?

Here is the step-by-step process for the JoSAA registration below.

1. Visit the official website of JoSAA.
2. Log in to the website by entering the JEE Main 2026 application number, password, and security pin.
3. Read the instructions shown on the screen.
4. Press the ‘I Agree’ button.
5. Fill in the required details in the JoSAA registration form.
6. Check the information entered and press ‘Confirm Registration’ to submit the form.

### Step 2: Choice Filling and Choice Locking

The next step after successful JoSAA Registration is to make your preferences regarding your chosen colleges and courses. This process is done according to the choices made by the candidates and their rank. Here are the step-by-step instructions for the JoSAA Choice Filling and Locking process given below.

- Choose your preferred institutes and academic programs from the available selections.
- Add, modify, reorganise, or delete options as needed.
- Examine all chosen preferences carefully.
- Finalise your choices before the deadline.
- Candidates may amend and lock their preferences during the counselling sessions, as permitted by JoSAA.

### Step 3: JoSAA Counselling Seat Allotment

The JoSAA Counselling Seat Allotment Result will be announced through various rounds, depending on the candidate’s rank in JEE Main 2026, category, available seats, and the courses selected during the counselling process. The candidate can find the allotted institution and course from his/her end by using the JoSAA portal.

Upon the announcement of the JoSAA Counselling Seat Allotment Result, those who have been offered admission can follow any one of the below-mentioned methods:

- **Freeze:** This is the first choice for candidates who like their allotted seat. They will not be called for further counselling rounds.
- **Float:** This is another choice for those who accept their allotted seats but would want another chance in other rounds of counselling.
- **Slide:** Those who have chosen this step may get an upper preference of courses in that particular institute in future rounds of counselling.

### Step 4: Seat Acceptance Fees For JoSAA Counselling

Candidates opting for either freeze or float should submit the JoSAA Counselling Fees online to confirm their allocated seats. Additionally, they need to upload the scanned copies of the relevant documents.

Following the successful submission of fees and verification of documents, JoSAA will issue the provisional seat allotment letter to the candidates. The candidates need to download the letter and act accordingly by paying the balance fee and reporting to the institute. Take a look at JoSAA Counselling Fees for the Seat Acceptance below

| Category | Seat Acceptance Fee |
| --- | --- |
| SC, ST, PwD, SC-PwD, ST-PwD, OBC-NCL-PwD, GEN-PwD | ₹20,000 |
| All Other Candidates | ₹45,000 |

### Step 5: Reporting to the Allocated Institute

The fifth and final step of the JoSAA counselling process is reporting to the allocated institute. Candidates should report to the allocated institute to complete the admission formalities within the stipulated date as mentioned in the allotment letter. During reporting, candidates should keep the following points in mind.

- Pay any outstanding admission fees, if any.
- Present all the original documents for verification.
- Provide all the certificates and other documents necessary for admission.
- Admission formalities at the level of the institute should be completed.

It should be noted that the failure to report within the stipulated period will result in the loss of the seat allocated to them. Candidates can select the institutes/programmes as per their choices.

## JoSAA Counselling Cutoff 2026

The JoSAA Counselling 2026 Cutoff is released after every round of seat allotment for admissions to NITs, IIITs, IIEST Shibpur, and many other government-funded technical institutions. The cutoff is an important parameter that indicates the minimum rank at which a candidate must score in order to get admission into a certain institute and program.

The cutoff for the JoSAA Counselling 2026  highly varies depending on many aspects, like the number of candidates, seats, the difficulty level of the exam, reservations, and the popularity of the courses.

### What is JoSAA Counselling Opening and Closing Rank?

JoSAA Counselling 2026 Opening and Closing Ranks refer to the ranks between which candidates are offered admission in the given institute/branch/cut-off category. These ranks are announced after every counselling round and give insights to the students regarding their chances of admission in IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.

- **Opening Rank:** Lowest rank from where admissions commence for a certain course and college in a particular counselling round.
- **Closing Rank:** Highest rank till where admissions take place in a particular counselling round for a certain course/college.

For example, in a certain NIT branch, the opening rank is 5,000, and the closing rank is 15,000; then it means that ranks between 5,000 and 15,000 were offered admission in that counselling round.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What is JoSAA Counselling?

JoSAA Counselling is the centralized online seat allocation process conducted for admissions to IITs, NITs, IIITs, IIEST Shibpur, and other Government Funded Technical Institutes (GFTIs). Candidates who qualify JEE Main 2026 or JEE Advanced 2026 are eligible to participate.

### 2. What is the JoSAA Counselling Registration Fee?

There is no separate registration fee for JoSAA Counselling. However, candidates who are allotted a seat must pay the prescribed seat acceptance fee to confirm their admission process.

### 3. What is the process of JoSAA Counselling Seat Allotment?

The JoSAA seat allotment process is based on the candidate’s JEE rank, category, seat availability, and the choices filled and locked during the counselling process.

### 4. When does JoSAA Counselling 2026 begin?

JoSAA Counselling 2026 is scheduled to begin on June 2, 2026, at 5:00 PM with registration, choice filling, document upload, and other admission-related activities.

### 5. What is the last date for JoSAA Counselling Registration 2026?

The last date for JoSAA Counselling 2026 registration and choice filling is June 11, 2026, at 5:00 PM. After this deadline, the choices will be automatically locked by the system.

### 6. When will the JoSAA Mock Seat Allotment Result 2026 be declared?

The first JoSAA 2026 mock seat allotment result is scheduled to be released on June 8, 2026, while the second mock seat allotment result will be announced on June 10, 2026.
