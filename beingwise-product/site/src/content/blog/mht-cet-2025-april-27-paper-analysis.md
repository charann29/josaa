## MHT CET Counselling 2025

MHT CET counselling process 2025 is conducted online. Below are the steps candidates need to follow for MHT CET 2025 counselling:

- **Registration:** Enter your personal, contact, and security details on the MHT CET official website to start the registration process. Once these details have been entered, you will need to create a password. After setting the password, fill in the other required fields. After that, applicants should submit their qualifications, including their overall and subject-specific grades from grades 10 and 12, along with the names of the relevant boards.

- **Uploading Scanned Documents:** In the second step of MHT CET counselling, candidates are required to upload the given documents:
  - 10th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - 12th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - MHT CET Scorecard 2025
  - Migration Certificate
  - Category Certificate (if applicable)
  - Character Certificate
  - Domicile Certificate (if applicable)
  - Print out the MHT CET Counselling registration Form

- **Payment of Counselling Fees**– Candidates are required to pay the counselling fees online, depending on their category:-

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Courses** | **Registration Fee for JEE Main Applicants (All India Quota and Institutional-Level Quota)** | **Registration Fee for MHT CET Qualified Candidates** |
| BTech | INR 800 | INR 600 |
| BArch | INR 800 | INR 600 |
| BTech (Lateral Entry) | INR 800 | INR 600 |
| BPharma (Lateral Entry) | INR 800 | INR 600 |

- **Merit List Release:** The committee will make the MHT CET merit list public. The Class 12 and CET scores will be used to determine the marks in a 50:50 ratio. You can submit objections on the website if you find any inconsistencies in the merit list.

- **Choice Filling:** In this step, you must choose your college as well as your preferred branch and specialisations. Seats will be distributed by the committee based on the merit list ranking.

- **Seat Allotment:** Seats will be distributed according to the selections made and the merit positions of the candidates. Candidates must pay the admission fee and show up at the designated college to confirm their seat.

## MHT CET PCM Exam Analysis: Student Reviews For April 27th Shift 1 and Shift 2

According to student reviews from the MHT CET 2025, April 27th – Shift 1 & Shift 2, here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

**Shift 1:** Students considered the MHT CET paper pattern to be moderate to difficult. The Maths paper was particularly found to be challenging as students reported not only the degree of difficulty but also mentioned options being incorrect, or didn’t even have an answer option.

**Shift 2:** The MHT CET paper pattern was still moderate, and Maths had a long section. But students didn’t have the technical issues that were found in shift 1. Most students managed the paper and their time

## Conclusion

The MHT CET 2025 PCM papers, conducted on April 27th, presented mixed experiences for candidates across both shifts. Shift 1 was characterised by a severe technical glitch, especially in the Mathematics papers, with multiple questions presenting incorrect options or missing options altogether, causing a real cause for concern for students, leading to significant consequences. Whilst shift 2 was executed relatively smoothly and bore a more even structure, however, time-intensive questions were there, especially in Mathematics and Physics

Across both MHT CET PCM papers, Chemistry was the easiest and most scoring section, with Chemistry sourced mostly from NCERT textbook questions. Physics examined students on the understanding of concepts, but they also need to calculate things quickly and coordinate their time during the test. Lastly Mathematics section, students must have skills in problem-solving and efficient time management, as noted still primarily from shift 1’s experience. However, every candidate still ultimately had an experience.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. **********Was MHT CET 2025 Shift 2 easier than Shift 1?**********

Yes, the majority of students felt that Shift 2 of MHT CET PCM was better balanced and fairer than Shift 1, which had all-around higher difficulty and technical issues.

### 2. ************Should I focus more on the NCERT for Chemistry in MHT CET 2025?************

Most Chemistry questions were directly from NCERT, so if you want to score well, you must focus on NCERT.

### 3. ******************Are there any repeated or familiar questions from previous years or a mock test in MHT CET?******************

Yes, there were a number of questions in the MHT CET question paper of Chemistry that were either taken from NCERT or were similar to questions in typical practice/mock tests, which helped better-prepared students.

### 4. **************How difficult was managing time in both shifts of MHT CET 2025?**************

In MHT CET Shift 1, time pressure was exacerbated due to wrong options in Maths, leading to confusion. MHT CET question paper of Shift 2, time was an issue because of the lengthy Physics and Maths questions.

### 5. **Was there any section that was easier for students to score in MHT CET 2025?**

Yes, MHT CET question paper of Chemistry was the easiest and highest-scoring section in both shifts, allowing students to complete it quickly and giving them more time to work on the more difficult sections.
