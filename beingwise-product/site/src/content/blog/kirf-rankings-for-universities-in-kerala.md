## Table of Contents

KIRF rankings, published every year by the Kerala Institutional Ranking Framework, are published based on several parameters. KIRF ranks the universities on certain parameters such as Teaching and learning resources, Graduation Outcomes, Research excellence, etc. The Kerala Institutional Ranking Framework is wing under the KSHEC, which ranks the higher educational institutions based on the parameters mentioned above. In this blog, let’s understand the top 10 universities ranked under the KIRF rankings 2024. Individuals can also visit the official website for a complete list of universities ranked under the KIRF Rankings <https://kirf.kshec.org>.

## Overview of the KIRF Rankings for Universities in Kerala

The KIRF ranks the universities in Kerala based on a set of parameters. KIRF considers five major parameters and ranks all the participating universities in Kerala State. Let’s check how KIRF ranks the Universities in Kerala:

### Parameters of KIRF Rankings for Universities in Kerala

KIRF considers the following five parameters while ranking the universities in Kerala.

**1. Teaching, Learning & Resources(TLR):** This parameter measures the teaching and learning outcomes, etc. TLR will be given a score of 10,0, which is divided across several other parameters such as Student Strength(SS), Faculty-Student Ratio (FSR), Faculty Qualification and Experience(FQE), Online Education(OE), Subscription to e-Journals(SEJ), and Financial Resources and their Utilisation(FRU).

**2. Knowledge Dissemination and Research Excellence(KDRE):** The impact of the knowledge dissemination and the institution’s commitment to research is measured through these parameters. This parameter is measured through several other parameters, such as Research Productivity(RP), Research Impact(RI), Intellectual Property Rights(IPR), and  Research Footprint/Consultancy/Startups (RFCS).

**3. Graduation Outcome(GO):** Institution’s excellence in providing students with better future endeavours is measured through this parameter. This section includes metrics such as the Metric for University Examinations (GUE), the Metric for Entrance Examinations UGC-NET/JRF/CSIR/GATE/GRE (GEE), Placement and Higher Studies (PH).

**4. Outreach and Inclusivity(OI):** This parameter measures outreach and inclusivity of the college. Parameters such as Percentage of Students from outside the State/Country (Campus/Region Diversity-RD), Percentage of First Generation Learners (FGL), Facilities for Differently Abled (FDA), and Social Inclusiveness(SI) are considered while measuring this.

**5. Scientific Temper and Secular Outlook(STSO):** The university’s commitment to promoting scientific temper and a secular outlook among the students is measured using this parameter, with the help of several other parameters like Initiatives/programmes organised/participated by students and faculty of the institutions reflective of scientific temper and secular outlook (STSO Ql), Adoption of Green Technology (GT), and Accreditation and Ranking (AR).

## How to Use KIRF Rankings for College/University Selection?

KIRF rankings provide an overview of the university’s or [college’s performance](/blog/engineering-colleges/top-engineering-colleges-in-kerala/) across various areas such as teaching methods and learning outcomes. Students can consider these factors while selecting their colleges, and here’s how you can decide:

**1. Use KIRF Rankings as a Starting Point:** Consider the top 50-100 colleges in the KIRF rankings, and also consider checking the NIRF rankings and list out your preferences accordingly.

**2. Consider fees and ROI:** Research about the placement opportunities, fees, and other factors to determine whether they provide a good return on investment and better opportunities..

**3. Consider location and accessibility:** Consider proximity, networking opportunities, and cost of living when you are aspiring to join colleges located far from your home.

**4. Consider faculty and academic environment:** Check faculty qualifications through the college’s official website or from platforms like LinkedIn, etc. Faculty is important to consider while aspiring to join a college, as they support your learning needs.

**5. Branch/Stream over College:** It is important to choose your preferred branch over a reputed college. It can engage you and keep you on track for a longer time.
