## What is the VITEEE Result Calculation Method?

VITEEE results are calculated using a statistical method to make the examination fair for all participants appearing in different shifts and dates. Given that the exam is held in multiple sessions and question papers are of different difficulty levels, the authorities use a normalisation process.

This prevents students from being wrongly rewarded or penalised based on question difficulty. For this, VIT adopts an Equi-percentile Equating method, which transforms raw scores to percentile ranks to compare them on a common scale.

The percentile rank is the relative standing of a candidate’s performance and is equitable for ranking and preparing merit lists. For instance, a higher percentile means a better performance compared to the rest. This approach provides transparency in the evaluation and eliminates any bias due to sets of question papers.

### What is Equating Methodology?

Equating refers to adjusting the scores on different but comparable test papers so they can be compared.

- It enables scores from different versions of a test to be compared.
- This helps create an unbiased merit list and rank list of all students.
- VIT uses the Equi-percentile Equating method to derive students’ percentile ranks.
- Students with the same percentile in different question papers are deemed to have performed equally.
- A percentile rank is a student’s standing compared to all other candidates.
- For example, a score of the 90th percentile indicates that the candidate has scored higher than 90% of the candidates, and 10% have performed better.
- The percentile is thus a reliable measure of performance across the entire test-taker group.
- VITEEE, being an online test, uses standardised testing.
- Once the results are out, the scores are final, and no re-evaluation can be done.

## VITEEE 2026 Counselling & Seat Allotment

The dates for VITEEE counselling and seat allotment for the year 2026 have not been declared yet. The process takes place online, and there are five rounds that take place depending on the AIR of the student.
