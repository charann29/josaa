## Factors influencing Minimum Marks in JEE Main Qualify for JEE Advanced

Due to increasing competition, the JEE Mains 2026 minimum marks for the JEE Advanced vary each year. Here are some factors that directly or indirectly influence the IIT JEE Mains Cutoff 2026 for JEE Advanced:

- **Number of Applicants:** The more applicants there are, the tougher the competition becomes, raising the qualifying percentile and marks.
- **Difficulty of the Question Paper:** The easier it is, the higher the scores; and vice versa.
- **Normalization System Based on Percentile Method:** As the examination is conducted in two shifts, normalization of marks is done, and thus, the same marks can result in different percentiles.
- **Distribution of Student Marks:** If many students obtain marks in a narrow range, then even slight mark differences make a big difference in terms of percentile and rank position.
- **Reservation Percentiles in Categories:** As the reservation percentiles vary for each category, this impacts the cutoff marks.
- **Number of Seats in IITs:** The limited number of seats raises competition and hence influences cutoffs.
- **Cutoff Trends Over the Years:** Past trends have shown that cutoffs follow a trend, and an increasing trend implies tough competition.
- **Number of Examination Sessions:** With multiple sessions (January & April), one can improve their marks to boost cutoffs.
- **Tie-Breaking rule:** If the percentiles are the same, then subject-wise marks are compared and considered.

## IIT JEE Advanced 2026 Eligibility Criteria

IIT Roorkee has released the eligibility criteria for the JEE Advanced exam 2026. Apart from securing the Minimum Marks Required in JEE Mains 2026 to qualify for JEE Advanced, you are required to fulfill those eligibility criteria as well

| Criteria | Details |
| --- | --- |
| JEE Mains Performance | Candidates must rank within the top 2,50,000 in JEE Main 2026 across all categories (General, EWS, OBC-NCL, SC, ST, PwD) with the required qualifying percentile. |
| Age Limit | Candidates should be born on or after October 1, 2001. For SC, ST, and PwD candidates, the relaxed limit is October 1, 1996. |
| Number of Attempts | A candidate can attempt JEE Advanced a maximum of two times in two consecutive years. |
| Class XII Requirement | Candidates must have appeared for the Class 12 (or equivalent) exam for the first time in 2025 or 2026, with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as core subjects. |
| Minimum Aggregate Marks in Class 12 | Candidates must score at least 75% aggregate marks (65% for SC/ST/PwD) or be in the top 20th percentile of their respective boards. |
| Previous IIT Admission | Candidates who have already been admitted to an IIT are not eligible, even if they did not join. However, those who were allotted a seat but did not report or complete admission formalities may still be eligible. |

## JEE Main and JEE Advanced Counselling Process

The counselling and admission process for JEE Main and JEE Advanced is conducted through the centralized platform of the Joint Seat Allocation Authority, which manages seat allocation for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.

Following the announcement of the JEE Main results, students who meet the **qualifying JEE Mains cut off 2026** are eligible for admission to NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs, while those in the top 2.5 lakh ranks are eligible to take the JEE Advanced exam to secure seats at IITs. After declaring the JEE Advanced result, students must register with JoSAA for the seat allocation process.

### Stages of JoSAA Counselling

The Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) counselling method follows a stage-by-stage system to allocate seats in IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. Check the Step-by-Step JoSAA Counselling provided in the table below.

| Stages | Details |
| --- | --- |
| JoSAA Registration | Candidates must register on the Joint Seat Allocation Authority portal by filling in personal, academic, and exam details to participate in counselling. |
| Choice Filling & Locking | Candidates select preferred IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs along with courses, arrange them in priority order, and lock choices before the deadline. |
| Seat Allotment Rounds | JoSAA conducts multiple rounds where seats are allotted based on rank, category, preferences, and institute-wise opening & closing ranks, with chances for upgrades. |
| Seat Acceptance Options | Candidates choose Freeze (accept and exit), Float (wait for better option), or Slide (same institute, better branch) after seat allotment. |
| Document Verification & Fee Payment | Candidates upload required documents and pay the seat acceptance fee to confirm provisional admission and verify eligibility. |
| Final Admission | Candidates confirm their seat, report to the allotted institute, and complete all formalities to secure admission. |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What are the marks required in JEE Main 2026 to qualify for JEE Advanced?

The minimum marks required depend on the candidate’s category. For the General category, aspirants are expected to score around 120–140 marks or approximately the 93–95 percentile to qualify for JEE Advanced 2026.

### 2. How many students will qualify for JEE Advanced?

The top 2.5 lakh candidates from JEE Main, across all categories, will be eligible to appear for JEE Advanced 2026.

### 3. Is JEE Main sufficient for securing admission into IITs?

No, candidates must first qualify for JEE Main and then clear JEE Advanced to secure admission into IITs through the JoSAA counselling process.

### 4. Does the JEE Main cutoff differ every year?

Yes, the JEE Main cutoff changes every year depending on factors such as exam difficulty level, number of applicants, normalization process, and seat availability.

### 5. What should be the Class 12 percentage required to appear for JEE Advanced?

Students should have secured at least 75% aggregate marks in their Class 12 board examinations. SC, ST, and PwD candidates require a minimum of 65% marks or must be within the top 20 percentile of their respective boards.
