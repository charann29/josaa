## Subject-wise MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis

Based on the MHT CET Paper Analysis, we rated the subject-wise difficulty as Maths> Physics> Chemistry. Let’s now go through the subject-by-subject key topics that are asked in this shift.

### MHT CET Physics Analysis

The physics portion, according to the MHT CET 18 April Shift 1 Paper Analysis, is moderate and well-balanced, with an adequate balance between formula-based and concept-based questions. The percentage of numerical-based questions was about 60-70%.

**Key Topics of this section are as follows**

- Gravitation: Emphasis was laid upon the variation of g with height and depth, wherein questions related to the value of g two times the earth’s radius and up to heights of 300km
- Rotational Dynamics: The concept of Angular Momentum was covered, wherein angular momentum in terms of linear momentum, and I×α were discussed.
- Semiconductors: Topics covered include Logic Gates (based on circuits, Boolean expressions)
- Current Electricity: Current Sensitivity was the topic covered in this chapter

### MHTCET Chemistry Analysis

As per the MHT CET 18 April Paper Analysis, chemistry turned out to be the easiest section due to its easy and repetitive questions. Chemistry acted as a time-saving section, allowing students to allocate more time to Mathematics.

**Key Topics of this section are as follows**

- **Bio-molecules:** Important topics covered were sucrose formula, glycosidic bond, Classification & Hydrolysis of Carbohydrates like Glucose, Fructose, and raffinose.
- **Solutions & Colligative Properties:** Important topics included Types of Solutions (sea water is a solid-in-liquid type of solution) and Numericals of colligative properties and Raoult’s Law.
- **Chemical Kinetics:** Major focus area was First Order Reaction, involving Integrated Rate equations, Half-Life Periods, and Graph Interpretation.
- **Amines:** Main topics asked were the basicities of different amines (1°, 2°, 3°), Aromatic and aliphatic, and name reactions like Hofmann Bromamide Degradation and Hinsberg reagents.
- **P-Block Elements:** The most important topics were P-Block elements covering Groups 16, 17, and 18, Oxidized acids, and Amphoteric oxides like Al 2O 3.
- **D & F Block:** Main emphasis was on Colorful Ions due to DD Transitions & Unpaired Electrons in d-orbital.

### MHT CET Maths Analysis

The Mathematics section of April 18 Shift 1 was the most time-consuming aspect of the paper. While the majority of the problems were based on common formulas and familiar principles, they needed many steps, calculations, and careful examination, resulting in a longer time per question.

**Key Topics of this section are as follows**

- **Integration:** Definite integrations with examples, including the substitution method where tan (x/2) = t, Integration by Parts, and the temporary substitution of x
- **Differentiation:** The logarithmic differentiation method, derivatives (second order), and inverse trigonometric functions
- **Trigonometry:** Inverse Trigonometric Functions, the ranges of these functions, especially for cos−1 and sin−1, and trigonometric formulae such as sin3θ
- **Conics (Ellipse):** Calculating values through the condition of tangency c2 = a2m2 + b2
- **Probability:** Questions on Binomial Distribution with examples, such as the probability of obtaining exactly x successes from n trials
- **Logic:** Determining the negation of implication statements
