## JEE Main Paper Analysis Jan 22 Shift 1

The JEE Main exam analysis 2026 Jan 22 Shift 1 suggested that the overall paper was moderate. As per the JEE Main Students Reaction shift 1, they feel this was the simplest and easiest when compared to the previous shifts.

Coming to the JEE Main 2025 exam difficulty level analysis, Chemistry and Physics were rated as easy and moderate, respectively.  JEE Main Jan 22 Shift 1 Mathematics section was doable and easy compared to physics.

### JEE Main Analysis 2025 Jan 22 Shift 1 for Mathematics

Many students have felt that the JEE Mains Mathematics paper was ‘doable’ and moderate, with some questions being lengthier or calculative rather than conceptually difficult. However, coordinate geometry questions were standard that rely on the basic formulas like eccentricity and latus rectum; it was suggested that students weak in geometry should at least study the formulas. Moreover, questions on relations used direct formulas rather than requiring manual checking of properties. Some problems, particularly in Sequence & Series and Binomial Theorem, were noted as calculative.

### JEE Mains Paper Analysis Shift 1 for Physics Section

According to many students’ feedback, the paper was considered balanced and easy to moderate, with questions often being formula-based. However, there was a significant weightage on standard topics like Logic Gates, Rotation (Moment of Inertia), and Current Electricity, which appear in almost every shift. Moreover, there are many questions regarding instruments (Venturi meter, Microscope) and practical topics (Errors, Optical path shift) were prominent.

### JEE Main Paper Analysis Jan 22 2026 Shift 1 for Chemistry Section

Most of the students were happy as the paper was NCERT-based, particularly for Organic reactions. As per the students’ report, questions from Physical Chemistry were standard, while some questions were calculation-intensive (e.g., combustion analysis and practical organic chemistry using Barium Sulphate for Sulphur estimation). Also from the Inorganic Chemistry, questions were very direct and taught repeatedly in classes, such as the ‘Butterfly structure’ of chromium peroxide and specific hybridisation cases like [Ni(CN)4 ]2− vs [Ni(CO)4 ]. Moreover, practical Organic Chemistry (POC) appeared in the form of the Carius method, linking mole concepts with practical estimation.

## Official NTA Guidelines for JEE Mains 2026 Session 1 Exams

All the students who are now going to appear in the Shift 2 of the January 22 exam should carefully go through the crucial NTA guidelines mentioned hereunder.

- **Unlock Aadhaar Biometrics:** Candidates who intend to present their Aadhaar for entrance must ensure that their biometrics are unlocked before arriving at the facility.
- **Appear at Exam Centre 1 hour before:** Candidates who did not register using Aadhaar must arrive early on the exam day to have their biometrics recorded at the exam centre.
- **Dress Code:** Although there is no official dress code for JEE Mains, the NTA advises against wearing shoes with heavy soles or clothing with many buttons. Sikh candidates may carry religious or customary artefacts, such as a Kara or Kirpan.
- Things to Carry: Students were only allowed to bring a translucent, simple ballpoint pen and a clear water bottle. Diabetic patients can bring their food and medicines to the exam hall
- **Prohibited items:** Electronic devices, pencil cases, and even some types of jewellery were restricted.
