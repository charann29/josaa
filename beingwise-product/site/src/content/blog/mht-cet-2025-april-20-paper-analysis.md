## MHT CET PCM Exam Analysis: Student Reviews For April 20th Shift 1 and Shift 2

According to student reviews from the MHT CET 2025, April 20th – Shift 1 & Shift 2, here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

## **Shift 1**

### Physics:

Students found the Physics paper moderately difficult, with a relatively equal distribution of numerical and conceptual questions. Many topics were covered, mainly Modern Physics, Rotational Dynamics, and Current Electricity, and certain questions were a bit lengthy with all the steps involved.

### **Chemistry:**

Students found Chemistry to be the easiest of the three subjects. This section was mainly theory, completely based on topics from the NCERT and HSC books. Organic and Inorganic Chemistry were important topics here as well.

### **Mathematics:**

The hardest section of the three. There were many long and complicated calculations involved, especially in the topics of Calculus, Integration, and Probability. Questions were also application-based, so students needed to speed up, with the clarity of concepts was essential to complete the paper on time.

## **Shift 2**

### **Physics:**

In Shift 2, Students found the Physics paper to be at a moderate to difficult level, the same as the first shift. Mark distribution was very large for Mechanics, Optics, and Thermodynamics. There were somewhat complex questions that required a lot of critical thinking and steps to get to the final answer.

### **Chemistry:**

Fairly easy again, with many theory questions in Organic and Inorganic Chemistry comments. Very few straightforward questions for Physical Chemistry.

### **Mathematics:**

For the Maths section, unlike shift 1, it was moderately difficult. However, there were a few questions that even the best students have stumped and required strong reading skills and high-level problem-solving. Topics such as Vectors, 3D Geometry, and Matrices were common.

## Conclusion

The MHT CET 2025 exam, held on April 20 for Shift 1 and Shift 2, had an overall moderate difficulty level, similar to previous years. Students found a well-balanced distribution in the questions across the subjects of Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, with a few shifts showing a slight tendency towards more conceptual and formula-based questions.

Physics focused on strongly conceptually based questions, with Mechanics and Modern Physics having the majority of the weightage. Chemistry was more organically and physically based, with less emphasis on Inorganic. Mathematics also relied heavily on Calculus, Integration, and 3D Geometry, challenging students with lengthy and calculation-based problems.

Overall, the paper itself sought to test speed but also accuracy. Students who were more firm with their fundamentals and consistently did the previous year’s papers were able to take advantage and show their knowledge.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ******What was the overall level of difficulty in the April 20th MHT CET 2025 paper?******

The overall level of difficulty was moderate in both shifts. Maths was time-consuming, Chemistry was easier and mostly NCERT-based, and Physics had a fair mix of conceptual and numerical-based questions.

### 2. **Which subject was the hardest in both MHT CET 2025 shifts?**

During the 20th April MHT CET shifts, most students felt Maths was the hardest because of the lengthy, time-consuming calculations and the application-based based tricky questions.

### 3. ******Were there questions in the MHT CET 2025 paper from the NCERT?******

Yes, in Chemistry, many questions came directly from the NCERT book or were based more on the NCERT book, especially from Class 12.

### 4. ******Which chapters had the most weightage in Physics in MHT CET?******

In Physics, the most weightage subjects were Mechanics, Modern Physics, and Current Electricity. Thermodynamics and Optics had comparatively fewer questions.

### 5. **Did the difficulty change between MHT CET Shift 1 and Shift 2?**

The overall pattern was very similar between shifts, but students reported that Mathematics was a little more difficult and tricky in Shift 2 as compared to Shift 1 for all subjects. Shift 1 had more balanced, easy, and hard questions in each subject.
