## Conclusion

The MHT CET 2025 exam, conducted from April 19th to 27th across multiple shifts, maintained a consistent pattern of moderate overall difficulty with specific nuances among the three subjects. Students found Mathematics to be the most challenging and time-consuming section, primarily due to lengthy, calculation-intensive problems in Integration, 3D Geometry, and Vectors. Physics was generally rated as moderately difficult, emphasizing application-based concepts such as Rotational Dynamics, Current Electricity, and Capacitors, blending both numerical and conceptual questions. Chemistry was the easiest and most scoring section, with many direct questions sourced from the Maharashtra HSC textbooks, focusing on Redox Reactions, IUPAC Nomenclature, and Coordination Compounds.

Overall, MHT CET 2025 demanded strong conceptual clarity, problem-solving skills, and time management, especially in Mathematics and Physics, while Chemistry offered relatively straightforward scoring opportunities. Candidates should prioritize the highlighted core topics across subjects for effective preparation and performance in future sessions.
