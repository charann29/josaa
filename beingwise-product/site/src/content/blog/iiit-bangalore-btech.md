The **International Institute of Information Technology Bangalore**, commonly known as **IIIT Bangalore**, is a deemed university located at Electronics City, Bengaluru. It was established in 1998 and is supported jointly by the Government of Karnataka and the IT industry. IIIT Bangalore is a registered not-for-profit society that focuses on technology education, research, and entrepreneurship.

IIIT Bangalore offers a 4-year B.Tech programme in three branches. Admissions are NOT done through JoSAA. This is one of the most important things to know before applying. Students must apply through the IIIT Bangalore admissions portal at [admissions.iiitb.net](http://admissions.iiitb.net). This article covers everything about IIIT Bangalore B.Tech 2026, i.e., courses, seats, eligibility, admission process, cutoff, fees, scholarships, placements, campus, and rankings.

## IIIT Bangalore B.Tech Admission 2026

IIIT Bangalore does NOT admit B.Tech students through JoSAA. The institute runs its own admission process through its admissions portal. Admission is based on the **Best JEE Rank (BJR)**.

### What is Best JEE Rank (BJR)?

IIIT Bangalore computes a rank called Best JEE Rank by taking the better of a candidate’s JEE Main CRL and JEE Advanced CRL. For example, if a candidate has JEE Main CRL 12000 and JEE Advanced CRL 11000, the BJR is taken as 11000. Seat allocation is done in order of BJR based on the candidate’s stated programme preferences.

### IIITB: Types of Admission Rounds

IIIT Bangalore admission is conducted in two types of rounds. For more details, you can refer to the table below:

| Round Type | How It Works |
| --- | --- |
| **Off-Campus Round** | Offer letter sent by email. Candidate accepts by paying fees online. No campus visit needed. |
| **Live Online Round** | Conducted via online meeting. All applicants can participate. Programme preferences entered at application time are NOT considered in this round. Seat is allocated purely by BJR. |

### How Seat Allocation Works

Candidates enter up to 5 programme preferences at the time of application. Seats are allocated starting from Preference 1. If Preference 1 is full, Preference 2 is checked. This continues up to Preference 5.

#### IIIT Bangalore B.Tech Admission Seat Allocation: Important rules to know

- Once a candidate gets an offer at any preference and accepts it, they are eligible for upgrade to higher preferences in future rounds only. They become ineligible for lower preferences in all subsequent rounds.
- If a candidate declines an offer, they lose eligibility for that preference and all lower preferences. They remain eligible for higher preferences only.
- If a candidate does not receive an offer in a round, they remain eligible for all their preferences in the next round.

**Seat Allocation Scenarios (from official IIIT Bangalore document):**

| Scenario | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| **Gets Preference 1, Accepts** | Ineligible for Preferences 2 to 5 in future rounds |
| **Gets Preference 1, Declines** | Ineligible for ALL preferences (1 to 5) in future rounds |
| **Gets Preference 2 (Pref 1 full), Accepts** | Eligible for upgrade to Preference 1 in next round. Ineligible for Preferences 3 to 5 |
| **Gets Preference 2 (Pref 1 full), Declines** | Eligible for upgrade to Preference 1 only. Ineligible for Preferences 2 to 5 |

### IIIT Bangalore Internal Rank (IIR)

After the application window closes, IIIT Bangalore assigns each applicant an internal rank (IIR) based on their position among all applicants. This is published alongside cut-off announcements. It helps candidates estimate how many applicants lie between their rank and the cut-off.

### Special Allocation: Olympiad Seats

Up to 5 seats across all programmes are reserved for candidates selected for the International Mathematical Olympiad Training Camp (IMOTC) or the International Olympiad in Informatics Training Camp (IOITC). If more than 5 eligible candidates apply, seats are allocated in order of BJR.

## IIIT Bangalore Scholarships

IIIT Bangalore offers multiple scholarship schemes for B.Tech students. Students do not need to separately apply for most of these.

**Utkarsha Scholarship 2026**

This scholarship offers a 100% tuition fee waiver for top-ranked JEE candidates joining IIIT Bangalore. Candidates with JEE Main or JEE Advanced NTA CRL Rank between 1 and 999 are eligible. For B.Tech students, the scholarship covers 100% of tuition fees for the first three years.

**Dean’s Merit List Scholarship**

All enrolled students are automatically considered. Selection is based on CGPA order of merit. Students need a CGPA of 3.6 or above to qualify for the Dean’s List. The top 10% of students in each programme receive this scholarship. It is awarded annually by the office of Deans.

**Prerna Scholarship for Girl Students (2026-27)**

This scholarship supports girl students admitted to the B.Tech and Integrated M.Tech programmes at IIIT Bangalore. The total number of Prerna scholarships is limited to 10% of the batch intake per programme.

| Programme | Batch Size (2026-27) | No. of Prerna Scholarships |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CSE (B.Tech + Integrated M.Tech) | 180 | 18 |
| ECE (B.Tech + Integrated M.Tech) | 90 | 9 |
| AI & DS (B.Tech) | 90 | 9 |
| **Total** | — | **36** |

**Corporate Scholarships**

IIIT Bangalore facilitates scholarships from companies including Reliance Foundation, Tata Foundation, Micron, Intel, HGS, and CGI. These are announced by the companies from time to time.

**Student Benevolent Fund (SBF)**

This is a need-based scholarship. Students whose parents’ or guardians’ annual income is below ₹11 lakh and who cannot secure a student loan are eligible. An income certificate must be submitted. SBF covers education, boarding, health, and other essential needs not met by other scholarships.

## IIIT Bangalore Hostel

IIIT Bangalore has a fully residential campus. Hostel accommodation is mandatory for students enrolled in residential programmes including B.Tech.

There are three hostels on campus. **Lilavati** is the girls’ hostel. **Bhaskara** and **Visveshwaya** are the boys’ hostels. Together they accommodate 850+ students across 700+ rooms.

Room allocation follows a year-wise pattern. First-year students are allotted triple-sharing rooms. From third year, boys get single rooms in Visveshwaya (which has attached bathrooms in triple-sharing rooms). Girls have single and double-sharing rooms from first year onwards in Lilavati hostel, which has 179+ rooms.

**Hostel Facilities:** Fully furnished rooms (bed, table, chair, wardrobe), Wi-Fi, 24×7 security, laundry, RO water, common rooms, separate gymnasiums (Calorie Bee for girls, Shape Up for boys), indoor games area, electricity backup.

**Mess:** The campus mess serves 4 meals a day — breakfast, lunch, evening snacks, and dinner. Non-vegetarian meals are available three times a week. Jain food is also available. The menu is curated monthly by the student food committee.

## Is IIIT Bangalore Government or Private?

IIIT Bangalore is neither a purely government college nor a private college. It is a **deemed university** with not-for-profit status. It is jointly supported by the Government of Karnataka and the IT industry. The institute holds UGC 12B status, which makes it eligible for central government research funding. However, it is not a centrally funded institution like an IIT or NIT.
