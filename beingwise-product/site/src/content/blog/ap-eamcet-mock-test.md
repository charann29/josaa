AP EAPCET (Andhra Pradesh Engineering, Agriculture, and Pharmacy Common Entrance Test) is a state-level entrance examination conducted by the Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, (JNTU), Kakinada. The AP EAMCET 2025 exam for the **Engineering stream** will be conducted from **18.05.2025 to 23.05.2025** and the **Agriculture & Pharmacy Stream** examination will be held from **16.05.2025 to 17.05.2025.**

### Syllabus Coverage in AP EAMCET Mock Test

 AP EAMCET mock test is based on the official Intermediate (10+2) syllabus prescribed by the Andhra Pradesh Board. These mock tests cover all chapters and important topics so that students can practice better to perform better in the exam.

#### Mathematics Syllabus

The AP EAMCET mock test includes important units and chapters from both Class 11 and Class 12 Mathematics. Topics covered include Algebra, Trigonometry, Vector Algebra, Probability, Calculus, Coordinate Geometry and other relevant topics as per the AP Board Intermediate curriculum.

#### Physics Syllabus

Physics mock tests are designed to include conceptual, numerical, and application-based questions from the prescribed syllabus. Key topics include Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Waves and Sound, Optics, Electricity and Magnetism, Modern Physics and other important units.

#### Chemistry Syllabus Coverage

Chemistry mock tests cover Physical, Organic, and Inorganic Chemistry topics to ensure balanced preparation. Important areas include Atomic Structure, Chemical Bonding, States of Matter, Thermodynamics, Chemical Equilibrium, Electrochemistry, Organic Chemistry and more.

#### Biology Syllabus Coverage (For Agriculture and Pharmacy Students)

Biology mock tests ensure complete practice of both Botany and Zoology sections. Major topics include Diversity of Living World, Cell Structure and Function, Plant Physiology, Human Anatomy, Genetics, Biotechnology and other essential chapters.

## AP EAMCET 2025 Eligibility Criteria

Before applying for AP EAMCET 2025, candidates must fulfil the eligibility criteria. These include nationality, domicile, age limits, and educational qualifications.

#### Nationality

Candidates must be Indian Nationals or Persons of Indian Origin (PIO) or Overseas Citizens of India (OCI).

#### Domicile Requirement

Candidates must belong to Andhra Pradesh or Telangana and should satisfy local or non-local status requirements as per the state government rules.

#### Age Criteria

Different age requirements apply depending on the course category. The table below explains the minimum and maximum age limits.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Course Category** | **Minimum Age (as of Dec 31, 2025)** | **Maximum Age** |
| Engineering and Pharmacy | 16 years | No upper limit |
| Agriculture and Certain Engineering Courses | 17 years | 22 years (General), 25 years (SC/ST) |

#### Educational Qualification

Candidates must have passed or appeared in the Intermediate (10+2) examination. They must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics for Engineering courses, and Physics, Chemistry, and Biology for Agriculture and Pharmacy courses. A minimum of 45% aggregate marks is required for General category candidates and 40% for SC, ST, and BC candidates.
