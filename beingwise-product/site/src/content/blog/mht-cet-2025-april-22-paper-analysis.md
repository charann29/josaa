## MHT CET Counselling 2025

MHT CET counselling process 2025 is conducted online. Below are the steps candidates need to follow for MHT CET 2025 counselling:

- **Registration:** Enter your personal, contact, and security details on the MHT CET official website to start the registration process. Once these details have been entered, you will need to create a password. After setting the password, fill in the other required fields. After that, applicants should submit their qualifications, including their overall and subject-specific grades from grades 10 and 12, along with the names of the relevant boards.

- **Uploading Scanned Documents:** In the second step of MHT CET counselling, candidates are required to upload the given documents:
  - 10th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - 12th Standard Pass Certificate & Mark Sheet
  - MHT CET Scorecard 2025
  - Migration Certificate
  - Category Certificate (if applicable)
  - Character Certificate
  - Domicile Certificate (if applicable)
  - Print out the MHT CET Counselling registration Form

- **Payment of Counselling Fees**– Candidates are required to pay the counselling fees online, depending on their category:-

| --- | --- | --- |
| **Courses** | **Registration Fee for JEE Main Applicants (All India Quota and Institutional-Level Quota)** | **Registration Fee for MHT CET Qualified Candidates** |

- **Merit List Release:** The committee will make the MHT CET merit list public. The Class 12 and CET scores will be used to determine the marks in a 50:50 ratio. You can submit objections on the website if you find any inconsistencies in the merit list.

- **Choice Filling:** In this step, you must choose your college as well as your preferred branch and specialisations. Seats will be distributed by the committee based on the merit list ranking.

- **Seat Allotment:** Seats will be distributed according to the selections made and the merit positions of the candidates. Candidates must pay the admission fee and show up at the designated college to confirm their seat.

### **MHT CET PCM:** **Shift 1**

**Overall Difficulty Level** **of MHT CET paper pattern PCM****: Moderate to Tough**

**Physics** – Students rated Physics rated tough to moderate. Multiple conceptual as well as numerical questions are in this section. Topics such as Mechanics and Optics were prominent. Some of the numericals, especially from Heat and Thermodynamics, were quite lengthy and needed a lot of time.

**Chemistry**: Followed a similar theme as in Shift 1, with primarily textbook-themed theory questions. Students found the questions were largely straightforward and required a fairly good recall of the standard ideas associated with these topics.

## Conclusion

The MHT CET 2025 exam that happened on April 22 for the PCM group had specific challenges during both shifts. Physics had a medium level of difficulty, with a good variety of theoretical and numerical questions, focusing on Modern Physics and Rotational Dynamics.

MHT CET paper pattern of Chemistry in both shifts was fairly easy for the majority of students, which is great news, since the vast majority of questions were based on NCERT material, covering topics such as States of Matter, Redox Reactions, and Organic Chemistry. Chemistry was the easiest and highest-scoring of all sections.

MHT CET question paper of Mathematics was potentially the hardest section for both shifts, primarily because both shifts had long, sentence-based, and application-type questions, especially for Vectors, 3D Geometry, and Matrices. Candidates stated that time management was important, as these questions needed a large number of calculations and lots of problem-solving strategies.

Overall, the MHT CET 2025 April 22 paper had a good mix of easy, moderate, and hard questions. Chemistry was easy for the majority of students, while Physics and Mathematics both tested students on application and conceptual knowledge.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. **Which was the easiest subject in both MHT CET shifts?**

The Chemistry section was the easiest in both shifts, with theory-based questions being largely contained in the NCERT syllabus for Class XII. Students found Chemistry to be more intuitive than Physics and Mathematics.

### 2. ******Which topics were covered in the Physics section in MHT CET 2025?******

The Physics section covered specific topics from the syllabus, such as Modern Physics, Rotational Dynamics, Current Electricity, Capacitors, and Resistors with Tolerance. The shift 1 paper had a moderate proportion of theory questions, while the shift 2 paper was numerically heavy.

### 3. **********How difficult was the Mathematics section in MHT CET 2025?**********

The MHT CET question paper of the Mathematics section was the toughest, with a lot of application-based questions and questions from different chapters like Vectors, 3D Geometry, and Matrices.

### 4. **********Was the MHT CET 2025 exam time-consuming?**********

Yes, especially on the Mathematics section, which had long questions that forced the candidate to manage their time prudently. Candidates felt that they could not finish all the questions in time, particularly in shift 2.

### 5. ********Was the difficulty level between MHT CET 2025 shift 1 and shift 2 the same?********

The basic structure of the exam was relatively the same between shift 1 and shift 2 regarding questions. MHT CET question paper Shift 2 was marginally more difficult, especially in Physics due to the proportion of numerical problems, and the Mathematics questions were relatively tougher.
