## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What is the difference between NIAT’s upskilling program and a regular university degree program?

NIAT’s upskilling program focuses on industry-ready skills through training modules, project-based learning, and structured skill maps. The university awards the degree, while NIAT enhances practical knowledge and job readiness beyond traditional academic learning.

### 2. What is an IRC, and how is it different from a university degree?

An IRC (Industry Ready Certificate) is a skill-based certification awarded by NxtWave. It validates practical skills and achievements but is not equivalent to a university degree or diploma, which is awarded separately by the university.

### 3. Are NIAT’s trainers the same as the university’s teaching faculty?

No. NIAT’s trainers, mentors, and success coaches are separate from university faculty and focus solely on industry-aligned skill development.

### 4. What kind of content does NIAT cover in its upskilling program?

The program includes industry-aligned learning modules, training modules, project-based learning, and a structured skill map, updated regularly based on inputs from leading companies.

### 5. If I enrol with NIAT, does that confirm my seat at the collaborating university?

No. Enrolling in NIAT does not guarantee university admission. Each university has its own admission process and eligibility criteria, and admission is granted solely at the university’s discretion.

### 6. Can I visit the university space where NIAT conducts its upskilling program before enrolling?

Yes, students can book a free one-day visit through the official NIAT website to explore the training environment before enrolling.

### 7. What fees does NIAT charge, and what does it cover?

NIAT charges an upskilling fee for its training program. This does not include university tuition or admission fees, which are handled separately by the university. The total cost may range between ₹8–18 lakhs for 4 years depending on the program and institution.
