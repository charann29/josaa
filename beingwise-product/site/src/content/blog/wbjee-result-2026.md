## What Is on the WBJEE 2026 Rank Card?

The WBJEE Result 2026 will not come as a pass or fail certificate. The scorecard will display subject-wise marks, total marks, ranks, and other key details. Here is what the rank card contains:

- Candidate’s name and photograph
- Application number and roll number
- Date of birth
- Subject-wise marks: Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry
- Total marks secured
- General Merit Rank (GMR)

No physical copy of the rank card will be sent by post. The rank card is valid only for the 2026–27 academic session. Any discrepancy in marks or personal details should be reported immediately to WBJEEB.

## WBJEE 2026 Merit List: GMR and PMR Explained

The WBJEE Result 2026 comes with two separate merit lists. Understanding the difference matters because they determine which courses and colleges you are eligible for.

### General Merit Rank (GMR)

WBJEEB issues the GMR based on the total scores obtained in Paper I (Mathematics) and Paper II (Physics and Chemistry) together. Admission to all engineering, technology, architecture, and pharmacy courses at Jadavpur University will be on the basis of GMR.

Separate reserved category merit positions are also indicated for SC, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B, PwD, and TFW categories. However, GMR is what applies for competitive seat allotment and counselling.

### Pharmacy Merit Rank (PMR)

The PMR is prepared based on students’ scores in Paper II (Physics and Chemistry) only. It applies to admission to all degree-level pharmacy courses in enlisted pharmacy colleges and universities, except the pharmacy course at Jadavpur University, where GMR is used instead.

Candidates appearing in both Paper I and Paper II will receive both a GMR and a PMR if they score above the cutoff. Candidates appearing only in Paper II will receive a PMR only. Candidates who appear only in Paper I will not receive any rank.

## WBJEE 2026 Tie-Breaking Rules

When two or more candidates score the same total marks, WBJEEB applies a specific tie-breaking sequence to assign unique merit ranks.

**Tie-breaking rules for GMR (in order of priority):**

- The candidate with fewer negative marks in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry combined is ranked higher.
- If the tie persists, more positive marks in Mathematics and Physics together give preference.
- If still tied, more positive marks in Mathematics and Chemistry together are considered.
- Then, fewer negative marks in Mathematics and Physics together.
- Then, fewer negative marks in Mathematics and Chemistry together.
- Then, more positive marks in Mathematics for only the 2-mark questions.
- Then, more positive marks in Physics for only the 2-mark questions.

If the tie still exists after all subject-based criteria, the older candidate is given preference based on date of birth. If even that does not resolve it, the application number in ascending order is the final decider, meaning the candidate who applied earlier gets the higher rank.

## What to Do After the WBJEE Result 2026 Is Declared?

Once you have your rank, the next steps move quickly. Here is what to focus on immediately after the WBJEE Result 2026 comes out:

- **Check your rank card carefully:** Verify your name, application number, marks, and rank. If there is any error, contact WBJEEB without delay. No requests for score review are entertained after the deadline.
- **Estimate your college options:** Use your GMR or PMR to check previous year cutoffs for colleges you are interested in. For CSE programmes in the open category at top colleges like Jadavpur University, a rank in the range of 1 to 150 has been the expected requirement based on 2025 data.
- **Start document collection:** Gather your 10th and 12th marksheets, admit card, domicile certificate, caste certificate if applicable, and identity proof for the counselling process.
- **Register for counselling:** The WBJEE counselling 2026 registration is expected to begin in August 2026. Missing the registration window means missing seat allotment for that round.

## WBJEE 2026 Counselling: A Brief Overview

The WBJEE Result 2026 is the entry point to the counselling process. Qualified candidates go through a structured sequence of steps before a seat is finally confirmed. Here is how it works:

### Step 1: Register for Counselling

Visit the official WBJEEB portal and register for counselling by logging in with your roll number and password. Make sure all details submitted are correct, as they cannot be edited after submission. There is only one registration window for all rounds, so late entries are not entertained under any circumstances.

### Step 2: Pay the Counselling Fee

Pay the counselling fee online through net banking, debit card, or credit card. Keep the payment confirmation handy as proof of registration.

### Step 3: Fill in Your College and Course Preferences

Choose your preferred courses and colleges in order of preference. Selection must be done carefully and in the order you want, since seat allotment will be based on your rank and the preferences you submit.

### Step 4: Seat Allotment

The seat allotment result will be released on the official website. Seats will be assigned based on your WBJEE 2026 rank, your category, and the college and course preferences you filled in.

### Step 5: Accept Your Seat and Pay Seat Acceptance Fee

After seats are allotted, you will need to pay a seat acceptance fee of ₹5,000 online to download your allotment letter and confirm your seat.

### Step 6: Upgradation Round (Round 2)

WBJEEB is expected to conduct two rounds of counselling. Candidates who are not satisfied with their Round 1 allotment can opt for upgradation in the second round, where a better seat may be available depending on vacancies and rank.

### Step 7: Mop-Up Round

A mop-up round is typically held after the main rounds to fill seats that remain vacant. This is the final opportunity for candidates who did not secure a seat in the earlier rounds.

### Step 8: Document Verification

After seat allotment is confirmed, report to the allotted institution with your original documents. Required documents include your 10th and 12th marksheets, 10th admit card, domicile certificate, and caste certificate if applicable.
