JoSAA registration and choice filling is the single most important week of your admission season. This is the stage where your ranks turn into an actual seat, and a small mistake here, such as a wrong order or a missed lock, can cost you a college you were eligible for. This BeingWise guide walks you through the whole thing in plain English so you and your parents know exactly what to do on josaa.nic.in.

If you want the big-picture map of the entire process first, start with our [complete JoSAA counselling 2026 guide](/blog/josaa-counselling-2026-complete-guide), then come back here for the hands-on steps.

## Who has to register

If you want a seat in the IITs, NITs, IIITs, or other Government Funded Technical Institutes (GFTIs) through the common counselling system, you must register on JoSAA. In short:

- Qualified JEE Advanced and want an IIT seat: you must register, and you can also list NIT and IIIT choices.
- Qualified JEE Main only: you register for NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.
- Did not qualify or do not want any of these institutes: you do not need to register.

Registering does not lock you into anything. It simply makes you a participant in the rounds. You only commit money and intent later, when you accept an allotted seat.

## What to keep ready before you start

Sort these out before you sit down at the portal. It saves you from scrambling mid-session, especially with the timeouts we describe later.

- Your JEE Main application number and password.
- Your JEE Advanced registration number and password, if you qualified.
- Your roll number, date of birth, and category details exactly as in your JEE records.
- A working mobile number and email for OTPs and notifications.
- Your category and any special-category certificate details (PwD, etc.), since these affect eligibility.
- A clear, written list of the colleges and branches you actually want, in priority order.

That last point matters most. Do not decide your order live on the portal. The order of your choices decides your seat, so plan it on paper or a spreadsheet first. Our [choice-filling order guide](/blog/josaa-choice-filling-order-2026) explains how to sequence dream, realistic, and safe options so you never end up "stuck" with a low choice you ranked too high.

## The registration flow on josaa.nic.in

Here is the typical step-by-step registration flow. The exact screen labels can change slightly year to year, so always confirm the live wording and the schedule on josaa.nic.in.

1. Go to josaa.nic.in and open the candidate login for the current cycle.
2. Log in using your JEE Main credentials (and JEE Advanced credentials if prompted).
3. Read the instructions page carefully and accept the declaration to proceed.
4. Verify your personal details: name, date of birth, category, state of eligibility, and contact details. Confirm these are correct before moving on.
5. Verify your academic and exam details, including your ranks as pulled from JEE records. You are confirming, not re-entering, this data.
6. Set or confirm a choice-filling password if the system asks for one, and note it down safely.
7. Move to the choice-filling section once your profile is confirmed.

There is no separate registration payment at this stage. Registration is free.

## How choice filling works

Once your profile is confirmed, you reach the choice-filling section. This is where you build your priority list of institute-and-branch combinations. There are three things you do here, and you repeat them until you are happy.

### 1. Add your choices

The portal shows you the institutes and branches you are eligible for. You add the ones you want to your list. Add generously at first, then trim. A common beginner mistake is adding too few choices; if your high choices do not come through, more options below them keep you in the running.

### 2. Arrange and reorder

After adding, you arrange your choices from most wanted at the top to least wanted at the bottom. This is the heart of the whole exercise. JoSAA always tries to give you the highest choice you are eligible for. So:

- Put what you genuinely want most at number 1, even if it feels like a stretch.
- Never put a "safe" college above a more-preferred one just because you fear missing out. If a higher choice is reachable, you will get it, and a wrongly placed safe option can block a better seat.
- Keep realistic and safe choices lower down as backups.

Because ordering decides everything, do not improvise this on the portal. Bring your planned order with you. The [choice-filling order guide](/blog/josaa-choice-filling-order-2026) goes deep on building this list well.

### 3. Save

Save your choices frequently. Saving is not the same as locking. Saved choices can still be edited; locked choices cannot. You can add, remove, and reorder as many times as you want while the window is open, as long as you save before your session ends.

## Use mock allotment, then lock

Before you lock, JoSAA usually releases one or more mock seat allotments during the choice-filling window. A mock allotment takes your currently saved choices and your rank and shows what seat you would likely get if the round closed right then.

Treat the mock result as a free dry run, not a promise. Use it to:

- Check whether your top choices are realistic at your rank.
- Spot a wrongly ordered list, for example a backup sitting too high.
- Decide whether to add more choices lower down for safety.

After each mock, refine your order and save again. Our [mock seat allotment explainer](/blog/josaa-mock-seat-allotment-explained) shows how to read mock results without panicking or overreacting to them.

Only when you are confident in your order should you lock.

## Portal tips that save your seat

The JoSAA portal is reliable but strict. These habits prevent the small errors that cause big regrets.

> Portal survival box
>
> - One active session only. Do not log in from two browsers or devices at once; it can log you out or cause errors.
> - Expect roughly 20-minute timeouts. If you stay idle, the portal logs you out, and unsaved work can be lost.
> - Save frequently. Hit save after every few changes, not just at the end.
> - Fill in chunks. Add and order a batch, save, then continue. Do not try to do everything in one marathon session.
> - Never share your OTP or password. BeingWise, JoSAA, and no genuine helper will ever ask for them. Anyone who does is trying to scam you.
> - Use a stable internet connection and avoid filling at the very last minute when servers are busiest.

## Locking, and what happens after

Locking is the final confirmation of your choices for the round. Once you lock:

- Your choice list becomes final for that round and cannot be edited.
- The system runs the allotment based on your locked order and your rank.
- You receive a seat at the highest choice you are eligible for, or no seat that round if none of your choices are reachable.

If you do not lock manually before the deadline, the portal usually auto-locks your last saved choices when the window closes. Locking yourself is safer because you confirm exactly what you intended.

After allotment is published, you decide what to do with the seat you get. Accepting a seat is the point where you pay the Seat Acceptance Fee, which is approximately Rs 30,000 for general candidates and approximately Rs 15,000 for reserved categories. Confirm the current figure on josaa.nic.in before paying. You then report and upload documents as required. Get this ready early using our [documents and reporting checklist](/blog/josaa-documents-reporting-checklist) so you are not chasing paperwork at the last moment.

From there you may have options like Freeze, Float, or Slide across rounds, which decide whether you keep the current seat or try to upgrade. Those are covered in the complete guide.

## A simple, calm sequence to follow

If you remember nothing else, follow this:

1. Plan your ordered choice list on paper before logging in.
2. Register and confirm your details on josaa.nic.in.
3. Add choices generously, then arrange them strictly by genuine preference.
4. Save often, work in chunks, and respect the timeout.
5. Use each mock allotment to refine, then save again.
6. Lock yourself before the deadline.
7. Only later, when you accept a seat, do you pay the SAF.
