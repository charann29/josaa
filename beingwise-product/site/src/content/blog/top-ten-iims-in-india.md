## What are the Eligibility Criteria for IIMs in India?

Candidates aspiring for admission into the top IIMs in India have to understand the eligibility criteria of the IIMs. Understanding the admission criteria can ensure students fulfil all the requirements and are well prepared for admission in the top IIMs. Check the eligibility criteria for IIMs in India below:

IIMs in India follow a three-stage admission process. In stage 1, candidates have to appear for an entrance exam, i.e. **Common Admission Test(CAT),** where candidates are tested on different sections involving aptitude, data interpretation, etc. Upon qualifying for the entrance exam, candidates will be called for Stage 2. In Stage 2, IIMs calculate the aggregate of the candidate considering several criteria such as their academic background in Class X, XII, Bachelors, etc. CAT Score, work experience, gender diversity, etc, and call for the **Written Ability Test and Personal Interview.** Based on the above criteria, in Stage 3, candidates with an aggregate in the 80th percentile are offered admission.

**IIM Qualification Factors:**

Candidates have to fulfil a few more qualifications to be chosen. Have a look at the admission criteria for admissions:

- Candidates must have completed their graduation with a minimum aggregate of 50%(General Category) and 45%(reserved category) from a recognised board.
- Candidates who are pursuing their final year are also eligible to apply.
- Must score above the IIMS CAT cut off.

**Note: IIMs Cutoff and admission criteria might slightly vary, and candidates are advised to visit the official websites for precise information.**
