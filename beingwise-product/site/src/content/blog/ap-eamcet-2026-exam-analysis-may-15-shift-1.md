## What are the Students’ Reactions to the AP EAMCET May 15 2026 Exam?

Overall students found the paper to be moderate to difficult, and they heavily emphasised that strict time management is crucial to navigating the lengthy Mathematics section without missing easier scoring opportunities in Chemistry and Physics.

**Mathematics**

- Students generally felt the Mathematics section was moderate to difficult.
- A major complaint from students was that the questions were highly calculative and time-consuming, leaving many feeling that the allotted time was insufficient.
- Students noted that even with practice from previous years’ papers, the questions still took a significant amount of time to solve.
- Some students expressed that apart from one or two easy questions, the majority of the section felt hard, leading to expectations of lower scores in Math.
- According to initial feedback, roughly seven out of ten students rated it as moderate, while three considered it tough.

**Physics**

- Students reacted to Physics as being moderate to difficult.
- While 5 to 6 theory-based and direct statement questions were perceived as easy, students felt the rest of the section was tricky, lengthy, and somewhat different from expectations.
- Students highlighted that the questions heavily tested formulas and conceptual understanding.

**Chemistry**

- Chemistry received the most positive feedback, with students calling it easy to moderate and a major “score-boosting” subject.
- Students were very happy with this section, observing that the questions were directly taken from NCERT and Intermediate Academy textbooks.
- Although some equation-type questions took a bit of time, many students confidently reported expecting high scores, with several claiming they could score 32, 33, 35, or even 38 marks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How many marks are needed for a top 1000 rank in AP EAMCET 2026?

Candidates generally need to score 120+ marks to secure a rank below 1000. To achieve this score range, students often target around 35 marks in Chemistry, 30 marks in Physics, and 50 to 60 marks in Mathematics.

### 2. How difficult was the Mathematics section and what were the key topics?

Mathematics was considered moderate to difficult and highly calculation-intensive. Important topics included Calculus, Matrices, Geometry, Vectors, Complex Numbers, Binomial Theorem, and Trigonometry. Questions from Calculus and Trigonometry were especially time-consuming.

### 3. What is the best strategy for time management during the exam?

Experts generally recommend attempting Chemistry first because it is relatively easier and helps secure quick marks. Candidates can then move to Physics and finally dedicate the maximum remaining time to the lengthy Mathematics section.
