## WBJEE Exam Pattern

WBJEE consists of three subjects Mathematics, Chemistry, and Physics. All questions will be Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQ), with four options against each question. There will be three categories of questions in each subject.

The number of questions, as well as the maximum marks for each, are given in the following table:

|  |  |  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Subject** | **Category-1 Each Q carries 1 (One)mark (-ve marks =- 1/4)** | **Category-2 Each Q carries 2 (two)marks (-ve marks =- 1/2)** | **Category-3 Each Q carries 2 (two)marks (No -ve marks)** | **Total Number of Questions** | **Total Marks** |
| **Mathematics** | 50 | 15 | 10 | 75 | 100 |
| **Physics** | 30 | 5 | 5 | 40 | 50 |
| **Chemistry** | 30 | 5 | 5 | 40 | 50 |

## **How to Use WBJEE Previous Years’ Question Papers Effectively**

Previous year’s question papers are a great source to enhance your exam preparation. Here are a few tips to use the previous years’ question papers effectively:

- **Start Early and Prepare a Schedule:** Begin solving previous years’ papers well in advance and schedule at least one paper per week.

- **Attempt Papers in Exam-like Conditions:** Simulating exam-like conditions can help students effectively manage their time and find where they need improvements. Choose a quiet place, set a timer, and attempt the paper without any interruptions.

- **Refer answer keys:** Use the official answer keys to check your answers. This will not only help in understanding the correct approach but also improve conceptual clarity.

- **Revise answers:** Analyze your mistakes and revise the concepts if you fail to answer the related concepts.
- **Increase the frequency:** Start with older papers and gradually move to recent ones. This helps in understanding the evolving exam pattern and preparing for the latest trends.
