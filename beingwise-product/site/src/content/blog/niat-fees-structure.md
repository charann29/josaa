NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies (NIAT) upskilling training program through collaboration with various Universities. The NIAT collaborating universities offer BTech in CSE with specialisations in Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and Machine Learning. The fee structure for Universities collaborating with the NIAT Upskilling Program ranges from INR 2 Lakhs to INR 4.5 Lakhs in the first year; however, the total fee ranges from INR 8 Lakhs to INR 18 Lakhs.

Students are enrolled in an industry-aligned program with a zero-nonsense approach. It was developed based on feedback from over 3,000 companies, including Fortune 500 giants. NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies gathers industry insights from a network of 3,000+ companies and 10,000+ tech professionals and shares these reports with collaborating universities. However, curriculum updates are handled only by the universities through their academic bodies, following the guidelines of the University Grants Commission (UGC) and All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE).

While universities manage lectures, seminars, and academic delivery, NIAT conducts parallel skill-building sessions, workshops, and practical training modules to strengthen students’ industry-ready skills. Students can explore the NIAT Skill Map to understand how it develops critical thinking, problem-solving, and leadership abilities. As a result, students benefit from an industry-aligned program that keeps pace with rapid technological advancements.

As far as placements are concerned, Universities collaborating with NIAT have partnered with 3000 companies such as Microsoft, Google, and Amazon, and feature a dedicated placement department that supports students in securing job offers.

## What are the fees of Universities Collaborating With NIAT Training Centre?

Fees for the NIAT Upskilling program and the collaborating university range from INR 8-18 lakhs (INR 2-4.5 lakhs per year), depending on the collaborating university. However, apart from this, you also need to pay a one-time non-refundable NAT registration fee of INR 1200. You can visit the official website to complete **[NAT registration details](https://apply.niatindia.com/login)**.

## I’m confused, who do I pay the fees to: Collaborating University or NIAT?

NIAT Collaborated Universities will collect fees for their B.Tech degree programs, including tuition, examination, library, and other academic-related fees. The university sets, collects, and manages these fees.

NxtWave charges program fees for its high-quality training, practical learning, and industry-relevant skills for specialisations like Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning, Data Science, Cyber Security, with hands-on experience.

## Are Fees of universities collaborating with NIAT Upskilling Program Worth It?

Whether the fees of **NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies (NIAT)** are worth it depends on what a student expects from the program. The total cost for the 4-year program ranges between **INR 8 lakh and INR 18 lakh**, depending on the collaborating university and specialisation.

For many students, the fees can be justified because the program focuses on **industry-oriented learning, coding practice, and real-world projects** rather than only theoretical education. Reviews mention that the NIAT skill map is designed to be practical and helps students build job-ready skills, while placement support and internship preparation are also provided. Also, students get a [**paid internship of INR 30K per month**](https://www.niatindia.com/student-internships) from the very first year.

However, opinions about the value of the fees are **mixed**. Some students appreciate the practical training, mentorship, and career preparation, but others feel the fees are relatively high compared with traditional engineering colleges.

## Do I need to pay any additional Fees & Expenses apart from Tuition Fees?

Students can check the Additional fees they need to pay, in addition to tuition, for universities collaborating with the NIAT.

- **Registration Fee:** INR 1200 is charged by the Universities collaborating with NIAT for NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) registration. This is a one-time and non-refundable fee. It will not be refunded even if the student does not appear for the exam.
- **Hostel Fees to the Collaborating Universities:** Various private hostels partnered with the collaborating Universities of NIAT and provide separate hostel facilities for boys and girls. Students pay additional charges beyond tuition fees for accommodation.
- **Workshop Fees:** NIAT conducts several workshops and AI-based projects. Along with the skill map, this will incur extra charges for participation.
- **Computer Lab/Other Laboratory Charges for the collaborating Universities:** Students at NIAT must pay separate lab charges and computer lab fees.

Yes. Students simultaneously experience academic learning through the university and practical training through NIAT, which helps them develop both theoretical knowledge and real-world skills.

### 7. Why does NIAT collaborate with multiple universities instead of running a standalone college?

NIAT focuses primarily on industry-oriented tech training, while partnered universities provide the official B.Tech degree. This collaboration allows students to receive a recognised university degree along with intensive practical tech training.

### 8. Does the NIAT fee include both collaborating university tuition and training costs?

Yes, the total fee generally includes the tuition fees of the collaborating university along with the industry-ready upskilling program provided by NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies. However, some components, such as hostel charges, lab fees, and workshops, may be charged separately.

### 9. Is the NAT registration fee refundable?

No. The registration fee of INR 1200 for the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT) is typically non-refundable, even if a student chooses not to appear for the test.

### 10. Are there any additional academic expenses apart from tuition fees?

Yes. Students may need to pay some additional fees charged by the collaborating universities, like lab fees, project workshops, software tools, and examination fees, depending on the program and university requirements.
