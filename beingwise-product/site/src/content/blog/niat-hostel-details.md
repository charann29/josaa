## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Is NIAT a university or college that grants degrees?

No. NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) is an upskilling program, not a university or college. Degrees are awarded only by the collaborating universities as per their academic regulations.

### Q2. Will enrolling in NIAT’s upskilling program automatically secure me a seat in a collaborating university?

No. Enrollment in NIAT does not guarantee university admission. Each university has its own eligibility criteria and admission process. NxtWave only facilitates the process with your consent.

### Q3. What is the NAT, and is it the same as a university entrance examination?

NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) is an internal assessment conducted by NxtWave. It is not a university entrance exam and does not directly grant admission to any university.

### Q4. What certification will I receive from NIAT upon completing the upskilling program?

You will receive an IRC (Industry-Ready Certification) from NxtWave. This is a skill-based certification and not equivalent to a university degree or diploma.

### Q5. What fees does NIAT charge, and does it cover university tuition or seat reservation?

NIAT charges an upskilling fee only for its training program. It does not include or cover university admission fees, tuition fees, or seat reservation charges.

### Q6. Who delivers the upskilling sessions at the NIAT Training Centre?

Upskilling sessions are conducted by NIAT’s trainers, mentors, and success coaches, who are separate from university faculty.

### Q7. Is NIAT’s upskilling program content part of the university’s degree curriculum?

No. NIAT’s content is independent of the university curriculum and is designed to complement academic learning with industry-relevant skills.

### Q8. Does NIAT have its own campus or academic buildings?

No. NIAT operates within training centres or facilities provided by collaborating universities and does not have its own campus.

### Q9. Is NIAT approved by AICTE, UGC, or NAAC?

No. NIAT is not an academic institution, so approvals like AICTE, UGC, or NAAC are not applicable. These apply only to degree-granting institutions.

### Q10. What kind of infrastructure will I have access to as a NIAT student?

Students will have access to infrastructure provided by the collaborating universities, including labs, classrooms, cafeteria, sports facilities, medical support, and hostel accommodations.
