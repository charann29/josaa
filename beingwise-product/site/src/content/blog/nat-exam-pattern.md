The NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT) is conducted by NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies as part of its enrolment process for industry-ready upskilling programs. The application is available in online mode.

The NIAT is an upskilling program, so it does not have a fixed admission eligibility like universities. Generally, students who have completed or are pursuing 10+2 (Class 12) and are interested in technology can enroll in its industry-aligned training modules. Students who have completed or are appearing for 10+2, preferably with a PCM background (based on program requirements), are eligible to take the NAT. NAT evaluates cognitive abilities and problem-solving skills for the upskilling program. It is important to note that NAT is not a university entrance exam, and admission to degree programs depends entirely on the collaborating university’s eligibility criteria, while NIAT only facilitates the process by sharing student details with universities upon consent.

NAT is a 90-minute online test conducted in CBT mode that evaluates students’ cognitive abilities, problem-solving skills, and overall readiness to benefit from NIAT’s industry-aligned training modules.

## How to Apply For NAT?

NAT registration is through the official website of NIAT, i.e. niatindia.com. Students need to pay INR 1200 as the registration fee to apply for the NAT 2026. Check the steps given below to know the NAT registration process.

**Step 1:** Visit the official website of NIAT: <https://www.niatindia.com/>

**Step 2:** Click on “Apply Now” in the top right corner of the homepage. The students can also go to the “Admission Counselling” tab and click on “Apply For Counselling”.

**Step 3:** Give your mobile number and verify it with an OTP to proceed with the registration process. Make sure you give a valid mobile number and that you are within the network area to successfully receive the OTP.

**Step 4:** Now you will have two options in front of you!

- **For Students:** Add your name, address, phone, and mail ID, along with your relevant educational background. The students also need to add their parents’ details, which include names, family income, phone number, and occupation category.
- **For Parents:** If you are a parent filling out the registration form, then you need to add your name, phone number, family income, and occupation category, along with all the student details.

**NOTE: Make sure every personal detail you are adding is correct, and there are no discrepancies.**

**Step 5:** After carefully filling out all the details, students can proceed to book the NAT slot. Here, the student can opt whether they want to appear for the test “ Right Now” or “Later”. If later, then they can select the date and time of the NxtWave Assessment Test.

**Step 6:** Congratulations on the successful registration. The next step involves payment of the application fee.

**Step 7:** Pay the non-refundable application fee of INR 1200. Click on “Continue to Pay”. This fee will not be refunded even if the student does not appear for the test.

**Step 8:** The payment can be made by card, UPI modes, and NetBanking through ICICI and Axis Bank. Students can also use Wallets like MobiKwik, Ola Money, etc., to make the payment. Add your card or UPI ID after selecting the method to pay the registration fee.

**Step 9:** Upon successful payment, your NAT slot is booked. Now, you need to prepare for the NAT  to proceed with exploring upskilling opportunities with NIAT.

## Frequently Asked Questions

#### 1. If I have a PCB in my 10+2 level, can I appear for NAT?

No, if you have Biology in your class 12 level, you cannot appear for NAT. The upskilling pathways supported by NIAT require PCM at the 10+2 level to be eligible.

#### 2. Can I get a fee concession for enrollment in the NIAT Collaborating University in Hyderabad?

Yes, based on your academic records, performance in the NxtWave Assessment Test, and 1:1 counselling sessions, you might get a fee concession.

#### 3. What are the 1st year fees for the Universities where the NIAT upskilling program is offered?

The first-year fees at universities where NIAT’s upskilling program is offered vary based on the location and course specialisations, and they can range from ₹2 Lakh to ₹4.5 Lakh.

#### 4. What are the eligibility criteria for enrollment in NIAT’s upskilling initiative?

For enrollment to NIAT, students need to have a 10+2 level with PCM subjects and qualify in the NAT test + the eligibility criteria of the NIAT collaborating university.

#### 5. How do I confirm admission to a university where NIAT’s upskilling initiative is offered?

Admission to a university where NIAT’s upskilling program is offered is confirmed by the university itself, based on its own eligibility criteria and admission policies. NxtWave only facilitates the process by sharing the student’s details with the prospective university, with the student’s written consent. The final admission is based on the collaborating University’s guidelines.

#### 6. Is the NxtWave course free?

No, the NIAT’s upskilling program is not free. However, eligible students who perform well in the NAT and counselling session may get a fee waiver.

#### 7. Does NxtWave conduct classes in online mode?

No, NxtWave doesn’t conduct any online classes. The upskilling programs are offered in an offline mode at collaborating university campuses.

#### 8. Does clearing NAT guarantee admission to a university?

No. Clearing NAT does not guarantee admission to any university. NIAT shares the student’s details with the prospective university only with the student’s written consent. The final admission decision rests solely with the university, based on its own eligibility criteria and admission policies. NAT is necessary for enrolment in the NIAT’s upskilling program.

#### 9. What certification does a student receive after completing NIAT’s upskilling program?

Upon successful completion of NIAT’s upskilling program, students receive an IRC from NxtWave. This certificate recognises the student’s skills and achievements in the upskilling program. The academic degree is awarded separately by the collaborating university upon fulfilling its own program requirements.

#### 10. Is NAT the same as a university entrance exam?

No. NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) is conducted by NxtWave as part of the enrolment process for NIAT’s upskilling program. It is entirely separate from any entrance examination conducted by the university. Each university independently manages its own admission process based on its established eligibility criteria.
