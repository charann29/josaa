## BITSAT Exam Analysis 2026: Subject-wise Detailed Analysis

Each subject showed a consistent pattern across shifts, with some variation in difficulty. Here is a section-by-section breakdown based on actual student reports and memory-based questions from the BITSAT question paper.

### BITSAT Exam Analysis: Physics Section

Physics stayed moderate across all four shifts. The section had a mix of formula-based and theoretical questions. Around 35 to 40 per cent of questions were theory-based, and the rest were numerical. Some questions on April 15 combined concepts from two or three topics at once, which made them more time-consuming. Topics asked across shifts:

- Current Electricity and Circuits
- Kinematics and Laws of Motion
- Heat and Thermodynamics
- Carnot Engine
- Electrostatics and Capacitors
- Magnetic Effects of Current
- Modern Physics and Nuclei
- Work, Energy and Power
- SHM and Oscillations
- Gravitation, Semiconductor, Units and Dimensions

### BITSAT Paper Analysis: Chemistry Section

Chemistry showed the most variation across shifts in terms of BITSAT subject wise weightage. It was easy to moderate on April 15 Shift 1, but it became the toughest section on April 16 Shift 2. The section-wise order in most shifts was Organic, followed by Inorganic, then Physical. Topics asked across shifts:

- GOC and Reactions
- Carboxylic Acids and Derivatives
- Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure
- Chemical Thermodynamics
- Electrochemistry
- Biomolecules, Polymers, Surface Chemistry
- States of Matter, Isomerism
- Redox Reactions, Mole Concept
- S-block and P-block Elements
- Solid State, Salt Analysis

### BITSAT Exam Analysis: Mathematics Section

Mathematics was the most challenging section across all four shifts of this BITSAT exam paper. Questions were lengthy and calculative. Students who managed their time well in Maths had a clear advantage. The section was still easier than JEE Mains but required strong fundamentals and quick calculation. Topics asked across shifts:

- Calculus (Integration, Differential Equations)
- Coordinate Geometry (Circles, Parabola, Conics)
- Probability and Statistics
- Vectors and 3D Geometry
- Linear Programming
- Matrices and Determinants
- Trigonometry and Complex Numbers
- Binomial Theorem, Sequence and Series
- Sets and Limits
- Permutation and Combination

### BITSAT Exam Analysis: English Proficiency Section

English was the easiest and quickest section across all shifts. This is consistent with the standard BITSAT exam pattern, where English is designed to be scoring and time-efficient. Most students finished this section in under 10 minutes. Topics asked:

- Synonyms and Antonyms
- Sentence Completion and Correction
- Error Detection and Grammar
- One-word Substitution
- Reading Comprehension
- Homophones

### BITSAT Exam Analysis: Logical Reasoning Section

Logical Reasoning stayed consistent with its reputation of being a scoring, manageable section across all shifts. This section follows a predictable BITSAT question paper pattern and rewards students who have practiced variety. Topics asked:

- Number Series and Letter Series
- Coding-Decoding

- Direction Sense
- Syllogism
- Puzzle-based questions

- Chart Logic

### BITSAT Question Paper Pattern: Physics Memory-Based Questions

The Physics questions below were reported mainly from April 16 Shift 2. They were numerical and concept-based, requiring application of formulas from Mechanics and Thermodynamics.

**Q1:** An inclined plane makes an angle of 45 degrees. A circular loop rolls down with acceleration a. Write a in terms of g.

**Q2:** Combustion of a fuel-air mixture occurs at 3100 K and exhaust gases leave at 300 K. Calculate the efficiency of the Carnot engine.

**Q3:** A 2 kg particle has 1D motion with potential U(x) = x squared + x to the power 4. The particle is released from rest at x = 1 m with V(x=1) = 0. Find acceleration at x = 0.

**Q4:** Speed v(x) = 2x squared + 1 m/s. At x = 1 m, find the acceleration.

**Q5:** At sea level, water boils at Ts. At 1 km above sea level, it boils at Tm. How are Ts and Tm related?

## BITSAT Exam Pattern and Paper Pattern

BITSAT follows a fixed exam pattern. The total duration is 3 hours with a set number of questions in each section. After attempting all 130 questions, you can try 12 bonus questions. These include 4 questions each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics or Biology. Many students skip them due to time pressure. If you manage your time well, these questions can help increase your score.

| **Detail** | **Info** |
| --- | --- |
| Mode | Computer-Based Test |
| Duration | 3 Hours |
| Total Questions | 130 MCQs plus 12 Bonus |
| Marking | +3 correct, minus 1 wrong, 0 unanswered |
| Bonus Questions | 12 extra questions unlocked only after all 130 are attempted |

## BITSAT Total Marks: What Is the Maximum Score?

A lot of students search for BITSAT total marks before and after the exam to understand where they stand. The total marks in BITSAT are 390 for the 130 mandatory questions. If you attempt all 12 bonus questions correctly, the maximum possible score goes up to 426. Here is a quick breakdown:

## BITSAT Result 2026: When and How to Check

BITSAT result process works in two stages. On the day of the exam, your raw score is displayed on the screen immediately after you finish. You must note this number before exiting the hall since it is your provisional score. After both Session 1 and Session 2 are complete, BITS Pilani releases the official moderated scores. These are adjusted for difficulty variation across shifts and are used for the final merit list. BITSAT result is expected to be released in the first week of June after Session 2 concludes in May 2026.

### How to check the BITSAT result 2026?

1. Visit the official BITS Pilani admissions portal
2. Log in using your application number and password
3. Go to the results section on your dashboard
4. Your moderated score and rank will be displayed
5. Download and save your scorecard for future reference

## How to Check BITSAT Score After Exam?

BITS Pilani does not release an official public answer key, but you can still check your score easily using the steps below:

- After completing the exam, your score is shown on the screen
- Note it down before leaving the exam hall, as this is your provisional score
- After all shifts are over, BITS Pilani releases moderated scores
- These scores are adjusted based on difficulty across shifts and used for the final merit list
- To calculate your score yourself:
  - Add 3 marks for every correct answer
  - Subtract 1 mark for every wrong answer
  - Unanswered questions carry 0 marks
  - The maximum score is 390
- You can also refer to memory-based answer keys released by coaching institutes within 24 to 48 hours
- These are not official, but they help you estimate your score
