- NCERT Or SCERT for basics
- One good reference book for a deeper understanding
- A practice book or PYQs for revision

## **WBJEE Preparation Books: How to Choose the Right Ones**

Many students think that using more books will improve their preparation, but that’s not true. Too many resources often lead to confusion and incomplete revision. What actually works is keeping things simple and consistent. Following the simple steps below, you can easily prepare better with WBJEE Books:

- Stick to **one or two books per subject** instead of using too many
- Always begin with **NCERT** to build a strong foundation
- Choose books that are **easy for you to understand**
- Avoid **switching books frequently**, as it breaks your flow
- Include [**WBJEE** **previous year papers**](/blog/wbjee/wbjee-previous-year-question-papers/) **and** [**WBJEE mock tests**](https://collegedost.in/mock-test/wbjee) in your preparation
- If a book feels too difficult, you can change it—but **don’t keep changing again and again**

## **Subject-Wise WBJEE Books Strategy**

If you’re still unsure how to manage everything and prepare better with WBJEE books, our advice is to keep it simple:

- **Mathematics:** Maths is all about practice. Make it a habit to solve questions every day, even if it’s just a small set. Regular problem-solving helps you retain concepts better and improves speed over time.
- **Physics:** In Physics, clarity matters more than quantity. Instead of rushing through multiple books, spend time understanding each concept properly. Once your basics are strong, solving questions becomes much easier.
- **Chemistry:** Chemistry is highly scoring if revised well. Focus on NCERT, especially for Organic and Inorganic Chemistry, and revise it regularly. Consistent revision will help you remember reactions, formulas, and concepts more effectively.

## Step-by-Step Preparation Strategy with WBJEE Books

[WBJEE preparation 2026](https://collegedost.in/blog/wbjee/wbjee-preparation-tips-2026/) should follow a clear 70-day roadmap, as the exam is scheduled for 24 May. Out of this, keep the last 5-7 days only for light revision and a relaxed mindset, so your effective preparation time becomes around 70 days. Instead of wasting this extended time, use this 12-step preparation strategy with WBJEE books properly to strengthen your preparation.

### 1. Set a Higher Target to Push Your Performance

Your target should always be on the higher side. If your priority is a top government college through WBJEE, then you should aim for 150+ score.

- Minimum target: 150+ marks (for a decent government college)
- Ideal target: 170+ marks

### 2. Understand Subject-Wise Targets and Weightage

WBJEE has three subjects, with Mathematics carrying the highest weightage, while Physics and Chemistry are similar.

A practical target split can be:

- Maths: 70+ (aim for 75)
- Physics: 35+
- Chemistry: 45+

Chemistry is especially important because it is a high-scoring subject and can boost your overall rank significantly.

### 3. Focus on Maximum Marks, Not Perfection

Do not go into the exam thinking you will score full marks. In reality:

- You may not be able to solve every question
- Some questions will feel difficult or unexpected

### 4. Stay Mentally Strong During Preparation and Exam

If a paper feels tough, don’t panic. Remember:

- If a question is difficult for you, it is difficult for everyone
- Losing confidence will directly reduce your performance

### 5. Follow a Proper Study Sequence (Don’t Study Randomly)

Your preparation should be structured, mixing topics randomly will weaken your foundation. Your WBJEE Preparation journey should include:

- Complete topics in a proper sequence
- For example, in Chemistry:
  - Start from basics (Mole Concept, Atomic Structure, Periodic Table, Bonding)
  - Then move step-by-step to advanced topics
- If you start a section (Organic/Inorganic/Physical), complete it fully

### 6. Build Strong Concepts First

Before jumping into practice, make sure your concepts are clear.

- Use books, lectures, or online resources
- Focus on understanding, not memorising

### 7. Practice PYQs Seriously (10–12 Years Minimum)

WBJEE Previous Year Questions are a must.

- Solve at least last 10–12 years of WBJEE questions
- Also include [JEE Main PYQs](/blog/jee-main/jee-mains-pyqs/)
- Practice easy to moderate level questions

### 8. Focus on the Approach, Not Just the Answer

The goal is not just solving a question, it is understanding how to approach it.

- Don’t memorise solutions
- Learn the thinking process behind each question

### 9. Maintain Daily Question Practice Targets

Daily practice should be consistent . You should atleast practice:

- Maths: 50+ questions per day
- Physics & Chemistry: 30 questions each

### 10. Avoid Passive Learning

Just watching solutions or searching answers is not real preparation.

- Try solving questions yourself first
- If you can’t solve, then analyse the solution
- Identify where you went wrong

### 11. Study Consistently (Even If Not Perfect)

You may not be able to complete everything daily, and that’s okay. But remember these things:

- You should study every day
- Practice is must for every day practice
- Most importantly, you need to stay consistent

### 12. Use Extra Practice Resources if Needed

Apart from PYQs:

- You can use practice books
- Or follow coaching/online batches for additional questions

## Balancing WBJEE and Board Exam Preparation

Preparing for WBJEE along with board exams can feel like a lot, but in reality, both can be managed together if you approach it the right way. Since the syllabus overlaps to a large extent, you don’t need two completely separate strategies. The focus should be on using the same preparation in a smarter way.

- **Start with the common syllabus:** Most topics in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics are the same for both exams. Instead of studying them twice, try to cover them together. When your concepts are clear, you’re already preparing for both boards and WBJEE at the same time.
- Plan your study time wisely: Create a simple daily schedule where you divide time between theory and practice. You can use one part of the day for board-style preparation (theory, writing practice) and another for solving MCQs for WBJEE. Keep your plan realistic so you can actually follow it every day.
- **Don’t treat all topics equally:** Some chapters are more important than others. Focus more on high-weightage topics and adjust your preparation based on what’s coming first, your board exams or WBJEE.
- **Combine theory with practice:** After studying a chapter for boards, don’t stop there. Solve WBJEE-level questions from the same topic. This helps you understand how concepts are applied and improves your problem-solving skills.
- **Revise regularly:** With two exams, it’s easy to forget what you’ve studied. Keep revising formulas, reactions, and key concepts. Short notes can be very helpful for quick revision.
- **Practice both types of papers:** Solve previous year questions of WBJEE as well as board papers. This gives you a clear idea of how questions are asked in each exam and helps you adjust your approach accordingly.
- **Clear your doubts on time:** If you don’t understand a topic, don’t ignore it. Clear your doubts early, whether through teachers, friends, or reliable resources. Small gaps in understanding can create bigger problems later.
- **Take breaks and stay consistent:** Studying continuously without breaks can make you tired and less productive. Take short breaks, keep your mind fresh, and focus on being consistent rather than studying for long hours irregularly.
- **Work on time management through mock tests:** Mock tests help you manage time better and improve your speed. They also give you a real exam-like experience, which builds confidence.

## Common Preparation Mistakes to Avoid for WBJEE 2026

While preparing for WBJEE 2026, many students feel they are studying enough, but still don’t get the expected results. In most cases, the problem is not effort; it’s a few common mistakes that quietly affect preparation. Here are some mistakes you should try to avoid:

Starting without a proper study plan: Preparing without a timetable often leads to random studying. You may spend too much time on some topics and ignore others. A simple and realistic plan helps you cover the WBJEE syllabus in a balanced way.

- **Ignoring basics and directly solving tough questions:** WBJEE questions may look simple, but they are concept-based. If your basics are not clear, even moderate questions will feel difficult. Always start with fundamentals before moving to higher-level problems.
- Reading more, practising less: Just studying theory is not enough for WBJEE. Since it is a problem-solving exam, a lack of practice can reduce your speed and accuracy. Make sure you solve questions regularly along with studying.
- **Leaving doubts for later:** Many students keep postponing doubts, thinking they will understand later. This creates confusion, especially during revision. Try to clear your doubts immediately so your concepts stay strong.
- **Not solving WBJEE PYQs and mock tests:** Skipping previous year questions and mock tests is a big mistake. These are not just for practice, they help you understand how questions are actually asked in WBJEE, which topics are repeated or carry more weight, and how to manage your time during the exam. Without this exposure, even well-prepared students can struggle with speed and accuracy on the actual exam day.
- **Following too many WBJEE books:** Using multiple books at the same time can create confusion. Instead, stick to limited and reliable resources, and revise them properly.
- **Making an unrealistic study routine:** Studying for very long hours without breaks may look productive, but it often leads to burnout. A balanced routine with focused study sessions works much better.
- **Ignoring health and sleep:** During preparation, stress and irregular sleep can affect concentration. Staying healthy and well-rested is important to maintain consistency and perform well.

## **Revision Techniques for WBJEE**

As WBJEE gets closer, the way you study should change. This is not the time to keep adding new topics. What matters more now is how well you can revise what you already know. A lot of students feel stuck during revision because it takes too much time. You sit to revise one chapter and it ends up taking two days. That usually happens because the method is too slow. Instead of going back to full theory again, try a faster approach.

### Revise in your mind, not always on paper

You don’t need to write everything again. Take a question you’ve already solved, preferably a WBJEE PYQ and try to recall the solution in your head. Ask yourself:

- What was given in the question?
- Which concept or formula did I use?
- How did I reach the answer?

### Stick to familiar questions

Revision is not the time to try completely new questions. Go back to:

- WBJEE previous year questions
- Questions from your notes or practice books

You’ve already spent time on them once, so revising them helps you retain things faster. Think in steps, not just answers. While revising, focus on how the question was solved, not just the final answer. Try to connect the steps:
“This was given -so this formula applies-then this step follows.”

This habit helps a lot in WBJEE, where similar patterns often repeat.

### Don’t get stuck in calculations

You don’t need to solve every calculation again during revision. Your focus should be on:

- Understanding the method
- Remembering the approach

### If you forget, fix the gap immediately

It’s normal to forget things. When that happens:

- Look at the solution once
- Understand what you missed
- Then try to recall it again mentally

Before going to sleep, quickly think about:

- What did you get wrong?
- What the correct approach?

### Keep revision going throughout the day

Revision doesn’t always need a proper study setup. You can recall:

- Formulas
- Reactions
- Question patterns

### End your day with a quick recap

Before sleeping, take a few minutes to go over your day in your mind, ask yourself, ‘What did you study?’ ‘Which questions did you revise?’ Just mentally run through them once. It makes the next day’s revision easier.

### Use mock tests to support revision

Mock tests are not just for checking marks. After every test:

- Look at your mistakes
- Go back and revise those topics
