The Office of the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE) has started accepting applications for the Kerala Engineering, Agriculture, and Medical exam. Candidates can visit the official website, i.e. cee.kerala.gov.in, to apply for the KEAM 2025 exam. The application process commenced on **February 20th, 2025**, and the last date to apply for the exam is on **March 10th, 2025**. Candidates must have passed class XII th from a recognised board/university to be eligible to apply for the exam. Officials have announced that the KEAM exam will be held from April 22nd to April 30th, 2025. This article will give you insights into the KEAM exam pattern, important dates, eligibility criteria, etc.

## KEAM Registration 2025: Key Facts

Candidates are advised to stay updated with the exam pattern, registration fees, method of fee payment, etc. Here are some necessary details regarding the KEAM exam:

## Steps to Fill KEAM Application Form for 2025

Officials have started accepting application forms from February 20th, candidates aspiring for admission into undergraduate courses like engineering, agriculture, and pharmacy can register for the exam by navigating to the official website, cee.kerala.gov.in. Here are some steps to fill out the KEAM application process:

**Step 1:** Visit the official website and register, fill in the necessary details in the particulars to complete the registration process using the candidate registration portal. Here is the Link for Registration: [Click Here](https://cee.kerala.gov.in/keamonline2025/instruction)

**Step 2:** Log in to the candidate portal using the application number and password

**Step 3:** Fill up the application process by providing the required academic and personal details.

**Step 4:** Submit the application form by clicking on the ‘Submit’ option.

**Step 5:** Pay the application fee with the available modes of payment.

**Step 6:** Upload the images, like the signature and photograph, in the required dimensions and format.

**Step 7:** Take a printout of the application for further reference.

## KEAM 2025 Eligibility Criteria

Candidates are advised to go through the eligibility criteria before applying for the exam. Officials have announced the eligibility criteria based on their age limit, nationality, academic criteria, etc. Candidates can check the detailed eligibility criteria in the [official notification](https://cee.kerala.gov.in/keamonline2025/noti_view/572/Upload1), and here are a few of them:

- Candidates have to be Indian nationals in order to be eligible for the application process.
- Although there is no age limit to apply for the exam, the minimum age prescribed by the officials is 17 years.
- Candidates must have passed Class XII from a recognised board in Kerala.
- Non-Kerala are allowed to appear for the examination under the NKI and NK II categories.
- Candidates require a minimum of 45% in mathematics, physics, and chemistry in their Class XII th.

## KEAM 2025 Exam Pattern

KEAM is a state-level exam conducted by the Commissioner for Entrance Examination (CEE) for admission into undergraduate courses such as BTech, B.Arch, B.Pharm, and B.Agri. Candidates are required to attempt a total of 150 questions divided across three subjects. Mathematics includes 75 questions, physics has 45 questions, and chemistry comprises 30 questions. Candidates are awarded four marks for each question and are deducted one mark for every incorrect answer.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Subject** | **No of Questions** | **Marks** |
| Mathematics | 75 | 300 |
| Physics | 45 | 180 |
| Chemistry | 30 | 120 |
| **Total** | **150** | **600** |

## Note for the Candidates

- Only candidates belonging to Keralite are eligible for Communal/Special reservation or any fee concession.
- The candidates who have not cleared the nativity as ‘Keralite’ will not be considered for any reservation. Hence, defects in Nativity given in the memo in the ‘Candidate Portal’ should be rectified by uploading valid documents.
- Only those candidates who have qualified for the entrance examination for Engineering courses and submitted plus two (equivalent) marks online within the stipulated time will be included in the Engineering rank list.
- Candidates who have claimed NRI will be allowed to rectify defects later in the Candidate portal.
