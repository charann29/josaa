## KCET 2026 Exam Analysis Physics

The KCET 2026 Exam Analysis Physics for the April 23 morning session indicates that the paper was moderate in difficulty and largely concept-based. The Physics section featured a balanced mix of direct formula-based and application-oriented questions.

According to students, the paper covered important topics such as Mechanics, Current Electricity, Modern Physics, Optics, and Thermodynamics. Many candidates found the paper manageable with proper preparation, although a few numerical and conceptual questions required deeper understanding and careful time management. Overall, the Physics paper was considered balanced and student-friendly for those with strong fundamentals.

## What are the Students’ Reactions of KCET Physics Exam 2026?

According to students, the KCET 2026 Physics paper received mixed reactions, with many feeling they scored below their expectations. Students felt demoralised after the exam, and some said the paper was confusing and poorly balanced. Many were surprised by the high number of theory-based questions instead of numericals.

Students also felt the paper tested deeper conceptual understanding rather than quick formula-based solving. While some called it tough, others described it as moderate but frustrating. Overall, many students are now focusing on preparing well for the Chemistry paper.

## KCET Exam Analysis 2026 Chemistry

The KCET Exam Analysis 2026 Chemistry for the April 23 afternoon session shows that the paper was moderate in difficulty with a balanced mix of theory-based, conceptual, and a few numerical questions. The Chemistry paper was largely based on the NCERT syllabus, making it manageable for well-prepared students.

According to student reactions, Organic Chemistry carried the highest KCET weightage and was considered the most scoring section, while Physical Chemistry included some calculative questions from Kinetics, Thermodynamics, Electrochemistry, and Solutions. A few conceptual questions from Inorganic Chemistry were also reported as slightly tricky. Overall, students felt the paper was fair and balanced.

The Chemistry paper had strong coverage of Organic Chemistry, especially GOC, named reactions, alcohols, aldehydes, and amines. Physical Chemistry included numerical questions from Kinetics and Electrochemistry, while Inorganic questions were concept-based. Students who had revised the NCERT thoroughly found the paper easier to attempt.

## What are the Students’ Reactions of KCET Chemistry Exam 2026?

Students said the Chemistry paper was NCERT-based and manageable. Many candidates found Organic Chemistry highly scoring and direct. Physical Chemistry had some calculative questions, while Inorganic Chemistry included a few tricky conceptual questions.

## KCET Exam Day Guidelines

Students appearing for the KCET Exam 2026 should carefully follow all exam day instructions to avoid any last-minute issues at the centre. Arriving on time, carrying the required documents, and following the official KCET dress code are important for a smooth entry process. Candidates must also avoid carrying prohibited items and strictly follow the guidelines issued by KEA.

- Students appearing for the next papers must follow the official KCET dress code and exam day rules.
- Wear simple and comfortable clothing.
- Carry your KCET admit card without fail.
- Bring a valid photo ID proof for verification.
- Carry only the required stationery items.
- Reach the exam centre early, as late entry may not be allowed.
- Do not carry electronic gadgets inside the exam hall.
- Smart watches, calculators, mobile phones, and study material are strictly prohibited.
- Follow all instructions given by invigilators and exam authorities.
