### IIEST Shibpur Placement Process

The placement process at IIEST Shibpur is handled by the Department of Human Resource Management. It follows a step-by-step process from registration to final offers. Let’s have a look:

1. **Registration:** Final-year BTech students register with the placement cell. They submit their academic details and mention their preferred roles.
2. **Pre-Placement Talks (PPTs):** Companies give presentations before the hiring process. They explain the job role, salary, and work environment.
3. **Written or Online Tests:** Most companies start with a test. This can be an aptitude test, technical test, or coding round.
4. **Group Discussion:** Some companies, especially in consulting and finance, conduct group discussions to check communication and thinking skills.
5. **Technical Interview:** Shortlisted students go through technical interviews. Questions are based on subjects, problem-solving, and projects.
6. **HR Interview:** This is the final round. It checks communication skills, personality, and career plans.
7. **Offer:** Selected students receive offer letters. Students who get PPOs through internships also receive their final offers at this stage.

During the 2024-25 season, a notable number of PPOs were issued by Microsoft, Goldman Sachs, Texas Instruments, Wells Fargo, BNY Mellon, Deutsche Bank, JPMorgan Chase, and Accenture, among others.

## IIEST Shibpur Placement: Internship Opportunities

Internships play an important role in IIEST Shibpur Placement. They help students gain practical experience and also improve their chances of getting full-time job offers. Many students receive Pre-Placement Offers through internships, making them an important part of the overall placement process.

- Internships at IIEST Shibpur are important for learning and also help students get Pre-Placement Offers. The placement cell helps students get internships, and many students also find internships on their own.
- Internship participation is different for each branch. IT and CST students usually get more paid internships in tech companies. Civil, Mechanical, and Mining students mostly intern in core companies.
- For the 2025 batch, many top companies gave PPOs. These include Microsoft, Google, Goldman Sachs, Texas Instruments, Wells Fargo, BNY Mellon, JPMorgan Chase, Accenture, Serko India, and PwC.
- Civil Engineering students had around 90% internship participation, including both paid and unpaid internships. Some of the companies were PwC, JSW, BPCL, and TCS.
- Mechanical Engineering students did internships in sectors like automobile, steel, and manufacturing.
- IT and CST students who got internships in top tech companies reported monthly stipends between INR 60,000 and INR 1,50,000. This is based on student data, as the institute does not publish official stipend details.
- Many students also do research internships under faculty guidance. This helps them if they want to go for higher studies or research roles.
