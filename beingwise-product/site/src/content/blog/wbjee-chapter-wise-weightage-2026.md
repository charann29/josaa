## Table of Contents

WBJEE Chapter wise Weightage 2026 highlights the most important topics from the WBJEE syllabus that candidates should focus on while preparing for the exam. The West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board conducts the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination for admission to engineering, pharmacy, and architecture programs offered by participating institutes in West Bengal. The WBJEE 2026 exam will be conducted in offline mode and consists of two papers:

- Paper 1: Mathematics
- Paper 2: Physics and Chemistry

This blog on WBJEE chapter-wise weightage will help candidates identify high-scoring chapters and plan their preparation accordingly.

## **WBJEE Preparation Tips Based on Weightage**

Once you understand the chapter-wise weightage, the next step is to use it smartly in your preparation. You don’t need to study everything with the same intensity—some chapters deserve more time because they are asked more often. Focusing on high-weightage topics first can help you cover the most important areas quickly and improve your overall score. Here are some practical tips you can follow:

- Take mock tests to improve your speed and accuracy
- Start with high-weightage chapters to build confidence and secure marks early
- Solve previous year WBJEE question papers to understand the question pattern
- Practice numerical problems daily, especially for Physics and Mathematics
- Revise formulas, reactions, and important concepts regularly

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1.  How can I check WBJEE participating colleges for 2026?

Candidates can check the list of participating colleges during WBJEE counselling by logging into the official portal. The complete list of institutes and courses is available while filling in choices.

### Q2. How many colleges accept WBJEE scores?

Around 115–120 colleges, including government, private, and university institutes in West Bengal, accept WBJEE scores for B.Tech admissions.

### Q3. Which are the top government colleges under WBJEE?

Jadavpur University, Kalyani Government Engineering College, Jalpaiguri Government Engineering College, and Government College of Engineering & Ceramic Technology are among the top choices.

### Q4. **What is the fee range of WBJEE participating colleges?**

Government colleges usually have lower fees (around ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh for the full course), while private colleges may range between ₹3 lakh to ₹5 lakh, depending on the institute.
