According to the KEAM paper analysis April 18, the exam was average in difficulty but appeared comparatively long, and the candidates needed to have good time management skills. According to preliminary feedback, the KEAM 2026 difficulty level was not very challenging in concept, but the number of questions requiring calculations made it time-consuming. The KEAM exam pattern was the same, but the weightage of Mathematics was the highest, followed by Physics and Chemistry.

Multi-step problems made the KEAM Maths Question Paper the most difficult for the students, with Chemistry being relatively easy and easy to score. On the whole, the KEAM entrance exam was a test of conceptual fluency and speed, where accuracy was a major determinant of a good score. The trend is consistent with the past changes, where equal preparation and effective time management were instrumental in performance.

The KEAM paper analysis conducted on April 18 clearly indicates that the level of challenge for this time frame was not a matter of how difficult the questions were, but rather how well you executed answering the questions, given your time constraints.
