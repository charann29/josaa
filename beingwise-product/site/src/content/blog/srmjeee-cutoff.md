## What is a Good SRMJEEE Score?

A good score in SRMJEEE is based on the campus and branches one wants to get into, as every year the competition is different, and this is related to marks vs rank in SRMJEEE.

Typically, the higher the score, the better the branches you will be assigned, such as the main campus Computer Science branch. According to the SRMJEEE cut off marks, a score greater than 100 is considered to be a good score, and you may get admission into popular programs in most of the various campuses.

A score of 80-100 is also a good score and can give you a chance of admission to some of the more popular programs in each of the several campuses. You can get satisfactory opportunities in mid-level branches or other SRM campuses with a score between 60 and 80.

## Factors Affecting SRMJEEE Cutoff 2026

Factors such as those listed below affect the SRMJEEE 2026 cut-off for admission to SRM Institute of Science and Technology. These factors help determine the cut-off marks one must secure to enter the institute.

**Number of Applicants:** The more students who appear in the examination, the more the competition will increase, which may lead to an increase in the cutoff.

**Difficulty Level of the Exam:** The overall difficulty level of the SRMJEEE question paper is also a crucial factor in determining the trends of the cut-off. This is because a more difficult paper could lower the cutoff, and a less difficult paper could raise it.

**Availability of Seats:** The overall number of seats available in the SRM Campus and the Engineering Branch also plays a role in the Cutoff. Courses with limited seats generally have higher cutoffs.

**Popularity of Branches:** Demand among candidates makes the cut-off rank higher for highly preferred Branches like Computer Science Engineering.

**Previous Years’ Cutoff:** Historical admission patterns are taken into account by the authorities in deciding the cutoffs in the current admission cycle.

**Campus Preference:** In general, top campuses like Kattankulathur would have higher cutoffs than other campuses of SRM because of their better placement opportunities and higher demand by the students.

**Candidate Performance:** The performance of candidates in the entrance exam also affects the cutoff. However, an increase in the average scores of students may lead to a larger range of cut-off scores.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What is the expected SRMJEEE cutoff for CSE in 2026?

Computer Science Engineering (CSE) is expected to have one of the highest cutoffs across SRM campuses. The cutoff may remain around rank 2,000 for the Kattankulathur campus and up to around 9,000 for other campuses. The main Kattankulathur campus is generally the most competitive.

### 2. Does the SRMJEEE cutoff change every year?

Yes, the SRMJEEE cutoff changes every year depending on factors such as exam difficulty level, number of applicants, competition, seat availability, and admission trends.

### 3. What is considered a good score in SRMJEEE?

A score of 100 or above is generally considered good for securing admission to top branches and campuses. However, admission chances ultimately depend on rank, counselling trends, and seat availability.

### 4. Which SRM campus has the highest cutoff?

The SRM Institute of Science and Technology Kattankulathur campus usually records the highest cutoff trends due to its strong reputation, placement opportunities, and popularity among students.

### 5. Is there a common SRMJEEE cutoff for all branches?

No, each engineering branch has a separate cutoff range. High-demand branches such as Computer Science Engineering (CSE), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and related specialisations usually have higher cutoffs.
