## MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis for Physics Section

Based on the MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 May 15 Shift 2, the Physics section was considered easy by most candidates. The majority of the questions asked were formula-based and had direct links to HSC textbook concepts.

Candidates who practiced previous year question papers and went through standard formulas found the section easy and scoring. Get details on the important topics from the Physics section here.

- Current Electricity
- Electrostatics
- Modern Physics
- Thermodynamics
- Ray Optics
- Semiconductor
- Units and Dimensions
- Kinematics and Laws of Motion

## MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 for Maths Section

Based on the initial students’ reactions to the MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026, the Mathematics section was the toughest and longest section of the May 15 Shift 2 paper. Most candidates complained that tough and calculative questions came at the beginning of the section.

The paper contained both conceptual and formula-based questions, and some questions resembled past MHT CET attempts. Important topics from the Mathematics section are provided below.

- Integration by Parts
- Double Derivatives
- Matrices and Inverse of Matrices
- Vectors
- Dot Product and Triple Product
- Polygon and Diagonal-Based Problems
- Coordinate Geometry
- Application of Calculus

## MHT CET 2026 Exam Analysis for Chemistry Section

Among the three sections, Chemistry was believed to be the easiest and most scoring subject in the MHT CET May 15 question paper for Shift 2. Most questions in this section were direct and theoretical and were based on the HSC syllabus.

Students who were well-versed with textbook concepts and previous year questions found this section easy. Check out some crucial Chemistry topics covered in the question paper here.

- Effective Atomic Number (EAN)
- Haber Process
- Marshall’s Acid
- PHBV Monomers
- Mendius Reaction
- Osmotic Pressure
- Ionic Equilibrium and Buffer pH
- Thermodynamics
- Quantum Numbers
- First-Order Reaction Rate Constant Units

## Students’ Reaction to MHT CET May 15 Question Paper Shift 2

Here is some feedback from students who came out of the test centers after giving the MHT CET exam on May 15th, Shift 2. After collecting feedback about the overall MHT CET 2026 difficulty level from students, here are some reactions regarding each subject: Physics, Chemistry, and Math.

- **According to Veer Sharma,** Physics and Chemistry were relatively easier, while Math was relatively difficult in this particular paper. In his views, math was a tough nut to crack as the first half of the paper consisted of difficult and calculation-oriented questions.
- **According to Ananya Patil,** the questions in the Physics section were a combination of theoretical and numerical types. The Chemistry section was very direct and mainly focused on NCERT fundamentals. Mathematics was a little difficult, as multiple steps were needed to solve a few questions.
- **According to Rohit Kulkarni,** the Chemistry section was the simplest in the examination paper and boosted the attempts made. Physics was of a moderate level with formula-based questions, whereas Mathematics was quite lengthy due to Calculus and Coordinate Geometry problems.
- **As per Sneha Deshmukh,** the Physics section comprised manageable questions, provided the students had revised the formulas. The Chemistry section was highly scoring and simple. However, Mathematics required good speed and precision, as calculations took more time.
