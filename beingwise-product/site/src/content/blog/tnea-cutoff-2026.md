## TNEA Cutoff & Its Types

Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA) Cutoff is derived from the normalised Class 12 marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. It can vary based on category, college, branch, and ranks. TNEA Cutoffs are broadly categorised into TNEA Cutoff Marks and TNEA Cutoff Ranks:

**TNEA Cutoff Marks**: It is the minimum normalised marks converted from the Class 12 Marks in Physics, Maths, and Chemistry based on a formula. This mark is later required for seat allotment.

**TNEA Cutoff Ranks**: These are the closing ranks of the last admission in the last round for a certain specific college branch. It helps in predicting the seats based on the published TNEA rank list.

## TNEA Cutoff Calculator – How To Calculate TNEA Cutoff?

The cutoff for TNEA is only calculated with the scores secured in the class 12 examination in Physics, Mathematics, and Chemistry subjects. The scores are then scaled down to 200 marks. This process is referred to as the normalisation, generally conducted by the Directorate of Technical Education (DTE). It is done to ensure there is fairness in calculating cutoffs across different boards. Students can refer to the TNEA Cutoff Formula to calculate their probable cutoffs:

**Mathematics marks + Physics Marks / 2 + Chemistry marks / 2**

The TNEA Cutoff Calculator can be used online, where this formula is applied to predict cutoffs before they are even released. For example, if you have scored 95 in mathematics, 80 in Physics, and 70 in Chemistry. As per the formula, calculate the cutoff as follows:

**95 + 80/2 + 70/2 = 132.5**

To secure seats in the top engineering colleges, students need to have scores above 190 based on the trends of the TNEA Cutoff last year.

## What are the Factors Influencing TNEA Cutoff?

There are several factors that influence the TNEA cutoff variations. It directly affects admission trends in the engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu. Refer to the following section for the reasons for TNEA Cutoff fluctuations:

- The number of applicants and overall registrations received in a single year affects the cutoffs. As competition grows amongst students because of higher cutoffs.
- Seat availability becomes a factor in raising the cutoffs.
- Previous year cutoffs and trends set a pattern for the following year cutoffs, especially for popular branches.
- The level of difficulty in the board exam also influences cutoffs. As a tougher exams tend to yield lower ranks.
- Even categories of students matter in the cutoff fluctuations. As the OC category students’ cutoff is higher than that of the reserved category candidates.

## TNEA Counselling

TNEA Counselling is the centralised admission process conducted online by the Directorate of Technical Education (DoTE). It is done for B.Tech/B.E. seats in several types of engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu. Seat allotments are done for government, government-aided colleges, Anna University campuses, and self-financing institutions.

TNEA Counselling registrations have started from May 03, 2026. It includes 3 essential steps of registering online by filling in the required academic and personal details, uploading the required documents and paying the registration fee. After the registrations, rank lists are published based on cutoffs and every student is assigned ranks as per marks. After the students have locked their preferred branch and college based on their ranks, seats are allotted in rounds.
