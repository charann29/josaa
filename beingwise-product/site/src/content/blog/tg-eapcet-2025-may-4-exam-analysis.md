The Telangana State Engineering, Agriculture & Pharmacy Common Entrance Test (TG EAPCET) 2025, previously TS EAMCET, was conducted on **4th May in two shifts**. The first shift was from 09.00 AM to 12.00 PM, whereas the second shift was from 03.00 PM to 06.00 PM. It is one of Telangana’s most important examinations for engineering aspirants, assessing fundamental aptitude in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry.

CollegeDost has analysed both Shift 1 and Shift 2 for the May 4th exam and noted several differences in difficulty and subjects tested in terms of difficulty level and question pattern. Below is a summary of the two shifts compared in terms of difficulty level, weightage, and number of questions from each topic.

## TG EAPCET 2025 4th May Shift 1 Paper Analysis

The TS EAMCET 2025 exam, conducted on May 4 (Shift 1), was **moderate** in difficulty overall. According to the candidates’ initial reactions, the **Mathematics and Physics sections were relatively more challenging**, whereas **Chemistry was considered the easiest** of the three. Fifteen questions from chemistry were given as easy questions. Many students reported that a score of **75+** can be regarded as a good attempt for this shift.

## TG EAPCET Exam Analysis 2025: Student Reviews For May 4th Paper 1 and Paper 2

According to student reviews from the TS EAMCET 2025, May 4th – Paper 1 & Paper 2, here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

### **Shift 1**– May 4, 2025

**Toughest Paper: Mathematics**

The **TS EAMCET 2025 Morning slot** was generally rated as **moderate** in difficulty. Feedback from students indicated that the **Mathematics** section was the most challenging. It included 25 easy questions, 40 moderate questions, and 15 difficult ones, many of which were time-consuming and required significant calculations. **Physics** was rated as **moderate to difficult**, with 5 easy, 15 moderate, and 10 challenging questions. Topics like Thermodynamics, Current Electricity, and Work and Energy were particularly challenging.On the other hand, the Chemistry section was easier, with 15 easy, 15 moderate, and 10 challenging questions. The questions focused mainly on **Chemical Bonding**, **Equilibrium**, **Solutions**, **p-block Elements**, and **Surface Chemistry**.

### **Shift 2 – May 4, 2025**

Overall, students rated the **TS EAMCET 2025 Afternoon slot** as **moderate** in difficulty. The **Mathematics** section was manageable but lengthy, consisting of 30 easy, 20 moderate, and 30 challenging questions. Despite the difficulty of the questions, time management posed a challenge for many students. The **Physics** section was rated **moderate to difficult**, with 5 easy, 15 moderate, and 10 tough questions. Key topics like **Thermodynamics**, **Current Electricity**, **Electrostatics**, and **Moving Charges** made the paper tougher. The **Chemistry** section was the most straightforward, with 10 easy, 20 moderate, and 10 tough questions. It focused on **Biomolecules**, **Polymers**, **Equilibrium**, **Surface Chemistry**, and **Acids and Bases**, and most of the questions were theory-based and direct.

## TS EAMCET 2025: Answer Key and Response Sheet Details

**TG EAPCET 2025 answer key will be released today (5th May 2025) at 5:00 PM.** To download the response sheet, candidates need to visit the official website, <https://eapcet.tgche.ac.in/>. Check the steps below to download the TS EAMCET response sheet 2025.

### Steps to Download the Answer Key

- Go to the official TG EAPCET 2025 website.
- Select the TS EAMCET 2025 “Download Master Question Paper With Key (E)” link or “Download Response Sheet (E)”.
- Next, provide your hall ticket number, registration number, and date of birth.

- Click on “Get Candidate Response Sheet”.
- The answer key will appear on your screen.
- Candidates should download the answer key for later reference.

Candidates can use TG EAPCET answer key to compare with their answers for estimating their likely scores.

## TG EAPCET 2025 Answer Key: How To Raise Objections

Candidates can follow the steps below to object to the answer key of TG EAPCET 2025.

- Go to the TG EAPCET official website.
- Click on the “Objections on Preliminary Key (E)”.
  - **Note:** Candidates should only use the Master Question Paper to raise objections on the Preliminary Key.
- Read the instructions carefully and click on “Continue”.

- Next, provide your hall ticket number, registration number, and date of birth.
- Click on “Proceed”.
- **To object to any question or preliminary key, the candidate must pay a fee of INR 500 per question. If the objection is legitimate, the money will be returned within seven days of the results’ publication date**.
- Click on “Get Print of Already Raised Objections on Preliminary Key (E)” by giving your “Payment Reference ID”.

### **Observations**

**2025:** For May 4th, 2025, the application-based and numerical intensity continued in the Mathematics and Physics sections, though the difficulty was relatively more balanced compared to May 2nd and 3rd. In Shift 1, Maths remained lengthy and moderately challenging, while Physics featured conceptual and calculation-heavy questions, especially from Thermodynamics and Current Electricity. Shift 2’s paper was more student-friendly, with manageable problem-solving in Maths and Physics. Chemistry remained the easiest section across both shifts, heavily based on NCERT content, with direct, factual questions benefiting students with strong textbook preparation

**2024: TS EAMCET 2024 May 11 Shift 2** exam was considered **moderately challenging overall**. The **Mathematics section** was identified as the most difficult and lengthiest among the three subjects, with a focus on **algebra, trigonometry, and vectors**. The **Physics section** was also moderately challenging, similar to other exam days and shifts. In contrast, the **Chemistry section** included questions from **organic, inorganic, and physical chemistry**

## Conclusion

The TS EAMCET 2025 papers on **May 4th** presented varied difficulty levels across Shift 1 and Shift 2, offering students different testing experiences. Shift 1 was considered slightly more challenging due to a lengthy and complex Mathematics section and concept-heavy Physics questions. Chemistry, however, remained relatively straightforward in both shifts. Shift 2 was more balanced and student-friendly, with more straightforward questions across all subjects. Overall, students targeting 80+ marks in either shift were considered in a strong position for admissions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ****How was the overall difficulty of the TS EAMCET 2025 paper on 4th May?****

The 4th May paper was rated **moderate** overall. Mathematics was considered **lengthy and challenging**, Physics was **conceptual and numerical-heavy**, while Chemistry remained **the easiest**, with most questions directly from the **NCERT**.

### 2. ****Were any errors or grace marks expected for the 4th May TS EAMCET 2025 exam?****

Unlike the 2nd May Shift 1, **no major discrepancies were** reported in the 4th May shifts.

### 3. **********What were the most frequently asked topics in Mathematics on 4th May?**********

The most asked topics were **Calculus, Vectors, Probability, Circles**, and **Properties of Triangles**. Students reported that **integration and definite integrals** appeared in multiple questions.

### 4.  ******What Physics topics had the highest weightage in the 4th May exam?******

Important Physics topics included **Thermodynamics, Work and Energy, Motion in a Plane, Current Electricity,** and **Electrostatics**. Most questions were **numerically based**.

### 5. ****Was Chemistry easier than the other two subjects in the 4th May paper?****

Yes, Chemistry was considered the **easiest section**. Around **70–75%** of the questions were **direct NCERT-based**, with high weightage to **p-block elements, Surface Chemistry, and Biomolecules**.

### 6. **How did Shift One and Shift Two compare on 4th May?**

Both shifts were rated **moderate**, but **Shift 1** was slightly tougher in **Maths and Physics**. **Shift 2** was more **balanced**, though students faced **time constraints** due to lengthy Math questions.

### 7. **Will the 4th May TS EAMCET scores be normalised?**

Yes, like every year, **score normalisation** will be applied across all shifts to maintain fairness, especially since different shifts may vary slightly in difficulty
