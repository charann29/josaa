## **JEE Mains 2025 Marks vs Percentile**: **What is Normalisation in JEE Mains?**

**Normalisation in JEE Mains** is used to **adjust candidates’ scores** based on the difficulty of the exam paper. NTA conducts the exam in multiple shifts. The difficulty level across shifts varies. Therefore, normalisation ensures that no candidate gains an unfair advantage due to the shift in which they appeared.

### **Important Facts on Normalisation in JEE Mains:**

- **Adjusting for Difficulty Level:** Scores are balanced across different shifts to account for variations in question paper toughness.
- **Ensuring Fairness:** All candidates get an equal opportunity regardless of the exam session they appeared in.
- **Correcting Answer Key Errors:** Normalisation compensates for any discrepancies in the answer key, ensuring accurate scoring.
- **Accurate Rank Calculation:** It allows ranks to be calculated fairly, comparing students from different shifts consistently.
- **Handling Multi-Session Exams:** Since JEE Mains is conducted in multiple sessions, normalisation ensures a valid comparison of scores across all sessions.

In short, normalisation ensures **fairness, consistency, and accuracy** in converting raw marks into percentile scores for all candidates.

## How Normalized Scores are Calculated?

Normalized scores are calculated based on the statistical approach and involve data such as scores obtained by the individual candidate. The raw scores are considered, and the mean deviation, standard deviation are accounted for them. Percentile scores (relative measure of candidates who scored equal to or less than them) of candidates are also considered for the calculation of normalized scores.
The Percentile Score, rather than the candidate’s raw marks, will serve as the Normalized Score for the examination. It will be used to prepare the merit lists. Percentile Scores will be calculated to seven decimal places to minimize the bunching effect and reduce the occurrence of ties.

## **JEE Mains 2025 Normalisation: How to Break Tie Breaker**

If two or more candidates obtained the same total percentile score in JEE Main 2025, the tie is resolved by NTA using the following order:

- Higher Mathematics score.
- Higher Physics score.
- Higher Chemistry score.
- Candidate with fewer incorrect answers across all subjects.
- Candidate with fewer incorrect answers in Mathematics.
- Candidate with fewer incorrect answers in Physics.
- Candidate with fewer incorrect answers in Chemistry.

If the tie persists after all these steps, the candidates are assigned the same rank.
