The Maharashtra State Council Cell has released the MHT CET exam pattern. According to it, the exam will be conducted online as a Computer Based Test (CBT). The MHT CET exam paper consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs). There is no negative marking in the MHT CET exam, but the question level is similar to JEE Main.

The MHT CET exam is conducted for two streams: PCB (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology) and PCM (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics). To appear for the MHT CET exam, candidates must fill out the application form. The syllabus for the MHT CET exam will be conducted from class 11th with 20% weightage and from class 12th with 80% weightage.

The exam is conducted in 3 papers where paper-1 is for Mathematics, paper-2 is for Chemistry and Physics, and paper-3 is for Biology (Botany and Zoology). Each paper consists of 100 marks and the duration is 90 minutes.

## MHT CET Paper Difficulty Level

The MHT CET exam difficulty is similar to JEE Main. It’s about understanding the topics and thinking carefully when answering questions. To do well, practice with mock tests and previous year’s questions. Candidates who are aiming to pursue engineering must take both paper 1 and paper 2, whereas candidates who are aiming for pharmacy should take paper 2 and paper 3.

## MHT CET Passing Marks

The MHT CET exam consists of 200 marks for both PCM and PCB. Candidates must score a minimum of 45% of marks, equivalent to 90 marks out of 200, to qualify. For reserved category candidates, the passing marks are 40%, which is 80 marks out of 200. Candidates must note that passing marks do not guarantee admission to their preferred college or course. Candidates need to clear the minimum cutoff marks set by each college to secure admission.
