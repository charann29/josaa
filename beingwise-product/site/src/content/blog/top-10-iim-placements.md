## **IIM Placements Process**

Campus placements at Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) take several months to complete. These premier B-schools have students who made it through strict selection processes. Companies see them as perfect places to find top management talent.

- Summer Placements: Internships are arranged during March and April, giving students the opportunity to work with firms for two months. Guidelines for both candidates and recruiters strictly govern this process, and often leads to pre-placement job offers.
- Lateral Placements: Before the final placements, students with substantial work experience (typically 18 months or more) may participate in lateral placements. This process lets experienced candidates apply for senior or specialised roles.
- Final Placements: Held in December and January, this is the main round for full-time jobs across all IIMs. Most institutes follow a day-wise recruitment schedule, while some, such as IIM Ahmedabad, group companies by industry (using a cohort system) to allow extended, domain-focused evaluation periods.

### **What Recruiters Look For**?

Recruiters who visit IIMs seek students who excel in both academics and real-world skills. Top hiring managers say they notice candidates who demonstrate sharp analytical skills and work well with others. They judge candidates on four main areas:

- Domain knowledge: Deep understanding of business basics, mainly in their specialised fields
- Problem-solving abilities: Skills to tackle complex business challenges step by step
- Communication skills: Clear thinking and speaking, even under stress
- Cultural fit: How well they match the company’s values and work style

## Common Mistakes By Candidates During IIM Placements

During the IIM placements season, recruiters see countless talented candidates across the interview table. Yet only a few leave a lasting impression. IIM interview panels have spotted several recurring errors that hold back promising candidates:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Common Errors** |
| Confidence Issues | Under-confidence (hesitance), over-confidence (interrupting panellists) |
| Communication Flaws | Rambling, unclear explanations, poor articulation |
| Preparation Gaps | Weak domain knowledge, unprepared for basics like “Why MBA?”, unfamiliar with current affairs |
| Authenticity Issues | Bluffing, generic or rehearsed responses |

**What Makes a Candidate Stand Out**

- Remains calm and positive under stress—even smiling when faced with challenges.
- Speaks with genuine interest, avoiding bluffing or generic answers.
- Shows clarity of thought and adapts well to surprise questions.

**Final Selection Factors**

- Demonstrates composure, presence of mind, and clear motivation.
- Can explain failures and lessons learned, as well as achievements.
- Fits the program culture and shows potential to make a valuable contribution to the class.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What were the top recruiting sectors at IIMs in 2025?

Consulting dominated placements, accounting for 35-41% of offers across top IIMs. Other key sectors included finance, technology/analytics, and operations/supply chain management.

### 2. How did the newer IIMs perform in placements compared to the established ones?

While established IIMs like Ahmedabad, Bangalore and Calcutta offered average packages of INR 34-35 LPA, newer IIMs like Udaipur and Ranchi showed impressive growth with average packages ranging from INR 18-26 LPA.

### 3. How common were international placements at IIMs?

International placements remained rare, constituting only 1-5% of total offers even at top IIMs. The Middle East was the primary destination for global roles.

### 4. What emerging sectors are expected to influence future IIM placements?

Sustainability, ESG roles, healthcare technology, and fintech are emerging as promising sectors. Additionally, AI and analytics roles are on the rise across industries, reshaping the placement landscape at IIMs.
