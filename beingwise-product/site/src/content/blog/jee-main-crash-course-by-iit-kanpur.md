The Indian Institute of Technology(IIT-K), Kanpur, in collaboration with the Ministry of Education, has recently announced the launch of a 45-day Crash course, SATHEE. SATHEE stands for Self Assessment Test and Help for Entrance Exams. SATHEE is a 45-day crash course helping aspirants prepare for **JEE Mains 2025**, The Ministry decided the purview of booming coaching centers and increasing financial strain on students.

According to the announcement made by IIT Kanpur, the SATHEE online platform will offer a range of services ranging from educational tools to resources for **Jee mains 2025 preparation**.

The UGC Chairman Mamidala Jagadesh Kumar said “This SATHEE platform bridges the gap for students who can’t afford expensive entrance exam coaching, offering accessible resources and support for their success”. The UGC Chairman has also mentioned that the topics and videos included in this 45-day crash course are prepared by senior faculty members from IITs and IISc, helping students get high-quality lectures in this short span.

## Features of the SATHEE Crash Course for JEE Mains 2025

The SATHEE **Crash course for JEE Mains 2025** will commence on November 11, 2024. This 45-day crash course provides students with important resources, structured study plans, and strict preparation strategies to sharpen their performance for this highly competitive J**EE Mains 2025 exam**. Features of the SATHEE crash course include daily live sessions, mentored by experienced students covering all important topics and strategies. In addition, the course will consist of questions for daily practice, helping students to support their learning and practically apply the concepts. Mock test series are also made available to help students acquaint themselves with the real exam environment, boost their confidence, and assess their performance. The SATHEE crash course also offers dedicated feedback based on individual performance helping them focus specifically on areas of improvement. The SATHEE preparation material was made available in English, Hindi, and other regional languages of India.
