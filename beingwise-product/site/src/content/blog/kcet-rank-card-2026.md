## What is KCET Rank Card 2026?

The KCET Rank Card 2026 is the official document provided by KEA, which includes performance details of candidates who took part in the Karnataka Common Entrance Test. This document serves as proof of eligibility to participate in counseling and seat allocation processes.

It is common that students get confused between the KCET score card, the KCET marks card, and the KCET rank card, as all these documents’ names have been used synonymously; however, the rank card mainly shows the candidate’s rank.

## How is KCET Rank Calculated?

KEA follows a process to determine the rank for KCET where both the entrance test marks and qualifying examination marks are considered. The KCET rank calculation process needs to be known for candidates seeking admission in engineering as the final rank plays an important role at the time of counseling, allocation of seats, and admissions in colleges. Below is the KCET rank calculation process for candidates.

**Step 1: Appear for KCET Exam**

The first thing that students should do to determine their KCET rank is to write the entrance test. In the case of engineering admissions, the exam comprises Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics subjects.

**Step 2: Submission of Class 12/PUC Scores**

In addition to KCET scores, KEA also consider the marks scored in the qualifying examination like Karnataka PUC 2nd year, CBSE class 12, ICSE, or any other equivalent board exam. Marks scored in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics subjects are considered in the rank process.

**Step 3: Calculate KCET PCM Marks Percentage**

KCET marks percentage is determined in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics subject.

**Step 4: Calculation of Board Exam PCM Percentage**

Besides calculating the percentile of candidates based on their KCET scores, the authorities also calculate the percentage obtained by candidates in PCM subjects of Class 12 or PUC exams.

**Step 5: Use of 50:50 Formula for Engineering Admissions**

For admission into engineering programs, usually the formula followed is that 50% weightage of KCET marks and 50% weightage of PCM Class 12 or PUC marks are considered.

***Step 6: KCET Merit Score Preparation***

Once the KCET marks and board examination marks of candidates are considered, the final merit score of each individual candidate is prepared by KEA.

**Step 7: Implementation of Tie-Breaker Conditions**

In case there are multiple students securing the same marks, then tie-breaker conditions are followed like: Higher Mathematics Marks, Higher Physics Marks, Performance in the qualifying exam, Candidate Age.

**Step 8: KCET Rank List Publication**

After processing all these details, the final step is publishing the KCET Result, KCET Rank Card and KCET Rank List of KEA. Candidates can then download their KCET Rank Card from the official website.  This score contributes significantly to the final merit ranking.

## KCET Counselling 2026 in brief

The KCET Counselling Process is carried out by Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) for those candidates who wish to get admission into engineering and other professional courses in colleges in Karnataka. The KCET counselling procedure will commence once the KCET rank list has been declared and comprises different stages like documentation, choice filling, seat allocation, etc.

Candidates are required to complete all these counselling procedures successfully to get admission into their desired institution and programme.

| Counselling Stage | Brief Details |
| --- | --- |
| KCET Result Declaration | KEA releases the KCET result and rank card online for all candidates. |
| Document Verification (DV) | Karnataka 2nd PUC students usually complete verification online, while CBSE, CISCE, and quota candidates must visit KEA Helpline Centres with required documents. |
| Verification Slip Download | After successful verification, candidates can download the verification slip containing the secret key for counselling participation. |
| Seat Matrix Release | KEA publishes the list of available colleges, courses, and seat intake before option entry begins. |
| Choice Filling / Option Entry | Candidates log in using the secret key and select preferred colleges and courses in order of priority. |
| Mock Seat Allotment | KEA releases mock allotment results to help students understand their possible seat allocation. |
| Choice Editing Window | Candidates are allowed to modify, reorder, add, or remove options after mock allotment. |
| Final Seat Allotment | KEA announces the final allotted college and branch based on rank, category, and preferences. |
| Fee Payment | Students satisfied with the allotted seat must pay the admission fee online. |
| Admission Order Download | After fee payment, candidates can download the admission order for reporting to the college. |
| Reporting to Allotted College | Candidates must visit the allotted institute with original documents to confirm admission. |
| Subsequent Counselling Rounds | Students unsatisfied with allotment can participate in later rounds for better seat options. |
