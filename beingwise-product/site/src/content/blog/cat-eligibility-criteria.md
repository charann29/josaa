## **CAT Exam Eligibility Criteria 2025: Educational Qualification Requirements**

Academic qualifications are a vital aspect of the CAT eligibility criteria. Let’s take a closer look at the educational qualifications needed for CAT 2025 aspirants.

### **Minimum degree and recognised institutions**

A bachelor’s degree from a recognised institution serves as the foundation for CAT eligibility. Candidates must have a bachelor’s degree awarded by any university or educational institution established by an Act of Parliament or State legislature in India.

Candidates with international degrees need qualifications recognised by India’s Ministry of Education. The degree should represent 15 years of education according to the Indian system (10+2+3/4). A bachelor’s degree from any discipline works fine, which gives flexibility to students from different academic backgrounds.

The degree must have recognition from authorities like UGC/AICTE to be valid for CAT 2025. This will give a consistent standard for all applications and maintain quality measures for the examination.

### **Minimum Marks for Different Categories**

Different categories have varying minimum mark requirements. General, EWS, and NC-OBC category candidates need at least 50% marks or equivalent CGPA in their bachelor’s degree.

SC, ST, and PwD category candidates need to secure at least 45% marks or equivalent CGPA. This 5% relaxation makes quality management education available to more candidates from these categories.

**Note:** The conversion to a percentage follows the university’s certified procedure. If there’s no official conversion scheme, you divide the CGPA by the maximum possible CGPA and multiply by 100.

### **CAT Eligibility Criteria 2025 for professional degree holders (CA/CS/ICWA)**

Professional degree holders can also take CAT 2025. The exam is available to those who have completed qualifications like Chartered Accountant (CA), Company Secretary (CS), or Cost Accountant (ICWA/CMA). Fellows of India’s Institute of Actuaries (FIAI) can also apply.

In spite of that, these professionals must meet their category’s minimum percentage criteria – 50% for General, EWS, and NC-OBC candidates and 45% for SC, ST, and PwD candidates. This keeps the standards consistent across all applicant profiles.

A professional degree alone won’t make you eligible for the CAT exam. You need a bachelor’s degree along with your professional qualification.

Candidates should meet all educational requirements during the admission process. Verification can happen at any stage. These educational qualification requirements matter a lot for anyone planning to take the CAT 2025.

## CAT **Eligibility Criteria 2025 For Final Year and Backlog Students**

Students in their final year of a bachelor’s degree and those with academic backlogs frequently ask about their CAT examination eligibility. The CAT eligibility criteria welcome these candidates under specific conditions.

### **Can final year students apply?**

Students can apply for CAT 2025 during their final year. The examination allows participation from students who are completing their bachelor’s degree or waiting for their results. Candidates receive provisional admission until they complete their degree requirements.

Final year applicants must meet these requirements:

- Complete their bachelor’s degree by June 2026
- Score the minimum required percentage (50% for the general category and 45% for reserved categories)
- Present proper documentation from their institution during the admission process

This opportunity helps candidates smoothly transition from undergraduate studies to management education without losing an academic year.

### **Backlog rules and conditional admission**

The CAT 2025 welcomes students with academic backlogs, which comes as good news for many aspirants. CAT eligibility criteria and IIM admission policies allow candidates with backlogs, but they need to follow certain conditions:

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Backlog Situation** | **Eligibility Status** | **Requirements** |
| Currently pursuing supplementary exams | Eligible to apply | Must clear all backlogs before admission |
| Awaiting backlog results | Eligible with provisional status | Certificate from the institution stating supplementary exams have been taken |
| Completed degree with backlogs | Eligible if the overall percentage meets criteria | Minimum 50% (45% for reserved categories) despite backlogs |

Students with backlogs can get provisional admission. Their admission becomes confirmed after they submit proof of clearing all backlogs.

### **Required documents for provisional CAT 2025 eligibility**

Candidates need specific documents for provisional admission:

Final year students should submit a certificate from their Principal/Head of Department/Registrar/Director. This certificate must state:

- The candidate’s final year status or result-awaiting status
- Achievement of minimum 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD) based on the latest available grades
- Expected completion of bachelor’s degree requirements by the specified date

Selected students must submit an additional certificate by June 30, 2026, confirming their degree completion. They can submit their final degree certificate and marksheet until December 31, 2026.

The institution will automatically cancel provisional admission if students fail to submit the required documents by the deadlines. Students should stay in touch with their admitting institution since verification can happen anytime.

## **CAT Eligibility Criteria 2025: Reservation Criteria and Category Guidelines**

CAT 2025’s reservation policy adheres to government guidelines that promote diverse representation in management institutions.

### CAT 2025 **Eligibility documents required for reserved categories**

Reserved category candidates must submit specific documentation. SC/ST applicants need **certificates that specify their caste/tribe name, category identification, district, and state of residence, along with government approval status**. The registration process requires candidates to upload these certificates, present them during interviews, and submit them while joining programmes. Missing or improper documentation leads to registration rejection or disqualification.

### **PwD category: Accepted disabilities under RPwD Act**

The Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016 reserves 5% seats for candidates with at least 40% standard disability. Qualifying disabilities include:

- Blindness and low vision
- Deaf and hard of hearing
- Locomotor disabilities (including cerebral palsy, leprosy cured, dwarfism, acid attack victims, and muscular dystrophy)
- Autism, intellectual disability, specific learning disability, mental illness
- Multiple disabilities from these categories

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is there an age limit for taking the CAT 2025 exam?

No, there is no minimum or maximum age limit for CAT 2025. Candidates of any age can appear for the exam as long as they meet the educational requirements.

### 2. What are the reservation quotas for different categories in CAT 2025?

The reservation quotas are as follows: 15% for SC, 7.5% for ST, 27% for NC-OBC, up to 10% for EWS, and 5% for PwD candidates.

### 3. Can final year students apply for CAT 2025?

Yes, final year students can apply for CAT 2025. They must complete their bachelor’s degree with the required percentage by June 2026 and provide necessary documentation.

### 4. What are the minimum marks required for CAT 2025 eligibility?

The minimum marks required for the CAT eligibility criteria 2025 are 50% marks in their bachelor’s degree for General, EWS, and OBC category candidates. SC, ST, and PwD category candidates require at least 45% marks.

### 5. Is work experience mandatory for the CAT eligibility criteria 2025?

No, work experience is not mandatory for CAT eligibility criteria 2025. However, it is valued during the admission process, typically carrying 5-10% weightage at premier institutions.
