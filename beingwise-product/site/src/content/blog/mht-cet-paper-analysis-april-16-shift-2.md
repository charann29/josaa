## Subject-wise April 16 Shift 2 MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis

The MHT CET Question paper for April 16, second shift, was balanced overall, but Maths increased the difficulty level due to its lengthiness, while Chemistry helped in boosting scores. Let’s now look at the Subject-wise April 16 Shift 2 MHT CET Paper Analysis

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Mathematics Section

Maths was deemed the most challenging subject during the entire shift, with questions reported **to be JEE-level tough**. Maths was the most time-consuming portion, particularly this shift a little more difficult than the previous ones, owing to the calculations involved. Have a look at the main areas from which questions have been asked in this shift

### April 16 Shift 2 Physics Paper Analysis

In the April 16 Shift 2, Physics was generally regarded as moderate to easy. Some of the key and recurring topics highlighted in the sources include:

- **Optics:** YDSE is one of the very significant topics, with questions based on its fringes and slits. A statement-type question involving the topic of microscopes from the class 11 syllabus also came up
- **Oscillations and Fluids:** Questions based on simple pendulum time period, tension in strings, and phase differences in simple harmonic motion involving displacements, velocities, and accelerations. Capillary rise and fall, and isothermal processes, also made their presence felt in fluids
- **Electrodynamics and Magnetism:** LCR circuit analysis and logic gate operations (OR/NOT output)
- **Moment of inertia** of cylindrical bodies, magnetic field of a solenoid, and paramagnetism were also tested
- **Thermodynamics:** Work done in gases, adiabatic changes, and temperature variations in ideal gases

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

As per the MHT CET Paper Analysis of April 16th Shift 2, the subject chemistry was found to be easy by most students. The majority of the questions asked in this section were theoretical in nature. This is the section that takes the least amount of time to complete. The key topics include

- **Physical Chemistry:** Topics included Thermodynamics, Chemical Kinetics, Electrochemistry, and Solutions
- **Organic Chemistry:** Strong focus on Named Reactions, Hydrocarbons, and basic reaction mechanisms
- **Inorganic Chemistry:** Major topics included  p-Block, d-Block, and Coordination Compounds

## MHT CET Counselling Details

MHT CET 2026 counseling, also referred to as the Centralized Admission Process (CAP), will be done online through the State CET Cell, Maharashtra. There are three rounds in total for getting admitted to the engineering and pharmacy programs. Students must log in to the website to make registration and choice filling. Take key highlights of MHT CET Counselling 2026

- **Registration & Fee Pay:** To participate in the process, students have to make online registration along with paying the counselling fee – ₹1,000 for general category candidates and ₹800 for reserved category candidates.
- **Choice Filling (CAP Round):** Candidates have to fill in the choices separately in each round.
- **MHT CET Merit List:** There will be an initial release of a provisional merit list. Later, there would be the final merit list (released in July).
- **Seat Allocation process:** All seats will be allocated depending upon CET percentile, merit list ranking, preferences, etc.
- **Cutoff of Top Colleges**: Generally, getting admission into premier colleges like COEP or VJTI requires students to score 90–95+ percentiles. For securing a seat at a desired institute, students with 70-80+ percentiles need to use college predictor services.
