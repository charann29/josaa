## MHT CET PCM Exam Analysis: Student Reviews For April 23rd Shift 1 and Shift 2

MHT CET 2025 exam, conducted on April 23, based on student reviews and experts’ analysis, can be seen to show fluctuating difficulty levels over both shifts.

### **MHT CET PCM** **Shift 1:**

**Mathematics:** Students felt that MHT CET paper pattern of Mathematics was heavy on complexity and calculations, with significant variances coming from questions that required students to think further about Mathematics, like Vectors, 3D Geometry, and Integration.â€‹

**Physics:** The MHT CET question paper for Physics was moderately difficult. It comprised a combination of conceptual and numerical questions on current electricity and mechanics.

**Chemistry:** The MHT CET paper pattern of Chemistry was considered the easiest of the three subjects. Questions that were directly copied from NCERT and HSC textbooks came from topics such as Redox Reactions, Coordination Compounds, and Reactions of Carbonyl Compounds.

### **MHT CET PCM** **Shift 2:**

**Mathematics:** The difficulty remained as high for MHT CET question paper of Maths, with similar multiples connected in lengthy and complex questions coming from Differentiation, Integration and Trigonometry.â€‹

## Conclusion

The MHT CET 2025 exam for the PCM group, held on April 23, reflected a fair representation of difficulty across the two shifts. Chemistry was relatively less difficult and a regular scoring opportunity; Physics and Mathematics were both a bit tougher, and were mixed.

In **Shift 1**, Physics and Mathematics were of moderate difficulty, requiring solid conceptual understanding and proper time management, whereas Chemistry represented a more reasonable experience.

In **Shift 2**, Physics was slightly easier than Shift 1, and Mathematics remained the hardest portion, with difficult questions requiring a comprehensive understanding of the subject. Candidates looking to do well in the MHT CET exam for 2025 should look to strengthen their conceptual knowledge, especially for Physics and Mathematics, but also continue to improve on Chemistry. Candidates will also need to be effective in managing their time to help maximize their score for the tough sections.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. ****What was the overall difficulty level of the MHT CET 2025 exam held on April 23?****

The overall difficulty of MHT CET question paper was moderate, Mathematics was the most difficult section, followed by Physics. The easiest subject in both shifts was Chemistry.

### 2. ********How can students tackle the Mathematics section in MHT CET 2025?********

The MHT CET question paper of Mathematics section can be tough because of calculations, and the problems can be more application-based. So, students should work on their speed to solve the problems and be well prepared on topics like Vectors, 3D Geometry, and Calculus to do good in exams.

### 3. ************Were there any questions outside the syllabus in the MHT CET exam?************

No, all the questions in the exam are based on the MHT CET syllabus from Class 11 and Class 12 topics that were based on the curriculum of the Maharashtra State Board.

### 4. ************Which chapters carried the most weight in Physics in MHT CET?************

Mechanics, Modern Physics, and Current Electricity had the most weightage in Physics in MHT CET. However, Thermodynamics and Optics had far fewer questions.

### 5. **What were the important topics in Chemistry on April 23rd of MHT CET 2025?**

Organic Chemistry and Physical Chemistry had the highest weightage. Alkanes, Biomolecules, and Electrochemistry appeared in both shifts in MHT CET 2025.
