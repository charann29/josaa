### JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Maths Section

Mathematics was the most challenging section in this shift. Many students remarked that while the language of the questions was simple, the actual solving method was difficult and calculating.  Questions were conceptually easy for many students. Take a look at the topics where questions are asked in this shift

### JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

Chemistry was the best-scoring section with many direct questions, especially from the NCERT.

Chemistry was described with a greater emphasis on Physical Chemistry in this shift. Many students reported fewer Inorganic Chemistry questions compared to the morning shift. Biomolecules (particularly queries about insulin and thyroxine) were a recurring topic.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Topic Category** | **Key Topics** |
| Organic Chemistry | GOC, Amines, Biomolecules (NCERT-based) |
| Inorganic Chemistry | Coordination Compounds (Crystal Field Theory, Δ₀ order) |
| Physical Chemistry | Ionic Equilibrium (pH, buffer), Concentration Terms (molarity, molality, mole fraction) |

### JEE Mains Paper Analysis for Physics Section

**Physics was a more balanced and straightforward subject than mathematics.**  While formula-based, several students believed Physics was “rotated” or confusing. Compared to the morning shift, this course appeared to cover more 12th-grade syllabus information. Students frequently asked questions about Fluid Mechanics, including surface energy and terminal velocity.
