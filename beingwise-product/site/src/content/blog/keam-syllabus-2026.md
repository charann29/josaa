The KEAM syllabus 2026 is one of the most crucial factors to analyse before applying for the KEAM 2026, while the KEAM syllabus 2025 acts as an important reference point for students who are soon going to appear for the Kerala Engineering Architecture Medical (KEAM) entrance exam.

The KEAM 2026 syllabus clearly defines the subjects and topics from which the questions are going to be asked in the exam. Prescribed by the Office of the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE), the KEAM syllabus 2026 follows a well-defined and standardised structure.

The KEAM exam syllabus is based on the Class 11 and 12 curriculum of Kerala Higher Secondary Education and remains aligned with NCERT guidelines followed across India. The KEAM syllabus 2026 for engineering is divided into three major subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Students may refer to the following to go through the complete KEAM syllabus 2026.

## KEAM Syllabus | Detailed Engineering Syllabus 2026

Students can go through the detailed KEAM syllabus 2026 for each subject of the Engineering paper below. Students are provided with the topics and their respective units as well to make things clear.

### Physics:

Check out the KEAM syllabus 2026 for phsyics below:

- **Units & Measurement:** Need for measurement, systems of units, SI units, fundamental and derived units, significant figures, dimensional analysis and its applications.
- **Kinematics:** Motion in a straight line, velocity and acceleration, graphical representation of motion, uniformly accelerated motion, vectors, and projectile motion.
- **Laws of Motion:** Force and inertia, Newton’s laws of motion, momentum and impulse, equilibrium of forces, friction, and circular motion.
- **Work, Energy & Power:** Work done by constant and variable forces, different forms of energy, conservation of energy, and power.
- **Motion of System of Particles & Rigid Body:** Centre of mass, torque, angular momentum, rotational motion, and moments of inertia.
- **Gravitation:** Universal law of gravitation, Kepler’s laws, gravitational field and potential, escape velocity, and motion of satellites.
- **Properties of Matter:** Stress and strain, elastic behaviour of materials, elastic moduli, pressure in fluids, viscosity, and surface tension.
- **Heat & Thermal Physics:** Temperature and heat, modes of heat transfer, thermal expansion, calorimetry, and laws of thermodynamics.
- **Oscillations & Waves:** Simple harmonic motion and basic characteristics of wave motion.
- **Electrostatics:** Electric charges, electric field and potential, and basic concepts of capacitors.
- **Current Electricity:** Electric current, resistance, Ohm’s law, and basic electrical circuit concepts.
- **Magnetism & Electromagnetic Induction:** Magnetic effects of electric current, electromagnetic induction, and basic concepts of alternating current.
- **Optics:** Laws of reflection and refraction, and basic concepts of mirrors and lenses.
- **Modern Physics:** Dual nature of matter, atoms and nuclei, and basic electronic devices.

### Mathematics:

Check out the KEAM Maths syllabus below:

- **Algebra:** Sets, relations and functions, complex numbers, quadratic equations, sequences and series, permutations and combinations, and the binomial theorem.
- **Matrices & Determinants:** Matrix operations, determinants, inverse of a matrix, and solving systems of linear equations (up to 3×3).
- **Linear Inequalities:** Solutions of linear inequalities and their graphical representation.
- **Coordinate Geometry:** Straight lines, distance formula, and section formula.
- **Conic Sections:** Basic concepts of circle, parabola, ellipse, and hyperbola.
- **Vectors:** Vector addition, scalar and vector products, and their applications.
- **Three-Dimensional Geometry:** Coordinates in three dimensions, distances, equations of lines and planes, and skew lines.
- **Trigonometry:** Trigonometric functions, identities, and inverse trigonometric functions.
- **Functions, Limits & Continuity:** Concept of functions, limits, and continuity of standard functions.
- **Differentiation & Applications:** Derivative concepts, rates of change, and applications such as maxima and minima.
- **Integration:** Indefinite and definite integrals, area under curves, and methods of substitution and integration by parts.
- **Differential Equations:** First-order differential equations and separable equations.
- **Linear Programming:** Constraints, objective functions, and graphical solutions.
- **Statistics & Probability:** Mean deviation, variance, standard deviation, basic probability rules, and Bayes’ theorem.

### Chemistry:

Check out the KEAM Chemistry syllabus below:

- **Basic Concepts of Chemistry:** Importance and scope of chemistry, laws of chemical combination, atomic and molecular masses, mole concept, and stoichiometry.
- **Structure of an Atom:** Subatomic particles, atomic models, quantum numbers, and electronic configuration of atoms.
- **Classification of Elements & Periodicity:** Development of the periodic table and periodic trends in physical and chemical properties.
- **Chemical Bonding & Molecular Structure:** Ionic and covalent bonding, Lewis structures, and concepts of hybridisation.
- **Thermodynamics:** System and surroundings, heat and work, laws of thermodynamics, and enthalpy changes.
- **Equilibrium:** Chemical and physical equilibrium, Le Chatelier’s principle, ionic equilibrium, and buffer solutions.
- **Redox Reactions & Electrochemistry:** Oxidation–reduction reactions, electrochemical cells, Faraday’s laws, and basic concepts of corrosion.
- **Solutions:** Types of solutions, concentration terms, and colligative properties.
- **Chemical Kinetics:** Rate of reaction, order and molecularity, and collision theory.
- **D-Block & F-Block Elements:** Basic properties of transition metals, lanthanoids, and actinoids.
- **Coordination Compounds:** Ligands, coordination number, and basic rules of nomenclature.
- **Organic Chemistry – Fundamentals:** Basic principles of organic chemistry and classification of organic reactions.
- **Hydrocarbons:** Structure, properties, and reactions of alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes.
- **Haloalkanes & Haloarenes:** Structure and reactions of haloalkanes and haloarenes.
- **Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers:** Properties and reactions of alcohols, phenols, and ethers.
- **Aldehydes, Ketones & Carboxylic Acids:** Chemistry of carbonyl compounds and carboxylic acids.
- **Organic Compounds with Nitrogen:** Amines and other nitrogen-containing organic compounds.
- **Biomolecules:** Basic concepts of carbohydrates, proteins, and vitamins.

## KEAM Exam Pattern 2026

Along with the KEAM syllabus 2026, applicants must check out the latest KEAM exam pattern 2026 to stay updated regarding the question paper design and prepare accordingly. Refer to the following to know the complete KEAM 2026 exam pattern.

- Exam mode: Pen and paper (offline)
- Total papers: 2
- Paper 1: Physics and Chemistry
- Paper 2: Mathematics
- Question type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
- Total questions: 180
- Marks per question: 4
- Negative marking: No
- Total marks: 480
- Duration: 2 hours and 30 minutes for each paper
- Medium of examination: English and Malayalam
