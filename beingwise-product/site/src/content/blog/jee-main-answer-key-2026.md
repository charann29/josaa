### Common Mistakes Students Make While Using JEE Main Answer Key 2026

While doing the score calculation with the NTA Answer key JEE Mains 2026, Many students make small mistakes in evaluation, which can lead to wrong score estimates and false expectations. You should avoid these common errors to get an accurate idea of your performance.

1. **Using the Wrong Shift Answer Key:** JEE Main is conducted in multiple shifts. Using an NTA JEE Mains 2026 answer key from a different date or shift gives completely wrong results.
2. **Ignoring the JEE Main Response Sheet:** NTA releases the JEE Main response sheet along with the answer key. Some students only check the answer key and forget to match it with their response sheet, which shows the answers they actually selected.
3. **Calculating Marks.** When evaluating scores, ensure you are aware of the most recent NTA marking scheme. Failure to adhere to the official marking scheme can lead to inaccurate calculations.
4. **Not Considering Dropped Questions:** NTA may remove questions or award additional points. Students who neglect this frequently calculate lower or higher scores than the actual outcome.
5. **Missing the Objection Window:** If you discover an error but do not dispute it within the allotted period, you will lose the opportunity to have it repaired. It should be noted that the JEE Main Answer Key Challenge is open until February 6, 2026.
6. **Comparing scores with other shifts:** The JEE Main Session 1 takes place over numerous days and shifts, with varying difficulty levels. Comparing raw scores without normalisation adds unnecessary stress.

## JEE Mains Answer Key Challenge 2026

### Step-by-Step JEE Main Answer Key Challenge Process

Students may follow the step-by-step challenge process for the JEE Main answer key 2026 session 1, as discussed below:-

1. Visit the official website of NTA at <https://jeemain.nta.nic.in/>
2. Now, click on the  ‘**Challenge (s) regarding Answer Key JEE Mains 2026**‘.
3. Log in with your Application Number and Password, enter the Captcha Code as displayed, and submit.
4. Click ‘Challenge (s) regarding Answer Key’.
5. You will see the Question IDs in the order below sequential order for JEE (Main) – 2026 Session 1 Paper 1: B.E. /B. Tech 25+25+25 Questions Mathematics/Physics/Chemistry
6. The ID next to the question under the column ‘Correct Option’ stands for the most appropriate Answer Key by NTA.
7. If you wish to challenge this option, you may use any one or more of the Option IDs given in the next four columns by clicking the check box.
8. You may upload supporting documents, for which you can select ‘Choose File and Upload (all documents to be put in a single PDF file).
9. After clicking your desired option, for Mathematics / Physics / Chemistry for Paper 1 (B.E./B. Tech.), scroll down and ‘Save your claim’ and move on to the next screen.
10. You will see a display of all the Option IDs you have challenged.
11. Click on ‘Save your claim and Pay Fee finally’.
12. Select the Mode of Payment and pay a non-refundable processing fee INR 200/- for each question challenged. Make payment through Debit / Credit Card / Net Banking.

Students will not be told individually whether their objections have been accepted or not. The final results and NTA scores will be calculated using the updated JEE Mains final answer key.
