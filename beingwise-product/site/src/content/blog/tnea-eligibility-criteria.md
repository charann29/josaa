Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA) counselling offers a merit-based pathway for B.Tech aspirants to secure admission across top engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu. To apply for TNEA, students must have a clear understanding of TNEA eligibility criteria and regulations. Candidates need to satisfy specific age, academic, nativity, and category criteria set by the Directorate of Technical Education (DoTE). ## TNEA Eligibility 2026 – Nativity Criteria

- Students must have studied in Tamil Nadu
- A nativity certificate is not required for students who have studied classes 8 to 12 in Tamil Nadu.
- Candidates from other states can be eligible if they submit a valid, digitally signed nativity certificate issued by a competent authority. Further, students from other states can watch the following video to understand more about their eligibility criteria:

[Other State Students Engineering Admission in TN|Anna University440 Clg & Deemed University|English|](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5sWu22RLhA#:~:text=other%20state%20students%20in%20India%20can%20join,%23tnea2025%20%23tnea%20%23EngineeringAdmissions%20%23AnnaUniversityCounseling%20%23TamilNaduEngineering%20%23DeemedUniversityAdmissions%20%23CareerGuidance)

- Children of Central or State Government employees working for 5 or more years are eligible. They need a certificate issued by the employer mentioning the term of service.
- Students whose parents or guardians are working under any public sector organization or government-approved institutions are eligible as well, if the required documents are provided.
- Candidates whose parents are All India Service Officers working in Tamil Nadu are also eligible and do not require any nativity certificate.
- Applicants who are Sri Lankan Tamil refugees and have pursued education in Tamil Nadu are also eligible. Students need to provide refugee identification documents and apply under the OC category.

## TNEA Eligibility 2026 – Academic Criteria

Students applying for TNEA must have passed the 10+2 level examination or any equivalent exam with compulsory subjects of Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry. Vocational stream candidates who have studied Engineering and Technology are also eligible. Applicants must obtain the following minimum aggregate marks across different categories:

| Student Category | Minimum Aggregate Marks in PCM | Minimum Aggregate Marks (Related Subjects + Vocational Theory & Practical) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General Category (OC) | 45% | 45% |
| Backward Class (BC), including BC Muslims | 40% | 40% |
| Most Backward Class (MBC) & Denotified Communities (DNC) | 40% | 40% |
| SC/SCA/ST | 40% | 40% |

## TNEA Eligibility 2026 – Age Criteria

As per the TNEA Eligibility Criteria, students need to fulfill the following age criteria:

- Students pursuing general engineering courses under TNEA do not have an age limit to apply.
- Candidates applying for Marine Engineering should not exceed the age of 25 years as of admission.

## TNEA Eligibility Criteria For Marine Engineering Applicants

- Candidates must have passed the 10+2 level examination with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and English as compulsory subjects.
- Must have obtained a score of a minimum 60% in PCM and 50% in aggregate.
- Students must not be more than 25 years old.
- Students must have a minimum height of 157 cm.
- Students must have a minimum weight of 48 Kg
- Students also must have normal colour vision.
- In terms of fitness requirements, candidates must be declared physically fit with a legitimate, signed physical fitness certificate by a medical officer authorized by the Director General of Shipping.

## TNEA Eligibility Criteria For Mining Engineering Female Applicants

- Female applicants should not be employed in any part of a mine below ground. Only the mining areas above ground are allowed, except between 6 AM and 7 PM.
- According to Coal Mines Regulation 1957, any adolescent or other woman cannot descend or ascend a shaft in a cage or other means of conveyance unless accompanied by one or more adult males.
- Female applicants need to fulfill the academic criteria of Anna University.

## TNEA Eligibility 2026 – Reservation Criteria

Students applying for TNEA are provided seats as per the following reservation criteria:

| Category | Reservation % |
| --- | --- |
| Open Competition | 31% |
| Backward Class (BC) | 26.5% |
| Backward Class Muslim (BCM) | 3.5% |
| Most Backward Class (MBC) & Denotified Communities (DNC) | 20% |
| Scheduled Caste (SC) | 15% |
| Scheduled Caste Arunthiyars (SCA) | 3% |
| Scheduled Tribes (ST) | 1% |

## Conclusion

In conclusion, TNEA eligibility criteria mainly stress having completed intermediate education level with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as mandatory subjects. They need to have a minimum of 45% and no upper age limit to apply. Students under the reserved category can enjoy relaxations, while the NRI/OCI category students may have different requirements. Students are instructed to verify the latest guidelines and eligibility criteria from the official website of [TNEA](https://www.tneaonline.org/), as there may be changes from time to time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Ques. What is the TNEA eligibility for other state students?**

Ans. Students from other states can apply under the Open Competition category, provided they meet the required academic eligibility criteria.

**2. Ques. What is the TNEA eligibility for CBSE students?**

Ans. CBSE students are eligible for TNEA if they meet the general academic requirements and nativity criteria set by the Directorate of Technical Education (DoTE).

**3. Ques. How to calculate TNEA eligibility in Tamil Nadu?**

Ans. Students must score at least 45% in Class 12. The cutoff is calculated using the formula: Mathematics marks + (Physics marks / 2) + (Chemistry marks / 2).

**4. Ques. Is there any TNEA eligibility age limit?**

Ans. There is no upper age limit for general engineering courses. However, for marine engineering, the maximum age limit is 25 years.

**5. Ques. What is the TNEA eligibility cutoff for BC category?**

Ans. BC category candidates must secure a minimum of 40% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) in their 10+2 examinations.

**6. Ques. What are the TNEA participating colleges?**

Ans. Some of the top participating colleges include PSG College of Technology, Thiagarajar College of Engineering, College of Engineering Guindy (Anna University), Madras Institute of Technology, and Kumaraguru College of Technology.
