## **Reasons Behind JEE Main 2025 Low Registrations**

The number of candidates registered so far for JEE Mains 2025 is 5.10 lakh, which seems low compared to the previous years. However, people wondering about the JEE Main 2025 low registrations have to consider that there is still time for closing registrations, and the count can increase in the meantime.

The final numbers after November 22, 2024, can clarify the total number of candidates registered, so it is important to wait for the official announcement from NTA before concluding the registration count.

Experts believe the primary reason for the lowest registrations is the technical challenges faced by candidates, including the [**name-mismatch issue**](/blog/jee-main/jee-main-2025-updates/) between the Aadhaar card and the class 10 certificate. OBC and EWS students are also facing problems as they are required to obtain the Category Certificate ID, Date of Issue and the Name of the Issuing Officer.

## **Registration Challenges and Issues Faced by JEE Main 2025 Candidates**

Many students attempting to register for JEE Main 2025 have encountered a range of challenges, contributing to the historically low early application numbers. These challenges include:

- **Technical Difficulties:** Numerous applicants have reported issues with the online registration portal, including slow website performance, form submission errors, and difficulties uploading documents.
- **Application Hurdles:** The introduction of new requirements, especially for OBC and EWS candidates, has led to confusion. For the first time, students must provide the ID, issue date, and the issuing officer’s name for their category certificate. Many have struggled to access or update these details, causing delays and incomplete applications.
- **Verification System Problems:** A major issue has been verification errors, particularly related to identity checks and document uploads. Name mismatches between Aadhaar cards and Class 10 certificates have led to frequent errors, preventing candidates from advancing in the process.
- **Lack of Support and Guidance:** Several competitor sources note that the absence of a detailed FAQ or timely support from the NTA has left students feeling unsupported. Many are still awaiting clear resolutions to their problems, adding to frustration and uncertainty.
- **Confusion Over Updated Category Codes:** The NTA’s introduction of new category codes has left many applicants unsure how to proceed, further complicating the process.

These registration issues have resulted in significant delays and, in some cases, have discouraged students from completing their applications. As a result, the early registration numbers for JEE Main 2025 are much lower than in previous years.

## **Official Responses and Solutions from NTA**

In response to the widespread registration and verification issues for JEE Main 2025, the NTA) has taken several steps to assist applicants and address common challenges:

- **Advisory on Aadhaar Verification:** The NTA has issued clear guidance for candidates experiencing name-mismatch errors between their Aadhaar card and Class 10 certificate. Applicants are now advised to ensure that the details entered during registration match their Aadhaar card exactly to minimise verification errors.
- **Registration System Updates:** To streamline the process, the NTA has implemented minor amendments to the registration portal. This includes adjustments to help candidates bypass specific pop-up errors and proceed with authentication, especially when confirming names as per Aadhaar.
- **Encouragement to Double-Check Documents:** The NTA and various experts have urged students to carefully review all documentation, particularly category certificates (for OBC and EWS students), Aadhaar details, and personal information, before submission. This reduces the likelihood of form rejection or delay.
- **Call for Comprehensive Support:** While some improvements have been made, many applicants and education experts have highlighted the absence of a detailed FAQ or support document from the NTA this year. There have been calls for the NTA to release more robust guidance and real-time support to assist further students facing registration hurdles.
- **Recommendations for Applicants:** In addition to official advisories, applicants are recommended to:
  - Use the exact name format as on their Aadhaar card and educational certificates.
  - Prepare all required details for category certificates, including ID, issue date, and issuing officer’s name, in advance.
  - Reach out to the NTA helpline or official channels for unresolved technical or verification issues.

These steps and recommendations are designed to reduce errors, speed up the application process, and ensure that more students can complete their registration successfully before the deadline.
