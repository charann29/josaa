## WBJEE Eligibility Criteria 2026

Before submitting the WBJEE application form, candidates should carefully check the eligibility requirements specified by the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board (WBJEEB). These conditions determine whether a candidate is eligible to appear for the exam and participate in the admission process for engineering and pharmacy courses offered by institutes in West Bengal.

### **Academic Criteria**

Candidates seeking admission to B.Tech programs through WBJEE must have completed Class 12 or an equivalent examination from a recognized board. The qualifying examination must include Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects. Along with these two subjects, candidates should have studied any one of the following subjects:

- Chemistry
- Biotechnology
- Biology
- Computer Science
- Computer Application
- Technical Vocational Subject

To be eligible for admission, candidates must secure at least 45% aggregate marks in Physics, Mathematics, and the third subject combined. For candidates belonging to reserved categories such as SC, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B, and PwD, the minimum required aggregate is 40%. Candidates must also pass the English subject in the qualifying examination with a minimum of 30% marks.

### **Nationality**

Only Indian citizens are allowed to apply for the WBJEE examination.

### **WBJEE Age Limit**

Candidates appearing for WBJEE must be at least 17 years old during the year of admission. For most courses offered through the exam, there is no upper age limit. However, applicants interested in Marine Engineering courses must meet specific age conditions. For this program, candidates must be between 17 and 25 years of age as of December 31, 2026.

### **Recognition of Qualifying Board**

The Class 12 or equivalent examination must be conducted by a board recognized by the Central Government or the respective State Government. Only candidates meeting this condition will be considered eligible for admission.

## WBJEE 2026 Preparation Tips

Preparing for **WBJEE 2026** requires a focused approach because the exam tests concepts from **Class 11 and Class 12 Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics**. Since the exam includes two papers held on the same day, candidates must balance conceptual understanding with speed and accuracy. A good preparation strategy should combine **concept clarity, regular practice, and mock tests**. Instead of studying randomly, candidates should start by understanding the exam structure, identifying important topics, and planning their study schedule accordingly.

### Start by Understanding the WBJEE Exam Structure

Before beginning preparation, candidates should clearly understand how the exam is conducted. WBJEE consists of **two papers**:

- **Paper 1:** Mathematics
- **Paper 2:** Physics and Chemistry

Mathematics carries the highest weightage with **75 questions**, while Physics and Chemistry include **40 questions each**. Since Mathematics alone accounts for nearly **half of the total marks**, students should devote sufficient time to practicing numerical problems and improving problem-solving speed. Reviewing the **exam pattern, syllabus, and previous year question papers** will help candidates identify frequently asked topics and focus on relevant study material.

### Focus on Important Topics First

While preparing for WBJEE, it is helpful to begin with chapters that appear frequently in the exam. Covering these topics early can improve scoring potential and build confidence.

**Important Mathematics Topics**

- Calculus
- Coordinate Geometry
- Algebra
- Trigonometry
- Vectors and Probability
- Differential Equations

**Important Physics Topics**

- Current Electricity
- Magnetic Effects of Current
- Electrostatics
- Heat and Thermodynamics
- Modern Physics
- Mechanics and Waves

**Important Chemistry Topics**

*Physical Chemistry*

- Chemical Kinetics
- Thermodynamics
- Chemical Equilibrium
- Redox Reactions

*Organic Chemistry*

- Aldehydes and Ketones
- Carboxylic Acids
- Amines
- Aromatic Compounds

*Inorganic Chemistry*

- p-Block Elements
- d- and f-Block Elements
- Coordination Compounds
- Metallurgy

Studying these chapters first can help candidates cover a large portion of the exam syllabus efficiently.

### Suggested 3-Month Study Plan for WBJEE Exam 2026

A structured preparation plan can make studying more manageable and productive. Candidates preparing within three months can divide their preparation into three phases.

**Phase 1: Concept Building (First 5 Weeks)**During the initial phase, focus on understanding fundamental concepts and completing the core syllabus.

- Study Mathematics for **3–4 hours daily** and solve practice problems regularly.
- Spend **2–3 hours on Physics**, focusing on theory and numerical questions.
- Allocate **2–3 hours for Chemistry**, concentrating on reactions, formulas, and conceptual clarity.
- Attempt one mock test each week and analyze mistakes carefully.

**Phase 2: Practice and Revision (Next 4 Weeks)**Once the syllabus is mostly covered, shift the focus toward revision and problem-solving.

- Revise important chapters regularly from [WBJEE Previous Year Question Papers](/blog/wbjee/wbjee-previous-year-question-papers/).
- Attempt **sectional mock tests** for individual subjects.
- Take **2–3 full-length mock tests each week**.
- Identify weak topics and revise them thoroughly.

**Phase 3: Final Revision (Last 2–3 Weeks)**The final phase should be focused on revision and exam practice.

- Take **full-length mock tests regularly**.
- Revise formulas, key reactions, and important concepts.
- Avoid starting completely new topics during this stage.
- Focus on improving speed and accuracy.

### Maintain a Consistent Study Routine

Consistency is more important than studying for very long hours occasionally. Ideally, candidates should aim to study **8–10 hours daily**, dividing time across all three subjects. Regular revision, practice questions, and mock tests will help improve accuracy and confidence before the exam. By following a disciplined study routine and focusing on important topics, candidates can significantly improve their chances of securing a **good rank in WBJEE 2026**.

## WBJEE Answer Key 2026

**WBJEE answer key** will be released online by the **West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board** after the exam is conducted. Initially, the board will publish a **provisional answer key**. Candidates can download the answer key by logging in to the official website using their **application number and password**. If any discrepancies are found, candidates will have the option to **raise objections** against the provisional answer key within the specified time period. To challenge a question, candidates must submit supporting documents and pay an **objection fee of ₹500 per question**. After reviewing all the challenges, the authorities will release the **final WBJEE answer key**. The results will be prepared based on this final answer key, and no further objections will be accepted once it is published.

### Steps to Challenge WBJEE Answer Key

Candidates who wish to raise objections against the provisional answer key can follow the steps below:

1. Visit the official website at **wbjeeb.nic.in/wbjee**.
2. Click on the **WBJEE answer key** link available on the homepage.
3. Log in using the **application number and password**.
4. Select the question(s) you want to challenge.
5. Upload supporting documents and pay the **required objection fee online**.
6. Submit the objection before the deadline.

## WBJEE 2026 Counselling

WBJEE counselling process will be conducted online after the declaration of the exam results. Candidates who qualify in the entrance exam will be eligible to participate in counselling for admission to participating engineering and pharmacy colleges in West Bengal.  During counselling, candidates must log in to the official portal and register for the process. ### **WBJEE Counselling Process**

The counselling process generally includes the following stages:

1. **Online Registration:** Qualified candidates need to register for WBJEE counselling on the official website.
2. **Choice Filling:** Candidates must select and arrange their preferred colleges and courses.
3. **Seat Allotment:** Seats are allocated based on rank, preferences, and availability of seats.
4. **Document Verification:** Candidates must verify their documents as required by the authorities.
5. **Admission Confirmation:** After verification, candidates must confirm their admission and report to the allotted institute within the specified time.
