## What happens after clearing JEE Main

### 1. Qualifying for JEE Advanced (For IIT Admissions)

- The top **2,50,000** candidates (as per category-wise cutoffs) become eligible to appear for **JEE Advanced 2025**.
- Candidates need to **register separately** for JEE Advanced on the official website.

### 2**.** Admission to NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs (Based on JEE Main Rank)

- Candidates who clear JEE Main with a good rank can apply for admission into NITs (National Institutes of Technology), IIITs (Indian Institutes of Information Technology), and GFTIs (Government-Funded Technical Institutes).
- Admission is granted through **JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) Counselling**.
- **JoSAA releases cutoff ranks** for different categories and institutes on the official website

### 3. JoSAA Counselling Process

- **Registration & Choice Filling**: Candidates register on the JoSAA portal and fill in their preferred institutes and courses.
- **Seat Allocation Rounds**: Multiple rounds of seat allotment take place based on **JEE Main rank, category, and seat availability**.
- **Seat Acceptance & Reporting**: Candidates allotted seats must **accept the seat, pay the fee, and report to the allotted institute** for document verification.

### **4. CSAB Counselling (For Vacant Seats in NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs)**

- If seats remain vacant after JoSAA rounds, they are **filled through the CSAB (Central Seat Allocation Board) Counselling**.

### **5. Admission to State Engineering Colleges**

- Many **state-level engineering colleges** accept JEE Main scores for admission, including those in **Madhya Pradesh, Haryana, Rajasthan, and others**.
- Candidates need to apply through their **respective state counselling portals**.
