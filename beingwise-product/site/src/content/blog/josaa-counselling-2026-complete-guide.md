Your JEE rank is done. Now comes the part that actually decides your college: **choice filling**. Two students with the *same* rank can end up in very different colleges — purely based on how they ordered their list and which round-by-round decisions they made. And once you lock, there is no undo.

This is the complete, plain-English guide to JoSAA 2026. Use it as your map; each section links to a deeper guide on that step.

> **The one rule that matters most:** JoSAA gives you the *highest* choice in your list that you qualify for. So your list must be ordered by **what you want**, top to bottom — never by your rank, never college-by-college.

## What is JoSAA and who is it for?

JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) allocates seats across **121 institutes** — 23 IITs, 31 NITs, IIEST, 26 IIITs and ~40 GFTIs — using a single, common choice list. If you qualified JEE Main or JEE Advanced, JoSAA is how you get your seat. Leftover NIT+ seats then go to [CSAB special rounds](/blog/csab-special-rounds-2026).

## The JoSAA 2026 timeline

Counselling compresses into a few intense weeks in June–July. The exact dates change every year, so always confirm on josaa.nic.in. The flow is always the same:

1. **Registration & choice filling** — see our [step-by-step registration guide](/blog/josaa-registration-choice-filling-steps).
2. **Mock seat allotment** (1–2 rounds) — a free preview to fix your list. See [mock allotment explained](/blog/josaa-mock-seat-allotment-explained).
3. **Lock your choices** before the deadline.
4. **Round-wise seat allotment** (5–6 rounds).
5. **Accept (Float / Freeze / Slide), pay the Seat Acceptance Fee, report.**
6. **CSAB special rounds** for any remaining seats.

## Step 1 — Know which colleges you can realistically get

Before ordering anything, you need an honest read on what your rank can reach. The trap here is using **averages** or a single year's cutoff. We classify every seat against the **actual closing rank in each of 2023, 2024 and 2025** and tag it Safe / Likely / Dream — because a seat that looks "safe on average" can be a coin-flip in reality.

If you only have a percentile, start with [percentile vs rank in JEE Main](/blog/percentile-vs-rank-jee-main). Your [home-state quota, category and gender pool](/blog/home-state-quota-category-seats) also change what's reachable — get these right or you leave seats on the table.

## Step 2 — Fill your choice list in the right order

This is the step that decides everything. The proven structure is **Dream → Likely → Safe**:

- **Dream** (reaches) go at the very top. They cost you nothing — if you don't get them, JoSAA simply moves down your list.
- **Likely** options in the middle.
- **Safe** options at the bottom, so a bad round never leaves you with no seat.

The full method, with examples, is in [JoSAA choice filling order 2026](/blog/josaa-choice-filling-order-2026). Two common sub-questions: [how many choices should you fill?](/blog/how-many-choices-to-fill-josaa) and the [mistakes that quietly cost students seats](/blog/josaa-choice-filling-mistakes).

A decision almost everyone agonises over sits inside this step: [branch vs college](/blog/branch-vs-college-jee) — a better branch at a lower-ranked NIT, or a "worse" branch at a top one?

## Step 3 — Use mock allotment to fix your list

JoSAA shows you a **mock seat allotment** before locking — a free dry run of what your current list would get you. Most students misread it. Learn [how to read mock allotment and what to change](/blog/josaa-mock-seat-allotment-explained) before the real thing.

## Step 4 — After each result: Float, Freeze or Slide

After every round you must choose one option on your allotted seat. Pick wrong and you can lose an upgrade you were in line for, or risk the seat you have. This is the single most confusing part of counselling — we explain exactly [when to Float, Freeze or Slide](/blog/float-freeze-slide-josaa).

## Step 5 — Documents, fees and reporting

A seat can still be cancelled at verification over a document mismatch. Keep the right papers ready and avoid the common errors with our [documents & reporting checklist](/blog/josaa-documents-reporting-checklist).

## Step 6 — Keep your other options open (and avoid traps)

JoSAA isn't the only door. Track [CSAB special rounds](/blog/csab-special-rounds-2026), state counselling, and private/deemed colleges — but be careful about [private-college deposits you can forfeit](/blog/private-college-deposit-forfeiture).

And during this whole window, you will be targeted. The category is full of [free predictors that sell your number](/blog/free-college-predictor-spam-trust) and outright ["guaranteed seat" and management-quota scams](/blog/guaranteed-seat-scams-josaa). Know the signs.

## The honest bottom line

College counselling is a data and constraint problem, not an opinion problem. With your exact rank, category and quota plus multi-year official cutoffs, the right list is **computable, explainable and safe**. That's exactly what we do — and what this guide teaches you to do yourself.
