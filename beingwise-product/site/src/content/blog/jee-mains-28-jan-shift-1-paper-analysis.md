## Subject-wise JEE Mains 2026 Paper Analysis

JEE Main Analysis 2026 for January 28 shift 1 says that the overall shift 2 exam was moderate. Mathematics was difficult, whereas chemistry and physics were modest. The subject-wise JEE Main 2026 January 28 shift 1 question paper analysis is provided here.

### JEE Main Chemistry Analysis

In contrast to the common idea that chemistry can be completed in 40 minutes, this paper was unexpectedly demanding and highly calculative.

- **Difficulty Level:** Moderate
- JEE Mains Chemistry Questions from topics like Coordination compounds, the d-block, and zero error.

### JEE Main Physics Analysis

The physics section was on the easier side, with approximately 70% easy questions. However, it remained demanding for students with weaker foundations in the subject.

- **Difficulty level:** Moderate but Lengthy
- **High Weightage Topics in JEE Mains Physics:** In today’s paper, the JEE Mains Physics Questions asked fromRay optics, electrostatics, semiconductors, SHM, current electricity, rotation, moment of inertia, fluid mechanics, Potentiometer, vernier calliper, and thermodynamics.

### JEE Mains Maths Analysis

The maths section of the JEE Mains paper was considered very lengthy and featured new types of problems. Starting with math may have caused students to face issues completing the rest of the paper.

- **Difficulty level:** Difficult and Lengthy
- **Key Topics**: In today’s JEE Main question paper, the JEE Mains Maths Questions are asked from: 3d and vectors, ellipses, circles, AP, GP, Matrix, differential equations, limit, quadratic equation, sets & relation and integration.
