## CAT Previous Year Question Papers: Importance of Practicing

CAT 2025 aspirants should recognize the advantages of practicing the CAT last year’s papers, sample papers, and mock tests during their [**CAT preparation and exam strategy**](/blog/cat/cat-exam-strategy-2025/):

Previous year CAT question papers help in understanding the marking scheme and the current exam pattern. Solving CAT question papers improves speed and accuracy, aiming for 80-85 percent correctness in CAT 2025. Practicing these papers also aids in gauging the time required for different question types, facilitating exam day planning. Additionally, they reveal which sections may be more time-consuming, allowing candidates to adjust their preparation accordingly. Past CAT papers will also help the candidates improve their RC skills by practicing Reading Comprehension under timed conditions.

### When to Start Practicing CAT Previous Year Question Papers?

The best time to begin solving the CAT previous year question papers is about a month before the exam. Till then, all the aspirants can understand the syllabus and practice the concepts. Candidates are also advised to attempt enough mocks to understand the exam’s nature. This is when you should practice the actual previous year CAT question papers so your memory remains fresh for CAT 2025.

If you have already taken the CAT once, you can practice questions and sample papers alongside covering the syllabus, as your concepts and fundamentals should already be clear. After completing certain chapters, attempt a CAT sample paper or mock test to check if you’re on track.

## Common Mistakes Students Make While Solving CAT PYQs

Check out some of the common mistakes students make while solving the previous year CAT question papers.

**Ignoring the Pattern behind the Repeating Ideas:** Many students, who practice questions or solve PYQs routinely, miss the pattern, the traps, and the concepts that CAT tests repeatedly.

**Relying Completely on Shortcuts**: Many students are too reliant on shortcut tricks. This results in disaster when the questions are tricky and the format changes slightly.

**Ineffective Use of Time:** There are many students who spend an hour on tough PYQs or practice questions, ignoring the easy ones. This results in a complete failure of time management skills, and those skills could be the single most important thing for success on CAT.

**Not Simulating Test Conditions:** Solving PYQs or practice questions leisurely or not under time, or exam conditions, creates a false sense of students’ absolute readiness.

**Not Analyzing Mistakes:** Failure to analyze wrong or guessed answers results in lost opportunities to learn what the mistakes were and recognize areas of weakness.
