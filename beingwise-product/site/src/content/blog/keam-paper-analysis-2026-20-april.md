## What are the Students’ Reactions of KEAM 2026 20th April Exam?

According to the students who appeared for the **KEAM 2026 20th April exam**, the paper had a moderate difficulty level. Chemistry was seen as the easiest section, Physics was simple and scoring, while Mathematics turned out to be the most challenging part of the exam. Here is the subject-wise reaction shared by students.

### **Chemistry**

According to students, **Chemistry was the easiest subject** in the exam. Most questions were direct and based on the **NCERT syllabus**. Many students noticed numerical questions along with Organic Chemistry topics. Due to its easy level, several students chose to attempt Chemistry first.

### **Physics**

Students found **Physics simple and manageable**. Many said it was easier than the mock tests they had practised earlier. Questions were mostly formula-based with direct application, and there were no assertion-reasoning type questions. Some students completed this section quickly, while a few found one or two questions slightly lengthy.

### **Mathematics**

According to students, **Mathematics was the toughest section** of the paper. Many candidates said they could not attempt several questions easily and had to depend on their Class 11 and 12 concepts. Topics like Integration were considered lengthy and difficult, while some direct questions came from Vector 3D and Cramer’s Rule.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How is the KEAM Rank Calculated?

KEAM rank analysis is calculated using two factors: the entrance exam score and Class 12 board marks. Both are given 50% weightage each. Board marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics are considered after standardisation. Students can use this method to estimate their KEAM expected marks and likely position in the KEAM result analysis.
Final KEAM Rank = 50% (KEAM Score) + 50% (Standardised Class 12 PCM Marks)

### 2. What were the common strategies students used for subject order and time management in KEAM 2026?

Students mostly started with Chemistry, then moved to Physics, and kept Mathematics for the end. Some began with their strongest subject for confidence.
For time management, many used fixed time slots for each section, solved easier subjects quickly to save extra time, and skipped doubtful questions to avoid negative marking.

### 3. Were there any out-of-syllabus questions mentioned in KEAM 2026?

No, there were no out-of-syllabus questions mentioned. A student confirmed that the questions were direct, and there were also no questions asked from the deleted portions of the syllabus.
