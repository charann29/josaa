## How to Make Corrections In JEE Mains 2026 Application Form?

Candidates sometimes make errors while filling out the online application form due to uncertainty or lack of experience. To help applicants correct such mistakes, NTA provides a JEE Mains Application Correction window 2026 that allows them to edit specific details in their submitted form. Follow these steps to modify your application details:

- Go to the official NTA JEE Main website.
- Select the link titled ‘Application Form Correction for JEE (Main) – 2026’.
- Log in using your Application Number, Password, and Security Pin to access the correction portal.
- Choose the ‘Manage Form Particulars Correction’ option to proceed.
- Carefully read the displayed guidelines before making any changes.
- Make the necessary corrections in your form, keeping in mind that the exam center cannot be changed.
- Review and submit the corrected form. Pay any additional fee if applicable.

## Important Guidelines and Precautions for JEE Mains 2026 Application Form Correction

Before making any changes during the JEE Mains Application Form 2026 Correction Window, keep these critical guidelines and precautions in mind to avoid common mistakes and ensure your application remains valid.

1. **One-Time Opportunity:** The correction window is a one-time facility. Once you submit your corrections, you cannot make further changes. Double-check all modified details before final submission.
2. **Cross-Verify with Official Documents:** Ensure that all corrected information, especially your name, date of birth, and academic details, matches your Class 10/12 mark sheets and government-issued ID proofs. Discrepancies can lead to disqualification or issues during admission.
3. **Aadhaar-Verified Candidates:** If your application was Aadhaar-verified, specific fields like name, date of birth, and gender may be locked and non-editable. Attempting to change these may result in errors or rejection.
4. **Editable vs. Non-Editable Fields:** Do not attempt to edit non-editable fields (such as mobile number, email, or photograph). Editing restricted fields can lead to form rejection.
5. **Correction Fee:** Some corrections (such as changing the category or adding papers) may incur an additional fee. Make sure to pay any applicable charges to complete the correction process.
6. **Upload Documents Carefully:** If re-uploading certificates, photographs, or signatures, follow the NTA’s specified format, size, and quality guidelines.
7. **Review Before Submission:** Always review the entire application form after making corrections. Even minor mistakes (like spelling errors or incorrect category selection) can affect your eligibility.
8. **Print Confirmation:** After submitting corrections, download and print the updated confirmation page for your records. This document will be required for future reference and at the time of admission.
9. **Avoid Last-Minute Corrections:** Complete your corrections well before the deadline to avoid server issues or accidental omissions.
10. **Seek Help if Needed:** If any candidates face difficulties or are unsure about any changes, they are advised to contact the NTA helpline or email support before submitting their corrections.

## JEE Mains 2026 Registrations

The JEE Mains 2026 registration is ongoing and the deadline is on 27th November 2025. The NTA conducts the exam twice annually, first in January and then in April.

Candidates can register initially for Session 1 (January 2026) and submit the application fee. Later, they have the option to apply for Session 2 (April 2026) using the same application number. Those only interested in Session 2 may register directly once the respective application window opens. Payment for Session 2 can be completed during the given registration period.

- Visit the official website jeemain.nta.nic.in and click “Registration for JEE(Main)-2026 Session-1.”
- Choose “New Registration” for first-time users or “Log in” if registered already.
- Read the information bulletin, accept terms, and proceed.
- Enter basic details; get an Application Number and create a password.
- Log in and fill in personal, academic, and exam preferences, including city and paper choice.
- Upload required documents (photo, signature, Class 10 certificate, ID proof, PwD certificate if applicable).
- Pay the application fee online using Net Banking, Credit/Debit Card, or UPI.
- Download and print the Confirmation Page after successful payment.

## JEE Mains 2026 Admit Card

The JEE Main 2026 Admit Card is expected to be released about 3 to 4 days before the examination date. For the January session, the admit card is expected to be released in the third week of January 2026, with exams scheduled between January 21 and January 30, 2026. The admit card can be downloaded from the official NTA website at jeemain.nta.nic.in by logging in with the application number, date of birth or password.

The admit card includes crucial details such as the candidate’s name, father’s name, date of birth, gender, category, state code of eligibility, JEE Main roll number, application form number, exam date and shift timing, name and address of the examination center, candidate’s photograph and signature, parent’s signature, and important exam guidelines.

Yes, candidates are allowed to edit specific details during their JEE Mains application form 2026 correction window. Candidates can review the list of fields that are open for modification and those that cannot be changed once the correction facility is available.

### **3.********Can I add another paper (Paper 1, Paper 2A, or Paper 2B) during the JEE Mains 2026 correction period?**********

Yes, it is possible to add additional paper while making corrections to the JEE Mains 2026 application form. However, candidates must also pay the additional registration fee for the newly added paper.

### 4. ********Is there any charge for correcting the JEE Mains 2026 application form?********

The NTA may ask candidates to pay an additional fee if specific changes are made in the application form. This includes modifications such as changing the category from SC/ST/Girls/PwD to General/OBC-NCL or adding Paper 2 in addition to Paper 1. In such cases, candidates need to pay the difference between the previously paid and the revised fee. Since candidates from reserved categories pay a lower fee, they may have to pay an extra amount after the update. For other types of corrections, no additional fee is required.
