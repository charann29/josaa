## Exam Overview: KEAM, April 29th Shift 2

The KEAM exam for engineering follows a different pattern compared to the pharmacy stream. It comprises three sections: Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry, and is conducted over a duration of 3 hours. Candidates receive 4 marks for each correct answer and incur a deduction of 1 mark for every incorrect response. Refer to the table below for a detailed breakdown.

|  |  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Subject** | **No of Questions** | **Marks** | **Marking Scheme** | **Time** |
| Mathematics | 75 | 300 | +4 Marks for Correct Answer  -1 Mark for Incorrect Answer | 180 Minutes |
| Physics | 45 | 180 |
| Chemistry | 30 | 120 |
| **Total** | **150** | **600** |

## Answer Key & Response Sheet: KEAM 2025

The KEAM 2025 answer key has been officially released on the CEE website at [cee.kerala.gov.in](https://cee.kerala.gov.in). Candidates can also download the shift-wise answer key PDFS from our page for quick access. In case of any discrepancies, candidates are allowed to challenge the provisional answer key by paying a fee of ₹200 per question. All objections will be reviewed, and the final answer key will be published after the evaluation process is complete.

Additionally, the response sheet for each candidate is now available in PDF format. This sheet includes both the answers submitted by the candidate and the official correct responses for each question. Candidates can log in to the official website to view and download their individual response sheets for reference and score estimation.

## April 29th KEAM 2025 Exam Analysis

Here are some of the student reviews of KEAM 2025, April 29th:

**Aarav Menon (from Ernakulam):** *“I found the KEAM Engineering exam quite easy overall. Physics had mostly direct, formula-based questions, and Chemistry was very NCERT-centric, especially from topics like* ***Chemical Bonding*** *and* ***Solutions****. Mathematics was a bit lengthy in parts, especially questions from* ***Calculus*** *and* ***Coordinate Geometry****, but nothing too challenging. I even had enough time to recheck a few answers.”*

**Sahana Reddy (from Thrissur):** *“The paper was easier than most of my mock tests. Chemistry was quick to finish with straightforward questions from the* ***p-block*** *and* ***Organic Chemistry****. Physics had clearly framed questions from* ***Work, Energy & Power*** *and* ***Current Electricity****. Maths took a bit more time, particularly due to some tricky problems in* ***Matrices*** *and* ***Probability****, but it was manageable if you stayed focused. Overall, it felt like a smooth experience.”*

**Devraj (from Kollam):** *“Honestly, the exam felt very doable. I finished Chemistry quickly — many questions matched what we had practised, especially in* ***Equilibrium*** *and* ***Metallurgy****. Physics had a few conceptual but manageable problems from* ***Gravitation*** *and* ***Thermodynamics****. Maths was surprisingly better than expected — even though* ***Vectors*** *and* ***3d Geometry*** *took some time, the questions were not too tough. Overall, it was a balanced paper.”*
