## What is NIRF Ranking?

The full form of the NIRF is National Institutional Ranking Framework. It is an official ranking system launched by the Ministry of Education to rank Indian higher educational institutions. This ranking system evaluates colleges and universities on the basis of different criteria. It includes different rankings for engineering, medical, management, law, and university categories.

Every year, thousands of students who aspire to join engineering colleges refer to NIRF rankings to compare IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other private engineering colleges. Colleges with high NIRF rankings have good academic facilities, expert faculties, excellent placement records, and extensive research programs.

### Criteria for Ranking Institutions under NIRF Rankings 2026

NIRF Rankings 2026 involve evaluating various higher educational institutions through an organized method involving five important criteria that contribute to assessing the quality of the college or university accurately. Each criterion has its own importance and involves some measurable performance indicators to measure its performance in determining its final ranking in India. Here are the five important Criteria that are considered while making the NIRF Ranking 2026 Engineering Colleges list:

- **Teaching, Learning, and Resources (TLR):** Considers the quality of the faculty, number of students, and availability of infrastructural facilities.
- **Research and Professional Practice (RP):** Takes into account research publications, patents, research projects, and professional achievements.
- **Graduate Outcomes (GO):** Takes into account placement opportunities, higher studies of the students, and the graduation ratio.
- **Outreach and Inclusivity (OI):** Considers the degree of diversity and inclusiveness of the institution.
- **Perception (PR):** Reflects the overall reputation of the institution among employers, academics, and the public.

## Admission Process at Top Engineering Colleges in India

The process of getting admission into top engineering colleges that come under the NIRF Rankings of India involves various means, such as entrance tests at the national level, entrance examinations at the institution level, and direct admissions depending upon the results of class 12th.

| Admission Mode | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Admission based on JEE Main Exam | Admission to NITs, IIITs, and many government-funded engineering colleges is conducted through the Joint Entrance Examination – Main scores followed by JoSAA counselling. |
| Admission based on JEE Advanced Exam | Candidates seeking admission to IITs must qualify for the Joint Entrance Examination – Advanced after clearing the JEE Main eligibility requirements. |
| State-Level Counselling | Several engineering colleges accept scores from state entrance exams and provide admission through centralized state counselling processes. |
| Private University Entrance Exams | Many private engineering universities conduct their own entrance exams for BTech admissions and shortlist candidates based on performance. |
| Direct Admission Through Class 12 Marks | Some private engineering colleges offer direct admission based on Class 12 PCM marks without requiring entrance exam scores. |
| Management Quota Admission | Certain private institutes also reserve management quota seats where students can apply directly based on eligibility and seat availability. |
