### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Physics Section

As per the MHT CET 2026 Paper analysis, the physics section was moderate. Mostly formula-based questions were asked, but a few numerical questions needed conceptual clarity and calculation skills. Take a look at the important topics covered in the physics section.

- Current Electricity
- Rotational Motion
- Electrostatics
- SHM
- Modern Physics
- Wave Optics
- Semiconductor
- Magnetic Effect of Current
- Kinematics

### MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

Among the three sections in the MHT CET Exam paper,  the chemistry section was the easiest. The chemistry questions are direct and theoretical. We have listed the important topics covered in chemistry

- Chemical Kinetics
- Coordination Compounds
- Biomolecules
- Alcohols and Phenols
- p-Block Elements
- Thermodynamics
- Surface Chemistry
- Polymers

- Matrices and Determinants
- Probability
- Differential Equations
- Application of Derivatives

- Permutation and Combination

## Student Review on MHT CET Paper​ Analysis May 14 Shift 1

Once the exam was completed, we spoke to some candidates regarding their experience with the MHT CET May 14 Shift 1 Question Paper.

- **As per Rohit,** the overall difficulty level of the paper was average; however, the Mathematics portion took a lot of time to solve. According to him, there were many calculation-based questions in this portion.
- **As per Ananya,** the Chemistry portion was easy and contained the maximum number of marks in this exam. She stated that many questions were NCERT-based and theory-related; however, some questions of organic chemistry required proper option selection.
- **Karan** pointed out that the physics portion comprised a balance of numerical and theoretical questions from chapters such as current electricity, SHM, and modern physics.
- **According to Priya,** the mathematics paper was tougher compared to both Chemistry and Physics because it contained multiple steps from the topics of integration, probability, and vectors. She further pointed out that those students having fast calculation ability and practice would be able to solve the paper efficiently.
