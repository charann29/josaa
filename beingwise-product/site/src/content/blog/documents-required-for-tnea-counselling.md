## Mandatory Documents Required for TNEA Counselling 2026 Registration

Once students have submitted their online TNEA counselling 2026 application in May.  They are required to upload the documents required for TNEA counseling before the deadline on June 06, 2026. Students must keep the following mandatory documents required for TNEA counselling ready for upload:

- Marksheets of Class 10 & 12 Standard
- For Tamil Nadu Board students, an additional marksheet of Class 11 is required for verification.
- For Government school students who passed before 2026, a bona fide certificate is required.
- Transfer Certificate of the school or any other institute
- Passport-Size Photo
- Scanned Signature Copy
- TNEA Registration Fee Receipt

## What are the documents required for TNEA counselling under Category/Reservation?

Students under the reserved category and students from outside Tamil Nadu need to produce the following TNEA required documents or certificates:

- SC/SCA/ST/MBC/DNC/BC/BCM Candidates need to provide a Permanent Community Certificate.
- For Domicile status verification, Nativity Certificate (If Applicable)
- A Family income certificate for students who want a scholarship or a fee waiver,

## How to Upload Documents Required For TNEA Counselling Application?

Students need to upload the documents required for TNEA counselling application after the online payment and submission are done. In the  PDF/JPG/JPEG/PNG format, students must scan the above-listed documents and keep them for uploading. It is generally required for the TNEA Certificate verification before the TNEA rank list is released. Refer to the following steps to upload the documents required for TNEA counselling application:

- Go to the official [website of TNEA](http://tneaonline.org) before the deadline for document upload.
- Students need to log in to the TNEA portal with their application number and password.
- Select the upload certificate option on the homepage.
- Properly scan the copies of the documents required for TNEA Counselling. The size of the images should be within the following sizes:
  - Certificates – 150 KB – 1 MB
  - Photo/Signature – 20 KB – 50 KB
- Further, upload the TNEA required documents one by one.
- Upload each document in a single slot.
- Once uploading is done, verify the documents once by downloading the uploaded documents.
- Check the declaration and freeze the upload to confirm.

## Criteria For TNEA Certificate Verification Process at the TFC

After the Intermediate or Class 12th results are out, the [TNEA certificate verification](https://collegedost.in/blog/tnea/tnea-certificate-verification/) process is conducted. Students need to book their verification slot at the respective TFCs (Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions Facilitation Centres) from the TNEA portal.

 The TNEA Certificate verification process is mainly conducted for the verification and confirmation of the uploaded documents. At the facilitation centers, the uploaded documents are checked thoroughly against the original documents carried by the students. The entire process is conducted by the officials present at the TFC. In case of any mismatch, the application may be rejected. The TNEA Certificate verification process at the TFC is carried out based on the following criteria:

- Once the Upload status is checked for the documents required for TNEA counselling, the uploaded documents are verified.
- For students who have an incomplete status on the upload, follow the instructions below:
  - Scan the TNEA required documents into a single PDF and upload.
  - Once done, freeze them.
- For students who have a ‘Not started status’ on the upload, follow the instructions below:
  - Scan the TNEA required documents into a single PDF and upload.
  - Once done, freeze them.
- For students who have an ‘in progress’ status, it means their verification for the upload is in process.
- While a complete status is indicative of the completion of the certificate verification.

## FAQs

### 1. What are the documents required for TNEA counselling for the general category students?

The documents required for TNEA Counselling include 10th & 12th marksheets, TC certificate, Passport size photo, scanned copies of the students’ signatures, and TNEA registration fee receipt. A community or nativity certificate is also required if applicable.

### 2.  When is the deadline for uploading the documents required for TNEA counselling?

Generally, the deadline for uploading the documents required for TNEA counselling is around June.

### 3.  What is TNEA counselling?

TNEA counselling is the centralized process of seat allotment for B.Tech students across the engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu. This process of counselling includes registration, uploading of TNEA required documents, TFC Verification, Rank list publishing, choice filling, seat allotment, and the fee payment process.

### 4.  What is considered the original document required for TNEA counselling?

The original documents are the certificates or documents issued by competent authorities and not the scanned or xerox copy.

### 5. Is an income certificate mandatory for TNEA counselling?

A family income certificate is mandatory for students who have applied for Scholarships or a fee waiver.

### 6. **Can I use an e-certificate for community/nativity claims?**

Yes, students use e-certificates from the Tahsildar portals for community or nativity claims.

### 7. **Is an Aadhar Card mandatory?**

No, an Aadhar card is not mandatory for students.

### 8. **What happens if the uploaded documents are rejected at TFC?**

If the uploaded documents are rejected at the TFC, it will result in disqualification. But before the deadline, students can reupload the documents on the portal and revisit for verification at the TFC.

### 9. **Are attested documents accepted for TNEA Counselling?**

No, attested documents are not accepted for TNEA 2026 counselling.

### 10. **What if I get an error message while uploading the TNEA required documents?**

During peak times, students may get an error message while uploading a document. Students can wait, try again later, or try checking if the file size and format are within regulations or not. As a last resort, students can contact the TNEA helpline and raise a ticket for the issue.
