## CUET UG 2025 English Paper Analysis

Students found the English section to be moderate overall, with some describing it as slightly difficult. Reading comprehension was manageable, especially the passage on handicrafts, while the story-based ones tested interpretation skills. Sentence rearrangement stood out with 7–8 mostly easy questions. Moreover, the vocabulary section, which included synonyms, antonyms, and idioms, was extensive and challenging for those lacking preparation. However, idioms were relatively easy to decode through context. Phrasal verbs such as “run into” and “run through” were also tested. As per the students’ report, no questions came from One Word Substitution or homophones, but there was one on figures of speech. Overall, students felt that revising known topics was more effective than learning new ones.

## CUET UG 2025 Chemistry Paper Analysis

Students found the Chemistry section to be easy to moderate overall, with many feeling confident about 35–40 questions. Moreover, many recognized familiar concepts like Gabriel synthesis, coordination compounds, and vitamin-related biochemistry. Numerical problems on molarity and PPM were straightforward, testing basic calculation skills. However, a few questions, especially were in the match-the-following formats and some advanced reaction mechanisms, were tricky and demanded careful attention. Some students also noted subtle differentiators, like methanol vs ethanol applications, making the paper more conceptual toward the end.

## CUET UG 2025 Mathematics Paper Analysis

Students found the Mathematics section to be moderate overall, though opinions varied as some felt it was easy to moderate, while others struggled due to time constraints. Calculus had the highest weightage, with several questions on differentiation, integration, and application of derivatives. Moreover, topics like matrices, vectors, and probability tested a wide range of concepts, requiring both conceptual clarity and calculation speed. Students have also reported that there were Linear Programming was involved in optimization and feasible region analysis, which some found tricky. Despite the variety, many agreed that managing time was the biggest challenge.

## Student Reviews For CUET UG 2025 June 2nd Shift 1 Exam

Most students from different states found CUET UG 2025 Shift 1, which was held on June 2nd, to be moderate. Here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

**English**

**Student Feedback:** The paper was mostly moderate. RC passages were easy to follow, especially the one on handicrafts. Vocabulary was a bit tough, especially synonyms and antonyms like ‘sagacious’ – you needed prior prep. Sentence rearrangement came in bulk! Thankfully, most were easy, but time was tight.

**Chemistry**

**Student Feedback:** Overall, the paper was quite easy if you had revised well. 35–40 questions felt direct. Coordination compounds and biomolecules dominated. Numerical questions like PPM and molarity were simple. Matching-type questions were confusing, you had to know everything precisely, no guesswork.

**Mathematics**

**Student Feedback:** Moderate paper, but lengthy. Calculus and integration took a lot of time. Some standard questions were there, but time management was key. Vectors, probability, and LPP were balanced, though a few tricky questions caught me off guard. themes.

## CUET UG 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

Given below are a few CUET UG 2025 exam day guidelines that students need to follow.

- **Carry Only Required Items:** Only take your printed admit card (with passport-size photograph), your valid photo ID (Aadhaar or PAN card), a transparent ballpoint pen, and a transparent water bottle. Candidates can check all the CUET exam details on the admit card and are advised to adhere to the instructions.
- **Be On Time:** Report to the exam center well in advance relative to your reporting time (7:00 AM for Shift 1 and 1:00 PM for Shift 2). If you are late, you will not be allowed to enter.
- **Prohibited Items:** Do not bring mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, bags, books, or food if not medically required (with documentation).
- **Maintain Discipline:** Be seated in your assigned seat, only do rough work on the sheets provided, and do not perform any unfair practice or make any noise in the exam hall.
- **Time Management:** It is a critical challenge across subjects, especially with lengthy sections like RC or Quant. It’s advised to tackle easy questions first.
- **The Normalisation Factor:** This factor will account for variations in difficulty across shifts. The focus should be on attempting as many questions with 100% accuracy.
