The JEE Main 2025 is one of the most competitive exams in India, and securing a high rank can be challenging. However, in some cases, more than one candidate may score the same marks in JEE Main. This raises an important question on how are ranks assigned when two or more candidates achieve the same marks? To address such scenarios, the NTA has employed a tie-breaking rules that ensures a fair ranking system. This article explains the JEE Main 2025 Tie Breaking rules in detail, including the criteria and procedures used to resolve ties among candidates.

## What is a JEE Main 2025 Tie Breaking Rules?

A tie in JEE Mains happens when two or more candidates get the same score in the JEE Mains 2025 exam. Marks play a crucial role in your admission process, and they can make or break your chances of admission to prestigious institutions like IITs, NITs, etc. Henc,e a clear, fair, and transparent mechanism is required to avoid these discrepancies. The NTA’s tie-breaking policy outlines criteria for assigning ranks to candidates who score the same marks.

## **Order of the Tie-Breaking Rule for Paper 1**

The tie-breaking criteria for JEE Main 2025 will be applied in the following order:

- Score in Mathematics: Candidates with high scores in Maths are given priority.
- Score in Physics: If a tie still exists, Candidates with higher scores in physics are given priority.
- Score in Chemistry: In case if tie still exists, Candidates with high scores in chemistry are given priority.
- The ratio of Incorrect to correct answers across all subjects: The ratio of Incorrect to correct answers is taken into accoun,t and the one with the lowest ratio is preferred.

- The ratio of Incorrect to correct answers in Physics: The ratio of Incorrect to correct answers in physics is taken into account, and the one with the lowest ratio is preferred.
- The ratio of Incorrect to correct answers in Chemistry: This is taken into account, and the one with the lowest ratio is preferred.
- If a tie persists after all these steps, candidates will be given the same rank. This updated tie-breaking process streamlines the ranking system by eliminating age and application number as factors, ensuring a more equitable approach for candidates with identical scores.

## Significance of the JEE Main 2025 Tie Breaking Rules

The recent revisions to the JEE Main 2025 Tie Breaking Rules carry significant implications for candidates and the integrity of the examination process. By removing age and application number as tie-breakers, the NTA has shifted to a purely performance-based and merit-driven ranking system. This approach emphasizes fairness, transparency, and equity in evaluating candidates.The updated JEE Main 2025 Tie Breaking Rules ensure that all candidates are assessed solely on their academic performance in the examination, eliminating any non-academic factors that could introduce bias or perceived unfairness. This move strengthens the credibility of the ranking system by:

- **Promoting a merit-based assessment:** Only subject scores and accuracy ratios are used, ensuring that rankings reflect actual exam performance.
- **Enhancing transparency and fairness:** Every candidate now competes on equal terms, regardless of age or application sequence.
- **Reducing potential biases:** Eliminating age and application number from the process addresses concerns about arbitrary advantages and fosters greater trust in the results.
- **Ensuring equitable opportunities:** Candidates from diverse backgrounds and age groups are evaluated by the same academic standards, supporting inclusivity.

### Removal of Age and Application Number from Tie-Breaking Criteria

In JEE Mains 2025, the NTA updated the tie-breaking policy, removing age and application number as deciding factors in cases of a tie. Previously, if candidates remained tied after all performance-based criteria were applied, older candidates or those with a lower application number would be ranked higher. For 2025, only exam performance is considered for ranking. If a tie persists after all subject-wise and accuracy-based criteria have been exhausted, candidates will be awarded the same rank. This change ensures a fairer, more transparent ranking system that focuses solely on candidates’ performance in the examination.

## **Order of the Tie-Breaking Policy for Paper 2A (B.Arch)**

The tie between candidates with the identical total scores will be resolved in the following order:

- NTA score in Mathematics (Part I)
- NTA score in Aptitude Test (Part-II)
- NTA score in Drawing Test
- Candidate with a lower proportion of incorrect answers to correct answers across all subjects.
- Candidate with a lower proportion of incorrect answers to correct answers in Mathematics (Part I)
- Candidate with a lower proportion of incorrect answers to correct answers in the Aptitude Test (Part II)
- If the tie persists even after these steps, candidates will be assigned the same rank.

## Additional NTA Updates and Rules Changes for JEE Mains 2025

For JEE Mains 2025, the NTA has introduced several important updates beyond the changes to the tie-breaking policy. Candidates should be aware of these new policies and procedural changes to ensure a smooth application and examination experience.

**1. New Application Portal Domain:** The JEE Mains 2025 application process is now hosted on a new website: [jeemain.nta.nic.in](https://jeemain.nta.nic.in/). Candidates must use this updated portal for all official notifications, application submissions, and exam-related updates. This replaces the previous domain used in past years.

**2. Revised Provisions for Candidates with Disabilities:** The NTA has updated its policy on extra time for candidates with disabilities. The terms “extra time” and “additional time” are now officially referred to as “compensatory time.”

- Candidates eligible for scribes, readers, or lab assistants are now provided with at least 20 minutes of compensatory time per hour of the exam.
- Candidates with cognitive disabilities who qualify for a scribe are entitled to an extra hour for a three-hour exam, regardless of whether they actually use a scribe.
- For exams shorter than an hour, compensatory time will be calculated proportionally, with a minimum of 5 minutes awarded in multiples of 5.

**3. Admit Card and Result Announcements**

- Admit cards will be available three days before the exam date and can be accessed using the candidate’s registration number and date of birth.
