JEE Advanced is the second stage of the exam after JEE Mains. Aspirants who wish to secure admission and pursue their studies in IITS require good scores in JEE Advanced 2025. JEE Advanced consists of 306 marks spread across two papers, with 183 marks for each paper.

The JEE Advanced 2025 exam is scheduled for **May 18th, 2025**. Candidates among the top 2,50,000 who cleared JEE Mains 2025 can write JEE Advanced. An Admit card should be submitted while appearing for the JEE Advanced 2025 exam. The JEE Advanced Admit Card was released on the official website on May 12th, 2025. Candidates often face difficulty in downloading the JEE Advanced admit card. This article will help you with a step-by-step guide to downloading the JEE Advanced admit card.

## Steps to Download the JEE Advanced Admit Card

The JEE Advanced admit card is a mandatory document for candidates appearing in the exam. It contains essential details about the candidate, including personal information, exam centre, timing, and instructions for the exam day. Candidates failing to submit the admit card are not allowed to enter the examination center.

A detailed guide for downloading JEE Advanced admit card is mentioned below.

- Access the official website ([jeeadv.ac.in](https://jeeadv.ac.in/)) to download the admit card.
- Click on the link displayed on your homepage to download the admit card.

> [JEE Advanced Admit Card Link](https://cportal.jeeadv.ac.in/)

- You will be asked to provide the credentials to download the admit card on the following page.
- Please provide your application number, date of birth or password, and security pin and click on submit.
- Admit cards will be displayed on the screen. You can download and print out.

## How to Recover Your JEE Advanced Application Number?

Candidates often miss their application number, which is very important for downloading their admit card. You should save your application number until the admission process, as it is important information that you require throughout your exam process.

Here are the steps to recover your application if it is lost.

- Go to the official JEE Advanced 2025 website jeeadv.ac.in.
- Locate and click on the ‘Candidate Portal’ section.
- Select the ‘Find Registration Number’ option.
- Enter the date of birth and application number.
- Click the ‘Submit’ button to proceed.
- Your registration number will be displayed on the screen for easy reference.

## JEE Advanced 2025: Exam Day Guidelines

Candidates should follow the given exam day guidelines for JEE Advanced 2025.

- **Have the Required Documents:** It is essential to bring the JEE Advanced Admit Card along with a valid photo ID, such as an Aadhaar card, PAN card, or Passport. You will not be allowed into the exam hall without these documents.
- **Arrive at the Exam Center on Time:** Candidates should reach the exam center at least 60–90 minutes prior to the start time. No late arrivals will be permitted entry into the exam hall under any circumstances.
- **Adhere to Security Protocols:** All candidates must follow procedures regarding frisking and biometric checks. Ensure you do not bring any prohibited items into the exam hall, including mobile phones, calculators, smartwatches, or any other electronic devices.
- **Dress Code and Materials:** Candidates are advised to wear light and comfortable clothing without any metallic elements. You are allowed to bring a pen, a pencil, and a transparent water bottle. All rough work must be done on the provided scribble pad.
- **Carefully Follow CBT Guidelines:** The exam will take place in a Computer-Based Test (CBT) format. Candidates must carefully read the on-screen instructions before starting the exam.

### JEE Advanced 2025 Dress Code

**Clothes:** Candidates should report to the examination center in light, half-sleeved, and comfortable clothes, keeping in mind not to wear clothes with fancy or complicated designs, patterns, or logos that can raise doubts.

**Footwear:** It is best to wear slippers or sandals with thin soles, which can be easily and quickly checked by the authorities. Shoes are strictly prohibited in the examination hall.

**Accessories:** Avoid wearing any jewelry such as earrings, necklaces, bracelets, or rings. Candidates cannot wear belts, wristwatches, or anything else that is metallic.

**Dress Code for Female Candidates:** Simple kurtis with jeans, leggings, or palazzos. Leave dupattas, stoles, and scarves at home and do not wear them to the examination centres. Candidates should avoid decorative clips and just wear basic elastic non-metallic hair ties.

**Dress Code for Male Candidates:** Male candidates should wear either a half-sleeve shirt or a t-shirt. Do not wear clothes with large buttons, zippers, or big logos. Wear simple trousers or pants and avoid wearing jeans with multiple pockets or cargo pants.
