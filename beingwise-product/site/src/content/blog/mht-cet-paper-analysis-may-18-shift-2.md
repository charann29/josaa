## Subject-wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis 2026

As per the MHT CET exam pattern, the MHT CET May 18 Question Paper includes three sections, which are required to be completed in three hours.  The first 90 minutes are reserved for Physics and Chemistry, with the remaining 90 minutes for Mathematics. Let’s now take a look at the subject-wise MHT CET Paper Analysis for May 18 Shift 2 in the following section.

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Physics Section

MHT CET 2026 Exam analysis for the May 18 Shift 2 suggests the Physics Section is rated as moderately difficult. The numerical-based questions held more weightage. Many questions were based on formulae. A few of the questions asked were lengthier and involved calculations.

- Current Electricity
- Magnetic Effects of Current
- Electrostatics
- Modern Physics
- Rotational Motion
- Oscillations & Waves
- Semiconductor Electronics

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

Chemistry is rated as the easiest as well as the highest-scoring section in May 18 Shift 2. Most of the questions asked were concept-based. Organic Chemistry held a decent amount of weightage. Physical Chemistry questions were formula-based. Here are the important topics asked in this shift:

- Chemical Thermodynamics
- Coordination Compounds
- Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers
- Biomolecules
- p-Block Elements
- Chemical Kinetics
- Solutions

### MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis for Maths Section

Mathematics again turned out to be the hardest and lengthiest section, as per the MHT CET Paper Analysis. Many questions needed step-wise and calculative answers, which proved to be time-consuming.  Find here the important topics asked in this shift:

- Definite Integration
- Differentiation
- Probability
- Matrices & Determinants
- Vector Algebra
- 3D Geometry
- Complex Numbers
- Application of Derivatives

## Exam Hall Strategy for Upcoming MHT CET 2026 Shifts

To ace the forthcoming MHT CET 2026 shifts, students need to adopt a strategic approach for the exam halls, which should include time management and question selection.

- **Start with Chemistry First:** Since Chemistry is relatively the easiest and most scoring subject with questions from NCERT, attempt Chemistry.
- **Next, Attempt Physics:** Attempt formulas and conceptual questions. Do not try to answer lengthy numericals first, and do that after finishing the other sections.
- Maximum Time for Mathematics: Based on the previous MHT CET Paper Analysis, it is expected that Mathematics will be lengthy and calculative. Give at least 75-85 minutes to this section.
- **Never Waste Time on One Question:** When you see that one question is lengthy and seems to consume your time, move on to the other questions.
- **Accuracy Beats Attempts:** Do not indulge in random guessing. Fewer but more accurate attempts will get you a better percentile.
- **Use the last 10 Minutes Strategically:** During the last few minutes, review your answers, check calculations, and try answering the easy ones.
- **Maintain Composure During the Exam:** According to recent trends, MHT CET 2026 papers are moderate, requiring time management skills during the exam.
