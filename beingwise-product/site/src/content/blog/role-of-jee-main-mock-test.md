## Time Management with JEE Mains Mock Test 2026

Managing your time effectively during the JEE Main exam is one of the most crucial factors. A JEE Main exam is of 3 Hours or 180 minutes, where students need to answer 90 questions. To optimize scores, attempting the JEE Mains Mock Test is essential. Students can check a few of the time management strategies below while attempting the JEE Main Mock Test:

- Once you get the Mock Test paper, carefully scan the paper and answer the easy questions, mainly covering 40%-50% of the paper in at least 60 minutes.
- Secondly, approach the moderate questions, followed by the tough questions at the end.
- Set a timer of 1-2 minutes for each question. If you are stuck on one question for more than 90 seconds, move on to the next. With time to spare, you can return to it later.
- Prioritize sections based on subjects and strengths, and divide time accordingly. It is an initial practice during Mock Tests.
- Do not overthink beyond a minute for one question, as it kills the momentum for students.
- Answer the mock test without any disturbance or distraction, at one go. Use a strict timer to simulate a real-time exam environment.

### JEE Main Mock Test – Error Analysis

JEE Main Mock Test can become the biggest tool to reduce errors during the exams. After attempting the mock test, carefully analyse each error and silly mistakes. Convert these mistakes into targeted improvements. Some of the essential points to keep in mind during error analysis are as follows:

- Start by categorizing errors into certain groups, like Conceptual error, Calculation error, Misreading errors, and silly mistakes, to identify patterns and prevent repeating them.
- Compare your answers with the solution from the answer key books or reference books.
- Take note of the wrong answers and unattempted questions, and review each of them thoroughly.
- Identify the weak topics or chapters from each subject, and track the errors. Work on the weaker sections and target them to improve.
- Review your past mock tests and identify the repeated mistakes. Prioritize them in your study plan.
- Additionally, keep monitoring the progress and keep a track of the scores and improvements.
