## Student Reviews For CUET UG 2025 May 16th Shift 2 Exam

Most students from various states found the CUET UG 2025 Shift 2 exam, held on May 16th, to be moderately difficult. Here is a summary of their perceptions regarding the exam.

Several students reported that the English paper was relatively straightforward, with many completing it in just 45 minutes. While one student described the overall exam as very easy, others noted that specific sections presented challenges. Notably, questions related to topics such as Boat and Stream in the Quantitative Aptitude section and Antonyms in the English section were regarded as difficult or moderately hard.

While one student confidently labelled the entire paper as very easy, others had differing experiences. Sections like Mathematics and Quantitative Aptitude were mentioned specifically, particularly the questions on Boat and Stream, which students found to be tough and calculation-heavy. This variance in perceptions of difficulty underscores the importance of individual strengths in different subjects.

Most students appreciated the well-organised exam environment, with clear instructions provided by the invigilators. Although there were mixed reviews regarding difficulty, there were no major complaints about management or concerns over paper leaks.

Overall, the shift was well-managed, with no significant technical or administrative issues reported. While some students found the paper straightforward, others felt that the difficulty varied by subject, particularly in English vocabulary and certain math topics.

## CUET UG 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

Given below are a few CUET UG 2025 exam day guidelines that students need to follow.

- **Carry Only Required Items:** Only take your printed admit card (with passport-size photograph), your valid photo ID (Aadhaar or PAN card), a transparent ballpoint pen, and a transparent water bottle. Candidates can check all the CUET exam details on the admit card and are advised to adhere to the instructions.
- **Be On Time:** Report to the exam centre well in advance relative to your reporting time (7:00 AM for Shift 1 and 1:00 PM for Shift 2). If you are late, you will not be allowed to enter.
- **Dress Code:** You should wear light clothes without large buttons or badges. You may wear slippers or sandals only – no shoes or high heels.
- **Prohibited Items:** Do not bring mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, bags, books, or food if not medically required (with documentation).
- **Maintain Discipline:** Stay in your assigned seat, only do rough work on the sheets provided, and do not perform unfair practices or make noise in the exam hall.
- **Security & Entry Protocols**– Entry was strictly timed and subject to multiple frisking stages. Fingerprint scanning and face recognition were done before entry.

### What’s New In CUET UG 2025?

Check out some new changes UGC introduced and will be implemented from CUET UG 2025 onwards.

- Students can now take CUET UG exams in any subject, regardless of their Class 12th subjects.

- The GAT scores will decide the admission for the removed CUET subjects.
- There will be no optional questions.
