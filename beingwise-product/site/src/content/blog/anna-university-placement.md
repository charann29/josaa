## Anna University Placements For B.Tech – Placement Process & Eligibility

[Anna University placements](https://www.annauniv.edu/CUICCentre/placement.html) process for B.Tech is conducted by the Centre for University-Industry  Collaboration (CUIC) and targets final year students. Students need to fulfill the following eligibility criteria and selection criteria to register for the B.Tech placement drive:

- Minimum CGPA 6.5 – 7.0. CGPA must be scored every semester.
- No backlogs for the semester
- 85% + attendance
- 7th semester internship mandatory

Students need to register for the Anna University placements drive in the following section:

- Register on the CUIC portal
- Use a verified professional email ID
- Create a profile and appear for the required skill tests.
- Fill the ‘know your recruiter’ form
- Pre-placement talks are conducted
- Followed by an appearance in aptitude tests and group discussions
- Selected students need to appear for technical/HR interviews
- Shortlisted students are then sent offers through letters via the portal.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is Anna University a private or public university?

Anna University is a public university with four main campuses: CEG, MIT, ACTECH, and SAP in Chennai. It is UGC approved and NAAC A++ accredited, offering NIRF-ranked B.Tech programs. In 2025, its NIRF engineering ranking was 20th.

### 2. Does Anna University offer internship opportunities?

Yes, Anna University provides internship opportunities to students from the 2nd or 3rd semester through CUIC. It includes 6-month summer internships with a monthly stipend of around INR 11,800. At the MIT campus, students can also take 2–4 week internships in IT, core engineering, and manufacturing domains.

### 3. What are the average B.Tech packages at Anna University?

The average B.Tech package at Anna University ranges between INR 7 LPA and INR 8 LPA, with placements offered by top multinational companies.

### 4. When are Anna University placements usually conducted?

Placement training for final-year students typically begins in July, and the main placement drives are conducted between September and December.
