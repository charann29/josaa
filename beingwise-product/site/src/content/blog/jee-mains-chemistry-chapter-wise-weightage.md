## How to Prepare for the JEE Main Chemistry Section

The JEE Main Chemistary Chapter wise weightage is the most crucial weapon in your last-minute preparation. Success in JEE Main Chemistry isn’t just about hard work; it’s about strategic allocation of time. Because the chemistry weightage in JEE Mains is spread across three distinct sub-disciplines, your preparation style must adapt to each.

## Inorganic Chemistry Preparation Tips

Inorganic chemistry is often the fastest and most scoring section to complete during the exam. Most questions are derived directly from NCERT textbooks; thus, most of the students feel confident to tackle this section.

According to the JEE Mains chemistry chapter wise weightage, Ccoordination compoundsand Cchemical bondingare the heavy hitters, often accounting for 3–5 questions combined. Create a “Trend & Exception” chart. For example, while moving down a group usually increases atomic radius, there are specific anomalies in the d-block and p-block that the NTA loves to test.

Spend 30 minutes every morning reading NCERT. High chemistry weightage for JEE Mains in this section goes to those who can recall facts quickly.

### Organic Chemistry Preparation Guide

The organic chemistry weightage in JEE Mains is approximately 33% to 36%. You cannot master this through rote memorisation alone. Start with General Organic Chemistry (GOC). If your understanding of inductive effects, mesomeric effects, and hyperconjugation is weak, you will struggle with the rest of the syllabus.

Most of the students feel confused about the numerous reaction mechanisms. Instead of memorising 500 reactions, learn the 10–15 core mechanisms (SN1, SN2, electrophilic addition, etc.). Keep a dedicated notebook for reactions like Aldol Condensation, Cannizzaro, and Hoffman Bromamide. These are frequently featured in the Chemistry JEE Mains Weightage analysis.

### Physical Chemistry Preparation Tips

Physical chemistry usually dominates the “Section B” questions. In the JEE Main Syllabus, chapters like Thermodynamics, Equilibrium, and Electrochemistry carry significant JEE Main chemistry chapter wise weightage. You must know the formulas and, more importantly, the conditions under which they apply. Practise solving questions without a calculator. Many students lose marks not because they didn’t know the concept, but because they made a calculation error under pressure.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Are Physical or Organic Chemistry required to be emphasized for JEE Mains?

The ratio of weight given (to the two sections) is about the same (33-35 per cent, respectively), but Organic Chemistry is considered more important in multiple ways.

### 2. What is the highest-scoring chapter in Organic Chemistry?

The two chapters with the highest scoring possibilities are General Organic Chemistry and Hydrocarbons.

### 3. What is the total weightage of Chemistry in JEE Main?

In JEE Main, Chemistry carries 100 marks out of the total 300 marks, with 25 questions asked from the subject.

### 4. Which section has the highest weightage in JEE Main Chemistry?

Generally, organic chemistry and physical chemistry carry slightly higher weightage compared to inorganic chemistry, though the exact distribution may vary each year.

### 5. Which chapters are considered high-weightage in JEE Main Chemistry?

Some high-weightage chapters include Coordination Compounds, Hydrocarbons, Thermodynamics, Chemical Bonding, Electrochemistry, and Chemical Kinetics.

### 6.  How can knowing chapter-wise weightage help in JEE Main preparation?

Understanding the JEE main chemistry chapter wise weightage helps students prioritize important topics, allocate study time effectively, and build a more focused preparation strategy.

### 7. Are low-weightage chapters important for JEE Main Chemistry?

Yes, even chapters with lower weightage should not be ignored because one or two questions from these topics can still impact the final score significantly.

### 8. Is NCERT enough for JEE Main Inorganic Chemistry?

Yes, most questions from Inorganic Chemistry in JEE Main are directly based on NCERT textbooks, so thorough revision of NCERT is essential.

Since Environmental Chemistry has been deemed to no longer be predominantly part of the NTA syllabus, it is highly unlikely that JEE Main 2026 will contain any questions directly related to the same.

### 10.How many questions for JEE 2026 will be asked covering Chemistry in Everyday Life and Polymers?

Chemistry in Everyday Life and Polymers have been reduced or eliminated, as with the Environmental Chemistry Section.

Organic Chemistry is a high-weightage section of JEE Main, accounting approximately 30-35% of the overall chemistry score. Topics covered include General Organic Chemistry (GOC), Hydrocarbons, Aldehydes, Ketones, Carboxylic Acids, and Alcohol/Phenol/Ether.
