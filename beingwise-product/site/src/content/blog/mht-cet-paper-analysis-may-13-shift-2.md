## Student Review on MHT CET Paper​ Analysis May 13 Shift 2

After the shift gets over, we have talked to the students to get their reaction to the shift 2 MHT CET May 12 Question Paper

- **According to Aman,** the overall MHT CET May 13 Shift 2 paper was moderate, though the physics paper was tough compared to other shifts. Most of the questions were calculation-based. Moreover, he further stated that mathematics was very lengthy and challenging.
- **According to Riya**, the easiest section of the paper was chemistry, as a large number of questions in chemistry were based on direct concepts taken from the NCERT books and standard textbooks. Though she faced some confusion regarding certain options in chemistry.
- Aditya revealed that the physics paper consisted of questions related to Electrodynamics, Ray Optics, and Modern Physics. The reason for the lengthy nature of this section was the requirement of lengthy calculations and mathematical solving skills.
- According to Sneha, mathematics was the most challenging section of this examination paper due to the presence of multi-part questions from calculus, probability, vector algebra, and integration.
- Kunal found the paper moderately tough because both the mathematics and physics sections were very lengthy and tough. The reason behind that was chemistry, as it was comparatively easy and theoretical.

## Subject Wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis 2026

The MHT CET 2026 PCM Question Paper comprises 150 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) for 200 marks divided into three sections: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, with 50 questions in each. Let’s know the subject-wise MHT CET Paper Analysis for the May 13 Shift 2 below.

### MHT CET 2026 Exam Analysis for Physics Section

The Physics section in the MHT CET Paper Analysis for the 13th May Shift 2 was considered Moderate to Tough by most candidates. It involved many calculative, lengthy, and concept-based numerical questions, which made time management tough for aspirants. Take a quick look at the key Topics Asked in this shift.

- Electrodynamics
- Modern Physics
- Ray Optics
- Mechanics
- Laws of Motion
- Calculation-intensive numerical problems

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

Chemistry is considered the easiest part of the examination. As per student review, most of the questions were theoretical in nature, which had direct links with the concepts of HSC textbooks. However, there were some tricky statement-based questions too. Take a quick look at the key topics asked in this shift

- Haloalkanes and Haloarenes
- Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers
- Nitrogen-Containing Compounds
- D and F Block Elements
- Solutions
- Chemical Kinetics
- Polymers

### MHTCET Paper Analysis for Maths Section

The Mathematics section of the examination can be termed as the lengthiest and most calculation-intensive one. Several students reported that some questions were quite lengthy and required a good understanding of concepts. Take a quick look at the key topics asked in this shift

- Vector Algebra
- Differential Calculus
- Maxima and Minima
- Monotonicity
- Increasing and Decreasing Functions
- Continuity and Differentiability
- Properties of Definite Integrals
- Permutation and Combination
- Probability
- Binomial Theorem
