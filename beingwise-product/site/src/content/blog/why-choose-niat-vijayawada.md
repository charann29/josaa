## What is the Recognition and Accreditation of NIAT in Vijayawada?

NIAT (NxtWave of Innovation in Advanced Technologies) Vijayawada is an upskilling program, not a college, university, or academic institution, and therefore does not hold any independent accreditation from bodies such as NAAC, UGC, or AICTE. The academic credibility comes entirely from the collaborating university, NRI Institute of Technology (NRIIT), which holds NAAC ‘A’ accreditation and is recognised by both UGC and AICTE.

NRIIT independently awards the B.Tech CSE degree with a specialisation in AI and Data Science to students meeting their eligibility criteria, while NIAT operates its Training Centre within NRIIT’s campus, delivering industry-aligned training modules, project-based learning, and skill-building sessions that complement the university degree program.

### **Does NIAT’s Skill Map and Training Content Get Updated Regularly?**

Yes, but it’s important to understand how this process works. NIAT continuously gathers real-world industry insights from a network of 3,000+ companies, 10,000+ tech professionals, and its in-house R&D teams. These insights are carefully compiled into structured reports and shared on a regular basis with the collaborating university.

However, it is entirely the collaborating university’s academic bodies, such as their Board of Studies, that review these inputs and decide whether to incorporate them into their degree curriculum through their own formal processes, in line with UGC and AICTE guidelines. The university alone has the authority to manage the delivery of its courses, lectures, seminars, and academic sessions.

In parallel, NxtWave independently conducts its own skill-building sessions, workshops, hands-on exercises, and industry-aligned training modules through NIAT’s Training Centre. These are not part of the university’s academic curriculum but are designed to complement it, helping students sharpen their critical thinking, problem-solving, and leadership abilities in ways that are directly relevant to today’s job market.
