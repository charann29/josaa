## **Common Mistakes to Avoid While Using YouTube for CAT Preparation**

CAT aspirants often make serious mistakes by depending too much on YouTube preparation. Early detection of these mistakes can save time and streamline their preparation process.

### **Watching too many channels without focus**

Students who switch between multiple YouTube channels end up confused rather than clear. New CAT aspirants feel overwhelmed when different teachers present various approaches to the same concept. Studies show that following a few select channels based on your learning style works better than trying to learn from too many sources.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Common Error** | **Better Approach** |
| Following 10+ channels simultaneously | Select 2-3 channels that match your learning style |
| Watching random videos without structure | Follow complete playlists on specific topics |
| Changing teachers frequently | Stick with teachers whose methods you understand |

### **Skipping basics and jumping to advanced topics**

Students feel tempted to tackle advanced material right away. Building strong fundamentals is a vital part of preparation. Most students don’t realise that scoring just 50 marks can lead to a 99 percentile, without covering the entire CAT syllabus.

### **Not revising or taking notes**

Watching without active participation substantially reduces retention. Videos should supplement practice, not replace it. An experienced mentor states, “Watching videos isn’t even compulsory. They are a waste of time until you don’t need them”. Students should try solving problems on their own first and then look for video explanations of concepts they find difficult.
