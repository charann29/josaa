## **JEE Mains 2026 Preparation Strategies**

Now you have a list of resources, so choosing the right JEE Mains Preparation strategy for the upcoming exam should be much easier. However, before you start, you need to keep a few things in mind. Let’s check them out:

- Before starting your JEE Mains Preparation, get familiar with the syllabus, the exam pattern, and the type of questions usually asked. It becomes easier to plan when you know what lies ahead.
- A daily study routine helps more than long study hours. Even **4 to 6 hours every day** works better than a full-day study once in a while.
- Every chapter doesn’t hold the same weight. Put extra time into topics that are tough and high-scoring and keep less time for chapters that just need revision.
- Whenever a doubt appears, get it clarified from your mentors, friends, online discussions, or video lectures. Remember that these doubts may become bigger barriers later.
- Keep written notes for every chapter. It will come in handy during your last-minute revision.
- A quick revision every day of whatever you studied helps the concepts stay fresh and reduces last-minute pressure.
- JEE Mains Mock Tests play a big role. They help you get used to the real exam format, and the more you take them, the faster and more accurate you become.
- Short notes of formulas and must-remember points are life-savers during revision, especially in the last 10-15 days.
- Try solving chapter-wise mock tests once you finish a chapter, and give full-length tests after completing the whole syllabus to judge your complete preparation.
