## CUET UG 2025 GAT Paper Analysis

Students found the GAT paper somewhat unpredictable, especially struggling with the Quant section, where many felt unsure about their answers. Around 70% of the paper focused on Match the Column and Chronology questions. Quant questions dominated, covering topics like Profit & Loss, Time & Distance, Height & Distance, and Data Interpretation, while GK questions were few and considered unpredictable. Students also found that there are a few questions from Reasoning, mostly on Series and Clock/Calendar. Many students noted a chronological question on the Vijayanagar Empire dynasties. Overall, the test was rated moderate but required careful time management, particularly in the reasoning and quant sections.

## CUET UG 2025 History Paper Analysis

Students found the exam very easy, with questions perfectly matching what they had studied, allowing some to finish 20 minutes early. The paper included two passages on Mahatma Gandhi’s Nationalist Movement and the Constitution’s framing. Many students found that the questions were focused on placing events and dynasties like Sulvas, Tulvas, Sangam, Arvinda, and Vijayanagar in chronological order. There were match-the-following questions on topics like travellers’ origins (Ibn Battuta, Marco Polo), land tenure terms (Peasants, Zamindars), and social hierarchies. However, as per the students, there were many specific questions which covered punch mark coins, Mirabai’s devotion to Lord Krishna, the Harappan script’s number of signs, matronymics linked to Satavahana rulers, etc. Check the detailed analysis in the table below.

## Student Reviews For CUET UG 2025 May 23rd Shift 2 Exam

Most students from different states found CUET UG 2025 Shift 2, which was held on May 23rd, was easy to moderate. Here is a summary of what students perceived about the exam.

**GAT:** Students found the General Aptitude Test moderately challenging, with a balanced mix of quantitative, reasoning, and chronological questions. Time management was crucial, especially for quantitative problems involving profit & loss and data interpretation.

**English:** The English section was considered moderate, featuring diverse reading comprehension passages, vocabulary questions, and grammar exercises. Students appreciated the clarity of questions but found some vocabulary and idioms tricky.

**History:** Students found the exam very easy, as the questions perfectly matched what they had studied, enabling some to finish 20 minutes early. The History paper focused on chronological events, key historical figures, and social movements. The questions were largely NCERT-based, making them familiar and straightforward.

**Geography:** Students found Geography easy, with questions covering physical geography concepts and human geography topics. The paper included both conceptual and application-based questions, which students found fair and well-balanced.

Overall, students felt the exam was fair and manageable, but emphasized the importance of time management, especially in the GAT and English sections.

## CUET UG 2025 Exam Day Guidelines

Given below are a few CUET UG 2025 exam day guidelines that students need to follow.

- **Carry Only Required Items:** Only take your printed admit card (with passport-size photograph), your valid photo ID (Aadhaar or PAN card), a transparent ballpoint pen, and a transparent water bottle. Candidates can check all the CUET exam details on the admit card and are advised to adhere to the instructions.
- **Be On Time:** Report to the exam center well in advance relative to your reporting time (7:00 AM for Shift 1 and 1:00 PM for Shift 2). If you are late, you will not be allowed to enter.
- **Prohibited Items:** Do not bring mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, bags, books, or food if not medically required (with documentation).
- **Maintain Discipline:** Be seated in your assigned seat, only do rough work on the sheets provided, and do not perform any unfair practice or make any noise in the exam hall.
- **Time Management:** It is a critical challenge across subjects, especially with lengthy sections like RC or Quant. It’s advised to tackle easy questions first.
- **The Normalisation Factor:** This factor will account for variations in difficulty across shifts. The focus should be on attempting as many questions with 100% accuracy.

### What’s New In CUET UG 2025?

Check out some new changes introduced by UGC and which will be implemented from CUET UG 2025 onwards.

- Students can now take CUET UG exams in any subject, regardless of their Class 12th subjects.

- The GAT scores will decide the admission for the removed CUET subjects.
- There will be no optional questions.
