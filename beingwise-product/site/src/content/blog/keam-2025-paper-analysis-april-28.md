## Exam Overview: KEAM, April 28th Shift

The KEAM exam for engineering has a different exam pattern compared to those who appear for the pharmacy exam. The exam consists of three sections, namely mathematics, chemistry, and physics. The exam will be concluded in 3 hours. While the correct responses will earn them four marks, the negative responses will make them lose one mark. Refer to the following table for more details:

## Answer Key & Response Sheet: KEAM 2025

The KEAM 2025 answer key has been released on the official website [cee.kerala.gov.in](https://cee.kerala.gov.in/). Candidates can also visit our page to download the shift-wise answer key PDFs. If there are any discrepancies, candidates have the option to challenge the answer key by paying a fee of ₹200 per question. After reviewing the objections, the final answer key will be published by the authorities at a later date.

In addition, the response sheet for each candidate is available in PDF format. It displays the answers marked by the candidate alongside the official correct responses for each question. Candidates can access and download their individual response sheets from the official website.

## April 28th KEAM 2025 Exam Analysis

Check the students reviews for the KEAM exam conducted on April 28th:

**Aarav Menon (from Ernakulam):** *“I found the exam quite easy. Physics had mostly direct, formula-based questions, and Chemistry felt very NCERT-centric. Maths was a bit lengthy in parts, but nothing too challenging. I had enough time to recheck a few answers as well.”*

**Sahana Reddy (from Thrissur):** *“The paper was easier than most of my mock tests. Chemistry was quick to finish, and Physics questions were clearly framed. Maths required some time, but it was manageable if you stayed focused. Overall, it felt like a smooth experience.”*

**Devraj (from Kollam):** *“Honestly, the exam felt very doable. I finished Chemistry fast since many questions were similar to what we practised. Physics had a few conceptual questions, but they weren’t tough. Even Maths was not as difficult as I thought it would be.”*
