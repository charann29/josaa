## KEAM Counselling & Seat Allotment Process

Aspirants can check out the KEAM counselling and seat allotment process below:

**Step 1: KEAM Counselling Registration**

The first step of the KEAM counselling process is registration, followed by filling out the option entry on the official website. Candidates should log in with their credentials and check the information before kicking off with choice filling.

**Step 2: Check KEAM Seat Matrix**

Candidates should first analyse the KEAM seat matrix in detail before filling preferences to know the college-wise and branch-wise seats available. This enables students to develop a list of options that is realistic and strategic.

**Step 3: Fill and Lock College Preferences**

Candidates are required to choose their respective college and course of their first choice, then their second choice, and so on. At this stage, applicants are advised to think about previous cut-off marks, seat availability, and their own career objectives before finalising their selections.

**Step 4: KEAM Seat Allotment**

The KEAM seat allotment process is based on ranks, reservation criteria, preferences given by the candidates of the test and availability of the seats. The outcome of allotments is available online for each round.

**Step 5: Place the Allotted Seat.**

An aspirant is required to accept the allotment within the required time. If the necessary actions are not taken, the assigned seat may be cancelled.

**Step 6: Payment of the fee and document verification.**

Once candidates accept their allotment, they must pay the prescribed fee for admission and document verification must be completed according to the instructions of the authorities. This step is required to ensure that you get the reserved seat.

**Step 7: Report to the Allotted Institute**

All the candidates should report to the allotted college within the time schedule after completion of all the formalities. Reporting to the institute is an integral part of the KEAM admission process and an indication that the candidate has been admitted to the institute.

**Step 8: Take part in Further Rounds (If applicable)**

Students who are not happy with the allotment or haven’t got a seat in the first round of KEAM counselling can attend the later rounds of the counselling, as long as they are eligible and seats are available.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. When will the KEAM Seat Matrix 2026 be released?

The KEAM Seat Matrix 2026 will be published during the counselling process. Candidates are advised to regularly check the official CEE Kerala announcements for updates regarding seat availability.

### 2. Is the KEAM seat matrix the same for every counselling round?

No, the KEAM seat matrix may change after each counselling round. Vacant seats, newly added seats, and seat withdrawals can affect the seat availability in subsequent allotment rounds.

### 3. Can the number of KEAM seats change after the seat matrix is published?

Yes, seat intake can change due to regulatory approvals, institutional updates, corrections, or additional seat sanctions. Any such revisions are generally announced by the counselling authorities.

### 4. How can I use the KEAM seat matrix to improve my admission chances?

The KEAM seat matrix helps candidates identify colleges and branches where they have stronger admission prospects. It also assists in making informed choices during the option-entry and counselling process.

### 5. Does the KEAM seat allotment process consider category reservations?

Yes, KEAM seat allotment follows the reservation policies prescribed by the authorities. Seat allocation is carried out according to category-wise reservation rules and candidate eligibility.

### 6. Can I participate in later counselling rounds after getting a seat?

Yes, eligible candidates may participate in subsequent counselling rounds, subject to the counselling rules and seat availability. This provides an opportunity to secure a better college or branch through seat upgradation.

### 7. Why should I check previous years’ KEAM seat matrix data?

Previous years’ KEAM seat matrix data helps candidates understand seat distribution patterns, admission trends, and branch-wise availability. This information can be useful for making realistic and strategic counselling choices.
