The Andhra Pradesh Engineering Agriculture Pharmacy Common Entrance Test is a state-level entrance examination conducted by APSCHE. Over 2 lakh candidates appear for the exam every year for entrance into undergraduate courses like engineering, pharmacy, and agriculture. Candidates require a strict preparation and revision plan to perform well in the exam. However, one should not ignore the Previous year’s question papers while preparing for the exam.

Previous year’s question papers give you a brief idea about the exam pattern, difficulty level of questions, and specific areas where you have to focus. Candidates can visit the [official website](https://cets.apsche.ap.gov.in/eapcet/eapcet/eapcet_exampapers.aspx) to download or refer to the previous year’s questions. This article will discuss the importance of the previous year’s question papers and why you should not miss them while preparing for the exam.

## Why Previous year’s Question Papers are Important?

Previous year’s question papers are a credible source for candidates to unveil the exam pattern and the difficulty level of the questions asked from each subject. Candidates are advised not to ignore the previous year’s question papers as they enhance their exam preparation.

Here are some benefits of practising the previous year’s questions:

- **Unveils the Exam Pattern:** Previous year’s question papers help you understand the exam pattern and the level of difficulty helping students curate their preparation strategy.

- **Time Management:** Practicing solving Previous year’s papers in exam-like conditions can help you analyse how you manage your time and help you strategise your exam.

- **Important Topics:** Previous year’s papers unveil the key areas of each subject and help you understand where you should focus.

- **Boost Confidence:** Solving multiple previous year papers helps you feel confident in yourself, which in turn helps you perform better in the exam.

- **Reveals Weak Areas:** Previous year papers reveal areas where you lack and require more focus.

- **Helps in revising:** Practicing Previous year papers also help you effectively revise helping you apply the concepts learnt.

## AP-EAPCET Exam Overview 2025

AP-EAPCET exam is held for a total number of 160 marks that are divided across three subjects, of which Mathematics constitute 80 marks and physics and chemistry constitute 40 marks each. The duration of the examination is 3 hours,i.e. 180 minutes, and there is no negative marking in the AP-EAPCET exam.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Subject** | **Marks** | **Duration** |
| Mathematics Physics Chemistry | 80 Marks 40 Marks 40 Marks | 180 Minutes |

Here are some last-minute tips for candidates to enhance their preparation:

- Practice important concepts and master them.
- Attempt mock tests and analyse your performance based on your scores.
- Practice time management as you have to attempt a total number of 160 questions in 180 minutes.
