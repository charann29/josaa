## **The 6 New Changes by NTA during JEE Mains 2025 Updates**

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has introduced six significant JEE Main 2025 updates to enhance exam transparency and fairness. These updates include the removal of optional questions in Section B, the introduction of negative marking for numerical value questions, a revised tie-breaking policy, a reduction in exam centres, the elimination of the age limit, and a refined exam pattern. Understanding these changes is crucial for aspirants to strategise their preparation and excel in the competitive examination.

- Removal of Optional Questions in Section B: The optional questions that were introduced during the pandemic have been removed. Candidates must now attempt all five numerical value questions in Section B of each subject. This adjustment marks a return to the pre-2021 exam pattern, eliminating flexibility in question selection. The discontinuation of optional questions also reflects a shift to the traditional structure.
- Revised Tie-Breaking Criteria The tie-breaking rules have been updated, although the sources do not disclose the specific details. These changes are anticipated to streamline the ranking process for candidates who achieve identical scores.
- Elimination of Age Limit The age criterion has been completely removed. Any candidate who meets the necessary educational qualifications is now eligible to apply, regardless of their age.
- Introduction of Negative Marking for Numerical Value Questions (NVQs) Negative marking will now be applied to numerical value questions. For every incorrect response given in an NVQ, one mark will be deducted. This aligns the NVQ marking scheme with that used for multiple-choice questions (MCQs). This change requires aspirants to exercise greater caution during the exam.
- Reduction in Exam Cities The total number of exam centers has been reduced. For domestic candidates, the number of exam centers decreased from 300 to 284. International exam cities also saw a decrease, which may affect accessibility for some overseas applicants.
- Increased Weightage for the Drawing Section in Paper 2A (B.Arch): The structure highlights show that for Paper 2A (B.Arch), the weightage for the Drawing section has increased. This section now constitutes 30% of the total marks.

## **JEE Main 2025 Updates For Exam Pattern and Structure**

The JEE Main 2025 exam pattern and structure have undergone several significant updates, returning to a more traditional format while introducing some new elements. Understanding these changes is crucial for effective preparation.

**Paper Structure**

**Paper 1 (B.E./B.Tech):**

- **Total Questions:** 90 (30 each from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics)
- **Section A:** 20 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) per subject
- **Section B:** 5 mandatory Numerical Value Questions (NVQs) per subject (optional questions removed; all must be attempted)
- **Marking Scheme:**
  - MCQs: +4 for correct answer, -1 for incorrect
  - NVQs: +4 for correct answer, **-1 for incorrect (negative marking newly introduced for NVQs)**
- **Total Marks:** 300

## **NTA Advisory and Instructions on Aadhar Issues Faced by Candidates**

According to the new JEE Main 2025 updates and guidelines, candidates are required to submit their Aadhaar number as proof of identity in the application form. If the name on the application doesn’t match the name on their Aadhar card, the application process can’t be completed. JEE Mains registrations are open, and many applicants are raising concerns while registering for the JEE Mains 2025 exam due to a mismatch between their name on their Aadhar card and on their Class 10th Certificate.

To address the rising concerns, NTA issued specific instructions for candidates facing this problem. Follow this detailed guide to resolve the Aadhaar name mismatch issue if you are experiencing the same problem.

- If a name mismatch pop-up appears during Aadhaar authentication, close the pop-up to proceed.
- A new window will open where candidates must enter their name exactly as it appears on their Aadhaar card.
- This will allow candidates to proceed with the application process and complete their registration.

If the candidate’s name is incorrect on the Aadhaar card, it is advised to visit the nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra to make the necessary changes.

## **NTA Guidelines for Creating a Password While JEE Mains 2025 Registration**

NTA has set specific guidelines for password creation while registering for the JEE Mains 2025 exam. Here are the important points.

- Length: The password must be 8–13 characters long.
- Security Question: A security question and answer must be chosen while applying for JEE Mains 2025. This will help you recover your password when you have forgotten it.
- Case Sensitivity: The password should include one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, and one numeric value

These guidelines help safeguard the applicant’s registration process and simplify password recovery if needed in the future.

## **JEE Mains 2025 Application Form Correction and City Intimation Slip**

The National Testing Agency (NTA) provides a one-time form correction window for registered candidates to edit certain details in their JEE Main 2025 application form. This facility is essential for candidates who may have made mistakes during the registration process.

**Key points:**

- The form correction window is typically announced on the official JEE Main website and is available only for a limited period after registration closes.
- Candidates can log in to the application portal using their credentials to access the correction window.
- Editable fields may include personal details (except name and date of birth), educational qualifications, exam city preferences, and category. Certain fields, such as mobile number and email ID, may not be editable.
- A non-refundable fee may be charged for specific corrections.
- It is crucial to verify all details before the correction window closes, as no further changes are allowed afterwards.

### **City Intimation Slip**

After the correction window and before the release of the admit card, NTA issues the City Intimation Slip for JEE Main 2025. This slip informs candidates about the city where their exam centre will be located.

**Key points:**

- The City Intimation Slip is released separately for each session (Session 1 and Session 2).
- Candidates can download the slip from the official JEE Main website by logging in with their application number and password.
- The slip specifies only the exam city, not the exact center address.
- The City Intimation Slip is NOT the admit card. The admit card, which includes the exact exam center address and other details, is released later.
- Candidates should use the information on the City Intimation Slip to plan their travel and accommodation if needed.

## JEE Mains 2025 Results and Answer Keys

Once the JEE Main 2025 examinations are conducted, the National Testing Agency (NTA) will release the official answer keys and results in a structured manner. Understanding the timeline and process is crucial for all aspirants.

### **Provisional Answer Key**

- The NTA will first publish the **provisional answer key** for each session on the official JEE Main portal ([jeemain.nta.nic.in](http://jeemain.nta.nic.in/)).
- Candidates can download the answer key by logging in with their application number, password, or date of birth.
- Alongside the answer key, the NTA also releases the candidates’ recorded responses.

### **Challenging the Answer Key**

- A **challenge window** will be open for a limited period (usually 2–3 days) after the provisional answer key is published.
- Candidates can submit objections to specific questions by providing supporting documents and paying a non-refundable fee per question challenged.
- Subject experts review all challenges before the final answer key is released.

### **Final Answer Key**

- After considering the challenges, the NTA will publish the **final answer key**.
- The final answer key serves as the basis for evaluating candidates’ responses and calculating scores.

### **Result Publication**

- The **JEE Main 2025 result date** will be announced on the official website.
- Results are declared online in the form of a **scorecard** that includes the candidate’s percentile, normalized score, and **All India Rank (AIR)**.
- Candidates can download their scorecards using their application credentials.

### **Checking and Downloading Results**

- Visit the official JEE Main portal and log in with your credentials.
- Download and save your **scorecard** for future reference, especially for counselling and admissions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How to resolve my name mismatch problem in the JEE Mains application?

Following the NTA’s instructions, if you receive a pop-up message during Aadhaar authentication about a name mismatch, simply close the pop-up. A new window will appear, allowing you to enter your name exactly as it appears on your Aadhaar card.

### 2. What should I do if my Aadhaar name is correct, but I’m still facing issues during the JEE Mains 2025 registration?

If the name on your Aadhaar is correct but you’re still encountering issues, ensure that you’re entering the spelling exactly as it appears on your Aadhaar card during registration. If the problem persists, please contact the NTA helpdesk for additional support.

### 3. Can I update the name on my Aadhaar card after completing the JEE Main 2025 registration?

It’s essential to ensure that the name on your Aadhaar card matches exactly with the name entered in the JEE Main registration form. Changing the name on your Aadhaar after completing the registration could lead to discrepancies later on.

### 4. Why did NTA release Aadhar card Authentication Guidelines during JEE Main 2025 updates?

The NTA released the Aadhaar card Authentication in response to complaints received from candidates registering for JEE Main 2025 regarding the Aadhaar authentication process. The candidates are facing issues due to a mismatch between their names on the Aadhaar and Class 10 Educational Certificates.

### 5. Is Aadhar Card Mismatch a problem while applying for JEE Main 2025?

As per the NTA guidelines, if the candidate’s Aadhar name mismatches, they need to close the pop-up error and continue with the process and enter the Aadhar card name. The system automatically captures both names.
