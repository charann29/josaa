### Q**2. How does the NIAT Skilling Centre in Vijayawada’s enrollment process work?**

Enollment to the NIAT upskilling program in Vijayawada provided by the collaborating University is based on the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT), followed by counselling. Students need to score at least 50% marks in 10+2 with PCM subjects to appear for NAT. After the counselling session, NIAT will send the students (with their written consent) to NRIIT. The final admission will be confirmed according to the norms of NRI University.

### Q7. How often is the NIAT skill map updated?

Although NIAT provides real-time industry insights through its network of 3,000+ companies, 10,000+ tech professionals, and internal R&D initiatives, these inputs are compiled into structured reports and periodically shared with the partner university. ### Q8. What do students say about the overall learning experience at NIAT Upskilling Program in Vijayawada?

Most students mention that the learning approach is more practical than traditional colleges, with a strong focus on upskilling programs, projects, and real-world problem-solving rather than only theory.

### Q9. What is NAT?

The NAT (NxtWave Assessment test) is a 90-minute test required for qualifying for NIAT’s upskilling program. The test has three sections: a psychometric assessment, a critical thinking section, and a mathematics section. The exam is quite different from traditional entrance tests like JEE Main because it focuses on assessing a student’s aptitude and potential rather than rote memorisation. Importantly, NAT does not include physics or chemistry, as its main goal is to identify whether a student has the mindset suited for technology and software development careers.

Computer Science and related specialisations like AI & ML, Data Science, and IT are among the most preferred branches, along with core streams like ECE and Civil Engineering.
