## KCET 2025 Physics Question Paper Analysis

The KCET 2025 Physics paper was reported to be **easy to moderately difficult** by most candidates. The paper included a balanced mix of conceptual questions and numerical problems. A majority of questions were  asked from important chapters, such as:

- Kinematics
- Current Electricity

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What is the KCET exam question paper?

The KCET question paper consists of 60 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) from each subject, including Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology depending on the course selected by the candidate. Each question carries 1 mark, and there is no negative marking in the exam. The questions are based on the 1st and 2nd year PUC syllabus of the Karnataka Board.

### 2. What is the rank for 70% in KCET?

A score of around 70% in KCET (approximately 125–130 marks out of 180) may correspond to a rank roughly between 8,000 and 15,000, depending on the difficulty level of the exam and overall candidate performance in that year.

### 3. How can I score 100+ marks in KCET?

To score 100+ marks in KCET, candidates should focus on NCERT concepts, practice previous year question papers, revise formulas regularly, and attempt mock tests. Consistent practice and strong conceptual understanding of important chapters can significantly improve the score.

### 4. Is KCET tougher than JEE?

No, KCET is generally considered easier than JEE Main. The KCET exam is mainly based on the PUC/NCERT syllabus, and most questions are direct and formula-based, while JEE Main includes more advanced conceptual and problem-solving questions.

### 5. Are KCET previous year question papers useful for preparation?

Yes, solving KCET previous year question papers helps candidates understand the exam pattern, frequently asked topics, and difficulty level. It also improves speed, accuracy, and time management, which are important for performing well in the exam.
