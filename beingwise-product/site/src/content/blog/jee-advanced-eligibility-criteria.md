## Minimum Marks and Percentage in Qualifying Exam

Based on the latest JEE Advanced eligibility criteria 2026, we have shared the list of acceptable qualifying exams and the minimum qualifying percentage given below

### IIT JEE Advanced Eligible Qualifying Exams

The following examinations are regarded as equivalent to Class 12 and qualify for the JEE Advanced entrance examination:

- Final Exam of the 10 + 2 pattern, conducted by the Central or State Boards, recognized by AIU
- An inter or two-year Pre-University exam from a recognized board or university
- Final exam of the Joint Services Wing of NDA
- Senior secondary education from the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) with at least five subjects
- Any school/board/university examinations held in India or overseas are recognized as equivalent to the 10 + 2 pattern examination by the AIU
- HSC Vocational examination
- A diploma course of minimum three years, recognized by AICTE or the state board of technical education
- GCE Advanced (A) level (London, Cambridge, or Sri Lanka)
- Cambridge High School Certificate or IB Diploma Geneva

### Minimum Percentage Requirement in Qualifying Exam

To fill the JEE Advanced Eligibility Criteria and get admission to IIT, the candidates need to clear the minimum percentage or percentile cut-offs of their respective Class 12 (or equivalent) examinations.

- General and OBC candidates must secure at least 75% aggregate marks in the qualifying exam
- SC/ST candidates must obtain a minimum of 65% aggregate marks
- The aggregate percentage is calculated based on marks obtained in the following subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, One Language subject, and any one additional subject
- Apart from the percentage requirement, candidates can also qualify if they are within the top 20th percentile of successful candidates in their respective board.  This rule helps maintain fairness across different education boards.

## JEE Advanced Eligibility Criteria for Class 12 Exam appearance

To be eligible for JEE Advanced 2026, candidates must meet the following criteria related to their Class 12 (or equivalent) examination:

- Candidate must have appeared for the first time for Class 12 examinations in either 2025 or 2026 in PCM.
- Students appearing in Class 12 examination for the first time in 2024 or prior years will NOT be eligible for JEE Advanced 2026, irrespective of the number of subjects attempted.

It should be emphasised that “appearance in Class XII (or equivalent) examination” means that the Board involved proclaimed the result for that year, regardless of whether the examination was held. It will be considered an appearance even if the outcome of a certain candidate is concealed.

### Special Provision for the Appeared Candidates

- In case any board declared Class 12 results for the academic year 2023-24 on or after 18 June 2024, those candidates who appeared in 2024 will be eligible for JEE Advanced 2026, subject to other requirements.
- However, in case the results for 2023-24 were declared by the respective boards before 18 June 2024 and if there is any withholding of results, then those candidates will NOT be eligible for JEE Advanced 2026.

## JEE Advanced Age Limit Criteria

Apart from fulfilling the educational JEE Advanced Eligibility Criteria, you must ensure to satisfy the IIT JEE Advanced Age limit also.

- **General/OBC Candidates:** The candidates need to be born on or after October 1, 2001.

For example, a candidate born on October 2, 2001, would be eligible, whereas one who was born on September 30, 2001, wouldn’t be.

- **SC/ST/PwD Candidates (Age Relaxation):** An age relaxation of five years is given to these candidates. They need to be born on or after October 1, 1996.

For example, a candidate born on October 5, 1996, would be eligible, while one who was born on September 30, 1996, would not be.

## JEE Adv Eligibility Regarding Previous Admission to IITs

For taking admission in JEE Advanced 2026, there are certain conditions that candidates need to fulfill as far as prior admission in IIT is concerned:

- Students should NOT have taken admission into any IIT according to the programs covered in JoSAA 2025 rules, irrespective of:

- Whether they have continued the program or not, OR
- Whether they have accepted the seat either by reporting online or in person.

- Students whose admission to any IIT was cancelled due to some reasons, even after joining the program, are also ineligible for JEE Advanced 2026.
- Candidates who have taken admission in any preparatory course of IIT for the very first time in 2025 are eligible to take JEE Advanced 2026.

### Eligibility for Candidates Allotted Seat in Any IIT by JoSAA 2025

The students allotted any seat in any IIT by JoSAA 2025 can still be considered as eligible for appearing in JEE Advanced 2026 if they satisfy one of the following criteria:

- If they had not reported either online or in person, OR
- If they had withdrawn their seat in any earlier round, OR
- If their seat got cancelled in any previous round of the IIT seat allocation.

## JEE Advanced 2026 Number of Attempts Allowed

As per the JEE Advanced eligibility criteria, **a candidate is permitted to give the JEE Advanced exam in a maximum of two attempts within two successive years.** This implies that as soon as a candidate makes his/her first attempt, they will be permitted another attempt only after that, but in the successive year only.

For example, if a candidate gives an examination in 2025, then his/her last attempt would be made in 2026 only.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What is the syllabus for JEE Advanced 2026?

The JEE Advanced 2026 syllabus covers key concepts from NCERT Classes 11 and 12 in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

### 2. What is the exam pattern for JEE Advanced 2026?

The JEE Advanced 2026 exam is a 6-hour Computer-Based Test consisting of two compulsory papers (Paper 1 and Paper 2), each of 3 hours duration, conducted on the same day in English and Hindi.

### 3. What type of questions are asked in JEE Advanced 2026?

The exam includes a mix of multiple-choice questions (MCQs), numerical value-based questions, and match-the-following type questions from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

### 4. Is the JEE Advanced exam conducted online or offline?

The JEE Advanced exam is conducted in online computer-based mode at designated exam centres.

### 5. What are the key topics in the JEE Advanced 2026 Chemistry syllabus?

The Chemistry syllabus includes physical, inorganic, and organic chemistry topics such as chemical bonding, transition elements (d-block), coordination compounds, electrochemistry, and more.

### 6. What are the high-weightage topics in JEE Advanced 2026 Physics?

Important high-weightage topics include mechanics, electricity, electrostatics, optics, thermodynamics, and semiconductor electronics based on past trends.
