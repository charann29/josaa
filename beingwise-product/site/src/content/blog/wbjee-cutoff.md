## Table of Contents

WBJEE 2026 registration has already started. The exam is expected to take place on May 24, 2026. The cutoff will be released after the results, during the counselling process. It will come out in different rounds, and the ranks may change from one round to another depending on seat availability and student choices. WBJEE cutoff means the minimum rank needed to get admission into engineering colleges in West Bengal. It is different for every college, branch, and category.

Looking at recent trends, general category students usually need around 120 to 130 marks for top colleges. Jadavpur University CSE often closes within the ranks 1 to 120. OBC candidates may fall in the range of 150 to 250 for top branches. For SC and ST candidates, the cutoff can go higher, sometimes between 4000 and 11000. Private colleges like IEM Kolkata and Heritage Institute usually have higher closing ranks. For CSE, the cutoff in these colleges often falls between 6000 and 12000. These numbers help you understand what kind of rank is needed, though the final cutoff may change each year.

## **WBJEE Qualifying Marks 2026**

WBJEE does not officially define qualifying marks. Still, candidates need a minimum score to be considered for ranking and counselling. Based on past trends, general category students usually need around 45 to 50 marks. Reserved categories may qualify with a lower score, often between 25 and 40 marks. Scoring below this range makes it difficult to get a useful rank.

## **Good Score in WBJEE 2026**

A good score in WBJEE depends on your category, the difficulty level of the exam, and the competition in that year. Still, based on recent trends, certain score ranges can give a clear idea of what to expect. For the general category, a score above 130 is considered strong and can help you get top government colleges like Jadavpur University. Scores between 100 and 120 are usually enough for good private colleges such as IEM or Heritage. If the score falls below 90, college options become limited, especially for top branches. For OBC candidates, a score above 110 is considered excellent. Scores between 90 and 110 are still good and can secure admission in decent colleges. A score below 85 may reduce better options. For SC and ST candidates, the cutoff is more relaxed. A score above 75 is considered strong. Scores between 60 and 80 can still offer good opportunities. Even scores between 40 and 70 may help in getting admission to several colleges.

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Category** | **Excellent** | **Good** | **Average** |
| General | 130+ | 100 to 120 | 70 to 90 |
