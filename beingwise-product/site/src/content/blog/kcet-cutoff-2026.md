## Table of Contents

KCET Cutoff 2026 will be released by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) after each round of counselling. The cutoff represents the closing rank required for admission to a specific college and branch through the Karnataka Common Entrance Test. For highly demanded courses like CSE, the cutoff ranks are usually very competitive. For example, in KCET 2025, the CSE closing rank at RV College of Engineering was 949, while MS Ramaiah Institute of Technology closed at 2076 and BMS College of Engineering at 3870 in the final round.

Based on previous admission trends, the expected KCET cutoff 2026 for CSE in top colleges is likely to remain within 500-2000 ranks, while mid-tier colleges may close between 5000-15000 ranks depending on the branch and category. The KCET cutoff varies based on several factors such as **number of candidates, difficulty level of the exam, seat availability, reservation categories, and previous admission trends**. In this article, candidates can check the KCET 2026 expected cutoff, college-wise cutoff, category-wise cutoff, and previous year cutoff.

## How to Check KCET Cutoff 2026

Candidates can check the KCET 2026 cutoff online through the official website of the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA). The cutoff is released after each counselling round in the form of a PDF containing the opening and closing ranks for different colleges, courses, and categories. Candidates can follow the steps below to check the official cutoff:

1. Visit the official website of KEA at <https://cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea/>.
2. Click on the “KCET 2026 Cutoff” or “UGCET Cutoff” link available on the homepage.
3. Select the counselling round (Mock Allotment, Round 1, Round 2, or Final Round).
4. Open the cutoff PDF for the respective round.
5. Search for your college name, course, and category in the list.
6. Check the opening and closing ranks for the selected program.
7. Download and save the cutoff PDF for future reference.

## Factors Affecting KCET Cutoff 2026

The KCET cutoff changes every year depending on multiple factors:

- Total number of candidates appearing for the exam
- Difficulty level of the question paper
- Number of seats available in colleges and branches
- Reservation policies and quotas
- Popularity of specific courses like CSE or ECE
- Previous year admission trends

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What are the cutoff marks for KCET?

KCET does not have fixed cutoff marks. Instead, admissions are based on closing ranks for each college and course during counselling. The cutoff rank varies every year depending on factors such as exam difficulty, number of candidates, and seat availability.

### 2.  Is 7000 a good rank in KCET?

Yes, a 7000 rank in KCET is considered a good rank. With this rank, candidates may get admission to mid to good engineering colleges, especially in branches like ECE, Mechanical, Civil, or Information Science depending on the category and counselling round.

### 3.  Is 48,000 a good rank in KCET?

A 48,000 rank in KCET is considered an average rank. Candidates may still get admission in mid-tier or private engineering colleges, particularly in branches such as Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, or newer specializations.

### 4.  How many marks are required to get a 10,000 rank in KCET?

To get a rank around 10,000 in KCET, candidates usually need to score around 130–140 marks in the exam. However, the exact rank also depends on the candidate’s normalized Class 12 (PUC) marks, as KCET rank calculation includes both scores.

### 5. What rank is required for CSE in top KCET colleges?

For top engineering colleges such as RVCE, BMSCE, and MS Ramaiah Institute of Technology, the CSE cutoff usually closes below 2000 rank for the General category. The exact cutoff may vary slightly each year based on competition and seat availability.
