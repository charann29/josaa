## Understanding the NTA Score and Result Information

The JEE Main 2025 result for Paper 1 (BE/BTech) is released in the form of an NTA Score, also known as a percentile score. The NTA Score is not the same as raw marks; instead, it is calculated using the normalisation process to ensure fairness across multiple exam sessions.

### How is the NTA Score Calculated?

- The NTA Score (percentile) reflects a candidate’s relative performance compared to others who appeared in the same session.
- The formula for the NTA Score is:
  NTA Score = (Number of candidates who scored equal to or less than you in the session / Total number of candidates in that session) x 100.
- This process ensures that variations in exam difficulty across sessions do not impact a candidate’s performance evaluation.

### Presentation of Results

- The NTA Score for each subject (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) and the overall score are displayed on the result page.
- The result does not list raw marks; instead, it shows the percentile scores for each subject and the total.
- After both sessions are completed, the best NTA Score across the two attempts is used for the final result and to determine eligibility for JEE Advanced.

### Significance for Candidates

- The NTA Score is crucial for shortlisting candidates for JEE Advanced and for admissions through the JoSAA counselling process.
- It is also used to prepare the All India Rank (AIR), which is published separately.
- Candidates should understand that the NTA Score is not a percentage of marks but a percentile rank based on relative performance.
