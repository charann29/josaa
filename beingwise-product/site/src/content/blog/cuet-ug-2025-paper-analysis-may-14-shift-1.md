## CUET UG Exam Pattern 2025

The CUET UG exam pattern is conducted to test the candidates across three sections, namely language, domain-specific, and general aptitude. Candidates are allowed 60 minutes for each subject. Check the detailed CUET UG exam pattern from the following table:

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Section** | **No of Questions** | **Duration of the Exam** | **Questions Type** |
| Section IA- Language | 50 Questions | 60 minutes for each subject | Questions from reading comprehension are mostly asked to assess the language skills of the candidate. |
| Section II- Domain Specific | 50 Questions | MCQS on the domain-specific subjects. |
| Section III- General Test | 50 Questions | The section will evaluate a candidate’s proficiency in General Knowledge, Current Affairs, General Mental Ability, Numerical Ability, and Quantitative Reasoning, drawing on foundational concepts from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and measurement taught up to Grade 8. It will also test Logical and Analytical Reasoning skills. |

## Student Reviews for CUET UG 2025 May 14th Shift 1 Exam

Most students from different states found CUET UG 2025 Shift 1 (held on May 14th) to be easier to moderate compared to last year. Here are some student reviews for reference:

****Ananya (from Delhi):****

“Physics was a bit tougher than expected, especially because of the numericals. They took a lot of time. Chemistry felt easier, though Organic had some tricky questions. GAT was fine overall, but GK questions were tough and unexpected.”

**Rohit (from Jaipur):**

“For me, Physics was time-consuming due to lengthy calculations. Chemistry was simple and direct. In GAT, Reasoning questions, especially Venn Diagrams, came a lot. Maths was there but less. GK was confusing and tough.”

**Priya (from Lucknow):**

“I found Chemistry easy, but Organic was slightly tough. Physics had too many numericals, so it felt moderate to tough. GAT was unbalanced—Reasoning dominated. GK questions were out of syllabus types, quite difficult.”

**Kunal (from Mumbai):**

“Physics was tougher than the last shift, mainly due to lots of numericals. Chemistry was easy for me, except for some Organic parts. GAT had mostly Reasoning, especially Venn Diagrams. GK was a bit of a negative point.”

**Sakshi (from Bhopal):**

“Chemistry was the easiest section. Physics had heavy numericals, so it took more time. GAT was okay but had more Reasoning than Maths. GK questions were difficult and not from expected topics.”
