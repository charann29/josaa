## CUET 2025 Exam Pattern

The CUET exam tests the candidate’s skills across language, subject, and general aptitude. Candidates are allotted 60 minutes for each subject, and candidates can select up to five subjects in the CUET UG 2025 examination. Check the following table for the detailed exam pattern:

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Section** | **No of Questions** | **Duration of the Exam** | **Questions Type** |
| Section IA- Language | 50 Questions | 60 minutes for each subject | Candidates are tested on their English language proficiency, and questions of the type Reading comprehension are asked. |
| Section II- Domain Specific | 50 Questions | Domain-specific questions based on class 12th are asked in the exam. |
| Section III- General Test | 50 Questions | This section evaluates a candidate’s General Knowledge, Current Affairs, Logical and Analytical Reasoning, and Mental Ability skills. It also tests numerical and quantitative reasoning based on fundamental concepts from arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and mensuration up to grade 8. |

## Student Reviews for CUET UG 2025 May 19th Shift 1 Exam

Check what students say about the CUET exam, May 19th Shift 1 exam.

**1. Anjali (from Delhi):**
“The English section totally surprised me! I didn’t expect grammar questions—there were at least 7 of them. Thankfully, they were easy, but I lost time thinking about them. RCs and vocab were manageable. I’d say English was easy to moderate overall.”

**2. Rohan (from Lucknow):**
“The General Test felt endless! Quant was okay in terms of difficulty, but there were too many questions, and they took time. Logical reasoning was tricky, and GK was less this time. I managed to attempt only 38 questions. It felt moderate but tiring.”

**3. Sneha (from Hyderabad):**
“Physics was alright—mostly formula-based. There were more questions from Ray Optics than I expected. If you’ve revised the NCERT thoroughly and practised numericals, you’d find it doable. I’d call it moderate overall, leaning towards easy if you’re prepared.”

**4. Aditya (from Patna):**
The accounts paper was decent. But again, they added Computerised Accounting questions—easy but unexpected. Ratio-based questions were confusing. You really have to go beyond just the basics to be safe. I’d say it was mixed: theory and practical were well-balanced.”

**5. Mehak (from Jaipur):**
“Hindi was mostly NCERT-based, but they changed the pattern a little. Questions from Samas and Vilom Shabd were simple, but the passages were tricky. I think scoring is possible if you’re strong in basic grammar and read all passages carefully. It felt moderate to me.”
