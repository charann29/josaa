## How to Analyse JEE Mains Mock Test Score

Given below are the essential steps that consist of verifying totals and subject-wise breakdowns to classifying errors, tracking time, identifying weak topics, and maintaining a mistake notebook. Candidates can also revisit concepts, practise gaps, set goals, and compare with past performances for steady progress.

- Verify your total score, rank, and precision
- Look at subject-specific scores across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
- Divide questions into segments: accurately answered, incorrectly answered, and not attempted
- Look for any penalties and ascertain the cause behind each error
- Classify mistakes into: theory, computation, time-managed poorly, or just random
- Evaluate the time spent across each question and overall for each subject
- Mark any topics where performance was consistent and error-free
- Highlight topics where performance was subpar, and score loss was evident
- Evaluate whether the incorrectly answered questions could actually be answered, i.e., were they indeed solvable using appropriate methodologies
- Create a notebook or document specifically for mistakes
- Revisit the concepts pertaining to the questions that were handled incorrectly and those that were just skipped
- Answer questions that pertain to the areas where practice is insufficient
- Establish new goals for the upcoming mock exam
- Use prior mock exams to establish a baseline and draw comparisons for tracking progress.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid While Attempting JEE Mains Mock Tests

- Doing mock tests without having proper knowledge of the JEE Main syllabus and the latest exam pattern can lead to poor exam readiness.
- Attempting a JEE Mains mock test free online or a JEE Main free mock test casually, instead of treating it seriously like the real examination.
- Mismanaging time during a mock test, which later results in inadequate time management in the actual JEE Mains exam.
- Focusing only on the final score and neglecting detailed analysis of the errors made after each mock test.
- Guessing too many questions during mock tests increases the chances of negative marking in the real exam.
- Failing to revise weak areas identified through mock test performance.
- Taking mock tests at irregular intervals instead of following a consistent practice schedule.
- Solving mock tests without revisiting concepts from reliable JEE main preparation books, leading to repeated conceptual mistakes.

- Avoiding full-length mock exams and relying only on topic-wise practice tests, which do not simulate real exam conditions effectively.
