## Subject-wise April 17 Shift 1 MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis

Based on the students’ reaction to the MHTCET Paper Analysis, the Mathematics Section was the toughest section, where students had to calculate quickly. On the other hand, Physics was moderately difficult with conceptual and numerical questions. However, Chemistry was the highest-scoring section as it consisted of straightforward questions.

### April 17 Shift 1 MHT CET 2026 Paper Analysis  for Maths

Mathematics was one of the hardest sections due to its lengthy nature, despite being moderately tough. Mostly based on formulas and numericals, the questions tested conceptual clarity and quick problem-solving skills. Take a look at the key maths topics asked in this shift as follows:

- Calculus (Differentiation & Integration)
- Probability
- Vectors
- 3D Geometry
- Matrices & Determinants

Based on the MHT CET Exam Analysis, many students felt that it was challenging to complete the entire section in the given amount of time.  No question appeared out of the syllabus in this shift; however, due to the length of the paper, Maths became quite an important section in terms of scoring.

### April 17 Shift 1 MHT CET Analysis for Physics Section

As per the MHT CET Paper Analysis, the physics section was neither hard nor easy, but somewhere in between, including an equal number of conceptual and numerical questions. This time, the section aimed at testing conceptual knowledge rather than application. Take a look at the key physics topics below.

- Current Electricity
- Optics
- Rotational Dynamics
- Modern Physics
- Laws of Motion

Many students said that a few questions involved multiple steps in solving them, which made this section moderately time-consuming for the candidates. Those candidates who were well-versed with the concepts and had solved enough numericals found it easier than others.

### MHT CET Exam Analysis for Chemistry Section

Based on the MHT CET Paper Analysis, the Chemistry section was comparatively easier. It was the most scoring section amongst the three, as the questions asked were mostly straightforward theory-based questions from the HSC syllabus. The section took up less time than Maths and therefore helped the candidates gain some extra time for themselves. Here are some of the important chemistry topics that were asked throughout this shift:

| **Section** | **Topics Covered** |
| --- | --- |
| Inorganic Chemistry | Mole concept and stoichiometry, Chemical kinetics (rate laws, order of reaction), Thermodynamics (basic laws, enthalpy changes), Electrochemistry (numericals, formulas), Solutions and colligative properties, Simple formula-based numericals (easy to moderate level) |
| Physical Chemistry | Mole concept and stoichiometry, Chemical kinetics (rate laws, order of reaction), Thermodynamics (basic laws, enthalpy changes)Electrochemistry (numericals, formulas), Solutions and colligative properties, Simple formula-based numericals (easy to moderate level) |
