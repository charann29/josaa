## ****NTA Announces Changes to JEE Main 2025 Exam Centres****

NTA announced a few changes to the JEE Mains 2025 exam centers. According to the sources NTA conducted Jee mains for 2024 in 300 cities across India. As per the recent update, JEE Mains 2025 will be conducted across **284 cities.** The **count of a few international centers is also reduced.** The changes in **JEE Main exam centers** for 2025 aim to improve accessibility for both domestic and international candidates.

- In 2024, the JEE Main exam was conducted in 24 cities across 23 countries, but for 2025, it will be held in 15 cities across 13 countries.
- In India, Bihar will now have a total of 11 exam centers, including Bihar Sharif as a newly added location.
- Other cities in Bihar hosting exam centers include Patna, Aurangabad, Bhagalpur, Darbhanga, Gaya, Muzaffarpur, Purnia, Ara, Samastipur, and Rohtas.
- Bihar Sharif will host the JEE Mains exam for the first time, providing greater convenience for local students to take the test.
- Several international exam centers have been removed, including those in Canada, Sri Lanka, and Russia.
- For example, Ottawa, Canada, will no longer have a JEE exam center, while new centers have been established in Munich, Germany, and Abu Dhabi, UAE (Dubai).
- Other countries, including Bahrain, Indonesia, Kuwait, Malaysia, and the USA, will continue to host the exam. In Indonesia, Jakarta has been replaced by West Java as the new exam location.

### Understanding JEE Main 2025 Exam Centre Cities Codes and Details

When applying for JEE Mains 2025, each exam centre is assigned a unique city code by the National Testing Agency (NTA). These codes are essential for accurately selecting and identifying your preferred test location during the application process. You will need to enter the correct city code corresponding to your chosen exam centre in the application form.

**Key Points about JEE Main 2025 Exam Centres List Codes:**

- **Unique Identifier:** Every exam city has a specific code (e.g., DL01 for Delhi/New Delhi, AP18 for Visakhapatnam).
- **Required in Application:** You must enter the city code, not just the city name, to ensure your application is processed correctly.
- **Reference Table:** The complete list of cities and their codes is provided below for your convenience.
- **Updates and Changes:** Some cities may have new codes or may be newly added, such as Bihar Sharif (BR38), Nagercoil (TN24), or Jagtial (TL15). Always refer to the latest official list.
- **International Centres:** International locations also have unique codes (e.g., ZZ02 for West Java, ZZ05 for Munich).

**How to Use Exam Centre Codes:**

1. Review the list of available exam centres and their codes.
2. Decide your preferred exam cities (up to four choices).
3. Enter the corresponding codes in the application form in order of preference.
4. Double-check for accuracy to avoid errors in centre allocation.

If the NTA announces any updates or changes to centre codes, always refer to the official website for the most current information.
