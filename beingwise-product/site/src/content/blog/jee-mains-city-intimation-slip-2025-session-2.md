### **Difference Between JEE Mains City Intimation Slip 2025 Session 2 and Admit Card**

Candidates need to understand the distinction between the JEE Main City Intimation Slip and the Admit Card. The NTA issues both documents, but they serve different purposes and are released at different times.

**City Intimation Slip:**

- The city intimation slip is released first, typically 8 to 10 days before the exam.
- It only provides information about the exam city and the date of the examination allotted to the candidate.
- The slip does **not** mention the exact exam centre name or address.
- Its primary purpose is to help candidates plan travel and accommodation based on the allotted city.
- The city intimation slip is **not** a mandatory document for entry into the exam hall; it is solely for informational purposes.

**Admit Card:**

- The admit card is released a few days after the city intimation slip, usually 4 to 5 days before the exam.
- It contains all essential exam details, including:
  - Candidate’s name and roll number
  - Exam date and time
  - **Exact exam centre name and full address**
  - Important instructions for exam day

The admit card is a **mandatory** document. Candidates must carry a printed copy of the admit card and a valid photo ID to the exam centre. Entry will not be permitted without it.

## Corrections and Issues with the JEE Mains City Intimation Slip 2025 Session 2

If you encounter any problems with your JEE Main City Intimation Slip for Session 2, follow these guidelines:

1. **Errors or Discrepancies in the Slip:** If you notice any major errors in your city intimation slip, such as an incorrect name, wrong photograph, or other critical personal information, contact the National Testing Agency (NTA) immediately. Use the official helpdesk:

- Phone: 011-69227700 or 011-40759000
- Email: jeemain@nta.ac.in

Minor discrepancies that do not affect your identification or exam logistics may not require correction, but it is always safest to clarify with NTA.

2. **Lost or Misplaced City Intimation Slip:** If you lose your city intimation slip, you can easily download it again from the official JEE Main website (jeemain.nta.nic.in) using your application number and date of birth. There is no limit to the number of times you can download the slip before the exam date.

3. **Allotted City Not as Preferred:** The exam city is allotted based on the preferences you selected during registration and the availability of seats. If you are assigned a city different from your preferred choices, understand that the NTA’s allotment is final and cannot be changed. Requests for changing the exam city are not entertained under any circumstances.

4. **Slip Not Available or Download Issues:** If your city intimation slip is not available on the official website or you face download issues, contact the NTA helpdesk immediately for assistance.
