**National-Level Entrance Exams**

The most important national-level engineering entrance exams widely accepted by these institutions include:

- **JEE Main (Joint Entrance Examination Main):** Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), JEE Main is the primary national-level exam for admission into many private and government engineering colleges across India, including most in the Delhi NCR region. It tests Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics based on the 12th-grade syllabus and is often the baseline qualification for admissions.
- **CUET (Common University Entrance Test):** CUET-UG scores have recently been accepted by some private universities like Jaypee Institute of Information Technology and Noida International University as a mode of admission for various UG engineering courses.
- **Other National Tests:** Some colleges also accept scores of exams like SAT, ACT (for Shiv Nadar University), and GATE (for postgraduate engineering programs), showing flexibility in admission criteria at the national level.

**State-Level Entrance Exams**

For the Uttar Pradesh state region (which includes Noida and Greater Noida), the state-level common entrance test facilitates admissions:

- **UPCET (Uttar Pradesh Combined Entrance Test):** Formerly known as UPSEE, UPCET is a key state-level entrance test recognized by many private engineering colleges in Uttar Pradesh, including GL Bajaj Institute of Technology & Management and others. UPCET is often considered alongside JEE Main scores for seat allocation.

**Institute-Specific Entrance Exams**

Several universities conduct their own entrance tests in addition to accepting national and state exam scores:

- **Amity University:** Conducts the Amity Joint Entrance Exam (Amity JEE), a computer-based test aligned with CBSE 12th syllabus subjects, mainly Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and English. The exam evaluates preparedness for undergraduate engineering programs.
- **NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test)**: The NAT is an assessment for NIAT Noida that helps evaluate a student’s skills, interests, and aptitude to recommend the most suitable UGC-recognised university and NIAT Industry-ready Upskilling Program. It’s a crucial part of the admission process to determine eligibility for NIAT’s collaborative programs, which are designed to prepare students for the tech industry.
- **Shiv Nadar University:** Uses SNUSAT, a computer-based test with multiple sections (verbal reasoning, quantitative ability, abstract reasoning, essay) followed by an Academic Proficiency Test (APT) for engineering candidates. Alternatively, SAT or ACT scores are accepted to facilitate admission.
- **Sharda University:** Conducts the Sharda University Admission Test (SUAT) alongside accepting national-level exam scores like JEE Main.
- **Noida International University:** Has NSAT as its entrance exam, but also accepts JEE Main and CUET UG scores.

**Common Admission Process**

- Most colleges require candidates to meet eligibility criteria such as a minimum percentage in 10+2 with PCM subjects.
- Selection is typically based on entrance exam scores, followed by counseling or personal interview/group discussion rounds, especially in Amity University and Noida International University cases.
- Many institutes also consider merit and cutoffs based on seat availability and reservation policies.

**Admission Process**

1. Submit the NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) application form online by filling in personal and academic details and paying a non-refundable registration fee of INR 1200.
2. Appear for the NAT exam, a 90-minute test that assesses psychometric traits, critical thinking, and mathematical skills.
3. Upon passing NAT, attend a one-on-one counselling session to discuss goals and preferences.
4. Based on counselling and test performance, apply to Noida International University, which collaborates with the NIAT Noida. Final admission is subject to the guidelines of the UGC/AICTE.

**Documents Required**

NIU provides the document checklist and submission details during the admission process. Some documents may need to be uploaded online or emailed to the admission cell. Admission confirmation is issued upon receipt of payment and verification of documents.

- 10th mark sheet/certificate
- 12th mark sheet with at least 50% aggregate, preferably with Maths, Physics, and Chemistry
- Valid photo ID (Aadhaar Card)
- Passport-sized photographs
- Entrance exam scorecard (NAT)
- Other documents, like transfer/migration certificates, if applicable
