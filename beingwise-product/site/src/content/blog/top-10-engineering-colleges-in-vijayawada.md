### Entrance Examinations

These are the entrance examinations accepted by the Top 10 engineering colleges in Vijayawada:

- **AP EAPCET:** AP EAPCET exam conducted by APSCHE is a popular entrance exam for engineering. The exam scores are widely accepted across the state. Qualifying students have to appear for the counselling processes and are allotted admissions based on their rank and preferences.
- **JEE Main:** JEE Main is a national-level exam for admission to engineering and architecture programs in India. JEE Mains scores are accepted by many engineering colleges in Vijayawada.
- **NAT (Private Entrance Exam):** NxtWave Assessment Test is an entrance examination conducted by NIAT Vijayawada. Students are tested on their aptitude and basic mathematical skills. Upon qualifying, students are offered admission.

### Admission Process

The admission process for the engineering colleges in Vijayawada usually follows a step-by-step process of appearing for the entrance examination, counselling processes, and reporting to the allotted colleges. Check the admission process for the engineering colleges in Vijayawada below:

- Those students who are seeking admission through AP EAPCET have to qualify for the exam and appear for the counselling process. Students have to select their preferences based on their choice and are allotted admissions based on their rank, category, and vacancies. Students can participate in the subsequent rounds of the counselling process if they are not allotted colleges in the first round.
- Private Institutions like NIAT Vijayawada offer admissions for students who have qualified for the NxtWave Assessment Test (NAT).
- Students are also eligible for management quota admissions as 30-40% seats are filled through management quota.

### NIAT Vijayawada Admission Process

1. Submit the NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) Application Form online by filling in your details and paying a INR 1200 non-refundable registration fee.
2. Appear for the NAT, a 90-minute test assessing cognitive abilities:
   - Psychometric Test: 44 questions in 25 minutes (personality and emotional traits)
   - Critical Thinking: 20 questions in 30 minutes (analytical and adaptive skills)
   - Mathematics: 17 questions in 35 minutes (practical problem-solving)
3. If qualified, have a one-on-one counselling session to discuss goals and university preferences.
4. Based on the NAT (NxtWave Assessment Test) score and counselling, receive NRI Institute of Technology recommendation, collaborating with NIAT.
5. Apply to the NRIIT admission is subject to the NRI Institute of Technology’s discretion as per UGC and AICTE norms.
