## How Many WBJEE Mock Tests Should You Attempt?

There is no fixed number of mock tests that works for every candidate. The ideal frequency depends on your preparation level and how well you analyse each test.

- In the initial stage, start with one mock test per week to understand the exam pattern and improve basic time management
- As your preparation progresses, increase the frequency to two to three mock tests per week
- In the final phase, attempt full-length mock tests regularly to build confidence and exam readiness

## When to Start Taking WBJEE Mock Tests?

The timing of mock tests should align with your preparation stage. A phase-wise approach makes preparation more structured and helps you gradually move from concept building to full exam readiness.

- **Phase 1 (Early Stage):** Focus on completing the syllabus alongside topic-wise tests. This helps in strengthening basic concepts and understanding individual chapters
- **Phase 2 (Mid Stage):** Start attempting sectional tests to improve accuracy, identify weak areas, and work on time management across subjects
- **Phase 3 (Final Phase):** Shift completely to full-length mock tests along with regular revision. This stage is important for building exam confidence and simulating real exam conditions

## Section-wise Strategy for WBJEE Mock Tests

WBJEE has two papers, but the scoring dynamics are not equal. Mathematics alone carries 100 marks, while Physics and Chemistry have 50 marks each. Because of this, your mock test strategy should reflect where marks actually come from.

### For Mathematics (Paper 1)

Mathematics contributes nearly 50% of the total score, so your mock test performance here directly impacts your rank.

- Prioritise high-weightage chapters like Vectors, Probability, 3D Geometry, Matrices, and Calculus, as they can cover a significant portion of the paper
- While analysing mocks, check whether you are consistently scoring from these topics or missing easy marks
- Focus on calculation accuracy, especially in Category I and II questions where negative marking applies
- If a question feels lengthy, skip and return later, WBJEE Maths is time-consuming, so question selection becomes important

### For Physics (Part of Paper 2)

Physics questions in WBJEE are a mix of formula-based and numerical problems, often drawn from specific recurring topics.

- Focus on high-weightage areas like Current Electricity, Electrostatics, Magnetic Effects, and Thermodynamics
- In mocks, check if mistakes are due to formula gaps or calculation errors
- Attempt direct formula-based questions first before moving to lengthy numericals
- Track time spent per question—Physics can become time-consuming if not managed properly

### For Chemistry (Part of Paper 2)

Chemistry is generally considered the most scoring section in WBJEE, especially when preparation is revision-focused.

- Prioritise topics like Chemical Bonding, Kinetics, Organic Chemistry, and p-block elements, which frequently appear
- Use mock tests to identify memory-based errors rather than conceptual gaps
- Attempt Chemistry questions early during the test to secure quick marks
- Focus on improving accuracy, as most questions are direct and less time-consuming

## How to Attempt WBJEE Mock Tests Effectively?

The way you attempt a mock test plays a major role in how much you actually gain from it. Simply giving tests without the right approach won’t reflect your real performance, so it’s important to take each mock seriously.

- Sit in a quiet, distraction-free environment to maintain proper focus
- Stick to the exact time limit and avoid extending it under any circumstances
- Do not pause the test midway, even if you find questions difficult
- Treat every mock test like the actual WBJEE exam

## How to Use WBJEE Mock Tests for Revision?

Mock tests are not only meant to evaluate your preparation, but they can also be used as an effective revision tool. Instead of revising everything separately, you can use your mock test performance to guide what needs attention.

- Note down repeated mistakes after each test to identify weak areas
- Revise formulas and concepts from the questions you got wrong
- Reattempt difficult or incorrect questions after a few days to check improvement
- Track your performance over time to see which areas are improving and which still need work

## Best Strategy to Improve Mock Test Scores

Improving mock test scores is not about attempting more tests—it is about learning from each one. Many students keep giving tests but see no improvement because they skip the correction part. After every mock test, take some time to understand where you went wrong and what can be improved. This approach gradually helps in building both accuracy and confidence.

- Work on weak areas identified after each test
- Revise formulas regularly to avoid conceptual mistakes
- Solve previous year questions to understand exam trends
- Focus on maintaining a balance between speed and accuracy
- Avoid guesswork, especially in sections with negative marking

Mock tests alone are not enough. They should be used along with revision and previous year question papers to improve both understanding and performance.
