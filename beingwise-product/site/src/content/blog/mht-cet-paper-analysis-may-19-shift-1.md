## Subject-wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis for May 19 Shift 1

The subject-wise MHT CET PCM Paper Analysis assists candidates in understanding the difficulty level, question pattern, and key subjects covered in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Here is the complete subject-wise MHT CET 2026 May 19 Shift 1 Paper Analysis with important topics listed below.

### MHT CET Paper Analysis 2026 for Physics Section

As per the initial reactions of students, the Physics section was moderate in difficulty level. Questions in this section were mostly formula-based and numerical. Students claimed that more weightage was given to class 12 syllabus questions than class 11 questions.

Some numericals in this section were somewhat time-consuming, particularly from the Electricity and Magnetism chapters. But students who had practiced formula-based questions regularly found this section easy to solve. Let’s take a quick look at the important topics asked in the physics section.

- Current Electricity
- Electrostatics
- Magnetism
- Modern Physics
- Ray Optics
- Semiconductor
- Oscillations
- Thermodynamics

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Mathematics Section

According to the MHT CET Paper analysis, the Mathematics section was the longest section in the MHT CET exam held on May 19, Shift 1. While calculations in this section were time-consuming, the overall conceptual level of the questions was moderate.

Questions needed good time management and calculation skills. Calculus, Vectors, and Probability questions had relatively higher weightage. Some students also mentioned that some questions required lengthy multi-step solutions.

Take a look at the Important Topics Asked in Mathematics

- Integration
- Probability
- Vectors
- Line and Plane
- Differential Equations
- Matrices and Determinants
- Application of Derivatives
- Trigonometry

### MHT CET Paper Analysis for Chemistry Section

Chemistry was regarded as the easiest and highest-scoring section of the paper. Most questions in the section were directly from the NCERT and required simple conceptual understanding. For those who had prepared well using NCERT books, this section turned out to be very easy. Take a look at the important topics asked in Chemistry

- Chemical Bonding
- Coordination Compounds
- Electrochemistry
- Amines
- Biomolecules
- Thermodynamics
- Solutions
- p-Block Elements
