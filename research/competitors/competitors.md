## GetMyUni

**Site:** https://www.getmyuni.com/

**Business model:** Primarily a lead-generation and college-marketing/branding business, NOT a paid student-counselling service. GetMyUni monetizes by selling student leads and 'branding solutions' / 'cost-optimal marketing and communications' to colleges and universities (a one-stop student recruitment solution driving ~10,000 enrollments to 200+ universities, ~40M annual student visits). Predictor tools and content act as the lead-capture funnel: results require the student's name, email and phone, which feed the lead pipeline. Acquired by CollegeDekho in Feb 2022 (~INR 50 cr); within the group GetMyUni supplies 'leads and branding solutions' while CollegeDekho runs end-to-end enrollment/counselling. There is no transparent direct-to-student paid JoSAA counselling product; the large 'JEE counselling fee' figures shown on their pages (e.g. INR 45,000 general / INR 20,000 reserved) refer to the official JoSAA seat-acceptance fee, not a GetMyUni service charge.

**Pricing:** No published direct-to-student paid JoSAA counselling package or price tier. Student-facing tools (predictors, reviews, articles) are free but gated behind phone/email lead capture; revenue comes from colleges paying for leads/branding, not students. The fee numbers on their JEE counselling pages (INR 45,000 general; INR 20,000 SC/ST/OBC-NCL/PwD for 2025) are the official JoSAA seat-acceptance fees, not GetMyUni's own pricing. (Parent CollegeDekho markets 'customised counselling & admission services' but likewise publishes no clear standalone fee.) Comparable paid private JoSAA counselling in the market runs roughly INR 1,000 (per-slot, MyExams.ai) up to INR 15,000-25,000 (V4Edusolution standard/premium), indicating the price band a transparent paid co-pilot would compete against.

**Offerings:**
- College/course discovery database (42,000+ colleges) with city, course and exam filters
- Student-driven college reviews & ratings (247,000+ reviews) plus community Q&A/forums
- Exam info hubs for JEE Main, NEET, CAT, CLAT, etc. (dates, syllabus, pattern, mock tests)
- JEE Main College Predictor (rank/percentile -> probable colleges) and JEE Main Rank Predictor
- Other predictors: BITSAT, KCET, CMAT, MAT, JEE Advanced rank predictor
- Informational JoSAA/JEE counselling articles covering registration, choice filling, seat acceptance, freeze/float/slide, documents
- Scholarship database and study-abroad guidance (US/UK/Canada/Australia/Germany)
- Free mock tests / test series (testseries.getmyuni.com)

**User complaints:**
- Spam/repeat calls after entering phone number: user reported 'horrible experience they keep on calling if you enter your no... for the same institution... even though I have told them I am not interested' and asked how to get their number removed from the database (Trustburn/Scamadviser)
- Data-privacy concerns: students unable to opt out / get number removed; colleges complained their details were listed 'without taking any permission'
- Inaccurate information: user noted a wrong exam date posted ('Exam date mentioned it as wrong for ESIC')
- Predictor accuracy caveats: tools rely on previous-year data and are 'not final and accurate'; cutoffs vary by year, seat-matrix changes and new colleges, so outputs can mismatch real JoSAA allotments
- Credibility of on-site reviews questioned: reviewer warned 'don't believe the rating and reviews they are not legit'
- Employee allegations of dishonest practices: Glassdoor/Indeed reviews claim staff are 'trained to speak lies to the clients', made to give 'fake attendance' at webinars, and that the company 'charge the students extra and lie to them'
- Lead-gen funnel friction: detailed predictor results require name/email/phone, driving the very call spam students complain about

**Gaps we can exploit:**
- No guided choice-list builder: predictors output a flat probable-college list but offer no tool to rank, order and lock a JoSAA preference list with reasoning
- No float/freeze/slide simulator: students widely confuse these (e.g. choosing Float when they only want a same-college branch upgrade); GetMyUni only explains them in articles, never lets users simulate round-by-round outcomes
- No deadline command center: no personalized tracker for round dates, choice-locking, seat-acceptance-fee payment, reporting and document deadlines
- No no-spam guarantee: model depends on harvesting and selling student phone numbers, so it structurally cannot promise spam-free use - a clear trust wedge
- No live seat/round data: predictions lean on historical cutoffs rather than real-time seat availability across JoSAA rounds
- Conflict of interest / bias risk: college-paid lead-gen and 'branding' model creates incentive to steer students toward paying partner institutes rather than purely best-fit advice
- No transparent, student-aligned pricing: opaque monetization vs a flat-fee student co-pilot that students actually pay for and that owes loyalty to the student, not the college

## CollegeDekho

**Site:** https://www.collegedekho.com

**Business model:** Primarily lead-generation / enrollment-commission, NOT a paid consumer counselling product. Colleges and universities pay CollegeDekho a success-based fee for each student enrollment it facilitates, which is roughly 60-65% of revenue (FY24-25). The 'free counselling' and Common Application Form are the top of a funnel that captures student contact data and routes students toward fee-paying partner institutions - it states nearly 60% of all Indian students seeking private-college education use it. Secondary revenue: sponsored listings/native advertising for institutions, premium one-on-one paid counselling (subscription or one-time fee), education loans/ed-fintech, study-abroad services, and events/webinars. Backed by Girnar Software (CarDekho parent); has raised ~$78M from investors including Man Capital and Calega.

**Pricing:** No transparent public price for paid consumer counselling - it is sold via subscription or one-time fee quoted after a lead form / consultation, and third-party reviews call the services 'overpriced.' The headline 'counselling' is free because students are monetized through college commissions, not fees. The fee figures CollegeDekho publishes (e.g. JoSAA IIT Seat Acceptance Fee Rs 35,000 general / Rs 17,500 reserved; NIT+ Rs 35,000 SAF + Rs 45,000 partial admission fee) are the government's official JoSAA fees, not CollegeDekho's own charges. Refund policy: no refund once payment is received after a college's application deadline, and no guarantee of admission.

**Offerings:**
- Free 'expert counselling' funnel covering the full cycle: course selection, college shortlisting, application, and fee payment (used as lead capture)
- Common Application Form (CAF): single form to apply to 2,000+ partner colleges
- College/exam information portal across 35,000+ listed colleges plus QnA, articles, and news
- JEE Main / JEE Advanced / JoSAA college predictor tools based on previous-year opening/closing ranks
- Static JoSAA content (choice-filling, float/freeze/slide explanations, fees & refund articles) - informational only, no interactive simulator
- CollegeDekho Assured (job-ready/industry skills bundled with degree programs)
- Study Abroad counselling and visa/scholarship guidance
- Ed-fintech: education loans / 'zero percent interest' financing
- 'Right to Refund' service to help students recover college fees
- Acquired brands GetMyUni, PrepBytes, ImaginXP for listings and upskilling

**User complaints:**
- Spam / aggressive follow-up: registering or submitting a form leads to persistent counsellor calls and promotional contact; consent-withdrawal must be requested via compliance@girnarsoft.com
- Took money upfront with promises never fulfilled; point-of-contact then stopped responding and even blocked students on calls (MouthShut)
- Inaccurate information: wrong details on admission process, cut-off marks, syllabus, fees, and placement opportunities
- Biased/poor recommendations: students report bad course and college suggestions; model incentivizes steering toward fee-paying partner (largely private) colleges rather than best-fit
- Allegations of paid/fake reviews boosting CollegeDekho and listed institutions, hurting credibility
- Lack of transparency about what the service actually delivers and what is paid vs free
- Outdated content (e.g. study-abroad pre-departure / regulation info) that can mislead students
- Services described as overpriced; declining employee Glassdoor rating (~3.3/5, down 13% YoY) signaling internal churn behind the counselling

**Gaps we can exploit:**
- No guided choice-list builder: JoSAA choice-filling pages are static articles plus a generic 'predictor'; there is no tool that constructs and orders an actual preference list for the official portal
- No float/freeze/slide simulator: they explain these options in a table but offer no interactive sandbox to test how a decision plays out across rounds
- No no-spam guarantee: their model depends on lead capture and college commissions, so they are structurally unable to promise zero sales calls - a clean differentiator for a neutral co-pilot
- No deadline command center: no per-round JoSAA timeline / reminder system tracking registration, choice locking, seat acceptance, and document deadlines
- Conflict-of-interest neutrality gap: commission-from-colleges model creates bias toward partner (private) colleges; an unbiased, advice-only product that takes no college money is a wedge
- Accuracy/freshness gap: recurring complaints about wrong cut-offs and outdated info open room for a verified, official-data-synced tool
- Pricing transparency gap: opaque/quote-after-lead pricing vs a clear flat-fee or free advice-only co-pilot

## Independent edu-influencer / YouTuber paid JoSAA counselling services (category teardown)

**Site:** https://myexams.ai/jee-main-josaa-counselling/

**Business model:** Hybrid lead-gen + paid. The dominant pattern is a free funnel (free college predictor, free YouTube/Telegram strategy content, and a 'Get FREE Session') that converts students into paid 1-on-1 counselling. Two monetization variants exist: (1) flat-fee paid packages (MyExams.Ai, V4Edusolution, ATP STAR, WeWon) charged upfront before any seat is allotted, often non-refundable; and (2) performance-priced 'No Seat, No Fees' refund model (Counselling Suite). Influencers/coaching brands also monetize the audience itself: rank+phone data feeds aggressive outbound marketing, and the free predictor doubles as a paid PDF upsell. Delivery is human-mentor-driven and manual, not a software product.

**Pricing:** Wide range, mostly INR 2,499–25,000. MyExams.Ai: Free self-serve; Basic INR 2,499 + 18% GST (one-time expert assistance); Premium INR 9,999 + 18% GST (full Freeze/Float/Slide coaching + mock allotment); Combo (JoSAA+JAC Delhi) INR 14,999 + 18% GST. V4Edusolution: Free Basic; Standard INR 3,000 (one counselling); Premium INR 5,000 (JoSAA+CSAB+state), 10% pre-exam discount. Counselling Suite: undisclosed 'affordable' price, 'No Seat, No Fees' full refund, price only via phone. WeWon Academy: undisclosed, 'JOSAA' early-bird discount code. ATP STAR JMP and Divastra/Prabhat Ranjan: price not publicly listed (gated behind contact). Note: official JoSAA itself charges nothing for registration/choice filling — only the ~INR 35,000 Seat Acceptance Fee, which these services do not cover.

**Offerings:**
- 1-on-1 'expert counsellor' guidance through JoSAA/CSAB choice filling (e.g., MyExams.Ai, Counselling Suite, V4Edusolution, WeWon Academy, ATP STAR JMP)
- Rank-based college/branch recommendation list delivered as a downloadable PDF or via an online portal
- Round-wise Freeze/Float/Slide strategy coaching delivered ad-hoc over call/WhatsApp (not as a self-serve simulator)
- Mock seat-allotment analysis and 'preference optimization' done manually by the mentor
- Combo packages bundling JoSAA + CSAB + state counselling (JAC Delhi, REAP/UPTAC, etc.)
- Free college/rank predictor tools used as the funnel entry point
- Free Q&A / strategy content on YouTube, Telegram and Instagram (Harsh Sir, Snehit Mishra, WeWon Academy, ATP STAR) feeding the paid services

**User complaints:**
- Service often just generates a college-list PDF from the same free predictor data and provides no real support or doubt resolution — 'scammers take money and just use our predictor to generate a PDF of colleges'
- Students feel paid help is unnecessary because free predictors use the same official JoSAA closing-rank data the paid mentors rely on (could have done it myself)
- Aggressive spam: after rank publication students get a wave of congratulatory/admission calls and 'scam counselling services' — worsened by the JEE Advanced 2026 cloud data exposure (1.79 lakh records, names + mobile numbers) enabling third parties to scrape rank+phone
- Student databases (names, phone, parent info) openly sold for as low as INR 999, directly fueling admission/counselling spam calls
- Non-refundable, pay-before-allotment terms (e.g., 'all amounts paid are strictly non-refundable') with prices charged before any seat is guaranteed
- Pricing opacity — several services (Counselling Suite, WeWon, ATP STAR) hide price behind a 'free session'/phone-call lead-gen wall
- Coaching/influencer credibility concerns: documented industry pattern of misleading topper/rank claims and category ambiguity, eroding trust in influencer-led advice
- Generic, non-personalized advice and one-time/ad-hoc WhatsApp support rather than continuous, deadline-aware guidance

**Gaps we can exploit:**
- No guided choice-list builder: services hand over a static PDF or verbal advice; there is no interactive tool that helps a student construct and reorder their full preference list with reasoning per choice
- No Float/Freeze/Slide simulator: round-wise decisions are coached manually/ad-hoc over WhatsApp; no self-serve simulator showing the downstream outcome of freeze vs float vs slide
- No no-spam / data-privacy guarantee: the entire category is built on rank+phone data and outbound calls; a product with an explicit no-spam, no-data-selling guarantee directly counters the #1 trust complaint
- No deadline command center: no service offers an integrated, personalized tracker for JoSAA round dates, choice-locking deadlines, reporting, fee pre-payment and withdrawal windows
- No transparency: pricing is opaque and advice quality is unverifiable — a transparent, fixed-price, evidence-cited co-pilot beats hidden-price lead-gen funnels
- Not personalized at scale: human mentors cap throughput and give generic lists; a software co-pilot can personalize using official cutoff data + home-state/category logic instantly
- Round volatility ignored: critics note tools/advice using only one round's data ignore round-to-round seat shifts — a simulator modeling volatility is a clear differentiator
- Outcome accountability: most are flat-fee and non-refundable; a co-pilot with transparent, self-serve, low/no-cost access removes the 'paid but did nothing' risk

## Careers360

**Site:** https://www.careers360.com/

**Business model:** Hybrid: primarily a lead-generation and advertising portal layered with freemium paid products. Free predictors and content capture student phone/email/exam details (OTP-gated), and those leads are monetized by sharing/selling to colleges, coaching centers and admission brokers, plus on-page college advertising. On top of lead-gen, Careers360 sells paid products directly: a one-time paid college predictor (Session 2), paid e-learning 'Knockout' packages, and higher-ticket counselling 'Companion'/subscription bundles with expert calls. Company reported ~Rs 55.7 Cr annual revenue (FY ending Mar 2025) and raised ~$5.31M across 3 rounds (Series A, Gurugram, founded 2008).

**Pricing:** College predictor free for JEE Session 1, then a paid unlock (~Rs 499 reported by users) for Session 2 / full reports. Career counselling sessions reportedly Rs 1,500-5,000 per session, with multi-session/comprehensive packages up to Rs 10,000+. One user reported paying Rs 10,000 for a counselling membership; NEET MBBS Companion sold as a bundled subscription (predictor + Almanac + webinars + 3 expert calls). Products stated to be non-refundable. Many phone calls / WhatsApp (+91 6005956597, 0124-4444360) used for sales follow-up.

**Offerings:**
- JEE Main & JEE Advanced College Predictor (rank/percentile based) covering JoSAA IITs/NITs/IIITs/GFTIs
- CSAB and state-level (Maharashtra, WB, Delhi, Haryana, MP, etc.) college predictors
- Rank/score predictors for JEE Main, WBJEE, GATE, CAT, NEET, etc.
- JoSAA counselling editorial content: schedule, cutoffs, freeze/float/slide explainers, 'mistakes to avoid', FAQs
- Community Q&A forum where students post counselling doubts and get expert answers
- Free e-books, sample papers, previous-year papers and study material (lead-capture gated)
- NEET 'MBBS Companion' / counselling subscription: predictor + printed Almanac + webinars + 3 one-to-one expert counselling sessions, covering AIQ + 22 state counsellings
- Knockout JEE/NEET e-learning prep packages (5000+ concept videos, 20000+ question bank, mock tests)
- Careers360 mobile app (Android/iOS) bundling predictors, e-library, webinars and companion tools
- Advertising / lead-generation platform for colleges and institutes to reach prospective students

**User complaints:**
- Aggressive spam: users forced to enter phone/email and within ~24 hours get flooded with calls/SMS/emails from college-admission brokers and coaching centers; data shared with institutes without consent
- No easy way to delete/deactivate account or stop the spam once registered
- Payment failures on the paid (Rs 499) predictor - money debited but report not delivered, and being asked to pay again after already paying
- Predictor accuracy doubts: cutoff ranks not matching official JoSAA data; sometimes lists closed/defunct colleges or unrealistic college-rank pairings
- 'Not worth it' sentiment - paid Rs 499/Rs 10,000 and felt the value didn't match the cost
- Counselling membership bait-and-switch complaint: company responsive before payment, unresponsive after (Rs 10,000 membership)
- Non-refundable policy frustrates buyers who feel misled
- Predictor experience gated behind login/OTP, locking even basic use behind lead capture
- Generic, content-farm style guidance (articles/FAQs) rather than personalized, interactive help

**Gaps we can exploit:**
- No guided choice-list builder: they explain freeze/float/slide and 'mistakes to avoid' in articles but offer no interactive tool that helps a student actually order and lock their JoSAA preference list
- No float/freeze/slide simulator: nothing lets a student model what happens to their seat across rounds if they choose float vs slide vs freeze
- No no-spam guarantee: their whole model depends on monetizing the lead, so they cannot credibly promise zero spam - a clear trust wedge for a privacy-first co-pilot
- No deadline command center: counselling round dates, reporting windows, fee-payment and document-upload deadlines live scattered across news articles rather than a single personalized timeline with reminders
- Predictor is one-shot and static (rank in -> list out) with no scenario planning, no 'what-if' across categories/home-state/rounds, and no live integration with the JoSAA flow
- Counselling help is upsold human calls (slow, costly, non-refundable) rather than always-on automated guidance
- Bias/trust gap: as an ad/lead-gen platform there is perceived incentive to surface paying/advertiser colleges, vs a neutral co-pilot optimizing purely for the student's rank and preferences
- No transparent, free, login-optional experience - core utility is gated behind OTP and paywalls

## CollegeDunia

**Site:** https://collegedunia.com/

**Business model:** Primarily lead-generation and advertising, NOT a paid student counselling product. CollegeDunia monetizes by collecting student contact details (phone/email) when they register, use the predictor, or express interest in a college, then sells/shares those leads to colleges, universities, and coaching/consultancies. Revenue streams: Cost-Per-Lead (CPL), Cost-Per-Sale/admission commission (CPS), sponsored/featured college listings, banner and contextual advertising against high traffic, and premium analytics/ad campaigns sold to institutions. Its Study Abroad arm operates as a paid admissions consultancy (B2C service revenue). For JoSAA/JEE specifically it is an information + predictor + lead-gen play, with the student as the product (data monetized to colleges), not the paying customer.

**Pricing:** For Indian JEE/JoSAA students, CollegeDunia's tools (college predictor, counselling info, college search) are free to use — the student pays nothing because monetization is via lead sales and ads, not a counselling fee. There is no publicly listed per-student paid JoSAA counselling package from CollegeDunia itself (paid expert JoSAA counselling in the market comes from competitors like CounsellingSuite, MyExams.ai, BodMas, V4Edusolution). The paid side of CollegeDunia is B2B (colleges buying leads — e.g., a Trustpilot reviewer reported paying ~Rs 50,000 for 425 leads) and its Study Abroad consultancy (paid service, fees not publicly disclosed).
