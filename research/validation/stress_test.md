# Problem Stress-Test — Is this a good problem?

## Verdict

CONDITIONAL GO — but only as a wedge, not a standalone paid product. The problem is real, severe, and high-stakes (one irreversible decision determines a student's college/branch for 4 years), and people demonstrably pay ₹3,000–₹25,000 for human counselling. BUT the core "co-pilot" function (rank → ranked choice list) is already heavily commoditized by 8+ free predictors and free choice-list generators, and the business is brutally seasonal (one ~3-week window/year, near-zero retention per user). A paid AI co-pilot scores well on severity and WTP but poorly on defensibility and recurring revenue. Honest score 5.5/10. It is a GOOD problem to solve but a HARD problem to monetize directly — win only if you out-trust the lead-gen-spam incumbents and bundle the seasonal one-shot into a year-round funnel (prep → mentorship → counselling) rather than selling counselling alone.

**Score: 5.5/10**

## Market size signals (TAM)

- JEE Main 2025: 15,39,848 unique candidates registered, 14,75,103 appeared — a ~1.5M/year top-of-funnel that fully refreshes annually
- JEE Advanced 2025: 1,80,422 appeared, 54,378 qualified — the high-WTP IIT-bound segment
- JoSAA 2024/25: 121 participating institutes (23 IITs, 31 NITs, IIEST, 26 IIITs, ~29-40 GFTIs), ~60,000-62,000 seats — the constrained, high-stakes allocation pool
- Experts advise filling 40-100 ordered choices with no cap and 'hundreds of combinations' — genuine decision-complexity creating the pain
- CSAB Special 2024: of ~41,000 NIT+ seats allotted only ~28,700 accepted, leaving ~13,500 vacant — proves massive churn/confusion and a SECOND counselling event per year
- State counselling (MHT CET, WBJEE, KCET, TNEA, AP/TS EAMCET, KEAM etc.) multiplies the addressable aspirant base well beyond the ~1.5M JEE pool; MHT CET 2025 alone left 21,000+ seats vacant
- Even at 1-3% paid conversion of ~1.5M JEE aspirants at ₹3k-5k, the math implies a multi-crore seasonal market — but it is split across many incumbents

## Demand evidence

- Dozens of recurring Reddit threads every cycle on r/JEENEETards, r/JEE, r/Btechtards explicitly asking 'best choice-filling order', 'how many choices', 'freeze or float', 'I locked wrong choices' — same questions repeat every single year (context URLs span 2022-2025)
- Multiple community-built tools already exist BECAUSE of demand: 'we scraped 500 JoSAA choices', 'don't fill your choices blindly' automated-filling threads, and Engineering Hulk's JoSAA Choice List Generator built on 2024 closing ranks
- Careers360, Collegedunia, Shiksha, CollegeDekho, eSaral, MathonGo, JoSAA Live, RankMatrix all ship free college predictors — heavy supply chasing this demand confirms it is real
- Documented 'major mistakes' content (wrong order, not locking, filling only 1-2 choices) shows widespread, repeatable user error — i.e., an unsolved competence gap
- IIT-senior mentorship platforms (JEE Carnot, PW DISHA, UnchaAi, DhruvaMentors, Exprto) bundle JoSAA guidance — established players see counselling as a monetizable attach

## Willingness-to-pay evidence

- V4Edusolution sells JoSAA/CSAB counselling at Standard ₹3,000 (one counselling) and Premium ₹5,000 (all 6 JoSAA rounds + CSAB + state) — concrete live price points, claims 5L+ students served
- Cross-source consensus: 'JEE counsellor fees in India range ₹5,000–₹25,000 depending on package and rounds covered' — establishes the human-counselling ceiling
- Premium human-guided packages quoted up to ₹25,000 (end-to-end senior counsellor) — proves a high-WTP top tier exists
- Mentorship entry points as low as ₹149 (PW DISHA) and ₹249 (JEE Carnot) — shows price-sensitive floor and that students DO transact on guidance
- Official JoSAA seat-acceptance fee is ₹30,000 (₹15,000 reserved) on a decision worth a 4-year degree — anchors that paying a few thousand for guidance is rational relative to stakes

## Risks

- COMMODITIZATION: the core rank→college-list function is free from 8+ established players (Careers360, Collegedunia, Shiksha, CollegeDekho, eSaral, MathonGo, RankMatrix, JoSAA Live); hard to charge for what's already free
- SEASONALITY: demand compresses into ~3 weeks (JoSAA registration/choice-filling early-mid June) + CSAB/state tail through Sep; ~9 months of dead revenue and re-acquisition cost every year
- ZERO RETENTION per user: each student uses it once in a lifetime — pure acquisition treadmill, no LTV compounding, no recurring revenue
- TRUST GAP / LEAD-GEN SPAM: the category is polluted by admission-guidance firms that masquerade as counsellors to sell seats (documented 'career counselling is a scam' critique); a new paid entrant is presumed predatory until proven otherwise
- DATA RISK: predictions rely on prior-year JoSAA closing ranks; a bad call (student misses a seat) is catastrophic and reputation-destroying in a one-shot, irreversible process — liability and word-of-mouth downside are asymmetric
- INCUMBENT BUNDLING: PW, Allen, Careers360 can give counselling away free as a loss-leader to their captive prep audiences, structurally undercutting a standalone paid tool
- FREE-EXPECTATION CULTURE: multiple sources stress 'JoSAA registration/choice-filling is free' and 'many reliable predictors are completely free with no login' — strong anchoring against paying

## Kill criteria (when to walk away)

- KILL if you cannot beat free predictors on a dimension users actually value (e.g., personalized risk-modeling, regret-minimizing order, or trusted human-in-loop) — a marginally-better free-equivalent will not convert at any price
- KILL if paid conversion in a live pilot is <1-2% of free users, OR blended CAC exceeds ~₹3,000-5,000 ARPU given single-use, no-repeat economics
- KILL if you can't break the trust barrier — if early users associate you with the lead-gen-spam category (cold calls, selling seats), conversion and referrals collapse
- KILL if the model can only sell counselling and nothing else: a once-a-year, zero-retention SKU cannot sustain a standalone company; needs a year-round wedge (prep, mentorship, mock tests) it attaches to
- KILL if a wrong/over-confident recommendation in pilot causes even a handful of students to miss seats they should have gotten — the reputational asymmetry in a one-shot irreversible decision is fatal
- KILL if BITS/state-counselling/NEET expansion can't extend the usable window beyond the ~3-week JoSAA spike — otherwise the seasonality makes it un-fundable
