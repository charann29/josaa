You did the hard part — your JEE rank is in and JoSAA allotted you a seat. Now comes the step where people actually lose seats they had already won: **reporting and document verification**. Not because of rank. Because of a misspelled name, an expired certificate, or a deadline that slipped by.

This guide walks you through every document you need for JoSAA 2026, how the **Seat Acceptance Fee** works, and the exact order of steps after allotment. Use it as a checklist, not a one-time read. For the full picture of how counselling fits together, start with our [complete JoSAA 2026 guide](/blog/josaa-counselling-2026-complete-guide).

> **One line to remember:** an allotted seat is not a confirmed seat. You only keep it after you accept it, pay the fee, report, and clear verification — all inside the deadline.

## The standard JoSAA document list

Below is the well-known standard set most JoSAA candidates need. Treat it as your starting checklist — but **always verify it against the current year's business rules and reporting brochure** on the official portal, because formats and add-ons change year to year.

### Core documents (everyone)

- [ ] **JoSAA seat allotment letter** (download after the round result)
- [ ] **Provisional seat acceptance / fee receipt** (after you pay the SAF)
- [ ] **JEE Main 2026 admit card**
- [ ] **JEE Main 2026 scorecard / rank card**
- [ ] **JEE Advanced 2026 scorecard** (only if you wrote Advanced / are taking an IIT seat)
- [ ] **Class X marksheet and passing certificate** (proof of date of birth)
- [ ] **Class XII marksheet and passing certificate**
- [ ] **Photo identity proof** (Aadhaar / passport / voter ID / PAN)
- [ ] **Recent passport-size photographs** (keep 6–8, same photo as your JEE form ideally)
- [ ] **Medical / fitness certificate** in the prescribed format (some institutes ask for this)

### Conditional documents (only if they apply to you)

- [ ] **Category certificate** — SC / ST / OBC-NCL in the correct **central government (GoI) format**, issued recently and within validity
- [ ] **EWS certificate** — for the current financial year, in the prescribed format
- [ ] **PwD / disability certificate** — valid, from an authorised medical authority, if you claimed a PwD seat
- [ ] **OCI / PIO / foreign-national documents** — if applicable
- [ ] **DS (Defence Service) certificate** — if you claimed that quota
- [ ] **Name / DOB correction proof** — affidavit, gazette notification or board certificate, if anything differs across your documents

For how category and home-state quotas actually decide your seat, read [home-state quota and category seats explained](/blog/home-state-quota-category-seats).

> **Carry originals plus two sets of self-attested photocopies.** Verification is done on originals; the centre keeps copies. Keep clear scans on your phone and email as backup.

## The Seat Acceptance Fee (SAF), simply

The **Seat Acceptance Fee** is the official amount you pay on josaa.nic.in to confirm that you accept an allotted seat. No payment, no seat — it is that simple.

- It is approximately **₹30,000 for general** candidates and around **₹15,000 for reserved** categories (SC/ST/PwD). **Confirm the current figure on the official portal** before you pay — we never quote it as final.
- You pay it **once**, online, and it **carries across rounds**. If you chose Float or Slide and your seat upgrades in a later round, you do **not** pay it again.
- It is **adjusted against your institute admission fee** later. It is not money lost and not an extra charge.
- It is paid **only on the official portal** to JoSAA — never to any agent, consultant, or "reporting helper." Anyone asking you to pay a seat fee to them is a red flag.

## What happens after allotment: the four steps

After each round result, the process runs in a fixed order. Miss a step and the seat is gone.

### Step 1 — Accept the seat and choose your option

Log in, view your allotted seat, and select **Float, Freeze or Slide**. This choice decides whether you stay put or try for an upgrade in the next round. If you are unsure which to pick, our [choice-filling mistakes guide](/blog/josaa-choice-filling-mistakes) covers the traps here.

### Step 2 — Pay the Seat Acceptance Fee online

Pay the SAF on the portal and **download the receipt immediately**. Until this is done and confirmed, your acceptance is incomplete.

### Step 3 — Online reporting and document upload

Upload scanned copies of the required documents in the formats the portal asks for (correct file type, size, and clarity). This is where the system or a verifying officer does an initial check. **Watch the file-size and format limits** — a rejected upload because the scan was too large is a needless way to miss a deadline.

### Step 4 — Physical reporting and original verification

When the schedule calls for it, report **physically at the reporting centre or your allotted institute** with all originals and photocopies. Officials verify your originals against your uploaded copies and your claimed category. Only after this is your admission confirmed for that round.

> Steps and deadlines differ by round and by year. Re-check the official schedule every round — do not assume this round works exactly like the last one.

## The mismatches that quietly cancel seats

Most cancellations are not about rank or merit. They are small inconsistencies that surface at verification. Catch them **before** you reach the desk.

| Common mistake | Why it cancels a seat | How to fix it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Name spelling differs** across JEE form, marksheet and ID | Verifier can't confirm you are the same person | Carry a board certificate, affidavit or gazette correction proving the link |
| **Date of birth differs** between Class X and JEE/ID | DOB is verified from the Class X certificate | Carry the original Class X certificate and a correction affidavit if needed |
| **Category certificate in wrong format** (state format, not GoI) | Reserved seats need the central format | Get a fresh certificate in the correct GoI format before counselling |
| **Expired or old category/EWS certificate** | Validity (often current FY) has lapsed | Apply early for a current-dated certificate |
| **Photo mismatch / unclear photo** | Identity can't be verified | Use the same photo as your JEE form; carry extra prints |
| **Blurred or oversized uploads** | Portal rejects the file | Re-scan clearly within the size/format limits |
| **EWS income/asset criteria not met or wrong year** | Claim found invalid at verification | Confirm eligibility and certificate year before claiming |
| **Missing original at physical reporting** | Verification can't be completed | Build the full checklist; carry originals + two photocopy sets |

A single mismatch on a reserved-category document can cost you the reserved seat entirely — sometimes pushing you to a general-category cut-off you don't clear. This is the highest-stakes part of reporting, so treat your certificate format and validity as seriously as your rank.

## Prepare early: a simple timeline

You don't need to wait for an allotment to start. Most of this can be done the week your result is expected.

- **Before results:** Get every certificate in the correct format and within validity. Re-issue anything old. Fix name/DOB mismatches now — corrections take time.
- **Right after allotment:** Download the allotment letter. Note the accept-and-pay deadline in your calendar the same hour.
- **Same day:** Accept, choose Float/Freeze/Slide, pay the SAF, save the receipt.
- **Within the window:** Complete online reporting and upload clean scans.
- **On the reporting date:** Carry originals + two photocopy sets + extra photos. Reach early; queues are long.

> **Make a physical folder.** One labelled folder with originals, one with photocopies, plus a phone gallery album of scans. When you are tired and the queue is moving, organisation is what saves you.

## Quick pre-reporting checklist

Run through this the night before:

- [ ] Allotment letter and SAF receipt printed
- [ ] All originals in one folder
- [ ] Two self-attested photocopy sets of each
- [ ] Category / EWS / PwD certificate in correct format and valid
- [ ] Name and DOB consistent — correction proof carried if not
- [ ] 6–8 passport photos
- [ ] Reporting centre address, date and time confirmed
- [ ] Scans backed up on phone and email
- [ ] Deadline double-checked on the official portal
