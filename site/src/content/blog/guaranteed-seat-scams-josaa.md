The day your result comes out, the calls start. Some are spam. Some are something worse: a confident voice promising your child a **guaranteed seat in an NIT or IIT** — for a fee. During counselling, anxious families are the perfect target, and these scams cost real money. Here is exactly how they work and how to stay safe.

> **The one fact that defeats every "guaranteed seat" pitch:** JoSAA allotment is automated and rule-based. Your seat is decided by your rank, category, quota and the order of your choices — nobody, anywhere, can "arrange," "fix," or "guarantee" it.

## Why these scams thrive during counselling

Counselling is high-stakes, irreversible and confusing. Parents who don't fully understand the process are scared of one thing: their child losing a seat they deserved. Scammers sell relief from that fear. Two things make it worse:

- **Leaked data.** Rank-plus-phone-number data circulates and is sold during results season. That's why callers already "know" your child's rank — it feels legitimate, but it's just leaked data. (More on this in [free predictors and spam](/blog/free-college-predictor-spam-trust).)
- **Genuine complexity.** Because real counselling *does* involve choices, quotas and rounds, a fraudster can wrap a lie inside real-sounding jargon.

## The common pitches (and the truth)

| The pitch | The truth |
|---|---|
| "Guaranteed seat in NIT/IIT — pay now" | Impossible. Allotment is automated; no human controls it. |
| "Management quota seat in JoSAA" | Does not exist. Management quota is only in some private colleges, never in JoSAA. |
| "Pay a donation and we'll upgrade your seat" | Capitation/donation for seats is illegal; upgrades only happen through float/freeze/slide rounds. |
| "Special link to fill your choices for you" | Never share your JoSAA login/OTP. Only josaa.nic.in is official. |
| "Limited seats, decide in 10 minutes" | Pressure is the oldest trick. Real processes have published deadlines. |

### Legitimate management quota vs the fraud claim

To be fair: *some private and deemed universities* do have a legal management-quota admission, at their published higher fee. That is a private-college process. The fraud is claiming a "management quota" or "donation seat" inside **JoSAA / NITs / IITs** — there is no such thing. If you do consider a private backup, read [how private-college deposits can be forfeited](/blog/private-college-deposit-forfeiture) first.

## Red flags vs a legitimate service

| Red flag (walk away) | Legitimate guidance |
|---|---|
| Promises a specific college/seat | Promises a correct, well-ordered list and honest odds |
| Asks for your JoSAA password or OTP | Never asks for login/OTP — you submit yourself |
| Demands cash/UPI to a personal number to "block" a seat | Transparent, published price for advice only |
| Creates panic and a 10-minute deadline | Works to the official JoSAA calendar |
| Won't put claims in writing | Explains the process and points you to official sources |

## How to verify and protect yourself

1. **Treat every unsolicited call as suspect.** You did not ask them to call.
2. **Verify only on official portals** — josaa.nic.in and csab.nic.in. Nothing a caller says overrides the portal.
3. **Never pay to "fix" an allotment.** There is nothing to fix; the system runs on rules. The only legitimate payment is the official Seat Acceptance Fee on the portal.
4. **Never share login or OTP.** Anyone asking is trying to take control of your choices.
5. **Slow down.** Real deadlines are published days in advance. Urgency is a weapon.
6. **Protect your number** going forward — see [predictor spam](/blog/free-college-predictor-spam-trust).

If you want help you can actually trust, the test is simple: a real counsellor makes you *understand* your own list and never claims to control the result. That's the whole reason we explain the [full counselling process openly](/blog/josaa-counselling-2026-complete-guide) — informed families don't get scammed.

Anyone who guarantees you a seat is lying. Keep your money, trust the official portal, and make your decisions on data, not fear.
